Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Surrender & Faith (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 46:04 2023-05-11

What happens when the instinct to manage everything is set aside and the mind stays with helplessness, imperfection, and not-knowing? The talk moves from a vivid image of “all these hands” adjusting the knobs of samsara into a close examination of how practice asks for surrender rather than willfulness. It then broadens into a quiet argument that faith makes surrender feel safe, and that mindfulness, samadhi, and wisdom all depend on some willingness to let experience teach us.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 The urge to control - He opens with the image of many hands trying to adjust the world, and begins questioning our habit of always doing more.
  2. 02:37 A Christian contrast - Augustine’s language of restlessness and being remade is used as a vivid contrast for what surrender can mean.
  3. 04:06 Surrender in Dharma - He returns to Buddhism and argues that willfulness obscures the path, while surrender reveals aspects of practice we cannot force.
  4. 09:45 Conditioning and effort - Using William James and the dialysis metaphor, he describes habit, conditioning, and why retreat can stir the mind’s patterns instead of simply calming them.
  5. 17:35 Helplessness and faith - The talk turns toward helplessness as a basic human predicament and then toward faith as what makes surrender feel possible and safe.
  6. 24:50 Trusting the descent - He develops surrender as a gradual descent into experience, tied to love, trust, and the willingness to stop measuring progress all the time.
  7. 31:58 Mindfulness as release - Mindfulness, samadhi, and wisdom are recast as forms of letting go that soften rigid views and make room for receptive attention.
  8. 38:28 Opening to change - He closes by drawing on Ajahn Chah and predictive processing to show how letting go changes perception and loosens the claustrophobia of self.
