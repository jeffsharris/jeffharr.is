Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Helplessness and Power

AudioDharma 14:05 2022-04-11

What if helplessness is not a problem to solve, but the ground from which clearer power becomes possible? The talk moves from a set of paired teachings—suffering and well-being, impermanence and stability, selflessness and self-confidence—into a close look at how much of ordinary life is organized around resisting vulnerability and trying to stay in control. From there, it turns to a quieter kind of strength: opening to helplessness, stopping the fight against the First Noble Truth, and learning to use whatever power we do have more wisely. Along the way, the speaker connects helplessness with tears, self-blame, surrender, and the responsibility that comes with deeper practice.

Chapters

  1. 00:16 Nothing Dominates Everything - Matthew opens with William James and uses the word 'and' to point toward a teaching that holds opposites together rather than collapsing them.
  2. 00:54 Pairing the Teachings - He describes how retreat teachings on suffering, impermanence, and selflessness are paired with well-being, stability, and self-confidence.
  3. 02:16 The Reality of Helplessness - Helplessness is presented as a defining feature of life, one that can feel frightening, vulnerable, and deeply human.
  4. 05:01 Control and Self-Blame - Matthew looks at how the mind organizes itself against helplessness, including the urge to blame ourselves in order to preserve the illusion of control.
  5. 06:38 Opening to Surrender - He turns to surrender and letting go, suggesting that opening to helplessness can soften the heart and make change and death less startling.
  6. 09:12 Finding Wise Power - By stopping the fight against the First Noble Truth, he says, we can choose our battles more wisely and discover a more realistic form of power.
  7. 11:12 Practice Increases Impact - A teaching attributed to Kittisaro leads into a reflection on how deeper practice, ethics, concentration, and wisdom amplify both wholesome and unwholesome action.
  8. 13:18 Closing Reflections - Matthew closes by inviting listeners to keep what is useful, setting a calm and unfinished tone for the rest of the retreat.
