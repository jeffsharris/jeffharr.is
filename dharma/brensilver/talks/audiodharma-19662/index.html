Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation - Mindfulness of Feeling

AudioDharma 40:06 2024-01-24

The talk begins with the rain as a way into practice: letting sound soothe the body, noticing thirst, and settling into the repeated instruction to let go. From there, it turns to feeling as the place where worry, longing, and the sense that something must happen all register first, before they harden into emotional stories. The speaker keeps returning to the felt sense in the body, showing how to stay with urgency without obeying it, and how met feeling can become a quieter relationship to experience. By the end, the emphasis has shifted from reading life as right or wrong to meeting feeling with enough wisdom that it opens into love.

Chapters

  1. 01:00 Settling with Rain - The meditation opens by inviting the listener to rest with the sound of rain and begin softening into the body and the room.
  2. 02:47 Letting Go - Repeated phrases of letting go create a long settling phase, with attention easing away from effort and toward simple presence.
  3. 06:32 Feeling as Dharma - Matthew introduces the Buddha’s teaching that sight, sound, thought, and sensation all converge on feeling, making feeling the center of practice.
  4. 08:20 Body and Affect - He describes feeling as something grounded in the body, with pleasant, unpleasant, and neutral tones, often sensed through the front of the body or as a wider body-wide atmosphere.
  5. 11:34 The Inner Meter - After a silence, he explores how feeling can seem to tell us what is right or wrong in a moment and invites attention away from that judgment toward the feeling itself.
  6. 14:35 Equanimity and Soothing - Equanimity is presented as a changing relationship to experience, where we breathe into arousal or numbness and let the tides of feeling be felt without obeying them.
  7. 20:35 Anchors and Urgency - The practice widens to include breath, body, and soundscape as possible anchors while urgency, fidgeting, and anxious self-story are met with patient attention.
  8. 29:51 Listening Instead of Reacting - When feeling seems to demand action, the instruction is to respond gently and listen rather than immediately follow the impulse.
  9. 34:30 Feeling into Love - The close emphasizes that feeling met with wisdom and equanimity can open into love, peace, compassion, joy, and kindness.
