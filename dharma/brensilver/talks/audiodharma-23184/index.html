Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Morning Reflection and Walking Instructions

AudioDharma 12:18 2025-06-01

What does it mean to keep practice continuous when the mind is tired, restless, or ready to judge, fix, and compare? The talk begins with a plain-eyed morning reflection: the day may be beautiful, or not, and the work is to meet whatever shows up without collapsing into self-story. From there it turns to walking practice as a way to balance sitting, sustain awareness, and make the mind simple enough to stay awake, even when nothing obvious seems to be happening. It closes with a small experiment in varying the attention—seeing, hearing, feet, whole body, then loving-kindness—to keep the practice fresh.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Beginning Without Certainty - He opens by refusing easy optimism and naming the intensity that can come with the first days of retreat.
  2. 01:10 What Contact Reveals - The talk turns to noticing what phenomena bump into in us, and how open awareness can be around sensation, feeling tone, and selfing.
  3. 02:38 Continuity Through the Day - He frames the practice aim as keeping continuity by balancing sitting with movement and adjusting for the body’s needs.
  4. 03:46 Not Acquiescing to Delusion - Matthew warns against giving up into distraction, while also allowing rest in a way that still preserves wakefulness.
  5. 05:14 Bell, Inventory, and Mountain - He acknowledges the reflex to check energy and self-soothe at the bell, then points to the wider landscape as a reminder not to take the self too seriously.
  6. 06:45 How to Walk - He gives practical walking instructions: choose a place and pace that supports mindfulness, and simplify the practice if the mind is sticky.
  7. 08:13 Planting Seeds Quietly - The talk emphasizes that the goodness of practice may be invisible in the moment and only recognized much later.
  8. 10:16 A Fresh Walking Sequence - He offers a concrete experiment for walking meditation, alternating attention through sensory layers and ending in loving-kindness.
