Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Letting go of limitation

AudioDharma 26:50 2025-07-23

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation that moves from settling and grounding into a gentle inquiry into how ideas of limitation constrict the mind. He invites listeners to soften self-protective habits, feel safe enough to rest in breath and body, and notice how spaciousness can arise without effort. The talk later widens into equanimity and a quiet recognition that, when experience is no longer so compelling, it becomes more natural to lay burdens down.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Grounding and Settling - The meditation begins by stabilizing attention and finding something in experience that supports settling.
  2. 02:42 Putting Down Limitation - Matthew opens an inquiry into what it means to release ideas of limitation and the sense of a small, defended self.
  3. 05:31 Clinging and Vastness - He points to clinging as self-absorbing and suggests that loosening those habits can reveal space and a wider mind.
  4. 09:39 Safety in the Body - The practice turns toward enough inner safety to stop reiterating oneself and to melt into breath and body.
  5. 15:32 No Preference - Equanimity is framed as not preferring expansive states over contractive ones, which brings more space and stability.
  6. 16:12 Nothing to Manufacture - He emphasizes that this opening cannot be forced and leaves it to conditions and natural unfolding.
  7. 21:23 Samsara and Weariness - The talk later reflects on samsara as enchanting but eventually less compelling, where boredom can support letting go without aversion.
  8. 26:14 Closing Blessing - The meditation closes with a brief repeated blessing.
