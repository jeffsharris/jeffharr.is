Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Settling through Love, Deepening through Sound

AudioDharma 48:36 2019-05-15

What would life be without love, and how does a sit begin from that question? The talk moves from softening at the heart center into a sound practice that treats sounds, silence, and even thought as part of one field, with the invitation to listen without forcing anything. It gradually turns toward refuge: noticing how the mind keeps returning to the story of me, and seeing that pattern with enough continuity to rest in awareness instead. A brief Rumi passage near the end sharpens the emphasis on wholehearted practice, before the talk settles into a gentle, sustainable close.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Arriving Through Love - The meditation begins with reflection on how much life depends on love and uses that to help the mind settle.
  2. 01:42 Softening the Heart - He invites a gentle softening at the heart center and emphasizes that love can relax resistance, worry, and holding.
  3. 09:18 Kindness Without Pretense - The practice is framed as seasoning the mind with care rather than forcing a feeling, while allowing whatever needs to be felt.
  4. 13:36 Opening to Sound - Attention widens to the soundscape, with guidance on noticing sound, silence, and the possibility of an inner tonal anchor.
  5. 27:18 Sound as One Field - He explores hearing thoughts as another kind of sound and resting in a single field where inside and outside begin to collapse.
  6. 38:32 Rumi on Wholeness - A brief poetic section uses Rumi to encourage wholehearted commitment rather than half-measures or delay.
  7. 42:17 Refuge and Self-Story - He returns to the question of refuge, contrasting the Three Jewels with the habitual refuge in the story of me.
  8. 46:05 Resting Sustainably - The talk closes with a gentle reminder to be slow, sustain the effort, and let practice continue through ordinary transitions.
