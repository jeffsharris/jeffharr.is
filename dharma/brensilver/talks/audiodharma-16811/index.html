Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: No Fidelity to the Past Moment

AudioDharma 30:09 2022-08-16

What if the next moment does not have to obey the mood of the last one? The talk begins with a reading about grief, gratitude, anger, boredom, and happiness sharing the same stage, then turns toward the practice point: mindfulness does not try to keep one state going or force another to disappear. From there it stays close to the felt experience of sitting—landing, letting words melt away, breathing into the body, and noticing how intimacy can be lost in doing and doubt, then found again without narrativizing what meditation is becoming.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening Welcome - Matthew greets the group and prepares to begin the meditation with a short reading.
  2. 00:44 Reading on Mixed Feeling - He reads from Catherine Schultz on how grief, gratitude, anger, boredom, and happiness mingle rather than appearing one at a time.
  3. 03:52 No Fidelity to the Past - The reading becomes a Dharma reflection on not carrying forward the last moment or trying to engineer the next one.
  4. 05:34 Settling Into Practice - After the reading, he guides listeners to let words melt away, land in the body, and come back to intimacy with the present.
  5. 10:48 Listening With Spaciousness - The instruction deepens into listening to oneself with an open heart, allowing moods to persist without assuming they must stay.
  6. 13:58 Freshness and Discontinuity - He emphasizes not narrating meditation into a fixed story and staying open to the morphing of sensory experience and insight.
  7. 25:22 Moment-by-Moment Offering - The close returns to freshness, questioning what comes next, and resting in a simple offering of attention to the moment.
