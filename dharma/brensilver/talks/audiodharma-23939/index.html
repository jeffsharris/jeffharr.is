Guided Meditation: How distant is freedom?

AudioDharma 28:31 2025-10-01

How far away is peace, and what if the feeling of distance is partly made by the mind itself? This guided meditation turns toward the breath with a softer kind of attention, asking when focus is too tight, when it can relax, and how effort can quietly become control. The practice keeps returning to the body, to the sense of lineage and support, and to a simple trust that the moment does not have to be managed in order for mindfulness to be present.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 How Far Is Peace - The meditation opens with the central question of how far away peace or freedom really is.
  2. 01:00 All Practice Is Here - He invites a felt sense of everything already given to practice—effort, renunciation, suffering, love, and willingness.
  3. 03:54 Taking Our Cues - Attention turns to lineage and the shelter of a long tradition, then to breathing and feeling the body.
  4. 05:54 Softening Control - He explores how to orient attention toward the breath with the right mix of precision and ease, and how relaxing control reduces tension.
  5. 08:36 Boundaries Without Grasping - The instruction shifts toward giving the mind boundaries without turning meditation into another form of control.
  6. 11:24 Mindfulness and Ungovernability - He names mindfulness as a recognition of what we do not govern and a harmonizing of the heart with that fact.
  7. 13:07 Trusting the Moment - The teaching moves into a steadier confidence that there is already more than enough, and that we need not go searching for goodness.
  8. 22:09 Resting in Silence - The final section emphasizes trust that the moment will not harm the heart, letting go of compulsive checking and taking cues from silence.
