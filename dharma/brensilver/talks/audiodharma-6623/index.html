Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guded Meditation (Day 2): Breath

AudioDharma 50:45 2016-04-05

This guided meditation begins with settling the body through the breath, then asks what the mind brings to stillness and silence as the sit unfolds. The practice keeps returning to breathing in a gentle, matter-of-fact way, with repeated stretches of silence and reminders not to grasp tightly at the breath. The talk also turns to the strain of always trying to improve experience, and suggests opening to imperfection, letting thought and feeling pass without acting on their urgency, and relaxing the body-mind so the breath can stay in the foreground.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling In - The meditation begins by inviting a few comfortable breaths and noticing the mind’s associations with stillness and silence.
  2. 04:56 Breath as Receiving - Instruction shifts to letting the breath come naturally and using a gentle mental note to support equanimity.
  3. 11:15 The Cost of Striving - Matthew reflects on the human tendency to keep fixing and improving, and how that can fatigue the heart.
  4. 13:30 Meeting Imperfection - He encourages breathing with this moment as it is, and asks whether experience can be met in a way that softens the heart.
  5. 25:56 Foreground and Background - The teaching clarifies that attention to breath does not erase sound, emotion, or thought, but gives breathing the foreground.
  6. 32:01 Freedom from Urgency - He explores how thoughts and feelings can seem urgent, yet be allowed to come and go without being acted on immediately.
  7. 41:56 After the Practice - The closing instructions scan for bracing, then turn to the body after the bell and the way silence changes the feel of ordinary life.
  8. 46:13 Returning in Daily Life - Matthew ends by pointing to the sacredness of brief silence, the simplification that comes from less choice, and the practical details for practice discussions.
