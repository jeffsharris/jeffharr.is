Guided Meditation: Landing in the Body Breathing

AudioDharma 30:16 2022-08-15

Begin with the body breathing, then let awareness widen into the places where effort, worry, and the urge to fix life show up. The talk moves gently between settling with the breath, noticing how thinking is fueled by feeling, and bringing those charged places into patience and love rather than turning them into enemies. When the mind starts to gather fragments around plans and security, it keeps returning to the body as a steady reference point, with mindfulness presented as a quiet surrender of projects for the sake of awareness.

Chapters

  1. 00:13 Opening and settling - Matthew greets the group, names the format, and invites everyone to sit and begin the practice together.
  2. 02:10 Love as reference - He introduces love as a helpful reference point: not a feeling to manufacture, but a way of trusting that experience can soften rather than harden the heart.
  3. 05:13 Breath through the body - The meditation shifts into spreading the breath through the whole body and gathering attention into the felt sense of breathing.
  4. 07:28 Gentle discipline - He encourages a soft but purposeful attention that does not make experience into an enemy, while keeping the body-breath as the main anchor.
  5. 13:07 Meeting the urge to fix - The practice turns toward the impulse to secure life, noticing how worry and planning live in the emotional body and the circuits of thought.
  6. 16:06 Returning to breathing - After bathing the reactive energy in awareness and patience, he suggests coming back to the body breathing when it feels appropriate.
  7. 21:23 Mindfulness as surrender - In closing reflections, he describes mindful practice as privileging awareness over projects and as a deep kind of surrender.
