Dharmette: Dharma as Exposure Therapy for Samsara

AudioDharma 13:40 2022-08-18

Matthew Brensilver frames Dharma practice as a kind of exposure therapy: turning toward the thoughts, feelings, and bodily states we usually avoid. He moves from clinical language about experiential avoidance to the way meditation, especially retreat practice, gradually reveals dukkha and asks for a deeper intimacy with it. The talk closes with a few reflective references that sharpen the point that what is bitter can become nourishment when it is fully met.

Chapters

  1. 00:15 Three ways of framing Dharma - He briefly links earlier talks on attention therapy and cognitive therapy before introducing today’s frame: Dharma as exposure therapy.
  2. 01:02 Avoidance and suffering - Brensilver defines experiential avoidance, names it as a trans-diagnostic problem, and argues that many psychological problems are also bad solutions.
  3. 03:10 What exposure means - He spells out how exposure therapy works in clinical settings, including approaching feared experiences step by step and building new learning around safety.
  4. 05:43 Dharma and samsara - The clinical example becomes a Dharma claim: practice is unsystematic exposure therapy for samsara, where anything that can disrupt peace eventually will.
  5. 07:02 Purification through practice - He describes relinquishing familiar ways of soothing and neutralizing distress, and names the difficult but wholesome quality of purification.
  6. 08:08 Taste for purification - A reference to Shinzen Young leads into a meditation survey and then into the idea that stronger concentration often reveals more dukkha rather than less.
  7. 10:15 Meeting primal forces - He describes retreat as a move from relative peace into purification, where rage, terror, helplessness, and conditioning are repeatedly brought into relationship.
  8. 12:07 Nourishment from bitterness - A brief quoted journal passage and a final reflection bring the talk to a close, suggesting that what seems bitter can become knowledge once it is fully eaten and transformed.
