Dharmette: Working with emotional pain

AudioDharma 13:54 2026-07-22

When emotional pain arises, is the task to get rid of it, or to learn how to meet it without making it worse? The talk moves through listening to feeling, using skillful avoidance when needed, changing view, and noticing when pain can be softened by practice or simply known as inconsolable. Along the way, it frames emotion as carrying both wisdom and delusion, and treats impermanence and de-reification as forms of refuge.

Chapters

  1. 00:30 Feeling Matters - The talk begins by saying that what matters to us is feeling, and that emotional suffering creates a strong drive for relief.
  2. 02:03 Wisdom and Delusion - Pain is described as often feeling like grief, and emotion is treated as carrying both real intelligence and distortion.
  3. 04:07 Listening Without Forcing - The emphasis shifts to balanced attention: listening deeply, feeling directly, and balancing intensity with tranquility and self-soothing.
  4. 05:54 When Action Helps - The talk widens beyond inner work to include cases where action, boundaries, or spiritual friendship are necessary for healing.
  5. 07:17 Changing View - The speaker frames Dharma as a kind of cognitive therapy, using Rilke and the idea that pain may soften the heart rather than harden it.
  6. 08:48 Practical Thought Work - A sutta-based sequence offers direct methods for working with painful mind states through substitution, reflection, and, if needed, forceful intervention.
  7. 10:16 Untangling the Tangle - Feeling is then treated as something to de-reify: not a riddle to solve, but a tangle to deconstruct as impermanence becomes refuge.
  8. 11:57 Inconsolable Pain - The talk closes by acknowledging that some suffering cannot be consoled or improved and must simply be borne with reverence and care.
