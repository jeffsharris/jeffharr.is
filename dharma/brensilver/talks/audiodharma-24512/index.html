Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Spiritual vs Psychological Health

AudioDharma 46:32 2025-12-28

What is the relationship between psychological health and spiritual health? The talk moves through four possible answers: that they are separate, continuous, sequential, or deeply threaded together, and keeps returning to the practical question of what actually changes in ordinary life when practice deepens. Along the way, the speaker brings in psychotherapy, self-love, disidentification, and the limits of language to show why the issue is not just theoretical but bound up with suffering, healing, and freedom. The result is a careful argument for staying honest with both the psychological knots and the larger movement of Dharma.

Chapters

  1. 00:11 Framing the question - Brensilver introduces the central question of how psychological and spiritual health relate and begins naming the main possibilities.
  2. 01:18 Four possible views - He lays out four hypotheses: separation, continuity, psychological preparation for spirituality, and a threaded relationship between the two.
  3. 04:46 Language and the absolute - The talk turns to the relative and the absolute, with Wittgenstein used to question whether language sometimes obscures the issue.
  4. 07:01 Dharma as healing - He argues that Dharma can work like therapy in important ways, while also pointing to limits in psychological healing alone.
  5. 11:13 Where psychology ends - Freud and Maslow frame a ceiling for ordinary well-being, and Brensilver suggests spiritual practice begins where that ceiling is reached.
  6. 14:59 Preconditions for practice - Drawing on Jack Engler, he describes the need for some psychological stability before deep practice can be fully entered.
  7. 18:36 Threaded together - He settles into his preferred view: psychological healing and spiritual freedom develop together, with each revealing and supporting the other.
  8. 25:11 Self-love and not-self - Using Letheby and Ajahn Sumedho, he explores how self-acceptance can loosen identification with self-story without erasing personality.
  9. 30:20 Working with deep knots - The talk becomes more practical and tender as he describes how practice exposes old compulsions, shame, and karmic knots that require patience rather than willpower.
