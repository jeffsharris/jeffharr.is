Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Internal and External Sound and Silence

Dharma Seed 32:17 2025-09-20

What changes when listening stops favoring the mind’s inner narration over a bird, a plane, or the quiet around them? The talk guides attention through external sound, internal sound, and the silence between and behind them, with repeated invitations to soften, notice the body’s porous edge with space, and meet experience without preference. From there it turns toward how meaning-making often grows out of fear, and how simple listening can become a form of prayer and non-violence.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Entering Silence - The meditation begins by inviting silence to move through awareness while noticing nearby sounds without strain.
  2. 01:24 Outer and Inner Sound - Matthew turns to the difference between external sounds and the internal sounds of thinking, including how they are heard.
  3. 03:28 No Preference - The practice widens into hearing sound and silence without preference, allowing thoughts and quiet to be equally included.
  4. 07:31 Receptive Listening - He emphasizes simple wakefulness and uses Shakespeare to question how much meaning the mind adds to sound and thought.
  5. 12:09 Body and Space - Attention softens into the felt boundary of the body, experienced as porous and gradated rather than fixed.
  6. 15:10 Meaning and Fear - Matthew names the habit of narrating experience and suggests it often grows out of fear and the need to make meaning.
  7. 16:57 Dharma as Blessing - He briefly opens into gratitude for the Dharma and the surprising conditions that allow practice to unfold.
  8. 19:32 Listening in Prayer - A short Mother Teresa story frames prayer as listening, landing on the line that this is how we listen regardless of theology.
  9. 27:04 Nonviolence in Practice - The talk returns to non-violence toward phenomena, invokes Martin Luther King Jr., and closes by saying that this is how we listen.
