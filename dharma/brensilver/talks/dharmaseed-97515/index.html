Guided Meditation on Auditory Thinking

Dharma Seed 35:49 2026-06-05

What happens when thought is treated not as a private voice, but as something heard within experience? The talk uses auditory thinking as a doorway into mindfulness, showing how deep listening can loosen identification and reveal the gap between a thought and the story built around it. From there it turns toward a quieter practice: allowing unpleasantness, giving up the impulse to control, and meeting experience without making enemies of it.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Sound and Silence - The meditation begins by opening attention to hearing itself, with sound and silence as the first field of practice.
  2. 00:30 Thinking as Sound - The talk invites the soundscape to move through awareness so verbal thinking can be included rather than excluded.
  3. 01:58 De-reifying Thought - Thought is noticed directly as experience, not as a command or identity, and the tendency to take it as self is gently exposed.
  4. 05:20 Narrative and Impermanence - Impermanence is presented as something the mind resists through storytelling, control, and quick replacement of one thought with another.
  5. 12:34 Pain and Narration - The talk names the discomfort around thinking and not thinking, then points to a more bodily, less defended way of meeting unpleasantness.
  6. 16:06 Open Awareness - The practice widens into a very open stance, where self and sense data are not held apart so tightly.
  7. 22:39 No Enemies - A brief reference underscores that even the meditator self can narrate experience, while the instruction remains to listen without making thought an enemy.
  8. 25:07 Who Are You? - The closing movement looks at identification more closely, asking who one is when autobiography is not being repeated.
  9. 29:36 Closing Silence - The talk settles into a quiet ending after the central inquiry has been laid open.
