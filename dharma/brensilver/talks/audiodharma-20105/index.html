Guided Meditation: What's Already Here?

AudioDharma 28:16 2024-04-03

The talk opens with a simple but unsettling question: if peace and war can both come from the same human capacities, where do we actually turn to find freedom? From there, the speaker uses Ajahn Chah’s line about freedom and suffering arising in the same place to shift the whole meditation toward immediate experience. The guided practice then keeps returning to a quiet invitation: stop searching, let the breath come to you, and rest in what is already here. Over time, the sense of looking, strain, and self-doubt is meant to soften into being found by silence.

  5. 10:39 Being found - The emphasis deepens into a release of duality and self-doubt as the experience of being found softens the sense of a seeker.
  6. 17:10 Resting in presence - He returns to the theme that freedom and suffering share the same place, encouraging the body to embody that freedom rather than pursue it.
  7. 22:44 Healing the search - The practice closes by resting in what is already here, with stillness and silence offering a gentle healing of fundamental alienation.
