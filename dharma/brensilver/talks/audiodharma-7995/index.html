Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Surrender, Power, Sila

AudioDharma 43:30 2017-07-02

What does it mean to surrender in Dharma without giving away discernment? The talk begins with the charged atmosphere of retreat practice, then moves into a careful look at how surrender, power, and ethical conduct intertwine in teachers, students, sanghas, and institutions. Along the way, it weighs the pull toward control against the deeper release that can open practice, while staying alert to the real risks of misuse and projection. A question period brings the theme down to lived experience, including tears on retreat, long-term change, and what it means to let go safely.

Chapters

  1. 00:22 Retreat and Surrender - He begins with the charged atmosphere at the end of retreat and how silence, effort, and group intensity can soften the mind’s defenses.
  2. 02:33 Surrender and Power - Matthew frames surrender as both a Dharma request and a complicated human relation to autonomy, control, and self-reliance.
  3. 05:35 Augustine and Nietzsche - He contrasts the Christian language of longing and rest in Augustine with Nietzsche’s account of the will to power.
  4. 08:24 Giving Power Away - A satirical Onion headline becomes a way to think about the human attraction to strong authority and the risks of surrendering power.
  5. 11:30 Trust and Caution - He speaks personally about finding surrender difficult, and about needing confidence that Dharma power is held for liberation rather than control.
  6. 15:40 Mindfulness as Letting Go - Matthew argues that deeper practice goes beyond measured self-management, and that mindfulness itself is a form of surrender to experience.
  7. 20:25 Teachers and Boundaries - Using Adyashanti and then a discussion of teacher-student dynamics, he emphasizes that surrender must be paired with humility, boundaries, and awareness of power imbalance.
  8. 25:39 Sila and Misuse - He turns to ethical conduct, sexual misconduct, and Thanissaro Bhikkhu’s criteria for evaluating teachers, insisting that power must support students’ freedom.
  9. 33:05 Questions and Closing - In Q&A he responds to a retreatant’s tears and the many forms growth can take, then closes by blessing safe surrender and responsible use of power.
