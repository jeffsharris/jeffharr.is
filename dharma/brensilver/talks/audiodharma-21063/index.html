Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: You cannot know oneself while fixing oneself

AudioDharma 28:57 2024-08-14

The meditation keeps returning to a simple refusal: not to turn practice into another project of fixing the self. It begins with posture, grounding, and the breath, then repeatedly lets go of willfulness in favor of surrender, silence, and the body breathing on its own. From there the talk widens into noticing fears, thoughts, and phenomena as empty and wisp-like, while meeting whatever arises with patience rather than control.

Chapters

  1. 00:36 Settling the body - The meditation begins with posture, grounding, and a gentle sense of the spine and breath.
  2. 03:59 Breath and ease - Breathing is used to smooth the body-mind and create a steadier field of attention.
  3. 05:58 Putting down the project - Brensilver invites listeners to stop trying to fix life and to trust that help does not need to be hunted down.
  4. 09:14 Simple, beckoning practice - The talk narrows to the simplicity of body and breath, with a repeated sense that practice does not require willfulness.
  5. 15:41 Phenomena and fear - As thoughts and fears arise, the instruction turns to meeting them without freezing time or needing immediate reassurance.
  6. 17:23 Wisp-like experience - Experience is described as vivid yet attenuated, and the key line appears that if we only try to fix ourselves, we will never know ourselves.
  7. 23:28 Silence and patience - The closing instruction names silence as anchor and asks that every bit of noise be met with infinite patience.
