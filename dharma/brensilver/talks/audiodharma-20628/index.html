Dharmette: Love and Time

AudioDharma 11:33 2024-06-12

What makes love meaningful if everything we love is finite? The talk begins with a blessing at a family rite of passage and opens into a quiet meditation on how time, loss, and grief give love its depth rather than taking meaning away. From there, it moves through the claim that finitude is what makes responsibility possible, then widens into Buddhist images of love as the glue that holds the world together and as many different forms in relational life.

Chapters

  1. 00:09 A blessing request - He begins with the family ceremony that prompted the reflection and the question of what to say.
  2. 00:48 Love and time - Love and time arrive together as the central theme, and he begins to explore why they belong together.
  3. 02:14 Finitude and care - He draws on a philosopher’s argument that responsibility and care only arise when life is recognized as finite.
  4. 03:25 Grief in love - Using superhero imagery, he shows how vulnerability and the inevitability of loss are already part of love.
  5. 04:43 Love across years - He turns to the richness that comes from continuity, repetition, and the long shaping of relationships over time.
  6. 06:01 Time needs love - The talk reverses the argument: time matters because love gives life weight and direction.
  7. 07:26 Love holds together - He invokes a Buddhist image of love as the glue of the world and names the Brahma-viharas as classical forms of love.
  8. 08:47 Many faces of love - He widens the field to family, partner, friend, and child, ending with the idea that each day can be treated as a story about love.
