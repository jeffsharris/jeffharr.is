Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Non-tension

AudioDharma 29:15 2024-07-03

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation centered on non-tension: noticing how the body and mind respond to conditions, and learning not to wait for things to be perfect before letting go. The practice moves from settling into bodily awareness, to relaxing clinging and bracing, to resting with impermanence without pushing or pulling against it. The closing section uses a repeated image of falling into the present, with less baggage and fewer places for attention to snag.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and welcome - Matthew welcomes listeners and introduces the practice with a light, informal tone.
  2. 01:45 Temperature and the body - He recalls a teacher’s reminder that even the Buddha sweats and shivers, using discomfort to point toward direct observation.
  3. 04:13 Not waiting for perfect - The talk turns to letting imperfection become motivation to stop fighting and to give permission for the moment to be as it is.
  4. 12:05 Awareness in the body - Listeners are guided to fill the body with awareness in a way that feels expansive rather than forced.
  5. 14:54 Relaxing clinging - He names clinging as a source of tension and invites a settled, undefended aliveness.
  6. 18:52 Impermanence as practice - Matthew links attention with change and encourages not pushing or pulling on impermanence or seeking escape from it.
  7. 23:28 Falling into presence - The closing refrain uses the image of falling deeper into the present, with less baggage and fewer ways for attention to catch.
