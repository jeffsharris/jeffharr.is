Guided Meditation: Relax a Little Further (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 45:53 2025-10-21

If you still think you know who you are, the talk keeps returning to one instruction: relax a little further. It moves from loosening the grip on progress, control, and self-judgment into a practice of gentle attention, receptivity, and surrender—until suffering can be met without making it into identity. Later, the guidance widens into steadier, more open attention, a softer relationship to pain, and the sense that refuge is not confined to one special state or place.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Relax further - The meditation begins with repeated invitations to loosen assumptions about identity, progress, and control.
  2. 05:29 Doing and surrender - He frames practice as a gentle turn of attention that unfolds within surrender and the dissolving of willful doing.
  3. 09:57 Attitude and renunciation - The talk continues into wise attention, renunciation, and the effort to keep practice from being hijacked by control.
  4. 16:12 Attention opens - Matthew shifts to the aperture of attention and how steadiness of heart-mind can make the anchor feel spacious.
  5. 18:17 Care for pain - He names the self-drama of arrogance and self-hate and asks for exquisite care toward pain instead.
  6. 24:28 Receiving what is here - The meditation returns to the central rhythm of practice as a movement between doing and receiving.
  7. 30:44 Love without preference - He points toward loving suffering and bliss equally, with the heart opening to what has not yet been loved.
  8. 38:19 Refuge everywhere - The talk culminates in the sense that refuge is not bound to a special state or place, but can be found everywhere.
  9. 44:08 Retreat notes and close - Matthew closes with logistics for the retreat and a final reflection on night sitting, aliveness, and well-wishing.
