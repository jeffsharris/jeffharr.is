Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Allowing (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 41:11 2025-10-22

How do you stop fighting what is already here? The talk keeps returning to a simple, repeated permission: forgive all phenomena, do not fight your fight, and let the present moment be met without adding another layer of resistance. As the meditation unfolds, the instruction widens from softening the body and letting awareness receive thoughts, sounds, and sensations, to a final release of self-judgment: nothing you do in these minutes is wrong. Near the end, the talk briefly turns to retreat-community care and boundaries, using a harmful note as a reminder that practice also depends on how carefully people move around one another.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening forgiveness - The meditation begins with the instruction to forgive all phenomena, including love, hate, sound, and self.
  2. 01:00 Not fighting resistance - Matthew reframes practice as a refusal to add another layer of struggle, including the instruction not to fight the fact of fighting.
  3. 10:18 Softening into Dharma - He names how deeply the heart can resist suffering and invites relaxing, softening, and letting the goodness of Dharma permeate the body.
  4. 17:26 Settling the spasms - The talk points to spasms of self and fear, and to the habit of fidgeting for relief instead of letting the heart rest.
  5. 21:46 No autobiography needed - Listeners are told not to turn the moment into another chapter of personal story or meaning-making.
  6. 26:12 Yielding to awareness - Thoughts, sounds, and sensations are described as territory that can be given over to awareness rather than held as self.
  7. 31:20 Nothing is wrong - In the final minutes he offers a radical permission practice: loving, hating, grasping, focusing, or drifting are all treated as allowable.
  8. 39:14 Closing announcements - After the meditation, retreat housekeeping and a cautionary story about a harmful note lead into a reminder to stay careful and quiet.
