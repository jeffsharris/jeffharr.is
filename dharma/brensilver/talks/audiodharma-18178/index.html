Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Mortality and the Clarification of What Matters (3/5)

AudioDharma 14:07 2023-04-26

Matthew Brensilver reflects on how modern life floods us with information while leaving us without a story, and he uses that contrast to ask what keeps us from losing the plot. From there he turns toward mortality as a practical contemplation: not to frighten us, but to clarify what matters, loosen old resentments, and make life feel more complete. The talk moves through retreat experience, a David Whyte story, and an interview with Roland Griffiths to show how death can help expose false refuge and deepen wakefulness.

Chapters

  1. 00:17 Noise and Information - Matthew opens with the noisiness of modern life and Jennifer Egan’s line about information without story.
  2. 01:54 Losing the Plot - He explores how easily we absorb prevailing values, especially when life is going well, and how retreat reveals the fragility of the stories we live by.
  3. 04:03 A Life in a Moment - A David Whyte anecdote illustrates how a small turning can become a whole life and how drifting can settle into fate.
  4. 05:09 Remembering Death - He turns to mortality practice, describing how recollecting death helps us see what holds on, what falls away, and what would make life feel complete.
  5. 07:48 Wakefulness in Illness - Matthew briefly reflects on Roland Griffiths’ response to cancer as an example of suffering clarifying values and deepening gratitude.
  6. 09:25 Honey and Poison - Using the Buddha’s teaching on honey and poisoned root, he asks how not to be deceived by pleasures that carry harm.
  7. 11:38 Practicing Letting Go - The talk closes by linking practice with dying: each surrender to imperfection rehearses the larger letting go of death and makes the Dharma make sense.
