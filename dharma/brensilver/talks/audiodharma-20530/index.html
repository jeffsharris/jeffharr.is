Guided Meditation: Tranquility

AudioDharma 26:55 2024-05-29

How do you let the mind settle without forcing it, and then use that settling to see more clearly? This guided meditation moves gently through the body and breath, asking you to elongate the breathing, notice what is already resting, and let tension drain rather than be managed by effort. From there it opens into listening for silence, resting with the visual field behind closed eyes, and softening the habit of clinging long enough to return to stillness.

Chapters

  1. 00:09 Tranquility and clarity - He introduces tranquility as both a calming quality and a way of clarifying the inner landscape.
  2. 01:56 Resting the body - The practice opens with acknowledging fatigue, lengthening the breath, and sensing into what is already resting in the body.
  3. 05:18 Softening clinging - He invites releasing tension and meeting grasping, aversion, and control with a gentler breath and a wider field.
  4. 07:28 Body as a resource - Repeated cues to soften and widen lead into finding a natural bodily zone that can serve as an anchor.
  5. 10:54 Listening for silence - Attention shifts from breath to silence, including distant sounds and the space around sound, as a live quality rather than the absence of noise.
  6. 14:59 Silent listening - Mother Teresa's line about prayer frames a contemplative listening that can be practiced without any religious belief.
  7. 17:37 Visual field and darkness - He turns to the visual field with closed eyes, noticing brightness, images, and the darkness behind them while resting attention gently.
  8. 23:27 Letting clinging wait - Near the close, he names the impulse to cling and suggests promising it a return later, not now.
  9. 25:50 Closing rest - The meditation ends in sustained quiet, with repeated gentle cues and little further instruction.
