Guided Meditation: Relaxing into Love

AudioDharma 27:46 2024-09-04

This guided meditation begins by orienting the body toward ease without losing wakefulness, then moves into breath, heart center, and a softening toward love. Matthew Brensilver repeatedly returns to the idea that when the mind and body settle, awareness can take on the hue of kindness, and he invites a willingness to feel whatever is present—joy, sorrow, pain, or tenderness—without contracting around it. The practice widens into phrases such as “May I be well” and “May all beings be well,” ending in a quiet emphasis on non-violence, refuge, and benevolent wish.

  1. 00:00 Settling and posture - He begins by inviting relaxation balanced with an upright spine and a wakeful sitting posture.
  2. 01:25 Breath as softening - The attention moves to fuller breaths and filling out the body so relaxation becomes more embodied.
  3. 03:41 Awareness with love - He links settling with sensitivity to love and asks for a heart-mind that offers itself to the moment without contract.
  4. 05:32 Heart posture - The practice deepens into trusting that whatever arises can become love, with attention placed in the heart center.
  5. 10:09 Bathing pain in care - He acknowledges knots and pain in life and encourages meeting them with care, kindness, and an image or phrase that steadies the breath.
  6. 14:49 Lovingkindness phrases - The meditation shifts into explicit phrases such as “May I be well,” “May my heart be as wide as the world,” and “May I take refuge in love.”},{
