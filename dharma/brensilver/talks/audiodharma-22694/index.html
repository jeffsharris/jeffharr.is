Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: The Pull of Time

AudioDharma 27:07 2025-04-09

The practice begins by softening what can be softened, and then turns toward whatever tension, pain, or grasping remains without trying to force it away. From there, the talk moves through care for the body, the felt transmission of kindness, and a steady reminder that attention is often entangled with wanting and fear. A central turn comes with the recognition that we cannot control experience, so the practice is to let go and receive this moment; later, the talk widens that into a view that insight into one of the Four Noble Truths can be on offer in any moment.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Settling and Relaxing - The meditation begins by relaxing whatever can be relaxed and blessing what remains tense to stay as it is.
  2. 01:37 Tending the Body - Attention turns to bodily tightness or pain, with a suggestion to gently massage discomfort while staying in the flow of practice.
  3. 03:06 Care and Lineage - He frames self-care as an expression of loving-kindness and places the listener within a long lineage of love and care.
  4. 05:20 Practice and Human Longing - The talk widens to the wish to be free of suffering, the work of learning on the cushion, and the distinction between what is worthy of protection and what can be released.
  5. 09:48 Wanting and Fear - Brensilver reflects on how hope gets projected into the future and how renunciation loosens ownership, control, and our relationship to helplessness.
  6. 15:16 Letting Go to Receive - Using the phrase about not fooling Mother Nature, he emphasizes that we cannot control experience and must let go to receive this moment.
  7. 21:02 Insight on Offer - The meditation opens into a quieter teaching on recognizing what is happening, with the suggestion that insight can be available in any moment.
  8. 26:36 Not Missing It - The closing line returns to the simple task of doing our best not to miss the present opportunity.
