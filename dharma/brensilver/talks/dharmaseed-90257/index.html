Guided Metta Meditation - Metta for All Beings (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 39:34 2025-05-11

What if metta is not mainly a feeling, but a way of stepping out of self-judgment altogether? The talk begins by framing the Brahma-viharas as four forms of love, then moves through how loving-kindness can cultivate the heart, gather attention, and purify by bringing up what has not yet been loved. A long guided practice follows, starting with the body and breath, softening self-view, and widening toward phrases for oneself and then for all beings, even while the impossibility of ending suffering remains in view.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Four kinds of love - Matthew opens by framing the Brahma-viharas as different ways love meets goodness, suffering, success, and helplessness.
  2. 01:57 Love changes self-view - He uses a retreat story to show how even a brief moment of metta can reorganize the relationship to self and loosen the self-as-project mode.
  3. 05:08 Measureless and quiet - The talk turns to the immeasurables and the reminder that loving-kindness may be subtle, quiet, and not defined by intensity.
  4. 06:23 Cultivation and purification - Matthew explains metta as cultivation, concentration, and purification, including the way practice can bring buried grief or rage to the surface.
  5. 11:48 Settling into practice - The guided section begins with body, posture, and relaxation, then points to worthiness beyond self-judgment and the habit of proving oneself.
  6. 22:44 Phrases in the present - He introduces the idea of a mind made vast by love and uses simple phrases as a present-moment way to access the heart.
  7. 26:46 Resting in kindness - The practice widens through the recognition that whatever is arising can be held, whether by quiet rest or by bringing care to what has not yet been loved.
  8. 31:13 Love in hard places - Matthew names this as non-self-indulgent love and notes how tight spots can reveal the necessity of love that makes hatred less tenable.
  9. 33:15 All beings at ease - The meditation culminates in the wish for all beings to be safe, protected, and at peace, even while acknowledging that suffering does not end.
