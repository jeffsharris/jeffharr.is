Dharmette: Truth & Lies in the US in 2025

AudioDharma 14:52 2025-01-08

What happens when the impulse to correct lies turns into rage, and that rage starts to feel like its own trap? The talk begins with childhood anger at hypocrisy, then moves through suffering, honesty, and the damage deception does to shared reality, drawing on a Theravada commentary, Harry Frankfurt’s distinction between lying and bullshit, and a remark from Thomas Metzinger on truthfulness. From there it turns inward: the struggle to stay sane in a deceptive political moment, the pull toward outrage, and a quieter practice of treasuring truth without making one’s peace depend on the world honoring it.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Hypocrisy and early anger - He begins with a childhood sensitivity to hypocrisy and the feeling of being mobilized by lies and denial.
  2. 02:37 Honesty as moral care - He links truth-telling to empathy, trauma recovery, and the moral responsibility to help others know what is real.
  3. 04:24 Lies and bullshit - He turns to Buddhist ethics and Harry Frankfurt to distinguish deliberate lying from indifference to truth.
  4. 06:33 Truth in practice - He uses Thomas Metzinger to connect scientific rigor and spiritual practice through a shared commitment to veracity.
  5. 07:25 Political outrage cycle - He names the current U.S. political moment and describes how anger, evidence-gathering, and self-righteousness can become a closed loop.
  6. 10:20 Treasure the truth - He offers a practical shift: speak truth as best one can, stop depending on the world to reward truthfulness, and give up the debate.
  7. 12:24 Seeing self-deception - He reflects on motivated reasoning and Nietzsche's line about truth and strength, turning it into a wish to become more able to bear truth.
  8. 13:17 Sangha and letting go - He ends by naming sangha as a place where reality can be faced together and by reminding listeners to let go before clinging is broken.
