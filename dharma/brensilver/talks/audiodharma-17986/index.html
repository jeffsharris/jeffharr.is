Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Settling and Opening

AudioDharma 54:44 2023-03-16

A simple instruction runs through this guided meditation: body in the posture, mind in the body, relaxation in the mind. From there, the talk keeps returning to the breath as a place to meet fatigue, agitation, and the habit of turning sensations into stories about whether things are okay. It also opens out into walking practice, showing how to carry mindfulness through movement without trying to fix anything or force a result.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Simple settling - He introduces a compact instruction: body in posture, mind in the body, relaxation in the mind.
  2. 02:00 Fear and interpretation - The talk turns to the questions that arise in practice and the need to sense the fear beneath them rather than rush to answer.
  3. 07:25 Breath as refuge - A long guided breath sequence follows, using repeated breathing and counting to settle the body and soften mental questioning.
  4. 18:06 Questions dissolve - He names common retreat doubts—whether one is okay, doing it right, or progressing—and invites them to dissolve in the breath.
  5. 23:35 Cycles of breath - Matthew describes noticing the precise stages of the breath, then reframes the practice as taking refuge rather than fixing oneself.
  6. 33:37 Retreat opens the heart - He speaks about the intensity of being human and, invoking the Buddha's teaching, says retreat can let that intensity break the heart open.
  7. 38:20 Meeting what arises - The guidance shifts to how the mind predictably gets caught in preoccupation, and how to keep going without making sleepiness or agitation mean too much.
  8. 41:20 Walking with continuity - He gives detailed walking-practice instructions, emphasizing continuity, broadening or narrowing attention, and meeting boredom without chasing stimulation.
  9. 51:53 No conclusions today - The talk closes with practical retreat logistics and a quiet reminder not to draw conclusions about life or the future from the first day.
