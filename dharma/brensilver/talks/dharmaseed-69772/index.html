Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Love and its relation with wisdom.

Dharma Seed 47:42 2022-03-07

How does love remain trustworthy when the talk keeps returning to suffering, ethical strain, and the limits of wisdom alone? The speaker moves from a playful opening into a sustained reflection on love as the real transmission of Dharma, using examples from Thich Nhat Hanh, Martin Luther King Jr., William James, and the salt-and-river image to show how understanding expands the heart. The talk then turns inward, asking how practice changes a suspicious or hardened mind, and outward, toward how compassion, moral responsibility, and even long-term human survival belong to the same field. It ends by holding liberation and care together: freeing the heart is not the end of the question, but the ground from which love can be spent.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and intention - Matthew begins lightly, then turns quickly toward the reason he keeps speaking about suffering: concern for its absence and for what makes love trustworthy.
  2. 01:31 Love needs credibility - He argues that love carries weight when it comes from moral credibility, using Thich Nhat Hanh on Martin Luther King Jr. to show that Dharma transmission is really the transmission of love.
  3. 04:02 Practice as art - Practice is described as shaping the heart like a work of art, and he reflects on the claim that people change only through encounters that contain love.
  4. 07:10 Finite life, love's necessity - After a brief Dostoevsky reference, he turns toward mortality, legacy, and the idea that love is the most fitting response to a brief human life.
  5. 11:40 From suspicion to trust - He speaks candidly about his own aversion, sensitivity to the absence of love, and early skepticism, then describes how practice and relationship slowly opened him.
  6. 15:50 Recognizing goodness - William James and the example of saints help him frame goodness as something that awakens what is latent in others, especially in a season of grief and social strain.
  7. 19:40 Dharma love and the wings - He uses film, fiction, and the language of understanding to show a very human love that becomes Dharma love, then distinguishes the wisdom and compassion sides of the path.
  8. 26:42 The river of compassion - With the salt-and-river image, he explains how an expanded heart can receive suffering without becoming overwhelmed, and why understanding is another name for love.
  9. 29:03 Love in every stage - He says love is needed for learning, mourning, approaching pain, and seeing the wound of self, then brings in sangha and Shinzen Young's image of transmitting Dharma quietly through presence.
