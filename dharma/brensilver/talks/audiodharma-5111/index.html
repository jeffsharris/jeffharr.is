Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette

AudioDharma 16:06 2014-09-24

Matthew Brensilver offers a compact, practical framing of mindfulness as a threefold skill: concentration, clarity, and equanimity. He starts with the modern ambiguity around the word mindfulness, including Jon Kabat-Zinn’s use of it as a broad Dharma vehicle, then turns to a working definition shaped by Shinzen Young. The talk closes by suggesting simple questions for checking the state of practice and using that insight to guide energy more skillfully.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 What mindfulness means - Matthew opens by saying mindfulness is both the beginning and the fruition of the path, and that it is worth clarifying even if it cannot be pinned down perfectly.
  2. 02:20 Mindfulness in the mainstream - He draws on Jon Kabat-Zinn’s 2011 framing to show how mindfulness has become a broad umbrella term and why that can be both useful and confusing.
  3. 04:58 A threefold model - Matthew introduces Shinzen Young’s nontraditional synthesis of mindfulness as a working definition organized around three skills.
  4. 08:31 Gathered attention - He describes concentration as the ability to place attention where you want it, and explains how a stable mind changes the flavor of practice and daily life.
  5. 09:37 Seeing clearly - The talk shifts to clarity as higher resolution perception, where experience is seen in detail rather than collapsed into solid things and self.
  6. 12:16 Not pushing or pulling - Using the Bahiya Sutta, Matthew explains equanimity as allowing experience to arise and pass without grasping or resistance.
  7. 14:21 Checking practice - He closes by suggesting a practical self-inquiry into concentration, clarity, and equanimity, encouraging listeners to take what is useful and leave the rest.
