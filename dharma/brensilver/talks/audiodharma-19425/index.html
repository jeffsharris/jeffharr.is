Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Resting In What We Cannot Stop

AudioDharma 29:42 2023-12-14

This guided meditation begins with settling into a posture you can say yes to, then gently turns toward gratitude for being alive and conscious. From there, the talk explores the difference between the effort of directing attention and the quieter dimension of awareness that does not depend on will. The practice movement is to notice what is already happening, including how awareness blooms and collapses, and to rest in what cannot be stopped rather than trying to manage experience.

Chapters

  1. 00:52 Settling In - The meditation begins with posture, settling, and a gentle invitation to arrive in the room.
  2. 04:00 Gratitude for Awareness - He turns toward gratitude for being alive and conscious, and toward the improbable conditions that allow awareness at all.
  3. 09:32 Effort and Ease - Brensilver distinguishes the practical effort of directing attention from the deeper ease of letting things be.
  4. 11:37 Awareness Beyond Will - He explores awareness as something that does not depend on will, cannot be forced, and does not cost attention in the usual way.
  5. 20:23 Mindfulness as Grace - The talk lingers on the sense that mindfulness blooms and collapses without being manufactured, revealing the limits of self-control.
  6. 26:15 Resting in What Cannot Stop - The meditation closes by inviting rest in the awareness that cannot be stopped and therefore does not need to be managed.
