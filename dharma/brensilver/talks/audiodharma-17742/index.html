Dharmette: Surrender in the Dharma

AudioDharma 14:13 2023-02-14

Matthew Brensilver explores surrender as a Dharma practice that is distinct from blind submission or passive resignation. He moves from a Leonard Cohen reflection on the search dissolving, through Augustine’s language of restlessness and dependence, into a Buddhist account of relaxing willfulness, meeting not-knowing, and allowing stillness to change the mind. The talk gradually widens surrender from an ethical attitude into a practical way of relating to mindfulness, samsara, insight, and silence.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Opening with Cohen - Matthew begins with Leonard Cohen’s late-life reflection on suffering and the search dissolving, setting up surrender as the central theme.
  2. 01:05 What Surrender Means - He names surrender as unusual in Buddhist language, then introduces Augustine’s Confessions to show its beauty and force.
  3. 03:08 Inquiry and Independence - The talk balances surrender with the Buddhist emphasis on verifying teachings for oneself and being independent in the Dharma.
  4. 04:28 The Limits of Will - Matthew warns that willfulness, control, and possession can masquerade as practice while actually tightening the heart.
  5. 05:57 Ego Out of Moves - He describes the deeper opening that comes when the ego can no longer manage, and the path begins with admitting not knowing how to live.
  6. 07:50 Present-Moment Receptivity - Surrender becomes staying with the present instead of using mindfulness to secure the future or fix the moment.
  7. 10:11 Insight from Not Knowing - He turns to relinquishing old stories and explains that insight arises from a mind that does not know and cannot predict what it will find.
  8. 11:48 Groundlessness and Stillness - Matthew describes the practice of settling into groundlessness, trusting stillness as a refuge even when ordinary reference points disappear.
  9. 12:58 Power from Surrender - He closes by saying that surrender is not weakness but the place from which power and security are found.
