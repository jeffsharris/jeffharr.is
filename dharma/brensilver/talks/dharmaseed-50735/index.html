Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Turbulence and Peace (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 55:00 2018-06-16

What if sadness, laughter, and peace can all be present at once? The talk opens with a story from a dark breathing group, where grief in one person evokes tears, then a kind of wisdom-laughter, and then a deeper stillness underneath both. From there it moves into a guided exploration of noting experience, the pull of craving and clinging, and the way retreat intensity can become material for learning rather than something to escape. Later questions turn to secular mindfulness, renunciation, and the uneasy territory of free will, with a quiet emphasis on trying without pretending certainty.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Grief and Peace - A story from a breathing group opens the talk and shows grief, laughter, and peace arising together.
  2. 09:55 Noting Experience - He explains mental noting as a way of including more and more of life without standing apart from it.
  3. 16:08 Craving and Selfing - A compact teaching on how dislike, craving, and clinging crystallize into the felt corner of self.
  4. 24:20 Letting Go - He repeats the line that clinging never feels as good as letting go and advises not to add more clinging.
  5. 34:01 Retreat Energy - Matthew reflects on the energy released by intensive practice and how to meet transition without trying to return to an earlier state.
  6. 40:53 Mindfulness and Renunciation - In response to questions, he distinguishes ethical grounding from the missing piece of renunciation in secular mindfulness.
  7. 49:39 Agency and Trying - He takes up free will, not-self, and emptiness, then settles on the practical stance of continuing to try.
