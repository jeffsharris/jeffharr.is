Practice Note: Noticing Something New

AudioDharma 04:37 2018-05-16

The talk asks how attention can become so familiar that it stops noticing what is new. Using the image of a theater of the mind, it points to both the usefulness of steady, routine looking and the possibility of deliberately looking in fresh ways. The practice invitation is simple: pause, question your usual way of seeing, and see whether anything unexpected appears.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening Context - A short introduction from the meditation center lead-in before the teaching begins.
  2. 00:30 Grooved Attention - He begins with the idea that attention can become trained into familiar routes, much like looking only in the usual places.
  3. 01:00 Practice And Familiarity - The talk turns to the way practice develops efficient habits of looking at body, mind, and feeling, while also inviting a different kind of seeing.
  4. 01:49 Looking In New Ways - He asks what it would mean to look in new ways during practice and names the inner life as a kind of theater.
  5. 02:49 Letting The Frame Go - The imagery of stage, actors, director, and protagonist is used to show how our usual framing can obscure novelty.
  6. 03:28 Seeing With New Eyes - He suggests that the point is not to manufacture something new, but to look in a way that makes new seeing possible.
