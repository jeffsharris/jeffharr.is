Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Practice Note

AudioDharma 04:12 2016-09-14

A small shift in attention becomes the whole teaching: a noise stops, and the mind suddenly notices the relief that had been hiding in plain sight. The talk uses that everyday moment to point toward a practice of seeing suffering as it fades, and of recognizing contentment, ease, and the absence of aversion when they are present. The emphasis is simple and quiet: notice endings, notice what is no longer there, and let those moments become part of insight.

Chapters

  1. 00:09 Familiar Noise - A noisy engine becomes the example for how irritation can be present without being fully noticed.
  2. 01:19 Ease Goes Unnoticed - The talk points to how suffering can fade and be missed because the mind keeps scanning for problems.
  3. 01:58 Noticing Contentment - Matthew encourages recognizing relative ease and the moments when burden drops away.
  4. 02:44 The Fading Away - He links the ending of things to insight, emphasizing cessation more than arising.
  5. 03:07 Knowing Absence - The teaching broadens to include aversion's absence, suggesting that what is not present can also be clearly known.
  6. 03:38 Simple Practice - The talk closes with a plain invitation to notice endings and absence while walking and sitting, without overcomplicating practice.
