Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Day 3 Instructions and Sitting (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 37:54 2017-05-25

How do emotional sensations and internal talk shape experience, and what changes when attention can stay with them without bracing or trying to fix them? The talk begins with very simple attention on breath or sound, then widens to the bodily fingerprints of feeling—pleasant, unpleasant, diffuse, or localized—and to the way thoughts can be heard as inner speech. It ends by bringing feeling and internal talk together, showing their reciprocal pull and inviting a steadier, more patient way of knowing whatever arises.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Simple Attention - He begins by asking listeners to keep attention very simple, with breath or sound as the initial anchor.
  2. 05:47 Not Bracing - The instruction broadens into not bracing against pain or pleasure and letting experience come and go naturally.
  3. 09:02 From Breath to Feeling - He explains why breath can remain a lifelong practice, then turns the foreground of attention toward feeling and thought.
  4. 11:37 Body of Emotion - The talk maps emotion to bodily arousal, showing how different feelings and motivations have a felt fingerprint in the body.
  5. 16:14 Rolling Tides - A short period of guided silence settles into noticing emotional sensations, reactions, and the spread of feeling through the body.
  6. 23:37 Equanimity and Softening - He points out that awake awareness can reduce urgency and allow the body-mind to soften rather than react.
  7. 24:37 Listening to Thought - The teaching shifts to internal talk, inviting listeners to notice the auditory side of thinking and where thoughts are heard.
  8. 30:24 Hearing the Narrator - He describes how thought can vanish when noticed, or continue as a narrated stream, and encourages simple recognition without trying to stop it.
  9. 33:08 Feeling and Talk Together - In the closing section, he brings feeling and internal dialogue into the same field, emphasizing their reciprocal relationship and the option to place attention with one experience at a time.
