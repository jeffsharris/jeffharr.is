Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Five Spiritual Faculties - Wisdom (5 of 5)

AudioDharma 17:16 2023-01-27

What does it mean for wisdom to rule the other faculties, and how does it actually grow in ordinary life? The talk moves from a Buddhist framing of wisdom as the governing faculty, to a practical distinction between facts and “knowledge how,” then into the slow work of loosening delusion, becoming more porous, and letting insight generalize across life. It closes by stressing that wisdom needs quiet, supportive conditions, and repeated practice to hold and deepen.

Chapters

  1. 00:19 Wisdom Ruling the Faculties - He opens by framing wisdom as the governing faculty that orders mindfulness, concentration, faith, and energy.
  2. 00:59 What Wisdom Means - Brensilver offers a Buddhist and psychological lens on wisdom, including pro-sociality, emotional balance, and making good decisions under uncertainty.
  3. 02:01 Knowing How to Live - He shifts from definition to the practical question of how wisdom arises in one’s own experience and in relationship to suffering and happiness.
  4. 03:17 Knowledge How, Not Theory - Using the distinction between knowledge that and knowledge how, he emphasizes that Dharma wisdom is about learning how not to suffer, illustrated by the story of the old Thai monk.
  5. 05:37 From Insight to Generalization - He describes wisdom as moving from latent potential to insight and embodiment, while noticing the places where it still does not generalize to all of life.
  6. 07:37 Thawing Delusion - The talk turns to the slow loosening of wrong view, the need for porosity and surrender, and the gradual work of making the mind receptive to new understanding.
  7. 10:25 Happiness Opens the View - Brensilver argues that a settled enough happiness often comes before insight, because well-being broadens attention and lets wisdom arise more easily.
  8. 12:21 Reinforcing Insight - He describes how insight becomes real through repeated reinforcement, the support of spiritual friends, and the reminder to keep practicing.
  9. 14:36 Practice It Again - The closing section encourages listeners to act from wisdom even when the feeling is not yet present, and to keep cultivating the conditions that let insight deepen.
