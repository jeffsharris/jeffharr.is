Guided Meditation: Awareness is reverential about all phenomena

AudioDharma 27:40 2026-04-15

What happens when practice stops sorting experience into pleasant and unpleasant? This guided meditation begins by settling the body and softening the effort to get things right, then turns toward the idea that whatever is present belongs here and is not an accident. The talk gradually opens into a more reverent way of meeting experience: less preference, less strain, and more willingness to listen to what is already here.

Chapters

  1. 00:11 Settling In - The meditation begins by inviting the listener to adjust, settle, and find a center.
  2. 01:13 Feeling Good, Feeling Bad - He frames practice as both noticing what feels good and being willing to meet what feels bad consciously.
  3. 02:56 Releasing Preference - The talk turns to fantasies about pleasure and the tendency to divide experience into pleasant and unpleasant.
  4. 04:17 Just Breathe - Listeners are asked to relax the strain of sorting experience and simply breathe with what is present.
  5. 07:01 Reverence for Experience - Awareness is described as respectful of all phenomena, including pleasant, unpleasant, and neutral tones.
  6. 10:04 Steady Attention - The guidance points toward stable attention and a lighter, less forceful relationship to effort.
  7. 12:24 Effort and Ease - He explores how effort can be both necessary and beautiful while still allowing a felt sense of ease.
  8. 14:00 Meeting the Mind - The practice opens into meeting the mind as it is, with improvisational wisdom and less control.
  9. 18:59 Listening Within - The closing invitation is to ask what guidance one would offer another meditator, then say it to oneself and listen.
