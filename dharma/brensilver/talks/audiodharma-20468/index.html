Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Goodnight Moon

AudioDharma 29:10 2024-05-22

A children’s bedtime book becomes the frame for this meditation: the repeated act of saying goodnight to memories, pain, thoughts, feelings, and plans. The talk moves gently from that image into a guided sitting, with deep breaths, a soothing out-breath, and a practice of putting down whatever has been lingering from the day or week. It keeps returning to the question of how to release experience without bracing against it, so what remains can rest in a wider, quieter field.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the Meditation - Matthew welcomes the group and introduces the evening’s meditation, then recalls Goodnight Moon as the image that will shape the practice.
  2. 02:36 Saying Goodnight - He turns the book’s repeated refrain into an instruction to bless and release memories, pain, thoughts, feelings, and other pieces of experience.
  3. 09:15 Settling With Breath - The practice shifts toward breathing as a steady place to land, with attention and awareness described as gentle and open.
  4. 11:49 Open Awareness - He explores how the breath can be felt either within attention or arising in spaciousness, and encourages bidding goodnight to whatever can be put down.
  5. 15:04 Not Rejection - Matthew clarifies that putting something down is not the same as exile or aversion, and points toward patient, non-grasping attention.
  6. 16:08 Resting in Presence - The meditation deepens into a quiet surrender of effort, with an emphasis on placing hope in this moment and allowing awareness to hold what remains.
  7. 26:02 Fatigue and Brightness - Near the end, he includes fatigue itself as something that can soothe the body, while awareness leaves a brightness in its wake.
