Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Welcome and Opening Remarks

AudioDharma 43:55 2016-10-07

Matthew Brensilver opens this retreat by framing practice as a sincere welcome to every part of experience, including joy, sorrow, and the parts of ourselves we usually resist. He moves from that tone of permission into the practical supports of retreat: community, silence, safety, beginner’s mind, and the way ethics and refuge shape the container for practice. The talk closes with the formal refuges and five precepts, then a quiet invitation to rest and begin the retreat gently.

Chapters

  1. 00:09 A Real Welcome - Matthew begins by saying that welcome here is sincere and includes all parts of experience, not just the pleasant ones.
  2. 01:59 Everything Belongs - He develops the refrain that everything belongs, using it to soften judgment and point toward the basic distinction between suffering and freedom.
  3. 05:05 Listening With Care - The talk turns to self-listening, retreat as a gentler kind of practice than self-improvement, and the role of sangha as beloved community.
  4. 08:47 Shared Sincerity - Matthew describes retreat as co-created, where sincerity and willingness matter more than performance and where the group helps open each heart.
  5. 13:39 Surprised by Practice - He reflects on coming to retreat with expectations, being surprised by suffering and beauty, and discovering overlooked qualities of the heart.
  6. 16:41 Fresh Seeing - The talk moves through the beauty of retreat, beginner’s mind, the safety of a trusted container, and the need to notice how we relate to experience.
  7. 24:42 Refuge and Ethics - Matthew explains refuges and precepts as a reorientation toward truth, safety, and freedom, and as the ethical ground of meditation practice.
  8. 34:20 Reciting Refuge - He introduces the refuge chant in Pali and leads the recitation as a communal turning toward Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha.
  9. 41:17 Five Precepts and Rest - The precepts are taken as training commitments, and the retreat is formally opened with a gentle invitation to get rest and settle in for the night.
