Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Love, Relationship, Sexuality, and Dharma

AudioDharma 31:26 2015-03-19

How do love, relationship, and sexuality become part of Dharma practice without being reduced to rules or ideals? The talk begins with a vivid memory of a dance party at a Buddhist gathering, then turns toward the tension between embodied life, longing, clinging, and the wish to live and love with more awareness. Along the way, it draws on Adam Phillips, Rilke, Jack Kornfield, Freud, and Nietzsche to explore how relationship can reveal both our deepest conditioning and our capacity to grow.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening Memory - Matthew begins with a remembered gathering of Buddhist teachers and a dance party that sets up the talk’s embodied, relational tone.
  2. 04:28 Love and Dharma - He names the talk’s focus and frames romantic love as a meeting place of belonging, attachment, sexuality, and evolutionary force.
  3. 08:30 Listening Gently - Matthew pauses over the mixed emotions in love and asks listeners to meet this topic with tenderness and awareness.
  4. 12:02 Why Love Is Hard - He reflects on humbling experiences in relationship, brings in Rilke, and contrasts practicing for dying well with practicing for loving well.
  5. 15:34 Cultural Assumptions - The talk turns to the assumptions around heterosexuality, monogamy, and sexuality that can quietly shape our thinking.
  6. 18:18 Desire and Renunciation - Matthew describes sexuality as powerful and painful, then uses retreat and celibacy examples to show how renunciation can simplify the field.
  7. 24:10 The Past in Love - He explores how early conditioning and hidden patterns live inside adult relationships, with Freud and Nietzsche helping name the opacity of the heart.
  8. 27:05 Relationship as Practice - Matthew closes by suggesting that love, relationship, and sexuality can reveal clinging and help us grow freer for the sake of ourselves and others.
