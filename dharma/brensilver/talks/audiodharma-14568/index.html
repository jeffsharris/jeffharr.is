Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Don't Let Greed And Aversion Lurk Behind the Technique

AudioDharma 30:13 2021-11-19

The talk begins with a careful question: when does reaching for meditation technique become another way of avoiding what is already here? The speaker traces a subtle tension between technique as a genuine support and technique as a defense against greed, aversion, clinging, or the mind’s wildness. From there, the guidance moves into a precise breath practice, asking for steady attention, courage, and a kind of peace with whatever feeling and preoccupation arise in the background.

Chapters

  1. 00:45 Technique and avoidance - Brensilver reflects on his technique-oriented practice and cautions that the impulse toward technique can sometimes hide greed or aversion.
  2. 02:14 A subtle warning - He plants a seed to notice when practice itself becomes a defense against the wildness of mind and heart, then begins the meditation.
  3. 04:00 Settling the body - The instruction shifts toward arriving all at once, affirming aspiration, summoning courage, and bringing attention to the breath.
  4. 06:29 Precise attention - He guides attention to the clearest sensations of breathing and emphasizes steadiness, precision, and the beginning of the inhale.
  5. 11:28 When attention wanders - Brensilver describes how the breath can become brighter as concentration deepens, then names the pull of worry, preoccupation, and feeling beneath thought.
  6. 18:42 Shelter and release - The practice is framed as shelter from the storm, but only when there is peace with the storm, including releasing covetousness and displeasure.
