Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Feelings and their elaborations

Dharma Seed 54:09 2020-10-06

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation focused on feeling tone: pleasant, unpleasant, and neutral, and how quickly those tones can turn into story, aversion, and identity. The guidance stays close to direct bodily experience, repeatedly returning attention to breath, posture, sensation, and the simple fact of what is present. He closes with a brief Rumi quote and a short Damasio passage that reinforce the teaching that feeling accompanies mind throughout ordinary experience.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Arriving - The meditation opens by inviting a return to what it feels like to be here now.
  2. 01:58 Attention as care - Matthew frames meditation as a steady way of honoring life through awareness and love rather than complication.
  3. 08:33 Body first - The instruction settles into the body, breath, gravity, and posture as the first field of practice.
  4. 17:42 Feeling tone - He names pleasant, unpleasant, and neutral as the basic tones that shape experience and behavior.
  5. 24:32 From sensation to story - The talk explores how unnoticed unpleasantness can blossom into aversion, blame, and a whole world of interpretation.
  6. 36:35 Not building on feeling - He encourages patience and generosity while noticing the ebb and flow of feeling without building a self around it.
  7. 39:30 Rumi and transition - A brief Rumi quotation leads into the shift from formal guidance toward a wider reflection on attention and receptivity.
  8. 49:38 Damasio and closing - Matthew cites Antonio Damasio to emphasize that feelings accompany mind throughout perception and thought, then returns to the simple invitation to know feeling directly.
