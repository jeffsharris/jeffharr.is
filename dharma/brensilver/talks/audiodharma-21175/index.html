Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Saturday Guided Sit

AudioDharma 46:35 2024-08-31

A guided sit that begins by asking you to notice the heart’s inner posture and then ease off the habit of “doing” meditation. The practice moves through body awareness, the felt boundary between body and space, and the difference between arousal and valence—how feeling tones color experience without needing to become a story or action plan. From there, the talk turns toward receiving experience with clarity and equanimity, using even difficult sensations as invitations to let go and stay close to the path.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Settling the posture - He begins by inviting attention to the body and the heart’s inner posture, then encourages less doing and more careful stopping.
  2. 04:04 Whole-body awareness - The guidance turns toward sensing the whole body, noticing what is often missed, and feeling the body’s edges and density in space.
  3. 10:15 Arousal and valence - He introduces arousal and valence as key dimensions of experience, including how emotions and simple sensations are colored by them.
  4. 13:47 Clarifying feeling - The practice focuses on mapping feeling in the body and recognizing that pleasant, unpleasant, and neutral states all arise within the same field.
  5. 18:13 Receiving without counsel - Rather than using feeling to make decisions, he invites being softened by experience and repeatedly returns to freedom as available in each moment.
  6. 24:57 The self-story loosens - He describes how affect can seem to issue orders and create a sense of self, then shows how awareness and disidentification make that grip easier to release.
  7. 29:51 Feeling as practice - The talk names the Buddha’s teaching that all dhammas converge on feeling and uses it to frame feeling as a field to honor, not always interpret.
  8. 41:33 Retreat and closing notes - He ends with practical reminders about staying held by the path, noticing habits, protecting silence, and meeting the rest of the retreat with sincerity and openness.
