Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation - Deep Letting Go

AudioDharma 34:49 2024-01-26

If this moment had to be lived for all eternity, what posture would the heart take? The guided meditation keeps returning to that question as it invites a deep letting go of self-hatred, self-view, self-love, and self-care, then opens into a felt sense of futurelessness. From there, the practice loosens the burden of being sandwiched between memory and possibility, and explores how clinging creates the very sense of time it seems to inhabit. It ends by putting down models of the self to discover what remains when reference points and hope-fear patterns are allowed to melt.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening question - The meditation begins with a direct invitation to consider the heart’s posture if this moment were to last forever.
  2. 00:30 Letting go inwardly - The practice turns toward deep release, gently questioning self-hatred, self-view, self-love, and self-care as fixed positions.
  3. 03:56 Outside memory and future - Matthew introduces 'futurelessness' to point to the burden created by time and asks the listener to sense life between memory and possibility.
  4. 07:04 Time and clinging - He explores time as impermanence and notes that clinging and time reinforce each other through a subtle habit of reference.
  5. 11:09 Dropping reference points - The meditation repeatedly invites the listener to stop reiterating reference points such as me and you, self and world, past and future.
  6. 15:09 Becoming empty - The practice emphasizes emptiness as a way of not letting thoughts, feelings, and sensations strike or harden into the heart.
  7. 21:59 Hope and fear soften - Wanting and not wanting are treated as ways the present freezes, and awareness is used to melt the stiffness of hope and fear.
  8. 32:17 Putting down self-models - The close asks the listener to release all models of self and discover what remains when identity is no longer being assembled.
