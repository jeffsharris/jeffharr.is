Dharmette: Sourcing our Motivation from Love Rather than Fear

AudioDharma 14:24 2022-09-27

What if the force that shapes your practice is not fear, but care? The talk begins with a simple contrast drawn from a residential treatment setting: behavioral change can be pushed by fear, but lasting change is more likely to be sustained by love. From there, it turns inward, asking what kind of “behavioral modification program” we run on ourselves, and whether self-harshness, proving, and shame are quietly driving the work of practice. The talk then softens the whole frame with Suzuki Roshi’s line that we are “perfect just as you are” and there is still room for improvement. It moves toward curiosity, humor, and a lighter relationship to our flaws, suggesting that practice becomes less entangled when growth is sourced from tenderness rather than self-judgment.

Chapters

  1. 00:34 A Different Dharma Pleasure - He introduces the theme of finding pleasure in practice, especially the pleasure of sourcing motivation from love.
  2. 02:01 Working With Boys - A story from residential treatment illustrates how behavior programs can be driven by either fear or care.
  3. 02:56 Fear or Love - He names the tension between short-term compliance and the kind of change that can actually last.
  4. 04:08 What Drives Us - The talk turns inward, asking what kind of program we run on ourselves and where our motivation comes from.
  5. 05:17 Self-Harshness and Proof - He describes self-hatred, punishment, and gold-star thinking as ways of trying to change through fear and proving.
  6. 07:53 Curiosity and Humor - As shame loosens, limitations can be held with curiosity and even laughter rather than judgment.
  7. 09:41 Care Over Urgency - He points to tenderness, sympathy, and a sober appreciation of our condition as a steadier basis for growth.
  8. 12:01 Less Entangled - The close emphasizes lightness, discovery, and a motivation rooted in love rather than fear.
