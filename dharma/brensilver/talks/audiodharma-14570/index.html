Guided Meditation: Not Reiterating or Reimaging Self

AudioDharma 29:57 2021-11-18

What happens if, for one sitting, you stop trying to solve your life, improve the moment, or figure out what it is for? This guided meditation repeatedly returns to resting with the breath, then gently notices how the mind keeps re-imagining the body, the room, and the story of “me.” The practice movement is to soften that subtle self-making and let awareness hold even the urgent energies, rather than feeding them with more narrative.

Chapters

  1. 00:37 Settling In - The meditation begins with repeated invitations to sit and arrive in the body.
  2. 01:54 Awareness First - He frames the practice as a brief vow to prioritize awareness over the details of life.
  3. 05:21 Resting Without Agenda - The talk questions what we would do with the moment if we did not have to solve or use it.
  4. 09:52 Softening Self-Image - Attention turns to the mind’s subtle habit of imaging the body, the room, and the boundary of self and other.
  5. 12:46 Autobiography in Awareness - He describes the ongoing habit of updating the story of me and suggests letting those words be known as empty.
  6. 18:28 Meeting Urgency - The practice shifts to bodily urgency, inviting patience and love as the energy drains downward.
  7. 20:09 Space Moving Through - He points to a loosening of confinement in self and a sense of space moving through experience.
  8. 27:12 Nothing To Figure Out - The meditation closes by returning to the refrain that there is nothing to optimize or determine, only surrender to awareness.
