Morning Instruction: Reviewing the Factors of Enlightenment

AudioDharma 42:49 2025-06-21

Starting from the feeling that we have to become someone else before peace is possible, the talk opens a different experiment: enoughness, basic okay-ness, and the sense that you do not need to change yourself to be worthy of peace. It then moves through several factors of awakening as lived experience—mindful noticing, investigation, willingness, interest, tranquility, and equanimity—showing how the nervous system settles when it stops fighting itself. Along the way, the speaker keeps returning to the contrast between agitated future-thinking and a quieter way of resting in this moment, even while acknowledging that life and the world are still complex. By the end, the teaching turns toward a simple, intimate claim: the face of emptiness is love.

Chapters

  1. 02:20 Enoughness - He begins by inviting an assumption of basic okay-ness, suggesting we do not need to become different in order to be worthy of peace.
  2. 04:55 Mindful Seeing - He introduces sati and dhammavichaya as ways of knowing phenomena directly and investigating the causal webs behind suffering, love, and peace.
  3. 06:30 Willingness and Interest - A moment of radical willingness gives rise to rapt interest, which he describes as something genuine rather than performed.
  4. 08:12 Awareness and Tranquility - He turns to consciousness and the preference for awareness, then to the settling and gain of tranquility as the nervous system quiets.
  5. 09:43 Delight in the Path - He describes agitation draining away and encourages simple attention and enjoyment, including delight in being born and having a path.
  6. 13:24 Future-Lessness - As the heart settles, compulsive questions about what comes next fall away and the moment is met without projecting it into the future.
  7. 20:58 Equanimity - He names the ease that appears when the nervous system stops fighting itself and friction dissolves into upekkha.
  8. 25:58 Half-Dead, Half-Alive - Citing Shinzen Young, he contrasts ordinary life with Dharma life to suggest a fuller range of vitality and presence.
  9. 40:50 Love and Emptiness - He ends by saying there is love and repeating that the face of emptiness is love.
