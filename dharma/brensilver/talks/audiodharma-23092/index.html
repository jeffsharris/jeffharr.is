Guided Meditation: Embodiment and Allowing

AudioDharma 41:36 2025-06-03

What does it mean to become available to this moment, rather than trying to force the mind into place? This guided meditation moves gently through relaxation, embodiment, and the feel of breathing in the belly and bones, while also noticing how relaxation can reveal aversion, vulnerability, and the limits of trying to manage experience with words. The emphasis stays on softening, opening awareness, and treating each moment as a fresh beginning, even when practice feels impossible. By the end, the talk points toward a freedom that does not announce itself, but is met by loving what arises without grasping.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening Intention - The meditation begins by asking what it means to be available to the Dharma and to this moment.
  2. 04:58 Willingness Over Willfulness - He contrasts a softer willingness with forceful striving and frames effort as alignment rather than control.
  3. 06:48 Relaxing into Newness - The practice turns toward relaxing more fully and meeting each moment as a fresh beginning.
  4. 10:12 Body as Refuge - Mindfulness of the body is emphasized as a way to keep presence steady while sights and sounds arise.
  5. 13:37 Breath into the Bones - Attention moves deeper into the belly and bones, with an invitation to live the moment less through conceptual thought.
  6. 16:01 Coping and Openness - He reflects on thinking as a form of coping and asks how much openness awareness can hold.
  7. 17:55 Softening the Impossible - When practice feels impossible or helpless, the instruction is to soften, keep the heart open, and remain available.
  8. 28:09 Freedom Without a Sign - The talk closes with images of freedom, emptiness, and loving phenomena so completely that the selfing around them can loosen.
