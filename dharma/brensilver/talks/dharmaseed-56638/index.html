Guided Meditation: Body Settling, Pleasure of the Breath

Dharma Seed 30:18 2019-06-02

Fear and uncertainty are named right at the start, and the meditation answers them by inviting a simple spirit of love that can hold whatever is present. From there the talk settles into posture, body awareness, and breath, moving slowly from warming the whole body with attention to resting on the subtle pleasure of breathing in and out. Near the end, the speaker points to the mind’s habit of bracing and gently suggests relaxing even after the bell, noticing where unease still lingers.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Meeting Fear with Love - The meditation opens by naming retreat anxiety and offering love as the felt response to fear.
  2. 03:28 Posture and Grounding - Attention turns to posture, balancing a strong uprightness with softness and surrender to gravity.
  3. 04:42 Warming the Body - The body is gradually filled with awareness, including places that feel numb or less accessible.
  4. 09:08 Finding the Breath - Breath sensations are brought into the wider field of body awareness and welcomed without strain.
  5. 11:40 Sustaining Contact - The guidance steadies attention on the full cycle of inhalation and exhalation, with repeated reminders to let go.
  6. 14:21 Pleasure in Breathing - The talk shifts to subtle pleasure in breathing as a way to soothe body and mind.
  7. 20:58 Thought and Letting Go - Matthew names the pull of thinking and the difficulty of letting the rest of experience sit in the background.
  8. 22:44 Simple Return - The instruction becomes very simple: notice beginning and end, breathe in, breathe out, and relax right now.
  9. 29:11 After the Bell - The closing invites curiosity about the mind after meditation ends and notices how much unnecessary bracing can soften.
