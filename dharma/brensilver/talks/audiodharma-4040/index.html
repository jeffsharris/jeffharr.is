Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation

AudioDharma 35:34 2013-04-25

What happens when anger is no longer treated as one solid block, but as a mix of body sensation, inner talk, and mental images? The talk begins by laying out that question, drawing on a Buddhist map of experience and a three-part breakdown associated with Shinzen Young, then moves into a guided meditation that asks listeners to notice those strands separately. The practice stays close to direct experience: settling the body, sensing emotional energy, hearing thought as sound, and seeing images arise and pass away, so anger can feel more workable and less overwhelming.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Welcome and purpose - Matthew welcomes the group and introduces the practice as a way to unpack what anger is made of.
  2. 01:21 Breaking experience apart - He draws on the Buddha’s maps of experience and Shinzen Young’s three-part lens to separate body feeling, mental images, and internal talk.
  3. 03:31 How anger tangles - He describes how the three strands of anger reinforce one another and how that tangle leads to harsh conclusions and actions.
  4. 06:45 More manageability - Matthew contrasts overwhelm with the added manageability that comes from noticing each component on its own.
  5. 08:23 Settling into practice - The guided meditation begins with posture, balance, and a gentle settling into the body.
  6. 14:27 Feeling in the body - Attention moves to emotional sensation in the body, including how emotion shows up physically and how to stay with it without acting out.
  7. 23:25 Listening to thought - He shifts attention to the felt location of inner speech and invites listening to thinking as sound-like activity rather than identification.
  8. 29:17 Images and integration - The practice turns to mental images, then returns to the full picture of feeling, talk, and image as strands to meet with mindfulness, wisdom, and love.
