Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: entering the dharma realm

AudioDharma 26:18 2026-01-07

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation that frames practice as intentionally “entering a Dharma realm,” a field of mind shaped by goodness, kindness, clarity, and letting go. He moves through refuge, the subtle reorientation of perception, and the way this ground helps us meet suffering without adding more to it. The talk closes by turning toward love, vow, and service as expressions of a radical non-violent path.

Chapters

  1. 00:11 Taking Refuge - The meditation begins by settling into refuge as a way of meeting the world without becoming overwhelmed by it.
  2. 01:07 Why Refuge Matters - Brensilver reflects on how hatred and confusion shape us, and why we need repeated remembrance of refuge and non-harming.
  3. 03:52 Entering the Dharma Field - He introduces the idea of consciously entering a Dharma realm, where body, thought, and feeling are organized by practice.
  4. 07:08 Second Arrow - The talk turns to the second arrow teaching, distinguishing unavoidable suffering from the suffering added around it.
  5. 08:52 Goodwill and Renunciation - He emphasizes abiding together in goodwill, renunciation, and the intention not to harm.
  6. 11:24 Subtle Change - Using Shinzen Young’s phrase, he points out that these shifts can be very small yet still significant.
  7. 17:11 Breath and Love - Attention settles into cues of goodness, including images of what is loved and a conscious breath that recalls love.
  8. 22:58 What Love Asks - The closing questions ask what love asks of us, what we vow, and what we are here to serve.
