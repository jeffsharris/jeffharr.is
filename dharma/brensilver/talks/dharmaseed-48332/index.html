Matthew Brensilver, Sylvia Boorstein: Day 1: Evening Practice Session (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 1:40:21 2018-01-13

This evening session moves from a short period of sitting into a broad Dharma teaching on training the mind, the Four Noble Truths, and the Eightfold Path. Sylvia Boorstein then traces the path through story, personal memory, poetry, and examples of everyday generosity, showing how letting go, grief, and compassion are part of the same practice. Matthew Brensilver joins near the end to reflect on non-clinging, and the talk closes with a quiet emphasis on freedom from ownership and neediness.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening instructions - Sylvia sets up the evening sit and names a few ways to attend to experience, from breath techniques to a simple phrase of friendliness.
  2. 01:53 Shared practice - The group sits together in silence, then transitions into walking practice and a pause before dialogue.
  3. 49:49 Buddhist basics - Sylvia gives a brisk, story-shaped account of the Buddha’s life, awakening, first teaching, and the practical meaning of the Four Noble Truths and Eightfold Path.
  4. 01:03:11 Practice stays human - She widens the frame with examples showing that practice does not erase feeling, but changes how we relate to anger, grief, and ordinary discomfort.
  5. 01:13:18 Paramis and early inspiration - Sylvia recalls first being moved by Jack Kornfield’s teachings and turns to the perfections of the heart as qualities to cultivate deliberately.
  6. 01:15:37 Compassion and non-clinging - Matthew reflects on how wisdom and compassion support each other, using Joseph Goldstein’s changing answer about the Dharma to point toward non-clinging.
  7. 01:18:24 Letting go of ownership - The talk deepens into grief, impermanence, and the illusion of possession, drawing on Andy Olinsky and Catherine Schultz to show how loss can soften the heart.
  8. 01:29:53 Freedom through giving - Sylvia returns to the practical side of non-attachment with examples of giving, ordinary need, and the freedom of saying, in effect, 'I don't need it.'
  9. 01:36:58 Moonlight and closing - A Zen story about a hermit monk and the moon becomes a final image of generosity beyond possession, followed by the closing invitation to walk or rest without guilt.
