Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Faith in Silence

AudioDharma 29:13 2024-03-15

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation centered on learning to trust silence instead of using thought as a constant coping strategy. He gently argues that the body’s signals, the mind’s reactions, and the urge to solve everything do not need to be read as threats, and he repeatedly returns to the reassurance that it is okay to let things rest. The practice moves from settling the posture and soothing the body into longer stretches of silence, with reminders to soften, stop over-reading experience, and listen for what tranquility makes possible.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Settling In - He opens with the framing of the practice, naming coping and inviting a posture that can be sustained.
  2. 02:41 Soothing the Body - The practice begins with embodied easing, fuller breaths, and softening tension in the face and belly.
  3. 05:06 Putting Down Complications - He reassures listeners that it is not neglect to set aside problems for a while and rest in tranquility.
  4. 10:18 Faith in Silence - The teaching turns toward trusting silence, letting go, and noticing that the present moment is not trying to harm us.
  5. 15:52 Over-Reading Experience - He points to the habit of making too much meaning out of sensations and phenomena, then invites a brief stopping.
  6. 18:48 Heart Training - The talk emphasizes how innocent the mind’s scrambling can be, and how much the heart can be trained.
  7. 20:05 Listening To Silence - A long guided period follows, in which listeners are asked to listen to silence, take cues from it, and keep letting go.
  8. 25:20 Extended Quiet - The meditation continues into a sustained stretch of silence with only faint, unclear audio artifacts near the end.
