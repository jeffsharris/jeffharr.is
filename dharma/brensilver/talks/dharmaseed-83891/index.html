Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Feeling

Dharma Seed 52:29 2024-05-14

Matthew Brensilver leads a guided meditation centered on feeling, beginning with breath and a gentle turn away from compulsive problem-solving. The practice moves through the affective roots of reactivity, the ways feeling gets turned into story, and the invitation to let experience unwind without feeding it with thought. Later he broadens into retreat-specific reflection on futurelessness, death, effort, boredom, and how to keep practice steady without turning effort into self-strain.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling With Breath - The meditation begins with exhalation, body awareness, and a gradual settling into the retreat sit.
  2. 02:14 Problems and Dissolving - Matthew contrasts problems that need solving with problems that dissolve when held in awareness.
  3. 03:10 Commitment To This Moment - He frames practice as a commitment to stay with what is here rather than keep escaping into fixing.
  4. 06:25 Wanting And Knowing - The talk explores how wanting shapes knowing, and how relinquishment changes what perception can reveal.
  5. 09:46 Feeling And Reactivity - He names common reactions like sleepiness, agitation, craving, ill will, and doubt as movements rooted in feeling.
  6. 13:46 Stories From Feeling - Matthew describes how we keep narrating experience, and invites feeling to be just feeling without making a story.
  7. 20:12 Non-Thinking And Release - He distinguishes thinking from coping and encourages non-conceptual awareness supported by the breath.
  8. 23:11 Clinging And Heaven - The instruction turns toward letting go of clinging and noticing how easily the mind can cross into suffering.
  9. 28:35 Futurelessness And Death - Matthew reflects on the thin line between heaven and hell, the heaviness of the future, and how death clarifies what matters.
