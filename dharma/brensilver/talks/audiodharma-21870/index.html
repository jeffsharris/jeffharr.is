Dharmette: Dereification of Self-Model

AudioDharma 15:05 2024-12-11

What happens when thoughts, preferences, and even the sense of “me” stop feeling like solid reality and start appearing as objects in awareness? The talk begins with a childhood thought experiment about everything falling away—family, time, universe—then turns that experience into a careful explanation of emptiness as the progressive dereification of the self-model. From there it moves through ordinary examples like lunch, craving, aversion, ownership, and autobiography, showing how mindfulness and equanimity make what was transparent begin to stand out. It closes by pointing toward quiet as the condition for hearing the subtler noise of selfing and becoming more comfortable with not knowing.

Chapters

  1. 00:18 A Childhood Thought Experiment - Matthew opens with an early memory of imagining family, history, and the universe dissolving into nothingness, using it to introduce emptiness through lived experience.
  2. 04:18 What Dereification Means - He defines dereification as thoughts becoming mere thoughts rather than reality, and explains mindfulness as seeing phenomena as phenomena.
  3. 07:14 Thoughts Become Objects - Using Thomas Metzinger’s language, he describes how a thought like lunch can be recognized as an object of attention instead of being automatically absorbed into the self-model.
  4. 09:33 Layers of Selfing - The talk broadens from single thoughts to the ongoing song of self, showing how autobiography, body, and unnoticed preferences keep reinforcing identity.
  5. 12:17 Ownership and Self - He cites the Majjhima Nikaya to point toward ownership as a key mechanism of selfing, then links equanimity to loosening craving, aversion, and the sense of 'mine'.
  6. 14:11 Quiet and Not Knowing - Matthew closes with Ram Dass and Nisargadatta, emphasizing quiet, comfort with uncertainty, and the openness that comes when reference points soften.
