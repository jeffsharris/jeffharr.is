Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Emotions and Inner Safety

AudioDharma 47:45 2018-03-21

Our problems often feel solid because they rest on a conclusion, and this guided meditation keeps returning to the place before the conclusion hardens. The practice moves through the felt life of emotion in the body—pleasant, unpleasant, named or vague—asking for steady, gentle attention instead of clinging, bracing, or trying to solve everything at once. As the sit deepens, the talk widens into the relationship between body, mind, and the stories we build, using a brief story about a man in severe suffering to show how problems can look different in immediate awareness.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening reflection - He begins with the idea that our problems rest on conclusions and introduces the practice of folding those conclusions back into awareness.
  2. 07:11 Emotion in the body - The talk turns to how conclusions about happiness and threat are often driven by emotional sensation felt in the body.
  3. 10:39 Mindfulness and equanimity - He describes emotion as an engine of motivation and clinging, and frames steady awareness as a major development in practice.
  4. 17:05 Receiving emotion gently - The guided portion emphasizes courageously receiving emotion as it appears, with attention to pleasant and unpleasant sensations alike.
  5. 24:32 Body and mind - He explores the reciprocal way body sensations trigger thought and thought triggers bodily emotion, and invites simple noting of feeling in body and mind.
  6. 29:22 Radical permission - The instruction deepens into giving the feeling body permission to arise and pass, including as sitting becomes more tiring or painful.
  7. 37:34 After the bell - As the formal sit ends, he asks listeners to stay close to bodily sensation while opening the eyes and moving into visual experience.
  8. 42:27 Where are my problems? - A brief story about a homeless man illustrates how the solidity of problems can loosen in direct awareness, ending with the phrase about loving sensations to death.
  9. 46:37 Closing encouragement - He closes by encouraging a relaxed, sustainable form of awareness that can stay open without becoming strained.
