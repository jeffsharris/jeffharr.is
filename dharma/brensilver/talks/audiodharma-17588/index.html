Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Non-Doing & Liberative Dependent Orignation

AudioDharma 53:36 2023-01-15

Matthew Brensilver explores non-doing as a real trajectory of Dharma practice, moving from effort and courage into a quieter logic of surrender. Using the Therigatha, Augustine, and several contemporary Dharma teachers, he traces how faith, joy, tranquility, concentration, disenchantment, and dispassion can unfold when willfulness relaxes. The talk gradually shifts from the felt work of practice to the possibility that liberation is less something manufactured than something allowed.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening with humility - Matthew begins with a playful anecdote and uses it to set a tone of honesty rather than performance.
  2. 00:51 Friendship and trust - He reads from the Therigatha on friendship, trust, and making the mind a companion on the path.
  3. 01:59 Effort and surrender - The talk turns to effort, courage, and the limits of trying to control practice, then opens into surrender.
  4. 05:03 Hands off the wheel - Augustine’s restlessness and the image of Maggie driving illustrate the move from striving to getting out of the way.
  5. 08:11 A map of unfolding - Matthew introduces liberative dependent origination as a causal map of freedom rather than suffering.
  6. 12:02 Faith to joy - Faith arises from suffering and hope, then matures into gladness and a more companionable path.
  7. 18:39 Rapture to tranquility - He describes pīti, embodied delight, and the way it settles into tranquility when striving relaxes.
  8. 25:44 Stillness and samadhi - The talk emphasizes confidence, quiet happiness, and samadhi as unification rather than forced concentration.
  9. 33:22 Disenchantment deepens - Matthew uses Ajahn Brahm and Ajahn Sucitto to show how aligned intention, less coping, and clearer seeing loosen craving.
