Releasing Judging, Comparing, Fixing: Guided Meditation: Non-Fixing

AudioDharma 41:48 2026-05-27

What if meditation is not about making goodness happen, but noticing what is already here? The talk moves from releasing the habit of control and fixing toward a quieter practice of forgiving phenomena, where attention becomes reverential and the mind softens into love. It ends by holding the path as a living balance between dissolving and reification, light and heavy, surrender and power.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Goodness Already Here - The guided opening asks what goodness is already present and invites a shift away from self-made striving.
  2. 01:45 Settling Into Practice - The instruction turns toward simply beginning meditation without confusing effort with the creation of goodness.
  3. 06:05 Letting Go of Control - The talk reflects on how many ways the mind tries to govern experience and how relieving it is to stop.
  4. 07:10 Long Quiet Unfolding - A sustained stretch of silence and repetition supports the practice of non-fixing and listening without interference.
  5. 20:16 Space and Stillness - The practice widens into sensory space, mountains, and the sense of stillness running through awareness.
  6. 23:13 Forgiveness and Reverence - Forgiving all phenomena is presented as a way of uncovering an attention that feels reverential rather than grasping.
  7. 34:57 Surrender and Love - The talk says power is found through surrender and that spaciousness lets the mind take on the coloration of love.
  8. 39:02 Light and Heavy - An attributed saying about making what is light heavy and what is heavy light summarizes the path as a balance of dissolving and reifying.
