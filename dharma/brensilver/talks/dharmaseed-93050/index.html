Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Forgiving Body, Mind and World

Dharma Seed 40:48 2025-09-19

Matthew Brensilver offers a gentle guided meditation built around forgiveness as a practical loosening of friction rather than a forced state of peace. The practice moves from sensing what the mind needs, through body-based softening and truce with pain, toward a spacious awareness that can hold reactions, fear, hope, and ordinary sounds without struggle. It ends with a quiet inquiry into what awareness remains even when control drops away.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling into need - The meditation begins by distinguishing what the mind needs from what it craves and framing practice as both peace and the willingness to meet difficulty.
  2. 05:45 Making peace - He reframes practice as making peace with pain, helplessness, grasping, and aversion rather than trying to force perfect calm.
  3. 08:09 Forgive body and mind - The instruction turns directly toward forgiving the body, the mind, sorrow, the human condition, and the burden of past, pain, and death.
  4. 11:23 Global softening - The emphasis shifts to a whole-body relaxation and a refusal to fight oneself, replacing control with a simpler field of forgiveness.
  5. 16:29 Nothing left to do - After forgiving everything, the practice points to abiding with whatever remains when craving and hatred no longer need to do their work.
  6. 22:27 Breath and non-friction - The guided meditation deepens into natural breathing, softening, and a steady, low-friction attention that can rest in the body.
  7. 31:27 Everything tastes like Dharma - Awareness broadens to include reactions, longing, aversion, fear, hope, and even ordinary sounds as part of one continuous field of practice.
  8. 38:15 Rest in awareness - The closing inquiry asks what remains when the bell rings and whether there is an awareness that does not depend on effort, ending in simple resting.
