Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Between Gravity and Grace

AudioDharma 13:47 2024-09-18

Matthew Brensilver uses Leonard Cohen’s writing as a lens for dukkha, practice, and the uneasy beauty of living between falling and grace. He reflects on why people practice when it is not always comforting, how motivation changes over time, and why the Dharma can feel less like an answer than a relationship with wisdom and love. The talk stays close to the tension between honesty about suffering and a quiet trust that practice can still matter.

Chapters

  1. 00:14 Cohen’s Gravity and Grace - Brensilver opens with a biography of Leonard Cohen to frame art, suffering, and the image of falling bodies.
  2. 03:07 Why Practice at All - He turns from Cohen to the question of why anyone practices when the Dharma is hard, humbling, and not reliably pleasant.
  3. 05:36 Not Understanding Yet - A second Cohen reference shows how the Dharma may not be understood at first, yet still shapes how one lives.
  4. 06:37 Intrinsic Motivation - He says practice cannot be handed to us by a teacher and compares it to love, something felt more deeply than explained.
  5. 09:22 The Broken Cup - Ajahn Chah’s image of the broken cup and the already-flooded world sharpens the talk’s realism about impermanence.
  6. 10:10 Young Life, Old Suffering - Brensilver reflects on youth, ambition, ambivalence, and the sense that ordinary life did not feel like a path.
  7. 12:22 Enough Wisdom and Love - He closes by saying the Dharma is not perfect consolation, but wisdom and love may be enough to keep practicing.
