Guided Meditation: Being Changed by Experience Rather than Changing it

AudioDharma 30:37 2021-08-12

This guided meditation begins with settling the body and finding a posture that is supple but strong, then rests attention in a quiet openness to sights, sounds, sensations, and thought. The practice repeatedly turns away from pushing and pulling on experience, asking instead for a softer heart and a willingness to be impacted. As the talk unfolds, it names the tension between trying to shape life and letting experience do its work, ending by encouraging a steady, receptive way of feeling one's way through the flood of urges.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and arrival - Brief welcomes give way to settling in and preparing for the practice.
  2. 01:15 Finding the posture - Instructions emphasize posture as supple yet strong, with a fresh openness to phenomena.
  3. 03:53 Releasing tension - Attention turns to relaxing what can be relaxed and allowing remaining tension to stay without struggle.
  4. 06:54 Softening toward experience - The practice shifts toward easing the impulse to shape experience and letting the heart be softened by it.
  5. 13:27 Feeling at the center - He points to feeling as the place where all phenomena converge and where the limits of equanimity are noticed.
  6. 15:27 Willing to be changed - The meditation deepens into receptivity: being impacted, being still, and letting experience change us.
  7. 18:38 Standing down - He names the strain of trying to cobble together a life and invites settling back into direct feeling.
  8. 20:29 Closing turn - The talk appears to end mid-thought, still oriented toward not shaping life from the mind.
