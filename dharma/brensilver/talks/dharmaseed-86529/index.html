Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Love and Space

Dharma Seed 42:25 2024-07-11

The talk begins by warning not to look for redemption in words, then turns toward a quieter task: returning attention to what lies within. From there it moves through embodiment, the habit of measuring and sorting experience, and the pressure to keep building a story of “me,” offering instead a wider heart that can welcome the whole of experience. A repeated refrain—lay it down—keeps bringing the listener back from self-making toward a more open, less claustrophobic way of being.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening orientation - He begins by clarifying the role of teaching and pointing listeners back to what already lies within.
  2. 03:51 Body and earth - The talk settles into embodiment, gravity, and the soothing steadiness of the body on the earth.
  3. 07:48 Dropping the categories - He invites an experiment in letting go of familiar sorting into right and wrong, belonging and intruder.
  4. 10:16 Measuring falls away - Suffering is linked to measurement, and the listener is encouraged to stop treating the self as something to assess.
  5. 16:55 Lay it down - The teaching turns direct and insistent: stop trying to control or shape the self and let the story relax.
  6. 27:47 Autobiography grows remote - He describes how our stories can feel distant and unmoving, and suggests looking at selfing lovingly rather than looking through it.
  7. 35:13 Meaning and no meaning - Awareness is presented as wordless, and he explores moving between meaning, no meaning, and renewed meaning without compulsion.
  8. 39:11 Closing reflections - The talk eases into retreat announcements and a final invitation to stay in stillness and consider what arises next.
