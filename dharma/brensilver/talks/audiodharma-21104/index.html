Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Feeling and Verbal Thinking

AudioDharma 29:14 2024-08-21

How can a meditation practice stay open when it keeps being pulled toward fixing, explaining, and controlling? The talk begins by questioning the impulse to reach for technique, then settles into feeling the body directly: its pressure, arousal, and shifting emotional textures, with attention and patience instead of defense. From there it turns to inner speech, treating thought as something that can be heard rather than obeyed, and finally to the way feeling and story shape each other. The practice movement is simple and sustained: notice, allow, and let both feeling and words be held in awareness with less compulsion.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Arriving and Settling - Matthew welcomes the group and begins by inviting a simple sitting practice.
  2. 02:40 Letting Go of Technique - He questions the urge to use technique as coping and asks what it is like to sit undefended.
  3. 05:44 Breath and Release - The practice shifts into fuller breathing and a sense of relaxing and cleansing the body.
  4. 07:45 Feeling in the Body - Attention turns to emotional and reactive sensations in the body, with emphasis on their location, intensity, and changing quality.
  5. 11:02 Patience and Permission - He encourages bathing feeling states in attention and patience, allowing them to move and self-liberate in their own time.
  6. 14:19 Hearing Thoughts - The meditation explores how thinking can be noticed as auditory experience, using Shinzen Young's teaching as support.
  7. 19:18 Feelings and Stories - He brings together words and feelings, showing how emotion can prompt stories and explanations that seem to stabilize experience.
  8. 23:56 Equanimity and Blessing - The closing invites equanimity with both bodily urgency and the mind's narration, ending in awareness, patience, and love.
