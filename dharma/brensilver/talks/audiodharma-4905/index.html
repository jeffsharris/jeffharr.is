Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Practice Questions

AudioDharma 19:24 2014-06-26

How do you practice when desire is pulling in different directions—when goals matter, but the mind keeps turning them into a promise of happiness? The talk moves through a series of questions about wholesome desire and craving, why success can still leave us unsettled, and how taking care of this moment is a more reliable way of caring for the future. It then turns practical: how to keep practice alive in a busy life, how much solitude or contemplation is enough, and whether joy has a place in the path before the hard work feels complete.

Chapters

  1. 00:09 Opening Questions - Matthew invites questions about practice and integration of Dharma into daily life.
  2. 01:28 Goals and Craving - He distinguishes wholesome desire from craving and explains why practice asks us to watch how we hold goals.
  3. 03:47 Success and Letdown - Using Adam Phillips and examples of accomplishment, he points to the gap between success and inner fulfillment.
  4. 05:11 Take Care of This Moment - He brings in Thich Nhat Hanh and argues that caring for the future happens through present-moment attention.
  5. 06:52 Busy Life Practice - In response to a question about limited time, he suggests retreat planning, daily contact with the cushion, and keeping a thread to practice even in short sittings.
  6. 11:40 Solitude and Contemplation - He addresses the need for quiet and reflection, while warning against using solitude to cut off from life or relationship.
  7. 14:42 Dharma in Daily Life - The conversation turns to how reading, reflection, and lived practice deepen one another as the Dharma comes to shape the whole of life.
  8. 16:39 Practice and Joy - Matthew closes by saying that the path talks about suffering because dismantling suffering leaves goodness, relief, and a more natural joy.
