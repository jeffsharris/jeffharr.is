Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Following Interest

AudioDharma 28:10 2025-06-11

This guided meditation begins with a Thich Nhat Hanh image of settling, then turns toward letting experience rise rather than forcing it down. Matthew Brensilver repeatedly returns to the ease of not having to manage attention, inviting trust that practice can happen without strain or performance. The guidance then shifts into following what is genuinely interesting, treating curiosity as a kind of receptive, non-extractive attention.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Settling Like Nectar - An opening image from Thich Nhat Hanh frames meditation as a settling rather than a struggle.
  2. 01:01 Letting Things Surface - The guidance describes how less suppression allows what is hidden or active to rise on its own.
  3. 04:34 Trusting Non-Doing - Matthew challenges the urge to control attention and emphasizes that nothing needs to be manufactured.
  4. 08:41 Following Interest - The practice turns toward the thread of interest, asking what this moment and this life are.
  5. 09:29 Curiosity as Practice - Curiosity and investigation are presented as a supportive intention, independent of where attention lands.
  6. 15:15 Receptive Attention - Attention is reimagined as a blessing rather than a way of extracting something from the moment.
  7. 17:04 Counting and Closing - The talk moves into extended counting, with the final stretch feeling like a quiet settling out.
