Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Practice Notes

AudioDharma 07:00 2018-09-19

What does the mind need right now: effort, gentleness, reassurance, or a simple question that cuts through self-improvement? The talk moves through that practical territory by treating practice as a series of small, well-timed “pep talks” that can be tailored to the moment. It also opens briefly into the value of one-on-one practice discussions and the idea that Dharma is worked out in a very idiosyncratic way, person by person.

Chapters

  1. 00:30 Opening Practice Notes - He begins informally and introduces the morning's walking and sitting practice, along with the invitation for one-on-one practice discussions.
  2. 01:15 Personal Practice Discussions - Brensilver describes practice conversations as a place for whatever is closest to the heart, from technical questions to how the teachings fit daily life.
  3. 02:44 Why Guidance Must Fit - He reflects on how Dharma is taught broadly but received in highly individual ways, and why practice guidance needs to be tailored.
  4. 03:51 Learning Inner Pep Talks - The talk turns to the way practitioners gradually learn to give themselves timely, useful reminders that fit their own minds and the present moment.
  5. 04:49 Energy, Gentleness, Inquiry - He names several kinds of support the mind may need: effort, surrender, compassion, reassurance, or a question that exposes clinging.
  6. 05:56 Returning To Present Goodness - He warns against turning Dharma into a self-improvement project and suggests asking what goodness is already here so the present can be felt more clearly.
