Dharmette: Faith in Ourselves (4 of 5)

AudioDharma 13:13 2024-03-14

What does it really mean to have faith in oneself if it is not a polished story of who we are? The talk argues that confidence grows when the static of self-making loosens: when we stop leaning on grand self-images, and instead learn to trust direct knowing, bodily cues, and the small yes-or-no signals that show what is wholesome. It moves from the limits of self-esteem to a quieter kind of accountability to what we already know.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Self-esteem revisited - He begins by questioning the old self-esteem emphasis and why it often does not produce the hoped-for changes.
  2. 01:41 Faith beyond story - He reframes faith in oneself as something that arises when we stop taking refuge in the stories of self.
  3. 03:19 Not-self and confidence - Using a light musical example, he points toward anatta as a truer form of self-esteem and the end of proving oneself.
  4. 05:23 Trusting body and mind - He describes a different confidence rooted in grounding, trusting our view, and relaxing into not-self.
  5. 06:45 Intuitive knowing - The talk turns to the difference between simple, clean knowing and the wobble of doubt or rationalization.
  6. 08:15 Less self-deception - He says that as we listen more deeply, we become more sensitive to self-deception and clearer about what leads to happiness.
  7. 09:40 Subtle cues and data - He emphasizes attentive listening to subtle affective cues, treating bodily responses as useful data.
  8. 11:14 Accountable to knowing - He closes by describing faith as aligning with what we know and staying true to that clarity.
