Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Evening Reflections - A Poem

AudioDharma 04:08 2025-03-20

A retreat in the mountains becomes a quiet confrontation with impermanence: the beauty of the landscape opens into the fact that everything changes, and even stability doesn’t last. The talk turns through a poem about weakness, crumbling, and non-existence, then into a more personal reflection on fear, childhood dusk, and the way aliveness can widen when change is fully felt. It ends by naming what remains to do: remembering, letting go, and allowing in the warmth of grief.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Mountain Austerity - He opens with the feel of a retreat landscape and the way beauty and severity sharpen attention.
  2. 00:50 A Poem Appears - Matthew names and introduces the poem that will shape the rest of the reflection.
  3. 01:34 Stability Crumbles - The poem's lines on instability and non-existence are read as a direct challenge to ordinary assumptions of permanence.
  4. 02:20 Fear Wakes Up - He turns to the bodily force of impermanence, linking it to a childhood memory of dusk and to ancestral fear.
  5. 02:59 Aliveness Opens - What first felt like fear becomes a larger sense of aliveness as change is seen more clearly.
  6. 03:36 Remembering and Letting Go - The talk closes with a simple invitation to remember impermanence, release clinging, and allow grief to be felt.
