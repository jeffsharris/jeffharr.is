Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Sati

AudioDharma 49:24 2025-06-16

Matthew Brensilver offers a careful talk on sati, or mindfulness, as more than a bare attentional skill. He moves from the Dharma’s emphasis on letting go toward the fuller claim that awakening also includes what is cultivated, sustained, and taken up as a form of peace. Along the way he connects mindfulness with the seven factors of awakening, mind-wandering, reactivity, and the subtle ways identification with thought narrows experience.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Why the Dharma Lets Go - Brensilver opens by contrasting ordinary culture’s question of what to get with the Dharma’s emphasis on what to release.
  2. 03:18 Awakening as Cultivation - He uses a Samyutta Nikaya passage and a story of the sick Buddha to show that awakening factors are not only to be abandoned into, but also developed as support.
  3. 05:38 The Seven Factors Balanced - The talk turns to the seven factors of awakening as both path and fruit, including the balance between energizing and tranquilizing qualities.
  4. 09:32 What Sati Includes - He quotes the Satipatthana Sutta and expands mindfulness into a wider web of equanimity, concentration, and awareness of how experience is constructed.
  5. 14:06 Mindlessness and Restoration - Brensilver speaks candidly about attentional depletion, the longing for rest, and the difficulty of resting without falling into unconscious habit.
  6. 20:45 Thought, Narrative, and Identification - Using Michelle McDonald and Metzinger, he examines how thought and self-narrative pull attention into identification and away from direct knowing.
  7. 27:22 Mindfulness as Master Key - He quotes Nyanaponika Thera and then turns to scientific and practice-based descriptions of mindfulness as a whole set of capacities, including attention training and meta-awareness.
  8. 33:57 Holding the Mind Steady - An elephant-and-bamboo image illustrates how a meditation object steadies the mind, and Shinzen Young’s line underscores mindfulness as reduced self-interference.
  9. 40:29 Reactivity, Self, and Less Grip - The final stretch returns to equanimity, de-reification of self, and the quiet claim that less fixation on self allows more life to be present.
