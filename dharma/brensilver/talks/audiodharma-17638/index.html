Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Letting the Breath Come to You

AudioDharma 30:23 2023-01-23

The talk starts with a simple invitation to sit, then turns quickly to a set of questions about what actually changes when meditation begins: what tightens, what loosens, and where attention goes to hide or rest. From there, the practice opens out from a narrow object of attention to the larger fact of awareness itself, with the breath treated as something received rather than controlled. The guidance keeps returning to letting the breath come to you, trusting what cannot be stopped, and resting in the openness that includes everything without making the rest of experience suspect.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Arriving Together - A brief welcome leads into the invitation to sit, settle, and notice what changes as practice begins.
  2. 01:28 What Meditation Does - He asks what tightens, what loosens, and what we try to do when we start meditating, then points to what cannot be stopped.
  3. 04:41 Resting In Openness - The instruction turns toward resting in what remains present already, rather than adding effort on top of it.
  4. 06:51 Open Awareness - He emphasizes receptivity and the difference between directing attention and forgetting the larger field of awareness.
  5. 11:38 Starting With Wise View - He names wise view as the place from which practice can launch and frames the session as another round of sitting together.
  6. 12:42 Breath As The Circle - The breath is introduced as a practical object of attention, like drawing a circle around sensations of breathing.
  7. 15:51 Receiving The Breath - The practice deepens into receiving the rhythm of breathing and recognizing that attending to the breath honors the rest of life.
  8. 20:10 Nothing Outside Is Suspicious - He widens the attitude around practice, saying that what lies outside the breath is not suspect and that all experience can be met innocently.
  9. 21:24 Gentle Inclining - The meditation closes by encouraging a soft, repeated inclining toward the breath and letting the mind take its cues from its cycles.
