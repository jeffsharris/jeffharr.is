Matthew Brensilver, Sylvia Boorstein: Day 1: Afternoon Practice Session (part 1) (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 1:13:45 2018-01-13

What happens when the mind keeps stepping into old movies instead of staying with the breath, the body, and the present moment? The talk moves from a simple sitting instruction—resting in breath, hearing, and bodily feeling—into careful ways of working with distraction, pain, and emotional reactivity. It then widens into a quieter insight: practice is less about fixing every wound than learning how to soften around experience without hardening or getting lost in the realm.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the mind - The talk begins with the way a contracted mind spins up convincing stories and why they feel so real.
  2. 01:37 Walking toward clarity - Sylvia uses the Broadway and movie-house image to describe keeping attention aligned with where practice is going.
  3. 03:31 Sitting instructions - She offers simple cues for resting with breath, body, sound, and kind phrases that relax attention.
  4. 22:27 Naming the breath - A second instruction introduces gentle noting as a way to stay alert, buoyant, and in contact with the primary object.
  5. 32:48 Body awareness cue - Before opening the eyes, the group is given a brief instruction to notice what happens in the body, with a Thich Nhat Hanh reference.
  6. 34:31 Working with pain - The teaching turns to physical pain: when to stay, when to move, how to note sensation, and how to distinguish intensity from injury.
  7. 54:57 Emotional movie loops - A listener’s question about anger opens a discussion of retreat surfacing unfinished heart material and the hidden wound beneath repetitive reactivity.
  8. 01:00:08 Wounds and compassion - The conversation widens into grief, self-blame, and Sylvia’s story about Martha, pointing toward shared human vulnerability and softened suffering.
  9. 01:07:22 Out of the realm - The session closes with Zen realm language, then shifts into instructions for walking meditation with open and focused awareness.
