Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Cultivating Confidence in Practice

AudioDharma 44:50 2015-06-01

How can confidence in practice deepen not by fighting self, but by relating to it more kindly? The talk moves from the complications of emptiness and not-self into the practical work of rewriting the self-story, easing self-harshness, and finding that a loved self is easier to relinquish than a hated one. Along the way it connects insight to ethics, community, and the steadying effect of trusting the path even when the whole view is not yet clear.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Selflessness and its limits - He opens by framing emptiness and not-self as profound teachings, then quickly complicates the slogan by asking how much the self is actually a problem.
  2. 02:05 A functional relationship - Matthew argues that practice requires both loosening identification with self and developing a workable relationship to self, using Leonard Cohen to show that insight alone does not make suffering light.
  3. 05:15 Back to the present - He brings in Thanissaro Bhikkhu and Sam Harris to stress that these teachings matter because they point back to present-moment experience and the longing to be satisfied now.
  4. 08:30 Confidence and release - The talk turns to the apparent conflict between self-love and not-self, with Matthew claiming they are not opposites and that confidence can make impermanence and release feel safer.
  5. 12:51 Care that protects others - He links self-kindness to ethical conduct, suggesting that a loving relationship with one's mind makes harm less likely and deepens empathy.
  6. 16:47 Ego without battle - Using Jack Engler, Matthew questions spiritual ego-bashing and distinguishes healthy ego development from clinging to self-view.
  7. 19:15 Retelling the self - Drawing on Dan Dennett, he presents the self as narrative gravity and describes Dharma practice as a process of revising and blessing the story of one's life.
  8. 24:51 Belonging and trust - He speaks about bringing the past into awareness, the role of sangha in reflecting us back to ourselves, and the confidence that grows when the path starts to feel trustworthy.
  9. 30:35 Equanimity and enough - Matthew concludes with hospice reflections, ethical clarity, equanimity, and the idea that a loved self is easier to release, ending with E. L. Doctorow's image of driving at night by headlights alone.
