Dharmette: Awe in the Dharma

AudioDharma 14:02 2023-02-13

What if awe is not a distraction from practice, but one of the ways the path opens? The talk begins with a definition of awe as standing before something vast, then moves through a retreat memory, the rarity of human birth, and the emptiness of convention to show how wonder can loosen clinging. From there, it turns to the practical effect: when the self feels smaller, the world feels larger, and love and wisdom have more room to grow.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Opening the Theme - Matthew settles in, explains how he chose the topic, and arrives at awe and wonder as the subject for the talk.
  2. 01:42 What Awe Means - He offers Dacher Keltner’s definition of awe and links it to humility, prosocial feeling, and deeper spiritual response.
  3. 02:51 Falling in Love - A retreat memory with Galen Ferguson becomes a personal example of being touched by the Dharma’s vastness.
  4. 04:49 Human Life’s Rarity - He turns to awe for awareness itself and to the preciousness of being human as a rare opportunity to wake up.
  5. 06:39 Convention and Emptiness - The talk opens into the emptiness of social assumptions and the Buddhist idea of attachment to rites and rituals.
  6. 08:44 Wonder Without Clinging - Matthew contrasts wonder with grasping, suggesting that genuine interest in the mind carries less agenda and more freedom.
  7. 10:14 Small Self, Bigger World - He closes by describing awe as a shrinking of self and widening of experience, including the vastness of suffering and love.
  8. 13:39 Closing Blessing - Matthew briefly wraps up and offers the talk back to the listeners.
