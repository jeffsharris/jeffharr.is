Guided Meditation: Fresh Moment

AudioDharma 28:58 2025-05-21

This guided meditation starts by noticing how natural it is to expect the next moment to be more of the same, and then gently loosens that habit. The practice moves through relaxing what can be relaxed, settling into the body and breath, and meeting impermanence without being startled by it. Later, the speaker invites following whatever genuine interest arises, and then turns toward the freshness of not quite knowing, putting down expectations and sensing the quiet. The talk closes by asking what it means to cease reiterating the self and to simply breathe, feel the body, and rest in the moment.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Arriving and settling - A brief opening welcomes listeners and turns attention toward posture and beginning the practice together.
  2. 00:32 Not startled by change - The meditation frames practice as learning to meet impermanence without being jolted by it, softening resistance to the changing nature of experience.
  3. 03:08 Breath as soothing support - Attention turns to the felt experience of breathing as something gently holding and caressing the body.
  4. 06:26 Allowing the present moment - He encourages saying yes to the imperfections of this moment without making that a permanent endorsement, and describes each breath as a way of becoming more secluded and present.
  5. 12:12 Following genuine interest - When curiosity arises, he suggests treating it as a trustworthy thread for practice rather than trying to manufacture interest.
  6. 17:17 The aliveness of not knowing - The talk opens into the poetry of noticing familiar experience in slightly new ways and staying close to uncertainty without closing it down.
  7. 21:06 Releasing expectation - He invites listeners to put down expectations of finding themselves in the next moment.
  8. 21:46 Quiet and selfing - The meditation closes by asking what it means to cease reiterating the self, grounding that inquiry in breath, body, and quiet.
