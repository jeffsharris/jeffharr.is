Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Non-doing: Receptivity & Purification

AudioDharma 46:33 2023-01-13

Matthew Brensilver contrasts two instincts on the Dharma path: the drive to manage, improve, and control experience, and the quieter discipline of receptivity. He traces how effort can become tangled with self-view, then turns toward openness, sensitivity, and learning as alternatives to willfulness. In the last part of the talk, he frames difficult phases of practice not as failures but as purification, where grief, pain, and love can all be part of the same release.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Choice and choicelessness - Brensilver opens by contrasting wise choice with the less visible current of choiceless receptivity in practice.
  2. 04:11 Effort and willpower - He draws on Nietzsche and familiar practice rhetoric to show how effort can slide into force, self-rehabilitation, and spiritual striving.
  3. 11:20 The meditator program - Using Ajahn Sucitto, he names the meditator identity itself as another conditioned pattern shaped by self-criticism and control.
  4. 14:11 Receptivity as practice - He turns to openness, learning, and the idea that practice is not only about changing life but being changed by it.
  5. 23:24 Wanting shapes perception - The Mulapariyaya Sutta and reflections on perception show how desire and motivation color what seems to be simply seen.
  6. 28:28 Sensitivity and vulnerability - A Krishnamurti passage and Matthew's own reflections explore sensitivity, porousness, and relaxing the body's contractions.
  7. 34:13 Suffering and purification - He reframes the First Noble Truth through the difficult arcs of practice, where descent, helplessness, and grievance can become purification.
  8. 40:27 Grief, love, release - The talk closes with Shinzen Young and Leonard Cohen, tying purification to equanimity, grief, and a steadier love that survives loss.
