Dharmette: Confidence & Humility

AudioDharma 15:01 2023-12-11

What happens when confidence and humility are not opposites, but two qualities that have to be held together? The talk moves from the idea that wisdom changes with context, to the ways practice can be misused by picking the wrong teaching, and then into a careful unfolding of how confidence grows through experience rather than certainty. By the end, humility is framed not as self-doubt, but as openness to what is actually here—and as a safeguard against violence when we forget how often we can be wrong.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and framing - Matthew settles in, acknowledges the setting, and introduces the basic challenge of speaking about Dharma at all.
  2. 01:05 Wisdom needs context - He explains that wisdom changes with conditions and that delusion often comes from choosing the wrong teaching for the moment.
  3. 02:38 Learning the right tilt - The talk turns to practice as a skillful movement between paired qualities like determination and self-compassion.
  4. 03:49 Confidence from practice - Matthew names confidence and humility as the central pair, and describes how deepening practice can become devotional and trusting.
  5. 04:58 Practice confirmed - He cites Gil on awakening as confirmation that practice works, then describes a more intimate sense that life is simply here rather than elsewhere.
  6. 06:44 Enoughness and calibration - The teaching develops into a grounded self-confidence based on sufficiency and a better sense of what actually leads to happiness.
  7. 08:25 Humbled by realization - Matthew emphasizes how practice keeps exposing the gap between understanding and realization, and how easily the mind can deceive itself.
  8. 10:10 Openness without certainty - Humility is defined as staying open to present data without covering over mystery with self-serving conclusions.
  9. 11:53 Tentative knowing, nonviolence - He closes by connecting uncertainty with hedging, moral caution, and the move toward nonviolence.
