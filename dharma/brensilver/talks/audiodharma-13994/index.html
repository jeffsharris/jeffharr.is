Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Softening to the Ungovernability of Experience

AudioDharma 30:57 2021-08-11

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation on softening to the ungovernability of experience: meeting discomfort without needing to organize, fix, or sort it all out. The practice moves between settling the body, feeling the weight of gravity, and opening to the reality of dukkha with a steadier, more tender mind. He returns again and again to the possibility that equanimity is not cold distance, but a quiet route into love.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening Reflection - The talk begins with a brief introduction to the theme and the relationship between equanimity, dukkha, and love.
  2. 02:24 Settling Into Stillness - The guidance turns toward sitting, noticing how practice can feel like coming home or like entering cold water.
  3. 05:43 Body and Gravity - Attention shifts to posture, the spine, and surrendering the rest of the body to the earth's downward pull.
  4. 08:05 Not Sorting It Out - Brensilver points to the ordinary flood of mixed conditions and suggests equanimity as freedom from having to adjudicate everything.
  5. 11:05 Freedom In Experience - The meditation explores how freedom can be found inside each moment rather than elsewhere or later.
  6. 16:13 Bowing To Dukkha - The guidance focuses on softening the heart, bowing to the truth of suffering, and letting that bow itself become tenderness.
  7. 17:06 Silent Practice - A long period of meditation follows with minimal verbal guidance, allowing the themes to be felt directly in stillness.
  8. 30:15 Closing Bells - The sit ends with several bells, marking a quiet close to the practice.
