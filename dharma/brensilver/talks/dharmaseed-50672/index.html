Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Samadhi (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 49:50 2018-06-13

What if concentration is not something you force, but what appears as you stop creating friction with experience? The talk moves from the pressure of discursive thought and attention-fragmenting technology into a practical case for samadhi as steadiness, shelter, and the ground for insight. Along the way, the speaker questions the habit of treating thinking as the main way to solve life, and keeps returning to patience, letting go, and learning to be present without strain.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and presence - Matthew opens lightly, then settles into the sense of practicing together as a field of trust and attention.
  2. 00:57 Compassion and stability - He uses Brian’s bodhicitta talk as a bridge to show how an open heart depends on an already steady mind.
  3. 04:44 What samadhi means - He reframes enlightenment and concentration as less friction with experience, and as peace rather than a project of control.
  4. 10:06 Attention under pressure - A critique of technological distraction follows, including Tristan Harris and a personal reflection on phones and attention.
  5. 13:14 Shelter from the storm - He describes how samadhi narrows the field of awareness, softens bombardment, and supports insight into body and mind.
  6. 20:14 Thought and story - The talk turns to the habit of narrating experience, the fear of silence, and the need to meet thought without shame.
  7. 29:03 Pleasure and attachment - Using a Buddha sutta and the lake simile, he describes the joy of concentration while warning against making bliss the goal.
  8. 35:10 Patience and letting go - He emphasizes patience, dependence on causes and conditions, and the loosening of self-protective habits as the mind gathers.
  9. 45:36 Natural stillness - An Ajahn Brahm image of the leaf leads into a closing reminder that stillness cannot be engineered, followed by a short sit and thanks.
