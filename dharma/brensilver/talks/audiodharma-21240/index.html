Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Intimacy with Breathing

AudioDharma 28:18 2024-09-11

This guided meditation stays close to the breath while exploring how attention can become more intimate with sensation. Matthew Brensilver invites listeners to notice the breath as fresh, immediate, and sensed through the whole body rather than held as an abstract object. The practice also makes room for tiredness, loose ends, and sticky emotions, returning again and again to breathing as a simple home base.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Arriving With Breath - The meditation opens by orienting attention to the familiar breath and its fresh quality.
  2. 00:56 Images and Sensation - He explores how subtle visual imagery can guide attention into the felt sensations of breathing.
  3. 03:25 Thin Slices of Time - The instruction turns toward intimacy with breathing and the question of how to meet each moment more closely.
  4. 06:10 Breath Awakens the Senses - Breathing is described as something that can enliven the senses and meet us even when we are tired.
  5. 07:45 Peace and Refuge - The talk distinguishes real peace from false refuge and invites making peace with the loose ends of life.
  6. 13:29 Forgiving the Mind - He acknowledges the mind's patrolling and foraging, then points back toward reconnection with this moment and the Dharma.
  7. 15:28 Home in the Breath - The practice settles into simplicity, with the breath as a place where experience becomes small, clear, and enough.
  8. 19:14 Sticky Thoughts to Breath - He suggests meeting sticky emotional activation in the body first, then returning to the rhythm of breathing when it softens.
