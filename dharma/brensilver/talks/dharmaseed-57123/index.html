Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Body and Space (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 39:00 2019-06-21

The meditation begins with a very simple anchor: breath, breath, breath, until the body settles enough to notice where effort has been tight or artificial. From there, the practice opens into a softer honesty—relaxing bracing, letting the present moment be met without defense, and then widening attention from the breath to the whole body and the surrounding soundscape. In the later part of the guided sit, the attention moves to the edge between body and space, where sensations, pain, heartbreak, and love are all felt as changing process rather than fixed things.

  3. 10:04 Simple presence - Attention returns to the breath and then opens to different supports, including the body and the surrounding soundscape.
  4. 20:26 Letting go again - The guidance turns to awareness letting go of its objects and to knowing body and sound as changing events.
  5. 25:43 Body and space - Attention shifts to the space around and through the body, where pain, heartbreak, and love can all be held.
  6. 32:41 Everything is process - The talk lands on impermanence, with experience described as verbs rather than nouns, including the self.
  7. 38:40 Closing - The meditation ends with thanks and the standard Dharma Seed close.
