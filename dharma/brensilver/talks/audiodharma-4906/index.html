Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Some Fruits of Practice

AudioDharma 32:25 2014-06-26

Matthew Brensilver reflects on what Dharma talks can do beyond instruction, and then turns to the quieter fruits of practice: more ease in conduct, less conflict, a simpler life, and a heart that is less defended. He moves gradually from gladdening the mind to the way practice can integrate the past, soften self-clinging, and make everyday life feel like the path. The talk ends with a gentle encouragement to notice what is useful, trust your own goodness, and let that goodness spill outward.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Three functions of talk - Matthew opens with the Buddha's framing of Dharma talks as instructional, gladdening, and supportive, and explains why he wants this talk to emphasize support.
  2. 03:15 Why fruits matter - He talks about how practitioners often feel they are doing practice wrong, and why reflecting on the fruits can steady and encourage the heart.
  3. 06:05 Ethics becomes natural - The talk turns to how behavior can come into closer alignment with intention, and how sila can gradually feel less like sacrifice and more like ease.
  4. 10:04 Wholeness and trust - Matthew describes practice as digesting old suffering, reducing doubt, and making one's whole life feel like the path.
  5. 12:27 Path becomes personal - He explores how practice can feel deeply one's own, bringing safety, responsibility, and a quieter question about what really matters.
  6. 15:13 Wanting less - Using affective forecasting and resilience, he suggests that practice can make us more realistic about happiness, simpler in desire, and less burdened by wanting.
  7. 18:50 Self-clinging loosens - The talk moves into the burden of self-referential thinking, illustrated by Ajahn Sumedho and Ajahn Amaro, and distinguishes self-care from self-clinging.
  8. 22:09 The vase and the heart - Through the image of carrying a fragile vase, Matthew shows how protecting a phantom self constricts life, while letting that burden down makes life feel safer and more open.
  9. 25:49 Wisdom and love - He closes by saying that seeing clearly and loving deeply belong together, then uses a story about a tiny turn of attention becoming a life to encourage staying present.
