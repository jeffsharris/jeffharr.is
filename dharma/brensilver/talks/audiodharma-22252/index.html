Dharmette: The mirage of self evaporates as the fires cool

AudioDharma 14:45 2025-02-12

Matthew Brensilver opens with a quote from Ajahn Sucitto and uses it to explore nibbana as cooling, spaciousness, and the easing of compulsive selfing. He moves between Buddhist teaching, reflections on lay practice, and a few outside references to show why liberation is less about improving the self than about letting the fires of craving and fear settle. The talk ends by turning toward a quieter, less territorial sense of freedom that leaves more room for care.

Chapters

  1. 00:16 Ajahn Sucitto's frame - Matthew introduces Ajahn Sucitto's teaching on dispassion and cooling as the lens for the whole talk.
  2. 02:23 Practice turns inward - He reflects on how radical the path feels, especially for lay practitioners, and questions the wish to keep some of both samsara and nibbana.
  3. 04:52 Mara and conditioning - Using a brief Metzinger reference, he describes evolutionary conditioning, the reward system, and the self-model as forces that feed compulsivity.
  4. 06:19 Openness instead of denial - Matthew argues for respectful, radical openness to suffering rather than fighting or denying Mara, and names suffering as a kind of forgetting.
  5. 08:38 Space and cooling - He returns to nibbana as space in the mind and contrasts suffering's claustrophobia and heat with dispassion and tranquility that remain fully alive.
  6. 10:45 The self cannot own freedom - The talk turns to ego's tendency to colonize even spiritual practice, and then to the idea that freedom is subtraction, not an attainment.
  7. 12:50 What remains - With the image of the self as a hologram, he suggests that genuine dissolution of self leads to more care and a more open relation to life.
