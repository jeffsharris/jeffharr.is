Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Day 2 Morning Guided Instructions (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 50:38 2019-10-19

Rest is available even amid the moment’s imperfection, and the talk keeps returning to how to let the body and breath make that rest real. It moves from a gentle scan for bracing and fear, to settling into gravity, exhalation, and the feeling that the whole body is breathing out, then toward a steadier attention that can release self-judgment and the usual reference points of past, future, good, and bad. Near the end, the speaker broadens into trust in the logic of practice, briefly citing Sharon Salzberg to underline that we do not need to understand the whole path in advance; sincerity is enough for the learning to unfold.

Chapters

  1. 01:01 Rest Is Available - He begins by inviting a felt sense that rest can be available right now, through sound, silence, or ease in the body.
  2. 04:11 Scanning for Bracing - The guidance turns toward noticing where the body or mind is bracing, and gently releasing the fear of relaxing into the present.
  3. 09:17 Settling With Gravity - He returns to the practice by encouraging a downward sense of gravity and a soft, whole-body exhalation that helps the sit deepen.
  4. 13:58 Breath in the Body - Awareness is broadened to the whole body, and the breath is highlighted within that field without pushing away other experience.
  5. 20:38 Sustaining Attention - The instruction emphasizes aiming and sustaining attention through the full breath cycle, letting the breath become a clear and intimate object.
  6. 25:13 Letting Go of Sorting - He names the habit of evaluating each moment and invites the listener to put down self-judgment, reference points, and the usual categories of good and bad.
  7. 32:45 Falling Into Awareness - The talk opens into a quieter inquiry into the length of now and the way awareness touches phenomena, culminating in a release into awareness itself.
  8. 38:20 Trust and Practice Logistics - Matthew cites Sharon Salzberg while encouraging trust in practice, then shifts to retreat announcements, practice discussions, affinity groups, and questions.
