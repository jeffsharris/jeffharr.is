Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Emphasizing Tranquility

AudioDharma 27:27 2025-08-20

What happens if tranquility is less something we force than something we let settle? The talk keeps returning to that motion of easing off willfulness, yielding to silence, and noticing the restful texture already present in body and mind. It also names practical adjustments for tired or bright states, including how to work with agitation without demanding its absence. Near the end, the emphasis deepens into simplicity: as hunger in the mind subsides, less and less is needed, until just being alive can feel like enough.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling In - The meditation opens with a settling image from Thich Nhat Hanh and an invitation to let calm arise naturally.
  2. 00:30 Ceasing to Strive - Matthew emphasizes that relaxation comes less from doing than from stopping the urge to control the moment.
  3. 01:29 Yielding to Silence - He returns to the sense of silence drawing the mind inward, with a playful detour into a quoted line that is hard to place.
  4. 02:49 Finding the Quiet Beneath - The instruction turns toward yielding and sensing the silence beneath or beyond the noise of experience.
  5. 05:30 Brightness Or Rest - He adjusts the practice depending on energy level, suggesting either nurturing alertness or resting in subtle quiet.
  6. 07:26 Listening to Tranquility - After a brief pause in the guide, the meditation continues in the same spirit of open attention and receptivity.
  7. 10:51 Signs of Calm - Matthew notes that tranquility does not depend on total absence of agitation, only on noticing its signs.
  8. 15:01 Putting Down Burdens - The practice deepens into relinquishment, forgiveness, and the willingness to set down what can be released.
  9. 18:14 Enoughness and Closure - He closes by pointing to the mind's lessening hunger, Suzuki Roshi's reminder that being alive is enough, and the bell sounds that end the meditation.
