Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: don't wait to let go

AudioDharma 28:27 2025-08-27

The meditation begins with posture, breath, and a felt yielding to gravity: finding both suppleness and strength, then giving the rest of body and mind to the earth. From there, the talk turns toward impermanence, suggesting that when we stop imagining ourselves as permanent, even one breath can feel enough; that same recognition becomes the basis for the refrain, “don’t wait to let go.” The practice movement is simple and direct—soothing the body, loosening grasping, and noticing how surrender changes the way everything appears. Near the end, the talk widens into loving-kindness and a brief personal reflection on grief and a friend’s death.

  6. 22:25 Practice loving - He notes that love is needed because the human condition can feel too much, and invites a simple abiding in loving attention.
  7. 23:44 More space around pain - The meditation turns toward the body-mind as a place where experience can be felt more directly, with less control and more room.
  8. 25:59 Remembering Kathleen - Matthew briefly remembers a friend who died that weekend, using the memory to speak about grief, claustrophobia, and the relief of more space.
