Guided Meditation: Beginning with Goodness

AudioDharma 29:57 2023-01-24

This guided meditation begins by turning attention toward goodness already present: in oneself, in others, in peace, love, wisdom, and in the ordinary ground of the path. From there, the practice settles into the body and breath, using simple reminders of goodness whenever the mind gets tight or contracted, so the breath can be received with more ease and less effort. The talk keeps returning to the felt rhythm of giving and receiving through breathing, and to the possibility of resting in care without having to manufacture anything special. It closes with a poetic refrain that you do not need to manufacture the sacred, underscoring the central move of recognizing what is already here.

  3. 10:54 Breath as nourishment - The practice turns toward the felt relief of breathing in and out, with attention to the body being nourished and released.
  4. 18:09 Relaxing vigilance - Matthew names the habit of hedging against the present and encourages a gentler trust in what is happening now.
  5. 19:55 Devoting ourselves here - The focus narrows to the breathing body, with an emphasis on clarity, precision, and presence without tension.
  6. 27:33 The sacred already present - The closing quotation reinforces the teaching that the sacred need not be manufactured, but can be recognized in ordinary experience.
