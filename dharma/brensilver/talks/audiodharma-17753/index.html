Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Openness of Awareness

AudioDharma 29:27 2023-02-16

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation centered on how attention shapes experience. He moves from settling the body and softening vigilance into breath-based practices that brighten, soothe, and release, then opens into a wider sense of awareness. The talk closes by contrasting that grounded simplicity with the pull of hope and fear, and how the heart can see things differently when rest and seclusion are present.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Opening Welcome - A brief introduction leads into the central theme that how we attend shapes the world we experience.
  2. 00:51 Attention Shapes Experience - The talk turns to the basic relationship between attention, energy, and the quality of mind.
  3. 01:50 Settling the Body - Instruction emphasizes relaxing tension into the earth and attending without recapitulating agitation or vigilance.
  4. 05:34 Breath as Practice - The breath is introduced as something to welcome gently, with inhale and exhale supporting brightness and ease.
  5. 09:07 Release and Suspension - The practice deepens into a rhythm of brightening, soothing, and release, with moments of poised stillness at the end of the exhale.
  6. 13:15 Seclusion in Breath - Brensilver describes how attention can gather into a protected, secluded world and reveal the openness of awareness.
  7. 19:57 Hope and Fear - The teaching shifts from spaciousness to the ways hope and fear entangle the heart with worldly concerns.
  8. 23:57 Closing Reflection - The talk briefly returns to the same contrast between embroilment and the quieter clarity found in breath and simplicity.
