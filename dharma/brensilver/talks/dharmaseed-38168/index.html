Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Spirit Rock-Attention, Love and Service

Dharma Seed 45:10 2016-11-25

The talk begins with a simple three-part aim: to love what is already here, transform ourselves, and serve others. From there, it moves through the way suffering dominates attention, the danger of turning practice into self-improvement, and the need to balance seeing clearly with not getting trapped in the self. Along the way, the speaker brings in stories from mindfulness teachers, clinical psychology, a museum performance built around sustained gazing, and poetry to show how attention can soften the heart into love, compassion, and service.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Practice and what it serves - He opens by linking the aims of practice: to love what is here, transform ourselves, and serve others.
  2. 02:20 Goodness already here - Using Joseph Goldstein's phrase, he turns from striving toward noticing the goodness and support already present.
  3. 05:17 Beyond becoming - Ajahn Sumedho's warning about becoming enlightened is used to challenge spiritual self-improvement as an ego project.
  4. 06:03 Service and mutuality - Marsha Linehan's work and self-disclosure illustrate mindfulness as practical care, shared vulnerability, and skillful means.
  5. 12:00 Attention as love - He develops the idea that wisdom and compassion converge, and that deep attention itself can be a form of love.
  6. 22:33 The gaze that reveals - Rilke and Marina Abramović help frame a sustained gaze as something that exposes grief, tenderness, and the wish to be seen.
  7. 27:11 From empathy to freedom - He turns from shared suffering toward empathy, then toward love without clinging, including the freedom of the other.
  8. 34:38 Purifying care - The talk moves into how compassion can become tangled with grasping, and how mortality clarifies what matters most.
  9. 42:02 Start and continue - He closes by urging listeners not to aim for perfection, but to begin, continue, and let love and service stay honest.
