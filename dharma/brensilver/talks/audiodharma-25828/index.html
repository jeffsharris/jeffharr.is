The Idea of the Bodhisattva

AudioDharma 14:14 2026-07-08

How can an ordinary practitioner think of themselves as a bodhisattva without turning the ideal into a measurement game? The talk begins with Shantideva’s vast vows and then turns toward the discomfort they can arouse: admiration, self-doubt, and the sense of being nowhere near the standard. From there, the teaching gently reframes the bodhisattva as a lens for perceiving self and other, and as a vow of love that can be begun in whatever way is possible now. It closes by bringing the ideal down to an ordinary mishap, suggesting that the vow may show itself most clearly in the middle of daily embarrassment and repair.

  5. 09:40 Love against fear - The talk turns to fear, mortality, and pride, suggesting that love and selflessness are the medicine.
  6. 11:26 Daily remembrance - The speaker emphasizes repeating the vow, asking what love and goodness mean in practice and how life looks through that commitment.
  7. 12:53 Coffee on the floor - An everyday accident becomes the closing illustration of the bodhisattva vow arriving in real time, amid apology and cleanup.
