Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Self and Willfulness

AudioDharma 29:36 2024-06-05

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet guided meditation that keeps returning to a simple question: what happens if we stop trying to make meditation happen? He gently examines how willfulness can create a self that manages experience, and invites a softer trust in awareness, breath, body, and sound. The practice gradually shifts from effort and suspicion toward surrender, letting doingness dissolve into openness.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling In - The meditation begins with a brief welcome and settles into the instruction to meditate.
  2. 01:00 What Began Meditation - He asks listeners to look at what they do when they first begin meditating, then to notice the impulse to stop doing.
  3. 04:07 Trusting the Mind - The talk turns toward the suspicion that we must make something happen, and toward trusting mind and awareness instead.
  4. 06:31 The Self as Organizer - He reflects on how even simple attentional instructions can give rise to a self that orchestrates practice.
  5. 08:09 Anchor Everywhere - The teaching widens the sense of support, suggesting that what we trust may not depend on personal effort alone.
  6. 12:17 Willfulness and Job - He names willfulness as part of the self's work and questions the role we keep assigning ourselves in meditation.
  7. 15:25 Awareness Finds Us - The practice deepens into letting awareness meet us, rather than reaching for it through effort.
  8. 21:44 Surrender and Ease - The closing settles into surrender, asking practice to become a movement of trust and release.
