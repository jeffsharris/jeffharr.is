Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Love, Relationship, Sexuality and Dharma Part 02

AudioDharma 32:21 2015-03-26

Matthew Brensilver continues a talk on love, sexuality, and Dharma by examining how romantic longing mixes tenderness, projection, risk, and uncertainty. He moves from the wish to unite eros and metta, to the ways we idealize one another, to the deeper practice of seeing a partner’s freedom without trying to possess or complete them. The talk closes with reflections on monogamy, fidelity, and how relationship can become an ongoing path of practice rather than a settled solution.

Chapters

  1. 00:09 Love and Eros - He begins by framing romantic love as a possible union of passion, kindness, and tenderness, while wondering whether attachment can ever be fully separated from desire.
  2. 04:27 Projection and Seeking - Using Lacan, he explores projection, idealization, and the painful fact that no partner can be the end of our seeking.
  3. 07:00 Seeing the Other - He turns to the radical otherness of the person we love, and to the ordinary ways love can become a screen for our own hopes and fantasies.
  4. 11:17 Relationship as Practice - A story about Kittisaro and Thinisara becomes a way to speak about shared life, compassion, equanimity, and the difficulty of staying present with a partner’s suffering.
  5. 14:38 Risk and Desire - He uses Richard Russo and Stephen Mitchell to describe love and erotic desire as forms of risk that destabilize the self and invite control.
  6. 18:20 Not Knowing Together - The talk deepens into the challenge of living with uncertainty, resisting the deadening pull of routine, and staying open to what cannot be known in advance.
  7. 21:23 Freedom and Not-Self - Drawing on Otto Rank, Rilke, and the Dharma teaching on anatta, he argues that mature love sees the other’s freedom and avoids fixing them into a solid identity.
  8. 25:56 Monogamy as Adventure - A short piece by Iain Brown is used to show fidelity as an ongoing journey marked by longing, newness, and choices that never become entirely settled.
  9. 29:17 Closing Reflection - He ends with a brief silent reflection and a blessing that practice, whether single or partnered, support greater freedom of heart for self and others.
