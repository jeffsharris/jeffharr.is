Dharmette: Curiosity and Goodness

AudioDharma 13:19 2025-07-30

Matthew Brensilver explores how the Dharma’s goodness becomes visible over time, especially when practice is shaped less by striving and more by genuine curiosity. He contrasts control with open interest, then turns to stubborn “karmic knots” as places where acceptance, patience, and relationship matter more than technique. The talk closes by describing how interest and love can feed one another until practice feels less like effort and more like a natural unfolding.

Chapters

  1. 00:11 Opening into goodness - Matthew begins with a simple reflection on the Dharma’s goodness and how it may not be visible at first.
  2. 01:19 Goodness takes time - He describes the way the path can be idealized early on and how its deeper goodness emerges through patience.
  3. 02:35 Beyond instructions - He argues that the path’s goodness cannot be forced by advice alone and that something more inward is needed.
  4. 04:02 Curiosity as practice - Matthew develops dhammavichaya as sincere, non-transactional interest that supports mindfulness and energy.
  5. 06:17 Control or openness - Using Ajahn Chah, he contrasts the effort to control body and mind with simple presence that allows truth to appear.
  6. 07:28 Working with karmic knots - He turns to Michelle McDonald’s framing of karmic knots as painful places where acceptance matters more than trying to fix them.
  7. 09:35 Patience and Sangha - Matthew says interest cannot be commanded, and points to continued practice and spiritual friendship as conditions for it to return.
  8. 11:31 Love deepens interest - He closes by describing a virtuous cycle in which love, goodness, and curiosity enrich one another over time.
