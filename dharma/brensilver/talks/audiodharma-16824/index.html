Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Effort and Effortlessness

AudioDharma 29:10 2022-08-19

How do effort and ease belong together in meditation? The talk guides a steady practice of landing in posture, choosing an anchor for attention, and then noticing that the work of directing attention can support a quieter, more effortless awareness. Rather than trying to make experience disappear, it points toward relaxing the grip on thought, resting in what cannot be stopped, and letting experiences arise in open space.

Chapters

  1. 02:54 Landing and settling - The practice begins by arriving in posture and allowing the mind to land without trying to predict the session.
  2. 06:26 Choosing an anchor - Attention is directed to an object like the breath, while the rest of experience is allowed to remain in view.
  3. 13:12 Effort and awareness - Matthew draws a distinction between the effort of attention training and the quieter, seemingly effortless quality of awareness.
  4. 18:56 Relaxing the grip - The instruction shifts toward easing control and recognizing the space in which experiences arise.
  5. 20:17 Less mesmerized - As discursive thinking loosens, awareness feels less like a personal possession and more like open knowing.
  6. 25:01 Letting the face soften - A cited instruction from Pandit becomes a teaching on relaxation and non-identification with the face and body.
  7. 27:38 Opening further - The meditation continues with repeated invitations to let bodily self-image drop into the openness of awareness.
