Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Tranquility

AudioDharma 16:21 2026-05-13

What if tranquility is not flatness, but the condition that lets the mind actually see? The talk begins with a memorable line about the Dharma being “parasympathetically biased” and follows that thread into a case for steadiness, patience, and bodily calm as supports for truth rather than signs of emotional narrowing. It then turns practical, naming the ways hyperarousal, clinging, and compulsivity build stories, and offering low-friction ways to settle: music, smell, nature, touch, self-distanced speech, and simple labeling.

Chapters

  1. 00:09 A Wilder Friend - Matthew opens with a retreat memory and a contrast in temperaments that leads into the talk’s central question about calm and vitality.
  2. 01:23 Parasympathetic Dharma - He develops the idea that Dharma practice is oriented toward replenishment, rest, and soothing rather than mere control or emotional narrowing.
  3. 03:34 Why Tranquility Matters - He argues that knowledge and vision depend on steadiness in body and mind, and invites listeners to notice which states bring clarity or confusion.
  4. 05:03 Feeling Into Story - Matthew describes how arousal and discomfort generate meaning-making, and how stories can grow out of the pressure of feeling.
  5. 07:16 Tension and Selfing - He links afflictive states such as clinging, greed, hatred, and selfing with bodily tension, and frames deep relaxation as part of insight.
  6. 10:24 Practicing Refuge - The talk turns to the slow cultivation of refuge, love, and awareness, especially when resources are depleted and effort is limited.
  7. 11:37 Low-Effort Regulation - Matthew introduces a Harvard article on low-effort emotion regulation and uses it to support his emphasis on simple, accessible forms of soothing.
  8. 13:52 Small Soothing Supports - He names examples like music, smells, nature, touch, self-distanced language, and affect labeling as ways to bring tranquility into ordinary life.
