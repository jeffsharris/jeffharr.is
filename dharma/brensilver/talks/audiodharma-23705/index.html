Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Wholesome Qualities of Mind (1 of 5): The Parami of Truthfulness

AudioDharma 15:04 2025-08-18

Matthew Brensilver reflects on truthfulness as a foundational virtue in the Dharma path, moving from the parami tradition into personal memory, clinical insight, and social critique. He traces how honesty supports both inner sanity and mutual understanding, especially when deception and self-deception distort perception. The talk closes by turning truthfulness into a quiet practice of listening, resisting bad faith, and learning to tolerate what is real.

Chapters

  1. 00:24 The parami of truth - He introduces the ten paramis and frames truthfulness as a central virtue at the heart of the path.
  2. 01:24 Truth and rigor - Using Metzinger, he links scientific honesty and spiritual practice through a shared commitment to veracity.
  3. 02:02 Childhood aversion to lies - He recalls how hypocrisy and hiddenness stirred him strongly, and connects that reaction to the human need for truth.
  4. 03:37 Helping reality make sense - Drawing on child development and trauma, he describes honesty as part of the moral labor of helping others understand what is real.
  5. 05:17 Why lies harm - He turns to Buddhist teaching on deliberate lying and the idea that deception induces delusion and harm.
  6. 06:57 Bullshit and indifference - With Frankfurt, he distinguishes lying from bullshit and names indifference to truth as a deeper problem in public life.
  7. 08:14 Political rage and self-righteousness - He notices how disgust at deception can slide into anger, proof-seeking, and a self-righteous cycle.
  8. 10:00 Practicing veracity - He shifts into vows and reflections on how to honor truth, resist self-deception, and avoid bad faith debates.
  9. 12:25 Sangha and strength - He ends by emphasizing listening, communal truth-telling, and the strength needed to tolerate reality without illusion.
