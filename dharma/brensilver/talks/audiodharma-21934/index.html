Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided meditation

AudioDharma 28:32 2024-12-18

The talk begins with settling the body and finding whatever in the moment feels even slightly good or soothing—especially the breath, the pause, and the relief of simply stopping. From there, it explores how equanimity lets attention rest without immediately turning toward worry, planning, or trying to rearrange the world, and how that makes room for a more open, less snagged way of being with experience. The practice keeps widening: the heart is offered to the moment, the aperture of attention opens, and what felt heavy can start to feel lighter and more spacious.

Chapters

  1. 00:29 Settling the body - He invites listeners to find a posture and arrive in stillness in whatever position is available.
  2. 01:17 Finding what feels good - The guidance turns toward noticing small pleasantness in stopping and using that as a light anchor.
  3. 02:54 Less urgency, more equanimity - He reflects on worry, planning, and the ordinary pull of concerns, framing equanimity as staying with what is less urgent.
  4. 07:18 Breath as refuge - Attention narrows to the breath as something soothing and naturally regulating.
  5. 08:12 Mindfulness and letting go - He links mindfulness to setting aside greed and distress toward the world, and to turning inward without trying to fix the outside.
  6. 11:56 Offering the heart up - The meditation deepens into repeated reminders to meet the moment with trust and openness, without needing a particular outcome.
  7. 17:00 Widening attention - He invites a broader aperture of attention and explores how awareness can soften the heaviness objects and thoughts take on.
  8. 24:03 Lightness and space - The teaching returns to spaciousness, including a lighter relationship to autobiography and to attention moving without snagging.
  9. 27:50 Keep opening - The close offers a simple, repeated instruction to keep winding or opening, ending the practice gently.
