Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Going Home

Dharma Seed 1:32:42 2016-01-01

How do you leave a retreat without trying to hold onto it? The talk starts with reentry: afterglow, aftershock, a strange return to ordinary life, and the reminder that what has been touched here is not confined to the retreat setting. From there it moves into the practical question of staying connected—through practice, community, and patience—with a later turn to resources for continuing, the blessing cord, the three refuges, and a closing recitation of the precepts as something to carry back into daily life.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Returning Home - Matthew begins by framing retreat as training for ordinary life and naming what is carried back with us.
  2. 03:58 Afterglow and Aftershock - He normalizes the unsettled feelings after retreat and speaks about the need to let go of the form.
  3. 07:17 Staying Connected - He invites people to stay connected to practice and to one another, especially in the days after leaving.
  4. 11:34 Resources for Continuing - Matthew gives a practical overview of future retreats, classes, centers, and other ways to keep practicing.
  5. 17:32 Volunteer Gratitude - The talk pauses for extended acknowledgment and appreciation of the staff and volunteers supporting the retreat.
  6. 41:09 The Blessing Cord - A call-and-response precepts chant leads into the meaning of the cord as a walking reminder of the retreat.
  7. 50:28 Three Refuges - He ties the cord into the Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha and chants the refuge formula with the group.
  8. 54:29 Carrying It Forward - Matthew reflects on impermanence, the cord, and how the practice continues beyond the retreat setting.
  9. 58:06 Final Goodbyes - The final section opens space for closing words, then ends with the group parting and sending everyone home.
