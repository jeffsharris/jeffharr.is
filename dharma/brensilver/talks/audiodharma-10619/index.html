Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

How Goodness Accrues on the Path

AudioDharma 44:51 2019-11-03

Matthew Brensilver explores how goodness accumulates on the path by way of practice, teaching, and repeated ethical attention. He moves from the Buddha’s instruction to investigate suffering toward a quieter argument that real change is often gradual, idiosyncratic, and less centered on self-improvement than we expect. Along the way he brings in Suzuki Roshi, Rumi, Ajahn Sumedho, Jack Kornfield, and research on happiness to show how Dharma practice can steady the heart, soften self-concern, and deepen moral clarity.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Why Dharma Talks Matter - Brensilver frames the purpose of a Dharma talk as a way to help people reorient when suffering disorients and obscures memory.
  2. 01:35 Investigate, Don't Collapse - He emphasizes the Buddha’s step-by-step instruction: name suffering clearly, then investigate rather than blame, despair, or withdraw.
  3. 05:14 Transmission and Unsettling - The talk turns to how teachings can transmit a state of mind, sometimes soothing us and sometimes unsettling us in service of growth.
  4. 09:38 What Freedom Looks Like - Brensilver asks how goodness accrues, suggesting that awakening is more idiosyncratic and less tidily mapped than many charts imply.
  5. 14:06 Gradual Change - He contrasts dramatic before-and-after stories with the quieter accumulation of practice, using Suzuki Roshi’s image of getting wet in mist.
  6. 18:12 Wanting and Not-Searching - The talk shifts to the relief of having a path, the dropping away of self-doubt, and the growing honesty about what we actually want.
  7. 23:44 Desire, Goals, and Intuition - Brensilver distinguishes clinging from desire, reflects on failed possessions and affective forecasting, and describes the growing reliability of practice-based intuition.
  8. 30:17 Transparency and the Past - He speaks about becoming less defensive and more transparent, and about making peace with the past as it lives in the present.
  9. 36:02 Ethical Alignment - The final movement considers self-concern, ethical refinement, and the courage to stay with what we owe others until life feels complete.
