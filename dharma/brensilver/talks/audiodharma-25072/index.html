Guided Meditation: safe to relax

AudioDharma 26:22 2026-03-18

How much effort does this moment actually call for, and can that effort come from ease rather than strain? The talk repeatedly returns to the simple instruction to breathe in a way that tells the deep mind it is okay to relax, even amid fragility, imperfection, and the tenuousness of things. A brief Rumi quotation opens into a Buddhist reflection on stepping out of self-view, loosening identification, and letting go of the need to hold everything as "I" and "mine."

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Calibrating Effort - He begins by asking listeners to sense how much effort the moment calls for, without turning that into a fixed right or wrong answer.
  2. 01:53 Ease in Practice - The instruction deepens into offering effort from a place of ease rather than from self-view, with repeated reminders to breathe.
  3. 04:34 Safety in the Breath - Breath becomes a way of cueing the deep mind that it is okay to soften even while imperfection, fragility, and uncertainty remain present.
  4. 08:58 Rumi and Rest - A brief quote from Rumi frames true rest as release from entanglement, which Matthew then connects to Buddhist language about stepping out of identification.
  5. 13:19 Making No Objects - He points to how aversion and delusion turn life into objects, and to the measurelessness that can appear in letting go.
  6. 17:30 Trusting Release - The closing instruction returns to not settling on the vantage point created by identification, ending with trust in letting go.
