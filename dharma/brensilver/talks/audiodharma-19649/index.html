Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation - Landing in the Breath

AudioDharma 55:31 2024-01-22

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation that moves from settling fear and bracing into a close, steady contact with breathing. He emphasizes that discomfort, thought, and uncertainty can be met as present experience rather than warnings about the future, and gradually opens into walking meditation and the continuity of mindfulness across posture changes. The talk closes by normalizing the ordinary difficulties of retreat and framing surrender, simplicity, and patience as part of the practice itself.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling the Heart - The meditation begins by noticing bracing or fear and inviting the body and heart to soften with kindness.
  2. 02:23 Thoughts as Predictions - He reframes discomfort and worry as predictions about the future, then returns attention to present-moment experience.
  3. 06:21 Body Aliveness - Attention widens to the changing sensations of the body, including the subtle blur of experience with eyes closed.
  4. 12:38 Finding the Breath - He gathers awareness around the sensations of breathing and encourages a precise, steady contact with inhale and exhale.
  5. 19:38 Returning Without Judgment - The practice deepens into letting the rest of experience arise in the background while gently returning from narrative to presence.
  6. 26:47 Retreat Mind States - He names common energies of sleepiness, restlessness, desire, aversion, and doubt, and describes retreat as a place where all moments can be held in awareness.
  7. 40:54 The Obstacle Is Path - Using a teaching attributed to the Buddha, he normalizes the full range of mind states and says what looks like interference is part of the path.
  8. 47:23 Walking and Momentum - He turns to walking meditation, explaining how to meet movement, boredom, and visual impressions with continuity, and how practice builds quietly over time.
  9. 54:37 Surrender and Simplicity - The talk ends with a plain invitation to surrender to the retreat’s logic and trust the long practice tradition behind it.
