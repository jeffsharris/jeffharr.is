Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Breathing into Compassion

AudioDharma 29:12 2025-01-01

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation that begins with James Baldwin’s words on pain, connection, and the possibility of release through shared suffering. The practice then moves into sensing the body as a whole, settling attention, and using the breath to soften identification and open compassion outward. Near the end, he widens the frame to all beings and closes with a sense of how deeply lives intertwine.

Chapters

  1. 00:25 Baldwin and pain - Matthew introduces James Baldwin’s reflection on suffering and frames personal pain as a way to connect with others.
  2. 03:38 Sense the body - The meditation shifts into directly knowing experience as sensation and settling into the body as the main field of practice.
  3. 06:46 Breath across the body - Attention follows the breath to see where it reaches, then opens to the whole body without strain.
  4. 09:53 Simple resting - He invites a relaxed, receptive posture of attention, with imagery and the inner gaze resting quietly as sensation unifies.
  5. 13:38 Pain as connection - The practice returns to suffering and reframes it as something that can connect us to others and depersonalize samsara.
  6. 16:12 Compassion opens wider - Compassion is encouraged to become broad and practical, without getting stuck in details or the sense that suffering is too vast.
  7. 22:50 Breath as love - The meditation deepens into a sense that breathing can convey love through the body and honor the shared condition of sentience.
  8. 26:58 Shared life and closing - He ends by pointing to how intertwined lives are and closes with a brief recitation and the sound of the bell.
