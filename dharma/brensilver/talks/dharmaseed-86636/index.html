Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Attention and Anicca, Awareness and Anatta (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 44:58 2024-10-16

Matthew Brensilver explores the difference between attention and awareness through a retreat talk that moves from the felt practice of listening to a more philosophical account of consciousness and selfhood. He uses Simone Weil, Thomas Nagel, Ajahn Sumedho, William James, and others to show how attention can become prayerful, how it can also tighten into effort and self-making, and how practice can soften into receptive knowing. The talk closes by circling back to anatta: the loosening of the sense of a fixed center, and the possibility that love, more than control, is what remains compatible with emptiness.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening into listening - Matthew begins with a light personal story and uses it to open the question of what it means to truly listen to others and to oneself.
  2. 06:07 Attention as prayer - He brings in Simone Weil to argue that attention, not willpower, is the way we work with our faults and meet life more honestly.
  3. 09:37 Conscious experience - Nagel and Ajahn Sumedho help him frame consciousness as 'what it is like' while pointing toward the Buddha's focus on suffering, peace, and practice.
  4. 12:23 Below awareness - He argues that much of training happens beneath surface consciousness and then distinguishes attention from awareness through retreat experience and lineage comparison.
  5. 20:00 How attention works - Drawing on William James and current attention research, he describes alerting, orienting, and executive attention, and how mindfulness can feel like soft readiness.
  6. 25:55 Aiming and sustaining - Ajahn Sucitto's images of the finger pointing and hand opening help him explain vitakka and vicara as the mechanics of concentrating attention.
  7. 29:02 Impermanence and selfing - He turns attention toward anicca and shows how effortful practice can deepen concentration while also reinforcing the sense of a meditator who is managing experience.
  8. 35:17 Resting in awareness - A story about a tired child becomes a doorway into the claim that awareness is not something the self controls, and that surrender can bring relief.
  9. 39:52 Non-identification and love - The talk closes with anatta, love as non-identification, and a quiet philosophical description of awareness as open, unpartitioned, and not centered on a self.
