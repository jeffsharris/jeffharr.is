Dharmette: Humor and Playfulness in the Dharma

AudioDharma 14:21 2023-02-15

What happens when seriousness loosens its grip and the path opens into play? The talk begins with a comment about teachers and humor, then moves through a self-deprecating story about being called “Captain Buzzkill” to explore how ego makes everything feel tight, defensive, and overly solemn. From there, the speaker asks where we can still play — even with our foibles, clinging, and shame — and uses Winnicott’s ideas about play to show why humor can belong to healing and Dharma practice. The talk closes with an image of tears and deep laughter coexisting together, with a quiet sense that grief, earnestness, pleasure, and lightness can all be part of the same life.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Retreat update - A brief opening update about the cyclone, Ajahn Sucitto, and the retreat center sets the context before the teaching begins.
  2. 00:46 Humor and depth - Matthew introduces the theme by recounting a retreatant's comment that humor may signal real depth in a teacher.
  3. 01:40 Captain Buzzkill - He shares the story behind his nickname and uses it to show how seriousness and low excitement shaped his temperament.
  4. 02:49 Seriousness and ego - The talk turns toward the idea that life's poignancy and humor are not incompatible, and that ego often makes practice feel heavier than it needs to be.
  5. 04:01 What play looks like - Matthew names the qualities of play—non-vigilance, less self-consciousness, ease, and intrinsic reward—and connects them to Dharma practice.
  6. 06:34 Winnicott and therapy - He quotes Winnicott on play in psychotherapy and extends the insight to practice, asking where we can and cannot play with our own foibles and shame.
  7. 08:03 Self-humor as relief - The talk suggests that as self-clinging softens, what once felt painful can become humorous, and even self-mockery can arise from love rather than criticism.
  8. 09:36 Relating to defilements - Matthew briefly invokes a Buddha saying about not deprecating yourself, then describes humor as a way to meet our defilements without harshness.
  9. 11:03 Tears and laughter - In a final story from a breathing session, he describes grief, humor, laughter, and peace arising together, closing with a wish that these qualities coexist in our lives.
