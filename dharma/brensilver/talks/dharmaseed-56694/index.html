Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: How the world impacts the emotional body

Dharma Seed 46:40 2019-06-04

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation that begins with a brief reflection on how the body carries emotional response, then moves into a long period of practice centered on breath, hands, and the felt sense of emotion. He emphasizes that sound, memory, pain, irritation, grief, and restlessness all register in the emotional body, and that meeting those sensations with steady awareness can soften the heart and make mindfulness a refuge. The talk closes by widening the teaching into daily life, including urgency, self-judgment, and the idea that practice unfolds in many chapters rather than one fixed method.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening reflection - Matthew introduces the talk as both reflection and guided practice, then begins distinguishing ordinary bodily sensation from emotional responsivity.
  2. 02:46 Clinging and the body - He frames clinging as central to suffering and explains why emotional sensations in the body are so important to notice.
  3. 06:48 Making peace with feeling - The teaching turns toward welcoming grief, memory, pleasure, and aversion without collapsing into thought or resistance.
  4. 11:38 Mindfulness as refuge - He names mindfulness as a refuge and prepares the transition from reflection into formal guided practice.
  5. 13:48 Breath and hands - The practice begins with breath awareness and a simple rhythm of inhale, exhale, hands to keep attention moving and grounded.
  6. 18:29 Emotional circuits - Attention turns to sensations that respond to sound, memory, and thinking, with repeated invitations to notice what is arising.
  7. 24:06 Permission and contact - He emphasizes allowing sensations to arise, intensify, fade, and vanish, and notes that deeper contact with the emotional body can lessen loneliness.
  8. 29:08 Extended stillness - A long stretch of quiet practice follows, with repeated prompts to stay aware of bodily feeling, restlessness, and the urge to fix things.
  9. 40:18 Grief and daily life - The talk closes by connecting patience, grief, and letting go, then offering the emotional body as a reference point for the rest of the day.
