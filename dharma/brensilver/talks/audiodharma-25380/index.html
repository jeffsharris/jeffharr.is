Guided Meditation: Difficult for the Self to Understand what it can't do

AudioDharma 27:00 2026-04-29

The talk centers on the uneasy gap between what we can actually do in meditation and what has to unfold on its own. It moves from simple instructions to find the body, breath, and a relaxed way of pointing attention, then keeps returning to the distinction between attention as an activity and awareness as something we do not manufacture. Along the way, the speaker asks listeners to notice the pull of hope and fear, sort out what is and is not their job, and see how the self can mistake its own effort for the whole Dharma path.
