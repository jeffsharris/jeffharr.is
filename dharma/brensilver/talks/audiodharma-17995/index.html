Guided Meditation_Not reading the tea leaves of the body

AudioDharma 37:13 2023-03-18

The talk asks what happens if we stop trying to read a hidden message in the body and instead meet each sensation as an invitation to let go. It moves from the ways the mind builds a life story out of hope, fear, and prediction into a simpler practice of receiving bodily experience without turning it into a problem to solve. A brief reference to the Mula Sutta frames liberation as the core of Dharma, and the meditation returns again and again to the same quiet instruction: let go.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening into many worlds - The talk begins by describing the shifting inner worlds we inhabit through hope, fear, and self-centered story.
  2. 04:41 Receiving the body - The practice turns toward simply receiving the data of the body rather than trying to solve life’s problems.
  3. 11:53 Autobiography and prediction - The speaker explores how each moment becomes another chapter in the mind’s autobiography and a forecast of the future.
  4. 17:02 Safety and false alarms - Within the safety of the setting, alarm signals from body and mind are treated for now as false alarms.
  5. 22:06 Reading tea leaves - The meditation names the habit of interpreting aches, fatigue, pleasure, and pain as messages about what will happen next.
  6. 28:53 Liberation as the core - A brief reference to the Mula Sutta supports the teaching that the core of phenomena is liberation.
  7. 30:00 Doorways of perception - The practice broadens into seeing walls, doors, and ordinary experience itself as doorway to Dharma.
  8. 33:43 Renouncing words - The talk closes by renouncing the urge to build the world out of words and resting in intimate, repeated letting go.
