Dharmette: Five Spiritual Faculties - Faith (1 of 5)

AudioDharma 13:04 2023-01-23

Faith begins, here, not as a belief you force yourself to hold, but as a response to seeing that the old way of trying to manage life is not going to work. The talk moves from doubt and suffering into a more grounded confidence: trust in practice, trust in experience, and trust that the heart can return to its own ground. Along the way, the speaker uses images of being lost in a jungle, falling in love, and taking refuge to show faith as something alive, renewing, and less rigid than doctrine.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and Theme - Matthew introduces the series on the five spiritual faculties and names faith as the first topic.
  2. 00:45 Faith Begins in Doubt - He says practice often begins with the recognition that the old way of living will not work.
  3. 01:25 Suffering as Support - Matthew argues that suffering can become the condition that gives rise to faith and sincere practice.
  4. 02:40 Not Dogmatic Belief - Using the Buddha and Gil Fronsdal, he contrasts faith with fundamentalism and rigid views.
  5. 04:05 Trusting Experience - He cites Ajahn Amaro to describe faith as trusting the implications of experience rather than filling uncertainty with certainties.
  6. 04:50 Faith Like Falling In Love - Matthew compares commitment to the path with falling in love, something opaque, renewing, and not reducible to reasons.
  7. 06:32 Its Own Evidence - With Emerson and the language of refuge, he describes faith as self-authenticating and continually renewed.
  8. 07:38 Stable Yet Living - He reflects on how faith matures into a steadier threshold, bringing security, relaxation, and less self-absorption.
  9. 09:17 Ease and Consolation - The talk closes by describing faith as a quiet confidence that does not erase suffering, but offers solace and helps us keep going.
