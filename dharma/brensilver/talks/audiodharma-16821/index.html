Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Getting Somewhere by Going Nowhere

AudioDharma 29:09 2022-08-18

Wanting something is one thing; getting it is another, and the talk begins by turning that familiar tension into a meditation question. It moves through the idea that practice is both goal-oriented and non-striving: we are headed toward more freedom, but we get there by relaxing the goal-making mind, softening grasping, and settling into one moment at a time. The guided portion returns again and again to awareness in the body, then opens into the sense that every sensation and thought can be received as an invitation to liberation rather than something to control.

Chapters

  1. 00:27 Goals and Dukkha - Matthew begins by examining the problem of goals: they give meaning, but they also carry unsatisfactoriness because wanting and getting are never the same.
  2. 01:56 Telic and Atelic - He introduces the distinction between goal-directed and non-goal-directed activity and uses it to frame meditation as both aspirational and non-striving.
  3. 03:01 One Moment at a Time - The talk turns toward practice as a steady cultivation of qualities like samadhi, done moment by moment without grasping or engineering.
  4. 05:29 Aspiration Without Urgency - Matthew names the deepest aims of practice as wisdom, compassion, understanding, and love, and encourages reverence for those qualities without pushing to get anywhere.
  5. 08:38 Settling Into the Body - The guided meditation opens into body awareness, inviting a full, intimate attention to sensation and a softening into the present field.
  6. 15:33 Liberation in Phenomena - He brings in the Buddha's teaching that all phenomena have liberation as their core, contrasting this with how grasping makes experience feel solid.
  7. 17:58 Relaxing the Struggle - The practice continues by releasing clinging, tension, and the urge to get organized, shifting toward a simpler willingness to be aware.
  8. 23:29 Be Blissful - The closing stretches into repeated encouragement to rest in bliss and let awareness come before the struggle to manage life.
