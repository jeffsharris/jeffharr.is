Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Moved by goodness: Reflections and Guided Metta Meditation (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 35:57 2023-03-29

The talk starts with mindfulness as a kind of deconstruction: taking apart the tangle of selfing, fear, and clinging to see what is actually happening. From there it turns toward reconstruction through love, especially metta as a free, measureless love that is not bound up with control and that can be informed by grief without becoming naïve. The session then moves into a guided loving-kindness practice, using simple phrases and an invitation to sit as if one could do no wrong, with attention to the quiet reverberations of the words in the body.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Deconstruction and love - Brensilver opens by describing mindfulness as taking experience apart, then turns toward the complementary work of reconstructing the heart through love.
  2. 02:17 What love means here - He defines this afternoon’s theme as love, emphasizing nonviolence, the dead end of hatred, and a free love that does not measure self or other.
  3. 05:28 The four divine abodes - He names the brahma-viharas and distinguishes metta, compassion, joy, and equanimity as different faces of love in relation to goodness, suffering, happiness, and the vastness of samsara.
  4. 09:43 Moved by goodness - He suggests metta may begin when we are touched by our own goodness and by the goodness in others, which makes hatred less tenable.
  5. 12:35 Cultivation and purification - Using a story from Michelle McDonald, he describes metta as something that can emerge after long practice, even quietly, and as a practice that brings hidden pain to the surface for healing.
  6. 17:12 Settling into practice - The guided meditation begins with sitting in a way that grants complete permission to experience what is present, without self-accusation or inner combat.
  7. 25:15 Self-blessing phrases - He invites listeners to imagine how a deeply compassionate being would see them, then offers gentle phrases such as 'May I be happy,' 'May I be safe and protected,' and 'May I be at peace.'
  8. 31:40 Expansiveness of love - The closing returns to the felt contrast between contracted suffering and the relief of love, ending with a sense that what we call self and other can dissolve into the field of metta.
