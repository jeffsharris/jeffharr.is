Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Entering the Dharma Field

AudioDharma 29:27 2023-04-24

The meditation begins by settling into posture and breath, not as a narrow technique but as a way of remembering the whole weight and beauty of practice. The speaker moves from simple attention to the breath as refuge, a place to fold in thoughts, fear, hope, anticipation, and memory until they are softened and held. In the final stretch, the practice widens into the image of entering a Dharma field: a realm where even mistakes can become reminders of Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha.

Chapters

  1. 00:42 Arriving and settling - The meditation opens with posture, welcome, and a reminder that simply sitting can evoke the whole path.
  2. 04:52 Breath as reminder - Breathing is introduced as a way to remember the Dharma and return to what is already here.
  3. 07:26 Opening and refuge - The talk distinguishes opening to life’s intensity from taking refuge in simple attention to the breath.
  4. 09:20 Breath as shelter - Breath is presented as a small but complete world, a temporary shelter for the heart and a place to set down the riddle of life.
  5. 14:21 Folding thoughts back - When the mind wanders into stories of life, memory, and anticipation, those movements are folded back into breathing.
  6. 18:51 Hope and fear - The breath is described as something that can absorb anxiety, excitement, and the body blows of hope and fear.
  7. 21:51 Invoking the Buddha - A prolonged repetition of the Buddha’s name deepens the devotional and recollective quality of the practice.
  8. 25:06 Entering Dharma field - Matthew cites Ajahn Sucitto’s phrase about entering Dharma fields and expands it into a view where everything, including mistakes, can remind us of Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha.
