Dialogue on What's Beyond Judging, Comparing, Fixing

AudioDharma 43:58 2025-06-05

What happens when judging, comparing, and fixing start to loosen their grip? The talk moves from retreat reflections and quotes from Ajahn Sumedho, Ajahn Chah, Philip Moffitt, and Dogen into a back-and-forth exploration of how practice can mature without turning into self-perfection. It keeps circling the question of psychological healing and awakening, asking how much the personality needs to be integrated, what freedom actually looks like, and why trust and non-grasping seem to matter more than control.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Judging, Comparing, Fixing - The talk opens by naming the three habits the retreat has been exploring and asking what lies beyond them.
  2. 00:53 Ajahn Sumedho's Doorway - Quotes from Ajahn Sumedho and Ajahn Chah frame awakening as natural joy, non-grasping, and something beyond language and perception.
  3. 03:10 Not Perfecting the Self - The speakers soften perfectionism by saying the personality does not get enlightened and by emphasizing ease, humor, and a lighter relationship to conditioned mind.
  4. 07:47 Sincere Practice - The conversation shifts toward discernment, renunciation, inner dignity, and the movement from trying to be an ideal practitioner to being a sincere one.
  5. 11:44 Radical Inner Shift - A Philip Moffitt passage is used to describe how the Four Noble Truths can reframe suffering without denying pain, disappointment, or uncertainty.
  6. 14:11 Trust Beyond Thought - Dogen's teaching on the inconceivable realm of the Buddhas supports an invitation to trust what cannot be reached by intellect alone.
  7. 18:31 Psychology and Awakening - Matthew and Dana explore how psychological integration and spiritual practice can complement each other, and why awakening must not be narrowed into a single map.
  8. 26:11 Safety, Depth, and Breadth - They discuss the need for inner safety, the dangers of unintegrated insight, and the way freedom can remain distinct from harm even after deep realization.
  9. 34:06 Ordinary Freedom - The talk closes with reflections on self-love, forgiveness, the persistence of grooves and suffering, and a freedom that feels quiet, ordinary, and close to love.
