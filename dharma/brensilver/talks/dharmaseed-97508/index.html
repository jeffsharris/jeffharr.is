Compassion and Equanimity

Dharma Seed 35:34 2026-06-04

How do compassion and equanimity answer the different faces of suffering? The talk begins with a simple metta practice and then widens into a careful distinction: first letting the heart receive tenderness, then meeting another’s pain without becoming overwhelmed, and finally resting in the quiet strength of equanimity when nothing can be controlled. It closes by showing how these two forms of love keep one another honest—compassion preventing coldness, equanimity preventing burnout or grandiosity.

Chapters

  1. 00:13 Opening the Heart - The talk begins with a simple practice of choosing someone easy to care about and letting metta arise without strain.
  2. 01:31 Receiving Tenderness - The focus shifts to receiving care anonymously and allowing the mind to be infused with tenderness.
  3. 04:02 Metta for All Beings - The practice expands from one person to all beings, emphasizing ease, safety, peace, and a calm heart.
  4. 09:25 Turning Toward Suffering - A new contemplation brings to mind someone who is suffering, and the instruction is to get close enough to feel the weight without becoming drowned in it.
  5. 14:27 Compassion as Release - The movement shifts from feeling suffering to pouring love into it, wishing that burden to be lifted and the pain reduced.
  6. 20:38 Love and Helplessness - The talk reflects on the limits of care: our love is larger than our power, and the heart must face helplessness without collapsing into frenzy or despair.
  7. 25:00 The Quiet Love of Equanimity - Equanimity is described as a deep, wide, long view that stops trying to control what cannot be governed while keeping love intact.
  8. 30:23 Balancing the Two - The talk closes by joining compassion and equanimity as mutually correcting forms of love that prepare us for suffering, change, and even dying well.
