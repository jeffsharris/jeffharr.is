Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

The Parami of Truthfulness

Dharma Seed 47:33 2020-10-05

How does truthfulness become freeing if we can’t claim a final, capital-T truth? The talk starts with the promise of “the truth will set you free” and then moves into a more modest but practical claim: that honesty, fidelity, and a willingness to look closely are enough to loosen suffering. From there it turns to the ways the mind rationalizes, hides, and self-justifies, using references to ignorance, the three characteristics, and research on bias and secrecy to show why practice has to move against our default habits. The talk ends by returning to truthful speech and inner transparency as forms of alignment with reality, even while allowing for the possibility that we may still be wrong.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the question - Matthew introduces the theme of truthfulness and frames it as a practical question about how truth liberates.
  2. 01:32 Truth and humility - He reflects on John 8:31, the Buddhist link between ignorance and suffering, and why final truth is a difficult idea to hold.
  3. 06:12 Fidelity to reality - The talk shifts toward small-t truth, fidelity, and the commitment to look deeply without rationalizing one’s life.
  4. 09:36 Courage to see - He describes how practice requires courage, curiosity, and an honesty practice that faces the present moment as it is.
  5. 15:57 Against the current - Drawing on evolution and the three characteristics, he argues that Dharma practice moves against our default tendency toward delusion, identity, and control.
  6. 25:25 Refining the view - He balances trust in experience with refining view, then examines how we build philosophies from preferences and conditioning.
  7. 31:18 Secrets and shame - The talk turns to secrecy, lying, and shame, showing how concealment sustains a rigid self and keeps us out of touch with our own values.
  8. 38:19 Truthful speech - Matthew connects honesty with interpersonal trust and cites Bhikkhu Bodhi to show truthful speech as alignment with actuality.
  9. 42:18 Humility and closing - He ends by emphasizing that we may never be done with delusion, but that honesty makes us more careful, more transparent, and less harmful.
