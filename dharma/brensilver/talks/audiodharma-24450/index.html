Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Lightly Guided Meditation

AudioDharma 25:39 2025-12-17

The talk begins by asking what happens when control is set down and the body is allowed to relax. From there, it moves into a quiet instruction to stop rehearsing identity, to let pleasant be pleasant and unpleasant be unpleasant without adding more story. The middle of the meditation leans on equanimity as the condition for non-proliferation: unpleasantness can be felt as just the end of a sentence, with nothing more to say or do. It closes with a tender image of the moment as something that meets pain only where healing is needed, inviting the whole body-mind to soften and trust that whatever is discovered can deepen the case for love.

  1. 00:12 Settling the body - The meditation begins by inviting relaxation in the body and a loosening of control.

  6. 11:03 Softening into love - A closing image of the moment as a skilled healer frames discomfort as something that can support tenderness and courage.
  7. 12:39 Closing bell meditation - The remainder is quiet closing time marked by bells and space for the practice to settle.
