Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Grounding and Opening

AudioDharma 30:00 2025-01-15

The talk begins with simple instructions to settle in, then keeps returning to the body as the place where attention can land: posture, belly, bones, gravity, and the feeling of “taking your seat.” A repeated image of the Buddha touching the earth becomes the central grounding move, shifting practice away from the head and heart and back into embodied presence. From there, the guidance widens to include emotional activation, letting it be bathed in awareness and patience rather than measured against a plan for how life or meditation should unfold. Near the end, the emphasis softens into a release of performance, preference, and the need to get it right.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Beginning Together - He invites the group to practice and settle into the meditation.
  2. 00:30 Posture and Belly - The practice begins with finding posture and bringing attention down into the belly, gravity, and the bones.
  3. 02:25 Touching the Earth - He uses the Buddha’s earth-touching gesture as a repeated image for grounding, asking listeners to know themselves as earth and breathe down into the ground.
  4. 04:53 Settling and Listening - After the initial grounding, the practice continues in a quieter register of resting, listening, and staying with bodily presence.
  5. 08:53 Affect in the Body - He turns toward emotional activation as bodily circuits that can be met with awareness and patience.
  6. 11:22 Dropping Expectations - The teaching shifts toward releasing hidden models of how life or meditation should unfold and using everything to become more alive.
  7. 12:57 Open Practice - The meditation continues in a broad, steady way, supporting simple presence without needing to control the experience.
  8. 19:04 No Performance - He closes by setting aside pleasure, preferences, and the pressure to perform.
