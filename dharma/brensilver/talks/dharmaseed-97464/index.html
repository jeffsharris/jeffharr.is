Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Breath and Sound

Dharma Seed 37:34 2026-06-03

What happens if meditation is less about forcing calm and more about making room for what is already here? The talk guides a gradual settling into posture, then into breath, and then into sound and silence, repeatedly returning to the idea that practice is simple, humble, and can include imperfection, hope, and fear without fighting them. A brief reference to Sharon Salzberg sharpens the contrast between trying to make practice work and simply doing the practice.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling the posture - The practice begins with small adjustments to find a posture that balances relaxation and alertness.
  2. 02:19 Good enough to begin - The talk invites commitment to an imperfect posture and a willingness to rest in the goodness already present.
  3. 06:01 Arriving with mindfulness - Attention widens to the edge of thinking and to whatever needs to feel safe before settling in.
  4. 09:13 A bigger room - Practice is framed as opening into spaciousness rather than squeezing the mind into a narrow slot.
  5. 12:48 Breath as enjoyment - Breathing becomes more vivid, with emphasis on the felt pleasure of inhalation and exhalation.
  6. 18:46 Simply doing practice - A brief reference to Sharon Salzberg underscores the shift from trying to make meditation work to just practicing.
  7. 23:48 Breathing with hope and fear - Hope and fear are not pushed away but met through the breath, so the practice stays open to difficulty.
  8. 28:51 Sound, silence, and trust - The anchor shifts to sound and silence, including the ends of sounds, faint sounds, and silence as a kind of hearing, before closing in quiet trust.
