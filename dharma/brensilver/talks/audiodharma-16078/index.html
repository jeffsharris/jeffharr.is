Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Grief and Love

AudioDharma 15:11 2022-04-13

Matthew Brensilver reflects on grief as a natural part of loving in a changing world, beginning with his father’s death and widening to history, ecology, and ordinary loss. He then turns toward spiritual practice, arguing that maturation, ethical growth, and deepening compassion often carry a grief-like feeling as we loosen grasping and expand our care. The talk closes with a quiet case for making room for grief rather than treating it as a problem to be eliminated.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Loss Reveals the World - Matthew opens from personal loss and uses Katherine Schultz’s language to show how grief can make the hidden order of loss feel visible everywhere.
  2. 02:56 Why Grief Belongs - He argues that grief is woven into life because love and care necessarily meet change, impermanence, and death.
  3. 04:21 Growing Up Spiritually - The talk turns to spiritual maturation as a kind of growing up, with ethical development bringing its own pains and clarifications.
  4. 05:48 Learning Better - Using Maya Angelou’s line about doing better once we know better, he connects grief to aligning more deeply with values and widening moral concern.
  5. 07:11 Love Meets Suffering - Matthew describes grief and compassion as byproducts of love meeting suffering, and links the felt work of letting go with the experience of loss and gain.
  6. 08:34 Permission to Grieve - He speaks gently about grieving without turning it into a project, describing it as part of the path and something many people feel relieved to be allowed to do.
  7. 10:24 Beyond Yet Not Without - Matthew cites the traditional phrase about going beyond grief, then complicates it by saying he wants an awakened being who can still grieve.
  8. 12:13 A Deeper, Truer Love - In the close, he says grief can redeem and deepen love, makes room for the ego’s limits in sorrow, and ends with a poem about entering the darkness of grief together.
