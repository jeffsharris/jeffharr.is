Practice Note: Vision

AudioDharma 03:43 2015-12-09

How often do we treat the visual field as if it were just the solid world, rather than an experience that arises and passes? This short practice note points to seeing itself as a mindfulness bell: the breath, body, and emotions may already be familiar reminders, but vision can quietly pull the mind into association and proliferation. The invitation is simple and practical—while walking or going about the morning, notice the visual field as seeing, then let it fall away and begin again.

  1. 00:10 Dharma reminders - He begins by naming the many ways practice can remind us of the Dharma, including the breath, suffering, body sensations, and emotions.
  2. 00:50 The overlooked sense - He points to vision as an experience that often slips beneath mindfulness and can become a doorway into mental proliferation.
  3. 01:14 Seeing as practice - He notes that some traditions use seeing as a central anchor and invites sensitivity to the fact that seeing is itself an experience.
  4. 01:36 Associations in the visual field - He describes how we lose track of the visual field and how sights quickly gather associations that shape thought.
  5. 02:30 Walking with open eyes - He connects this challenge to walking meditation, where open eyes make visual experience especially evocative.
  6. 03:04 Seeing arise and pass - He ends by encouraging moments of simply knowing the visual field as seeing that arises and passes away.
