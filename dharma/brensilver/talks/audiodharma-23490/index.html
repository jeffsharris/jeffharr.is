Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: If you're looking, you're lost

AudioDharma 28:24 2025-07-30

The talk begins by turning meditation away from effortful searching: rather than looking for a better state, the instruction is to find what genuinely inspires longing for the Dharma and then notice what serves in this moment. From there, the emphasis shifts to a very direct practice of inclusion—letting pleasure and pain be met more equally, and seeing how willfulness and doingness can quietly block the path. The closing movement is toward less and less strain: attention no longer needs to be propped up so aggressively, and awareness is described as continuing even when that effort is dropped.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Settling In - The meditation opens by welcoming the group and inviting a shared settling of body and mind.
  2. 01:24 Finding Inspiration - He suggests beginning with something genuinely inspiring that evokes longing for the Dharma.
  3. 02:14 Longing, Not Craving - The talk distinguishes dharma longing from the craving to get somewhere, then turns toward where attention is naturally drawn.
  4. 04:29 What Serves - Listeners are asked to notice the aperture of attention and choose an anchor in a way that fits the mind's present conditions.
  5. 07:20 Don't Go Looking - The instruction becomes more direct: don't look outside or even inside for something to find, because looking itself can obscure what is already present.
  6. 14:07 Radical Inclusion - Brensilver invites relinquishing the preference for pleasure over pain and widening the range of what can be held in practice.
  7. 17:17 Letting Go of Willfulness - He addresses the fear that goodness will be lost without effort, and gently questions the sense that more doing is still needed.
  8. 23:58 Awareness Needs No Job - The meditation closes by pointing out that awareness does not need to be propped up, and that the letting-go of effort can feel both sad and relieving.
