Practice Note: Note your life away

AudioDharma 03:52 2019-03-20

How does a simple mental note change what we take ourselves to be? The talk moves from the practical use of noting—breath, walking, bodily movement—to a subtler claim: that whatever is noted is no longer quite the ground we stand on. By naming body, room, weight, emotion, and thought, the speaker shows how practice can turn experience into something seen as arising and vanishing, rather than something to identify with. In that sense, the talk says, we keep "noting our life away."

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening frame - The talk is introduced as a practice note from the Insight Meditation Center.
  2. 00:30 Noting as awareness - Brensilver explains noting as silently acknowledging experience and letting awareness soak into it.
  3. 01:14 More than stabilization - He says noting is not only a way to steady attention but also has a deeper function in the mind.
  4. 01:52 Stepping off identification - What is noted is described as no longer being exactly our life, so attention begins to loosen its grip.
  5. 02:26 Everything can be noted - He names body, room, gravity, emotion, heart, and thinking as all available to awareness.
  6. 03:11 Noting life away - The practice is summed up as taking no part for granted and seeing life as arising and vanishing experience.
  7. 03:42 Closing wish - The talk ends with a brief, lightly self-aware closing wish for the morning.
