Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Attention in the body

AudioDharma 26:57 2026-05-13

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation centered on feeling attention in the body as a way to settle, unify, and soften the mind. The practice moves from posture and breath into subtler body sensations, including the back of the body, while emphasizing relaxation without force and equal care for pleasant, unpleasant, and neutral experience. He also frames meditation as a way of soothing the bodily atmosphere that underlies anxiety, hope, and fear.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Settling In - The meditation begins with posture, gathering the mind, and arriving in the present moment.
  2. 01:01 Making Peace - He invites a sense of blessing and asks what peace needs to be made with this moment.
  3. 03:47 Body and Breath - Attention moves into the body and breath, including places that are easy to miss or feel numb.
  4. 05:47 Subtle Sensation - The practice deepens into finer bodily energy and awareness of the back of the body.
  5. 08:10 Relaxing Resistance - He encourages relaxing what can be relaxed while allowing remaining tension to be there.
  6. 10:40 Cohering the Field - Breath and attention are described as gathering experience into a more unified field, rather than fighting sensation.
  7. 14:59 Hope and Fear - The talk turns to how attention can snag on hope and fear, and how forgiveness can restore steadiness.
  8. 19:06 Soothing the Body - Meditation is presented as a way to soothe the bodily feeling that underlies our problems and to speak more gently to the body.
