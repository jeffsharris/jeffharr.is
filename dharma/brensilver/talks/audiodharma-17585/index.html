Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Morning Instruction: Emotions

AudioDharma 43:29 2023-01-14

Feeling is not treated here as a forecast, but as something to meet directly. The talk begins with sound and breath as a surrounding, soothing field, then turns to how emotions register risk, reward, and the question, “Am I okay?” Rather than extrapolating from intensity into the future, the instruction is to open to the knots, surges, longing, resistance, and dead zones as they arise, with awareness that can hold all of it without discrimination.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 RAIN and being held - He begins with RAIN and the feeling of being surrounded, held, and soothed by sound and breath.
  2. 02:22 Breath in the body - The instruction widens into the body, with rain, breath, and an inclusive awareness that can contain experience.
  3. 07:48 Emotion as signal - He explains emotion as part of how small creatures sense risk, reward, and the felt direction of life.
  4. 10:49 Not a forecast - Feeling is described as something we often interpret as a prediction, but practice asks us not to extrapolate into the future.
  5. 12:54 Opening to intensity - The teaching stays with urgency, knots, surges, longing, resistance, and the various textures of feeling without discrimination.
  6. 19:00 Am I okay? - He turns to the question "Am I okay?" and frames the Buddha's instruction as an experiment in how to answer it.
  7. 22:27 Soothed and sustained - The practice settles into repeated reminders of being soothed by sound, breath, love, and the lineage of practice while staying awake to shifting emotion.
  8. 31:45 An experiment only - He clarifies that this is not a rule for all of life, but a temporary experiment in treating intensities as the ground of practice.
