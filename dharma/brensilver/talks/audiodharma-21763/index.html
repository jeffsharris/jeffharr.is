Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Vitality and Release, In Breath, Out Breath

AudioDharma 29:02 2024-11-20

What if the breath itself is teaching both vitality and release? The talk begins with a simple contrast between in-breath and out-breath, then turns that into a practice of learning to love both alertness and surrender: the rush of energy, the brightness of presence, and the softening that lets go of vigilance, fear, and the impulse to grasp. It keeps returning to the sense that meditation is not a rehearsal for some other life, but the direct texture of this one—breath, thought, feeling, seeing, hearing—met moment by moment with clarity and release.

Chapters

  1. 00:14 Arriving and orienting - The meditation opens with a warm welcome and a check-in with the heart, then names the basic theme of learning the rhythms of practice.
  2. 01:49 Vitality and surrender - Brensilver introduces the idea that some practitioners lean toward the in-breath while others prefer the out-breath, framing maturity as learning to love both.
  3. 03:06 Settling the body - He invites a comfortable posture and a bright, steady presence as the body breathes in and out.
  4. 04:50 The in-breath's vividness - Attention turns to the rush, brightness, and poignancy of inhalation, including the way it can disrupt monotony and remind us not to miss our lives.
  5. 07:10 Releasing the out-breath - The out-breath is explored as a letting go of tension, vigilance, fear, and subtle ownership, with repeated encouragement to breathe out.
  6. 12:21 Breath and clarity - The talk deepens into the breath cycle as a moment-by-moment expression of clarity, release, brightness, and darkness.
  7. 17:31 Letting go of becoming - Brensilver links the out-breath with softening the impulse to become someone slightly better, and the in-breath with shaking off the idea that this moment is only rehearsal.
  8. 24:10 This is our life - The closing section widens the frame: meditation is not separate from life, because life is found in breath, thought, feeling, seeing, and hearing here and now.
