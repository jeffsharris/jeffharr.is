Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Metta for Self

Dharma Seed 33:14 2024-09-26

What if self-love is not a soft sentiment but a steady way of meeting suffering, goodness, joy, and helplessness? The talk moves from the four Brahma-viharas as four species of love into a practical account of loving-kindness: how it can cultivate kindness, narrow and settle the mind, or bring grief, rage, numbness, and fear to the surface. It then turns into a guided offering toward the self, using phrases like “may I be happy” and the image of a merciful gaze that sees innocence without fixing on flaws.

Chapters

  1. 00:34 Love and human life - He opens by saying he wants to talk about love, then frames love as what makes the human situation bearable.
  2. 01:51 Four kinds of love - He names the Brahma-viharas as four species of love and distinguishes metta, compassion, joy, and equanimity.
  3. 04:00 How metta works - He explains metta as cultivation and as a radical sensitization to goodness, especially when the practice becomes quiet and focused.
  4. 06:29 What metta reveals - He describes how loving-kindness can also pull grief, rage, numbness, or fear to the surface, and suggests meeting that material with kindness.
  5. 09:25 Finding your own path - He encourages each person to find a native, sustainable way into love, then begins turning toward direct self-directed practice.
  6. 12:28 A merciful gaze - He invites listeners to see themselves through kind eyes and imagine how a deeply loving person would look at them.
  7. 18:10 Blessing the self - He brings in the example of a teacher's compassionate gaze, then moves into repeated phrases of wishing safety, ease, and happiness for oneself.
  8. 24:52 Problems dissolving - He emphasizes that loving-kindness does not solve problems so much as soften them, and repeats that the burden of 'my life' can be put down.
  9. 29:14 What has not been loved - He returns to the parts of experience that have not yet been loved and closes with a note that love makes the mind vast and measureless.
