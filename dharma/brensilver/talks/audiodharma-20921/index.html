Guided Meditation: Too Busy Knowing

AudioDharma 28:47 2024-07-24

What changes when the mind stops patrolling experience and instead lets life be felt directly? This guided meditation starts with relaxing whatever can be relaxed, then asks for a deeper relinquishment of vigilance: assuming safety for a little while, surrendering to sensations, and using the breath, sound, or body as an anchor. The practice keeps returning to clarity and high-resolution attention, with impermanence as a cue to notice how experience snags and then lets go.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Settling and Relaxing - The practice begins by relaxing whatever can be relaxed and noticing that softening is not about forcing anything away.
  2. 04:10 Safety and Relinquishment - He invites a temporary assumption of safety so vigilance can ease and the mind can surrender to what is present.
  3. 06:56 Anchors and Clarity - Breath, sound, the hands, or bodily pleasantness can serve as anchors while attention remains clear rather than foggy.
  4. 09:23 Snagged by Experience - Attention catches on sensations and phenomena, like fabric snagging on a hangnail, and the practice notices that snagging without panic.
  5. 10:07 Moment by Moment Knowing - Impermanence is brought forward as the mind learns to know experience in motion instead of holding it still.
  6. 20:58 Too Busy Knowing - The talk ends on the idea that intimate knowing can be so continuous that it leaves little room for a fixed identity.
