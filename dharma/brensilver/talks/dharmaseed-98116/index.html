Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Opening Night (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 60:31 2026-06-27

How do you arrive in a retreat when part of you is still busy, guarded, or afraid? The talk begins with a few minutes of settling, then unfolds into an invitation to let the room, the body, and the mind be welcomed just as they are. From there it moves into retreat as a place to meet both pleasantness and discomfort, to trust the support of others, and to take the precepts as a way of creating non-fear.

Chapters

  1. 00:08 Arriving on the cushion - The talk opens with settling the body, feeling contact points, and noticing how hard it can be to simply arrive as oneself.
  2. 02:16 Welcoming what’s here - It turns toward allowing excitement, nervousness, and whatever the body and mind are carrying to be present without being pushed away.
  3. 09:46 A retreat memory - A brief personal story about earlier retreat connections and a visit back to old friends leads into the sense of shared practice history.
  4. 13:12 Koyasan and lineage - A memory of Koyasan, silence, and temple bells becomes a reflection on lineage, sacredness, and why retreat can feel so charged.
  5. 16:24 Feeling good, feeling bad - The talk reframes retreat as a rhythm of pleasant and unpleasant experience, where soothing and trust help us digest what has not yet been loved.
  6. 21:59 Being here together - The focus shifts to the room itself: presence, friendly contact, introductions, and making a temporary monastery together.
  7. 33:29 Refuge and community - The Triple Gem is introduced as trust in awareness, in the truth of experience, and in the support of sangha.
  8. 36:27 Fear and non-fear - The teaching deepens into fear as a root of suffering and mindfulness as a way to touch non-fear and loosen vigilance.
  9. 40:57 Precepts as care - The precepts are described as a practical ethics of non-harming, restraint, silence, and putting down the phone so the retreat can protect attention and tenderness.
