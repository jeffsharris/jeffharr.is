Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Letting the Winds of Feeling Blow

AudioDharma 29:05 2023-01-27

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet guided meditation centered on feeling as something to trust rather than manage or explain. He begins by inviting a careful “Dharma posture,” then gradually turns toward the body’s surges of emotion and impulse, framing practice as learning to let feeling move through without immediately turning it into story. Near the end, he links that surrender to confidence in Buddha, Dhamma, and Sangha, so the practice lands in faith rather than effortful control.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling In - Opening housekeeping gives way to a brief settling before the meditation begins.
  2. 00:54 Dharma Posture - He invites a careful inner alignment, comparing the posture of the heart to deliberately entering a yoga pose.
  3. 03:19 Moved by Goodness - The meditation is framed as becoming more sensitive to goodness and letting oneself be moved by it.
  4. 05:36 Wisdom and Love - He broadens the frame to the lineage of practice, emphasizing that meditation techniques should be seasoned with presence and love.
  5. 12:54 Feeling and Freedom - Feeling is named as closely tied to freedom, and also as the place where grasping and aversion get fueled.
  6. 14:28 Letting Feeling Pass - The central instruction appears: let the winds of feeling blow through without assuming the heart will be blown over.
  7. 17:13 Not Making Meaning - He explores the habit of turning feeling into story, and encourages experimentation with not over-interpreting the moment.
  8. 23:26 Surrender and Refuge - The talk turns toward surrendering not-knowing and resting in faith in Buddha, Dhamma, and Sangha.
