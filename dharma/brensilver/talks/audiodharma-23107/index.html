Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Love and Dissolving Boundaries

AudioDharma 15:26 2025-06-04

The talk begins by noticing how quickly life’s goodness becomes invisible, and how the mind shifts into constant fixing and optimization. From there, the practice turns toward appreciation for this body, this breath, and generosity, letting gratitude soften the tendency to see only problems. Renunciation is framed not as withdrawal, but as a kind of love: a quiet openness where urgency dissolves and nothing has to stick. The guided sequence then repeatedly invites softness, easing wherever the world seems to need you until only softness remains.

  1. 00:00 Appreciating good fortune - The talk opens by noticing how easily we overlook the many conditions that support our lives and bodies.

  5. 08:23 Quiet openheartedness - Love is described less as intensity and more as a steady, quiet openness that lets nothing stick.
  6. 11:42 Softening the world - The practice turns to softening wherever the world seems to need us, widening into a sustained instruction to soften.
  7. 14:52 Conditions well enough - The meditation closes by resting in the sense that conditions are already well enough, ending in a quiet thank you.
