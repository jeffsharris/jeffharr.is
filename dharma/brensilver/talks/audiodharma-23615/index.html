Dharmette: Fear, Love and Political Chaos

AudioDharma 14:27 2025-08-13

What does love ask of us when the political moment feels chaotic, fear is present, and there may be no clean response? The talk stays with that question rather than trying to solve it, moving through balance and burnout, wholesome and inefficient suffering, and the possibility that compassion becomes clearer when it is less entangled with control. It then turns toward helplessness, mortality, and anicca, asking what kind of love is possible when there may be nothing left to lose. Ajahn Chah’s image of the already broken cup appears as a quiet way to contemplate impermanence and the freedom it can bring.

Chapters

  1. 00:21 Living the Question - He opens by naming the political moment and framing love as an inquiry rather than a problem to solve.
  2. 01:37 Balance and Moral Fire - He explores the tension between balance and the lives of moral heroes who seem to burn hot for what matters.
  3. 03:20 Not Squandering Pain - The talk turns to suffering as something that should deepen love and service rather than spin into useless anguish.
  4. 04:56 Compassion with Equanimity - He distinguishes compassion from frantic fixing and points toward a steadier, more spacious kind of love.
  5. 06:44 Seeing Dukkha Clearly - Using the First Noble Truth, he asks how a fuller comprehension of suffering might purify compassion from control.
  6. 08:16 Helplessness and Mortality - He reflects on helplessness, aging, and anicca, asking what love and rest look like when no move can be made.
  7. 09:45 The Broken Cup - Ajahn Chah’s broken cup teaching becomes a way to contemplate impermanence and the possibility of a heart that does not shatter with loss.
  8. 11:06 Fear, Courage, and Care - He acknowledges fear as a distorting force while wondering when love needs to become more radical and less guarded.
  9. 13:24 Good Questions - He closes by saying the point is not to force answers, but to keep close to the questions that can guide practice.
