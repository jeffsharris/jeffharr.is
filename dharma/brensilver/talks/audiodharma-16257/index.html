Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Lightly Guided Meditation

AudioDharma 29:17 2022-05-09

The talk begins with a simple practice of settling into awareness, relaxing the body, and letting attention rest where it naturally cannot be stopped. From there, the speaker turns to a subtle distinction: while attention can be directed to breath, body, feeling, or sound, awareness itself is always already present and does not require effort to arise. A repeated invocation of Shantideva becomes part of that movement, helping fold the familiar ground of strategizing and fretting back into spacious awareness.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Settling In - The talk opens with a brief welcome and an invitation to settle the body and mind into awareness.
  2. 03:01 Relaxing the Body - Matthew guides a systematic softening through the face, jaw, shoulders, belly, and hands, while allowing remaining tension to stay.
  3. 05:56 What Can't Be Stopped - He turns toward the aspect of awareness that cannot be shut off, encouraging rest in that already-present knowing.
  4. 07:48 Directing Attention - The teaching distinguishes ordinary attention to breath, body, sound, or feeling from the dimension of awareness that does not need effort.
  5. 13:41 Suffering and Identification - Matthew explains that suffering often depends on being glued to something, and that awareness reveals another truth at the same time.
  6. 16:04 Shantideva Refrain - A repeated naming of Shantideva functions like a chant, gathering experience and folding the familiar ground of thought and concern into awareness.
  7. 21:57 Folding Life In - He returns to the central gesture of taking whatever arises—strategy, worry, or habit—and letting it be included in awareness.
  8. 28:43 Closing Bells - The meditation ends with bells and a quiet closing.
