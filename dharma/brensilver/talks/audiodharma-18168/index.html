Dharmette: Mortality - Fear, Helplessness & Self (4/5)

AudioDharma 14:12 2023-04-27

What happens when mortality exposes the ego’s need to manage, defend, and stay in control? The talk moves from death as a humbling force to helplessness as a practice area, arguing that meditation can become a way of willingly meeting powerlessness instead of constantly trying to fix or steer experience. Along the way, the speaker links fear to the making of self, then turns to not-self as a real but limited consolation in the face of loss, ending with Derek Parfit’s image of life loosening from a glass tunnel into open air.

Chapters

  1. 00:09 Mortality Clarifies Value - He revisits the earlier point that contemplating death can cut through noise and bring what matters into focus.
  2. 00:48 Helplessness and Selfing - The talk shifts toward self, willfulness, and the sense that life does not fully belong to us.
  3. 01:51 Death as Humbling - He describes death as both humbling and threatening to the sense of self as a center of control.
  4. 03:14 The Mirage of Control - Drawing on an external quote, he reflects on adulthood, autonomy, and the hidden forces that shape a life beyond our plans.
  5. 05:08 Training With Helplessness - Using Michelle McDonald’s phrase, he frames practice as learning to relate to helplessness, surrender, and equanimity.
  6. 07:29 Loss and Vastness - He notes that letting go of defense can open the heart, and recalls a teaching attributed to Dugu Khyentse Rinpoche about the mind becoming very small or very vast at the end of life.
  7. 08:50 Self as Effect - He argues that the self is better understood as an effect of fear and clinging than as their cause.
  8. 10:49 Not-Self and Death - He says insight into not-self does not erase grief, but it can meaningfully soften the terror of death and loss.
  9. 12:04 Parfit and Open Air - He closes with Derek Parfit’s image of the glass tunnel giving way to open air, linking philosophical insight to less self-concern and more concern for others.
