Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Lightly Guided Meditation

AudioDharma 30:35 2022-04-13

How do we practice when the mind keeps pulling toward the wound, the plan, or the next thing to manage? This lightly guided meditation keeps returning to the simple act of stopping—opening to what is here, drawing a clear but flexible circle around the chosen focus, and resting with balanced energy. The talk frames attention as something like a gust of wind: neither braced against, nor held onto, and then widens into the sense that every experience can become a doorway when the agenda is put down.

Chapters

  1. 00:13 Welcome and settling - He greets the group and invites listeners to settle into the practice.
  2. 00:58 Stopping with courage - The instruction turns to stopping all at once or easing in, with attention to the courage and faith involved.
  3. 04:09 Loose ends of life - He names the unfinished, unresolved quality of human experience and the practice of not tying everything up.
  4. 06:12 Wind through awareness - Phenomena are compared to a gust of wind, met without bracing or trying to hold on.
  5. 08:49 Drawing the circle - He describes how to define the focus of practice, whether on breath, sound, body, or the full field of experience.
  6. 11:00 Balanced resting - The guidance emphasizes balanced energy, neither resisting experience nor clinging to it.
  7. 15:34 Everything as a door - Relinquishing agenda opens all phenomena as gateways to wisdom and love, and the sitting continues into simple walking through.
  8. 23:25 Sentience and love - Near the end, he reflects on the condition of being sentient, the wish for safety and peace, and the arising of a love that softens self-judgment.
