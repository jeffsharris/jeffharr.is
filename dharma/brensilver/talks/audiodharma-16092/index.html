Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Meaning And Nonconceptualization

AudioDharma 14:28 2022-04-14

What happens when the mind’s need to make meaning becomes so strong that it starts to shape the whole world? The talk moves from the usefulness of Buddhist categories and beautiful thoughts like metta into a deeper question about fear, interpretation, and the compulsion to know. It then turns to a canonical exchange on how appearances disappear through not conceiving, and ends with a simple instruction: come to no conclusions, and let intimacy with experience generate stories that are more spacious and less entangled.

Chapters

  1. 00:37 Stories and struggle - Matthew opens with the idea that we live by stories, while noting that no story or life is free of tension.
  2. 01:37 Useful Dharma thought - He turns to the Buddha’s analytic teachings and metta as examples of thought that is beautiful, practical, and heart-opening.
  3. 03:14 Meaning and fear - The talk develops the idea that meaning-making is often driven by fear, vigilance, and the basic need to know.
  4. 05:19 When thoughts feel real - He describes how being spellbound by interpretations can make life feel surreal and alienated, and how intimacy with experience thins that compulsion.
  5. 08:30 Non-conceiving - A canonical teaching on appearances disappearing through non-conceiving is used to show that practice is not another idea about disappearance.
  6. 10:35 No conclusions - Matthew gives a direct meditation instruction: do not land on conclusions, and do not let any conclusion become final.
  7. 12:08 Dharma autobiography - He closes by reframing autobiography as a Dharma story, where meaning and meaninglessness coexist with more space and less entanglement.
