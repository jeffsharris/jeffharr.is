Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Unconscious Acting-Out

AudioDharma 15:09 2025-07-09

When a small, unskillful action seems to come from nowhere, what does that reveal about the mind? The talk begins with the familiar feeling of a self that seems close, coherent, and available to inspection, then turns to Freud, Copernicus, Darwin, and Yogacara to unsettle that picture and make room for unconscious forces, habitual patterns, and karmic seeds. From there it moves into shame, repair, and the practical question of how meditation can loosen these pressures without forcing everything into words. The closing emphasis is on investigation, self-knowledge, and the need for other people in the work of waking up.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 The felt self - He begins with the self as immediate, accessible, and seemingly unitary, setting up the basic intuition the talk will question.
  2. 00:55 Three humblings - Freud and Dennett frame the argument that the self is not as central or self-governing as it feels.
  3. 03:22 Storehouse consciousness - Yogacara’s alaya vijnana is introduced as a Buddhist way to think about latent habits and karmic potential.
  4. 04:25 An unskillful moment - Brensilver shares a recent instance of acting in a way he could not fully explain, using it as a concrete example of unconscious influence.
  5. 06:49 Leakage and shame - He describes unconscious behavior as over-disclosure, where the performance of self cracks and shame can fill the gap.
  6. 08:59 Non-shame investigation - The talk softens toward breathing, self-understanding, and Freud’s idea of making the unconscious conscious without moral collapse.
  7. 11:36 Remorse and repair - He distinguishes clean remorse from shame and suggests that even without full verbal understanding, meditation can loosen hidden pressures.
  8. 13:42 Wisdom together - The closing stresses that we cannot do this work alone and that insight often arises in relationship rather than in isolation.
