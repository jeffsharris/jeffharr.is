Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: moving towards compassion

AudioDharma 28:37 2025-09-10

How can compassion be grown without denying the fact of suffering? The talk begins with a brief Issa poem about dukkha and then settles into guided practice: first softening the body and gathering the mind, then meeting heaviness, sorrow, and helplessness with a thoroughly undefended kindness. From there it turns toward the way love can be mixed with pain, supported by mindfulness, stillness, and the quiet wish that suffering lessen.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and Issa poem - Matthew introduces the theme with a short poem by Kobayashi Issa and uses it to frame the coexistence of suffering and openness.
  2. 01:47 Settling the body - The guided practice begins by settling into posture and asking what conditions support love, including tranquility, collectedness, and temporarily putting the world down.
  3. 06:11 Awake to experience - Mindfulness is established as being awake to this moment, including sorrow, happiness, and whatever heaviness is present in the heart.
  4. 10:42 Meeting pain gently - He encourages an undefended relationship to suffering and invites imagery or silently repeated phrases that evoke care.
  5. 13:53 Love meets suffering - The talk names the meeting of love and suffering as a home for the Brahma-vihara and points to the universality of pain as a basis for wishing others suffer less.
  6. 16:50 Turning toward love - Matthew reflects on the abrasiveness of cruelty and offers a grounded encouragement not to dismiss the reality of love because it is incomplete.
  7. 20:55 Body as antenna - In the quieter close, the body is described as a kind of antenna that can tune the whole being toward compassion.
  8. 23:32 Planting non-violence - The practice ends with resting, planting seeds of non-violence, and letting love stay close to the surface in ordinary moments.
  9. 25:47 Closing reflection - The final note returns to suffering as a cause for deeper understanding.
