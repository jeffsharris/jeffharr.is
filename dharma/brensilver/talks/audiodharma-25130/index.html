Guided Meditation: Dedicating our practice

AudioDharma 27:56 2026-03-25

What does it mean to arrive here, body and mind, without bracing against experience? This guided meditation begins with settling and “putting it down,” then turns toward dedicating the practice—not as pressure, but as a quiet recognition that practice matters because life is finite and not a dress rehearsal. As the talk unfolds, love is invited to gather in the background while the breath, the path, and the wish for peace are honored. The practice movement is gentle but serious: softening defensiveness, letting love stand beside what cannot be controlled, and sensing how the world looks different when love meets finitude.

  3. 03:20 Dedicating Practice - Brensilver invites reflection on whom we practice for and on the seriousness of practice in light of birth and death.
  4. 10:29 Love and Finitude - The talk explores how love may be larger than our power while still remaining steady and strong.
  5. 12:16 Quiet Repetition - A long guided stretch uses repeated phrases to let love gather in the background while breathing and honoring the path.
  6. 19:44 Seeing Differently - The meditation closes by linking love, finitude, and the way the world looks when they come into clearer view.
