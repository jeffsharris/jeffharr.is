Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Somebody, Nobody, Self-Love, Anatta

AudioDharma 13:46 2025-04-30

What is the difference between a healthy sense of self and the insight of not-self? The talk moves from an old teaching line — that you have to be somebody before being nobody — into a careful reworking of what self-love means: not ego-affirmation, but forgiveness, non-clinging, and a first loosening of attachment to self-story. From there it turns toward anatta as a deeper kind of disidentification, where what once felt like the place from which we see becomes just another thing seen.

Chapters

  1. 00:15 Looking Back - He opens by reflecting on old notes from his earliest Dharma talks and the humility that comes from seeing one’s own earlier confusion and continuity.
  2. 02:12 Somebody Before Nobody - Matthew revisits Jack Engler’s line about needing to be somebody before being nobody, and uses it to suggest that deconstruction in practice needs a base of psychological strength.
  3. 04:43 Language and Self - He brings in Wittgenstein to show how the word self can mislead, especially when the same word is used in very different Dharma and psychological contexts.
  4. 05:11 Self-Love as Forgiveness - Matthew distinguishes self-love from self-affirmation, describing it as forgiveness, innocence, and non-clinging rather than a celebration of a fixed identity.
  5. 07:45 Not-Self Is Flexibility - He clarifies that not-self is not confusion or diffusion, but a flexible relationship to traits and life events that loosens attachment and aversion.
  6. 09:59 The Self Model Opens - Using a philosophical reference to Letheby, he describes anatta as a deeper unbinding in which self-related contents become visible as objects in awareness.
  7. 12:27 Return to Innocence - He ends by circling back to the practical arc: conditioning can be seen clearly, growth can be wished for without clinging, and the self can be met with more innocence each time it reappears.
