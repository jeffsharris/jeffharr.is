Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Equanimity (1 of 5): Introduction

AudioDharma 15:16 2021-08-09

Matthew Brensilver opens a week on equanimity by situating it as a practical foundation for the path, especially in relation to non-clinging and the root defilements. He moves from a broad description of wise attention into several working definitions of equanimity: inner balance, non-compulsion, widened perspective, and the space to meet difficulty without laundering it into a story. The talk closes by showing how equanimity can be developed with greed, aversion, and delusion, so that the heart does not have to keep defending or justifying them.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Opening and context - Matthew greets the group and situates the talk as a continuation of Gil Fronsdal’s prior teaching.
  2. 01:19 Attention and wisdom - He distinguishes mindfulness from wisdom, emphasizing that attention becomes skillful only when joined with wholesomeness.
  3. 02:44 Equanimity and non-clinging - Equanimity is introduced as a foundation for the path, closely tied to non-clinging and freedom from compulsion around preferences.
  4. 05:00 Widening perspective - Drawing on Sarah Lazar, he describes equanimity as a broader perspective that takes in more sensory experience and less reactivity.
  5. 06:16 Balance and realism - He cites Shinzen Young and Michelle McDonald to frame equanimity as the middle between suppression and identification, with realism and aspiration.
  6. 07:32 Opening to dukkha - Matthew speaks about how much equanimity is needed simply to bear the intensity of suffering and the human condition.
  7. 09:41 Why it matters - He turns to the practical value of equanimity, calling it generative and closely linked to peace, love, and release.
  8. 11:47 Working with the defilements - The talk ends by showing how equanimity helps meet longing, imperfection, and delusion without feeding the stories that disguise them.
