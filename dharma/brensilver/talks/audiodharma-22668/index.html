Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Much Becomes Incidental

AudioDharma 28:16 2025-04-02

What changes when you stop trying to build, manage, or improve experience, and simply listen? This guided meditation keeps returning to silence as something to attune to directly, even when noise and thought are still present. From that ground, pleasant and unpleasant, personal story, and even control itself are invited to become incidental for a little while, so the practice can rest in receiving the moment. The movement is toward surrender: no projects, no self-making, just space receiving space, with the suggestion that if anything is built, let it be love.

  6. 09:27 Receiving space - The practice widens into a spacious resting in which space seems to receive space.
  7. 16:54 A new chance to let go - The talk returns to the ordinary urge to medicate feelings and thought, and frames the session as another opportunity to release control.
  8. 20:18 Emerging unchanged - The closing notes suggest that life will still be there afterward, the same and yet slightly different after practice.
