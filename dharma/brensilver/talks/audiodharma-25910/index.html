Note on Teaching and Vulnerability

AudioDharma 05:29 2026-07-22

What happens when a talk is given from the heart and the mind immediately wants to judge it? This short reflection explores the vulnerability that can follow teaching, especially when shame, being seen, and fear of being misconstrued all arise together. The talk moves toward a simple protection: speaking as best we can from love, and then letting the rest be out of our hands.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 A Question About Vulnerability - The talk begins with a question about the aftermath of speaking openly, especially after a talk that feels emotionally exposed.
  2. 01:00 Teaching Feels Wild - The speaker reflects that teaching can be unpredictable and says the inquiry opens a way to speak about being seen.
  3. 01:56 Consent and the Dharma Field - He describes how vulnerability is shaped by context, especially whether a group has genuinely consented to enter a Dharma setting together.
  4. 02:45 Seen and Misconstrued - The talk turns to the strange experience of feeling both visible and not fully knowable, with a concern less about exposure than misunderstanding.
  5. 03:37 What Leaks Out - The speaker suggests that words and unconscious expressions can exceed intention, and that training includes becoming less embarrassed by what slips through.
  6. 04:34 Speaking From Love - The talk closes by naming shame-proneness and imposter syndrome, then pointing to a simple protection: speak as best we can from true love.
