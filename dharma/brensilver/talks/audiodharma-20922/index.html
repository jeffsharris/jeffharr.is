Dharmette: The Wholesome Disorientation of Feedback

AudioDharma 13:46 2024-07-24

Matthew Brensilver reflects on feedback as a practical mirror for seeing self-clinging, defensiveness, and the places where practice is still tender. He tells a few humorous, slightly exposing stories, then broadens into a simple map of head, heart, and belly as different capacities that can develop unevenly. The talk ends by treating surprise and disorientation not as a problem to avoid, but as part of the path of growth and self-understanding.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Opening with feedback - He begins with years of receiving feedback and why it has always felt worth paying attention to.
  2. 01:10 Defensiveness and anatta - He frames feedback through anatta, arguing that defensiveness itself is a useful signal of clinging.
  3. 02:37 The Kinko's story - He shares a sharp piece of feedback about how he looked on the dais, and notices the truth and delight hidden inside it.
  4. 05:19 Head, heart, belly - He introduces a three-part map of practice and names belly, or grounded embodiment, as his own underdeveloped area.
  5. 07:31 Teachers and students - He says teachers should share both wisdom and delusion, then uses Nietzsche to point toward the student outgrowing the teacher.
  6. 08:46 Growth we miss - He reflects on how practice can grow around our blind spots and how some strengths can hide what is less developed.
  7. 10:28 Startled by change - He describes growth as something a little startling, like turbulence that cracks open our settled self-image.
  8. 12:21 Love beyond discovery - He closes by saying there is nothing we can discover that should become a reason not to love ourselves, and that self-exploration can become fearless.
