Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Romance, Relationship, Sexuality & Dharma

AudioDharma 57:27 2016-10-09

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet, practical talk on how love, sexuality, and relationship become a genuine field of Dharma practice. He moves from the limits of retreat insight into the everyday tests of dependency, projection, aversion, and the need for belonging, while drawing on stories and quotations from Bob Thurman, Rilke, Stephen Mitchell, Lacan, and others. The talk gradually turns toward a workable vision of love: tender, clear about impermanence, respectful of the other’s freedom, and less burdened by self-view.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Practice Meets Relationship - Matthew opens with Bob Thurman’s question about practice versus performance and turns it toward the lived test of how we relate to others.
  2. 02:00 Relational Life as Mirror - He describes relationship as a place where retreat insights are tested, and where family and intimacy reveal our deepest conditioning.
  3. 07:28 Clinging and Belonging - The talk looks at attachment, sexual desire, and the ache of belonging, showing why neither relationship nor solitude can solve everything in the heart.
  4. 11:44 Love’s Difficulty - Matthew names the tenderness of this territory and uses Rilke to frame love as demanding work rather than sentiment.
  5. 15:37 Desire, Dependency, Risk - He explores projection, idealization, and Stephen Mitchell’s view that desire is bound up with dependency, danger, and the wish to control.
  6. 24:54 Eros and Freedom - The talk develops a fuller image of love as the union of passion and metta, then asks what it means to honor the other person’s freedom.
  7. 36:37 Aggression and Not-Self - Matthew shifts to aversion, respectful conflict, and the empty-boat teaching, using anatta to show how loosening self-view softens offense.
  8. 48:27 Commitment and Refuge - He closes with monogamy as an adventure, then widens the frame to say that relationship is real practice, but not the only refuge.
