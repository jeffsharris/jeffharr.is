Dharmette: Non-clinging

AudioDharma 15:38 2024-07-03

What does it actually mean to stop clinging when clinging is wired into desire, habit, and even love? The talk begins with a simple question—how to overcome clinging—and then resists any neat technique, arguing that letting go is less an algorithm than a knot of conditions: renunciation, energy, resolve, patience, and equanimity working together. Along the way, the speaker uses Joseph Goldstein, Shantideva, the Buddha’s image of anger’s honeyed tip and poisoned root, and even a brief Freud reference to show why release can require both giving something up and reinvesting life elsewhere.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening Questions - Matthew greets the group, acknowledges the questions, and sets up the topic of clinging as the central concern.
  2. 01:32 What Clinging Means - He takes the question directly and uses Joseph Goldstein’s summary of practice to frame non-clinging as the heart of the path.
  3. 02:53 Why No Simple Method - Matthew argues against formulas for letting go and warns that willfulness can simply repeat the same clinging it tries to end.
  4. 04:18 Conditions and Consent - He turns to the way our choices and investments make some clinging likely, and asks whether we can consent to that reality without denial.
  5. 05:56 Reinforcement and Habit - Using Shantideva and a gambling analogy, he shows how clinging is conditioned, rewarded, and therefore persistent even when it causes suffering.
  6. 08:29 The Paramis of Release - Matthew describes letting go as the combined expression of renunciation, energy, resolve, patience, and equanimity rather than a single technique.
  7. 12:10 Reinvesting Energy - He suggests that release can involve ritual, renewal, and a redirection of energy or love instead of a purely internal act.
  8. 14:01 Living With Loss - The talk closes by distinguishing ordinary clinging from the way one may live alongside absence, letting love grow around pain.
