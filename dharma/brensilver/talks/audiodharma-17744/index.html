Guided Meditation: Surrendering to the Present

AudioDharma 29:54 2023-02-13

What does it mean to meet the present without trying to manage it? This guided meditation moves gently from settling the body and relaxing attention into an inquiry about clinging, hope, and control, with pauses to notice whatever wants awareness’s blessing so it can be put down. The practice keeps returning to embodiment and equanimity, suggesting that when we stop reaching to steer each moment, the heart becomes more available to receive what is here.

Chapters

  1. 01:02 Arrival and settling - The meditation begins by inviting everyone to sit and arrive together.
  2. 02:20 Relief and embodiment - Listeners are guided to relax, direct attention, and establish a felt sense of embodiment and mindfulness.
  3. 05:04 Blessing what remains - The teacher suggests offering awareness to whatever needs it so it can be put down, even when it cannot be resolved.
  4. 09:53 Body and mind together - The practice emphasizes posture, body awareness, and relaxing the mind as a way of being present.
  5. 15:05 Clinging and loss - The talk explores the hope invested in clinging and the grief of seeing that it cannot deliver what we want.
  6. 21:32 Less entanglement - Hopes and fears are described as still poignant, but less binding as the sense of self loosens.
  7. 23:34 Receiving the moment - The practice culminates in receiving the present without trying to control it, letting the heart rest.
