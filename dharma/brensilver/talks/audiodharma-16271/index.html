Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Lightly Guided Meditation

AudioDharma 29:46 2022-05-13

This lightly guided meditation invites a gradual softening of vigilance, self-protection, and mental reference points. Matthew Brensilver repeatedly returns to the felt sense of this moment, encouraging a relaxation into experience rather than a struggle to manage it. Midway through, he quotes a teacher’s instruction—“Don’t fight with yourself at any level”—and uses it to open into radical permission, intimacy, and equanimity.

Chapters

  1. 00:57 Opening to Experience - He begins by inviting a broad, immediate embrace of the whole field of experience.
  2. 01:58 Safe Enough to Melt - The guidance turns toward easing vigilance and allowing the body-mind to soften into the present moment.
  3. 02:42 Letting Armor Unwind - He names bracing, numbing, and disconnection, then suggests they can all melt into aliveness.
  4. 06:02 Dropping Reference Points - The talk shifts to releasing the frameworks that divide past and future, self and other, and preference and aversion.
  5. 10:00 No Fighting - A teaching attributed to his teacher becomes the pivot into radical permission and non-resistance.
  6. 15:50 Tolerating Not Knowing - He returns to the story of self and the habit of taking a position, then asks for tolerance of uncertainty and not getting.
  7. 20:30 Resting the Heart - The meditation settles into rest, equanimity, and not picking sides with experience.
