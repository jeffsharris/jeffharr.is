Matthew Brensilver, Phillip Moffitt, Tuere Sala: Dialogue on aspects of freedom.

Dharma Seed 52:03 2021-10-01

What if freedom is not a one-time breakthrough, but a slow loosening shaped by sitting, returning, and letting practice work on habit? In this dialogue, the talk moves from the contrast between depth and breadth, into questions of non-self, self-improvement, striving, and the way the Dharma shows up in ordinary life rather than as a project of becoming someone else. It also turns toward the role of suffering in maturing love, with stories and images that keep bringing the conversation back to practice as something gradual, ordinary, and quietly transformative.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the dialogue - The hosts introduce the conversational format and frame the talk as an exploration of different dimensions of freedom.
  2. 01:07 Depth and breadth - They distinguish freedom’s vertical depth from its horizontal spread into ordinary life, and begin questioning whether freedom is a category or a continuum.
  3. 03:56 Practice as conditioning - The conversation turns to gradual accumulation, choice points on the cushion, and the idea that practice trains the mind without self-forcing.
  4. 10:35 Non-self and reactivity - Tuere describes how sitting with unpleasantness helped her become less reactive, and Matthew connects that to non-self and organic steadiness rather than self-made improvement.
  5. 13:52 Against spiritual ambition - The group examines the familiar story of a flawed self moving toward enlightenment, and warns against turning Dharma into a self-help project or a new possession.
  6. 19:34 Striving and purification - They speak candidly about years of torment, over-efforting, and the shift toward purification of motive, authenticity, and letting the Dharma work more than the self does.
  7. 26:59 Dukkha opens love - The discussion widens to grace, human suffering, and the way mature love and metta arise after really feeling the pain of one’s own life.
  8. 38:16 Learning the plateau - George Leonard’s 'love the plateau' image leads into a reflection on gradual change, hidden transformation, and the healing of egoic shame and self-blame.
  9. 44:54 Why keep practicing - They end by naming habit energy, the need to keep renewing wisdom, and the quieter answer that practice continues because love and compassion keep drawing them back.
