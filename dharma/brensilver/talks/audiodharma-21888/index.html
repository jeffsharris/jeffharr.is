Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Mostly Quiet Sit

AudioDharma 28:18 2024-12-13

This guided sit moves from settling the body into a quiet, spacious attention that does not force experience to become anything else. The talk keeps returning to simple instructions: attend where it feels appropriate, let go, and not be afraid of silence. Along the way, the speaker softens the practice with the image of the sangha as a field of goodwill, then closes by framing the whole sit as a kind of radical permission for conditions to be as they are, without turning that into passivity in every moment.

Chapters

  1. 01:24 Settling In - After the welcome, the practice turns toward sitting and sensing where attention feels appropriate.
  2. 03:34 Personalizing Practice - Matthew frames the Dharma as something we learn from the Buddha and lineage, but also have to discover in our own life.
  3. 09:39 Letting Go - The guidance softens into a quiet release, with an image of the Buddha whispering to let go and not fear silence.
  4. 16:08 Sangha Support - The silent presence of the sangha is invited as a field of goodwill that can soothe the heart and make letting go feel safer.
  5. 21:44 Radical Permission - The meditation opens into a deeper acceptance of present conditions, alongside a reminder that wisdom later determines the balance between receptivity and action.
  6. 27:17 Closing Bells - The sit ends with a short sequence of bells and quiet closing space.
