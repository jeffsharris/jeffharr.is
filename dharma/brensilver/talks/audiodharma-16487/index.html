Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Sunday Evening Reflection

AudioDharma 11:51 2022-06-19

The talk turns on a paradox: how can you be totally acceptable as you are and still honestly commit to change? Using a line attributed to Suzuki Roshi, the speaker argues against the usual half-and-half compromise, where acceptance becomes a tactic and growth becomes another project. Instead, the invitation is a full yes to the messiness of life and a clear recognition that it is possible to suffer less, without bargaining with yourself.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Bohr's Paradox - Matthew opens with Bohr to frame the talk through the idea that profound statements can have profound opposites.
  2. 00:18 Suzuki Roshi's Line - He introduces Suzuki Roshi's saying about being perfect as you are and having room for improvement.
  3. 00:55 Acceptance and Growth - The central teaching takes shape as a call to totally accept ourselves while totally committing to growth.
  4. 01:53 No Spiritual Bargaining - Matthew notices how people try to change in order to become acceptable, or accept themselves in order to get something in return.
  5. 02:35 A Deep Yes - He emphasizes unconditional acceptance of one's life and past as something worthy of love.
  6. 04:00 Change Without Desperation - From that acceptance, the wish to change becomes clear and unforced, grounded in the simple recognition that suffering can lessen.
  7. 09:31 End of Retreat Day - Near the close, he shifts into the practical mood of the evening, acknowledging the sweetness and effort of the first retreat day.
  8. 10:12 Rest and Regather - The talk ends with ordinary guidance to rest well, allow whatever dreams or complexity arise, and return tomorrow.
