Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Seven Factors of Awakening

AudioDharma 45:09 2022-09-25

The talk asks what changes when practice is no longer only about letting go of suffering, but also about cultivating the qualities that support awakening. From there it moves through the seven factors as a living balance: mindfulness, investigation, energy, joy, tranquility, samadhi, and equanimity, showing how they depend on one another rather than forming a tidy list. The speaker keeps returning to the practical question of what steadies the mind, softens clinging, and makes Dharma feel real rather than merely understood. References to the Buddha, Kabat-Zinn, and Shinzen Young are used to sharpen that movement from concept to lived realization.

Chapters

  1. 00:25 Why This List Matters - Brensilver opens by revisiting why Buddhist lists can become invisible through familiarity and why they still matter deeply in practice.
  2. 02:49 Path and Fruit - He frames the Dharma as release from suffering and introduces the seven factors of awakening as the active wholesome qualities that support that release.
  3. 05:02 A Teaching in Sickness - A sutta story of the Buddha asking for the factors at night leads into the idea that these qualities are practical support, not abstract doctrine.
  4. 08:19 Mindfulness and Balance - Using Tejaniya’s causal framing, he describes the interplay between the energizing and tranquilizing sides of practice and rethinks what mindfulness means.
  5. 11:48 What Mindfulness Does - He argues that mindfulness is a broad umbrella term, then narrows it as awareness that sees suffering without being compelled by it and gradually becomes a habit.
  6. 17:45 Curiosity and Investigation - Investigation is presented as sustained, curious attention rather than rumination, with faith growing through careful looking and repeated inquiry.
  7. 22:40 Energy and Rapture - The talk turns to effort as necessary resistance against habitual suffering, then to rapture as the surprising, pleasurable vitality that can arise from letting go.
  8. 29:51 Tranquility and Samadhi - Brensilver describes the pivot into calming the nervous system, where steadiness, unification, and a more durable realization of Dharma become possible.
  9. 38:53 Equanimity and Release - He closes with equanimity as a softening of clinging, a quiet nearness to awakening, and a place where peace and love are not far apart.
