Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Metta practice

Dharma Seed 39:44 2022-03-03

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided metta practice that begins with a quiet reflection on why lovingkindness matters, especially when the alternatives are hatred, apathy, or self-judgment. He then moves into a steady seated practice with phrases for self, other, and all beings, while emphasizing that metta can work as cultivation, concentration, and purification. The talk stays grounded in the ordinary conditions of human life, showing how tenderness can include whatever is present rather than wait for a perfect feeling state.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Why Metta Matters - Brensilver introduces the practice and explains why love, rather than hatred or apathy, is central to the path.
  2. 04:53 Three Functions - He describes metta as cultivation, concentration, and purification, including how it can reveal what is not yet love.
  3. 09:13 Settling the Body - The guided practice begins with posture, breath, and sensing the state of the heart in this moment.
  4. 13:41 Self-Compassion Phrases - He invites phrases for one’s own heart and reflects on meeting experience without measuring worth.
  5. 19:48 Extending to Others - The practice widens into offering phrases for others, including a visualization of being seen through loving eyes.
  6. 24:53 Warmth and Care - He emphasizes that the practice is not self-indulgence and that even a small hint of warmth is enough.
  7. 30:01 Pain as Practice - Brensilver names how metta can bring up undigested pain and says that too can be held with love.
  8. 34:01 All Beings at Ease - The closing broadens into universal wishes for happiness, safety, and ease, repeated gently as the practice deepens.
  9. 39:01 Closing - The guided practice ends in silence and thanks.
