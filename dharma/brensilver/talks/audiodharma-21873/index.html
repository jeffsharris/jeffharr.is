Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Self-acceptance

AudioDharma 29:00 2024-12-12

How do you stop sorting yourself into acceptable and unacceptable parts long enough to meet your life with kindness? The talk moves from a short settling into a clear invitation to rest in the body, then widen into the sense that a person is shaped by countless forces—history, conditioning, joy, and sorrow—that cannot be fully controlled. From there it turns toward a radical yes to experience: not to excuse harm or avoid change, but to let acceptance end shame and make room for compassion, equanimity, and a simple blessing practice of “may I be happy, may I be safe, may I be at peace.”

Chapters

  1. 01:52 Settling In - The meditation begins by inviting stillness and an attentive arrival into practice.
  2. 04:04 Body and Awareness - Attention is poured into the body and the body is encouraged to feel bright and vivid with awareness.
  3. 06:23 Kindness for Conditioning - The teaching widens into compassion for the forces, seen and unseen, that have shaped this moment and this life.
  4. 09:12 Accepting My Life - He frames acceptance as a way of being with the whole of one's life, including suffering, good fortune, and helplessness.
  5. 15:22 A Radical Yes - The talk turns to the habit of sorting ourselves into acceptable and unacceptable parts and asks for a complete stop to that pattern.
  6. 17:59 End the Inner Game - Brensilver encourages dropping self-criticism, shame, and the constant behavioral correction project, and simply opening to the totality of what one is.
  7. 26:23 Loving-Kindness Closing - The practice closes with repeated phrases of goodwill: may I be happy, safe, protected, and at peace.
