Dharmette: The Divine Abodes (1 of 5) Orienting to Love

AudioDharma 15:44 2024-12-09

What if love, not aversion, became the place the mind falls back on when life goes wrong? The talk moves from that question into a practical account of non-hatred: how hatred depends on holding on, why love can coexist with letting go, and how ethical conduct and nervous-system reality shape what is actually possible. From there, the speaker turns to the Brahma-viharas—loving-kindness, compassion, sympathetic joy, and equanimity—treating them less as ideals than as trainable resting places for attention. The talk ends by inviting a flexible, improvisational relationship to aversion, so the week becomes a chance to notice where love can be strengthened.

Chapters

  1. 00:50 Choosing Love First - Matthew frames love as a commitment that changes how experience lands, especially when life does not go well.
  2. 03:00 Why Hatred Arises - He reflects on samsara, the inevitability of things to object to, and how pain often seeks a target.
  3. 04:48 Non-hatred and Letting Go - The talk turns to non-hatred, renunciation, and the way quiet attention reveals the burden that aversion places on the heart.
  4. 07:13 Four Species of Love - Matthew introduces the Brahma-viharas and interprets metta, compassion, mudita, and equanimity in vivid, practical terms.
  5. 10:33 Where the Mind Rests - He emphasizes that these qualities are not ideals to admire but places where the mind can increasingly come home.
  6. 11:54 Training Good Habits - The teaching shifts to discipline and repetition, suggesting that practice makes love more available and hate more effortful.
  7. 14:00 Depth and Breadth - Matthew broadens the teaching to show love as something that can pervade the mind deeply and extend outward across situations.
  8. 15:29 Closing Invitation - He ends with an invitation to watch aversion and stay flexible, with the promise of continuing the theme in later talks.
