Friday Morning Instructions: The non-compulsion of awareness

AudioDharma 53:59 2020-12-11

The talk begins with settling into posture in a way that feels welcoming, then quickly moves into a quiet inquiry: what if awareness does not compel us, even though body and mind are full of habit, memory, and urge? From there, the speaker traces how the present moment carries the past, how clinging shows up as tension and drivenness in the body, and how practice can become a gentle act of digesting life rather than sorting it into right and wrong. Later, the teaching turns toward liberation as something that frees experience itself, and closes by treating Dharma as a collection of skillful means that has to be felt out moment by moment.

  6. 34:15 Freeing Experience - The teaching turns outward from self-improvement toward freeing clinging and all experience with kindness.
  7. 40:24 Putting Down Judgment - Using the phrase 'The All-Knowing Mind,' he speaks about relinquishing the burden of sorting everything into right and wrong.
  8. 48:34 Skillful Means - He closes by naming the Dharma as many skillful means and encouraging listeners to notice what is helpful in each moment.
