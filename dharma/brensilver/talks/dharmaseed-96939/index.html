Guided Meditation: Letting Go into the Breath (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 54:56 2026-05-02

Held first by the silence and the sangha, this guided meditation asks what the deep mind needs in order to feel safe enough to let go. The practice moves from scanning the body and softening urgency into feeling the breath more fully, then into a careful noticing of each in-breath and out-breath as something to meet with patience rather than control. From there, the talk widens into walking meditation as a way to train the mind to let go and begin again, balancing the energy of sitting and movement without forcing a special state.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Held by the Sangha - The meditation opens by resting in the support of silence and community, with a brief body scan to arrive.
  2. 03:04 Reassure the Deep Mind - Matthew asks what the heart needs to feel safe enough to let go, naming self-soothing and patience as part of the path.
  3. 08:01 Breath in the Whole Body - Attention widens to the whole body as the breath is sensed more fully and the mind is asked to stabilize.
  4. 17:24 Trust the Breath - He frames attention to breathing as a way of stepping out of routine hope and fear and building trust.
  5. 22:29 Subtlety of Each Breath - The practice turns toward the exact beginnings and endings of inhalation and exhalation, including the release at the bottom of the exhale.
  6. 31:02 Dharma Happens - In a more reflective teaching mode, he says practice involves setting up conditions while recognizing that awareness and Dharma do not depend on selfing.
  7. 41:30 Walking as Practice - Matthew explains walking meditation as training the mind to stop, let go, and begin again, using each turn at the end of the path as a reset.
  8. 48:17 Balance Energy and Steadiness - He closes by describing how walking and sitting balance energy, helping the mind stay calm without sleepiness and interested without strain.
