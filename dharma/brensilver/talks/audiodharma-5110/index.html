Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Practice Note

AudioDharma 05:24 2014-09-24

Matthew Brensilver offers a short practice note on how the mind turns ordinary life into a series of projects, and why that habit brings stress. He then points to a gentler alternative: doing what needs to be done without fixing each moment into a goal to complete. The emphasis stays practical, with examples from walking, tea, and the ordinary movements of a morning practice period.

Chapters

  1. 00:18 Life as a Project - He introduces the tendency to turn life into tasks and explains why that creates strain.
  2. 01:09 Not Doing Nothing - He clarifies that releasing project-thinking does not mean becoming passive or avoiding responsibility.
  3. 02:13 Meditation as Task - He notes that even walking meditation can become another project, and invites a softer way of practicing.
  4. 02:51 Relaxing the Schedule - He describes how the mind organizes practice by time, steps, and outcomes, and what changes when that grip loosens.
  5. 03:51 Ordinary Actions - He brings the point into daily life with examples like walking, using the bathroom, and having tea.
  6. 04:38 Open to What Is Skillful - He closes by inviting a whole-morning attitude of openness to the present moment and what is skillful.
