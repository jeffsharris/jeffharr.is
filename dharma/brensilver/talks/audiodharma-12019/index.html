Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Preparing Citta not to Burn - IRC mini-retreat

AudioDharma 21:48 2020-08-26

How do you practice when suffering is already layered on top of suffering, and the world itself feels fragile? The talk moves from fire season, climate grief, and William James’s image of people skating on a melting frozen lake into a quieter question: what does it mean to face reality without spinning out or hardening over? From there, it turns to gratitude, adaptation, and the limits of technique, arguing that practice is less about putting out every fire than about cultivating a heart that burns less easily.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening with Dukkha - Matthew begins with the pressure of multiple crises and frames dukkha as suffering layered on suffering, using fire season and smoke as a vivid backdrop.
  2. 02:18 Frozen Lake Image - He turns to grief and gratitude, quoting William James to describe human life as skating on melting ice and needing to study the cliffs.
  3. 04:35 Ordinary Gratitude - A passage from Amy Krauss Rosenthal leads into a practice of seeing ordinary things freshly and appreciating how much goodness is already here.
  4. 06:28 Balancing the Dharma - Matthew recalls prior teaching on balancing compassion and equanimity and broadens it into the tension between two aims in the Dharma.
  5. 07:51 Tradition and Adaptation - He asks how much Dharma teaching should shift with COVID, George Floyd, and the broader social moment, then contrasts traditional fidelity with active adaptation.
  6. 11:07 Teaching with Caution - Matthew speaks about emphasizing grief, love, and refuge while also being wary of projecting his own wobble into the teaching.
  7. 15:10 Not Putting Out Every Fire - He argues that practice does not erase every painful condition, and that sometimes the honest response is simply to suffer with as much grace as possible.
  8. 19:03 Preparing Citta - The talk lands on the idea that the heart can be trained so fires are less likely to ignite, creating well-being that can be offered to others.
