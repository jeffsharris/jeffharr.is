Dharmette: Coming Home and Leaving Home

AudioDharma 12:29 2024-05-08

Dharma practice is framed here as a double movement: coming home to what the heart already knows, and leaving home to learn how to love what is new. The talk moves from recollection and refuge to the harder question of transformation—why change usually cannot be forced by will alone, but grows through small gestures, repeated practice, and revised priorities. A brief story from Alfred Adler and a quotation from Agnes Callard help sharpen that argument, while the closing image is quiet and practical: we rejoice in one home in order to leave it.

Chapters

  1. 00:18 Coming Home - The talk opens by naming Dharma as a return to what is familiar, loved, and long known.
  2. 01:13 Recognition and Refuge - Matthew explores the feeling of recollection, where the teaching seems to awaken something already present in the heart.
  3. 03:23 Loving New Things - He turns to the second movement of the path: learning to value peace, space, and awareness over older habits.
  4. 04:18 More Than Willpower - An Alfred Adler story is used to show that transformation is not solved by simply deciding differently or forcing change.
  5. 06:46 How Values Shift - Drawing on Agnes Callard, he describes change as gradual, shaped by repeated actions, altered priorities, and time.
  6. 09:01 Small Gestures - He offers ordinary practices—quiet sitting, Sangha, reflection, and moments of wakefulness—as the seeds of new love.
  7. 10:24 A New Home - The talk closes with the idea that Dharma slowly becomes home, even as practice continues to open outward.
