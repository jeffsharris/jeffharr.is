What would it be like without narrative?

AudioDharma 05:03 2015-03-25

What would experience feel like without the usual inner commentary? The talk starts with that question and follows it into a quiet inquiry about how story, self-concern, and even the wish to understand can shape and fatigue experience. Rather than rejecting story outright, it points toward a brief, simple dropping of the need to know who we are, what is happening, or what comes next.

Chapters

  1. 00:24 Opening Question - He begins by asking what life would be like without narrative or story.
  2. 01:00 Story and Fatigue - He softens any anti-story stance, while noting how constant story can wear us down.
  3. 02:10 Dropping Below Story - The talk turns to what happens when we try to move beneath self-concern, worry, or ordinary mental chatter.
  4. 03:13 Even Insight Can Grasp - He points out that trying to understand or have insight can itself become another kind of holding.
  5. 04:11 Relinquishing Orientation - The invitation becomes a willingness to release the need to know who we are, what is happening, and what comes next.
  6. 04:52 Simple Exploration - He ends by returning to simplicity, saying that fighting story only creates more story.
