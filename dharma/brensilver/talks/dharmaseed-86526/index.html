Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Throughlines of Self and Not-Self

Dharma Seed 45:29 2024-07-11

How do self and not-self become a lived flexibility rather than a doctrine to defend? The talk begins by reframing practice as a path between pleasantness and necessary discomfort, then moves into a practical argument that the fruit of practice is less suffering, more fulfillment, and a heart that can respond differently to changing conditions. From there, it turns to the balance between self and not-self, using the themes of safety and power to warn against rigid identity, spiritual bypassing, and communities that lose their footing. The latter half focuses on two supports for this balance—renunciation and patience—showing how saying no can make yes trustworthy, and how gradual practice can harden into traits of ease, openness, and care.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the retreat - He begins with a light, conversational opening and frames the first day of retreat as a poor time for conclusions.
  2. 01:17 Pleasure and suffering - The talk lays out practice as a dialectic between pleasant and unpleasant feeling, including the role of wholesome difficulty.
  3. 03:08 The fruit of practice - He describes the fruit of practice as less suffering, more fulfillment, and a kind of delight joined with love and generosity.
  4. 05:42 Necessary suffering - This section distinguishes necessary from unnecessary suffering, including purification, grief, moral sensitivity, and the emotional turbulence of growth.
  5. 10:20 Stress reduction and self - He links dukkha to stress reduction and shifts into how ease is found both in the self-story and in moments when self drops away.
  6. 13:33 Flexibility of awareness - Using Todd Cashton and Ajahn Sucitto, he argues that health and awakening involve flexibility, moving between self and not-self without rigidity.
  7. 20:14 Power and safety - He warns that insight into not-self can increase power, so it must stay balanced with self-understanding and safety for oneself and others.
  8. 24:26 Renunciation and enough - The talk turns to the parami of renunciation, the difficulty of knowing what is enough, and why the ability to say no makes yes trustworthy.
  9. 30:43 Patience through the flood - Drawing on the Thai forest tradition and the Buddha, he presents renunciation and patience as essential disciplines for moving through turbulence without forcing outcomes.
