Releasing Judging, Comparing, Fixing: Landing Here

AudioDharma 38:15 2026-05-25

What happens when the habit of judging, comparing, and fixing drops away long enough for experience to settle on its own? The talk moves from simple questions about what is already still and what needs soothing, into a practice of softening the body and mind so attention can land here without measuring against an ideal. It then opens into a sustained reflection on how comparison creates suffering, and how mindfulness can become just being a body breathing, with less and less reference to self-story.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 What Is Already Still - The talk begins by asking what is already still and what, if anything, needs to be soothed before attention can settle.
  2. 02:24 Softening Toward Arrival - The speaker explores using breath, memory, images, and words as supports that help the mind arrive and become learning-ready.
  3. 07:09 Mind in the Mind - The emphasis turns toward softening posture, body, and mental strain so awareness can be present without trying to correct everything.
  4. 12:41 Breath Without Fixing - Rather than reaching out to fix sensations or thoughts, the practice becomes simply noticing how everything affects the breathing body.
  5. 20:27 Renouncing Comparison - The talk names the suffering built into measuring self against ideals, other moments, or other minds, and points toward a field of awareness uninterested in that comparison.
  6. 23:39 Just a Breathing Body - The inquiry narrows to what it is like to be only a body breathing, with less and less referencing to story or identity.
  7. 29:59 Experience and Experiencer - The closing reflection suggests that when experience is complete, the distinction between experiencer and experience fades, and mindfulness no longer depends on remembering who you are.
