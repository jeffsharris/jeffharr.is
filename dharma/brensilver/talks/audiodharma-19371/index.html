Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation - Love and Grief

AudioDharma 28:09 2023-12-02

This guided meditation begins with settling the posture, then moves into a meditation on how love and grief are bound together rather than opposed. A passage from Leonard Cohen’s “Treaty” opens into a reflection on whether grief itself might be honored as a kind of practice, even a fifth Brahma-vihara, before turning toward impermanence and the possibility of grieving anicca. The talk then grounds those reflections in metta phrases and closes by linking intimate knowledge of our own suffering with a deeper compassion for others.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling the Body - The meditation begins by finding the posture and establishing a calm, embodied starting point.
  2. 07:40 Leonard Cohen's Treaty - A brief quotation from Leonard Cohen frames the wish for reconciliation between love and pain.
  3. 09:09 Starting the Practice - Matthew returns to the posture and reorients the session toward the lived experience of the body.
  4. 11:42 Love and Grief - He reflects on the claim that love and grief are inseparable and suggests grief may deserve more recognition in practice.
  5. 15:17 Grieving Impermanence - Impermanence is named both as a release and, at times, as something we may need to grieve.
  6. 16:33 Opening Into Metta - The meditation gradually turns toward kindness and prepares for a loving-kindness sequence.
  7. 21:23 Wishing Well - He leads classic metta phrases, offering wishes for happiness, safety, and freedom from suffering.
  8. 23:05 Compassion for Others - The closing reflection shows how repeated contact with our own suffering can make others' pain feel more immediately knowable.
