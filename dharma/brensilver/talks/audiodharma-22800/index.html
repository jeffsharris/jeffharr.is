Guided Meditation: Softening the Self

AudioDharma 29:38 2025-04-23

What happens if you stop trying to manage the mind and instead relax around it? This guided meditation begins with posture, then moves through softening, widening, and resting attention on the mind’s brightness without forcing it. The talk returns again and again to the felt knot of selfing, asking how much loosens when clinging, striving, and willfulness are allowed to unwind.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Arrive and Settle - The meditation opens by welcoming listeners, acknowledging the day's condition, and inviting a settled posture.
  2. 01:00 Relax into Brightness - He guides a full-body softening while asking the listener to stay tethered to the mind's natural brightness and luminosity.
  3. 02:40 Held by Love - The practice widens into a sense of shared presence, with repeated reminders to relax even the urge to do more.
  4. 05:17 Clinging and Letting Go - He names the strain of clinging, striving, and willfulness, then asks what remains when those habits are relaxed.
  5. 11:43 The Knot of Self - The talk shifts to the self as a knot of tension and explores how safety reduces trance and grasping.
  6. 15:42 Softening and Widening - Using the image of a snagging hangnail, he repeatedly invites softening and widening until attention feels more spacious.
  7. 21:21 Impermanence and Porosity - He brings in anicca and asks how deeply impermanence runs, pointing to a self that can melt, become porous, and fluid.
  8. 28:37 Closing Reflection - The meditation ends with one more invitation to notice how the self may be grasped or may open into something larger.
