Matthew Brensilver, Sylvia Boorstein: Day 2: Morning Practice Session

Dharma Seed 3:29:26 2018-01-14

Start with the body, then keep widening the circle: this morning session moves from standing warm-ups and qigong into posture, sitting, and a long metta practice. Along the way, the talk keeps returning to one question: how do we stay present with sensation, discomfort, and changing mind states without turning away? The movement is quiet but practical, ending in phrases of goodwill that spread from self, to friend, to familiar stranger, to everyone in the room, and finally to all beings.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and schedule - The session is set up, with a brief orientation to the morning and what will happen next.
  2. 01:01 Tsunami and timing - A retreat memory about a tsunami warning opens into reflection on how meditation changes our sense of time and urgency.
  3. 13:07 Body as entry point - The practice begins with standing, warm-ups, and attention to the body as the most immediate doorway into awareness.
  4. 22:42 Qigong and grounding - Standing movements, balance, and rootedness are explored through qigong and tai chi-style alignment.
  5. 33:38 Posture and support - The talk shifts into practical guidance on sitting posture, cushions, and how to support alertness without strain.
  6. 43:46 Letting go into love - After a quoted reflection on releasing the burden of the heart, the talk turns to metta as concentration, purification, and cultivation.
  7. 01:14:13 Metta and universal goodwill - Stories about boundless love and Poonjaji lead into a guided walk centered on wishing well for self, others, strangers, and all beings.
  8. 01:41:48 Mindfulness and ethics - The discussion returns to the Four Foundations of Mindfulness, impermanence, and the need for wisdom to stay joined with ethical responsibility.
  9. 02:58:54 Guided loving-kindness - A full metta meditation develops in stages, moving from self to benefactor, familiar people, the room, and then the whole world.
