Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Non-possessiveness and Joy

AudioDharma 29:03 2022-05-12

Matthew Brensilver leads a guided meditation on non-possessiveness and sympathetic joy, beginning with settling the body and softening the mind’s need to sort every problem at once. The practice moves from releasing hope and fear into letting go, then into mudita: becoming porous to the happiness of others without comparison or threat. The closing invites an expansive wish for joy to increase for all beings, including those not yet born.

Chapters

  1. 00:25 Settling In - The meditation opens by inviting the body and mind to settle.
  2. 00:55 What Is Here - He points to the many things right and wrong in any moment, and the relief of not having to sort everything out at once.
  3. 02:38 Putting Down the World - The practice asks us to momentarily set down the world, even if that feels unsafe or irresponsible, because it matters to the heart.
  4. 03:38 Hope Into This Moment - He shifts from hope and fear toward resting all hope in this moment and abiding there.
  5. 08:26 Letting Go and Joy - Letting go is framed not only as renunciation or grief, but as a doorway into ease and joy.
  6. 11:08 Porous to Others - The teaching develops mudita, emphasizing a heart that is not territorial, comparative, or threatened by another person’s good fortune.
  7. 14:56 Wishing Happiness Well - The meditation turns toward a specific person, silently wishing their happiness continue and increase while sensing their joy in the body.
  8. 20:43 No Self, Just World - He describes abiding in delight and the dissolving of self-centered separation so the happiness of others is known as our own.
  9. 24:26 Extending the Wish - The closing broadens the wish to all beings, even into the distant future, asking that all available goodness and happiness increase.
