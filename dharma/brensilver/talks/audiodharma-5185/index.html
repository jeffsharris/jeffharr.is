Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Practice Questions

AudioDharma 22:31 2014-10-23

Matthew Brensilver opens this talk as a practice conversation, responding first to anxiety and then to confusion. He frames evening restlessness as the residue of a full day and suggests meeting it in the body without rushing to solve it in thought. Later he turns to uncertainty in meditation, encouraging equanimity with not knowing and a slower, less reactive relationship to progress.

Chapters

  1. 00:09 Opening the practice questions - Matthew invites questions about technique and how to work with whatever is arising in practice.
  2. 02:54 Anxiety as residue - He reframes evening anxiety as the leftover intensity of the day and uses digestion as a way to understand practice.
  3. 06:21 Working with anxious energy - He offers a response to anxiety that emphasizes bodily awareness, letting thoughts unspool, and not immediately turning the feeling into a problem.
  4. 10:38 Choosing another object - He notes that breath or sound may feel less interesting than anxiety, and suggests sometimes using walking meditation or deliberately resting attention elsewhere.
  5. 12:59 What confusion feels like - A second question shifts the talk toward confusion, not knowing, and the discouragement that can come from feeling stuck.
  6. 15:56 Equanimity with not knowing - Matthew describes confusion as a good place to practice tolerance, urging the listener not to fight for answers, progress, or conclusions.
  7. 18:58 Not every breath - He closes by warning against compulsively checking practice after every breath and encourages a slower rhythm of self-assessment.
