Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation - changing conditions

Dharma Seed 47:55 2021-05-22

This guided meditation begins by settling the body and noticing how silence feels, with an emphasis on allowing experience rather than forcing a particular inner posture. It then opens into investigation of hope and fear, dukkha, and the way the mind resists impermanence, using images of water, river, and flow to point toward anicca. The closing movement is gentle and practical: loosening the story of self, resting in change, and recognizing how that can support a more open love.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Settling In - The meditation opens with posture, arriving, and a first quiet settling into the room.
  2. 01:38 Silence and Temperature - He invites close attention to the felt quality of silence and how it lands in the body and mind.
  3. 02:54 Hope and Fear - The practice widens to include emotional weather, especially the landscape of hope and fear, without forcing it away.
  4. 06:25 Body and Sensitivity - Attention returns to bodily ease and a bright, sensitive awareness that can hold shifting emotions gently.
  5. 10:16 Opening to Dukkha - He frames suffering as a basic human pattern and suggests that comprehending it can soften and open the heart.
  6. 13:35 The Weight of Change - The talk dwells on the poignancy of life and the way seeing dukkha more clearly changes the heart's response.
  7. 21:20 Anicca in Experience - Impermanence is explored as something directly known in experience, beyond the idea of breath or conceptual stillness.
  8. 29:25 River Without Banks - Through the image of a river with no banks, he points to the illusion of standing apart from the flow of change.
  9. 38:32 Letting Go to Love - The closing section releases the story of me, honors the momentum already built in practice, and suggests this opens the way to love more radically.
