Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: New Questions

AudioDharma 14:24 2024-04-03

The talk begins with the question of how the Dharma is actually learned, not just explained: how it gets woven into a life, and how our minds change in the presence of others. From there it moves between homecoming and unsettlement, using the image of being both settled and "thrown out of the nest" to show why practice needs a balance of comfort and disequilibrium. It closes by asking whether Dharma can give us words for new questions about self, love, death, and what kind of peace is possible.

Chapters

  1. 00:10 Opening and return - Matthew opens warmly, reflects on returning to teach, and speaks about why he feels drawn back to the seat.
  2. 01:09 Who speaks, who listens - He considers the strangeness of teaching and the way self-understanding changes when we are with others.
  3. 03:02 Sangha and the mind - The talk turns to spiritual friendship, emphasizing that the mind is shaped by conditions and by the company we keep.
  4. 04:46 How learning happens - Matthew shifts into pedagogy, asking how Dharma is actually absorbed and woven into us.
  5. 05:59 Impasses and balance - Using cognitive science, he describes learning as arising from contradictions and the balance between stability and disruption.
  6. 07:57 Home and exile - He develops the image of the path as both homecoming and being thrown out of the nest, with familiar teachings becoming stale if left untouched.
  7. 10:06 Cracks in the frame - The talk deepens into the opening that comes when ordinary reference points loosen and we meet the mystery of another person or the depth of dukkha.
  8. 12:23 Words for new questions - Matthew closes with Judea Pearl's line about questions and language, and frames the Dharma as making room for fresh inquiry into life, love, death, and peace.
