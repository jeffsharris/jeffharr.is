Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Day 1: Metta for Self (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 32:00 2017-05-23

Matthew Brensilver introduces a day of self-directed metta practice by gently distinguishing loving-kindness from mindfulness, then suggesting that the clearer we see human vulnerability, the more natural care becomes. He returns to the Buddha’s instruction to “make of yourself a refuge,” using it to frame self-love as steadiness, safety, and reliability rather than self-indulgence. The talk then moves into the felt practice of repeating simple phrases, listening for their effect in the body, and recognizing that even resistance or harshness can become part of the practice.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening Orientation - Matthew welcomes retreatants, especially newcomers, and sets up the evening practice space.
  2. 00:45 Heart Practices - He introduces metta, forgiveness, and compassion, and contrasts them with mindfulness in a useful but limited way.
  3. 01:45 Seeing More Clearly - He suggests that deeper seeing of human life naturally opens the heart and loosens separation.
  4. 03:55 Refuge and Safety - He cites the Buddha’s teaching on making oneself a refuge and explores what it means to be safe for others.
  5. 05:23 The Wish To Be Happy - He reframes self-love as responding to the universal wish for happiness, safety, and peace.
  6. 07:19 Settling Into Phrases - The talk turns toward practice instructions, including posture, breath, and the repeated phrases of metta.
  7. 13:06 Heart of the Practice - He offers images of parent and child, doctor and patient, and explains the phrases as a way of contacting the heart now.
  8. 16:57 Listening Inwardly - He describes ringing the phrases in the mind and listening for their resonance without forcing a particular feeling.
  9. 25:37 Three Ways of Practice - He closes by outlining metta as cultivation, concentration, and purification, including how to work when difficult states arise.
