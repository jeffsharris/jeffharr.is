Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Resting In Care

AudioDharma 29:34 2023-12-15

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation centered on resting in care, beginning by inviting a felt sense of love, gratitude, compassion, or even grief as a doorway into the heart. From there he explores how care can color the whole body and make each breath feel significant, then turns toward the freedom that comes when love is steady enough to hold what is not love without forcing anything. The practice closes with a gentle wish for release from suffering.

Chapters

  1. 01:06 Settling In - The talk opens with a brief welcome and a transition into stillness.
  2. 02:43 Begin With Love - He invites a starting point of love, gratitude, compassion, joy, equanimity, or grief as access to the heart.
  3. 07:25 Care Flavors Experience - He points out how love, like irritation or sadness, can color everything, even the breath.
  4. 09:39 Body Suffused With Care - The practice deepens into a more embodied sense of tenderness and aliveness spread through the body.
  5. 13:45 Freedom Still Beckons - He reflects on the promise that practice is possible in any moment and that love and freedom are not foreclosed.
  6. 16:25 Steadier Love - The meditation emphasizes letting love become less ambivalent and more stable, without needing force.
  7. 22:04 Holding What Is Not Love - When difficult states arise, they can be placed into a larger container of love rather than fought or suppressed.
  8. 23:23 Closing Wish - The meditation ends with a gentle blessing for freedom from suffering.
