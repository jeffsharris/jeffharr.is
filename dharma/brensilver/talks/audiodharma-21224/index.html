Guided Meditation: Not Predicting Me

AudioDharma 30:08 2024-09-10

What happens when the mind keeps predicting the next moment, and the self feels so familiar it turns invisible? This guided meditation turns toward that habit of self-story and asks what it would be like to meet experience without expecting to find exactly the same person again. The practice moves from noticing the song of “me” to letting it become an object of awareness, then relaxing into breathing and a wider, quieter wakefulness.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Arrival - A brief welcome and invitation to practice together without a fixed plan.
  2. 01:00 Predicting Ourselves - He explores how the mind keeps rehearsing a model of self from moment to moment.
  3. 02:57 Opening to Not-Knowing - The talk widens into questions about what it would be like not to expect the next moment to feel like the same person.
  4. 06:05 The Song of Self - He names the recurring self-story as something that can be heard, noticed, and slightly alienated from.
  5. 10:46 Love It to Death - Using a phrase attributed to Shinzen Young, he suggests meeting selfing with enough familiarity that it loses its grip.
  6. 12:59 Settling Into Breath - The practice turns toward surrendering into breathing as expectations and moral pressure begin to loosen.
  7. 25:21 Global Wakefulness - In the closing instructions, he emphasizes that no story can be a lasting home and invites a wider wakefulness to all of life.
