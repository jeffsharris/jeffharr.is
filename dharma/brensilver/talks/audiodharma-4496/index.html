Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

How We Relate To Each Other

AudioDharma 14:59 2013-11-27

How do the insights of practice actually show up in the way we speak, listen, and meet one another? The talk begins with the holiday season and quickly turns to relationship as the place where meditation is tested, especially through wise speech, careful listening, and the gradual noticing of when speech is out of alignment with deeper intention. It then moves toward a quieter challenge: loosening rigid self-definition so we become less defensive, less easily offended, and more able to receive others fully. A final image of the empty boat brings the teaching into focus, suggesting that when self-clinging thins, reactivity has less to hold onto.

Chapters

  1. 00:14 Holiday Relationship Theme - Matthew introduces the talk as a brief reflection on bringing practice into how we relate to each other during the holiday season.
  2. 00:35 Relational Beings - He describes human beings as fundamentally relational and says interpersonal life is where meditation insight is tested and integrated.
  3. 02:00 Practice in Relationship - He emphasizes that insights from the cushion need time and must take form in ordinary dealings if they are to matter.
  4. 03:10 Wise Speech - Matthew turns to speech as a direct field of practice, citing Joseph Goldstein and the simple standard of speech that is true, kind, and timely.
  5. 04:26 Trust and Feedback - He describes how careful speech creates trust and how mindfulness reveals when words are out of alignment with deeper intention.
  6. 06:50 Deep Listening - Matthew expands the practice to listening, including therapeutic listening, body awareness, and the way being heard can settle another person.
  7. 09:04 Letting Go of Ownership - He shifts to the teaching that nothing should be clung to as I or mine, connecting rigid self-definition with impaired love.
  8. 10:35 The Heavy Vase - Using the image of carrying a precious vase, he shows how self-protection narrows attention and makes us less open to others.
  9. 13:35 The Empty Boat - Matthew closes with Xuanzang's empty-boat teaching, suggesting that when the sense of self is emptied, anger and opposition ease.
