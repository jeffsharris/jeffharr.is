Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Balancing Equanimity and Compassion

Dharma Seed 33:57 2024-09-28

The talk begins with steadiness: settling the body, and noticing how the steadiness of love depends on attention to the world around us. From there it moves into a guided compassion practice—bringing to mind someone who is suffering, sensing their pain without defense, and offering simple blessings such as, “May your suffering be eased.” The second half widens the frame to equanimity, asking how love can stay open without becoming frantic or collapsing in the face of helplessness, gravity, and the limits of what can be changed. It closes by bringing that balance into the everyday gesture of walking and looking down at the ground, letting love flow into the gaze.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling and Intention - The talk begins with settling the mind and framing love as something sustained by steady attention.
  2. 01:30 Preparing the Heart - Matthew invites stability, groundedness, and an attunement to what the heart, mind, and body need right now.
  3. 05:32 Meeting Suffering - Compassion is defined and then cultivated by bringing to mind someone who is suffering and sensing their pain directly.
  4. 12:25 Offering Love - The practice deepens into silently offering blessings, unconditional attention, and wishes for easing suffering.
  5. 18:17 Shared Human Ground - The teaching turns toward tenderness for one's own heart and the recognition that compassion grows from seeing ourselves in others.
  6. 20:28 Compassion and Equanimity - Matthew brings compassion into conversation with equanimity, naming helplessness, vastness, and the need not to collapse.
  7. 27:21 Refining Compulsion - Equanimity is described as helping strip away desperation and other compulsive efforts to force change.
  8. 30:39 Love in Daily Life - The closing invitation is to let this quiet love infuse the gaze and ordinary movement, even when pain remains present.
