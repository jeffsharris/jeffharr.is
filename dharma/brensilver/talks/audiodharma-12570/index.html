Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Sunday Closing Dharmette

AudioDharma 24:05 2020-12-13

The talk asks what awakening looks like when it is measured not only by deep moments, but by the breadth of one’s life that slowly comes under the care of the Dharma. Using Ajahn Sucitto’s line about enlightenment as “death by 1000 cuts,” the speaker shifts attention from dramatic breakthroughs to gradual cultivation, ethical conduct, and the parts of the heart that still remain untouched. From there the talk widens into sila: becoming safe for others, letting goodness ripen through grief and letting go, and seeing how love, responsibility, and moral incompleteness keep unfolding. It closes by inviting listeners to ask what the silence and stillness of practice imply for their conduct in the world.

Chapters

  1. 00:27 Welcome and Sit - Matthew greets the group, outlines the period of silent sitting, and previews the brief talk and closing.
  2. 02:43 Depth and Breadth - He contrasts the usual focus on dramatic awakening with the slower accumulation of practice and goodness.
  3. 04:47 Gradual Cultivation - Using Ajahn Sucitto’s line about enlightenment as 'death by 1000 cuts,' he emphasizes repeated, incremental transformation.
  4. 07:39 Sila as Refuge - He shifts from insight language to conduct, saying breadth shows itself most clearly in ethical life and how we meet the world.
  5. 08:21 Radical Heart - A Buddha quote about making oneself a refuge opens into a fuller account of self-discovery, humility, grief, and care.
  6. 10:16 Love, Debt, Dying - He explores how ethical life keeps widening, how ego complicates goodness, and how mortality clarifies what we have given away.
  7. 12:50 Implications of Stillness - Matthew invites listeners to consider what the silence and softening imply for their conduct, without prescribing a conclusion.
  8. 14:01 Closing Gratitude - He closes with apologies for any shortcomings, candidly reflects on the limits of teaching, and expresses gratitude to the organizers and lineage.
  9. 20:09 Announcements and Break - The manager offers retreat announcements about the social time and donations, followed by a brief logistical close.
