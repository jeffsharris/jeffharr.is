Guided Meditation: Relaxation and the Tension of Dukkha, Self, Clinging

AudioDharma 30:26 2024-06-12

What if the way into practice is not more effort, but a deeper and more honest relaxing? The talk moves from a small image of a tired child asking for the bed that is already there, into a sustained inquiry into how clinging, selfing, greed, hatred, and delusion show up as tension in the body-mind. Through breath, gravity, and repeated softening, the speaker suggests that when attention keeps releasing its grip, what has been held by suffering can finally be felt and let go.

Chapters

  1. 00:14 Opening story - Matthew begins with a family story that turns into a playful question about where awareness and the Dharma are found.
  2. 01:43 Relaxation and suffering - He introduces the idea of awakening through deep relaxation and names tension as a signature of greed, hatred, selfing, and suffering.
  3. 05:36 Right here to sit - The talk turns toward the immediacy of practice, with the reminder that the relief we seek may be close at hand.
  4. 07:23 Let tension speak - He suggests allowing tension permission to be present, while using breath and awareness to invite ease rather than struggle.
  5. 10:21 Gravity and ease - The guidance shifts into body-based support, especially the soothing downward pull of gravity and an upright but released posture.
  6. 13:35 Self as contraction - Matthew explores selfing as a dense, contractive force and invites a relinquishing of ownership, proving, and defensiveness.
  7. 18:28 Clinging and hate - He describes clinging as complicated but simplifying when released, then turns to why hatred cannot ultimately work.
  8. 21:42 Delusion and mortality - The meditation widens to delusion, disappointment, and the turning toward death as a way of softening attention.
  9. 24:15 Putting things down - Drawing on Shinzen Young's phrase, Matthew closes by encouraging continued softening until awareness feels weightless.
