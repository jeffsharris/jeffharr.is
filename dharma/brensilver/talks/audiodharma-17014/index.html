Dharmette: The Pleasure of Spiritual Friendship

AudioDharma 14:36 2022-09-30

What if friendship is part of the path itself, not just a pleasant support around it? The talk moves from the idea of Dharma pleasures into the particular joy of spiritual friendship: being seen clearly in both strength and imperfection, and learning to direct attention toward goodness. With a brief reflection on Sangha and a line from Kinnell about reteaching a thing its loveliness, it settles into the claim that friends help us remember what is beautiful, urgent, and worth practicing.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the Theme - Matthew introduces the talk as part of a series on Dharma pleasures and names friendship as the focus for this dharmette.
  2. 01:02 Seeing and Being Seen - He uses a colleague’s definition of intimacy to frame friendship as mutual visibility, including being known at both our best and worst.
  3. 02:58 Known in Imperfection - The talk turns to primary relationships and the need to be seen clearly, both lovingly and honestly, without hiding our flaws or our strengths.
  4. 04:59 Sangha and Goodness - Matthew describes Sangha as a community of spiritual friends whose attention is directed toward goodness, and he quotes Kinnell on reteaching things their loveliness.
  5. 06:17 Shaping the Mind - He reflects on how porous and suggestible we are, and how our surroundings quietly train our views toward wealth, power, wisdom, or love.
  6. 07:57 Reminders of Dharma - Using a road trip through California as an example, he suggests we need conditions and friends that keep reconnecting us to wisdom and the urgency of practice.
  7. 09:48 Connoisseurs of Goodness - Matthew compares practice to developing an eye for art: we become more sensitive to beauty in others and more able to revere the same things together.
  8. 11:43 Closing Blessing - He ends by speaking about teaching as mutual recognition, thanking listeners and volunteers, and resting the talk in gratitude and goodwill.
