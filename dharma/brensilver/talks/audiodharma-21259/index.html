Dharmette: Interpersonal Dharma (5 of 5): A Training in Loving Well

AudioDharma 16:21 2024-09-13

Did I love well? The talk begins with that question and then shifts away from a Dharma framed mainly as moment-by-moment choice toward something slower: training the heart over time. From there it moves through ethical sensitivity, attention, poise, and wisdom as quiet habits that make loving well more natural and less forced. Along the way, the speaker argues that practice is less about sudden breakthrough than the gradual melting of self-deception, defensiveness, and harm in ordinary interpersonal life.

Chapters

  1. 00:26 Did I love well? - Matthew opens with Jack Kornfield’s question and shows how it became a formative way of understanding practice.
  2. 01:22 Beyond simple choice - He questions a Dharma model centered mainly on instant choices, control, and doing, and turns toward training and gradual change.
  3. 03:50 Small gestures of the heart - Practice is described as prevention, habit formation, and trusting the path through patient, repeated movements of the heart.
  4. 05:45 Training for relationship - He applies the training view to interpersonal life, noting that the Dharma should make us less reactive and more of a refuge for others.
  5. 08:05 Ethics and remorse - Sīla is presented as increasing sensitivity to harm, helping remorse become clearer and intention more steady.
  6. 09:59 Love as attention - Turning to mind training, he defines love as the willingness to pay attention and connects that to stable, receptive awareness.
  7. 12:20 Impermanence makes love urgent - He links poise, finitude, and anicca to a love that becomes more urgent and more grounded in reality.
  8. 14:02 Stepping out of self-deception - Wisdom practice is framed as seeing craving, aversion, and self-deception more clearly so we can love with less defensiveness.
  9. 15:45 Slow change, continued - Matthew closes by emphasizing the quiet, gradual accumulation of strength in the heart and offering the teaching for reflection.
