Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation

AudioDharma 29:48 2022-09-26

This guided meditation begins by settling into a sustainable posture and then asking the listener to let the Dharma, muscle memory, and prior practice lead rather than the thinking mind. The attention is gently tilted toward whatever brings tranquility and ease — the breath, sensations in the hands or feet, the darkness behind closed eyes, even ambient noise — so the mind can take its cues from silence and soothing presence. From there, the talk rests in the idea that tranquility can help digest what is unfinished or tender in the heart, while also staying with the wholesomeness of intention and the wish to wake up. In the end, it returns to a simple gesture of awareness and letting time be an ally.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling In - The meditation begins with a brief welcome and a direction to find a sustainable posture and settle the body.
  2. 00:48 Letting Practice Lead - The instruction turns toward allowing Dharma, habit, and prior practice to guide the body rather than the thinking mind.
  3. 02:18 Opening to Stillness - Attention is invited to quiet, silence, and the felt sense of being here as the meditation deepens.
  4. 06:12 Finding Soothing Objects - He suggests noticing phenomena that bring ease, such as the breath, bodily sensations, or the darkness behind closed eyes.
  5. 08:19 Resting In Ease - The practice continues in silence with repeated reminders to let the mind take cues from calming objects of attention.
  6. 12:41 Tranquility and Healing - He names tranquility as healing and as a way for the heart's unfinished places and transient aches to be held in awareness.
  7. 14:43 Goodness of Intention - The teaching widens to include whatever is arising, along with the reassurance that the intention to wake up is itself soothing and good.
  8. 19:55 Time As Ally - Near the end, he emphasizes that practice is not a project, but a way of letting time and awareness do their work.
