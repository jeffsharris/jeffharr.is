Samvega and Love of the World

AudioDharma 15:11 2022-04-15

What if disenchantment with the world and love for the world are not opposites but two parts of the same path? The talk begins with the paradox of crossing over the flood without pushing forward or staying in place, then turns to samvega—spiritual urgency, shock, and dismay at the limits of ordinary life—as a force that can loosen our grip on samsara. From there it asks how renunciation, especially in lay practice, can make room not for indifference but for deeper care, until letting go becomes a space filled with love for suffering beings.

Chapters

  1. 01:28 Crossing the flood - A sutta-style exchange introduces the paradox of moving without pushing or freezing in place.
  2. 02:30 Disenchantment and care - Matthew names the tension between seeing suffering clearly and still caring for the world.
  3. 04:23 Lay and monastic paths - He reflects on how a lay path may differ from monastic renunciation while still honoring the lineage.
  4. 06:19 What samvega means - Thanissaro Bhikkhu’s definition of samvega frames the shock, urgency, and dismay that can energize practice.
  5. 09:00 Not building on quicksand - The talk turns toward giving up false refuges in pleasure and identity, and toward the hard question of enough.
  6. 10:53 Love after letting go - Matthew argues that loosening our grip on samsara can make room for deeper care and compassion.
  7. 13:12 A robe wide enough - He closes with a poetic image of compassion that reaches toward all suffering beings, even while knowing it cannot be complete.
