Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Monday Night Dharma Talk

Dharma Seed 59:30 2019-03-25

Matthew Brensilver frames mindfulness as a living curiosity rather than a duty, arguing that practice begins where the student actually is. He then develops that theme through investigation, honesty, and the willingness to look closely at craving, delusion, secrecy, shame, and the ways we manage our self-image. The talk closes by turning outward: other people, especially trusted feedback, help reveal blind spots and make the heart more transparent and trustworthy.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Curiosity Over Discipline - Brensilver begins by contrasting curiosity with discipline and saying practice needs a personal spark rather than mere adherence.
  2. 05:49 The Engine of Practice - He uses Tejaniya’s framing of the seven factors of awakening to show that mindfulness, investigation, and energy are the causes, while the calmer factors are effects.
  3. 10:47 Do Not Force Interest - He describes how practice cannot be faked or imposed, and brings in Michelle McDonald to suggest that painful material should only be approached when genuine interest is present.
  4. 15:13 Investigation as Learning - The talk broadens into the difference between passive listening and active inquiry, including Joseph Goldstein’s line about not knowing one has a mind before practice.
  5. 25:25 Seeing Suffering Clearly - He turns to greed, aversion, and delusion, then to the three characteristics, emphasizing that investigation means seeing experience as fleeting, ungovernable, and not worth clinging to.
  6. 32:58 Inquiry and Distance - Brensilver distinguishes investigation from rumination and draws on a psychology idea of self-distancing to describe how meditation can create enough space to look without becoming detached.
  7. 38:41 Secrets and Honesty - He explores secrecy, shame, and deception, using Bella DePaulo, Sam Harris, Rilke, and Gil Fronsdal to argue that mindfulness is a practice of honesty and transparency.
  8. 47:12 Feedback and Disorientation - The final section emphasizes learning from other people, especially criticism, and becoming willing to feel disoriented as the old story of self loosens.
