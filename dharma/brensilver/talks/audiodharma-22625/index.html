Guided Meditation: Mostly Quiet Sit

AudioDharma 34:14 2025-03-21

If you cannot stop awareness, the talk begins, rest there: it turns the meditation away from force and toward what is already present. From there it moves through possible anchors in breath, body, soundscape, and feeling tone, then keeps returning to a simple refrain: soften, widen, relax. Later, the speaker invites sound itself to pass through the head without making inner speech special, and closes by linking careful attention with love, so that what is heavy can become lighter and more spacious.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Rest with awareness - The meditation opens by asking listeners to stop fighting awareness and rest with what remains present.
  2. 04:48 Choose an anchor - He offers several possible anchors, including breath, body, soundscape, and hedonic tone.
  3. 06:54 Ease the selfing - The practice turns toward noticing how self-identified effort, pressure, and patrol can accumulate in the field of awareness.
  4. 09:56 Soften, widen, relax - A long, repeated period of instruction settles into simple cues to soften, widen, and relax without pushing experience.
  5. 24:42 Include inner sound - He suggests listening to auditory thinking as sound, without making it special or pushing it away.
  6. 30:09 Heavy becomes light - The closing teaching uses a poetic line about burden, emptiness, and love to frame difficult experience as something to be held carefully.
