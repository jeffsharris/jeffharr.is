Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Lightly Guided Meditation

AudioDharma 30:26 2022-04-15

The talk moves from settling the body and taking fuller breaths into a simple question of receptivity: can the heart become available to experience rather than brace against it? From there, the speaker connects softening and relaxing with seeing each moment as dharma, where acceptance deepens into gratitude and even pleasure and pain are held as gifts. The later movement turns toward judgment, self-worth, and self-indictment, loosening them into a larger field of presence in which innocence, care, and love feel increasingly natural.

Chapters

  1. 00:30 Settling In - The meditation begins by inviting the body to straighten, settle, and take a few fuller breaths.
  2. 02:29 Becoming Available - Matthew uses Thich Nhat Hanh’s language of becoming available to the Dharma to point toward softening and relaxing into receptivity.
  3. 06:47 Each Moment as Gift - As the heart becomes available, each moment is framed as Dharma and gratitude becomes the deepest form of acceptance.
  4. 11:23 Beyond Judgment - The focus turns to self-worth, deserving, and judgment, with an emphasis on a vast, non-moralistic field of presence.
  5. 19:13 Basic Innocence - He speaks about how indicting the mind and experience obscures the basic innocence of being.
  6. 20:25 Releasing the Past - The guidance gently opens toward letting go of what might have been and becoming radically open in this breath.
  7. 22:00 Awareness Holds It - Aversion, self-judgment, and greed are described as folded into awareness itself, where letting go feels natural.
  8. 24:56 Love Flavors Everything - The closing movement leans toward love, tenderness for the human condition, and the sense that one drop of love flavors everything.
