Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Attention and Anicca, Awareness and Anatta

AudioDharma 44:05 2024-12-20

What happens when attention is treated as love, and then that same attention is turned back to expose impermanence and not-self? The talk moves from a humorous story about trying to “fix” a partner, through Simone Weil, William James, and retreat practice, into a careful distinction between attention and awareness. From there it asks whether practice is really about control at all, or about learning to rest in what cannot be governed, until the sense of a centered self begins to loosen.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 From Fixing to Listening - Matthew opens with a comic story about trying to solve a partner’s problems before turning to the simpler discipline of truly listening.
  2. 02:22 Attention as Love - He links listening, meditation, and love, then introduces Simone Weil’s idea that attention can function like prayer.
  3. 05:43 Unmixed Attention - Using Weil more fully, he describes attention purified of craving and selfing as something that sanctifies ordinary experience.
  4. 09:51 Right Now, Like This - Ajahn Sumedho’s phrase and reflections on conscious experience lead into a contrast between what we feel and the deeper currents beneath awareness.
  5. 16:00 What Awareness Means - A story about asking Shinzen Young what awareness is opens a discussion of attention, awareness, and the different emphases of Buddhist lineages.
  6. 21:33 How Attention Shapes Experience - He draws on the Buddha, William James, and attention science to describe attention as the condition through which phenomena, concentration, and training arise.
  7. 28:44 Attention and Impermanence - The talk turns to anicca, the costs of effortful practice, and the way attention can strengthen the sense of an agent at the center of experience.
  8. 34:35 Awareness Without Control - A child’s cry in the night leads into a gentler account of awareness as dependent on conditions rather than personal will, and of love as seeing the three characteristics clearly.
  9. 40:21 Surrendering the Center - The close shifts into a deeper relinquishment of the narrative self, with a philosophical note on silent, non-conceptual awareness and the innocence of self-reappearance.
