Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Sangha & Self

AudioDharma 29:09 2025-03-05

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation on Sangha and self, beginning with a brief reflection on why community matters for Dharma practice. He draws on Agnes Callard’s Open Socrates to frame wisdom as something we arrive at with others, then leads the group into a meditation on mutual dependence, self-view, and the subtle ways identity organizes experience. The practice opens toward noticing how the sense of “I” is built, and how that can soften when seen clearly.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and context - Matthew greets the group, responds briefly to a question about substitute teachers, and explains why the evening will focus on community and practice together.
  2. 03:19 Open Socrates - He introduces Agnes Callard’s Open Socrates and uses its argument about needing others to think well as a bridge into Dharma practice.
  3. 05:53 The jewel of Sangha - He names Sangha as a true jewel, equal to Buddha and Dharma, and invites the group to feel the goodness of collective practice before settling in.
  4. 06:58 Mutual dependence - The meditation begins with breath and goodwill, emphasizing that we cannot wake up alone and that we stand on one another’s shoulders.
  5. 10:44 Seeing self-view - The teaching turns toward self-models, self-view, and the way another person can help reveal assumptions that are usually hidden.
  6. 19:46 Noticing the vantage point - Matthew points to the ways experience is organized around a presumed vantage point and encourages dis-identifying from it through careful noticing.
  7. 24:25 Subtle images and words - The close narrows in on the subtle feeling, words, and images that become the center of gravity of 'I am-ness' when unnoticed, and empty when seen.
