Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette 1 - Aspects of Love

AudioDharma 21:15 2023-12-02

Matthew Brensilver explores love as more than a feeling: a bodily tone, a state of mind, and a way perception itself gets colored. He moves from the difference between intense and quiet forms of love into how love changes attention, softens self-absorption, and alters what seems prominent or threatening. The talk closes by inviting a simple practice of “ringing the bell” of metta and noticing the reverberations, with the Brahma-viharas framed as a complete set of medicine for experience.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Love Beyond Feeling - Matthew opens with the idea that love is more than emotion alone, drawing from a retreat anecdote to frame the talk.
  2. 01:05 Feeling and Mind State - He describes love as a mind state that widens attention, changes motivation, and is joined with other wholesome qualities.
  3. 02:36 Where Love Lives - The talk turns to love as a bodily experience, with examples from research on how different forms of love are felt in the body.
  4. 04:59 Intensity and Quietness - Matthew distinguishes intense, full-bodied love from quieter forms, using the image of distant stars to point to a subtler metta.
  5. 07:04 Feeling Shapes Consciousness - He brings in Lisa Feldman Barrett and then the Buddha’s teaching on vedana to connect feeling tones, arousal, and lived consciousness.
  6. 09:32 Love Infuses the View - The teaching shifts to how love and motivation are not separate from knowing, and how love can interrupt suffering and self-absorption.
  7. 14:00 Space, Bias, and Fear - Matthew links love with spaciousness, describes how perception of others changes, and names love as an antidote to fear.
  8. 17:57 Practice and Return - He invites a practical pause to notice love directly, then closes by naming the Brahma-viharas as a complete set of responses to life.
