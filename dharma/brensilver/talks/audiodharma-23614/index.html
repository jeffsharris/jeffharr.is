Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Lightly Guided Meditation

AudioDharma 25:53 2025-08-13

What happens when meditation starts not with effort, but with a felt sense of goodness? The talk moves from goodwill and the “longing for less suffering” into a gentle practice of settling the body, narrowing attention without hostility, and resting with breathing, sound, and body as the foreground while everything else is left in the background. It then opens into a broader reflection on how trust in goodness can make the future feel less intimidating and make rest feel possible, even when care and responsibility have been running hard. Near the end, the talk turns to the need for cycles of love and rest, suggesting that always staying on duty can erode the very capacity one wants to preserve.
