Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Relax and release

AudioDharma 26:45 2025-10-29

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet guided meditation on relaxing body and mind, with attention to the ways future-thinking, control, and spiritual striving can tighten experience. The practice moves toward letting go more deliberately, including a gentle but sometimes firmer sense of renunciation, and then into refuge and a simple resting with meaning-making set aside. The talk stays close to the felt experience of this moment, returning again and again to stability, clarity, and enoughness.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Settling In - An opening invitation to relax body and mind and begin the practice.
  2. 01:00 Present Moment - The talk turns toward the burden of future-thinking and the invitation to fall into what is here now.
  3. 03:30 Steady Attention - Attention is directed skillfully to support stability, clarity, and alertness without becoming rigid.
  4. 05:49 Not Clinging - The guidance warns against turning technique itself into another form of grasping, control, or aversion.
  5. 08:48 Renouncing Effort - Brensilver contrasts endless problem-solving with the practice of letting go, which may be gentle or firm.
  6. 10:05 What We Carry - The meditation reflects on the habits of ego and the ways we try to keep things together.
  7. 13:18 Taking Refuge - The instruction shifts toward Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha as a refuge that is not dependent on circumstances.
  8. 18:23 No Need to Mean - The practice opens into a quieter stance of not insisting on meaning and letting things be just what they are.
  9. 20:11 Closing Rest - The meditation closes in silence and a soft settling, with the final guidance thinning to almost nothing.
