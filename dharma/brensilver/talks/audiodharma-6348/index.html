Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Morning Instructions - Awareness and Objects

AudioDharma 46:23 2016-01-17

The talk begins with the habit of trying to get somewhere, then gently turns toward relaxing that effort and offering awareness to whatever is already here, with no strings attached. From there it follows a simple practice movement: notice what’s present, let it be known, and trust that this shift from identification to knowing brings a quiet kind of freedom. In the later discussion, the talk returns to how language can help the mind wake up from being caught in fear, envy, or self-story and settle into direct experience.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 The Striving Mind - He begins by describing the deep habit of trying to get somewhere and the pressure to squeeze something from the present moment.
  2. 01:58 Relaxing Into Practice - The instruction shifts toward releasing ideas of progress, insight, and concentration, and toward offering the heart to whatever is present.
  3. 05:45 No Strings Attached - He emphasizes a steady instruction to let awareness meet whatever is arising without using attention as a means to an end.
  4. 14:00 Courage and Heart - The teaching turns toward the courage needed to open to challenge and the often underestimated capacity of the heart.
  5. 20:40 Simple, Relaxed, Wise - After a long stretch of silence, he returns with a reminder that forgetfulness is not personal failure and awareness is not a personal achievement.
  6. 31:18 Questions on Study - A brief discussion follows about the role of reading and study, and how practice eventually asks for quiet rather than more teachings.
  7. 37:21 Being Known - A question about the phrase ‘being known’ opens a clearer explanation of how experience can be related to as something known rather than something possessed.
  8. 39:03 Language and Right View - The exchange deepens into how careful language, right view, and direct experience can loosen identification and support freedom.
  9. 44:06 Closing the Retreat Day - He ends with practical retreat announcements and a gentle note of encouragement to stay sincere and see what unfolds.
