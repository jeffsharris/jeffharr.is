Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation on Body and Affect (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 59:22 2022-05-17

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation that begins with relaxing the face, shoulders, and belly, then widens into breath and whole-body awareness. From there, the talk turns toward affect: how feeling signals alarm, how vigilance and grasping show up in the body, and how equanimity can let experience remain without needing to fix it. In the second half, he shifts into retreat practice more broadly, especially the challenge of carrying mindfulness into walking, eating, and the ordinary moments where habits and self-consciousness most easily return.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Relaxing the body - He guides a progressive softening of the face, shoulders, and belly, asking the listener to relax whatever can be relaxed without forcing the rest.
  2. 05:14 Whole-body breath - Attention widens to the aliveness of the body and the breath sensed across the whole field of sensation.
  3. 10:31 Smoothing intensity - The breath is used to smooth out bodily intensities and dullness, preparing for a closer look at feeling.
  4. 15:44 Affect and alarm - He names the alarm bells of affect, the sense that something must be done, and invites equanimity toward that layer of experience.
  5. 18:56 Permission to settle - The instruction deepens into radical permission and a gentler surrender of vigilance and compulsive prediction.
  6. 22:02 Releasing grasping - He turns to the impact of clinging and thinking on the feeling body and points to the possibility of being awake without building identity around these impulses.
  7. 26:32 Continuity beyond the cushion - The talk broadens into the ethics of practice in retreat life, including the reminder that the world does not need a self, but does need you.
  8. 41:33 Mindfulness and eating - He explores how suffering hides in habits and then gives a candid, humorous teaching on eating meditation, shame, distraction, and the gap between pleasures.
  9. 53:31 Retreat instructions - He closes by encouraging grounding, honesty, and flexible mindfulness, then offers practical retreat reminders about silence, movement, and meeting times.
