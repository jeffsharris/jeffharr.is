Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Passivity & Action

AudioDharma 15:52 2023-12-14

Is equanimity really a kind of passivity, or can receptivity make action wiser? The talk argues that retreat-like experiments in letting go are not an end in themselves, but a way to see what our habits cost, what they buy us, and how to act with more clarity. From there it moves into impermanence, craving, self-knowledge, and a practical "theory of love" that helps us understand our own limits and other people’s fragility.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Equanimity Is Not Apathy - He opens by correcting the idea that equanimity means passivity and frames it as a relationship to experience rather than indifference to the world.
  2. 01:25 Passivity in Service - Matthew says receptivity is often a practice that prepares us for more skillful action, not a refusal of action itself.
  3. 02:25 Retreat as Experiment - He describes retreat as a radical experiment in giving up choices in order to see what the present moment actually offers and what our habits cost us.
  4. 04:30 Action Becomes More Effective - The talk turns to how practice refines our inner life, making our actions more efficient and helping us respond from clarity rather than compulsion.
  5. 06:22 Seeing Impermanence Clearly - Drawing on the Buddha, he argues that understanding dukkha, impermanence, and craving makes action more realistic and less driven by fantasy.
  6. 08:06 Forecasting and Love - He brings in Alison Gopnik to suggest that we are continually developing theories of love, self, and other people in order to make better choices.
  7. 10:11 Knowing Our Limits - Matthew emphasizes non-moralistic self-knowledge, including the limits of our equanimity and the need to respect our own clinging and triggers.
  8. 12:17 Respecting Others Carefully - He extends the same discernment outward, urging care around other people's fragility and the ways goodwill can reduce conflict.
  9. 14:24 A Virtuous Cycle - The talk closes by returning to the idea that practice shapes the mind, the mind shapes life, and receptivity and action belong together.
