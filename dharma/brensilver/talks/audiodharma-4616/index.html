Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Anxiety

AudioDharma 41:54 2014-02-13

Anxiety here is treated less as a private flaw than as a mix of useful alarm, restless thinking, and an uneasy hope that worrying will somehow keep life under control. The talk moves from fear on the edge of a cliff, to the joke of a 3 a.m. new worry, to psychological research on how worry stays trapped in future scenarios and overestimates what it can predict. From there, it turns toward practice: loosening the grip on control, working with uncertainty, untangling thought from body sensations, and learning that safety comes more from relinquishing than from finding the perfect path.

Chapters

  1. 00:09 What Anxiety Feels Like - He begins with a close look at the felt sense of anxiety as restless, changing thought layered over a deeper hum of worry.
  2. 04:34 Fear and Alarm - Matthew distinguishes fear from anxiety, using an Ajahn Chah example to show fear as a useful response to immediate danger.
  3. 10:12 Worry as a Habit - A short Onion parody introduces the strange productivity of worry and sets up the psychological discussion that follows.
  4. 12:23 Future-Making Mind - Drawing on Thomas Borkovec, he explains worry as self-talk aimed at preventing imagined harm and notes how it can keep life lived in illusion.
  5. 17:00 Forks in the Road - He questions the fantasy that one decisive choice will solve everything and reframes the first noble truth as a more honest relationship to uncertainty.
  6. 23:00 Groundlessness and Practice - Using Joseph Goldstein and mindfulness research, he points toward learning safety amid change rather than trying to secure fixed ground.
  7. 28:16 Working with Anxiety - Matthew compares exposure therapy with mindfulness, then describes how attention can untangle thought, image, and body sensation when anxiety becomes overwhelming.
  8. 35:06 Honesty and Relinquishment - He closes by naming shame and the fear of being found out, then returns to the idea that care for the heart and relinquishment are the deeper forms of safety.
  9. 39:54 Closing Dedication - A brief closing sit and dedication of benefit ends the talk in a restrained, wishing tone.
