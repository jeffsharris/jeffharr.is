Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Samadhi and Insight (a few pointers)

Dharma Seed 51:26 2010-01-21

What if samadhi is not a narrow effort to hold attention, but a whole-body settling that supports insight? The talk moves from correcting the usual idea of concentration, to showing how samadhi, emptiness practice, and even the hindrances all belong in one larger field of training. Along the way, the speaker keeps returning to a practical question: how do we work without self-judgment, and let the mind become steadier, softer, and less fabricated?

Chapters

  1. 00:01 What Samadhi Means - Burbea defines samadhi as unified, settled well-being rather than just contracted concentration, and shifts it from an on-off achievement to a spectrum.
  2. 02:45 Parallel Retreat Practices - He explains why the retreat is asking practitioners to cultivate emptiness and samadhi side by side, not as competing aims but as complementary avenues.
  3. 05:13 Why It Matters - Using the Majjhima Nikaya and the river Ganges simile, he argues that samadhi naturally inclines toward liberation and gives the mind malleability, steadiness, and subtlety.
  4. 09:42 Well-Being and Cushioning - He describes how samadhi cushions fear around emptiness, softens the heart, and creates a good climate for investigation, while warning against shrinking practice into mere performance.
  5. 17:50 Working With Hindrances - Burbea turns to practical obstacles—restlessness, dullness, doubt, self-judgment, impatience—and reframes them as part of the training rather than personal failure.
  6. 26:13 Attention and the Body - He offers concrete ways to work with sinking, drifting, tightness, and over-effort, emphasizing the whole body, receptive attention, and embodied metta.
  7. 34:45 Body, Energy, and Letting Go - The talk develops the role of bodily feeling and subtle energy in samadhi, then shows how emptiness practices can loosen blocks and let samadhi arise.
  8. 42:09 Balancing Samadhi and Insight - He cautions against imbalance in either direction, briefly names jhana/dhyana, and keeps returning to kindness, non-comparison, and a healthy relationship to practice.
  9. 46:54 Samadhi and Emptiness - In the closing section, Burbea explores a deeper link between samadhi and emptiness: as fabrication decreases, self and world are built less, and the practitioner learns to rest in a lighter, freer sense of experience.
