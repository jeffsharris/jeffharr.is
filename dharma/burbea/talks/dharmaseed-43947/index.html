Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Shaping Vessels

Dharma Seed 13:48 2017-04-02

Rob Burbea closes a retreat by asking what actually supports practice once people leave the retreat container. He reflects on loneliness, vulnerability, and the difficulty of sharing what feels most alive, then explores vessels for practice: silent retreat, study, dyads, imaginal sharing, and careful speech. The talk gradually turns toward how intimacy and mutuality can sanctify experience and help practice keep growing in ordinary life.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening Thanks - He begins by thanking the group for their practice and the quality of their participation in the retreat.
  2. 00:41 Practice Beyond Retreat - He asks what supports practice, growth, and soulmaking after retreat ends, framing the central question of the talk.
  3. 02:27 Loneliness In Dharma - He reflects on how dedicated practitioners can still feel isolated, especially when what matters most is hard to share.
  4. 05:58 Vulnerability And Simplicity - A story about a dog opening her belly becomes a contrast to human guardedness and complexity.
  5. 07:55 Kinds Of Vessel - He names different containers for practice, including retreat, study, discussion, and the sharing of images.
  6. 09:45 Temenos For Images - He introduces the idea of temenos, or sacred holding, and shows how structured sharing can deepen imaginal perception.
  7. 11:34 Right Speech Reconsidered - He turns to right speech as something that shapes consciousness and perception, not just conduct.
  8. 13:10 Closing Question - He ends by returning to the practical question of what will support the life of practice going forward.
