Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Working With The Emotional Body (Instructions and Guided Meditation: Day Six)

Dharma Seed 50:42 2011-08-04

This guided meditation and teaching explores emotional practice through the simple move of allowing experience to be exactly as it is. Rob Burbea begins with posture, care, and a strong emphasis on kindness, then develops the idea that aversion and grasping add much of the suffering we feel. He goes on to connect this with anger, fear, the second arrow, and a deeper reflection on how relationship helps shape what we experience.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling and kindness - The talk opens as a guided sitting, grounding in posture, balance, and a sense of practice as kindness rather than self-measurement.
  2. 03:21 Meeting what is prominent - Attention turns to whatever is most present in the body or mind, with instructions to draw close and know it intimately.
  3. 08:17 Allowing as practice - Burbea develops allowing as full welcoming and softening, repeating it as the central gesture of the meditation.
  4. 15:15 Softening and letting go - The instructions continue through welcoming, melting, and letting go, with a strong emphasis on presence over precision.
  5. 24:45 Anger and fear - He pauses for questions and then explains how anger and fear are sustained by aversion, contraction, and a wish to push experience away.
  6. 32:23 The second arrow - The teaching returns to the frame of suffering and reactivity, using the second-arrow image to show how allowing eases added pain.
  7. 35:09 Relationship shapes experience - Burbea moves into a more subtle point: experience is affected by the way it is met, and he links this to emptiness and the mind's way of looking.
  8. 41:27 Letting be, not control - He warns against turning allowing into a hidden strategy and offers alternative words like welcoming, embracing, and letting be.
  9. 46:07 Doership and closing advice - The close touches on allowing even the wish for things to go away, the fading of doership, and a brief practical reminder for the rest of the day.
