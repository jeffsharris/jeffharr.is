Breathing with the Energy Body (Guided Meditation)

Dharma Seed 44:03 2019-12-19

A guided exploration of the energy body begins with posture, then widens into whole-body awareness, asking how the breath affects texture, tone, vibration, and spaciousness. The practice keeps returning to a gentle, comfortable breath and then tours different points in the body—navel, solar plexus, throat, crown, heart, sacrum—checking what ripples out from each. Near the end, the talk steps back to clarify that there is no single right sequence, only experimentation with what supports well-being and samadhi.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling the posture - The meditation begins by adjusting posture, sensing contact and uprightness, and noticing how posture and mind shape one another.
  2. 02:50 Whole-body awareness - Attention is widened over the whole body space and the invitation is to notice texture, vibration, and the energy of the body.
  3. 05:38 Long breath and expansion - The breath is encouraged to become long, slow, and comfortable while the whole energy body is felt as expanding, contracting, and being energized.
  4. 09:32 Breath at the navel - The practice shifts into a tour of imagined breath entering near the navel, using the image to notice how different breaths affect the whole field.
  5. 13:14 Moving through the midline - The breath point is moved to the right side, then the midline below the navel and up toward the solar plexus, with attention to what feels right.
  6. 17:53 Heart and throat tour - The tour continues upward through the heart, then the front of the throat, exploring ripples, currents, and the effect on the whole body space.
  7. 23:46 Crown, neck, and back - The breath is imagined at the crown, then at the base of the neck and the back of the heart, with emphasis on imagination, kinesthetic feeling, and responsiveness.
  8. 34:07 Sacrum and release - The tour reaches the sacrum and tailbone, then returns to whole-space awareness and lets the breath settle into whatever it wants to do.
  9. 37:20 Practice reflections - After the meditation, Burbea explains that there is no fixed order, that the energy body practice offers many possibilities, and that other supports like mettā can be used as secondary ingredients when helpful.
