Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Aspects of the Imaginal (Part 5)

Dharma Seed 1:22:43 2017-12-19

How can imaginal practice be fuller than self-improvement, and what changes when the image is not there for my growth but for soul-making itself? The talk works through that question by distinguishing intention, autonomy, two-ness, and the way meaning emerges without collapsing an image into a single interpretation. It moves from a reflection on eros, logos, and divinity into concrete examples—a rapturous Dartmoor image, a warrior god figure, and a flying dragon—to show how images can open into deeper relationship, not just symbolism.

Chapters

  1. 00:35 Fullness of Intention - Burbea defines the 12th element as an intention that moves beyond self-growth toward soul-making, divinity, and Buddha-nature.
  2. 18:07 Autonomy and Dependent Arising - He introduces the imaginal other as autonomous while keeping dependent arising as the underlying Buddhist frame.
  3. 23:18 Dartmoor Tentacles - A walk on Dartmoor becomes an example of an image that is rapturous without meaning union or dissolution.
  4. 27:45 Two-ness and Free Choice - He extends the discussion to self and other, then brings in freedom, necessity, and the role of intention in soulmaking.
  5. 35:00 Ethics and Freedom - Viktor Frankl’s testimony is used to show how ethical choice can remain present even under extreme constraint.
  6. 40:26 Soft Edges of Being - Burbea develops the 14th element: boundaries in imaginal perception are indefinite, soft, and elastic rather than fixed.
  7. 52:03 Echoing and Resonance - He links soft boundaries to the sense of infinite echoing, then illustrates how an image can mirror a person’s life and practice.
  8. 01:05:11 Meaning and Values - The final stretch clarifies that meaningfulness includes, but is not limited to, specific meanings, and it ends by tying imaginal life to values and their open-endedness.
