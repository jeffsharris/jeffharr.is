Compassion As We Take Action

Dharma Seed 53:08 2014-04-20

Jack Kornfield speaks at a Spirit Rock Earth Day gathering about how compassion can carry action without being driven by guilt, anger, or exhaustion. He weaves in stories about Gary Snyder, Gabriela Mistral, Hawaiian voyaging, elephants, chimps, and Buddhist teachers to suggest a quieter kind of activism rooted in love, interconnection, and long horizons. The talk closes with a guided meditation and blessing-cord ceremony that turns intention into a lived practice of refuge, non-harming, and vow.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening questions - He invites the room to name what would help as they return from the Earth Day gathering back into ordinary life.
  2. 04:26 Act from love - Using Gary Snyder's advice, he argues that effective action comes from love and tenderness rather than guilt, bitterness, or fighting.
  3. 09:11 Grief into song - A Gabriela Mistral poem and a line from Dina Metzger frame grief, beauty, and the possibility of transforming hurt into something luminous.
  4. 11:38 Finding our way - The Hawaiian voyaging tradition becomes an image for orientation, embodied wisdom, and what to do when we feel lost.
  5. 18:01 Interbeing and seeds - Stories of chimps, dolphins, and world-wide generosity lead into the image of carrying seeds of intention rather than trying to hold the whole world.
  6. 22:44 Long horizons - Ariyaratne's peace plan, the Dalai Lama's response, and Thomas Merton's counsel all point toward patient work without attachment to results.
  7. 27:25 A bodhisattva vow - He names the bodhisattva path and reads Diane Ackerman's vow as a public commitment to nature, healing, wonder, and peace.
  8. 30:36 Meditation on vastness - A long guided meditation expands the listener from breath and heart into a vast identity, then returns them to a personal vow and compassionate presence.
  9. 42:51 Blessing cords - He explains the symbolic cords, ties them to refuge, compassion, and intention, and has people share their vows with one another.
