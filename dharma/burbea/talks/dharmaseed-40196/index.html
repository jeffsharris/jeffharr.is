Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Wisdom, Art, Balance (Part 2)

Dharma Seed 44:40 2017-01-29

How do you keep eros from becoming either a narrow fixation or an ungrounded rush? The talk argues that balance is not a fixed middle, but a dynamic capacity built through samadhi, equanimity, rootedness, inquiry, and the kind of nourishment that makes one less dependent on sense pleasures or ego boosts. It then turns to imaginal practice, using the earth-touching Buddha and Buddhist mandala figures such as Akshobhya, Amitabha, Ratnasambhava, and Amoghasiddhi to show how image, emptiness, emotion, and action can be brought into a workable counterpoint.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Picking up the thread - Burbea recaps the earlier material on ethics, trust, equanimity, and skill with emotions and energies before continuing the list of supports for this work.
  2. 01:07 Samadhi beyond focus - He redefines samadhi as well-being and less fabrication, not simply fixed attention.
  3. 03:43 Nourishment and balance - The talk explores how meditative nourishment reduces dependence on sense pleasure and ego reassurance, making the practitioner less easily thrown off balance.
  4. 07:01 Why understanding matters - Burbea argues that understanding how practice works changes what we choose in meditation and how we regard thoughts, images, and emotions.
  5. 11:21 Rootedness and earth - He turns to hara practice, earth connection, and the Buddha touching the earth as examples of imaginal rootedness and strength.
  6. 13:53 Spacious equanimity - The talk contrasts solidity with spacious, insubstantial forms of equanimity that allow things to come and go without entanglement.
  7. 16:58 Strength, libido, vitality - Burbea discusses images of strength, the role of libido and vitality, and the need to avoid pathologizing a person's natural tendencies.
  8. 22:00 Dynamic balance - He frames balance as fluid and responsive, then uses the jhanas to show that qualities of stillness and equanimity can be accessed, revisited, and trusted over time.
  9. 25:29 Range and inquiry - The talk widens into a range of being that includes arousal, stillness, mindfulness, and investigation, while questioning fixed ideals of balance.
