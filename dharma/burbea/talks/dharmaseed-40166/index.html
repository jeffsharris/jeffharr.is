Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Dilemmas and Delineations: How did we get <em>here</em>?! (Part 4)

Dharma Seed 37:37 2017-01-21

How do eros, image, and practice fit together without collapsing into either craving or a flattened psychology? The talk argues for a wider Dharma language that can recognize fantasy and imaginal life as already present, then uses contrasts like holiday and pilgrimage to show the difference between mere pleasure-seeking and soul-making movement. From there it carefully distinguishes love, sex, eros, and metta, and develops an eros-psyche-logos dynamic in which perception can either solidify through clinging or open, deepen, and become more alive.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Unfamiliar ideas - Burbea acknowledges that these ideas may feel unfamiliar and explains why they are harder to introduce than culturally familiar blends of Dharma with neuroscience or psychotherapy.
  2. 03:50 Fantasy and image - He argues that image and fantasy are already present in practice and life, and that a fuller Dharma psychology must be able to recognize them rather than dismiss them as papanca.
  3. 09:03 Holiday or pilgrimage - Using an oncologist conversation as a contrast, he distinguishes secular ideas of quality of life from a pilgrimage-like movement of soul-making.
  4. 13:30 Find out for yourself - He returns to the Buddha’s ehipassiko teaching and warns against flattening Dharma into quick conclusions, especially about desire and suffering.
  5. 15:51 Eros in practice - Burbea says eros is already part of imaginal practice, often intertwined with love, and can sometimes open samadhi or deepen compassion in unexpected ways.
  6. 21:17 Distinguishing love and eros - He carefully separates eros from love, sex, metta, and attraction, while showing how the terms can overlap without being identical.
  7. 28:01 Eros and perception - The talk develops the eros–psyche–logos dynamic and links it to dependent origination, showing how clinging solidifies perception while eros can loosen and enrich it.
  8. 31:59 Small and large eros - He closes by distinguishing a narrower definition of eros from the wider soulmaking constellation it implies, and notes how realism or blockage can turn eros into craving.
