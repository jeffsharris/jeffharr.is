Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Creative Samadhi

Dharma Seed 57:09 2014-11-02

What if samadhi is not just concentration on a point, but a way of working that includes the whole body, a steadier energy field, and a deeper openness of heart? The talk starts by questioning a narrow idea of practice, then builds a broader map: the path aims beyond mere presence, and it has two wings, insight and cultivation. From there, it moves into practical advice on deepening samadhi—relaxing effort, valuing quality over quantity, opening the whole body in awareness, and noticing how kindness, gratitude, and letting go support the mind’s settling.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the question - Burbea introduces samadhi, asks what the path is really for, and invites listeners to question inherited assumptions about practice.
  2. 05:56 The path goes somewhere - He critiques a thin reading of 'just be present' and reframes the path as aiming at the undoing of fundamental delusion.
  3. 10:21 Two wings of practice - The talk turns to the paired roles of insight and cultivation, and to wholesome qualities like generosity, kindness, and mindfulness as sources of nourishment.
  4. 16:08 Samadhi as wholeness - Samadhi is described as a spectrum of deepening consciousness that involves the whole body, unification, and a felt sense of well-being.
  5. 19:01 Why focus is not enough - He lays out common one-pointed methods, then shows how mindfulness, patience, kindness, and the wider field of practice matter more than mere steadiness.
  6. 26:13 Ways to deepen - Burbea offers practical refinements: widen the conception of practice, value quality over quantity, allow subtlety, and include the whole body as a background field.
  7. 31:05 Whole-body experiment - He leads a guided inquiry into relaxing, opening awareness, sensing energy, and using breath or imagined channels to feel the body as a living field.
  8. 39:45 Space, letting go, and looking - The instructions widen into open awareness, where phenomena arise and pass while attention steadies on space, relationship, and release.
  9. 45:56 Open heart, open practice - In the closing movement, he questions the ideal of no effort, reflects on doing and desire in life, and returns to the importance of nourishing the heart through gratitude, nature, beauty, and community.
