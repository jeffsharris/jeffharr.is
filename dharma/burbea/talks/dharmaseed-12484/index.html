Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Nature of Awareness

Dharma Seed 52:33 2006-11-22

Rob Burbea explores awareness as a central Dharma question, moving from familiar images of mind as a clean mirror or vast space into a more searching examination of whether awareness itself can be found as something independent. He traces how practice can reveal first clarity, then spaciousness, then the possibility that even the witness or pure consciousness is conditioned, empty, and inseparable from perception. The talk stays close to practical inquiry, using not-self and dependent origination to point toward a freedom that does not rest on any fixed view of mind.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the question - Burbea frames awareness as a huge subject, but one that matters directly for liberation rather than philosophy.
  2. 02:06 Practice over theory - He contrasts an intellectual approach with a practitioner’s inquiry, and introduces a working set of terms for awareness, mind, consciousness, and that which knows.
  3. 04:02 The mirror metaphor - Awareness is first approached as a mirror that can be clouded by papañca, views, and moods, making clarity a useful early-stage practice.
  4. 07:11 Clean but still seen - He extends the mirror image into the sense of an unaffected witness, then asks whether this still captures the true nature of awareness.
  5. 11:02 Spacious awareness - The talk turns to practices that open awareness into space, including relaxing attention and softening the push and pull around experience.
  6. 16:31 Vastness and oneness - Burbea describes the sense of awareness as vast, timeless, and one, bringing in Advaita-like language and the feeling that everything is made of the same substance.
  7. 24:10 Not to stop there - He questions whether these beautiful states are enough, and recalls a teacher’s advice not to move on too quickly from them.
  8. 32:15 Undercutting the witness - The inquiry deepens into the possibility that consciousness and perception arise together, so that the seeming witness is not separate from what it knows.
  9. 40:00 Groundless and free - Using not-self, dependent origination, Shantideva, and Huang Po, he points toward consciousness as unfindable, non-self, and finally not a basis for liberation in the usual sense.
