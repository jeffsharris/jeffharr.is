Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

An Ecology of Love (Part 1 - Introduction)

Dharma Seed 50:56 2015-12-21

Rob Burbea opens a talk on love, nature, and sacredness by asking what we actually mean when we say we love the earth. He gradually moves from beauty, wonder, and environmental concern into a wider claim: our views shape our love, and our love shapes what we can perceive and value. The talk then turns to modern secular culture, the eclipse of sacredness in public life, and the possibility that climate and ecological crisis ask for more than technical or political response.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening Questions - Burbea begins with probing questions about what it means to love the earth and what exactly we are naming when we say nature.
  2. 04:21 Beauty and Wonder - He expands the inquiry through beauty, wonder, biological complexity, and the earth's support and danger.
  3. 10:32 Sacredness in View - The talk asks whether sacredness belongs in our experience of nature, and how that sense may have been pushed out of modern discourse.
  4. 18:31 Modern Secular Culture - Burbea uses examples from politics, climate discourse, and activism to show how sacred language has become marginal or taboo.
  5. 24:50 Climate as Values Crisis - He argues that environmental crisis is not only technical or political but a crisis of values rooted in views, perceptions, and sensibilities.
  6. 30:06 What Activism Needs - The talk widens to the needs of activists, including heart, emotional skill, courage, and ways of communicating love without burnout.
  7. 37:27 Ecology of Love - He introduces the title's double meaning and distinguishes metta from eros, defining eros as a desire to connect.
  8. 44:21 Views Are Not Fixed - Burbea shows how love always involves view, then draws on changing cultural ideas of nature and dependent arising to argue that views can be practiced and transformed.
