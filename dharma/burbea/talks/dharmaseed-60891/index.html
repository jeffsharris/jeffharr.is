Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Third Jhana

Dharma Seed 1:22:08 2019-12-28

What if the third jhana is less about a fixed set of factors and more about a very refined shift from rapture into peaceful contentment? The talk moves from the canonical description — equanimity, mindfulness, alertness, and the body pervaded by sukha — into a close experiential map: three internal levels, ways of entering by “drinking” the second jhana, and signs that the body and mind are becoming deeply unified. From there it widens into the larger Dharma point that perception itself changes the world we inhabit, so the practice is also an inquiry into dependent arising, emptiness, and how push and pull quiet down.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening the frame - Burbea introduces the talk, situates it in retreat pacing, and argues that the Buddha gave jhana a prominent place in the teachings.
  2. 02:46 Canonical third jhana - He reads the standard third-jhana passage closely, highlighting the fading of rapture, equanimity, mindfulness, alertness, and bodily pervasion by sukha.
  3. 07:01 Peacefulness over rapture - He explains why the third jhana is better understood through peacefulness and refinement than through the bare factor list of sukha and ekaggata.
  4. 11:25 Three levels inside - Burbea offers his own practical division of the third jhana into three layers, from contentment to a vast field of peacefulness.
  5. 15:12 How to enter it - He describes ways into the third jhana by drinking the happiness of the second, or by riding a spectrum of subtlety with great attention.
  6. 25:07 Oasis and pond - A desert-oasis simile and a cool lotus pond image clarify the progression from first to second to third jhana as thirst, drinking, and immersion.
  7. 27:05 Working the field - He discusses mastery, body-based attention, breath as a bridge, and the need to tune the whole energy-body to a very refined frequency.
  8. 42:08 Stillness and one taste - Burbea describes the third jhana as a quantum jump in stillness, with a faint movement inside it and a sense that all phenomena share one taste.
  9. 53:53 Insight, emptiness, and escape - The talk broadens into dependent arising, the after-effects of jhana on perception, and two ways of understanding jhana as either non-dual or an escape from the world.
