Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Samadhi in Metta Practice

Dharma Seed 67:03 2007-01-22

What if samadhi is less about narrowing the mind and more about letting the mind, body, and heart gather into one place? The talk unfolds by rethinking samadhi as collectedness, depth, and unification within metta practice, then moving into the practical questions that shape it: steadiness, happiness, gratitude, effort, and the way kindness softens tightness. It also explores why deepening can bring fear, how hindrances are recognized without self-blame, and why love and receptivity matter if practice is to bear fruit. Near the end, the talk turns toward emptiness through the simple insight that many emotional states only arise when the conditions are there.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 What samadhi means - Burbea introduces the term samadhi and sets up his broader use of it as collectedness and unification rather than narrow concentration.
  2. 03:07 Collectedness and depth - He develops the image of mind sinking into depth, stressing samadhi as a gradual, lifelong exploration with real nourishment and receptivity.
  3. 07:41 Why it matters - The talk turns to the practical value of samadhi for well-being, freshness, and insight that actually lasts and goes deep.
  4. 12:14 How it develops - He discusses steadiness of practice, handling of hindrances, and the importance of a broad, kindly mindfulness rather than a clenched effort.
  5. 17:21 Happiness as a support - Drawing on the Buddha, he explains happiness, gratitude, ethical ease, appreciation, and simplicity as conditions that feed samadhi.
  6. 21:02 Working with obstacles - Burbea describes the non-linear nature of practice, including backlash, aversion, worry about suppression, and the need not to personalize hindrances.
  7. 29:51 Effort, softness, and enjoyment - He explores how to balance effort and relaxation, receive metta as well as give it, and allow quiet enjoyment to deepen the practice.
  8. 39:06 Rapture and fear - The talk moves through brahmaviharas, dwelling in a wholesome inner home, pīti or rapture, and the fear that can arise when the mind settles into unfamiliar states.
  9. 53:26 Samadhi and emptiness - He closes by showing how samadhi softens body and self-view, clarifies emotions as conditioned, and points toward freedom through emptiness.
