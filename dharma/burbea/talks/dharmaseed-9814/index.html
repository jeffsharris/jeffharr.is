Breath and Space - Reactivity, Emptiness, Piti (4th Instructions)

Dharma Seed 21:12 2009-09-12

What if the problem is not the pain or pleasure itself, but the grasping, pushing away, and drifting that arise around it? The talk moves from this basic picture of reactivity and suffering into the practice of calming reactivity so that space, selflessness, and the sense of solidity can be seen more clearly. It then turns to breath meditation as a way to cultivate modest comfort, gradually spread it through the body, and develop pīti—an energised, pleasurable quality—as a steady resource rather than a special event.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Reactivity and suffering - The talk begins by naming the usual reactions to pleasant, unpleasant, and neutral experience, and locating suffering in those reactive habits.
  2. 02:30 Space and emptiness - He explains how quietening reactivity makes suffering lessen and makes the spacious quality of awareness more apparent, leading into emptiness and dependent arising.
  3. 05:28 Breath as resource - The emphasis shifts to breath meditation as a deliberate way of nurturing bodily comfort and ease, including simple ways of working with the breath.
  4. 09:00 Colours of pleasure - He describes different qualities of meditative comfort, from calmness to joy and lightness, and notes the role of the long breath in balancing calm and energy.
  5. 12:05 Developing pīti - Rob introduces pīti as the more energised range of meditative pleasure, and encourages gradual cultivation without pressure.
  6. 15:48 Enjoyment and skill - He addresses fear, attachment, and the practical question of how to relate to pleasure, stressing openness, steadiness, and the spreading of comfort through the body.
  7. 20:06 Dependent arising - In the closing stretch he returns to emptiness and dependent origination, suggesting that pleasant and unpleasant are not fixed and can be trained in perception.
