Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Energy and Transubstantiation: The Alchemy of the Body (Q & A)

Dharma Seed 43:03 2016-07-28

How do you work with the energy body without turning it into one fixed experience? The talk moves from clarifying language and the difference between energy body and pīti, into practical guidance on resting with pleasure, steadiness, or agitation, and then into how imagery and imagination can open perception without losing contact with the body. Later it widens further to jhana, the flexibility of the practice, and the idea that perception itself can re-enchant the world.

Chapters

  1. 00:58 What the Energy Body Means - Rob explains why he says “the energy body” rather than “my energy body,” using it to loosen identification and reduce the sense of self around bodily experience.
  2. 03:21 Getting Familiar - He responds to questions about difficulty sensing the energy body outside the hall, encouraging easier conditions, repeated contact, and a gradual widening of awareness.
  3. 06:16 Awareness, Space, Core - Rob suggests enlarging awareness or gently centering in the body, and introduces imagination as a practical support for sensing a central axis.
  4. 09:03 What Is Pīti - He defines pīti as pleasant bodily-space experience from a non-sensual origin, then distinguishes it from the broader field of the energy body.
  5. 12:03 Pleasure and Samadhi - Using a musical overtone analogy, Rob explains different wavelengths of bodily pleasure, how they can be explored, and how they relate to the Buddha’s map of the jhanas.
  6. 20:51 Working With Jhana - He outlines three broad modes of practice—steadiness in samadhi, sensitivity to emotional resonances, and imaginal work—and notes that jhana is a territory rather than a switch.
  7. 23:41 Love Figure and Cosmopoiesis - Rob answers questions about the imaginal figure of love, contrasting personal dissolution with a more distinctive “cosmopoietic” unfolding that keeps the figure’s character alive.
  8. 30:14 Agitation and Care - He reassures listeners that agitation, contraction, and grief are part of the field, and emphasizes responding with care, warmth, spaciousness, and attention to aversion.
  9. 41:04 Re-Enchantment - The talk closes by returning to the retreat’s language of re-enchanting the cosmos, with perception described as creative and poetic, something that can sing beauty into being.
