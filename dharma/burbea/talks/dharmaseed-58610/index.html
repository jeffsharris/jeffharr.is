Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Dukkha and Emotions in Soulmaking Practice

Dharma Seed 59:26 2019-03-25

How do you work with dukkha when there is no formula, and when what seems like ordinary suffering may need either basic emotional repair or a more imaginal response? The talk moves through a patient, improvised account of anger, depression, energy, and the need to rebuild neglected foundations before anything can become soulmaking. From there it widens into climate grief, sacred responsibility, and the possibility that some inner tensions are not to be harmonized but lived as part of a larger, polytheistic psyche.

Chapters

  1. 02:23 No formula for dukkha - He opens by saying the dukkha-soulmaking nexus is too complex for fixed methods and must be approached improvisationally.
  2. 05:48 Anger and hidden pain - A case study shows how disallowed anger can turn inward into depression, suggesting that basic permission may be needed before deeper work.
  3. 08:22 Foundations first - He develops the idea of rebuilding emotional and psychological foundations so blocked parts of the psyche can come alive again.
  4. 12:02 Images after repair - An example shows how, once the basics settle, imaginal material can emerge and liberate what was stuck.
  5. 14:17 Lattice and leverage - He points to the non-linear lattice of elements and says any one of them may become the opening that changes everything.
  6. 20:26 Crucible of emotion - Using Catherine McGee’s framing, he describes how a proper stance and enough energetic mindfulness can make emotion into a crucible rather than a meltdown or a mere letting-go practice.
  7. 28:20 Dharma and soul - He contrasts ordinary Dharma moves like reducing self-story with Soulmaking moves that allow self, narrative, and object to become imaginal together.
  8. 35:50 Ecological grief and roles - Questions about climate crisis lead him to discuss sacred-world views, imaginal roles such as warrior or hospice worker, and the relative freedom and cost of such callings.
  9. 48:21 Polytheistic psyche - He ends with the tension between conflicting inner gods and images, suggesting that soulmaking may bring both freedom and sacrifice rather than simple harmony.
