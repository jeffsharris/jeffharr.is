Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Sensing with Soul (Part 4)

Dharma Seed 1:14:32 2017-12-24

What changes when experience becomes less fixed, and can be sensed as already alive with image, value, and sacredness? The talk moves from a broad map of “sensing with soul” to the conditions that support it: intimacy with what is present, sensitivity in the whole energy body, loosening clinging, and then a readiness for whatever imaginal quality is most helpful. It then turns to examples — a student’s emptiness practice turning vedana holy, a ritual with tangerines and a gold bowl, and an eating meditation that opens into interdependence, compassion, and devotion. Near the end, the talk asks how intention itself can help or hinder this kind of sensing, especially when the aim is not just to reduce suffering but to let perception deepen and reconfigure it.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the Theme - Rob resumes the thread of sensing with soul and offers a broad view of how imaginal practice matures.
  2. 09:35 Four Conditions - He begins outlining the conditions that tend to support sensing things with soul rather than only as fixed objects.
  3. 12:21 Intimacy and Energy Body - The talk turns to openness, sensitivity in the whole energy body, loosening clinging, and receptivity to imaginal elements.
  4. 15:38 How the Process Works - Rob explains the mutual dependence among these factors and uses the framework to prepare for concrete examples.
  5. 20:54 Vedana Becomes Holy - A student’s report shows emptiness practice and imaginal perception meeting, with feeling-tone becoming sacred rather than merely noted.
  6. 25:48 Tangerines and Gold - He recounts a retreat ritual with a gold bowl, chanting, and tangerines to show how shared ritual can sanctify ordinary food and create a soul field.
  7. 42:44 A Meal of Interdependence - Another student’s eating meditation illustrates how interdependence, gratitude, desire, and embodied imagination can deepen perception.
  8. 01:03:51 Two Perceptions, One World - He closes by describing how ordinary perception can coexist with imaginal perception, and how a medical intention to reduce suffering may sometimes narrow the fuller opening of soul-making.
