Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Beyond Impermanence

Dharma Seed 59:16 2008-12-31

Impermanence is the starting point, but the talk keeps asking what lies beyond simply noticing that things change. It moves from ordinary daily shifts in mood and circumstance, through aging, death, and grief, toward the question of how practice changes the way we relate to experience, time, and self. Along the way, it distinguishes the tenderness of going with the flow from a deeper freedom that may include letting go of even subtle views and constructions.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Impermanence as practice - He begins by saying impermanence is not just something to understand, but an avenue for training how we look.
  2. 02:35 Tracking ordinary change - Using mind states, bodily feeling, and daily life, he shows how change can be noticed in small, practical ways.
  3. 05:41 Beauty, grief, and loss - He balances the consoling and painful sides of change, bringing in nature, grief, and the old Zen poem of simple traces.
  4. 10:05 The scale of disappearing - The talk expands into disappearance, relationships, and mortality, with Eliot and the idea of holy discontent deepening the tone.
  5. 15:48 Aging and inheritance - He turns to aging, illness, and the habits we inherit, arguing that what matters most is the quality of relationship we cultivate now.
  6. 25:09 Death, urgency, compassion - Mortality becomes a source of urgency and sympathy rather than fear, including Buddha, Nagarjuna, death row prisoners, and the Dalai Lama.
  7. 33:20 Changing the way we see - He shifts from impermanence to uncertainty and from experience to view, emphasizing moment-to-moment seeing and the flow beneath the story.
  8. 43:02 Beyond the flow - He qualifies the appeal of vastness and stillness, pointing instead to the unfabricated, unbinding, and the deeper possibility beyond change.
  9. 50:56 Constructed time and objects - The closing section uses dependent origination and classical Mahayana reasoning to question time, objects, and appearances, ending on the freedom to pick up and put down views.
