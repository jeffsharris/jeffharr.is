Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Breath Meditation (2nd Instructions/Guided Meditation)

Dharma Seed 48:37 2009-09-10

Rob Burbea leads a long guided breath meditation that begins with posture, kindness, and widening the field of awareness through the whole body. He then invites listeners to explore the breath from several centers—below the navel, the heart, throat, mid-head, crown, base of spine, upper back, and elsewhere—treating each as an experiment in what feels most open, comfortable, or enjoyable. The final section shifts into practical advice for working with pain and discomfort, emphasizing relaxation, spaciousness, imagination, and the freedom to change posture when needed.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and setup - A brief opening joke and introduction to a second guided meditation on the breath.
  2. 00:26 Posture and kindness - The practice begins with posture, body presence, and establishing a kind, receptive attitude.
  3. 02:10 Whole-body awareness - Awareness is broadened to fill the whole body, using images of a balloon or sail filled by wind.
  4. 03:18 Lower belly and right side - The breath is explored at the lower abdomen, first on the left and then on the right, with attention to what feels best.
  5. 09:34 Midline and heart - The center moves to the midline and then up to the heart and throat, with encouragement to experiment with breath length and subtlety.
  6. 15:59 Head and crown centers - The practice shifts to the middle of the head and then the crown, inviting luminous, expansive breath awareness.
  7. 21:55 Base and upper back - The center drops to the base of the spine and then rises to the upper back, continuing the theme of flexible exploration.
  8. 29:05 Review and possibilities - Rob reflects on the range of possible approaches to breath and breath-energy practice and how different forms help different people.
  9. 39:23 Working with discomfort - He gives practical advice for pain and constriction: relax the rest of the body, keep awareness spacious, use imagination, and change posture when needed.
