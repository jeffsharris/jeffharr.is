The Image of Ethics (Part 6 - An Inconclusive Conclusion, or A Prolegomenon)

Dharma Seed 58:03 2020-02-19

Can the five precepts really carry the whole weight of ethics, or do they only mark a beginning? The talk moves from that question into a steady critique of complacency in Buddhist ethics, then widens into a discussion of value, hierarchy, and why questions of what is of value may matter more than simple right and wrong. Along the way, it touches on climate change, power abuse, ceremonies, and the practical problem of how much context listeners need before hearing soulmaking and imaginal teachings.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening the inquiry - He frames the talk as a partial, provisional continuation of earlier ethics and soulmaking material rather than a final conclusion.
  2. 04:06 Why precepts are not enough - He argues that every ethical system is incomplete, including the Buddhist precepts and even Soulmaking Dharma as an ethical approach.
  3. 09:46 Complacency and shadow - He looks at the dangers of easy confidence in the precepts, including different interpretations, power abuse, and passive aggression.
  4. 13:38 From rules to values - He distinguishes precepts from values and asks why Buddhism was so slow to respond to climate change and other ethical crises.
  5. 17:30 Ceremony and safety - He considers whether retreat openings should include a precepts ceremony, while insisting that safety alone is still not enough.
  6. 19:43 Prerequisites and readiness - He turns to the order of practice, asking how much emptiness understanding people need before Soulmaking Dharma, and whether the teachings should be publicly accessible.
  7. 27:57 Hartmann and hierarchy - Using Hartmann, Augustine, and examples like altruism and egoism, he develops the idea that ethics starts with what is of value, not only what is right or wrong.
  8. 37:18 Nobility and the remote - He explores historical shifts in values through Christianity and warns against over-prioritizing distant or lofty ideals at the expense of ordinary human care.
  9. 46:41 Imaginal safeguards - He returns to imaginal practice, distinguishing eros from craving, outlining safeguards in the middle way, and revisiting the question of who should receive these teachings and when.
