Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Metta to phenomena

Dharma Seed 34:58 2007-02-04

What happens when metta is turned away from people and toward the raw phenomena of experience itself? The talk guides a steady practice of noticing whatever is most prominent in consciousness—body sensations, thoughts, emotions, states of mind—and bathing it in kindness, compassion, and warmth. It also shifts into a more receptive mode of complete welcoming, where precision matters less than an open, tender holding of whatever arises, including resistance, numbness, fear, or tiredness.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Turning Toward Experience - The meditation begins by redirecting metta and compassion from beings to whatever is prominent in awareness.
  2. 01:28 Bathing the Moment - He encourages repeatedly bathing body sensations, emotions, thoughts, and mind states in kindness and love.
  3. 04:07 Holding with Metta - The practice continues with phrases and a steady, tender holding of whatever is arising in experience.
  4. 07:30 Open Reception - Burbea introduces a more receptive mode of complete welcoming and an open door to whatever appears.
  5. 09:51 Softening Repeatedly - The instruction settles into repeated softening, welcoming, and bathing the moment in warmth.
  6. 13:47 Less Precision, More Kindness - He asks listeners to let go of the usual emphasis on precise mindfulness and allow the edges of experience to blur if they do.
  7. 18:07 Welcoming All States - Even resistance, numbness, anger, fear, and tiredness are included in the scope of kindness.
  8. 20:58 Long Receptive Silence - The rest of the sit unfolds in extended silence, with the inquiry into relationship and experience continuing underneath the meditation.
