Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Soulmaking Rivers (Q & A)

Dharma Seed 51:32 2018-02-06

What if the question is not whether God, soul, or imaginal life are invented, but how far that question can be taken? The talk begins with a difficult relationship to divine language, then moves into a careful unpacking of fabrication, emptiness, and the sense that self, meaning, and even godlessness may all need to be investigated rather than assumed. From there it widens into a discussion of temenos, shared image, and how imaginal practice can either loosen into the unfabricated or deepen into a particular, soul-making richness without collapsing into abstraction.

Chapters

  1. 01:30 God-language and resistance - He begins with the listener's visceral resistance to words like God, sacred, and divine, and looks for ways the language can be loosened without forcing it.
  2. 07:11 Mystery, value, and poetry - Burbea reframes divine language as open-ended, uncapturable, and precious, more like poetry pointing toward mystery than fixed doctrine.
  3. 14:01 What is fabricated? - He turns the question of fabrication into a Buddhist investigation that keeps going beyond papanca, self, meaning, and even godlessness.
  4. 21:10 Temenos and shared image - The talk moves into temenos as an alchemical container where images can land, resonate, and be shared without becoming a rigid religion.
  5. 25:40 Open image libraries - Burbea discusses how culture, group context, and the aliveness of another person's imagery can seed an open-ended library of images.
  6. 31:34 Soul-making and suffering - He suggests that personal and cultural difficulties can be folded into soul-making, changing how life events are held and interpreted.
  7. 36:15 Paths toward the unfabricated - In response to questions about awakening, he explains how samatha, metta, and imaginal practice can all move toward less fabrication in different ways.
  8. 42:43 Images fading, essence remaining - The close of the talk uses Aslan and a dissolving skeleton to show how an image can lose its form yet retain its emotional and imaginal essence, before returning to the larger mandala of practice.
