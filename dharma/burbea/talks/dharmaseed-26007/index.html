Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Buddhism Beyond Modernism

Dharma Seed 1:22:55 2014-11-19

In this second part of a three-part talk, Rob Burbea examines the assumptions hidden inside Dharma practice: what counts as truth, what counts as real, and what gets left unasked. He moves from the role of ideas and inquiry, through Thomas Kuhn and modern science, into critiques of modernism, bare attention, and reductionist ways of explaining experience. The talk then opens toward imagination, myth, and fantasy as shaping forces in practice, including a careful questioning of the stories we tell about awakening, the Buddha, and liberation itself.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Setting up the inquiry - Burbea explains that this talk will not follow a linear teaching style, but will instead open up the assumptions beneath Dharma practice and awakening.
  2. 04:40 Ideas and their power - He defines ideas as the lenses shaping perception, and shows how concepts, fantasies, and mythos can open life or close it down.
  3. 14:50 Freedom to inquire - Using Einstein and the Gaia House sign, he argues that real inquiry needs freedom, and that inquiry must include what feels taboo or hard to question.
  4. 21:36 Kuhn and the Dharma - Burbea uses Thomas Kuhn's account of paradigms and revolutions to ask whether Dharma itself can become trapped in conceptual boxes.
  5. 28:41 Modernism examined - He turns to Richard Tarnas and sketches modernism as a worldview shaped by materialism, objectivity, and a narrow sense of what counts as real.
  6. 35:27 Breaking the vessels - With Heisenberg, Einstein, and Lurianic Kabbalah, he explores how crises shatter inherited structures and make room for new ways of holding experience.
  7. 43:36 Styles of inquiry - He contrasts Apollonian, Dionysian, and Hermes-like modes of inquiry, while questioning the modern preference for bare attention and closed systems.
  8. 53:32 Multiple worlds of meaning - Burbea widens the frame to show how different metaphysics, stories, and images generate different worlds, and why no single discourse can claim final authority.
  9. 01:05:53 Liberation and fantasy - He closes by arguing that liberation, awakening, and the Buddha are always entangled with fantasy, interpretation, and cosmology, and that recognizing this is part of honest practice.
