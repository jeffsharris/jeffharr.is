The World and More: Immanence, Tantra, and Transcendence (Part 1)

Dharma Seed 1:28:48 2017-02-11

What if passion is not the problem, but the force that helps the world arise? This talk moves from a critique of how modern insight practice has narrowed its inheritance into a wider weaving of emptiness, tantra, eros, and imaginal practice, using a tantric couplet, Jung, Nietzsche, and Kabbalah as points of comparison. It then deepens into a careful distinction between craving and a holy, inexhaustible eros, with a long imaginal example that shows how erotic and sacred perception can transform how the senses, self, and world are known. The movement of the talk is toward a double perspective: loosening fabrication through emptiness while also allowing divine appearance, devotion, and participation in the world.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Weaving the threads - He opens by framing the talk as a continued weaving of themes already introduced in the retreat.
  2. 02:08 Insight as an evolving tradition - He describes the insight meditation tradition as selective, modern, and still developing, with influences from Zen, Dzogchen, and Mahamudra.
  3. 07:44 Tantric verse and Vajrasattva - He introduces a tantric couplet about passion and reads it as a bridge between world-arising, emptiness, and the diamond being.
  4. 16:41 Desire and world-making - Using Jung and the Buddha's language of bhava-tanha, he distinguishes craving from eros and tracks how desire fabricates worlds at different levels.
  5. 23:23 World rejection and transcendence - He places Buddhism within a broader religious pattern of world rejection, then contrasts that with a more tantric or imaginal affirmation of the world.
  6. 29:21 A living imaginal sequence - He shares a detailed personal sequence of a burnt man and a healing goddess that develops into a highly erotic, devotional image of sacred commitment.
  7. 41:13 Inexhaustible eros - He contrasts tanha with the holiness of eros, then develops the idea that erotic energy can widen divinity, re-enchant the world, and work across many kinds of objects.
  8. 56:50 Nietzsche and the senses - He reads Nietzsche on loving the world and the senses, using the passage to support a transfiguring, non-puritanical spirituality.
  9. 01:07:13 Buddha mind and Kabbalah - He compares Buddhist, tantric, and Kabbalistic accounts of transcendence and immanence, including the erotic union of wisdom and appearance.
