Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Samadhi

Dharma Seed 63:51 2006-11-07

What happens when samadhi is treated not as a tight, microscopic focus but as a gathered, unified depth of mind? The talk moves from a basic definition of collectedness into a wide view of practice: one-pointed attention, open awareness, vipassana, the hindrances, happiness, whole-body sensitivity, and the way calm can support insight without strain. It also keeps returning to a quieter question underneath the technique—how to work with effort, pleasure, fear, and emotion without turning practice into another form of grasping.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 What samadhi means - Burbea defines samadhi as collectedness, gathered mind, and depth of meditation, contrasting it with the narrower idea of concentration.
  2. 03:49 Right samadhi in context - He situates samadhi within Buddhist teaching, mentions the eight jhanas, and broadens the idea to include breath, metta, vipassana, and open attentiveness.
  3. 12:03 A path to awakening - He argues that samadhi is not secondary but can itself lead to awakening, and that it matters across a lifetime of practice.
  4. 17:35 Happiness and appreciation - Turning to the Buddha's teaching that happiness supports samadhi, he invites appreciation, gratitude, and receptivity as practical conditions for settling the mind.
  5. 25:20 Tightness and balance - He examines common objections about goals, striving, and attachment, and suggests a softer balance between probing and receiving in practice.
  6. 31:29 Whole-body practice - Burbea emphasizes whole-body awareness, including the role of the body in breath and metta, and warns that tight effort can actually increase restlessness.
  7. 38:13 Effort, goals, and pīti - He explores the delicate play of effort, the realism of goals, and the arising of pīti or pleasant energy as the mind becomes less entangled.
  8. 45:57 Fear and self-softening - As practice deepens, he describes fear, self-softening, and the need for love and receptivity so that samadhi remains transforming rather than barren.
  9. 54:40 Less doing, less self - In the closing section, he reflects on emotional healing, conditionality, and the way samadhi leads toward doing less, less selfing, and a more direct sense of emptiness and freedom.
