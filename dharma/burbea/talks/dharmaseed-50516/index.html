Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

A Temenos Exercise (in fours)

Dharma Seed 64:27 2018-02-05

How do you stay rooted in your own body while meeting three other people with kindness, then let what you feel become something you can actually share? The talk unfolds as a timed group exercise: first settling into the energy body and mutual mettā, then expressing present-moment emotional experience through words, voice, gesture, or movement, while the others witness and notice how they themselves are affected. Near the end, the frame widens again into silence, reflection, and a short Pali blessing for all beings.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling and Metta - The talk opens by grounding in the body and introducing the exercise as a shared practice of wishing one another well.
  2. 05:35 Explaining the Choreography - Rob lays out the timed four-person format, including the bell, the minute turns, and the range of ways to express what is present.
  3. 12:18 First Round of Sharing - The group begins the first stage, with the emphasis on witnessing, receiving, and noticing whatever arises without needing to get it right.
  4. 23:07 Second Stage: Emotional Expression - He introduces the second stage, where participants share what is emotionally present and explore how others receive them with fuller sensory attention.
  5. 39:32 After-Effects and Resonance - After the first rounds, the group pauses to notice reverberations in body, heart, self-image, and perception of others.
  6. 45:51 Deepening the Witness - The practice continues through more sharing, with attention to the effect of speaking and listening and an invitation to imagine the witnesses more poetically, even as angels.
  7. 58:06 Return to Goodwill - Rob brings everyone back to the foundation of kindness and metta before widening the blessing outward.
  8. 01:01:08 Pali Blessing - The closing chant is explained as a blessing meaning may all beings be happy, offered first to the room and then to all beings everywhere.
