Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

From Insight to Love

Dharma Seed 1:19:07 2007-01-27

Rob Burbea explores how insight can nourish metta and compassion rather than pull them apart. He moves from a simple definition of insight as the release of clinging, through a story of Baba Amte and Gandhi, into practical ways of working with fear, impermanence, dukkha, and not-self inside lovingkindness practice. The talk stays grounded in ordinary experience: how fear tightens, how comparison becomes a habit, and how the heart opens when clinging relaxes.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Insight and metta - He frames the retreat as a training in lovingkindness, compassion, and the way insight can support them.
  2. 03:59 What insight means - Insight is defined as seeing in a way that releases clinging and therefore releases suffering.
  3. 06:31 Baba Amte's example - A story of Baba Amte illustrates how fear closes us down and how a life can organize around love and service.
  4. 10:47 Working with fear - He turns to fear in practice, showing how it arises, how aversion feeds it, and how kindness and spaciousness can meet it.
  5. 24:53 Insight in practice - He returns to the main theme and outlines how insight can be woven into metta and compassion as a support rather than a complication.
  6. 26:55 Three characteristics - Impermanence, dukkha, and anatta are presented as lenses on experience that can deepen freedom and love.
  7. 36:17 Not-self and openness - He develops the movement from impermanence and unsatisfactoriness toward the direct sense that self is not fixed, using a Yom Kippur story to warn against making not-self into identity.
  8. 48:45 Unhooking me and mine - The talk becomes more practical: unhooking from identification, loosening selfing, and seeing others through the Buddha's analysis of aggregates.
  9. 57:42 Habits, grooves, and trust - In the close and questions, he emphasizes mental habits, the power of repeated practice, the great security of the path, and fear as a future-oriented clinging that can be loosened.
