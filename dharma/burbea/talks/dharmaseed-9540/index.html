Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Concepts, Views, Reality

Dharma Seed 1:26:49 2010-01-30

Rob Burbea examines how concepts, views, and assumptions shape experience, then asks whether bare attention and “not thinking” are enough for genuine insight. He contrasts simplified Dharma readings with a more careful account of emptiness, dependent arising, and the way self and world are constructed through clinging. The talk moves between canonical references, examples from Western Dharma, and practical cautions about balance, ethics, and the heart’s response to inquiry.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the Terrain - Rob frames the talk around the diversity of practitioners' backgrounds and how that shapes what teachings can land.
  2. 01:51 Views Shape Experience - He lays out the central claim that attitudes and preconceptions profoundly colour life and practice, often without our noticing.
  3. 06:44 Bare Attention And Suchness - He explores bare attention, then uses Huxley and Siddhartha to question the modern romance of vividness, flow, and 'suchness.'
  4. 16:37 Beyond Impermanence - A canonical passage and the theme of not-knowing are used to argue that awakening points beyond simply accepting change or suspending judgment.
  5. 27:32 Concepts That Undo Themselves - He brings in a sutra image of fire consuming the branches to show how analysis and concepts can be used skillfully until they are no longer needed.
  6. 41:56 Right View, Not No View - Rob distinguishes helpful views from false ones, and warns against over-simplifying emptiness into a vague refusal of all views or ethics.
  7. 54:02 Ethics And The Limits Of Relativism - He pushes back on the idea that anything can be a path, insisting that integrity, ethics, and genuine discernment still matter.
  8. 01:04:43 Emptiness And Dependent Arising - He clarifies that by ultimate reality he means emptiness and dependent origination, then explains why 'knowledge and vision' means more than bare perception.
  9. 01:16:18 Mutual Dependence And Reverence - The talk closes by deepening dependent arising into a non-linear, mutually conditioning view of self and world, and ending with reverence rather than nihilism.
