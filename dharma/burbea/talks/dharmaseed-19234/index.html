Reflections on the Day

Dharma Seed 26:30 2013-04-20

Nina Wise offers a short, embodied opening before moving into a larger reflection on climate grief, love, and collective responsibility. The talk weaves together a film about melting glaciers, a story of a prison garden, and a final image of interdependence that loosens the sense of a separate self. It ends quietly with an invitation to see what is alive, fragile, and connected right where one is.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening into the body - Nina begins with a brief, humorous introduction and a playful standing sequence that wakes up the body and the room.
  2. 00:49 Motion theater and release - She introduces motion theater, explains the form, and continues a lively series of movements, sounds, and loosening exercises.
  3. 04:43 From me to we - The talk turns from embodied play to the idea that change requires moving beyond the isolated self toward a larger communal identity.
  4. 10:27 Glaciers and grief - A viewing of Chasing Ice becomes a direct encounter with climate loss, and she follows that clear seeing into grief and love.
  5. 14:49 A prison garden begins - She tells Catherine Sneed’s story of turning a jail yard into a garden through cleanup, bureaucracy, and patient work with the men.
  6. 20:02 Planting and recognition - The story continues as a boy with a gun appears, the tree is planted, and the scene shifts from threat to a moment of shared human recognition.
  7. 23:22 Stardust and non-self - The final story links water, clouds, wind, sun, stars, and the hand, using that web of causes to point toward interdependence and the easing of 'me'.
  8. 25:04 Closing in the hills - Nina closes by imagining the drive home, the brown hills as gold, and a final attention to lupine and stars.
