Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Unshakeable Heart (Part 1)

Dharma Seed 59:06 2008-10-04

How do impermanence and practice together loosen the grip of praise, blame, gain, loss, success, and failure? The talk moves from the eight worldly conditions to a more intimate question: what makes the heart less dependent on changing circumstances, and what kind of inner happiness can support that shift. It then widens the frame through cultivating wholesome qualities, working with reactivity, and asking what remains when experience begins to fade and simplify.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the frame - Burbea introduces the talk as an overview of the principles that support equanimity and the wider picture of how it develops.
  2. 01:04 The eight worldly conditions - He reviews praise and blame, success and failure, gain and loss, and pleasure and pain as the basic swings everyone faces.
  3. 02:59 Practicing impermanence - The talk turns to anicca as a practical contemplation, with attention to changing feeling tone, uncertainty, and the limits of control.
  4. 07:21 Why insight is not enough - Burbea argues that impermanence alone rarely suffices, and adds the need for inner resources and a different way of seeing.
  5. 11:01 Qualities that support joy - He names aspiration, loving-kindness, generosity, renunciation, wisdom, and concentration as qualities that deepen happiness and make equanimity possible.
  6. 14:45 Collectedness and conviction - The discussion moves from equanimity based on multiplicity to equanimity based on singleness, then to the way a deep conviction about what truly brings happiness reshapes fear and self-view.
  7. 24:33 The heart shapes perception - Burbea explores how joy, loving-kindness, and fulfillment change the way events are experienced, especially around relationships, success, and failure.
  8. 33:46 Working with reactivity - Using a magnet-and-figure story and examples like an itch, he shows how craving and aversion build suffering, distort time, and can be met through skillful attention.
  9. 48:16 Simplifying and letting go - He closes with ways of decomplicating experience through mindfulness, a flexible view of loss, and a final poetic image of the still point at the center of change.
