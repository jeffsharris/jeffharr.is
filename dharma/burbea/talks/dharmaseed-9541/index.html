Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Dependent Cessation and the Unconditioned

Dharma Seed 69:04 2010-02-14

What if the deepest freedom is not a special state you can possess, but the gradual unbinding of the way experience gets built? The talk moves from a broad map of practice—healing, ethics, intimacy, samadhi, insight—to a close examination of nibbana as the unconditioned, with a steady warning not to mistake absorption or vast awareness for the end of fabrication. Along the way, the speaker leans on Buddha passages, a Nagarjuna citation, and a few analogies of fading, sheaves of reeds, and a roof beam collapsing to show how craving, clinging, and the subject-object-time structure can cease together.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Practice as tools - Burbea frames emptiness and selfing as practical tools in service of reducing suffering and increasing love, then stresses flexibility in choosing the right approach.
  2. 05:48 Many strands of practice - He broadens the map of practice to include intimacy, ethics, earth connection, healing, creativity, wholeness, and the cultivation of beautiful qualities.
  3. 11:19 Reasoning for freedom - The talk turns to insight and reflective reasoning, with a brief clarification that these analyses aim at conviction in emptiness, not confusion.
  4. 14:05 What nibbana means - Burbea introduces nibbana as the unconditioned, unfabricated, unborn, or deathless, and asks what listeners actually hope such a term means.
  5. 19:47 Transcendent or not - He weighs different interpretations of the Buddha's language, brings in Nagarjuna, and asks listeners to notice their own bias toward or against transcendence.
  6. 29:50 Fading and samadhi - Using sutta passages and a pedal-boat anecdote, he shows how absorption can resemble but not equal the unconditioned, and how fading depends on practice and understanding.
  7. 38:09 Dependent cessation - Burbea reads awakening language through dependent origination, the aggregates, and the sheaves-of-reeds image to show how experience is built and can stop being built.
  8. 49:10 Language and footing - He turns to passages on the limits of language, consciousness without footing, and a Zen description of pure mind to clarify what is and is not being pointed to.
  9. 56:33 Unbinding and release - The close gathers several canonical images for the unconditioned, ending with Ajahn Chah's word 'unbinding' and a return to the central point that understanding matters more than experience.
