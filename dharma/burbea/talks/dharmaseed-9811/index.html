The Space of Awareness - Impermanence (1st Guided Meditation)

Dharma Seed 40:30 2009-09-09

How does awareness change when the field is opened wide enough to notice impermanence itself? This guided meditation starts with body sensations, then expands to include sound and even thoughts, returning again and again to the shifting, flickering texture of experience. The practice keeps the attention light and open, using the perception of change to support letting go rather than concentration on content.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the practice - Burbea introduces the spacious-awareness practice, explains its relation to the breath work, and frames impermanence as the lens for this sitting.
  2. 02:14 Settling the body - The meditation begins with posture, intention, and a single changing area of body sensation, with attention resting on bare sensation and its fluctuation.
  3. 05:06 Widening to the body - Attention expands from one area to the whole body, noticing the global pattern of sensations as coming and going within a larger field.
  4. 10:10 Including sound - The field opens further to sounds, which are also held as bare, changing events arising and vanishing in space.
  5. 14:11 Only the change - He keeps redirecting practice away from content and back to the texture of change, even when thoughts or inner material arise.
  6. 18:46 Working with difficulty - Areas that feel contracted or problematic are opened into a wider space, so that their shifting patterns can be seen as part of impermanence.
  7. 24:02 Everything in the field - The instruction broadens to include all experience—outer, inner, thoughts, images—while staying with the fact of change itself.
  8. 28:56 Space and flicker - Burbea emphasizes the steadiness of awareness itself and repeatedly points back to the flickering, sparkling quality of all phenomena within it.
  9. 34:12 Letting go, closing - Seeing impermanence is linked with letting be and letting go, and the talk closes by situating this as one of two ongoing retreat practices.
