Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Beyond the Prison of the Self

Dharma Seed 52:36 2006-02-11

Rob Burbea explores how the sense of self quietly structures suffering, and why Buddhist practice is not about destroying the self but understanding it well enough to loosen its grip. He moves from generosity, compassion, and service into a close inquiry into grasping, measuring, comparing, and self-view, showing how these conditions build the feeling of “me” and “mine.” The talk ends by bringing not-self into immediate experience—breath, body, thought, and awareness—toward a lighter, less burdened way of living.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Why practice turns inward - He begins with the ordinary wish for relaxation and relief, then shows how practice gradually reveals that suffering is tied to self-sense.
  2. 03:40 Not-self, not nihilism - Using the Buddha’s silence in response to “is there a self?” he clarifies that Buddhism is neither self-denial nor self-reification, but understanding that frees.
  3. 08:30 Cultivating the heart - He turns to loving-kindness, compassion, calm, and equanimity as qualities that soften self-preoccupation and unburden life.
  4. 13:26 Generosity as release - Generosity is presented as a real practice of openness, with the Buddha’s radical statements about giving used to show how giving loosens fear and possession.
  5. 18:51 Service beyond self - He extends the discussion to service, right livelihood, and anonymous giving, emphasizing a shift from self-centeredness toward interconnection.
  6. 24:47 Looking without possession - Through simple acts like looking at possessions or even one’s hand, he shows how the sense of ownership can soften and self-views can begin to loosen.
  7. 28:35 Self-views and conditions - He examines painful identity patterns, depression, and reactivity through dependent origination, arguing that moods and behaviors arise from conditions, not essence.
  8. 32:46 Nothing to identify with - The talk deepens into the Buddha’s radical non-identification, including the suggestion that even awareness need not be taken as self.
  9. 37:32 Breath, grasping, and freedom - He applies not-self to breath, body sensations, thinking, and the whole self-project, tracing how grasping and comparison build self and how letting them go reveals freedom and love.
