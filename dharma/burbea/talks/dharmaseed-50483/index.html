Between Ikon and Eidos: Image & Hermeneutics in Meditation (Part 7 - The Scripture of Body and Earth)

Dharma Seed 1:28:58 2018-01-14

What if the body, a sound, or a remembered image were not just objects to notice, but texts to be interpreted, and reinterpreted, by the heart? The talk moves from ideas and emotions in practice into a close exploration of how sensing with soul can widen what body, matter, and reality seem to be, using examples from energy-body work, imaginal practice, and a long retreat report about a nun image, grief, and healing. It then turns outward to hermeneutics, sacred scripture, tantra, Sámi yoik, and the three bodies of the Buddha, asking how a more participatory way of listening can make the world feel less flat and more alive.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Ideas and the Soul - He opens by revisiting how ideas can limit or enlarge experience, and how emotions such as devotion and mattering nourish sensing with soul.
  2. 05:04 Eros and the Imaginal Body - He explains how soulmaking expands perception, especially through the energy body and the body becoming imaginal.
  3. 10:41 Playing With Body Images - He suggests deliberate experiments with imagined body forms, from blue light to denser earth imagery, and explores body awareness outside ordinary location.
  4. 17:34 Three Layers of Body - He lays out bodily awareness as sensation, image, and concept, then shows how energy-body practice can re-enchant the body and reshape ideas of matter.
  5. 27:34 Hermeneutics of Existence - Using Heidegger and Gadamer, he argues that interpretation is never neutral and that body and world are always being read through assumptions.
  6. 37:18 Retreat Report in Practice - He reads a student's retreat account to show how images, grief, devotion, and healing can unfold together in actual practice.
  7. 50:57 Sound, Prayer, and Yoik - He turns to language and sacred sound, including tantric hearing and Sámi yoik, to suggest that speech and song can participate in making reality.
  8. 01:07:41 Bodies of the Buddha - He uses the three bodies of the Buddha and the formula 'thus have I heard' to widen the sense of scripture, perception, and purity.
  9. 01:21:55 Praise and Healing - He closes by returning to open hermeneutics, the middle way, and praise as a healing force that opens heart and soul.
