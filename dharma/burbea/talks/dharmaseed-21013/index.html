Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Art of Letting Go

Dharma Seed 56:13 2005-12-17

What if letting go is less about getting rid of something than about seeing how clinging, pushing away, and even self-judgment keep suffering going? The talk begins by tracing attachment through body image, emotion, roles, relationships, and ownership, then keeps turning toward a quieter question: can the relationship itself be relaxed. It also warns against a cold version of non-attachment and instead points toward mindfulness with warmth, patience, and acceptance, where suffering is touched directly and seen to arise through grasping.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Letting Go Begins - Rob opens by suggesting that the whole Dharma can be understood as non-attachment and begins naming the many places it shows up in life.
  2. 03:51 Where Attachment Lands - He examines how attachment grips body image, emotion, mind states, people, and roles, and offers a simple working definition of attachment as gripping.
  3. 06:11 What It Feels Like - The talk turns to the felt signs of pushing away and clinging, and to the way the heart closes and opens in relation to them.
  4. 09:11 Kindness Over Self-Judgment - Rob warns against making non-attachment into a dry self-improvement project and emphasizes suffering, kindness, and spaciousness as better guides.
  5. 13:48 Mindfulness And Training - He links letting go with presence, patience, and the cultivation of wholesome qualities that quietly strengthen the heart and provide a stable basis for insight.
  6. 20:12 Relationship And Strength - Using Bob Dylan and everyday examples, he shows why ethics, calm, love, and equanimity matter more than a merely negative idea of letting go.
  7. 27:45 Roles, Ownership, Love - Rob explores attachment to roles, possessions, the body, and loved ones, including a reading from Khalil Gibran on children not belonging to us.
  8. 37:20 Suffering As Teacher - He uses the poem and then the Buddha's framing of suffering to show how awareness of pain can reveal the mechanism of clinging and the possibility of freedom.
  9. 47:44 Welcoming And Emptiness - The closing section contrasts precise noticing with radical acceptance, then moves into a deeper view in which experience depends on relationship and separation loosens.
