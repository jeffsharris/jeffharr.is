Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Visions of the Beyond

Dharma Seed 1:44:13 2017-03-30

What if the first question about practice is not whether your view is correct, but whether it is soulmaking? The talk begins with simple questions about what you love and want from the path, then moves into a wide-ranging comparison of path-conceptions: classical transcendence, one-dimensional craving, a realism of coping, and tantric or soulmaking ways of opening. Along the way, it keeps returning to eros, fantasy, the senses, and the idea that the path may either narrow life or make it more dimensional and alive.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening Questions - He begins with reflective questions about what one loves in practice, what one wants from it, and whether the answers feel complete.
  2. 07:22 Culture and Self - Burbea turns to how our sense of self and freedom are conditioned by culture, and asks whether our view of awakening is merely inherited or genuinely alive for us.
  3. 12:32 Soulmaking Test - He reframes authenticity as soulmaking, contrasts this with the Pali canon’s unfabricated goal, and discusses the traditional link between lessening fabrication and ending suffering.
  4. 20:02 Eros and Beyond - The talk develops the idea that a sense of a beyond can constellate eros or craving, and that fear, self-measurement, and anti-libidinal defenses can block desire.
  5. 29:26 Horizontal Craving - Burbea explores what happens when eros cannot open into depth, showing how it becomes endless acquisition in a one-dimensional world of more and more.
  6. 42:11 Four Path Imaginations - He contrasts the realist, existential path with other images of the path, including the medical, religious, scientific, and artistic fantasies.
  7. 56:06 Tantra and Imaginal - Burbea presents a tantric view of awakening in which emptiness, appearances, wisdom, means, and divine appearances are held together in an open-ended way.
  8. 01:04:40 Open-Ended Awakening - He shows how soulmaking and tantra keep awakening open-ended, with many possible directions rather than a single finish line.
  9. 01:23:56 Questions to Carry - The talk closes by asking listeners to notice their dominant fantasy of practice, what drives their devotion, and whether the soul loves soulmaking.
