Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Sailing the Oceans of Soul (Q & A)

Dharma Seed 33:46 2018-06-27

What happens when an image starts to fade: is that emptiness, loss of clinging, or a sign to turn the attention differently? The talk moves through a practical Q&A on imaginal practice, from reframing the Heart Sutra as “form is imaginal, imaginal form” to noticing how eros, discernment, and what the speaker calls the “lens” can either dissolve an image or bring it back into richer detail. It then widens out to movement, stillness, sound, concepts becoming image, and the different ways form, energy, and imagination can interpenetrate without forcing one fixed method.

Chapters

  1. 00:49 Heart Sutra Reframed - He uses the Heart Sutra phrase about form and emptiness, substituting imaginal for emptiness to keep an image vivid without letting it dissolve completely.
  2. 01:36 Why Images Fade - He explains fading through dependent origination, reification, and clinging, showing why images lose solidity when their conditions are seen through.
  3. 03:35 Modulating the Fading - He offers ways to notice what in the lens is causing fading, and suggests easing off that habit rather than forcing the image to stay fixed.
  4. 04:02 Eros Restores Form - He describes eros as desire and attraction that can intensify the image again and increase its complexity.
  5. 05:06 Particulars and Fluidity - He turns to the soulmaking value of particulars, showing how attention to elegance, movement, and subtle qualities can enrich an image.
  6. 06:04 Form and Emptiness Edge - He frames tantric practice as learning to hold the middle ground where form remains translucent, then widens the frame through jhana and the Buddha’s classical absorptions.
  7. 09:10 Universal Figures - Using Kuan Yin and related examples, he shows how a figure can become more universal while still retaining a felt form or quality.
  8. 14:42 Discernment Deepens Practice - He argues that naming, differentiation, and discernment are part of the practice itself, especially when states are subtle or difficult to put into words.
  9. 21:33 Movement and Stillness - He answers questions about movement, explaining how movement can become image, how image can attach to movement, and how stillness often preserves sensitivity more easily.
