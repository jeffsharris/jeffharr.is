Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Freedom From Fear And Anxiety

Dharma Seed 56:55 2007-07-16

Rob Burbea explores fear and anxiety as ordinary human experiences that can still be worked with skillfully. He moves from the Buddha’s own fear in the forest to practical methods: mindfulness of sensation, noticing reactions of aversion, bringing kindness, and challenging fear more directly when needed. The talk closes by widening the frame to self-judgment, future anxiety, and the cultivation of inner resources as a real way of caring for what comes next.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Fear is universal - Burbea opens by normalizing fear, using the Buddha’s forest story to show that fear can arise even in serious practice and still be workable.
  2. 03:23 Many forms of fear - He broadens the lens to body, emotions, social judgment, self-view, retreat forms, and even fear of freedom itself.
  3. 11:37 Three approaches - Burbea outlines three complementary ways of working with fear: mindfulness, noticing reactions to fear, and more direct challenging of fear.
  4. 15:58 What fear feels like - He describes the bodily, mental, and attentional narrowing of fear, and shows how the fear of fear can feed the whole pattern.
  5. 24:12 Kindness and metta - The talk turns to metta, self-acceptance, and kindness toward fear itself, including a personal story about embracing apprehension on retreat.
  6. 33:18 Opening the space - Burbea explores how physical space, listening, movement, and small acts of experimentation can loosen the constriction of anxiety.
  7. 39:55 Questioning assumptions - He works directly with thought, examining hidden assumptions, future catastrophizing, and the power of approval-seeking and social pressure.
  8. 49:28 Cultivating the future - The closing section emphasizes building an inner reservoir through wholesome qualities, then ends by showing how fear is shaped by views of self, present, and future.
