Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Soulful Movement and Elements of the Imaginal (6 - 10)

Dharma Seed 43:52 2018-02-05

What if stillness is only one way to discover soul, and movement, sound, and subtle bodily energy are another? The talk opens by questioning the retreat culture of silence, then turns to how imaginal perception can be deepened through noticing, deliberate activation, and a little grain of trust. From there it moves into soft edges, dimensionality, and the sense that images and beings are never just flat appearances, before setting that view beside broader Buddhist framings of emptiness, mindfulness, and fabrication.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Movement and silence - He opens by contrasting the retreat’s culture of stillness with the possibility of discovering soul through movement, sound, and subtle bodily activity.
  2. 04:09 Opening practice wider - He asks whether practice can include movement and sounding, especially in forms like yoga, tai chi, or walking meditation, without losing depth.
  3. 09:12 Seeds, not dogma - He sketches the list of imaginal notes as flexible prompts for discernment and warns against turning them into rigid doctrine.
  4. 15:30 Trust and activation - He explains how a small amount of trust, or deliberate attention to a note like the energy body, can ignite the whole imaginal field.
  5. 20:16 Soulmaking and edges - He describes soulmaking as the eros-psyche-logos dynamic and then explores how imaginal perception has soft, elastic boundaries rather than fixed ones.
  6. 26:18 Dimensionality and divinity - He develops the sense that imaginal perception carries other dimensions, including a sense of divinity and beyondness within the appearance itself.
  7. 33:10 Three Dharma framings - He compares three broad ways Dharma is taught: transcendence into emptiness, mindfulness of things as they are, and the imaginal engagement with appearances.
  8. 37:37 Fabrication and freedom - He argues that seeing how experience is fabricated can loosen clinging and even legitimate soulful fabrication rather than rule it out.
  9. 42:57 Closing quiet - He briefly gathers the talk, invites listeners to sense their own way into these terms, and ends in silence.
