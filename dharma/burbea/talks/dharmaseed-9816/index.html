Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Opening the Sense of What's Possible

Dharma Seed 63:37 2009-09-08

What if practice is not only about learning to be with difficulty, but about refusing to let your sense of what’s possible stay small? The talk moves from the preciousness of retreat and the limits of a narrow focus on suffering into a wider invitation: to notice intentions, question self-doubt, and consider aspiration as something deep, honest, and life-shaping. It then opens out further into bodhicitta, ethical responsibility, and the possibility that practice can become a force for inner and outer transformation. Along the way, the speaker weighs spacious acceptance against the need to challenge ourselves, and ends by returning to silence, simplicity, and the conditions that let deeper longing surface.

Chapters

  1. 00:05 Welcome and tone - He greets the retreat at Gaia House and sets a quiet, appreciative tone for the week ahead.
  2. 01:38 Retreat as devotion - He describes retreat as precious time, devoted to our deepest wishes and the question of what we really want.
  3. 05:14 Aspirations and intentions - He distinguishes deep aspirations from the many intentions that shape daily life and can obscure what matters most.
  4. 11:08 Insight and its limits - He names the strengths of Insight Meditation—learning to be with experience and meet dukkha—while suggesting that this can become too narrow if overemphasized.
  5. 15:54 Freedom as duty - He turns to the Buddha’s teaching on suffering and its end, and to Dharma as an invitation to freedom rather than mere obligation.
  6. 19:38 Beyond the self - He develops bodhicitta as practice for the sake of others, linking inner work with service, generosity, and care for the world.
  7. 27:41 World and responsibility - He connects practice with climate and social संकट through Havel and Ben Okri, arguing for an inner shift in values and conscience.
  8. 32:44 Blockages and confidence - He names the inner critic, fear of failure, and low confidence as common reasons aspiration contracts, and points to silence and small successes as support.
  9. 40:16 Cultivation and investigation - He outlines two sides of practice—cultivating beautiful qualities and investigating what leads to freedom, including a clearer seeing of emptiness.
