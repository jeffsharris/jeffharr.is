Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Heat and the Material

Dharma Seed 1:58:02 2019-05-28

Rob Burbea explores how emotions can either be pacified through mindfulness or become material for imaginal and soulmaking practice. He keeps returning to a central question: what kind of relationship with emotion allows image, eros, and a sense of the divine to emerge, and what kinds of habits block that process. The talk moves through caution about cultural assumptions, the alchemical crucible metaphor, and concrete examples of anger, power, grief, and inner stretch.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening cautions - He begins by framing the talk around emotions in soulmaking practice and warning against simple prescriptions about healthy psychology.
  2. 05:46 Cultural assumptions - He questions contemporary and historical assumptions about emotion, using the idea of passionate love to show how easily people are pathologized or misunderstood.
  3. 12:26 Emotional style and skill - He distinguishes different temperaments and points out that some people need help riding the force of their emotions rather than suppressing them.
  4. 21:58 The crucible of emotion - He develops the alchemical image of a vessel that can hold heat, describing how emotions may need to be felt and respected so they can ferment into images.
  5. 36:02 From mindfulness to image - He contrasts standard mindfulness, which tends toward pacification and less fabrication, with the imaginal mode where emotion becomes transformed rather than simply reduced.
  6. 51:53 Stretch and mutual dependence - He argues that soulmaking and stretch arise together: opening, breaking, and difficulty can bring forth images, and images can in turn create more stretch.
  7. 01:03:40 Images and emotion - He sets out a tripartite contrast between fixated imagery, mindfulness deconstruction, and imaginal practice, then explains how the emotional field changes in each case.
  8. 01:19:31 Wide fields of cause - He invites listeners to broaden the field around emotion, including narrative, values, trust, love, beauty, and other possible causes beyond family history.
  9. 01:32:51 Practice examples - He offers a detailed guided-style example involving a mule image and shows how attention to the body, power, integrity, and self-sense can unlock a more living imaginal response.
