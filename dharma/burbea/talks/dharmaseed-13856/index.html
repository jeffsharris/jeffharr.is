Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Working with the Emotional Body (Instructions and Guided Meditation: Day Three)

Dharma Seed 51:50 2011-08-01

How do emotions change when attention stops arguing with them and starts noticing the mind’s commentary as part of the field? The talk moves from reviewing a body-based practice, to exploring labeling and how naming can shape experience, to opening the whole body as a wider field that can sometimes be bathed in ease and pleasure. From there it turns to the mind’s judgments, assumptions, and self-conclusions, then closes with short experiments: seeing whether a thought like “this is exactly right” changes the feel of practice, and whether briefly setting aside past and future can lighten the load of an emotion.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Reviewing the approach - Burbea reviews the earlier practice of attending along a central axis and orienting toward bodily and emotional experience.
  2. 04:07 The energy body - He broadens the practice into a larger field or energy body and explains why such images can be useful without being taken literally.
  3. 06:33 Feeling and enjoyment - The instruction turns toward sensing the texture of the whole space, including permission to enjoy pleasant feeling when it is present.
  4. 08:24 Mind's commentary - He asks practitioners to notice judgments, assumptions, and conclusions the mind adds to experience, including how they shape self-view.
  5. 14:46 Short practice experiments - A set of brief exercises invites direct checking-in, noticing commentary, and seeing what happens when attention is refreshed quickly.
  6. 20:09 A supportive view - Burbea suggests experimenting with the thought that this moment is exactly right, and explores how views affect experience.
  7. 24:18 Unweighting the moment - He introduces the idea of shortening the felt span of past and future so attention can outweigh the emotion's pull.
  8. 28:53 Quickness and kindness - The talk ends by reframing distraction as normal, encouraging short check-ins, and returning to a climate of kindness.
