Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Metta Instructions and Guided Meditation 5

Dharma Seed 47:57 2010-08-10

Rob Burbea offers practical metta instructions that move from the body, to difficulty, to an increasingly boundless field of kindness. He emphasizes that the practice is hard to do “wrong,” and keeps returning to flexibility: comfort in the body, use of phrases or visualization, and kindness toward whatever is present. In the second half, the talk opens into the Metta Sutta, a discussion of how metta changes perception, and a long guided meditation that widens from a bodily sense of ease to all beings.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Metta is forgiving - Rob begins by reassuring listeners that metta is difficult to get wrong, then frames the practice as gently leaning toward bodily well-being and ease.
  2. 02:43 Working with difficulty - He describes several ways to meet discomfort: stay with the phrases, open to bodily pain, allow spaciousness, or let kindness trickle into what feels blocked.
  3. 04:37 Care for the body - The instructions turn practical, including moving posture, avoiding getting pulled into pain, and using whole-body awareness to stay connected to what feels okay.
  4. 09:03 Energy and imagination - He explores subtle energy opening in practice and suggests using imagination, including images of flow, blockage, and tenderness through the hands and feet.
  5. 14:14 The Metta Sutta - Rob reads the Buddha's verses on loving-kindness and uses them to ground the retreat's direction toward boundless goodwill for all beings.
  6. 16:44 How metta changes perception - He outlines different ways to expand metta, then explains that as kindness deepens, perception changes and even ordinary problems like rain are colored by aversion or ease.
  7. 23:42 Guided practice begins - The guided meditation starts with bodily comfort, awareness, and a gentle invitation to offer kindness wherever it is easiest.
  8. 25:56 Opening to all beings - The meditation gradually expands into a sphere of kindness radiating in all directions, including human and non-human beings, with warmth and reverence.
  9. 34:12 Seeds without pressure - Rob keeps the practice light and persistent, reminding listeners that feeling or no feeling does not matter and that metta can be planted steadily with faith.
