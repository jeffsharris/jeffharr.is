Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Place of Love in Insight Meditation

Dharma Seed 47:31 2005-12-29

Rob Burbea explores how love and kindness belong at the center of insight practice, not as decoration but as part of how the mind learns. He contrasts a narrow, self-improving approach with a wider practice that includes acceptance, community, and concern for all beings, then shows how that changes both suffering and perception. The talk gradually moves from metta as nourishment into a deeper view in which love, mindfulness, and emptiness are closely connected.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Nourishment and inquiry - He opens by framing the path as a balance of wholesome nourishment and investigation, each supporting the other.
  2. 02:17 Kindness as climate - The talk turns to the inner climate needed for growth, warning against pressure, rejection, and hidden self-improvement agendas.
  3. 05:41 Love beyond feeling - Using Catherine’s metta instructions, he explains love as intention, goodwill, and acceptance rather than only a felt emotion.
  4. 07:18 Sangha and silence - He broadens metta from self-kindness to concern for the whole retreat community and describes how silence can carry care.
  5. 12:25 Beyond self-concern - He questions practice driven only by personal gain and shares a story from the beach to show how quickly the mind judges.
  6. 21:49 Compassionate seeing - Drawing on the Lotus Sutra, he shifts from precise noting toward a radical welcoming of experience with love.
  7. 24:24 Welcoming resistance - He shows how acceptance can include resistance itself and how kindness changes the felt shape of emotions and body sensations.
  8. 33:33 Love and emptiness - He argues that what matters is the relationship to experience, and connects this to emptiness and freedom.
  9. 38:50 Changing the view - He explores how agenda and grasping color perception, then ends by opening the question of the many depths of love.
