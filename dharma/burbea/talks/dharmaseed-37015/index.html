Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

On Orientations in Practice (Live and Shortened version)

Dharma Seed 47:57 2016-07-28

Rather than trying to stay continuously mindful, the talk keeps returning to a more flexible question: what changes when practice moves deliberately between different ways of looking? It begins with the energy body as a felt field around the body, then shows how that sensitivity can support samadhi, emotional work, and the signs that a practice is on track. From there, the speaker widens into a critique of entrenched Dharma habits—focus on one object, atomistic attention, strong reality claims, and self-sufficiency—and invites a more experimental, creative, and soul-guided approach.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the retreat - Rob opens the morning talk and says the retreat will begin with emphasis on the energy body.
  2. 01:21 What the energy body is - He defines the energy body as a field of vibration and a different sensibility from ordinary mindfulness of sensation.
  3. 04:23 Why it matters - He explains how the energy body supports samadhi, emotional work, and a kind of inner navigation that can show when practice is on track.
  4. 09:46 Listening beyond the box - He pauses to reflect on how we hear teachings inside our existing framework and invites listening for the larger structure.
  5. 13:47 Loosening entrenched habits - He questions the assumptions of continuity, one-pointed focus, atomistic attention, realism, and self-sufficiency.
  6. 21:08 Flexibility and precision - He presents deliberate shifts in ways of looking as the path to insight, and says precision lies in subtle distinctions, not microscopic scrutiny.
  7. 25:40 Subtlety and soulfulness - He develops the role of subtlety in practice and encourages using soul resonance to tell whether a practice is alive.
  8. 32:29 Practice as art - He describes the retreat as a studio for experimentation, where the deeper point is loosening, beauty, sacredness, and a more spacious knowing.
  9. 40:02 Sitting and walking instructions - He ends with practical guidance for open-schedule sitting and walking meditation, asking students to keep the energy body in view and adjust pace and posture by what helps.
