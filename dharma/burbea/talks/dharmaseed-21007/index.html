Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Compassion and Emptiness

Dharma Seed 55:26 2005-11-22

Rob Burbea explores compassion as a natural response of the heart, then opens that idea out into its conditions, distortions, and deeper contemplative roots. He moves from practical reflections on empathy, fear, anger, pity, and self-view toward the way compassion grows through insight into emptiness, common humanity, and interdependence. The talk closes by widening into a more spacious view of love as something mysterious, universal, and not separate from seeing clearly.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening Definition - Burbea begins with a working definition of compassion as the heart's natural response to suffering when it is not preoccupied with self-interest.
  2. 02:30 Compassion as a Composite - He unpacks compassion into empathy, giving, equanimity, wisdom, kindness, joy, and humor, stressing that it is more than a feeling.
  3. 05:55 Why We Value It - The talk turns to why compassion matters so much, including connection, relief from self-enclosure, and the way practice makes it a central commitment.
  4. 10:48 Questioning the Right Way - Using the Buddha and a Gertrude Stein anecdote, he emphasizes that the quality of our questions shapes practice and that compassion should be investigated causally.
  5. 13:24 What Nurtures Compassion - Burbea points to openness to suffering, insight practice, action in the world, association with compassionate people, and happiness as conditions that allow compassion to grow.
  6. 19:46 Fear and Anger - He describes common blocks such as fear and anger, and shows how they contract the heart and obscure the dependent arising of suffering.
  7. 24:59 From Blame to Understanding - A personal story about Boston and 9/11 leads into a broader reflection on ignorance, judgment, pity, and the need to soften self-righteousness.
  8. 31:29 Empathy and Spaciousness - Burbea distinguishes empathy from grief and explains how compassion deepens into a spacious, steady, and less self-centered quality of mind.
  9. 39:53 Emptiness and Oneness - He brings compassion and emptiness together through self-compassion, changed perception, the poem 'Kindness,' cosmic interdependence, and the claim that seeing emptiness well should result in love.
