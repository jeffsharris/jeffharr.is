Orienting to This Jhana Retreat

Dharma Seed 1:29:31 2019-12-17

What if jhana depends less on sealing out experience than on learning to meet it with happiness, openness, and a steadier heart? This opening talk orients the retreat around a flexible, practice-led approach: an open schedule, interviews, a current “playground” level, and a base practice that can include breath, metta, or insight ways of looking when they genuinely open pīti and ease contraction. It also widens into the emotional weather of retreat and life itself—grief, activism, illness, and other pressures—then turns that into a practice of devotion, resolve, gratitude, beauty, and the five precepts as a shared ethical ground.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Welcome and setup - Rob welcomes the retreat, introduces the teachers, and explains the unusual conditions that made the retreat possible.
  2. 05:42 Open schedule, open mind - He lays out the retreat rhythm and stresses that jhana practice should be soft, pliant, and open to conditions like noise and change.
  3. 10:46 Listening and the playground - Rob explains the open schedule, interviews, and the idea that each practitioner has a current level or 'playground' where practice should be lived for a while.
  4. 22:45 Base practice and pīti - He describes jhana as a lifelong exploration, then turns to base practices and the conditions that allow pīti and samadhi to arise.
  5. 28:31 Insight as support - Rob briefly names insight ways of looking, including a reference to Seeing That Frees, and says such practices can unblock contraction and help well-being arise.
  6. 39:50 Intention and inertia - He emphasizes sustaining energy through clear intention, serving others, and noticing inertia in practice rather than staying on automatic pilot.
  7. 49:45 Hindrances and emotions - Rob treats hindrances as part of the work, then broadens into a larger perspective on emotions, healing, and the retreat's first-choice way of simplifying difficult states.
  8. 58:35 Grief, devotion, resolve - He leads a short contemplation on grief, loss, and climate and political distress, using posture, devotion, and resolve to find a base of strength and care.
  9. 01:13:42 Kirsten and Sari - Kirsten and Sari welcome the group, speak about silence, the five precepts, and the retreat container, and Sari invites participants to leave phones and settle in.
