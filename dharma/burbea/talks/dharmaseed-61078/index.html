Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Practising with Death and Dying

Dharma Seed 2:35:07 2020-02-20

Death here is not approached as a single doctrine, but as a question that keeps slipping between impermanence, nihilism, eternalism, and the way experience is actually shaped by our inherited views. The talk gradually turns from those familiar frames to a phenomenological inquiry grounded in Soulmaking Dharma, where death can be met through emptiness, gift, duty, beauty, and deep relationship rather than reduced to a blank end or a simple afterlife story. A long middle section works through physics examples, then returns to lived experience, including illness, grief, and the sense that a beloved person may remain present as image, even after death.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening the Question - Burbea introduces death and dying as a topic and begins by naming the common cultural approaches that usually shape the conversation.
  2. 03:17 Views To Set Aside - He surveys impermanence, annihilationism, eternalism, and rebirth, showing how each can become a limiting frame for death.
  3. 11:41 Physics And Perception - A detour through quantum entanglement and observation leads into the larger point that inherited assumptions shape what appears real.
  4. 23:50 Phenomenology Of Death - He turns decisively toward Soulmaking Dharma, emphasizing ways of looking, mixed cultural inheritance, and what can make death feel more open than mere resignation.
  5. 43:49 What Helps Death - Burbea considers ordinary strategies like achievement, longevity, and impermanence practice, then contrasts them with a deeper opening through emptiness and direct knowing.
  6. 53:46 Gift, Duty, Beauty - He develops four main supports: life as gift, trying to do one’s duty, living toward the worthwhile, and receiving the beauty of a life already shaped by these currents.
  7. 01:11:35 Burying The Beloved - Using John Coltrane, Bach, and Clement of Alexandria, he explores grief, providence, and the possibility that even tragic death can be held as gift and beauty.
  8. 01:29:55 Image And Relationship - Burbea builds a detailed account of image, analogue, and soul-making relationship, arguing that personhood is already multi-stranded and not exhausted by physical life.
  9. 02:04:47 A Death Re-Enchanted - He reads a friend’s written reflections on metastatic cancer and a black snake dream as examples of death becoming imaginal, beloved, and even eternal in a non-literal sense.
