Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Home and Homelessness

Dharma Seed 57:01 2007-03-15

Rob Burbea explores the deep human need for home and security, then turns that question toward practice: what we protect, what we fear losing, and what kinds of rootedness actually free the heart. He moves from the early Buddhist ideal of leaving home through the risks of worldly attachment, then into more subtle homes in ethics, refuge, loving-kindness, awareness, and finally the possibility of non-abiding freedom. The talk is reflective and unsparing, but it keeps returning to a practical question: where, if anywhere, can we truly rest?

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the question - Rob introduces home and homelessness as a basic human concern and asks how we respond to the need for security.
  2. 01:28 Leaving household life - He reads the early discourses on going forth, then places them in their historical setting as a contrast with fixed household roles.
  3. 05:04 Lay life and its risks - The talk turns to modern lay practice, the possible losses of over-protection, and the need for honesty about what we sacrifice.
  4. 11:20 Money and self-protection - He examines possessions, money, and future thinking as ways the heart can close, using a small personal story as a reminder about where real security lies.
  5. 18:01 Rooted in the Dharma - Rob develops the Buddha’s language of making a home in the Dharma, ethics, right view, and the Four Noble Truths as a true orientation.
  6. 23:53 Seeing groundlessness - A story of the Buddha’s sheltered life and the Jewish rabbi’s remark about being passing through lead into the felt pain of uncertainty and disconnection.
  7. 29:41 Changing experience, changing homes - He looks at making a home in the present moment and in impermanence, then asks whether enough inner well-being has been cultivated to make letting go possible.
  8. 38:38 Inner resources and refuge - The Brahma Viharas, refuge in Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha, and even resting in awareness are presented as increasingly subtle forms of home.
  9. 44:38 Security, emptiness, and freedom - The talk opens into nirvana as refuge, the emptiness of the world we build, and the paradox that true home and true homelessness may finally be the same.
