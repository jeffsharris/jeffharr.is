Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Psychodynamics in Meditation (Part 3 - Dimensions of the Personal Journey)

Dharma Seed 66:52 2010-11-26

Rob Burbea explores how meditation can work with emotion, view, and identity in a flexible way rather than a single fixed method. He moves from practical ways of meeting difficult feelings, through emptiness and the Four Noble Truths, to a larger question: whether the personality itself can be a site of ongoing unfoldment and freedom. The talk closes by contrasting traditional not-self practice with a more affirmative way of relating to the personality as a living thread of discovery.

Chapters

  1. 01:05 Flexibility with emotion - Burbea finishes the list of approaches to emotional life, emphasizing vedana, warmth, compassion, determination, and shifting relationship to what is present.
  2. 05:22 Resources already in the field - He shows how calm, peace, and past insights can be found or deliberately cultivated as supports for difficult states.
  3. 08:58 Fabrication and emptiness - The talk turns to how experience is built through aversion, grasping, and identification, leading toward the emptiness of self and phenomena.
  4. 13:42 Milarepa's example - Burbea reads a verse attributed to Milarepa to illustrate how an empty view can transform even confusion and suffering into support for practice.
  5. 16:15 Four Noble Truths - He frames the Dharma as dukkha, craving, delusion, and the path that pierces the seeming reality of things.
  6. 18:24 The power of emptiness - He contrasts emptiness with other Buddhist framings and stresses how seeing fabrication loosens oppressive certainty and opens freedom.
  7. 26:53 Personality as journey - The talk shifts to the personality level, asking whether liberation might also involve a fuller unfolding through qualities, sexuality, creativity, and life direction.
  8. 34:47 What locks it down - Burbea names the forces that inhibit unfoldment: self-image, limiting beliefs, reductionist views, and the failure to ask bold questions.
  9. 47:23 What unlocks it - He ends with six supports for unfolding—questioning, care for genuine needs, desire, trust, a kinder reading of difficult drives, and a nuanced way of identifying with the thread of unfolding while keeping emptiness in view.
