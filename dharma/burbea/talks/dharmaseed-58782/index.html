Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Soulmaking Dyad Practice

Dharma Seed 2:39:19 2019-06-03

Rob Burbea offers detailed guidance for soulmaking practice in dyads and small groups, moving from the simplest forms of paired attention into more open imaginal and relational fields. He keeps returning to the need for skill, tenderness, and clear boundaries, especially where erotic charge, projection, or old wounds can quickly shape the encounter. The talk also develops the idea of temenos: a protected container, and even a "tail" of care after the formal practice ends.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening the practice - Rob introduces the talk as practical guidance for working in soulmaking dyads and names it as a large subject that needs careful handling.
  2. 03:59 Early exercises and scales - He revisits earlier dyad and temenos exercises, especially balance of attention and the three-part check-in with energy body, emotion, and image.
  3. 08:19 Why dyads are sensitive - The talk turns to the potency of relational practice, the ease with which it can spark beauty or contraction, and the need for real skill.
  4. 14:06 Prerequisites for practice - Rob lists the capacities needed for this work, including emotional awareness, mindfulness, energy body skill, and clarity of intention.
  5. 20:04 Not rushing ahead - He encourages listeners to pitch the practice appropriately, and to value basic attention training and simple relational presence before more advanced imaginal work.
  6. 27:56 Attention, gaze, and metta - He describes specific ways to work with attention, including noticing habitual patterns, relaxing the gaze, and widening the field through metta.
  7. 37:37 Temenos as container - Rob explains temenos as the sacred, protective space of the practice, shaped by posture, formality, metta, intention, and careful reception.
  8. 49:09 After the encounter - He extends the idea into the "temenos tail," where the effects of a dyad continue after the formal session and need thoughtful care.
  9. 01:03:49 Shared framework and stages - The talk returns to earlier exercises and emphasizes that soulmaking dyads need shared ontological commitments and a common conceptual frame.
