Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Talk Three: Wise Effort and Wise Attachment

Dharma Seed 60:52 2008-08-10

Rob Burbea explores wise effort as a nuanced middle way between striving and passivity, starting with our relationship to goals and spiritual aspiration. He then turns to the four right efforts, wise attachment to ethics, concentration, and insight, and the inner critic that so often distorts practice into self-judgment. The talk ends by reframing effort as something responsive, light, and balanced, with practical images from the bird, the raft, and the potter.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the question - He introduces wise effort and wise attachment, then broadens the question from moment-to-moment effort to our whole relationship with goals and progress.
  2. 06:49 Striving and aspiration - He reclaims the word striving, distinguishes healthy aspiration from self-defining success and failure, and argues that desire can be part of the path.
  3. 11:34 Craving as leverage - He cites the Buddha on using craving to abandon craving, then lays out the four right efforts as the direction of wise effort.
  4. 18:37 Wise attachment - He makes the case for healthy attachment to sila, samadhi, and insight, using the raft and car images to show how supports can be used without being fetishized.
  5. 24:58 Working with the inner critic - He turns to the inner critic as a major obstacle, then offers ways to question it, shift from self-judgment to judging actions, and build self-respect around intention and care.
  6. 38:31 Learning the bigger picture - He shows how samadhi reveals that practice depends on conditions rather than essence, and brings in Michelangelo to emphasize ongoing learning.
  7. 45:13 Conditions for success - He introduces the Buddha’s four bases of success and shows how desire, persistence, full attention, and ingenuity work together in practice.
  8. 50:24 Balancing the effort - He closes with the bird and potter images, stressing that effort must be light, responsive, and sensitive to too much and too little effort.
