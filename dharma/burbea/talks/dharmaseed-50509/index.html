Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Elements of the Imaginal (1 - 2)

Dharma Seed 35:54 2018-02-03

What makes an image more than fantasy, and where does the imaginal begin? The talk opens by distinguishing ordinary imagination and papanca from mindful uses of image in death contemplation, metta, and compassion, then moves into a careful working definition of the imaginal as a spectrum of qualities rather than a yes-or-no category. From there it sketches the first nodes of a larger lattice — energy body, love, eros, beauty, trust, soulmaking, dimensionality, beyondness, reverence, humility, autonomy, twoness, theatre, and more — and shows how noticing or activating one node can open the whole field. The emphasis stays practical: less on fixed doctrine than on giving practitioners a set of distinctions that can deepen how they enter relationship with image.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the frame - Burbea sets up the retreat’s focus and distinguishes imagination from the imaginal as a working definition for the days ahead.
  2. 02:58 Skillful imagination - He uses death contemplation and metta-style imagery to show how imagination can serve renunciation, compassion, and embodied practice.
  3. 05:16 What imaginal means - He compares his usage with Hillman, Jung, and Corbin, then argues for a light, non-dogmatic way of naming the imaginal.
  4. 09:39 The first nodes - Burbea begins listing the elements of the imaginal, starting with lattice, energy body, love, eros, beauty, trust, soulmaking, and related qualities.
  5. 18:08 A constellation of qualities - He continues through the list, adding themes such as divinity, beyondness, timelessness, reverence, grace, humility, and non-reduction.
  6. 23:57 Why the distinctions matter - The imaginal is presented as a multi-aspected network rather than a single feature, with the point that activating one node can enliven the whole field.
  7. 27:42 Openings and entry points - He briefly names emptiness, timelessness, and humility as practical doorways into imaginal experience and warns against treating the cutoff rigidly.
  8. 30:12 Lattice and energy body - Burbea returns to the first two nodes, showing how the image is not inherently imaginal unless the relationship, aliveness, and energy body are present.
