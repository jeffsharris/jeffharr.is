Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Eros and Desire (Q & A)

Dharma Seed 68:53 2019-03-27

In this Q&A, Rob Burbea explores how twoness, eros, and imaginal practice shape one another: why relationship needs polarity, how eros differs from merger, and why particularity matters. He then turns to questions about eros for the self, the danger of craving, and how imaginal practice can widen into a sanctified sense of world rather than collapse into fantasy or self-absorption. The talk closes with practical advice on moving between imaginal and emptiness practices, and a short exchange on feminine figures such as Aphrodite, Tara, and Ragarati.

Chapters

  1. 00:30 Twoness and relationship - Burbea explains why twoness matters in relationship and why eros depends on a retained polarity rather than full merger.
  2. 06:31 Eros needs polarity - He contrasts eros with oneness, emphasizing tension, particularity, and the difference between attraction and dissolution.
  3. 13:08 Eros for the self - In response to a question about narcissism, he argues that the issue is the quality of eros, not whether the self can be its object.
  4. 21:45 Imaginal practice and cosmos - He describes imaginal practice as a way of sanctifying the self's world, then paraphrases a vivid scene of self, friend, and lover on the grass.
  5. 37:16 Emptiness opens into image - A question about emptiness leads into his distinction between skillful fabrication and skillful unfabricating, and how emptiness can itself become imaginal.
  6. 43:14 Eros and craving - Burbea develops the idea that eros and craving may share a root, then shows how balancing self, object, and world keeps the field open and alive.
  7. 57:10 Body as beloved - He addresses the body as an object of eros, noting that charge and pain often help imaginal practice become vivid and meaningful.
  8. 01:00:26 Feminine figures of desire - The final exchange turns to Aphrodite, Tara, Kuan Yin, and Ragarati, with Jung used to clarify archetype as a forming principle rather than a fixed image.
