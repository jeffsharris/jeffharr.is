Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Working with the Emotional Body (Instructions and Guided Meditation: Day Two)

Dharma Seed 55:44 2011-07-31

Rob Burbea offers a set of practical instructions for working with tiredness, physical discomfort, and emotional difficulty in meditation. He emphasizes a patient, exploratory attention: softening aversion, broadening awareness, and noticing how experience changes moment by moment. The talk includes a guided practice and short reflections on how a more open field of attention can become a source of safety and resource.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Introducing the approach - Burbea frames the practice as non-linear and invites patience with the gradual unfolding of skills.
  2. 07:03 Tiredness and aversion - He explores tiredness as a mix of bodily sensation and resistance, and suggests looking for the smaller experience beneath the sense of overwhelm.
  3. 09:58 Working with discomfort - He turns to itch and pain, recommending relaxation, broader awareness, and a gentler relationship to discomfort.
  4. 13:54 Softness and detail - Burbea contrasts hard, laser-like attention with a lighter touch, then shows how even calm or space can be noticed and cultivated.
  5. 20:39 Cézanne and patience - A passage on Cézanne supports the teaching that sustained, patient looking can make experience vivid and alive.
  6. 24:15 Guided central line practice - He begins the meditation, asking listeners to settle, touch the center line, and stay with whatever is most prominent without pressure.
  7. 33:51 Expanding the field - The practice widens from the center line into a balloon-like space of awareness that includes the whole body.
  8. 40:30 Inquiry and resourcing - Burbea responds to participants' comments, clarifying the difference between detailed attention and the more diffuse, protective field of awareness.
  9. 50:51 Two practices to explore - He closes by distinguishing the central-line and bubble practices, and encourages playful experimentation with both during walking and sitting.
