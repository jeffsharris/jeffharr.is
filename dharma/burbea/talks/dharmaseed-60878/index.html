Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Q & A

Dharma Seed 1:15:48 2020-01-01

In this Q&A, Rob Burbea moves from practical distinctions in jhana practice to a wider discussion of how attention shapes experience. He answers questions about pīti and sukha, how to work with primary nimitta versus side qualities, and how to tell when to stay with jhana and when a different practice is more appropriate. In the later part of the talk, he turns toward insight practice, dependent arising, and a reading of the Bahīya and Satipaṭṭhāna teachings that emphasizes how perception is fabricated rather than merely observed.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening questions - Rob opens the Q&A and frames the first question about jhana terminology and practice experience.
  2. 02:08 Primary nimitta and side qualities - He distinguishes the main jhana sign from nearby heart qualities like love, devotion, and nobility, and explains why the retreat stays on the jhana path.
  3. 08:40 Soulmaking and practice scope - Rob contrasts bounded dharma training with the open-ended field of soulmaking, then returns to questions about pīti, sukha, and refinement within jhana.
  4. 16:50 Pīti, sukha, and self - He clarifies bodily pervasion, the range within each jhana factor, and how less self-identification can naturally appear in second jhana.
  5. 27:50 Insight and concentration - Rob cautions against applying insight too early to jhana and describes how a stable concentration can later become an object for insight ways of looking.
  6. 35:07 Opening, probing, and steadiness - He works through how receptive openness and directed probing interact, using lake-diving and other images to show why the balance is fluid rather than formulaic.
  7. 44:41 Sustaining intensity - Rob addresses the tendency to probe too hard or to seek pleasure too passively, and suggests cultivating intensity without grasping while noticing how attention affects pain.
  8. 53:12 Ways of looking at pain - He develops the point that mindfulness is never bare, showing how different factors in attention can fabricate or unfabricate pain and how this opens into insight practice.
  9. 01:02:40 Bahīya and Satipaṭṭhāna - Rob rereads the Bahīya and Satipaṭṭhāna teachings as instructions about the arising and dissolution of experience, ending with a quiet closing and tea.
