Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Practicing in Times of Big Changes

Dharma Seed 34:50 2013-09-14

This talk reflects on practicing through instability, with a long opening of chant followed by a teaching on how change can be met without panic or resignation. Ayya Santacitta uses the butterfly story, the Buddha under the Bodhi tree, and dependent origination to show how clear seeing, patience, and mindfulness can meet breakdown without being overwhelmed by it. The discussion ends with a close look at feeling, grasping, and the small gap where practice can interrupt reactivity.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening chant - The talk begins with an extended chant and settles the room before the teaching starts.
  2. 20:10 Facing unavoidable change - She turns to the reality that things will not return to an old pattern and frames practice as a support for meeting what is here.
  3. 21:37 Butterfly transformation - A caterpillar-to-butterfly story becomes an image for cultural breakdown, latent change, and the conditions for a new worldview.
  4. 24:47 Seeing without forcing - She emphasizes that change is born from looking closely and staying with what is happening rather than trying to manufacture an outcome.
  5. 26:07 Mara becomes flowers - The Buddha’s night under the Bodhi tree is used to show how not moving away from difficulty can let wisdom and compassion grow.
  6. 27:28 Inner and outer climate - She links personal practice to collective conditions, including the climate crisis, while allowing for the need to rest and turn away sometimes.
  7. 31:01 Paññā and insight - Paññā is described as the capacity to be with things as they are, and repeated clear seeing is said to mature into insight.
  8. 33:21 Where practice interrupts - The closing question turns to feeling, grasping, and the quick moment where mindfulness can stop the cycle before it becomes anger or clinging.
