Aspects of the Imaginal (Part 6)

Dharma Seed 1:30:39 2017-12-20

How do imaginal images become more fully imaginal: by being noticed, lightly questioned, deliberately nudged, and held with enough delicacy that one quality can brighten the whole field. The talk moves through a practical list of aspects—meaningfulness, timelessness, the imaginal middle way, participation, and concept—showing how each can be tuned in practice rather than treated as an abstract definition. Along the way, the speaker returns to examples like body-as-light or body-as-jewels, and keeps insisting that impermanence is not denied but held together with a subtler sense of sacredness and poetic truth.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Framing the Teaching - He restates the purpose of the series and the practical reason for naming the qualities of the imaginal.
  2. 01:49 Amplifying the Image - He explains three ways to work with an imaginal node: notice it, question it, or deliberately turn it on.
  3. 05:58 Subtlety and Intention - He emphasizes delicacy in practice and gives examples of humility, love, and fullness of intention as catalysts.
  4. 10:31 Bodies of Light and Jewel - He offers deliberate imaginal images of the body, including body as light, mantra as jewels, and body as earth.
  5. 13:33 Meaningfulness and Devotion - He describes meaningfulness as larger than any single meaning, with a sense of pregnant significance and orientation.
  6. 17:01 Timelessness and Sacred Time - He develops the sense of eternality, contrast it with narrative time, and shows how it can open the imaginal world.
  7. 28:09 Holding Impermanence - He guards against misunderstanding timelessness as denial of impermanence and insists both dimensions can be held together.
  8. 32:27 Imaginal Middle Way - He distinguishes the imaginal middle way from ordinary realist categories and connects it to the theatre-like quality of imaginal experience.
  9. 50:54 Way of Looking - He explains that the imaginal depends on the perceiver's way of looking and that this dependence does not make it unreal.
