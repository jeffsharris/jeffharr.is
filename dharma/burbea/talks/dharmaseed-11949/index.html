Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Compassion Part Two (Compassion for Ourselves)

Dharma Seed 18:19 2008-02-05

Rob Burbea continues a talk on compassion by turning from compassion for others to compassion for oneself. He explores common resistance, the role of tenderness and empathy, how self-compassion can support insight, and why it need not feed ego. The talk closes by linking compassion to non-self and offering a few practical ways to keep the practice simple and deep.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and transition - He briefly corrects the previous talk and introduces the shift from compassion for others to compassion for the self.
  2. 01:29 Why self-compassion feels difficult - He names the common thought that others suffer more and tells a story from work in a leprosy community in India to unsettle easy assumptions about suffering.
  3. 03:10 Learning from our own pain - He explains why our own suffering is the most intimate and therefore the most useful place to learn about the arising of suffering and the roots of wisdom.
  4. 05:06 Balancing empathy and giving - He distinguishes compassion from pity and emphasizes balancing resonance with suffering and the felt quality of what is given out to keep practice sustainable.
  5. 07:08 How self-compassion works - He contrasts standard insight instructions with the softer, more enclosing quality of self-compassion, where attention rests around the pain rather than driving into it.
  6. 08:43 Ego, phrases, and delicacy - He addresses the concern that self-compassion might feed ego and suggests that tender, non-judgmental attention actually loosens selfing; he also notes that compassion can be a lighter, more delicate practice than metta.
  7. 11:26 Compassion as insight lens - He describes using a compassionate climate to notice subtle suffering, story-making, grasping, and identification, so that these patterns can unhook organically.
  8. 15:25 Compassion and non-self - He turns to a more radical possibility: seeing that what we call self is just changing moments of mind, which can open into anatta and increase compassion.
  9. 17:37 Choose one well - He closes with a Buddha image about digging one well deeply and encourages listeners to work with just one or two possibilities rather than scattering attention.
