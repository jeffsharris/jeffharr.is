Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

A Little Exercise for the Imagination

Dharma Seed 31:19 2015-08-08

In this short guided talk, Rob Burbea leads a series of simple imaginal exercises using a peach, an apple, a rose, a bedroom, a childhood home, a loved one, the seaside, a forest, and a piece of music. The point is not to produce vivid visual pictures, but to notice the felt sense, emotional tone, and energetic resonance that can come with an image. He then reflects on why this matters, especially for people who think they “don’t have images,” and suggests that imaginal sensitivity can be trained.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Settling In - The talk begins with a brief settling of posture and attention into the body and energy body.
  2. 01:59 The Peach Exercise - A peach is used to explore how imagination can involve texture, smell, taste, and subtle felt presence.
  3. 04:27 Apple and Rose - The practice shifts to an apple and then a rose, noticing how different senses and resonances may come forward.
  4. 07:21 Rooms and Home - The bedroom and the room you are in are brought into imagination, along with their mood, atmosphere, and relational feel.
  5. 10:34 Childhood House - An earlier home is used to deepen the practice, including entering rooms and noticing emotional associations.
  6. 13:15 Someone Loved - The imagery turns to a loved person, emphasizing their presence, personhood, and the particular quality of love felt toward them.
  7. 19:03 Seaside and Forest - Two larger scenes, a solitary beach and an autumn forest, are used to include movement, bodily sensation, and the atmosphere of the scene.
  8. 24:54 Music in Imagination - A familiar piece of music is imagined, with attention to how it shifts energy, mood, and emotional texture.
  9. 27:08 What the Exercise Shows - Burbea explains that the exercise is meant to show that imagination is broader than visual imagery and that imaginal sensitivity can be cultivated.
