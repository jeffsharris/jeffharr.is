Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Sharing Soul Sense (A Temenos Exercise)

Dharma Seed 1:15:27 2018-06-28

Rob Burbea offers a relational exercise for experienced practitioners, beginning with grounding, metta, and simple awareness of the energy body. The practice then moves through sharing in pairs or groups, first around bodily experience, then emotions, and finally something that feels touched by soul. He returns again and again to the sense that speaking, witnessing, and being seen all change the field, and frames the whole exercise as a protected temenos held in kindness.

Chapters

  1. 01:15 Setting Up the Exercise - Rob introduces the purpose of the practice, explains the optional stages, and has people form groups of three.
  2. 03:55 Grounding and Metta - He guides a brief settling into posture, grounding, and an initial opening to metta toward self and others.
  3. 09:24 First Round: Energy Body - Participants begin the first round by expressing what is happening in the energy body while others witness silently.
  4. 12:29 Witnessing and Response - He clarifies the role of the witness, the lack of commentary, and the importance of tracking how speaking and listening affect the body.
  5. 25:40 Second Round: Heart and Emotion - The exercise expands to include the heart and emotions, with attention to patterns in what people tend to share or leave out.
  6. 29:06 Metta as Background - Rob returns to metta as the quiet basis underneath the exchange, and the group continues rotating through the second stage.
  7. 40:09 Soul Desire and Image - The practice shifts inward to what the heart longs for, what brought each person here, and how devotion or longing may become image.
  8. 49:20 Soulful Sharing - He introduces the final sharing round, where anything that has touched the soul can be expressed through words, movement, or image within the temenos.
  9. 52:25 Closing in Reverence - The exercise continues through the last rotations and ends with a return to metta, gratitude, and the sense of being seen and seeing others.
