Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Emptiness and Ways of Looking

Dharma Seed 2:09:37 2020-02-11

How do you keep emptiness from collapsing into a slogan, a belief, or a privileged final view? The talk moves through a careful unpacking of “ways of looking,” showing how perception, clinging, and fabrication shape self, world, time, and suffering, and why that means there is no neutral place outside the lens. It then keeps reopening the inquiry: against views of a pure flux, against “seeing through” language that smuggles in realism, and against emptiness being treated as a conclusion rather than a practice that can be picked up and put down. Along the way, it connects emptiness to ethics, trauma, the imaginal, eros, and the question of whether modern practice needs more than insight alone.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the inquiry - Rob introduces the series, situates it in relation to earlier teachings, and names ontology, emptiness, and ways of looking as the main threads.
  2. 03:30 Ways of looking - He defines the ways-of-looking approach and explains how clinging and fabrication shape what is perceived, including self, world, and time.
  3. 09:48 No final resting place - He clarifies that there is no moment of consciousness without some way of looking, and warns against imagining a privileged or final lens of truth.
  4. 21:50 Seeing as, not through - He distinguishes his language of seeing as from other idioms of seeing through, and broadens fabrication from gross papanca to a wide spectrum of non-fabricating.
  5. 31:59 Subtle distortions - He takes up common misunderstandings about anatta practice, the self, and the unfabricated, especially where aversion or fixed beliefs distort the inquiry.
  6. 46:42 Practice and flexibility - He presents ways of looking as an art that requires experience and confidence, and shows how emptiness, liberation, love, and soulmaking all depend on it.
  7. 01:01:05 Ethics and imagination - He argues that dependent arising and ethics are built into this approach from the beginning, and then turns to imaginal practice and different ontological statuses.
  8. 01:21:55 Eros in practice - He says emptiness practice needs eros, curiosity, and real interest, then applies the same open-ended method to trauma rather than choosing between extreme views.
  9. 01:36:54 Quantum parallels and awakening - He briefly compares the teaching with quantum physics, returns to the Buddha’s middle way and dependent arising, and ends by asking whether awakening today must include more than emptiness insight alone.
