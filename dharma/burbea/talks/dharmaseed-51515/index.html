Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Growing the Energy Body (Q & A)

Dharma Seed 35:46 2018-06-23

In this Q&A, Rob Burbea clarifies how the energy body can be approached through simple awareness, supportive qualities like peace or happiness, and the use of image. He then explores the practical question of when to stay with what already works and when to experiment, including the relationship between samadhi, imaginal practice, and soulmaking. Later questions widen the frame to collective practice and the subtle difference between ordinary energy-body work and the larger field of sacred, imaginal sensitivity.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening the inquiry - Rob introduces the Q&A as a space for questions about the energy body and how to practice with it.
  2. 02:51 Qualities and tuning - He explains how attention can be tuned to qualities like peace, happiness, and rapture, and how this supports samadhi.
  3. 05:28 Working at the edges - Using Catherine's guidance, he discusses exploring what feels unfamiliar or uncomfortable without discarding useful habits.
  4. 10:43 Staying or shifting - He answers questions about balancing ease with the places that seem blocked, and distinguishes simple mindfulness from energy-body practice.
  5. 16:27 The imaginal fork - Rob addresses the relationship between soulmaking and samadhi, distinguishing sacredness, image, and the weighting of attention.
  6. 21:27 Collective field - The discussion turns to whether an energy body can be shared, and Rob affirms both collective energy-body and collective imaginal experience.
  7. 26:08 Mysterious resonance - He reflects on subtle, perhaps uncanny forms of shared experience and warns against collapsing the imaginal into the merely paranormal.
  8. 29:26 Wider sensitivity - In the closing section, Rob distinguishes the older skills of emptiness practice from the broader sensitivity of soulmaking, and encourages trust in the indicators already present.
