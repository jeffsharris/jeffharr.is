Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Opening Talk for Work Retreat - Working and Awakening

Dharma Seed 37:03 2008-03-05

What happens when work, meditation, and ordinary life are not treated as separate worlds, but as one field of practice? The talk opens by questioning the usual split between retreat life and everyday activity, then moves into a practical emphasis on balancing service, formal practice, awareness, intention, and view. From there, it turns repeatedly to small but decisive questions—what is being put first right now, how is this difficulty being seen, and how can the heart be cared for through silence, gratitude, and the precepts?

Chapters

  1. 00:00 What a retreat is - He begins by welcoming people and reflecting on why human beings have long stepped aside from ordinary life to inquire into it.
  2. 03:02 Work and meditation - He describes the work retreat as a distinctive form in which work and formal practice are meant to be taken as equally part of the path.
  3. 07:54 Service as practice - He speaks about service to Gaia House as an offering and points to its often-untended spiritual potential.
  4. 12:16 Awareness and intention - He names mindfulness as central, then turns to intentionality and how retreat simplicity can reveal what we really want.
  5. 17:13 Renewing the aim - Using viriya, or persistence, he explores how intentions fade into comfort, self-protection, or image, and how practice is to notice and reset.
  6. 20:40 View and difficulty - He links right view to freedom in the face of things not going as planned, and reframes unwanted experiences as occasions for inquiry.
  7. 25:14 Caring for the heart - He suggests taking care of the heart through appreciation, gratitude, and noticing the many conditions that support retreat practice.
  8. 29:43 Silence and simplicity - He explains how silence and a simple schedule help deeper intentions surface and encourages people to settle into them.
  9. 34:08 Precepts and trust - He closes with the five precepts, showing how shared ethical commitment creates trust and helps the heart relax and open.
