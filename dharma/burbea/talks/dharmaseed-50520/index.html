Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Elements of the Imaginal (15 - 18)

Dharma Seed 31:09 2018-02-06

Rob Burbea continues a retreat teaching on the imaginal by introducing four more nodes in the lattice: non-reducibility, autonomy, twoness, and theatre. He emphasizes that soul-making is intermittent and should be met with a fluid mix of practices, not forced into one mode all day. The talk then moves into a careful critique of reductionism, a discussion of images as primary and autonomous, and a closing account of the imaginal as a middle way between real and not real.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening and context - Burbea begins by situating the evening talk after a full day and sets up the plan to move quickly through four more nodes.
  2. 04:59 Grace as gift - He returns to Catherine's note on grace to frame soul-sensing as an intermittent gift rather than a stable state one can possess.
  3. 06:02 Moving between practices - He recommends a wide, responsive palette of practices and warns against overusing imaginal practice alone.
  4. 09:14 Nodes are interwoven - Burbea explains that the nodes are not linear stages and that receptivity and technique, grace and effort, can work together.
  5. 11:04 Against reductionism - He develops the 15th node by showing how scientific and psychological reductionism flatten the richness and mystery of imaginal perception.
  6. 15:17 Images as primary - He offers a stronger imaginal view in which images shape life and perception rather than merely reflecting past causes.
  7. 17:58 Double autonomy - The 16th node is presented as the autonomy of both the imaginal object and the practitioner, who must freely assent to the relation.
  8. 20:52 Twoness and eros - He describes the 17th node as preserving distinction and erotic tension rather than collapsing into undifferentiated unity.
  9. 24:52 Theatre and middle way - The final section turns to the 18th node, where the imaginal has a theatre-like quality: neither simply real nor unreal, but a distinct and liberating mode of truth.
