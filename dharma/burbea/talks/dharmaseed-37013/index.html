Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Mindfulness and Myth

Dharma Seed 1:19:09 2016-07-27

Rob Burbea explores how mindfulness changes when it is grounded not in hidden realism, but in emptiness, dependent origination, and the flexibility of ways of looking. He contrasts this with secular, psychological, and religious forms of realism, and argues for a wider, more plural field of practice that can include imaginal and re-enchanting approaches without losing rigor. The talk also offers practical guidance on how to listen to Dharma: for relevance, for the bigger picture, and for what may not fit existing conceptual boxes.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Setting the spectrum - He clarifies the retreat's basic orientation by describing a spectrum between freedom and healing on one side and beauty, sacredness, and soulmaking on the other.
  2. 02:33 Personal framework - He frames teachings as personal confessions, then explains that the retreat's emphasis is a response to the surrounding cultural and Dharma context.
  3. 05:18 Realism everywhere - He surveys religious, secular, psychological, and Dharma forms of realism and argues that non-realist practice is rare and often unstable in received forms.
  4. 09:26 Secularism and disenchantment - He critiques aggressive secularism, compares it with religious fundamentalism, and begins to describe the broader cultural loss of enchantment.
  5. 18:25 Scientism and flat cosmos - He turns to modern disenchantment, scientific materialism, and scientism, contrasting these with emptiness as a radically different basis for practice.
  6. 26:36 Ways of looking - He explains practice as the flexible entertaining of different ways of looking, with all Dharma concepts treated as skillful perspectives rather than truths.
  7. 38:16 Emptiness as basis - He argues that emptiness and dependent origination can become the actual ground of the path, and that many modern renderings of mindfulness still smuggle in realism.
  8. 01:05:31 Listening structurally - He shows how imaginal practice can coexist with emptiness, then shifts to the practical question of how to listen for what is new, relevant, and structurally coherent.
  9. 01:16:25 Teaching in chunks - He closes by reminding listeners that teachings arrive in pieces, that later sections can reframe earlier ones, and that the retreat's themes are mutually dependent.
