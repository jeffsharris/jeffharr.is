Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Working with the Emotional Body (Instructions and Guided Meditation: Day Seven)

Dharma Seed 18:33 2011-08-05

How do emotions arise, and what changes when we stop treating them as fixed things? The talk moves between several ways of working with emotional life: seeing an inner critic as a character or as passing thoughts, simplifying experience down to body sensation, and noticing how stories and self-views can lock feeling into place. It also points to quieter moments in meditation, where emotion is seen beginning to form, and asks whether we have the courage both to feel difficult states and to let them go quiet.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 One Perspective Among Many - Burbea frames the talk as a tying-together of previous instructions rather than a new practice.
  2. 01:19 Inner Dynamics and Characters - He explores emotions as dynamic forces or as inner persons, especially the useful and risky ways of working with an inner critic.
  3. 03:19 Stories and Simplicity - The talk turns to how stories can either simplify experience through direct sensation or harden it into a fixed self-view.
  4. 05:18 How Emotion Forms - He describes seeing emotion arise in quiet meditation and suggests that emotions may be habitually fabricated rather than simply stored and released.
  5. 07:42 What Fabrication Gives - Burbea asks what an emotion may be giving us, including not only suffering but sometimes richness, soulfulness, or depth.
  6. 10:56 Self-View and Looping - He examines the loop between self-view and emotion, illustrated by the practice of exposing a self-blaming story until it becomes visible and absurd.
  7. 14:12 Courage to Feel - The talk widens to courage on both sides: to feel what is difficult, and also to let feeling quiet down when that threatens identity.
  8. 17:05 Resources Becoming Accessible - Burbea closes by distinguishing cathartic release from releasing strength or power into accessibility, then gathers the many perspectives into a final synthesis.
