Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Between Ikon and Eidos: Image & Hermeneutics in Meditation (Part 5 - Bodhicitta)

Dharma Seed 60:33 2018-01-13

Rob Burbea explores what people usually mean by “living life to the full,” and gently questions the modern assumptions hidden in that phrase. He moves from a retreat walk story into a wider critique of flat mindfulness, then opens the discussion toward meaning, image, value, bodhicitta, and Buddha-nature. The talk ends by asking how these ideals become personal, embodied, and alive in a unique life.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Living life fully? - Rob begins with the phrase “living life to the full” and uses a retreat walk story to show how the idea can become self-conscious and miss what is immediately present.
  2. 01:53 Limits of modern mindfulness - He unpacks how common ideas of presence and mindfulness can stay flat, materialist, or one-dimensional unless a deeper dimensionality is explicitly named and supported.
  3. 07:16 Beyond flat presence - The talk widens into the role of ideas in teaching culture, and how language that seems open can still conceal a modernist framework that limits depth and imagination.
  4. 13:32 World and transcendence - Rob asks what “fullness” means, brings in Robert Bellah on world-rejection, and contrasts modern assumptions with Buddhist aspirations toward transcendence and non-rebirth.
  5. 19:43 Meaning, death, and beauty - He develops fullness as inseparable from meaning, fantasy, and image, and then shows how deep love of life also includes death, insouling even what is impermanent.
  6. 28:40 Bodhicitta and image - Rob turns to bodhicitta, distinguishing ultimate and relative forms, and shows how aspiration, values, and tantric image can function as a living imaginal orientation beyond this life.
  7. 38:29 Buddha-nature as matrix - Drawing on Shenpen Hookham, he explores tathagatagarbha as womb, matrix, embryo, and hidden treasure, emphasizing its poetic flexibility and meditative use.
  8. 48:27 Personal blossoming - He closes by stressing that bodhicitta and values must become personal, distinguishing generic ideals from a unique path shaped by images, Jungian individuation, Henri Corbin’s angel out ahead, and Hartmann’s account of personality through values.
