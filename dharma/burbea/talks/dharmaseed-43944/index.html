Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Modes of Soulmaking (Q & A)

Dharma Seed 36:24 2017-04-01

What if emptiness is not a deflationary view, but a way of opening into mystery, divinity, and a wider range of mystical experience? In this Q&A, the talk moves from the relation between samadhi and emptiness as less-fabricated states, into how imaginal practice can spill into the world as cosmopoiesis, changing how perception itself makes reality feel. It then turns to eros, the pull of image and otherness, and how the path is less about forcing results than trusting what feels most alive as it unfolds in different ways for different people.

Chapters

  1. 02:19 Emptiness and divinity - He contrasts a deflationary reading of emptiness with an understanding of emptiness as a doorway into mystery, divinity, and multiple kinds of mystical experience.
  2. 07:14 Samadhi and less fabrication - Rob explains that samadhi and emptiness are related by progressively less fabrication of self, other, and world, and places the classical lists of practice inside that movement.
  3. 10:39 Cosmopoiesis explained - He defines cosmopoiesis as the way perception helps create the cosmos in a beautiful way, and shows how an imaginal image can spill over into the world.
  4. 13:04 The tree example - Using an image of a tree, he shows how imaginal qualities can spread in subtle, partial, and gradual ways rather than appearing as a single dramatic shift.
  5. 16:52 Not making it happen - Rob encourages trusting what is already beautiful and noticing the imaginal rather than forcing results, while also describing the fourfold interplay of self, other, world, and eros.
  6. 21:02 Trusting eros - In response to questions about directionality and gendered language, he says eros should be followed where it is most alive and allowed to unfold in its own order.
  7. 23:42 Otherness and merging - He clarifies that eros tends to generate more otherness and multiplicity rather than simple fusion, even when the experience feels like merging with an image.
  8. 26:14 Soulmaking and Tantra - Rob compares soulmaking with Tantra, noting similarities but also important differences in openness, prescription, and the sense that the tantric field may be effectively infinite.
  9. 28:59 Hillman and influence - He briefly names James Hillman as an important influence in developing the conceptual language around soulmaking.
