Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Thoughts and Images in Meditation

Dharma Seed 56:45 2012-11-06

Thoughts are first treated as a problem to be softened rather than fought: when the mind is buzzing or scattered, the talk keeps returning to the body, to patience, and to a gentler level of attention. From there it moves into more active methods—labeling, questioning beliefs, and noticing how emotion and body sensation feed obsessive loops—before opening into a wider field where thoughts, sounds, and sensations appear and pass like fireworks in space. The later part shifts again, arguing that thought itself is not the issue so much as the views we believe, and then broadens into the skillful use of images, including metta, light imagery, deity practice, and the subtle work of loosening self-view.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the topic - Burbea frames the talk as a practical look at thought and image in meditation, while noting that the subject is not simple.
  2. 02:13 Scattered thought and effort - He describes buzzing, drifting, and restlessness, emphasizing softness, body awareness, patience, and experimentation over forcing the mind.
  3. 10:20 Labeling and relieving - The talk turns to subtle trains of thought, labeling, and the relief that can come from cutting a thought short and feeling that release in the body.
  4. 17:10 Obsession and belief - Burbea examines obsessive thinking as something that rests on beliefs, assumptions, and often unspoken views that can be questioned.
  5. 23:38 Emotion in the body - He offers a close method for working with obsession by finding the emotion in the body, tuning in to vedana, and sometimes meeting anger, hurt, or fear more directly.
  6. 29:00 Open awareness - The practice widens into an open field where body sensations, sounds, and thoughts arise and disappear like fireworks, loosening identification.
  7. 33:18 Skillful use of image - Thought and image are then presented as deliberate practices in metta, breath, jhana, cemetery contemplation, light imagery, and deity imagery.
  8. 43:48 Views and conceiving - In the final movement, Burbea argues that suffering rests not on thought alone but on believed views and conceivings, which shape perception and must eventually be seen through.
