Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation - Cittamatra

Dharma Seed 44:34 2009-02-02

The meditation begins by settling awareness into the body, letting calm gather through the breath or mettā, and then gradually opens into listening, stillness, and silence. From there, the practice widens into a sky-like awareness: sounds, sensations, thoughts, and even self-sense are invited to appear and fade without struggle, control, or rejection. Near the end, the talk links this spacious seeing to three characteristics practice, suggesting a way of sensing experience as impressions in awareness rather than solid things.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling the body - The meditation begins by settling awareness into the body and nurturing calm through breath, mettā, or another familiar support.
  2. 02:17 Body and stillness - Attention deepens into bodily sensation, with an emphasis on filling the body with awareness and sensing stillness permeating it.
  3. 05:51 Sound and silence - Awareness opens to sound, then to the silence around and within sound, as a way of sensing the relationship between appearance and stillness.
  4. 08:25 Awareness as sky - Burbea introduces the image of mind like a clear sky, spacious enough to receive sensations, sounds, and thoughts without strain.
  5. 11:02 Letting things be - The practice turns toward non-struggle, non-rejection, and non-control, while emphasizing that whatever arises is embraced and undisturbed.
  6. 25:55 Impressions in awareness - Experience is reframed as appearances, reflections, or impressions in awareness, including body sensations, thoughts, images, and the sense of self.
  7. 36:27 Waves on the ocean - He extends the imagery with waves and ocean, showing experience as one substance taking different forms and then dissolving back again.
  8. 41:36 Three characteristics and practice - The talk closes by linking the imagery to three characteristics practice and suggesting ways to explore it with eyes open, eyes closed, walking, or standing.
