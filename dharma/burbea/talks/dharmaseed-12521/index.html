Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Freeing the Self

Dharma Seed 55:31 2011-02-06

How does a rigid sense of self tighten suffering, and what happens when that grip is loosened? The talk begins with the Four Noble Truths and then stays close to practice, showing how self-definitions like “I’m a failure” or “I’m the kind of person who…” can feed a loop of pain, while careful attention can reveal relief, love, and a broader sense of possibility. It also distinguishes between seeing emptiness as a practical way of looking and misunderstanding it as nihilism, self-erasure, or a flattening of personality. Toward the end, the focus widens to the subtler felt sense of self across different moods, emotions, and meditation states, and to how that sense can become more open and less solid.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Setting the frame - Rob lays out the evening’s plan and places the retreat in a broader map of practice.
  2. 00:59 Four Noble Truths - He summarizes the Dharma through suffering, craving, misunderstanding, and the possibility of freedom.
  3. 04:55 Middle way and emptiness - Using the Kaccayana teaching, he explains emptiness as the middle way beyond existence and non-existence.
  4. 06:58 Practicing with selflessness - He shifts from philosophy to practice, emphasizing that understanding comes by loosening identification, not by intellectual debate.
  5. 09:47 Responses to emptiness - He names the range of reactions to the word emptiness, from openness and beauty to fear, bleakness, and annihilation.
  6. 13:33 Working with self-definitions - Rob distinguishes personality-level and more subtle identifications, then shows how painful self-definitions can be seen through in practice.
  7. 21:53 Love and relief - He argues that loosening self-definitions brings more love, and he illustrates this with examples of work, attention-seeking, and intimacy.
  8. 31:34 Stories of hidden dimensions - Two stories show how identity can obscure deeper loves and capacities, and why emptiness should not be mistaken for erasing personality.
  9. 41:53 Sense of self and balance - He turns to the felt sense of self across social stress, meditation, and metta, ending by presenting self and emptiness as complementary tools.
