Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Heart Work

Dharma Seed 1:13:40 2010-08-08

Rob Burbea explores two sides of caring for ourselves: learning to meet difficult emotions skillfully, and learning how metta can be nourished, deepened, and made more spacious. He treats mechanical practice as normal, then moves into a detailed discussion of fear, vulnerability, judgment, body-based softening, and practical ways to reframe the inner response to suffering. Later he shifts toward what feeds loving-kindness — wonder, spaciousness, intention, and everyday action — before closing with Roz Savage’s story as a concrete example of how small, repeated choices can reshape a life.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Metta Has Nuance - He opens by naming the richness of metta practice and the need to look closely at its many moving parts.
  2. 03:24 Three Practice Modes - Using a sailing metaphor, he outlines three ways practice can unfold: stable metta, metta with difficulty, and turning directly toward difficulty.
  3. 06:08 Caring for Suffering - He frames self-care as both meeting difficulty and cultivating well-being, then begins the first extended exploration of emotional suffering in practice.
  4. 12:48 Fear and Opening - He describes how metta can bring fear, vulnerability, tears, and even resistance, and how to work with those reactions without forcing them away.
  5. 18:44 Judgment and Space - He turns to judgmental reactions, relanguaging inner criticism, and the value of spaciousness, curiosity, and softened awareness.
  6. 23:14 Seven Skillful Responses - He offers a set of practical responses for meeting difficult emotions, including kindness, widening the frame, self-compassion, and body-based softening.
  7. 32:25 Story and Sensation - He distinguishes emotional story from raw bodily sensation, and shows how moving between those levels can loosen fixed views of the self.
  8. 40:37 Feeding Metta - He turns to what nourishes loving-kindness: seeing practice as kindness, beginning with what is easy, and inviting wonder and spaciousness.
  9. 54:16 Intention and Change - He concludes with the power of intention, the malleability of the heart and mind, and Roz Savage’s life as an illustration of repeated small actions making deep change.
