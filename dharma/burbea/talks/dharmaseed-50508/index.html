Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Body and Soul (Q & A)

Dharma Seed 32:43 2018-02-03

In this Q&A, Rob Burbea explores the energy body as a broad, flexible field rather than a fixed technique or object. He traces how bodily resistance can be understood as clinging, how relaxing that clinging changes perception, and how different energetic experiences can all belong to one open range of practice. The talk then moves into the relationship between samadhi, the rupa jhanas, and soulmaking, including how body-based well-being, image, and meaning can support each other.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the Q&A - Rob frames the session as an open question-and-answer time and invites people to bring whatever is most useful in the moment.
  2. 01:32 Many Intelligences in the Body - In response to a question about organs and multiple consciousnesses, he describes the energy body as a stretchable concept that can include different kinds of bodily intelligence.
  3. 04:06 Growing the Energy Body - He emphasizes that the energy body can become more complex, beautiful, sacred, and soulmaking as practice deepens.
  4. 06:51 Resistance as Clinging - A question about resistance leads into a clear explanation of clinging, fabrication, and how relaxation makes experience more fluid and less painful.
  5. 09:27 Different Energetic Fields - Rob works through a question about two distinct bodily fields and explains that both belong to the energy body, which can appear subtle, intense, pleasant, or neutral.
  6. 15:12 Samadhi Direction - He distinguishes the energy-body work that supports samadhi from other uses, suggesting that one can gently let pleasantness spread without grasping.
  7. 18:22 Jhana as Spectrum - He then turns to jhana as meditative absorption, presenting the four rupa jhanas as a continuum of bodily rapture, happiness, peacefulness, and stillness, leading into spacious refinement.
  8. 22:26 Care and Navigation - Rob broadens the discussion to emotional sensitivity, practice navigation, and how energy-body awareness can help a person recognize when a practice is aligned.
  9. 25:08 Soulmaking, Image, and Opening - He closes by distinguishing felt sense, body image, and imaginal image, using a rosebush image and a discussion of practice devotion to show how soulmaking can arise through beauty, meaning, and the quieting of image in jhana.
