Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Metta Instructions and Guided Meditation 2

Dharma Seed 48:24 2010-08-08

The talk keeps returning to one question: how do you stay gently with metta without forcing it? The speaker describes practice as something light, responsive, and flexible, using the image of sailing to show how one adjusts to conditions rather than pushing through them. He moves through practical guidance on phrases, bodily sensitivity, and the different ways metta can feel, then introduces the friend as the next object of practice and opens into a guided meditation. Imagination is treated as part of the method here, a way to let warmth, tenderness, and well-wishing take form.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Setting the tone - Burbea introduces the day’s focus and frames metta as light, spacious, playful, and responsive rather than effortful.
  2. 03:42 Working with hindrances - He names common obstacles like sadness, sleepiness, doubt, restlessness, and anger, and suggests flexible ways to meet them without abandoning metta.
  3. 06:36 Flexible practice forms - The talk shifts to the practical range inside metta: choosing categories, using what is easiest, and varying phrases from repeated sentences to a single word or silence.
  4. 10:16 What metta can feel like - He describes metta as a broad field of qualities—calming, healing, warm, bright, gentle, bubbly, or compassion-colored—and normalizes the absence of a strong feeling.
  5. 13:58 Body and resonance - Burbea emphasizes sensitivity to the whole body, listening for how different phrases resonate, and noticing subtle bodily warmth or openness without forcing it.
  6. 19:32 Simple self and basics - He explains the move from narrative self toward a simpler body-being sense, then introduces the friend category and begins the guided practice.
  7. 24:21 Using imagination - During the practice he encourages visual and kinesthetic imagination—warmth, softness, light, and tenderness—as a way to support the heart’s response.
  8. 32:49 Metta for a friend - The guided meditation turns to a friend, evoking their goodness and wish for well-being, and extending loving-kindness through images, touch, and radiating warmth.
  9. 43:04 Closing the practice - The meditation winds down by letting the sense of care settle, then returning to the body and ending quietly.
