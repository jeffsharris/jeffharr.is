Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

This Fire, This Longing (Q & A)

Dharma Seed 47:26 2016-08-02

What does an image ask of us once it leaves the cushion? The talk moves through that question by considering how imaginal practice can continue off-retreat through reflection, writing, subtle changes in life, or simply reverence, rather than by any fixed rule. It then turns to the tension between healing and devotion, the difference between a mental agenda and the heart’s longing, and how imaginal practice can widen into a change in perception that “re-enchants” the world. In the final stretch, the conversation broadens to image and idea, artistic making, striving and non-striving, and a closing note on responsibility, keeping the focus on what actually helps a practice stay alive.

Chapters

  1. 01:46 Images Off Retreat - He describes how imaginal figures can be honored through meditation, reflection, writing, creative work, or subtle daily reverence.
  2. 06:49 Healing and Devotion - He responds to the tension between practicing for oneself and practicing for something larger, and suggests holding both without forcing a dichotomy.
  3. 11:59 From Mind to Heart - He distinguishes a mental agenda from the heart's yearning and links imaginal healing to a broader re-enchantment of perception.
  4. 15:12 Image and Idea - He clarifies that image and idea are deeply intertwined, including in insight practice, and that meaningful things often live as both.
  5. 23:32 Dukkha and Soulmaking - He considers whether suffering can become soulmaking and points to the hidden ideas that shape how dukkha is experienced.
  6. 25:16 The Artist's Dilemma - He reflects on being an artist and a practitioner at once, including the pull between process and result.
  7. 29:02 Striving and Result - He uses Natalie Goldberg and his own creative life to question Dharma ideas about effort, stress, and caring about the outcome.
  8. 33:16 What Is Helpful - He widens the discussion to intention, the danger of subtle self-inflation, and the place of striving and non-striving in different traditions.
  9. 39:37 Energy Body and Responsibility - He closes by discussing modulating effort in practice, the energy body across the jhanas, and the responsibility we have to the soul and to what feels soulmaking.
