Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Fourth Morning Instructions

Dharma Seed 18:46 2008-08-12

How do concentration, perception, and comfort change when the talk stops treating the breath as a fixed object and starts treating experience as something we can gently work with? The talk reviews three practical levers in meditation: the quality of the breath, the way the breath is conceived in the body, and the amount of effort or receptivity in attention. From there it moves into how labeling can alter the felt experience of pain, restlessness, and tiredness, and ends by returning to steadiness as the quiet discipline of not feeding restlessness and letting samadhi deepen. Briefly, an anecdote about Ajahn Chah underscores the patience of this kind of training.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Review the practice - He sets aside fixed ideas about jhana and reviews the morning instructions as an open-ended practice with the breath and body.
  2. 05:33 Breath and attention - He outlines three things to experiment with: breath quality, how the breath is conceived in the body, and the effort level of attention.
  3. 09:06 Perception and labels - He shifts from technique to perception, showing how naming the body as breath can change experience over time.
  4. 11:28 Working with discomfort - He describes how to meet pain, constriction, and tiredness with allowing awareness rather than immediate resistance.
  5. 13:44 Seeing differently - He explores chopping up solid sensations, perceiving brightness, and even choosing pleasantness in the midst of unpleasantness.
  6. 15:24 Steadiness feeds samadhi - He closes by urging steadiness in sitting, walking, and ordinary retreat mood swings, presenting it as a quiet way of nourishing samadhi.
