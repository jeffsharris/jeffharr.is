Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

"Until All is Liquid" (Emptiness and Sensing with Soul)

Dharma Seed 1:41:34 2019-05-29

How does loosening perception without losing form make imaginal practice possible? The talk begins with an alchemical maxim—wait until all is liquid—and uses it to trace a middle territory where things are less fabricated but not yet vanished. From there it moves through emptiness as a support for soulmaking, the danger of turning practice into a “should,” and the shift from striving for more fading to curiosity, ease, and insight into dependent arising. Later it turns to examples from illness, lineage devotion, and prayer, showing how even modest emptiness can deepen image, self-sense, and a sense of sacredness without flattening them.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Until All Is Liquid - Rob opens with the alchemical maxim and uses it to describe the loosening of perception needed for imaginal practice.
  2. 06:05 When Emptiness Helps - He explains why some practitioners need emptiness as a basis for soulmaking, while others may not, and why eros matters more than obligation.
  3. 14:34 Curiosity Over Shoulding - The talk turns to emptiness as a journey, with emphasis on curiosity, ease, and the shift away from striving for more fading.
  4. 23:38 Partial Fading, Real Insight - Rob argues that even incomplete fading can reveal clinging, dependent origination, and the constructed nature of perception.
  5. 33:37 Ways of Looking - He develops the central idea that perception is shaped by ways of looking, and that these ways can become imaginal rather than merely philosophical.
  6. 47:38 Empty Yet Soulful - A detailed example from illness shows how a simple 'just appearances' practice can support stamina, and how appearance can be transparent rather than dismissive.
  7. 01:06:12 Parallels Across Traditions - Rob briefly brings in Corbin, tantric practice, Schneur Zalman, and tummo to show that emptiness, eros, and image are woven together in other lineages too.
  8. 01:16:17 Emptiness in Daily Practice - He returns to the practical scale of the work, showing how even small degrees of emptiness can open prayerfulness, love, and imaginal depth.
  9. 01:26:50 Middle Ways and Flexibility - The closing section distinguishes the imaginal middle way from the emptiness middle way and emphasizes that the right degree of reification varies with person and situation.
