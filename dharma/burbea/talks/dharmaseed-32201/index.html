Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

An Ecology of Love (Part 2)

Dharma Seed 57:32 2015-12-21

What happens if love is not just a feeling, but a way perception changes shape? The talk begins with metta as intention, action, and universality, then asks why that expansive, even equanimous, opening does not always lead to activism or care for the natural world. From there it turns toward eros as the desire to connect more, showing how that wanting can deepen into image, fantasy, and a richer, more dimensional sense of nature, matter, and the sacred.

Chapters

  1. 00:20 Metta as intention - He begins by defining metta as intention rather than feeling, and as something that must become acts of kindness and care.
  2. 03:43 Universal love, equanimity - He follows metta as it spreads outward, deepens into oneness, and can sometimes cool into equanimity that does not always lead to activism.
  3. 10:10 Eros and pothos - He shifts to eros as the desire to connect more, adding pothos as the impulse that always wants further connection and opening.
  4. 13:26 Boundaries opening - Eros is described as pushing through boundaries, involving psyche and soul, and enlarging the sense of the beloved or of nature beyond the merely sensual.
  5. 19:47 Nature becomes multidimensional - He develops how eros complicates and enriches perception, adding image, fantasy, and vertical dimensions to the experience of earth and nature.
  6. 30:16 Spirit and matter - He brings in tantric and alchemical language, including Buddha nature and the spiritualization of matter, to show perception as a practice of transformation.
  7. 36:52 Stories from the garden - Small stories from Gaia House and a discussion of George Monbiot illustrate how ordinary ecological facts can be felt either mechanistically or as imaginal and sacred.
  8. 45:26 Horizontal and vertical - He contrasts interconnectedness in a horizontal, ecological sense with a vertical sense in which nature echoes or expresses the divine.
  9. 53:11 Poetic sensibility - He closes by framing this as a poetic way of seeing that opens through eros and brings more soulfulness to the world.
