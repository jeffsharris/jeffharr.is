Bob Doppelt, James Baraz: Earth As Witness: International Dharma Teachers' Statement on Climate Change

Dharma Seed 63:47 2014-01-16

How do we meet climate change without treating it as something outside our own lives? The talk begins with simple breath-based inquiry and expands into the carbon cycle, the greenhouse effect, and the urgency of rising emissions, then turns toward a Dharma teachers' statement that links suffering, craving, and interdependence to climate response. From there it becomes practical: household changes, mindful consumption, collective action, policy pressure, and a final move into loving-kindness for the Earth. The overall arc is sober but not despairing, holding the crisis as a call to wake up and act together.

Chapters

  1. 01:53 Opening the conversation - James Baraz introduces Bob Doppelt, his climate work, and the book that connects Dharma principles with climate response.
  2. 04:57 Breath and interdependence - Bob begins by asking how we are part of the Earth's climate, using breath, oxygen, carbon, and daily life to show direct participation in Earth systems.
  3. 12:39 The science of warming - He explains greenhouse gases, the long time lag of climate effects, and why the present crisis is both serious and still responsive to action.
  4. 17:11 Facing the suffering - Bob names the fear and sorrow in the room and prepares listeners to hear the teachers' statement as something to feel, not only analyze.
  5. 19:09 Earth as Witness - He reads the climate statement from Dharma teachers, linking climate change to suffering, craving, aversion, delusion, and the need for swift reductions in emissions.
  6. 31:53 Dharma principles for action - The statement unfolds into wisdom, ethical conduct, mindfulness, collective effort, and a call to reshape institutions and daily habits.
  7. 43:55 Hope through visible change - James and Bob discuss cautious optimism, the importance of talking about concrete changes, and how shared examples can shift consciousness.
  8. 47:46 Individual and systemic shifts - Bob gives examples from home energy use, solar power, policy, corporations, and regulation to show how personal and institutional change reinforce each other.
  9. 56:57 Tipping points and metta - The talk closes with the idea that a small committed shift can matter, a reminder that suffering can become initiation, and a loving-kindness practice for the Earth.
