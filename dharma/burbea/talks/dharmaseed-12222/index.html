Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Ocean of Patience

Dharma Seed 55:22 2007-09-17

How do we stay with practice when the mind wants a result, a breakthrough, or an escape? The talk moves from the grit of retreat life—aches, boredom, hindrances, waiting in line, waiting for the bell—into a steadier inquiry into what patience actually is: perseverance, calm endurance, and the willingness to meet difficulty without adding more suffering. It then widens into daily life, showing how impatience is fed by time-thinking, self-judgment, and the belief that we need to get somewhere else before things can be okay.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening Reflections - He introduces patience as a central quality and names two main directions for the talk: patience in practice and patience in ordinary life.
  2. 02:53 Practice Needs Patience - Using retreat experience and Ajahn Chah, he frames practice as working with suffering in ways that lead toward liberation rather than more struggle.
  3. 04:53 Joyful Acceptance - He cites Gampopa and the Bodhisattva Bhumi to expand patience into joyful acceptance of the difficulties involved in awakening and Dharma work.
  4. 09:00 Long View of Effort - Through examples from music, poetry, and relationships, he shows how worthwhile things often require long, patient commitment.
  5. 13:12 Gradual Transformation - He argues that healing and awakening are usually gradual, and that the mind is a shapeable collection of habits rather than a fixed self.
  6. 18:43 Urgency and Wisdom - He holds patience together with urgency, then turns to the Buddha’s images of farming and the right care of ethics, concentration, and understanding.
  7. 23:22 Investigating Impatience - He examines impatience as a habit that closes the heart, and uses the question 'what is feeding this?' to shift attention from outer objects to conditions in mind.
  8. 30:55 Present Moment and Allowing - He offers ways to work with impatience through respect for the present moment, careful questioning, and allowing painful parts of experience to soften.
  9. 43:27 Time, Solidity, and Release - He brings the inquiry to the mind’s habit of building time and situations into something solid, ending with Krishnamurti’s line that true patience is not of time.
