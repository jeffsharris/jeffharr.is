Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Eros Unfettered (Part 3)

Dharma Seed 59:44 2017-01-25

Rob Burbea continues his exploration of eros, psyche, and logos by showing how soulmaking depends on ways of looking that leave room for depth, mystery, and further unfolding. He contrasts rigid materialist or reductionist views with less-fabricating contemplative perspectives, then turns to examples from sexuality, pornography, relationship, and difficulty to show how perception can either flatten experience or re-enchant it. The talk closes by returning to the practical question of how concepts can be lived, flexibly used, and allowed to open rather than close the world.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the frame - Burbea sets up the question of what is needed for soulmaking and why the ideas have to be revisited and digested in lived practice.
  2. 02:46 Eros as open-ended - He develops the provisional definition of eros as desire to contact more, emphasizing that touch, opening, and knowing have no fixed endpoint.
  3. 07:57 Three ways of looking - He lays out three broad modes of perception: reductionist realism, less-fabricating practice, and soulmaking ways of looking.
  4. 10:49 Mystery and depth - The talk turns to the sense of unfathomability in another person, nature, or an image, and how that mystery itself feeds eros.
  5. 15:19 Eros and logos - Burbea shows how eros generates more image and more world, while logos and psyche must also be able to open enough to receive that movement.
  6. 21:43 When view flattens - Using materialism as a clear example, he shows how a narrow concept of reality can prevent imaginal depth and force eros into one dimension.
  7. 28:31 Pornography and greed - He uses pornography and related patterns as examples of objectification, soullessness, and blocked erotic expansion, while noting that kindness may still be present.
  8. 33:56 Multidimensional soulmaking - Burbea broadens the frame to show how any shrinking of reality to one level limits soulmaking, and how eros, psyche, and logos function together.
  9. 41:14 Relationship and re-enchantment - He works through fixed images of self, sex, and marriage, then ends with the possibility of meeting difficulty, even dukkha, in a way that restores imaginal depth.
