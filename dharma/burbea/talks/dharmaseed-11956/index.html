Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Using Insight To Deepen Love and Compassion

Dharma Seed 1:49:51 2008-02-02

How can love deepen when the mind looks more closely at clinging, fear, impermanence, and self? The talk moves from the basics of metta practice into a series of practical experiments: softening fear, noticing how clinging closes the heart, and using reflections on death, change, and not-self only when they actually open compassion. Later, the speaker turns toward emptiness, questioning the idea of a fixed self and the assumption that things exist independently of how they are perceived, while keeping the emphasis on what is helpful in practice rather than on abstract theory.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Cultivating the heart - Burbea begins by describing metta as something that can be cultivated, and emphasizes repetition, faith, and body sensitivity as part of that reconditioning.
  2. 04:50 Insight as release - He turns to the relationship between insight and love, defining insight as a way of seeing that releases clinging and suffering rather than simply waiting for a special experience.
  3. 08:28 Fear and its grip - Burbea explains how fear blocks love, how it is tied to aversion and selfing, and how metta can meet fear as a response to that contraction.
  4. 12:29 Working with fear - He looks closely at fear in practice, including body sensations, thoughts, and the shrinking of consciousness, and offers ways to soften the reaction that feeds it.
  5. 30:35 Impermanence in metta - The talk then turns to anicca, with practical suggestions for using mortality, uncertainty, and moment-to-moment change as supports for loving-kindness rather than as sources of bleakness.
  6. 43:13 Dukkha and selfing - Burbea explores suffering, clinging, and the heart’s opening and closing, then uses a cautionary story about not-self to show how spiritual practice can easily become another form of selfing.
  7. 58:33 Not-self and practice - He develops the not-self teaching through the five aggregates, self-judgment, and the mutual dependence of self and clinging, while stressing that emotional openness and emptiness both matter.
  8. 01:35:29 Ways of seeing - In the closing section, he clarifies that mature practice means moving between perspectives rather than fixing on one view, and redefines emptiness as seeing dependent co-arising rather than a blank or purely abstract state.
