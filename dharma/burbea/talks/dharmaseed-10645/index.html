Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Working with Anger and Aversion

Dharma Seed 57:16 2008-09-14

When anger flares, what is it really doing to perception, relationship, and the body? The talk starts by distinguishing the wish to hurt from the larger, more complex terrain of anger and aversion, then moves through consequences, self-fuelling loops, and the ways blame narrows the world. From there it offers practical ways to work: reflective questioning, empathy, a wider view of impermanence and mortality, and a mindfulness that returns from the story to the felt experience in the body.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening the topic - Rob introduces anger and aversion as a large, complex subject and names the devastation they create in personal and collective life.
  2. 02:48 Harm and restraint - He distinguishes the desire to hurt from the broader complexity of anger, using the burning-coal image to show why harmful anger is never helpful.
  3. 06:49 Anger's many forms - The talk turns to the spectrum of anger and aversion, including passive aggression, subtle dislike, and the way these states show up in ordinary life.
  4. 13:23 Three kilesas - Rob uses the language of the three root defilements and returns to the Buddha's practical question of what leads to suffering and freedom.
  5. 16:11 Consequences and stories - He reflects on the effects of anger, tells a personal story about punching a bag, and then reads Dhammapada verses on brooding, hatred, and non-hatred.
  6. 24:35 Empathy and death - The talk shifts to metta's neighbor, empathy, Jo Berry's meeting with Pat McGee, and the sobering reflection that awareness of death can quiet quarrels.
  7. 29:08 Reflection and courage - Rob draws on a Dhammapada-style grid of reasons for anger and then emphasizes questioning, reflective mindfulness, and the courage to keep probing the mind.
  8. 36:04 Body, story, and letting go - He shows how mindfulness returns to bodily sensation, how anger often rests on hurt or sadness, and how truthful communication may need the desire to harm to settle first.
  9. 44:17 How aversion builds reality - The closing section examines how aversion and greed feed each other, how aversion colors and enlarges perception, and why fully understanding it is a doorway to freedom.
