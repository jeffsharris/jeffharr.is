A Mystical Kiss (...for Mystics, Anti-Mystics, Lovers and Realists...)

Dharma Seed 1:10:52 2011-08-04

Mysticism here is treated less as a set of strange experiences than as a question about how perception itself is formed. The talk moves from dictionary meanings of mysticism, through Christian via negativa and Buddhist emptiness, to the claim that gratitude, reverence, and letting go change what the world seems to be. Along the way it brings in poetry, devotional practice, and modern physics to ask whether the issue is not what is ultimately real, but what kind of seeing opens the heart and loosens the self.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Setting the frame - He situates the talk as a continuation of an earlier retreat thread and says he wants to explore mysticism, reality, and perception more broadly.
  2. 02:06 What mysticism means - He works through dictionary definitions of mysticism and notes how people often mix contemplative experience with a pejorative sense of confusion or obscurity.
  3. 04:21 Via negativa and via positiva - He contrasts the way of negation with positive devotional seeing, using examples from Christian mysticism, Mother Teresa, and Buddhist Buddha-nature language.
  4. 09:09 Sanctifying ordinary life - He asks what mystical experiences have in common and emphasizes sanctification, then contrasts this with the relative absence of sacralization in early Buddhist texts and the tantric response.
  5. 14:17 Gratitude and wonder - Using a poem by Ian Crichton-Smith, David Orr, Abraham Heschel, and John Wheeler, he connects reverence, gratitude, and wonder to a deeper sense of interdependence.
  6. 21:09 Bias, doubt, and science - He turns to the ways people pre-decide mystical questions, then brings in the Buddha, Niels Bohr, Wolfgang Pauli, and John Wheeler to question naive realism and materialist certainty.
  7. 30:33 Ways of looking - He argues that what we call reality depends on a way of looking, and that practice is less about deciding what is real than about what opens the heart, freedom, and love.
  8. 43:56 Devotion as deconstruction - He describes devotional and tantric practices as part of a larger Mahayana system that works to deconstruct reification rather than merely comfort the practitioner.
  9. 53:32 New eyes and emptiness - He presents a practical law of consciousness: more letting go, love, and insight into emptiness bring less self and more open perception, ending with a call to learn to see with new eyes and bow to life.
