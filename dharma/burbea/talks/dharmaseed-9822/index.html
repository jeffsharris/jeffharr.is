Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Freedom, Reality, and the Razor's Edge

Dharma Seed 58:57 2009-09-12

Is freedom found in seeing things more clearly, or in seeing through the very habit of building reality at all? The talk moves from ordinary mindfulness, where story and self begin to quiet, into sharper inquiry: impermanence, the feeling of a stable existential self, and then the more radical question of how suffering, self, and even experience get fabricated. It keeps testing the listener’s assumptions, including whether spacious awareness or a sense of a ground of being is really final, and repeatedly returns to dependent origination, emptiness, and the difficult “razor’s edge” between appearance and emptiness. By the end, the emphasis is less on having a special state than on learning not to build solidity, while letting that understanding open into freedom, compassion, and trust.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the Inquiry - Burbea situates the talk as a continuation of the previous evening and introduces the question of different levels of self-identification.
  2. 02:05 Delusion Beneath Clinging - He frames the path around understanding reality correctly, emphasizing that delusion underlies clinging and that the talk will move quickly across several levels of insight.
  3. 06:52 Quieting Story and Self - Mindfulness is presented as a way to let papanca and personality construction fall quiet, making room for a simpler contact with experience.
  4. 09:51 Ordinary Radiance - Haiku and brief examples are used to show how practice can reveal the vividness and beauty of ordinary things.
  5. 12:16 Impermanence and Groundlessness - He turns to impermanence as a deepening insight that weakens fixed self-sense and makes presence the most natural response.
  6. 18:56 Awareness as a Ground - Burbea examines the appeal of spacious awareness, oneness, and the sense of a ground of being, while noting how these views can still become subtly fabricated.
  7. 30:21 Dependent Origination - Using Nagarjuna and related teachings, he argues that self, suffering, experience, and even apparently basic units are built and can therefore be unbuilt.
  8. 44:00 Beyond Nihilism - He clarifies that emptiness is not a denial of experience, and that deeper freedom comes from understanding the mutual emptiness of all things.
  9. 50:32 The Razor's Edge - The talk culminates in the union of appearance and emptiness, where practice becomes a repeated, honest unbuilding that opens freedom, compassion, and trust.
