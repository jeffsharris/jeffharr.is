Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Wisdom of Non-Duality

Dharma Seed 65:39 2009-11-17

Rob Burbea explores non-duality as a family of teachings rather than a single doctrine, moving from ordinary dualities like pleasure and pain to subtler questions of subject and object, self and no-self, existence and non-existence. He repeatedly returns to a pragmatic test: whether a practice brings more freedom, ethics, compassion, ease, and clarity, and whether it reduces suffering rather than merely sounding profound. The talk also uses early Buddhist passages, Buddha nature language, and Rumi to show how different levels of non-dual understanding can function as stepping stones rather than final answers.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 What Non-Duality Means - Burbea introduces the theme, defines non-duality as not-two-ness, and lays out the many kinds of dualities that can lead to suffering.
  2. 04:17 Pragmatic Early Buddhism - He frames duality and non-duality as complementary approaches and brings in the Buddha’s teaching on skillful and unskillful searching.
  3. 07:47 What a Good Path Shows - The talk turns to the fruits of practice: freedom, ethics, compassion, openness, and a loosening of fixation on pleasure and pain.
  4. 12:16 Doing and Non-Doing - He examines the appeal and limits of non-doing, warning that it can become avoidance or a subtle philosophy unless it really changes the way practice feels.
  5. 19:00 Buddha Nature and Longing - Burbea discusses self-view, the inner critic, Buddha nature, and the question of how love and longing can reorient practice without turning it into pressure.
  6. 28:51 No-Self and Vastness - He traces the movement from no-self experiences into vast awareness, silence, and a sense that all experience is held within something larger.
  7. 43:27 Rumi and the Middle Way - Rumi’s poems and the Buddha’s teaching to Kaccayana are used to show both the beauty and the limits of oneness language.
  8. 50:06 Dependent Arising Deepens - The talk shifts into how self and phenomena become more or less solid through clinging, and how less grasping makes appearances fade and soften.
  9. 58:49 Training the View - Burbea closes by stressing repetition, familiarity, and using different perspectives as stepping stones toward the deepest non-dual freedom.
