Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

PS - Playing in the In-Betweens

Dharma Seed 64:24 2020-01-08

How do you work when jhana is close but not quite complete? The talk moves through the grey areas of practice: loosening rigid schedules, finding a posture that lets awareness inhabit the whole body, and using subtle shifts of attention when pain, dullness, or a partial jhana blocks the way. From there it turns practical in finer and finer ways—how to stabilize a level by returning to an earlier one, how to work with the breath, and how to let stillness, light, or presence deepen on their own.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening the Postscript - He explains that this talk is a follow-up to questions that arose during the retreat and introduces the theme of working in grey areas of jhana.
  2. 02:22 Responsive Practice Rhythm - He argues against rigid sit-walk schedules in favor of attuning practice length and rhythm to what is actually fertile in the moment, including for insight practice.
  3. 08:49 Posture and Embodiment - He turns to posture, describing it as a subtle support for jhana and sharing stories that show how bodily balance and citta shape each other.
  4. 15:56 Working with Difficulty - He offers ways to meet pain, fogginess, and other blocks by placing metta, breath, or light at the difficult spot rather than away from it.
  5. 18:38 Partial Jhana Skills - He discusses what to do when a jhana is present but not fully spread, including pausing, staying with what is already okay, and changing perception.
  6. 22:31 Moving Between Jhanas - He gives practical guidance on stepping back or forward between jhanas, bringing the breath back in third jhana, and using secondary nimittas to help a state cohere.
  7. 30:49 Fourth Jhana and Breath - He works with fourth jhana as pure bright awareness, then answers a question about nose and mouth breathing by linking breath subtlety to the subtlety of citta.
  8. 39:52 Closing Reflections - After a long stretch of garbled closing-list material, he returns to the main thread and explains jhana as a replenishing inner resource for work, service, and steadying life.
  9. 01:00:02 Dimensionality of Being - He concludes by saying repeated immersion in jhana changes how one senses human beings and the cosmos, leaving a durable imprint even when the states are no longer accessible.
