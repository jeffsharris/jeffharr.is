Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Towards the Imaginal

Dharma Seed 62:08 2018-06-25

Rob Burbea continues a retreat teaching on the imaginal, beginning with a practical invitation to listen actively and treat the talk as something to work with in experience. He outlines a flexible map of practice, then slowly builds a richer sense of what makes an image imaginal: relationship, trust, beauty, love, dimensionality, and a quality of soulmaking that is more important than initial doubt or visual clarity. Later he expands the “middle way” of the imaginal—neither real nor not real—using theatre, emptiness, and the Kaccayana Sutta, and closes by emphasizing experiment, maturing, and learning from one’s own practice.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Listening Proactively - Rob asks listeners to engage the talk actively, bookmarking what they do not yet understand and returning to it later.
  2. 01:46 A Flexible Practice Map - He lays out the retreat's open set of practices—samadhi, energy body, emotion, images, mindfulness, and metta—as a kind of buffet rather than a sequence.
  3. 04:32 Working Through Doubt - Rob addresses doubt as a common response to imaginal work and suggests staying with what arises long enough to see whether it bears fruit.
  4. 06:55 Soulmaking as Measure - He argues that the key test is whether a relationship with an image deepens soulfulness, beauty, and richness, not whether the image appears immediately significant or clear.
  5. 12:26 What Imaginal Means - Rob describes the imaginal as a spectrum or constellation of qualities rather than a fixed category, and explains how noticing can illuminate what is already there.
  6. 18:22 Love and Deliberate Cultivation - He uses love as an example of an imaginal quality that can be drawn out, and adds that practitioners can intentionally introduce elements that support the field.
  7. 22:43 The Full Constellation - Rob introduces a long list of imaginal qualities—trust, autonomy, two-ness, meaningfulness, participation, and more—to orient listeners to the structure of the practice.
  8. 29:05 Imaginal Relationship - He explains that imaginal experience is soulmaking through a relational dynamic of eros, psyche, and logos, and emphasizes the autonomy and reciprocity of the image.
  9. 38:01 Theatre and Middle Way - Using theatre as an analogy, he describes the imaginal as neither real nor not real, and begins to connect this to questions of ontology and emptiness.
