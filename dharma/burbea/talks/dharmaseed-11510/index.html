Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

First Morning Instructions and Guided Meditation

Dharma Seed 43:58 2008-08-09

Rob Burbea gives a guided meditation that begins with posture, relaxing the face, throat, shoulders, and belly, then opens into wide whole-body awareness. He gradually works with the breath as a gentle way to energize, soothe, and calm the body, and then shifts into finding a place of comfort and letting it be nourished without pressure. Near the end, he recaps the method as an exploratory practice of patience, responsiveness, and contentment, with comfort allowed to grow slowly and spread through the body.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Settling the posture - He begins by orienting the session and guiding a careful settling into an upright, open, relaxed posture.
  2. 04:24 Whole-body awareness - Awareness expands to the body as a whole, with attention to contact sensations and a broad, permeating presence.
  3. 11:36 Working with the breath - The breath is lengthened and explored in a large field of awareness, noticing its effects through the torso, throat, spine, and legs.
  4. 23:58 Breath as nourishment - He turns to using the breath to open, energize, soothe, and calm the body according to what is needed in the moment.
  5. 36:40 Play without forcing - Rob recaps the practice as playful experiment rather than control, encouraging questions like whether the breath wants to be longer, shorter, stronger, or more refined.
  6. 40:41 Comfort that can spread - He closes by emphasizing contentment with any small comfort that appears, allowing it to be supported gently and, when steady, to spread through the body.
