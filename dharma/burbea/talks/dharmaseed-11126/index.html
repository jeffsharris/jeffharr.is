Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Integrity and the Poetics of Unknowing

Dharma Seed 69:40 2009-02-01

What if the problem is not thought itself, but the deep habit of believing that what appears is solid, self-existing, and already true? The talk moves from mindfulness and bare attention, through a critique of romantic ideas of “flow” and “suchness,” into a sharper argument that emptiness is not just impermanence but seeing how self, world, and time are constructed through clinging and conception. It also keeps testing a live tension in the teaching: whether a serious inquiry into reality closes the heart, or whether it can open into a quieter, more precise kind of wonder without losing integrity.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Mindfulness and thought - Burbea begins by reflecting on the value of bare attention and the long practice of meeting experience directly, while questioning the common suspicion of thought.
  2. 05:12 Beyond vivid appearances - He argues that mindfulness can make experience more vivid without yet seeing through appearances, then uses suchness and Huxley to show how perception can still be subtly reified.
  3. 12:13 Impermanence is not enough - The talk turns to change and the popular idea of letting go, which he treats as helpful but still short of awakening.
  4. 16:12 Non-construction of reality - A long quotation attributed to the Buddha becomes the pivot: the awakened one does not construe seer, seen, or object, and emptiness is framed as deeper than impermanence.
  5. 24:46 Concepts shape suffering - He explains that suffering is rooted in conceiving and believing in inherent existence, not merely in having thoughts, and begins to show how the mind fabricates a world.
  6. 34:36 No other cause to peace - The talk sharpens around the claim that emptiness must be analyzed and cultivated for liberation, not treated as one option among many spiritual approaches.
  7. 47:01 Agnosticism and discernment - Burbea questions relativism and agnosticism, contrasting vague uncertainty with the more exact knowledge that phenomena lack inherent existence.
  8. 55:46 How self is built - He develops dependent origination as a mutually co-arising process in which self and clinging build each other, and explains that seeing this deeply can undo fabrication.
  9. 01:07:55 Beyond words - The closing section emphasizes how the deepest Dharma is hard to communicate, beyond ordinary language, and ends with a short period of silence.
