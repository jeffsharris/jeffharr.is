Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Image, Mythos, Dharma (Part Three)

Dharma Seed 60:02 2014-12-07

What if image, myth, and fantasy are not side effects of practice, but part of what actually moves us? The talk asks listeners to notice how even the Buddha, the Four Noble Truths, and ideas of awakening are shaped by the mythos we bring to them, then explores a looser, more imaginal way of working with self, emptiness, and liberation. It moves from critique of modern assumptions about the self into Corbin, Jung, alchemy, and Keats, ending by suggesting that Dharma may be as much about beauty, enchantment, and soul-making as it is about reducing suffering.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Image Before Meaning - He begins by suggesting that meaningfulness, love, and perception are already shaped by image, fantasy, and mythos.
  2. 06:12 What Drives Us - He contrasts image-led understanding with common modern assumptions that biology, upbringing, or the past are primary causes.
  3. 13:33 Corbin and the Imaginal - Using Corbin, Jung, and the alchemical image of liquidity, he reframes imaginal figures as larger than the personal self.
  4. 21:20 Buddha, Devotion, Fantasy - He reflects on changing frameworks, devotion to the Buddha, and how our sense of awakening is already shaped by fantasy.
  5. 28:09 The Four Truths Reframed - He argues that the Four Noble Truths are a skeleton that gets filled out by metaphysical assumptions about self, suffering, and liberation.
  6. 37:26 Beauty and Enchantment - He shifts from a purely therapeutic view of practice toward beauty, enchantment, and the possibility that Dharma itself is a mythos to live by.
  7. 46:22 Image as Epiphany - With Coleridge and Keats, he describes image as revealing the eternal in the temporal and names soul-making as a possible aim of practice.
  8. 54:34 Broadening Practice - He closes by asking whether awakening is more open-ended than usually assumed, and how imaginal figures may shape life without needing literal expression.
