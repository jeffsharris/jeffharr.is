Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Art of Perception

Dharma Seed 1:34:50 2016-07-28

What if perception is not a neutral report of reality, but a field we help make, inhabit, and transform? The talk moves from historical views of the imaginal, through emptiness and dependent arising, into a detailed account of how image, body, heart, and meaning work together in practice. Along the way it uses art, alchemy, and a series of literary and philosophical examples to argue that seeing image as image can loosen modern certainty without collapsing into fantasy.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 The imaginal realm - Burbea opens by sketching older views of an imaginal realm with its own reality and influence, before turning to the modern prejudice against it.
  2. 03:54 Religion and reading - He uses Walter Wink and Hugh of St. Victor to show how resurrection, ascension, and reading were once understood as transformative and sympathetic rather than merely literal or informational.
  3. 09:27 Knowledge shifts - A historical arc through Aristotle, Plato, Plotinus, and the rise of modernism shows how reasoning and fixed categories came to outweigh imaginal ways of knowing.
  4. 14:41 Truth and play - He brings in Niels Bohr and emptiness to suggest that truth is not neatly packageable and that openness allows a more playful relation to views.
  5. 19:11 Art of perception - Burbea develops the phrase 'the art of perception,' linking alchemy, image as image, and the transformation of perception into a poetic practice.
  6. 30:40 What makes the imaginal - He defines the imaginal through several qualities: soulfulness, depth, otherness, meaningfulness, and beauty, while stressing that it is more than ordinary fantasy.
  7. 44:32 Importing meaning - Nietzsche and other examples help him show that we always bring something to what we perceive, and that interpretation is open and multiple rather than fixed.
  8. 55:04 Imagination as salvation - With Blake, Jesus, and the Buddha bodies, he frames imagination as a way out of flatness and nihilism, and as a redemptive mode of perception.
  9. 01:05:55 Questioning the familiar - The closing movement broadens the talk into criticism, culture, and modernity: art, myth, and inquiry can loosen inherited assumptions and make perception a lived path.
