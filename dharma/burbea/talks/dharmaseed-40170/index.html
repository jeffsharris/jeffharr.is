Eros and the Brahmaviharas

Dharma Seed 1:13:57 2017-02-16

What happens when loving-kindness is no longer treated as the whole field of love, and eros is allowed to meet it instead? The talk carefully separates eros from metta, compassion, joy, and equanimity, then shows where they overlap and where they move in different directions: one complexifies and personalizes, the other simplifies and loosens fabrication. From there it becomes a practical inquiry into how erotic imaginal work can support compassion, steady the heart before suffering, and open new possibilities for practice without replacing the Brahmaviharas.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the inquiry - Burbea explains why he wants to spell out the topic more fully and sets up the question of eros in relation to the Brahmaviharas.
  2. 02:39 Eros and imaginal love - He defines eros in the imaginal realm as a form of love with attraction, beauty, and contact, and distinguishes it from simple imaginative support in mettā practice.
  3. 08:12 Shared ground with the Brahmaviharas - The talk covers important overlaps, including care, equanimity, and love directed toward phenomena as well as beings.
  4. 13:26 How eros differs - He clarifies that eros includes desire, delight, beauty, and a love for uniqueness, while mettā remains unconditional and not based on attraction.
  5. 19:47 Particularity and the modern self - Burbea explores personal love for particulars, the modern sense of self, and why uniqueness may need to be loved rather than erased.
  6. 26:31 Receiving love - He describes the difficulty of receiving love, ambivalence about being seen, and the role of trust, pacing, and imaginal figures in opening to a loving gaze.
  7. 35:15 Less and more fabrication - The discussion turns to how the Brahmaviharas simplify self and other, while eros complicates and enriches them, often bringing cosmic ease and expanded compassion.
  8. 48:14 Combining practice - Burbea suggests that eros, including sexual energy, can be brought into mettā and compassion practice in creative ways that add energy and range rather than distraction.
  9. 58:32 Eros as fuel - He closes by showing how erotic imaginal practice can support compassion, bliss, samādhi, and equanimity, while widening the sense of sacredness that practice can disclose.
