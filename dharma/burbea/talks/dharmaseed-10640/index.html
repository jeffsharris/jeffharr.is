Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

On the Essence of Being

Dharma Seed 59:39 2008-09-17

Rob Burbea explores the idea of essence by tracing how it appears in ordinary self-view, in meditation, and in Buddhist and Western philosophy. He contrasts essence-seeking with the Buddha’s emphasis on conditions, actions, and the ending of suffering, then turns to common reifications of Buddha nature, awareness, and being. The talk closes by pointing toward a freedom that is less about finding an ultimate thing and more about letting go of the need to conceive experience in essence terms.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the Question - Burbea introduces the problem of finding a useful title and frames the talk as an inquiry into essence rather than an abstract theory.
  2. 03:23 Upanishadic Background - He sets the Buddha against the Upanishadic search for Atman, describing a culture that looked for a hidden self and liberation through knowing it.
  3. 09:13 The Buddha's Shift - The talk turns to the Buddha’s refusal to land on either eternal essence or nihilism, and to his focus on suffering, freedom, and the Four Noble Truths.
  4. 15:00 Meditation and Perception - Burbea looks at how meditative states can be reified as true self or truth, and then shows how self-view is built from perception and conditions.
  5. 24:32 Seeing Conditions - Using the five aggregates and the example of anger, he emphasizes repeated practice in seeing experience as conditioned rather than personal essence.
  6. 30:54 Razor's Edge of Views - He examines the middle way between realism and nihilism, then treats Buddha nature and awareness as teachings that can help but also become new essence views.
  7. 43:12 Ground and Awareness - Burbea discusses vast awareness and the idea of a ground of being, showing how even refined meditative experiences can be made into a substrate or source.
  8. 48:00 Being and Self - He turns to the language of being, arguing that we know being only through experience and that even 'I am' depends on perception and construction.
  9. 54:48 Freedom Beyond Conception - The talk closes with Buddha citations about relinquishing essence, giving up questions of existence, and resting in a freedom beyond conceptual grasping.
