Seeing the System You Are Part Of

Dharma Seed 59:40 2012-06-28

James Baraz frames this talk as a response to ecological and social crisis, beginning with a reading from Ernest Callenbach’s final essay and the idea that hope and cooperation matter in hard times. He then turns to Bob Doppelt’s From Me to We, using it to lay out a Dharma-informed shift from individualism toward systems awareness, mutual responsibility, and practical action. The talk moves steadily from broad vision to specific principles, ending with a close look at interdependence through Thich Nhat Hanh and Lewis Thomas.

Chapters

  1. 00:30 Framing the Tipping Point - James opens by naming the cultural moment as a race between fear and consciousness and sets the talk within a larger tipping point.
  2. 03:26 Callenbach’s Final Vision - He reads from Ernest Callenbach’s Epistle to the Ecotopians as a hopeful model for meeting collapse with courage, mutual support, and practical wisdom.
  3. 08:15 Hope and Mutual Support - The reading highlights hope, cooperation, and shared effort as survival qualities, while James reflects on holding a positive vision without denial.
  4. 13:30 Hard Times, Regeneration - He continues through the essay’s emphasis on practical skills, organizing together, and learning to live with contradiction, decay, and renewal.
  5. 24:22 From Me to We - James introduces Bob Doppelt’s book and explains how its five commitments translate ecological concern into a Dharma-based shift in consciousness.
  6. 30:16 Five Commitments - He outlines the five commitments—seeing systems, accountability, morality, trusteeship, and intention—then focuses on the first as a doorway into practice.
  7. 40:26 Interbeing in the Body - Using Thich Nhat Hanh and Lewis Thomas, he closes by showing how a person is already an ecosystem, inseparable from the wider web of life.
