Seventh Instructions and Guided Metta Meditation: Metta and Emptiness

Dharma Seed 34:58 2011-02-12

How can loving-kindness widen without becoming forced or abstract? The talk moves from the familiar metta categories—self, friend, difficult person, all beings—into practical ways of opening the practice through body sense, space, and phrasing, while keeping the questioning alive about what is actually helpful in any moment. From there it turns toward a subtler point: as the mind softens, perception changes, and things that felt solid can be seen as empty of inherent problemness. The final stretch returns to everyday habits like wanting to hunker down, go to bed, or stay in when it’s cold, asking whether those movements are kindness, aversion, or a closing of the heart.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Metta categories - He introduces the six metta categories and emphasizes choosing what is most helpful in the moment.
  2. 02:53 Turning toward self - He speaks about the healing value of self-metta, while warning against turning it into a fearful withholding of love.
  3. 05:19 Methods for all beings - He describes ways of extending metta by direction or by categories of beings, then offers a simpler approach based on body sense and radiating spaciousness.
  4. 08:36 Space filled with love - He explains how phrases can be used or dropped, and how spacious awareness can reveal a sense of love woven into experience.
  5. 13:20 Perception and emptiness - He shifts to the main point that changes in perception matter more than dramatic experiences, and links this directly to emptiness.
  6. 17:11 Samadhi and insight - He notes that quieter, more settled states give insights a better chance to take root, even when the insight itself feels ordinary.
  7. 19:16 Aversion closes the heart - Using weather, tiredness, and comfort as examples, he shows how aversion narrows the body and consciousness and how that narrowing is connected to love.
  8. 24:11 Questioning habit - He explores how closing down can masquerade as care, and returns again and again to the importance of keeping inquiry alive rather than forcing a right answer.
  9. 30:26 Return to practice - He briefly returns to Catherine McGee's paired-practice framing, then offers a short guided settling and metta closing toward unbounded kindness.
