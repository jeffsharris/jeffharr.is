The Space of Awareness - Silence (2nd Guided Meditation)

Dharma Seed 40:09 2009-09-10

How does awareness open when it stops dividing experience into separate pieces? This guided sitting begins with posture, bodily poise, and kindness, then moves into listening to bare sound, the total field of hearing, and the felt contrast between openness and bright presence. From there it widens further to include body sensations, thoughts, and images, asking whether sound and silence can be sensed together, with silence as something that permeates and holds everything.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling the posture - The instruction begins by orienting toward expansive awareness, with careful attention to posture, balance, and a kind inner direction.
  2. 01:54 Kindness and grounding - He emphasizes kindness as the compass for practice and encourages a grounded sense of the seated body.
  3. 03:21 Listening to sound - The practice shifts to open, bare listening, receiving sound without naming or figuring it out.
  4. 05:21 Global sound field - Awareness widens to the totality of sound, with a balance of receptive spaciousness and vivid presence.
  5. 09:02 Sounds arising and passing - He points to sounds coming and going on their own, with no need to reject or cling to anything heard.
  6. 12:17 Body in awareness - Body sensations are included in the same open field, appearing and disappearing within vast receptive awareness.
  7. 15:27 Everything is included - The practice expands to thoughts and images as well, with an emphasis on equal treatment, heartfulness, and listening with the whole being.
  8. 24:33 Silence within sound - He invites a sense of silence as a quality around, between, and within sounds, rather than as the absence of experience.
  9. 30:20 Belonging to silence - The closing instructions deepen into stillness embracing the body and all phenomena, then end with a reminder to trust what feels opening and helpful.
