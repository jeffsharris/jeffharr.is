Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Beauty of Desire (Part 2)

Dharma Seed 63:24 2011-11-26

What if desire is not mainly a problem to suppress, but a current to understand? The talk moves from a brief return to letting go and the suffering of clinging, into a series of concrete case studies where asking “what am I really wanting?” shifts anger, craving, worry, and resistance into energy, tenderness, joy, or a wider sense of self. Along the way, the speaker questions simple spiritual assumptions about desire, draws on Buddhist and a few mystical references, and keeps returning to the possibility that the treasure is in the movement of desire itself rather than its object.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the inquiry - Burbea sets up the talk as a continuation of last week’s exploration and says he wants to open questions rather than land on conclusions.
  2. 02:34 Letting go of desire - He outlines a classical Buddhist practice of noticing desire and clinging, then repeatedly letting it go to see suffering, selfing, and solidity diminish together.
  3. 08:51 A different approach - He introduces a second method, framed as a research inquiry into consciousness, that will use case studies to explore desire more experimentally.
  4. 12:03 The bank story - A woman’s distress after a bank interaction becomes a way to test what she is really wanting, and the practice opens into empowerment, abundance, and love.
  5. 20:18 Craving and hype - He examines craving as a kind of mental hype that exaggerates distance and difference, then shows how deeper wanting can expand the sense of self.
  6. 26:40 Desire beneath the pain - Through several examples, he shows how frustration, worry, and resistance can reveal tenderness, peace, trust, and a desire to meet life more fully.
  7. 46:18 History and sacred desire - He questions simplistic teachings that flatten desire into clinging, brings in Tantra, Kabbalah, and Nietzsche, and suggests that the treasure may lie in the movement of desire itself.
  8. 01:00:07 Methods and closing questions - He summarizes the practical steps he has been pointing to and ends by asking what we want the answer to be, and how open inquiry can remain.
