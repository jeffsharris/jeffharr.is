Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Three Characteristics - Three Avenues to Freedom and Joy

Dharma Seed 65:44 2010-01-23

What if the point of meditation is not to endure suffering, but to see how the mind builds it and then learns to build it less? The talk moves through impermanence, dukkha, and not-self as practical lenses: first noticing change, then relaxing grasping and aversion, then loosening the habit of taking experience as me and mine. Along the way, the speaker distinguishes this approach from darker readings of the three characteristics and keeps returning to the same criterion: does the practice bring less suffering, more freedom, and more ease?

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Why Practice Matters - Burbea begins by framing the Buddha’s teaching as a response to unnecessary suffering and explaining that practice can reveal how the mind participates in building it.
  2. 04:20 Three Lenses - He introduces the three characteristics as meditative ways of looking, not a bleak philosophy, and contrasts this with darker commentarial interpretations.
  3. 08:24 Dukkha and Letting Go - Impermanence leads naturally into dukkha, and he describes how noticing unsatisfactoriness, grasping, and aversion opens the door to release or simple allowing.
  4. 11:51 Not-Self in Practice - He explains anatta as seeing experience as not me and not mine, with attention able to move microscopically or spaciously across one object, one sense-door, or the whole field.
  5. 21:28 Working With Difficulty - The talk turns to how these practices meet tiredness, pain, scatteredness, and other difficulties, showing that insight can bring ease rather than avoidance.
  6. 30:25 Impermanence, Emptiness, Suffering - Burbea distinguishes his approach from later fast-momentariness teachings, then emphasizes that the Buddha’s point is not to endure suffering but to understand how it is constructed and let it go.
  7. 37:39 Relational Craving - He develops the dukkha contemplation through feeling tone, craving, and the subtle push-pull with experience, and shows how letting go brings calm, spaciousness, and sometimes a sense of void.
  8. 51:55 Self, Strategy, and Freedom - The final section shows how clinging softens self-sense, how anatta works as a strategy rather than a metaphysical claim, and how it differs from but can overlap with metta and samadhi.
