Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Intention and Devotion (Guided Meditation)

Dharma Seed 37:40 2017-03-30

What if meditation is not just about settling down, but about sensing where life wants to open into sacredness? The talk moves from the contrast between simplifying practices and the more complex, generative movement of imaginal practice, then turns into a guided exercise of standing, walking, sensing the body, and letting intention become a quiet devotion. It closes by emphasizing that intention is impermanent and must be returned to gently, like tending a flame, while care for the heart and body is kept alive in movement.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Renunciation and desire - Burbea begins by contrasting simplifying practices with the more generative movement of opening to desire and imaginal practice.
  2. 02:17 Imaginal complexity - He describes imaginal practice as erotic, proliferating, and complexifying, with images generating deeper dimensions and sacredness.
  3. 05:49 The body becomes sacred - The talk turns to how the body can be re-imagined as object, subject, and locus of knowing, not just something functional or culturally defined.
  4. 09:39 Movement and stillness - Burbea opens meditation beyond strict stillness, allowing walking, gentle motion, or inward movement in stillness if it feels soulful.
  5. 14:24 Intention toward sacredness - He names intention as the wish to discover more sacredness in practice and in life, and invites the group into a shared exercise.
  6. 16:07 Standing exercise - Participants are guided to feel rootedness, heart, and energy body, then to sense longing and devotion as an orientation toward sacredness.
  7. 21:36 Walking with holy fire - The exercise expands into open-sense walking, with the intention to let all that is perceived be seen as sacred, including others and the room itself.
  8. 29:36 Returning to the flame - After a period of silence, he speaks about intention as impermanent and the need to return gently, maintaining care for body, heart, and soul.
  9. 34:30 Bowing and closing - The talk ends with bowing to the sacredness one wants to know, and a final reminder not to lose that care while rearranging the room.
