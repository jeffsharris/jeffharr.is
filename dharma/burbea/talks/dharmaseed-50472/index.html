Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Aspects of the Imaginal (Part 2)

Dharma Seed 48:14 2017-12-16

What makes an image imaginal, rather than just vivid or useful? The talk reworks that question by moving from a recap of imagination as a spectrum, through the roles of duty, trust, and selfless intention, into practical ways of opening to imaginal perception through samadhi, emptiness, dream figures, living people, emotions, and even the body itself. A long personal example shows how an apparently ordinary image can become meaningful through patient attention, while a more dramatic one can still miss the imaginal if trust, love, and depth are absent.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Recapping the terrain - He reviews the spectrum of imagination and re-sets the basic distinction between useful imagination and the imaginal.
  2. 05:35 Duty and trust - He adds duty, trust, and non-self-centered intention to the constellation of factors that support imaginal practice.
  3. 09:11 Opening the gates - He briefly points listeners to earlier instructions and then turns to how one might begin working with images in practice.
  4. 11:26 Ways into image - He offers a wide set of entry points: samadhi, emptiness, dreams, prior figures, deities, people, places, emotion, and the body.
  5. 19:39 The black coat story - A plain image of suit lapels unfolds into a fuller imaginal sense of a man, his devotion, and the meaning carried by the scene.
  6. 23:39 Vivid but not imaginal - He contrasts the previous example with a dramatic snake image that lacked trust, love, and dimensionality at first.
  7. 30:36 Patient transformation - He shows how introducing trust and related qualities can change an image over time, and reminds listeners that imaginal images are not always visual.
  8. 35:42 Technique and patience - He speaks candidly about not being naturally image-rich, and encourages experimentation, patience, and alternating imaginal work with other practices.
  9. 41:31 Life already imaginal - He closes by pointing to the imaginal already present in ordinary life, where self, body, conversation, inquiry, and world can all become image through the right mode of being.
