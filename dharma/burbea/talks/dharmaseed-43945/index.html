On Blessed Ground: Fantasies of the Self on the Path

Dharma Seed 32:07 2017-03-31

What if practice is not mainly a project of reducing suffering, but a field shaped by fantasy, eros, and the images that give it life? The talk starts by questioning the inherited medical model of Buddhism, then opens into other possible ways of understanding the self on the path: as researcher, artist, adventurer, or lover. Rather than trying to strip fantasy away, it asks whether the fantasies already at work are soulmaking—whether they deepen meaning, aliveness, and the sense of discovery.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the question - Rob introduces the talk as a way to open up another possibility in how practice is understood, rather than adding more content.
  2. 06:18 Medical model of practice - He describes the common Buddhist frame as a healing project shaped by the Four Noble Truths and the reduction of suffering.
  3. 09:30 Religion and soul-making - He contrasts a backward-looking religious fantasy with the more open question of what the practitioner actually wants, naming soul-making as a different aim.
  4. 13:27 Researcher and artist - He offers the self as researcher or artist, where practice becomes discovery and the poetry of perception rather than only cure.
  5. 16:39 Adventure and lover - He widens the field further with the fantasies of adventurer and lover, each carrying a different sense of time, purpose, and intimacy.
  6. 19:50 Fantasy as ground - Through a close example, he shows how practice may be animated by several fantasies at once, often without being fully recognized.
  7. 24:56 Reconstruction and freedom - He considers the fear that seeing fantasy will collapse the path, and argues instead that reconstruction is inevitable and the question is whether it is realist or poetic.
  8. 27:48 What enlivens practice - He closes by asking what really makes one feel alive in practice, whether the fantasy is soulmaking, and how it imagines completion or ending.
