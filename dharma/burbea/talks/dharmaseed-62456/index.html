Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Preliminaries, Regarding Voice, Movement, and Gesture (Part 5)

Dharma Seed 12:51 2020-03-05

The talk introduces a small exercise in which voice, posture, and movement are combined and then carefully varied to see what they stir in the body and feeling life. Starting from a simple repeated syllable or fragment, the speaker explores changes in gesture, pitch, volume, and melody, inviting a slow, attentive listening for shifts in energy body, emotion, and image. The emphasis stays practical: not on producing a result, but on training sensitivity and noticing subtle effects.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Setting Up the Exercise - Rob frames the practice as a modest exercise and situates it among earlier movement-based preliminaries.
  2. 01:01 Image, Voice, Movement - He describes two directions for the practice: letting image move into voice and posture, or starting with movement and noticing what it does in the soul.
  3. 03:02 Voicing a Simple Sound - He introduces the combined sound-and-gesture exercise and demonstrates a sustained syllable with rough rhythmic repetition.
  4. 05:45 Open and Crossed Postures - The talk turns to how different arm positions and slight torso rotation change the felt quality of the same voiced sound.
  5. 07:04 Varying the Gesture - Rob explores crossed hands, folded arms, and other postures while encouraging slow repetition and close attention.
  6. 08:58 Pitch and Loudness - He adds further variables, including register and volume, from loud voice to whisper and even internal sounding.
  7. 10:55 Noticing the Effects - The practice is summed up as a study in sensitivity, with attention to energy body, emotion, image, and soul resonance.
