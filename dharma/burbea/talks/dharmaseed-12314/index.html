Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Third Samatha Instructions and Guided Meditation

Dharma Seed 48:12 2007-04-02

The talk starts with posture, then moves into a slow guided exploration of breath and awareness by body center: lower belly, solar plexus, heart, throat, head, spine, and back. Rather than treating this as a fixed method, the speaker keeps returning to experiment—what breath feels most easeful, where attention can settle, and how the whole body changes when awareness is placed in one spot. In the second half, the talk opens into questions about difficult emotion, the balance between samatha and vipassana, and practical advice for adapting the practice when the breath is blocked or the body feels constricted.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling the Posture - The meditation opens with posture, ease, and scanning the body for obvious tension.
  2. 01:24 Lower Belly Center - Awareness is stationed in the lower abdomen, exploring the most comfortable breath and how the rest of the body responds.
  3. 07:05 Right Side and Belly - The center shifts to the right side of the lower belly, then deeper into the lower abdomen, with very light attention to bodily effects.
  4. 14:19 Solar Plexus and Heart - The practice moves upward through the solar plexus and heart center, including a suggestion to ease constriction and invite okayness.
  5. 18:49 Throat to Crown - Attention travels through the throat, the middle of the head, the crown, and the top of the spine, each time asking how the breath wants to be there.
  6. 28:32 Spine and Return - The base of the spine is explored, then the practice returns to one of the central body locations for a settled whole-body awareness.
  7. 31:59 After the Sit - Rob reflects on moving awareness around the body, the spider-web analogy, and how to work with difficult emotion without making the practice rigid.
  8. 37:18 Questions and Context - He clarifies traditional breath practices, discusses a simple mudra for blocked breathing, and explains how samatha can soothe emotion while staying part of a wider practice.
  9. 46:08 Closing Instructions - The talk ends with encouragement about group process, a short announcement, and a transition into walking meditation with appreciation.
