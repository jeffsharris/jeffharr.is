Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Insight Ways of Looking, Other Energy Body Possibilities, and Summarized Instructions

Dharma Seed 49:33 2019-12-21

Rob Burbea lays out two broad ways of working: using insight as a deliberate way of looking, and experimenting with the energy body as a springboard for concentration. He then leads a series of short imaginative practices, showing how posture, attention, and subtle mental framing can change the felt body. The talk closes with a compact summary of how to choose a base practice, work within a learning edge, and refine concentration without forcing it.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Insight as a way of looking - He distinguishes ordinary insight as something that happens from insight as an active lens that can be applied deliberately in the moment.
  2. 11:56 Using insight for practice - He explains how a chosen insight lens can become a sustainable practice and a springboard into steadiness and well-being.
  3. 12:22 Energy body without breath - He shifts to working directly with the energy body, beginning with posture, openness, and whole-body sensitivity.
  4. 18:50 Opening and brightening - A guided exploration follows, using whole-body awareness, a brighter attention, and noticing any already-present pleasantness.
  5. 24:12 Lines and luminous forms - He introduces several short imagination exercises: two points, three lines, intersecting lines, and a cloud-like luminous body.
  6. 31:24 What the exercises show - He pauses to name the common thread: the body experience is highly conditioned, and even small imaginative shifts can make a real difference.
  7. 42:09 Big-picture orientation - He summarizes the main principles: choose the practice that most reliably supports well-being, and spend time in the learning edge playground.
  8. 47:06 Simple concentration instructions - He offers a fallback instruction set: choose one object, return repeatedly, work with hindrances, and gently refine sensitivity and directionality.
