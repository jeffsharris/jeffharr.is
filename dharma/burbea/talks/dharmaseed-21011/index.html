Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Aspects of Mindfulness (Part 2)

Dharma Seed 63:40 2006-04-23

Rob Burbea places mindfulness back inside the full arc of Dharma practice, starting from the question of why we practice at all. He moves from freedom from suffering, to truth, to love, and then shows how mindfulness is only one part of a broader path that includes view, intention, speech, livelihood, effort, and collectedness. Later he offers a practical way to check practice when it feels stuck—recognition, acceptance, investigation, and non-identification—and ends by linking deep mindfulness with kindness, receptivity, and a softer relation to life.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Why practice mindfulness - He begins by asking why mindfulness matters and why practice is worth making room for at all.
  2. 01:08 Freedom from suffering - Mindfulness is first placed in the Buddha’s compassionate aim of reducing unnecessary suffering across the many areas of life.
  3. 05:39 Truth and love - He broadens the motivation for practice to include a hunger for truth and the aspiration to a deeper, more boundless love.
  4. 09:39 Mindfulness within the path - The talk turns to the Noble Eightfold Path, showing that mindfulness sits inside a whole-life training rather than standing alone.
  5. 13:20 What mindfulness is not - He corrects common misunderstandings, including the idea that practice is just emptying out stress or trying to be mindful all the time.
  6. 19:37 Presence and sensitivity - Mindfulness is described as restful, enlivening, and increasingly sensitive to body, emotion, mind, and the ordinary mystery of life.
  7. 29:42 RAN: a practice check - When practice feels entangled, he offers recognition, acceptance, investigation, and non-identification as a simple way to see what is missing.
  8. 36:44 Embodiment and heartfulness - He names bodyfulness, openness, spaciousness, wakefulness, and respect as dimensions of a full mindfulness practice that includes the heart.
  9. 42:46 Active inquiry - Mindfulness is shown as active investigation into what leads to happiness, what feeds suffering, and how even the watcher is constructed.
