Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Bob Doppelt, James Baraz: From Me to We

Dharma Seed 53:12 2013-04-20

Climate change is framed here as a test of whether awareness can move faster than fear. The talk begins with a call for sangha and teacher leadership, including a letter asking Buddhist teachers to offer guidance on the crisis, then shifts into a practical framework drawn from Bob Doppelt’s From Me to We. From there it becomes a series of clear Dharma-to-action movements: interdependence, cause and effect, moral conduct, trusteeship, and free will, each tied back to how we live, choose, and take responsibility in a closed planetary system.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening the gathering - The talk begins with the Earth Day setting, the climate focus, and the introduction of James Baraz and Bob Doppelt.
  2. 02:13 Sangha and teacher request - James Baraz speaks about collective refuge, the need for Dharma leadership on climate change, and the letter inviting teachers to respond.
  3. 09:50 Bob's framework - Bob Doppelt is introduced through his climate work and his book From Me to We, which becomes the bridge into his teaching framework.
  4. 15:33 Climate as closed system - Bob explains climate change as a global systems problem, introduces the blue marble image, and names the five commitments as a Dharma-informed sustainability model.
  5. 24:15 Breath and interdependence - A guided reflection uses the breath to point directly to oxygen, photosynthesis, and the web of life, leading into emptiness and dependent origination.
  6. 30:06 Awareness and systems - The teaching turns to increased awareness, systems mapping, and the first two commitments: seeing the systems you are part of and understanding cause and effect.
  7. 35:50 Moral choice - Bob develops the decision-making exercise and the third commitment, moral justice, emphasizing do no harm and the need for clearer ethical intention.
  8. 40:32 Trusteeship and restoration - The fourth commitment frames us as temporary trustees who must not only reduce harm but actively restore the earth.
  9. 45:09 Free will and closing - The final commitment is free will shaped by wisdom, followed by audience reflection, a brief discussion, and a closing John Seed quote about waking up and choosing change.
