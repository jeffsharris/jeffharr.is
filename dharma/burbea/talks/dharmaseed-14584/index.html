Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Instructions on Mindfulness of the Body

Dharma Seed 29:38 2011-11-04

How do you meet the body without turning it into a problem of self-image, comparison, or pain? The talk moves from the Buddha’s instruction to contemplate the body in the body into a series of practical ways of looking: knowing posture and movement, opening to fine sensation, and working skillfully with discomfort by relaxing tension, widening awareness, and softening aversion. It closes by showing how even ordinary bodily experience, and even impermanence and not-self, can become a refuge when they are taken up as a meditation theme.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 What Practice Is - Burbea opens by asking what practice is and framing it as learning ways of looking that reduce suffering.
  2. 06:06 Body as Foundation - He turns to the Buddha's instruction to contemplate the body as body, without comparison or self-identity.
  3. 09:44 Close Bodily Attention - He describes bare attention and close investigation of bodily texture, from warmth and pressure to vibration and expansion.
  4. 13:02 Working with Pain - The teaching shifts to bodily discomfort, showing how to relax secondary tension, widen awareness, and soften aversion.
  5. 20:07 Pleasure and Collectedness - He includes bodily pleasure in concentration and metta practice, encouraging enjoyment without grasping.
  6. 21:44 Ordinary Sensation - Burbea points to neutral bodily states as a field worth dwelling in, not just dramatic pain or pleasure.
  7. 23:20 Impermanence and Not-Self - He shows how sustained mindfulness reveals change and loosens the sense that sensations are mine or me.
  8. 26:08 The Body as Theme - The talk ends by returning to deliberate ways of looking and the possibility of making the body itself a refuge and sustained theme.
