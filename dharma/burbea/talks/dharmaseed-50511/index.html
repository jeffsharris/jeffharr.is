To Sense with Soul

Dharma Seed 12:08 2018-02-04

What if perception is not just seeing what is there, but sensing the world with added depth, aliveness, and relationship? The talk pauses the larger retreat teaching to clarify the phrase “sensing with soul,” contrasting it with the idea of imaginal life as merely private inner imagery. From there, it widens the frame through the Buddha Dharma idea of mind as a sixth sense, then turns to emptiness to show how words like soul and divinity can be used without turning them into fixed entities.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Why the Term Matters - He begins by pausing the larger retreat teaching to clarify a word choice and the misunderstanding he wants to avoid.
  2. 03:11 From Imaginal to Soulful - He explains “sensing with soul” as a near-synonym for imaginal perceiving, not a purely interior fantasy.
  3. 05:58 The World Filled Out - Using the five senses and the Dharma’s sixth sense, he shows how objects, people, and thought can be sensed with added dimensionality.
  4. 07:59 What Soul Means - He clarifies that soul is not being used as a fixed entity but as shorthand for soulful experience and texture.
  5. 09:28 Empty Words, Useful Language - He turns to emptiness to show why words like soul, deity, and divinity can be used provisionally without reification.
  6. 10:57 Making the Instrument - He closes by describing soulmaking as fabricating soulfulness and building the perceptual capacity to sense that way.
