Focusing on One Point (Intensity, Directionality, Subtlety) (Instructions)

Dharma Seed 29:44 2019-12-19

How can a narrow breath focus become steady without becoming tense? The talk moves from a practical choice between a small spot and a wider field into a careful unpacking of samadhi: keep a light whole-body background, notice over-efforting early, and treat effort as an ongoing adjustment rather than a fixed technique. From there, the speaker shifts to the felt quality of attention itself, arguing for subtlety, directionality, and just enough intensity to stay receptive rather than forceful. The discussion ends by suggesting that as the object becomes more delicate, the attention should become more delicate too, and that this delicacy can be quietly enjoyable.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening Context - Rob begins with the retreat conditions, his own limits, and why an imperfect setup can still support fruitful practice.
  2. 02:35 Jhana Direction - He frames the retreat around jhana practice and explains how different base practices can lead toward the same range of pleasant energy-body experience.
  3. 08:48 A Narrower Spot - He introduces the classic breath locations and the direct, localized way of working with them.
  4. 10:45 Samadhi Misconstrued - Rob pushes back against the idea that concentration means a tense stare and clarifies that a small focus is only one possible means.
  5. 12:03 Whole Body Background - He recommends keeping a light whole-body awareness in the background as a way to stabilize attention and notice over-efforting.
  6. 13:49 Effort As Art - He develops effort as an ongoing investigation, showing how background awareness reveals pressure, tension, and the need to soften.
  7. 17:02 Quality Over Quantity - Rob shifts away from counting time or distractions and toward the felt quality of attention in the present moment.
  8. 18:28 Subtlety, Direction, Intensity - He explains how samadhi deepens through increasing subtlety, how attention can be received rather than probing, and how intensity can be dialed up or down.
  9. 24:25 Delicacy And Delight - He closes by comparing attention to tasting, listening, and delicate poise, then returns to the practical value of a small point that naturally grows wider.
