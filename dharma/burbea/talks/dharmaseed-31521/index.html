Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

A Sacred Universe: Insight, Theophany, Cosmopoesis (Part 2)

Dharma Seed 46:13 2015-08-12

In this talk, Rob Burbea explores how imaginal practice depends on what we assume is real, and why the usual objections to images collapse under a deeper Dharma understanding of fabrication and emptiness. He moves through modernist assumptions about matter and shared reality, then into Corbin, Avicenna, Blake, and Buddhist Tantra to show how perception can become more fluid, individualized, and sacramental. The talk gradually widens into a quiet claim: emptiness, samadhi, metta, and imaginal practice can all open the world as divine without fixing it into a single worldview.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Questions of reality - Burbea frames imaginal practice inside questions about ontology, and notes that different practitioners need different amounts of philosophy to feel at ease with it.
  2. 02:25 Concepts as support - He says conceptual frameworks can be beautiful and useful, while remaining only provisional, and begins to answer objections about fabrication and fantasy.
  3. 04:53 Fabrication and emptiness - He argues that all perception is fabricated and that insight into emptiness dissolves the assumption that ordinary perception is somehow unmade.
  4. 09:46 What counts as real - He questions what matter means, how shared perception is used to define reality, and why that standard does not settle the status of imaginal experience.
  5. 13:43 The imaginal realm - Using Corbin and Robert Avens, he presents the imaginal as a mediating world that is neither merely subjective nor simply material, and emphasizes disciplined imagination.
  6. 20:16 Perception follows being - Through Swedenborg and Blake, he shows how the state of mind colors what is seen, and how that applies both to ordinary life and to imaginal seeing.
  7. 25:27 Avicenna and malleability - A long passage from Avicenna and Corbin is used to connect the unfabricated with an imaginal world that becomes light, fluid, and responsive after deep release.
  8. 30:41 Unfabricating practice - He links alchemy, emptiness practice, samadhi, and metta as forms of unfabricating that loosen solidity and can reveal the world as divine or theophanic.
  9. 36:02 Tantra and divine appearance - Burbea closes by unpacking tantra and mantra etymologically, then situates Dzogchen inside a broader tantric context of seeing all appearances as divine and persons as particular expressions of divinity.
