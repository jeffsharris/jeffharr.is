Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Breath Meditation (1st Instructions and Guided Meditation)

Dharma Seed 46:01 2009-09-09

Rob Burbea offers a guided breath meditation that begins with posture and bodily ease, then gradually widens into a whole-body awareness of breath, energy, and comfort. He repeatedly invites the listener to experiment gently with long or short breathing, to include subtle pleasure or ease when it is present, and to keep returning with patience when the mind wanders. Later in the talk he pauses the meditation to explain why this approach matters: it can build a quiet inner resource, support calm and brightness, and make insight more available.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Settling the posture - The meditation begins with posture, uprightness, softness, and easing tension in the face, throat, shoulders, chest, and belly.
  2. 04:49 Kindness and body space - Rob aligns the practice with kindness and opens awareness to the space of the body before introducing the breath.
  3. 13:47 Long breath, whole body - The breath is made long and smooth, with attention to whole-body expansion, contraction, energizing, and relaxing.
  4. 20:50 Choosing what helps - He encourages a light, receptive attention and invites listeners to notice whatever is most helpful, including subtle pleasure or comfort.
  5. 30:12 Why practice this way - The talk shifts from instruction into explanation, showing how breath practice can support energy, well-being, calm, and insight.
  6. 39:03 Six practical reminders - Rob reviews the main points: kindness, experimentation, not preconstruing the breath, including comfort, expanding awareness, and staying present.
  7. 43:29 Patience and adjustment - He closes by emphasizing enjoyment without pressure, patience with distraction, and adjusting the breath to what is actually appropriate in the moment.
