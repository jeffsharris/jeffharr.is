Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Liberation From the Inner Critic

Dharma Seed 1:10:01 2009-09-09

How do you meet the voice that says you are failing, unworthy, or not doing practice right? The talk traces that inner critic from its shame and pressure into a more workable relationship through kindness, metta, spacious mindfulness, and careful inquiry. Along the way, it keeps returning to a practical question: can the critic be seen as a pattern of conditions rather than a truth about the self, and can that seeing actually loosen its grip?

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Naming the critic - He defines the inner critic as a voice of negativity, shame, and self-blame, and makes the case that freedom from it is possible.
  2. 04:05 Where it comes from - He places the pattern in family history and Western culture, especially the rise of individualism and isolation.
  3. 07:01 How practice gets distorted - He shows how the critic turns practice into pressure, shoulds, and blocked questioning, and brings in the Buddha's practice questions and 'why not me?'
  4. 12:03 Metta as an ally - He argues for meeting the critic with kindness, metta, and support from community, using examples of people changing over time.
  5. 17:02 Friendly mindfulness - He describes mindfulness as affectionate curiosity and spaciousness, and shows how warmth in awareness can soften the pain around self-judgment.
  6. 24:23 Precision and deconstruction - He moves into careful investigation, showing how the mind joins separate sensations into a solid burden and how that can be undone in the present.
  7. 34:49 Questioning the story - He invites more direct questioning of what is believed, what the critic gets from us, and how dialogue can expose its impossible demands.
  8. 47:00 From shame to action - He reframes perfectionism and guilt by shifting from essence to action, and by seeing events as conditioned rather than personally defining.
  9. 59:59 Freedom and what remains - He closes by showing how repeated practice can empty the critic of force, then remove it altogether, ending with a short silence.
