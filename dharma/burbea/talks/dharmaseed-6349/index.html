Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Realizing the Nature of Mind

Dharma Seed 63:55 2009-05-21

What if the calm, spacious sense of awareness that meditation can open into is only a halfway view? The talk moves through several familiar ways of understanding mind — mirror-like clarity, momentary consciousness, vast space, one mind — and then keeps questioning each one, showing where it helps practice and where it can quietly harden into a view. By the end, the emphasis has shifted toward a harder but deeper inquiry: seeing how awareness, objects, and time are all dependently made, empty, and therefore less solid than they first appear.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the inquiry - Burbea introduces the topic as a practical exploration of how to realize the nature of mind rather than a philosophical overview.
  2. 03:30 Mind as mirror - He lays out the familiar mirror metaphor: mindfulness as polishing awareness so it reflects experience more clearly and with equanimity.
  3. 09:27 Momentary consciousness - A second model presents consciousness as rapidly arising and passing, which supports letting go through impermanence.
  4. 11:34 Awareness as space - He then turns to the sense of vast, spacious awareness, using night sky and cloud imagery to show how phenomena can seem to arise within it.
  5. 17:11 Attractive but incomplete - Burbea notes how easy it is to treat that spacious awareness as the unconditioned, and brings in the Buddha's warning not to judge awakening by radiance.
  6. 23:29 Questioning the view - He examines the limits of the mirror and vastness models, arguing for careful language, intellectual integrity, and continued probing rather than settling.
  7. 33:18 Dependence and emptiness - Using the Buddha's critique of reifying awareness, he shows how objects and knowing depend on each other, and how disidentification can open a deeper freedom.
  8. 41:29 Reasoning about time - He shifts into an analytic mode, including a Dalai Lama reference, to question whether a moment of consciousness can really be one or many, and whether time itself is fabricated.
  9. 53:19 Groundless mind - The talk closes by folding in the Buddha and Huang Po: consciousness is empty, illusory, and not a thing one can finally find, yet this very unfindability points toward unbound awareness and deeper release.
