Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Sensing with Soul (Part 2)

Dharma Seed 48:03 2017-12-22

What is actually being made when experience becomes soulful? The talk returns to that question by unpacking sensing with soul as a movement where Eros, image, and concept interweave: desire reaches toward more, imagery holds that reaching, and understanding gives it shape and durability. It also explores how this dynamic can stretch our ideas, deepen discernment, and open a larger sense of life, while noting that the whole subject resists any tight definition.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 What sensing with soul means - Rob reintroduces the term as a way of framing imaginal practice and explains why it matters for how we meet life, nature, and each other.
  2. 05:13 Making and receiving - He describes soulmaking as both something that happens to us and something we participate in through choice, desire, and responsiveness.
  3. 09:39 What soul names - Soul is unpacked as a set of qualities and then as an organ or capacity for ways of looking that open soulfulness.
  4. 14:23 Eros, Psyche, Logos - The talk lays out the three strands of the dynamic and shows how they interweave in imaginal perception and soulmaking.
  5. 20:40 Beyond and transgression - Rob traces the way Eros creates a beyond, including the possibility of transgression, while warning against reducing this to taboo or craving.
  6. 28:05 Why frameworks matter - He argues that understanding the conceptual structure is not secondary, but helps the whole practice become more robust, fertile, and usable.
  7. 33:15 Agile thinking and Feynman - The talk turns to the flexibility of Logos, with a brief Feynman anecdote illustrating why some subjects can only be explained simply after familiarity grows.
  8. 39:52 Undefinable but fertile - Rob reflects on why soul must remain partly undefinable so it can keep opening, and ends by showing how soulmaking sharpens discernment and distinction.
