Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Dukkha and Soulmaking (Part 1)

Dharma Seed 1:44:34 2017-12-28

What makes a path sustainable when imaginal practice, desire, and suffering all start to touch each other? The talk moves from the scope of dukkha and soulmaking into a careful map of what this work asks: emotional awareness, body and energy-body sensitivity, the ability to discern eros from craving, and enough insight to know when to let go and when to stay with what is present. It then turns to the emotional life in detail, including different ways of relating to self, the need for flexibility around psychological frameworks, and how the earlier seven instructions can reveal the dependent arising of emotion. In the later part, the emphasis widens to relationship, vulnerability, and temenos: the kind of sacred, careful space needed for imaginal sharing to remain alive rather than collapse.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Framing the subject - Burbea introduces the scope of the series and sets out the large questions around dukkha, imaginal practice, eros, and the Four Noble Truths.
  2. 03:45 Why care is needed - He emphasizes that soulmaking work can also intensify suffering if it lacks the right awareness, prerequisites, and sensitivity.
  3. 09:59 Foundational skills - The talk turns to the practical capacities needed for this path, including body, heart, mind, humility, reverence, and distinctions between eros and craving.
  4. 14:06 Sustainable practice - Using the Buddha and Tibetan Buddhist preliminaries as examples, he argues that meaningful paths need pacing, preparation, and foundations rather than just intensity.
  5. 19:55 Working with emotion - He unfolds a sequence of ways to relate to emotions, from bare sensation to compassionate self-inclusion, showing how attention, kindness, and flexibility change the experience.
  6. 47:25 Self, story, and view - The discussion widens to self-fabrication, imaginal selfhood, and the dangers of getting locked into one psychological framework or one style of mindfulness.
  7. 01:02:08 Seven instructions revisited - Burbea returns to the earlier seven practices, treating them as focused exercises that can reveal subtle emotion, support samadhi, and make dependent origination visible.
  8. 01:09:53 Relationship and vulnerability - He argues that soulmaking often becomes alive in relationship, where desire, image, shame, fear of rejection, and the need to be seen all become active.
  9. 01:30:31 Temenos and sacred space - The talk closes by developing temenos as a protective, reverent field for sharing imaginal experience, where timing, privacy, attunement, and care help soulmaking hold its ground.
