Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Into the depths of Silence

Dharma Seed 59:18 2009-05-09

Rob Burbea explores silence as more than the absence of speech or sound: as something that can reveal what we truly care about, soften fear, and bring buried memories into a more healing relationship. He traces how silence can deepen into sensitivity, love, spaciousness, and eventually a loosening of the self’s usual grip on experience. The talk moves from practical ways of listening to silence toward a more radical inquiry into nothingness, freedom, and what is actually real.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the Theme - Rob introduces the talk’s focus on silence and frames it as a complement to an earlier teaching.
  2. 05:09 Silence as Threshold - He describes the fear, unfamiliarity, and promise that can arise when entering silence on retreat.
  3. 11:16 Missing the Vastness - Using a brief story and everyday examples, he points to how small our habitual range of happiness can become.
  4. 17:47 Listening to Silence - He explains how silence can be directly attended to, not as an object, but as a field that holds experience.
  5. 27:30 Neruda and Collective Stillness - Pablo Neruda’s 'Keeping Quiet' is used to show silence as a spacious pause that interrupts striving and division.
  6. 29:26 Practice in the Field - Rob offers practical ways to listen: hearing the spaces between sounds, letting phenomena belong, and noticing how silence permeates experience.
  7. 39:53 Self, Reality, and Nothing - The talk turns toward self-inquiry, Dogen, Christian apophatic writers, Rumi, and the possibility of freedom in not defining anything.
  8. 52:45 Beyond Measurement - He closes by drawing on Thomas Merton and Sariputra to suggest that greed, aversion, and delusion manufacture significance and appearance.
  9. 57:47 Closing Silence - Rob gathers the talk into a brief concluding silence and thanks the listeners.
