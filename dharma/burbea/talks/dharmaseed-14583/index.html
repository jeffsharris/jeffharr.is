Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Body/Image - A Guided Meditation

Dharma Seed 54:37 2011-11-14

Rob Burbea offers a guided meditation that begins with grounding in the felt sense of the body and a gentle frame of kindness. The practice then moves through a detailed contemplative exploration of body parts and elements, shifting from anatomy into imagination, elemental reflection, and questions about solidity, space, and perception. In the final section, he introduces a luminous mind-made body as an optional imaginal exercise, then returns to stillness and integration.

Chapters

  1. 02:41 Settling In - The meditation begins by finding posture, feeling the body from within, and orienting toward a simple, kind way of practicing.
  2. 03:32 Practice as Kindness - He frames the whole exercise as a gift of kindness to oneself and suggests holding the meditation inside that perspective.
  3. 06:34 Exploring the Lower Body - Attention moves through the legs and feet with anatomical detail, while staying lightly connected to the whole body and the heart's response.
  4. 16:39 Pelvis and Digestive System - The exploration continues through the genitals, bladder, kidneys, and digestive organs, emphasizing playfulness and a sense of wonder.
  5. 24:23 Head and Nervous System - He turns to the head, eyes, teeth, skull, brain, spinal cord, and nerves, inviting a fuller sense of the body's intricate sensitivity.
  6. 28:38 Elements and Interbeing - Water, air, fire, and earth are contemplated as shared elements that blur the boundary between body and world.
  7. 35:35 Solidity and Mystery - The talk moves into atoms, space, and the appearance of matter, opening into non-thingness and the role of consciousness in perception.
  8. 41:51 Luminous Body - An optional imaginal practice invites the listener to draw out a radiant body of light, float and move freely, then return it to the physical body.
  9. 51:00 Integration - The meditation closes by re-grounding in the body, stillness, awareness, and a final return to kindness and care.
