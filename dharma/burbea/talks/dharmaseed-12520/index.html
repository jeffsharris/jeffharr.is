Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Deepening Into Emptiness (Question and Answer Session 4)

Dharma Seed 1:21:12 2011-02-16

In this Q&A, Rob Burbea works through vedana, aversion, clinging, and the felt sense of self, using concrete examples from sitting, walking, and pain. He keeps returning to how allowing, curiosity, and metta reveal deeper layers of experience, including space, love, and a quieter sense of being held. The talk then opens into emptiness: not as a doctrine to cling to, but as a way of seeing how self and phenomena arise dependently and can soften or dissolve.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and metta - Rob begins by encouraging metta for oneself and the room, then takes up a question about vedana and whether sensation, reaction, and clinging belong to one package.
  2. 03:08 Allowing the whole package - Using a loud engine during walking meditation, he distinguishes allowing the sound, the unpleasant feeling, and the aversive response, showing that each can be a valid entry point.
  3. 07:29 Loops of aversion - He describes fear of fear and aversion to aversion as self-reinforcing circuits, and stresses that genuine curiosity matters more than trying to make experience go away.
  4. 11:55 Clinging and pain - Rob explains how clinging and aversion often operate together, as with craving or the wish for comfort, and how tolerating the pain of wanting can let it crest and fade.
  5. 16:45 Lack and delusion - He traces the sense of lack to self-view and cultural conditioning, contrasting therapeutic explanations with a Dharma view that points to a deeper misperception of reality.
  6. 24:49 Body, self, and spaciousness - The discussion moves through self-inquiry in the body, bodily dissolution, and a Goenka retreat story that serves as a bridge to a more imaginal sense of liberation.
  7. 35:12 What emptiness means - Rob takes up the relationship between self and emptiness, warns against nihilism, and offers a practical 'two-box' way of moving between self and emptiness without forcing a final view.
  8. 48:19 Constructing self and phenomena - He shows how less aversion and clinging means less self, then extends the same logic to phenomena, emphasizing that what seems separate depends on how it is looked at.
  9. 01:03:29 Dependent co-arising - Rob draws the mutual dependence of self, aversion, clinging, and phenomena into a broader account of dependent origination, describing the whole display as a kind of magic show.
