Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Instructions on Mindfulness of Mind States

Dharma Seed 30:15 2011-11-06

What if the first thing to notice in practice is not the breath or the story, but the overall state of mind coloring everything else? The talk lays out citta, or mind-heart, as the third foundation of mindfulness and shows how emotions, subtle moods, bodily cues, and perceptions all feed one another in a loop that can either deepen suffering or open space. From there it moves into practical ways of working: recognizing the mind state, feeling it in the body, meeting difficult emotion with delicate attention, and also cultivating wholesome states like metta, compassion, and concentration.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Third Foundation Overview - Burbea introduces citta as mind-and-heart together and defines the talk’s focus as working with mind states rather than thoughts.
  2. 03:20 Why Mind States Matter - He explains how mind states shape perception, speech, action, and suffering through a reinforcing feedback loop.
  3. 06:18 Recognizing the State - He lays out practical clues for recognizing mind states through body sensations, thought patterns, perceptions, and the texture of the mind.
  4. 10:36 Meeting Difficult Emotion - The talk turns to holding difficult emotions with delicacy, using the body and Vedana as a simpler entry point into complexity.
  5. 16:41 Insight and Non-Solidity - Burbea explores questioning assumptions, seeing the self-view around emotion, and noticing the changing, gap-filled nature of even solid-feeling states.
  6. 21:44 Skillful Responses - He offers different ways of working depending on the state: calming agitation, energizing dullness, opening contraction, or turning to metta.
  7. 25:18 Cultivating Wholesome States - Burbea emphasizes metta, compassion, and concentration as deep resources that reset habitual grooves and support letting go.
  8. 28:21 Freedom and Capacity - He closes by linking practice to freedom in emotional life and to the heart’s capacity to hold both suffering and joy.
