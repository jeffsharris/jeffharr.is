Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Instructions on the Mindfulness of Thought

Dharma Seed 25:30 2011-01-01

Rob Burbea offers practical instructions for working with thought in meditation without demonizing it. He distinguishes helpful and unhelpful uses of thinking, shows how thought and mind state reinforce each other, and points to ways of loosening the grip of obsessive or self-critical loops. The talk ends with a short guided sitting that returns to the body, the breath, and a more spacious way of noticing thoughts arise and pass.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Why Thought Matters - Burbea frames the talk as a continuation on the third foundation of mindfulness, focusing now on thought rather than mind states.
  2. 04:25 Mind States and Feedback - He explains how mind states give rise to thoughts, and how clinging to those thoughts can reinforce the very states that trouble us.
  3. 07:36 Returning to the Anchor - For a thick or obsessive mind, he recommends patiently returning again and again to the breath or body as a stabilizing practice.
  4. 09:03 Metta and Emotion - He suggests metta as a way to redirect thinking energy, and then points to emotions or hidden beliefs that may be feeding the loop underneath.
  5. 12:07 Body as Vantage Point - When thought is less turbulent, he invites a grounded contemplation from the body and the use of simple labels like judging or planning to create space.
  6. 15:22 From Content to Process - Burbea shifts from identifying particular thoughts to noticing the process of thinking itself, supported by a wider awareness and listening.
  7. 17:19 Thoughts Arise and Pass - He describes thoughts as ephemeral, insubstantial, and largely unowned, using analogies such as shooting stars in the night sky.
  8. 19:43 Space and Freedom - The talk develops the image of awareness as spacious and unperturbed, so that thoughts can arise and fade without carrying authority.
  9. 22:43 Guided Sitting - He closes with a brief practice period, inviting ease in the body, attention to the anchor, and optional exploration of thought with curiosity.
