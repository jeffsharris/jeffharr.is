Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Dilemmas and Delineations: How did we get <em>here</em>?! (Part 5)

Dharma Seed 62:46 2017-01-22

Rob Burbea explores Emerson’s “two handles” aphorism as a way of approaching desire, eros, emptiness, imagination, and delineation with care. The talk moves from the power and danger of concepts into a subtler argument: distinctions can mislead, but they can also open practice, refine perception, and support soul-making. He closes by stressing that this is not a closed system, but an open field meant to be extended and revised.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Two Handles - Emerson’s aphorism is used to frame the whole talk: teachings and experiences can be taken up in more than one way.
  2. 02:51 Eros As Fire - Rob develops eros as a formidable, non-cutesy force that asks for sensitivity, boldness, and careful experimentation.
  3. 10:19 Diotima and Meaning - Drawing on Plato’s Diotima, he presents eros as a hermeneutical daemon that mediates between the human and divine, and between what is real and less real.
  4. 16:51 Limits of Understanding - He argues that eros can never be fully grasped and that its richness expands both the object of desire and our understanding of eros itself.
  5. 29:29 Dangers of Delineation - The talk turns to the risks of conceptual precision, including self-judgment, over-definition, and the way distinctions can create identity problems.
  6. 44:28 When Distinctions Help - Rob shows that some distinctions reduce fabrication while others increase it, and that both can sometimes serve insight and soul-making.
  7. 57:26 An Open Path - He ends by explaining that the teaching is meant to guide practice now while remaining open to additions, tributaries, and future creativity.
