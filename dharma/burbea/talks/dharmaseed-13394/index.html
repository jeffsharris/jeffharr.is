Healing Ecology - A Buddhist Perspective on the Eco Crisis

Dharma Seed 1:36:38 2011-06-07

If the separate self is a construct, what happens when we apply that same insight to civilization and the ecological crisis? The talk moves from a small tree-spirit story in the Vinaya to a wider argument that our habits of growth, control, and technological rescue mirror the very anxiety Buddhism points to in the individual mind. From there it widens again into evolution, cosmic creativity, and the possibility that practice is not only personal awakening but a collective change in how humanity lives within the biosphere.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the question - Loy introduces the topic and asks what Buddhism can really contribute to understanding the ecological crisis.
  2. 03:50 Trees and early Buddhism - He turns to the Buddha’s life under trees and the Vinaya story of the tree spirit as a limited but revealing environmental motif.
  3. 09:22 Self and separation - The talk develops the idea of anatta as a constructed self that produces anxiety, and awakening as seeing through separation.
  4. 14:56 Nonduality with earth - Loy brings in Thich Nhat Hanh and Dogen to show that mind and world, human civilization and the biosphere, are not truly separate.
  5. 24:46 Civilization as construct - He contrasts ancient inherited meanings with modern freedom and argues that our responses to insecurity often deepen alienation from the natural world.
  6. 36:35 Growth and control - The talk examines endless growth, technology, and energy use as collective attempts to secure what cannot be secured, including a critique of nuclear power and a call to reorganize around available energy.
  7. 55:03 Cosmic creativity - Loy moves into evolution, using Brian Swimme, Thomas Berry, and Dobzhansky to suggest that the cosmos is creative and possibly becoming more self-aware.
  8. 01:05:05 Practice and transformation - He reflects on compassion, meditation, and the Sixth Patriarch as ways of getting the ego out of the way so something deeper can manifest.
  9. 01:19:46 Questions and closing - In the Q&A he addresses population, denial, unconscious patterning, the Angulimala story, shunyata, and ends with the Diamond Sutra's invitation to let mind arise without fixation.
