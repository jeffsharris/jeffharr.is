The Place of Samadhi in Metta Practice

Dharma Seed 1:26:04 2008-01-29

What if samadhi in metta practice is not narrow concentration, but a steady, receptive way of dwelling that lets the heart settle, soften, and open? The talk starts by widening samadhi beyond the usual idea of “keeping the mind on one object,” then turns to the practical question of how concentration, kindness, and happiness support one another over a retreat. From there it moves through common objections—tightness, striving, attachment, suppression—and keeps returning to a quieter view: notice the conditions shaping experience, work gently, and let the practice become less fabricated and more livable.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening Orientation - He introduces samadhi as a central theme for the retreat and frames it as part of the path of metta and compassion.
  2. 03:00 Metta and Concentration - He notes the traditional view that lovingkindness and compassion can lead to concentration, and begins widening what samadhi means.
  3. 12:02 Why Samadhi Matters - He argues that samadhi deserves more attention, using the Buddha and examples of cultivated skill to show its significance in life and practice.
  4. 21:40 Steadiness and Well-Being - He describes the fruits of samadhi as healing, steadiness, happiness, and the kind of clarity that makes practice livable.
  5. 32:20 Whole-Body Softness - He turns to effort, tightness, and receptivity, urging a broader bodily awareness and a more responsive style of practice.
  6. 41:19 Dwelling Places of the Heart - He expands the image of brahmavihara and explains how samadhi and metta can become inner abidings rather than anxious or self-critical habits.
  7. 53:12 Pleasure and Fear - He addresses concern about attachment to meditation pleasure, the rise of pīti, and the fear that can appear when practice opens or the self softens.
  8. 01:03:02 Emptiness in Experience - He explores how emotions and identities are built from present conditions, and how repeated practice loosens self-story and reveals emptiness.
  9. 01:15:01 Questions and Practice - In the Q&A, he clarifies how phrases change as practice deepens and how to distinguish genuine appreciation from distraction or grasping.
