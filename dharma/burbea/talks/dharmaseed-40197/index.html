Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Wisdom, Art, Balance (Part 3)

Dharma Seed 53:39 2017-01-30

How do you work with desire without flattening it, obeying it, or becoming rigid? The talk begins with mastery as a fluid balance: learning to navigate, in the heat of practice, between letting go, staying with feeling tone and craving, meeting the underlying emotion with kindness, or deliberately turning toward nourishment and spaciousness. It then moves into imaginal practice, where the speaker explores how to stay with erotic image, notice what makes it hard to bear, and make subtle shifts of emphasis without leaving the field of the practice.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Mastery as navigation - He frames mastery as fluid balance: moment-to-moment responding, not rigidly repeating one method.
  2. 04:27 Why range matters - He contrasts habitual one-sided practice with the wider range needed for freedom and insight.
  3. 08:15 Letting desire pass - He explores noticing pleasure, seeing where it goes, and using impermanence to support letting go.
  4. 12:35 Vedanā and craving - He shifts to pleasant and unpleasant feeling tones, tracing craving and the pressure that can be tolerated and allowed.
  5. 17:26 Kindness for emotion - He introduces a gentler response: caring for the self and holding the underlying emotion with mindfulness and metta.
  6. 22:02 Subtle skill in practice - He emphasizes delicate shifts of attention and the artistry of micro-adjustments in the heat of practice.
  7. 25:21 Skillful redirection - He presents conscious turning away from desire toward nourishment, samadhi, metta, spaciousness, and less fabrication.
  8. 30:01 Opening desire's depth - Drawing on an earlier talk, he outlines a three-step inquiry into what is really wanted and how to open to desire's energy.
  9. 40:35 Working with eros - He focuses on staying with imaginal images, meeting what is hard to bear, trusting dissolution, and distinguishing eros from equanimity.
