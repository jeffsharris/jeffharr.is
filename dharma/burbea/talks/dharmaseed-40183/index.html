Opening The Dharma of Desire (Part 2)

Dharma Seed 1:19:13 2017-02-18

What if desire is not mainly a problem to be suppressed, but a way of inquiry that can open into soulmaking? The talk moves from questioning assumptions about craving and selfing, to distinguishing eros from papañca, to showing how an unavailable beloved or a teacher-fantasy can become an imaginal opening rather than just something to let go of. It then widens into a detailed argument that desire can arise from abundance, already be present in the imaginal, and even be transformed by how it is seen, held, and understood.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Inquiry as invitation - He begins by emphasizing permission to question, experiment, and uncover assumptions for oneself.
  2. 02:11 Reframing desire - He revisits the earlier practice of opening to a deeper current of desire and how it unsettles common views of selfing and defilement.
  3. 05:15 Eros and the imaginal - He distinguishes eros from craving and shows how imaginal perception can expand what feels stuck or impossible.
  4. 10:53 Peace without flattening - He argues that allowing eros can bring equanimity and spaciousness without losing soul or depth.
  5. 14:51 Teacher fantasy and soulmaking - He turns to teacher-student longing, keeps the boundary clear, and uses a student’s letter to show how attraction can reveal a whole world.
  6. 23:09 Psyche loves itself - He develops the idea that psyche is drawn to its own treasures and that boundaries can form a temenos or alchemical vessel.
  7. 29:17 Desire beyond lack - He questions the idea that desire comes from deficiency and explores longing, trust, and the possibility that desire is already flowing in the imaginal.
  8. 53:41 Ways of looking - He reviews several practices that transform desire through imaginal perception, including divine influx and hierophantic time.
  9. 01:07:51 Alchemy of desire - He closes by distinguishing many forms of desire, then gathers them into an alchemy in which craving can become eros and transubstantiation.
