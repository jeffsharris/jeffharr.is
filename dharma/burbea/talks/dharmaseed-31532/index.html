Opening, Tuning, and Relating to the Imaginal: Instructions (3)

Dharma Seed 69:44 2015-08-11

How do you keep an image alive without forcing it into a literal meaning or a fixed method? The talk moves through walking meditation as either samadhi or imaginal practice, then turns to how to meet images by asking what they want, how long to stay with them, and whether the relationship feels helpful and soulful. From there it opens into stories of healing, love, reverence, doubt, and the way images can mirror life or spill back into it without needing to be reduced to advice. Near the end, the speaker widens the frame again to the register or “octave” of an image and the way imaginal forms can become portable in daily life.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Walking as Practice - He clarifies that walking is a posture, and shows how it can host either samadhi or imaginal practice through energy-body awareness.
  2. 04:12 Walking with Images - He gives walking-metta and imaginal examples, then corrects the idea that images should be held for a fixed amount of time.
  3. 07:02 What the Figure Wants - He reframes the relation to an imaginal figure as asking what it wants, including being seen, honored, or looked at in a particular way.
  4. 09:53 Healing Imaginal Encounters - Through the lion, phoenix, and fox stories, he shows how images can bring bodily healing, protection, erotic charge, and reverence.
  5. 16:37 Love and Eros - He explores the particularity of love for imaginal figures, using Eric Dolphy and Mary Oliver to show how love and eros can deepen sacredness.
  6. 21:45 Images and Life - He shares a story of asking priest-like figures about writing a book on emptiness, then emphasizes that soulmaking matters more than advice.
  7. 28:35 Literal and Nonliteral - The moonlit lake and unicorn, then the painter's studio, illustrate how images can feel deeply real without being read as literal instructions.
  8. 33:02 Doubt and Experiment - He addresses doubt, fear of doing it wrong, and the worry of making things up, replacing them with experimentation and a soulfulness check.
  9. 59:52 Octaves and Portability - He closes by describing the register of an image, how it shifts with mind state, and how imaginal figures can be carried into daily life and wider perceptions.
