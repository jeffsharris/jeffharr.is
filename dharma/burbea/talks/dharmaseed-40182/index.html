Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Opening The Dharma of Desire (Part 1)

Dharma Seed 68:38 2017-02-17

In this talk, Rob Burbea revisits a practice for working with desire that begins by asking what desire really wants, then clearing away default Dharma assumptions about desire, and finally feeling the movement of desire in the body and energy body. He uses personal stories and teaching examples to show how desire can open into something more general and life-giving, rather than collapsing into craving or self-division. Later, he places the practice alongside emotional pain, dependent origination, and his broader work on eros and emptiness, while cautioning that the method is not the same as generic acceptance.

  4. 17:32 Examples of craving - He uses a romance interview and related reflections to show how craving amplifies difference and how deeper desire can be uncovered.
  5. 32:16 Desire becomes inquiry - A second autobiographical example shows how pressure and impatience around study can open into joy in ongoing inquiry.
  6. 40:02 Desire and lack - He pauses to note that desire is often mixed with lack and that sometimes distress or grief needs to be met first.
  7. 45:11 Experiment, don't flatten - He stresses flexibility, warns against reducing the teaching to simple acceptance, and encourages repeated experimentation until the method becomes a usable tool.
  8. 01:02:36 Relation to imaginal practice - He closes by contrasting this practice with imaginal work, then widens back out to clinging, dependent origination, and emptiness.
