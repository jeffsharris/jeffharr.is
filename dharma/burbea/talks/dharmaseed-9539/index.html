Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

An Introduction to Emptiness

Dharma Seed 1:12:21 2010-01-21

Emptiness begins here as a challenge to how experience seems: the talk asks how things, selves, and situations come to feel so solid when they are so dependent on view, context, and the mind’s own constructions. Rather than staying with abstract philosophy, the speaker moves through ordinary examples—fear, relationships, work, nations, lunch, weather—to show how the mind joins the dots and makes suffering out of what is more fluid than it appears. The practice emphasis is on deliberate contemplation: noticing where attention gets sucked into difficulty, questioning the story, and seeing the gaps that appear when experience is examined closely. Classical references to Nagarjuna, the Buddha, and other teachers are used to keep the teaching anchored in a middle way between reification and nihilism.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Setting the frame - He opens by situating the talk within a retreat that is still prioritizing samadhi while beginning the inquiry into emptiness.
  2. 05:46 Why emptiness matters - He reframes emptiness as joyful and freeing, using Nagarjuna and Dilgo Khyentse Rinpoche to link it with peace, love, and effortless care.
  3. 12:44 What emptiness means - He defines emptiness as a way of describing things, not a thing or state, and explains inherent existence, essence, and self-existence.
  4. 19:45 Root delusion - Through Chandrakirti and a contrast between bare attention and deliberate contemplation, he argues that emptiness must be seen actively, not merely noticed.
  5. 24:15 Everyday fabrication - He illustrates how the mind turns ordinary conflict, social status, and national identity into solid problems through time lag, abstraction, and cultural agreement.
  6. 31:18 Relationship and attention - He shows how relationships, roles, and retreat life are built into seeming realities, then explains how attention gets sucked into difficulty and needs space around it.
  7. 39:14 Dot-to-dot experience - Using a fear-of-homelessness example, he shows how mindfulness reveals emotions as strands and experience as joined dots rather than a single solid thing.
  8. 49:00 Dependent arising and mind - He deepens the teaching by arguing that emptiness is really about mind-dependence, citing the Buddha, Nagarjuna, and the idea of conceptual imputation.
  9. 01:04:39 Middle way and practice - He closes by warning against both blankness and nihilism, returning to texts that connect analysis of emptiness with nirvana, compassion, and peace.
