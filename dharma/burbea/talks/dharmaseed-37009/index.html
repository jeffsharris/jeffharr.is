Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Images of the Breath: Energy Body, Colour, and the Beloved (Guided Meditation)

Dharma Seed 45:48 2016-07-31

Rob Burbea offers a guided meditation that moves from the felt energy of the body into several imaginal ways of breathing: through different centers, through the whole field, as light, and as color. The practice then opens into blessing and relationship, including the possibility of meeting an imaginal beloved and breathing in harmony with that figure. The talk ends by inviting listeners to notice how image changes the sense of self, others, and world.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Why images matter - Burbea briefly frames imaginal practice as something that develops with familiarity and interest, and notes how it can interweave with samadhi and emptiness practice.
  2. 03:06 Settling the body - The guided meditation begins by establishing posture, spreading awareness through the body, and sensing the energy field as alive and responsive.
  3. 06:51 Lower centers and grounding - He explores breathing at the lower belly, perineum, feet, and lower back, inviting attention to what feels comfortable and good.
  4. 11:23 Heart and crown - The practice expands to the back of the heart and then the crown of the head, always emphasizing that the whole body remains involved.
  5. 14:32 Front of body opening - Attention moves to the chest and solar plexus, with the breath imagined as opening and softening the body from the front.
  6. 20:08 Porous field of breath - Burbea invites the sense of breath and energy arriving from all sides or growing from within, and lets the breath become very subtle or still.
  7. 23:51 Light and color - The breath is then felt as light, given color, and noticed for its different qualities, with green, red, and blue used as examples of distinct felt tones.
  8. 29:16 Blessing radiance - The light is allowed to radiate outward as healing or blessing, reshaping the surrounding world into a sacred mandala or Buddha realm.
  9. 34:14 Meeting the beloved - An imaginal figure, perhaps a beloved, is brought into the field and the meditation explores breathing together, passing breath back and forth, and opening to love.
