Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Refractions: Of Body, Sensuality, and Sexuality (Part 3)

Dharma Seed 1:13:11 2017-02-15

What happens when the impulse toward simplicity becomes a shadow that closes off self, sexuality, and body? The talk begins by questioning the habit of treating insight as if it only illuminates, then follows how every view also casts blind spots—using the Four Noble Truths, no-self, and renunciation as examples of teachings that are liberating but double-edged. From there it turns toward recent openness around sexual orientation and gender, imaginal practice, and the way eros can widen perception of the body into something more multifaceted, sensual, and alive. Near the end, the speaker contrasts a narrow, reductionist picture of the body with a view of it as endlessly revealing, a field of possible ways of knowing.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Shadow of Insight - Burbea defines shadow as the blind spot that any insight or perspective inevitably casts.
  2. 05:44 Sexuality and Complexity - He returns to the earlier discussion of sexuality as a historical and psychic deepening of selfhood, drawing on Foucault.
  3. 09:11 Against Simplism - He criticizes a lazy form of Dharma that uses no-self and renunciation to avoid engagement with complexity.
  4. 16:04 Gender and Opening - He suggests that cultural shifts around gender and sexual orientation can be understood as soulmaking movements.
  5. 20:33 Body as Expressive Field - The talk turns toward the body, energy body, movement, and the ways imaginal practice can widen perception.
  6. 41:33 Erotic Perception - Burbea explores how eros can alter the way the body appears, using examples from imagination, beauty, and lived context.
  7. 49:25 Freud and Polymorphous Eros - He contrasts soulmaking with Freud’s account of infant sexuality and the contraction of erotic possibility through training.
  8. 53:18 Infinite Modes of Knowing - The body is described as endlessly revealing, with more possible ways of sensing, knowing, and conceiving than modern reductionism allows.
  9. 01:03:35 Body, Soul, and View - He ends by treating the body as a soulscape and a locus of epistemic potential, asking what soulmaking wants to open next.
