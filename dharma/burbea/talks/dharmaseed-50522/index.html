Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Destiny, Autonomy, Love, and Soul (Q & A)

Dharma Seed 39:46 2018-02-07

How do duty, destiny, autonomy, and love fit together when an image begins to shape a life? The talk starts from a question about duty and gradually opens into a careful account of soulmaking: image, eros, and logos mutually affect one another, and what feels like a calling is both received and participated in. It then turns to darker or terrifying images, asking how to tell what is psychologically real, what is imaginal, and how trust, love, and conceptual framework change what can be met. The closing movement returns to loving and being loved by an image, showing how that relationship can become subtler, more alive, and more person-like.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening the Question - The conversation begins with the listener's layered question about duty, destiny, autonomy, emptiness, and how image and action belong together.
  2. 03:29 Duty and Soulmaking - Burbea explains duty as something that arises within soulmaking, where image, sensitivity, and participation all interweave.
  3. 10:33 Destiny and Choice - He reframes destiny as an offering that becomes destiny only when it is taken up, while insisting that autonomy remains present throughout.
  4. 17:48 Subtlety and Dark Images - The talk turns to how imaginal work becomes more delicate over time, including questions about terrifying or dark images and how to discern their meaning.
  5. 24:59 Conceptual Framework - Burbea discusses logos as part of the soulmaking field, showing how culture, belief, and trust shape what an image can become.
  6. 29:43 Loving and Being Loved - A second question asks how to recognize being loved by an image, and Burbea describes reciprocity, subtle communication, and the image's own desire.
  7. 33:54 Personhood of the Image - He closes by describing the image as having its own intelligence and personhood, and by showing how eros and logos can make the imaginal world richer and more alive.
