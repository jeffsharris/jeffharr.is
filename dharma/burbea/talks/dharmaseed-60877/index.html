Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Q & A

Dharma Seed 12:28 2019-12-20

How do subtle emotional shifts, a quiet breath, and even pain become part of one practice of deepening? The talk moves from four linked capacities—sensitivity, attunement, responsiveness, and refinement—into examples from metta, samadhi, and the jhanas, showing how attention can be tuned to increasingly subtle qualities rather than chasing intensity. In the second half, the focus turns to piti and pain: how to work with discomfort without just gritting the teeth, and how relationship and view shape whether energy opens or contracts.

Chapters

  1. 00:06 Four related capacities - He unpacks sensitivity, attunement, responsiveness, and refinement through a metta example and the changing feel of practice.
  2. 03:47 Subtle object, subtle mind - The talk shifts to what happens when the meditation object becomes subtle, and why attention has to become correspondingly subtle.
  3. 06:07 Working with piti and pain - He answers a question about piti, suggesting ways to stay with it, spread it, or change posture when pain becomes too much.
  4. 08:41 Resolve and letting be - A follow-up explores whether firmness alone produces piti, and he brings in allowing, insight, and release of clinging.
  5. 11:08 What is the playground - He closes by stressing that practice depends on knowing your current aim and the actual terrain you are training in.
