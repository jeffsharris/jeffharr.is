Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Unfabricated, The Deathless...

Dharma Seed 67:13 2009-02-15

What happens when the path is traced from ordinary mindfulness all the way into the fading of subject, object, and time? The talk begins with a broad map of practice—presence, relationship, healing, beauty, and samadhi—before narrowing to the question of what the Buddha means by the deathless, the unfabricated, and the unconditioned. From there it moves through repeated warnings not to pre-decide the answer, a long series of quotations from the suttas, and a careful distinction between pleasant absorptions, deepening emptiness, and the cessation of fabrication itself.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Practice as a wide path - He opens by framing the talk as a map of practice that includes much more than meditation technique.
  2. 01:28 What the path includes - He expands the path to include presence, intimacy, relationship, healing, creativity, and the cultivation of beautiful qualities.
  3. 06:47 Nirvana and the deathless - He turns to the Buddha’s shifting use of nirvana and argues that the suttas repeatedly point to the deathless, unborn, and unfabricated.
  4. 13:20 Not pre-deciding truth - He warns against deciding these questions from tradition or theory before practice has actually made them knowable.
  5. 17:00 Fading and samadhi - He describes gradual dis-identification, fading of experience, and how samadhi can steady the mind as perception thins.
  6. 23:19 Dependent arising deepens - He links fading to emptiness and dependent origination, stressing that the point is understanding rather than chasing a special state.
  7. 31:45 Reed, house, and cessation - Through canonical images of reeds and a house beam, he shows how consciousness and world depend on conditions and can cease together.
  8. 39:25 Beyond language and footing - He examines negative and positive formulations of nibbana, including consciousness without feature, and says ordinary language cannot hold it.
  9. 48:17 Questions at the edge - In dialogue he distinguishes emptiness from ordinary non-dual reports, then ends with Sariputta’s refusal of all conceptual answers about what remains.
