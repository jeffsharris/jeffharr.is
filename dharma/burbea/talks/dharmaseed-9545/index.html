Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Love, Healing, and Emptiness

Dharma Seed 1:11:21 2010-02-07

If emptiness is real, why does it so often feel like it leads to less feeling, less warmth, or even nihilism? The talk turns that question around, arguing that seeing the empty, dependent nature of self and phenomena should loosen clinging, soften judgment, and open a more natural love and compassion. Through examples from metta, anger, healing, karma, and the Mahayana idea of bodhicitta, the talk keeps returning to one movement: less selfing, more spaciousness, and a fuller capacity to care.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Emptiness and love - He opens by questioning the common fear that emptiness leads to nihilism and instead argues that emptiness should deepen love and compassion.
  2. 03:26 Rumi and purification - A Rumi verse and related reflections are used to show how emptiness can purify love of projection, neediness, and self-interest.
  3. 08:19 Heart practices and self - He turns to metta, compassion, and the sense of burden, arguing that compassion is not suffering and that self-interest is what closes the heart.
  4. 14:47 Giving back and anger - Dedication of merit and the image of giving the aggregates back to the universe lead into a careful deconstruction of anger and blame.
  5. 23:56 Categories and perception - He shows how metta categories are fluid and how our judgments of others depend on conditioning, self-benefit, and the heart's state.
  6. 33:12 Mara and dependent arising - The Buddha's awakening story, Mara's arrows, and the salt-crystal image are used to show that experience is shaped by conditions, not fixed essence.
  7. 47:08 Healing and fabrication - He questions a simple purification model, then argues that even passive awareness is active fabrication and that healing needs both personal and emptiness views.
  8. 58:37 Three levels of compassion - The talk closes with graded forms of compassion, culminating in emptiness-informed love, bodhicitta, and the fullness of giving.
