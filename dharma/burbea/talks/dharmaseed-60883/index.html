The Fifth Jhana (The Realm of Infinite Space)

Dharma Seed 1:34:18 2020-01-03

Rob Burbea explores the fifth jhana as the realm of infinite space, moving from the Buddha’s brief sutta description into a careful account of how perception shifts when solidity, resistance, and attention to diversity fall away. He then widens the talk into related themes: the role of metta and compassion in the higher jhanas, the practical use of insight ways of looking, and the way boundless space can change how body, world, and self are perceived. In the latter part of the talk he turns to desire, asking what a person truly wants, how that shapes practice, and how Soulmaking ideas can help sustain an authentic path.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the fifth jhana - Rob introduces the realm of infinite space and frames it as a topic that may feel remote or immediate depending on the listener.
  2. 02:35 The Buddha's description - He reads the sutta passage closely and explains the sequence of transcending form, resistance, and diversity before entering infinite space.
  3. 07:01 What space means - Rob unpacks the language of formlessness, subtle materiality, and the cosmological map that links jhana states with realms of experience.
  4. 16:57 How it opens - He compares the Visuddhimagga kasina method with his own approach, emphasizing that space opens by noticing non-solidity rather than by forcing expansion.
  5. 23:36 Fourth to fifth jhana - Rob describes the relationship between the fourth jhana and the formless realms, including the role of stillness, release, and secondary signs such as delight and oneness.
  6. 32:53 Metta and higher realms - He argues that metta and compassion can extend beyond the lower jhanas and can be mixed with infinite space and related states.
  7. 42:03 Insight as a way of looking - Using a Majjhima Nikaya sutta, he shows how impermanence, dukkha, and elemental perception can function as lived ways of looking that open into non-solidity.
  8. 51:49 Presence and worldviews - Rob questions the modern ideal of simply being present, showing how it rests on hidden assumptions about practice, value, and the world.
  9. 01:06:00 Desire, authenticity, and practice - The talk expands into a long reflection on authentic desire, cultural conditioning, Soulmaking, and the self on the path, before returning briefly to infinite space and closing.
