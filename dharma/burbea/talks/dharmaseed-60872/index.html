Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Q & A

Dharma Seed 1:30:33 2019-12-30

How does a retreat hold silence without turning it into dryness, and how do desire and equanimity deepen practice without becoming either indulgence or indifference? The talk begins with a quiet request to keep silence outside the Q&A, then moves into a long exchange on intention, risk, and the way desire can shape a life while still being conditioned and changeable. From there it turns to equanimity, the spectrum of clinging across the jhānas, the bodily texture of pīti and sukha, and the difference between jhāna’s movement toward dissolution and imaginal practice’s preservation of two-ness. Along the way, the speaker keeps returning to a practical theme: learn the subtle patterns, stay sensitive, and let the practice reveal what is actually happening.

Chapters

  1. 04:52 Silence on retreat - Rob begins by asking the group to keep silence outside the formal Q&A and explains silence as a way to let sensitivity and connection deepen.
  2. 06:56 Goal and intention - He answers a question about jhāna and shows how intention involves letting go of clinging, aversion, and the usual ways we chase goals.
  3. 14:35 Desire and devotion - The talk turns to the gravity of desire, self-doubt, and the difference between shallow wishes and deeper currents of devotion and Eros.
  4. 24:17 What equanimity means - Rob unpacks equanimity as equipoise, equal seeing, and non-grasping, then expands the teaching from worldly balance into deeper meditative refinement.
  5. 36:01 Clinging as a spectrum - He reframes push-pull and clinging as continuous and subtle, showing how deeper jhāna and insight reveal finer layers rather than a simple on-off switch.
  6. 45:39 Desire, fabrication, and practice - Rob distinguishes between useful and unhelpful ways of seeing desire as fabricated, and encourages learning from what moves, weakens, or sustains desire over time.
  7. 53:58 Pīti, sukha, and the body - He explains jhānic pleasure as whole-body, energetic experience and answers questions about emotion, bodily feeling, and the practical use of the energy body.
  8. 01:02:34 Jhāna and soulmaking - The later discussion contrasts jhāna with Soulmaking Dharma: imagery and eros keep polarity, while jhāna tends toward dissolution and less duality.
  9. 01:20:12 Bypass and closing questions - Rob closes by warning that no single practice is complete, then returns to how jhāna can reveal emotional patterns without simply suppressing them.
