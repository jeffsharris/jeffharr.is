Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Metta Instructions and Guided Meditation 3

Dharma Seed 44:04 2010-08-09

The talk begins with the difficulty of receiving love, and then turns that problem into practice by asking what it is to receive from nature, from other people, and even from one’s own metta. From there, the speaker introduces a gentle way of working with the neutral person: noticing common humanity, vulnerability, mortality, and the ways self-consciousness can block openness. The talk then moves into a guided mettā meditation, first with the easier figures and then with the neutral person, gradually widening the field of well-wishing and ending in a simple, open sensitivity to the body.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Receiving Kindness - Rob explores why receiving love can feel harder than giving it, and points to support already present in nature, retreat life, and self-metta.
  2. 08:28 Metta And Energy - He introduces the practice direction for the day, including how sexual energy can be worked with in the body and softened toward metta.
  3. 11:14 Neutral Person Reflections - Rob explains the neutral person, using common humanity, mortality, and vulnerability to make the practice more vivid and less flat.
  4. 18:30 Settling For Practice - The guided meditation begins with posture, openness, and a broad body awareness before any specific phrase of metta.
  5. 21:26 Self Metta And Easiest Person - He invites loving-kindness first toward whatever feels easiest, allowing the practice to become warm, tender, and playful.
  6. 32:08 Neutral Person Practice - The meditation turns to the neutral person, emphasizing shared wishes for well-being and the common fragility of human life.
  7. 35:52 Closing And Return - After periods of silent metta and repetition, the practice ends with a sense of preciousness and a return to simple bodily awareness.
