Preliminaries, Regarding Voice, Movement, and Gesture (Part 2)

Dharma Seed 1:50:27 2020-03-02

The talk begins with a practical question: if these voice, movement, and gesture exercises matter, why do some people never take them up or let them fade? From there, the speaker moves through the reasons these preliminaries matter for soulmaking, the risk of inertia, and the need to develop sensitivity without turning the practice into either a casual sideline or an obsession. The second half turns to five specific exercise forms—brief emphatic movement, being borne aloft, raging beast, immovable protector, and the serpent—each presented as a way to reopen blocked circuits so movement, voice, image, and soul can work together more freely.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Setting the frame - He situates this talk as a continuation of earlier material and asks whether listeners have actually worked with the previous exercises.
  2. 06:26 Why the preliminaries matter - He explains how these small practices reveal inertia, discernment, and the difference between merely hearing teachings and taking them up.
  3. 11:31 Sensitivity and holding back - He develops two main reasons for the practices: increasing sensitivity and noticing how body, voice, and energy are habitually held in.
  4. 24:51 Regret and the middle way - Using his own regret about jazz technique, he argues that these exercises are offered so people do not later regret not supporting what matters most.
  5. 32:00 Exercise three begins - He introduces the third exercise series and first names the 'thunderclap' practice, a brief emphatic movement that gathers intention, body, and soul.
  6. 42:00 Angels and the raging beast - He presents two more modes of practice: being borne aloft by angels, and allowing a raging beast to move through the body and voice.
  7. 46:55 Guardian and serpent - He adds the immovable protector and the serpent, keeping the names open-ended and emphasizing that each practice can be done physically or imaginally.
  8. 50:50 Circuits and consequences - He explains why these 'technical' exercises matter: they can reopen blocked circuits that limit imagination, emotion, and life choices.
  9. 01:05:22 Discernment in life - He closes by linking the exercises to being seen, judged, and called into life, including examples from Goenka retreats, the Ridwan school, and imaginal help from angels and demons.
