Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Energy Body and Image (Q & A)

Dharma Seed 45:45 2017-03-30

How do you work with experience when the body feels split between something wide, pleasant, and subtle, and something else contracted, jerky, or hard to breathe into? The talk moves from clarifying what is meant by the energy body to showing how breath, attention, and imagination can soften contraction, open movement, and shift what feels pleasant or difficult. It then turns toward imaginal practice: how images arise, how they can be received without grasping, and how relationship itself shapes what unfolds.

Chapters

  1. 00:16 Opening Question - A listener asks about the energy body, breath body, astral body, and the felt difference between subtle pleasantness and a more contracted physical experience.
  2. 03:18 Energy Body as Relation - Rob reframes the energy body as a way of looking, sensing, and relating, rather than a separate object or realm.
  3. 07:00 Maps and Dependent Arising - He uses different chakra systems and body maps to show how concepts fabricate experience and remain useful only when they serve exploration.
  4. 10:06 Working with Restlessness - The talk turns practical: if something feels stuck or restless, one can use attention, imagination, or breath to let energy move more freely.
  5. 16:03 Meeting Drowning Sensations - Rob addresses fear and shallow breathing with care, emphasizing gentle attention, kindness, and soothing the person who feels overwhelmed.
  6. 19:45 How Imaginal Perception Arises - Using sunlight, metta, and poetic language, he describes how ordinary perception can become imaginal when warmth, meaning, and receptivity come together.
  7. 24:34 Loosening Conceptual Grip - He expands on the conditions for imaginal life: loosened concepts, humility, reverence, and a more total involvement of the energy body.
  8. 40:23 Samadhi and Fabrication - In the final major section, Rob contrasts samadhi as less fabrication with imaginal practice as a subtler kind of fabrication that can still deepen and open.
  9. 43:27 Pīti and Relationship - He closes by discussing pīti, resistance, and the way relationship shapes body, world, emotion, mind, image, and energy.
