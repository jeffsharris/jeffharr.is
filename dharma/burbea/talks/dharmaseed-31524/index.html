A Sacred Universe: Insight, Theophany, Cosmopoesis (Part 5)

Dharma Seed 1:27:57 2015-08-13

What happens when perception is no longer treated as a neutral window, but as something always already shaped by conceiving? The talk moves from a critique of bare attention and fixed ideas of divinity into a series of detailed examples where words, sounds, memories, and images open into the sacred: a tap sounding like jazz, bird song as sacred text, studying at a desk as theophany, and even musical longing as a way into wider seeing. Along the way, the speaker keeps returning to the same tension: how to let the imaginal stay alive without forcing it into belief or locking it into a single meaning.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Perception and conceiving - He reopens a basic point from the retreat: perception is always already woven with conceiving, even when it seems nonconceptual.
  2. 02:16 Vague sacred concepts - He compares the vagueness of divinity to the vagueness of matter, and argues that sacredness is less a fixed idea than a trainable way of looking.
  3. 07:04 Where God is found - Using the Kotzker Rebbe, he shows how a concept of God can become a question of entry, range, and the opening of perception.
  4. 10:19 Not one true divine - He emphasizes the plurality of imaginal experience, warning against clinging to a single correct experience of God or the sacred.
  5. 15:09 Jesus, mythos, and belief - Through Christian material, Q scholarship, and his own history, he reflects on the difference between literal belief and an imaginal figure that still moves the soul.
  6. 21:15 Tzedakah and the gift - A meditation on the word tzedakah becomes an example of how a small cue can open an image of being a gift from God and giving oneself back.
  7. 28:40 Theophany in sound - He describes bird song, a garden tap, and nature as triggers for theophanic perception, where ordinary sound discloses a deeper level of the world.
  8. 39:53 Sacred text and sacred world - Bird song becomes a sacred text, then broadens into the whole body, room, and surrounding world as a field of meaning and praise.
  9. 51:26 Music, grief, and freedom - A long personal sequence follows, moving through studying, jazz memory, a dream guitarist, grief at leaving music, and the freedom these images still carry.
