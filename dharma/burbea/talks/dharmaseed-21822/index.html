Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Wise Responses to the Hindrances

Dharma Seed 53:29 2013-11-02

What if the five hindrances are not proof that practice is going badly, but invitations to change the way the mind is relating? The talk moves through sense desire, ill will, dullness, restlessness, and doubt, showing how each can be met with a mix of antidotes, body awareness, and a less personal attitude. Again and again, the emphasis falls on loosening self-judgment, widening the frame, and noticing how perception itself is colored by the mind state. By the end, the hindrances are presented not just as obstacles, but as places where insight and freedom can actually begin.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the Frame - Burbea introduces the five hindrances and sets up the talk as a look at practice attitude rather than just technique.
  2. 03:26 Not Personal Failure - He emphasizes that hindrances are part of being human and warns against taking them as evidence of inadequacy or lack of progress.
  3. 07:22 Samadhi Beyond Focus - The talk widens into a fuller account of samadhi as unification and harmonization, supported by kindness, openness, and letting be.
  4. 15:37 Working With Desire - Sense desire is met by returning gently, noticing pleasantness in the body, and learning to stay with subtler satisfactions.
  5. 20:24 Aversion and Ill Will - He offers ways to soften ill will by changing perception, feeling the pain inside aversion, and simplifying attention to what is actually felt.
  6. 24:29 Listening Underneath - Burbea shows how anger can carry useful energy and how boredom, resistance, and other states may be supported by aversion and deeper emotions.
  7. 28:32 Dullness and Energy - He turns to dullness and drowsiness, suggesting breath, posture, light, spaciousness, movement, and whole-body awareness as ways to brighten practice.
  8. 32:42 Restlessness and Subtle Effort - Restlessness and worry are met through the out-breath, space, and a careful tuning of effort, including the subtle forms of sinking and drifting.
  9. 45:25 Doubt and Perception - The talk closes with doubt, the value of questioning, and the larger insight that mind states color perception and can be known without believing them.
