Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Imaginal-Energy Body (Q & A)

Dharma Seed 1:17:43 2019-03-24

How does the body open when practice is no longer limited to bare sensations? The talk moves from a practical, relational beginning on retreat into a careful explanation of the energy body as felt sense, image, and idea, then widens further into imaginal possibility, the roots of soul, and the role of attention in shaping experience. It keeps returning to a simple question: what happens when contraction, eros, and kindness are read as live information rather than fixed states? The final stretch turns to jhana, asking how absorption and imaginal richness can inform one another without collapsing into a single approach.

Chapters

  1. 00:07 Retreat opening - Rob sets a warm, relational tone and explains the retreat’s practical culture of openness, while still honoring the need to turn inward when necessary.
  2. 06:48 One bite at a time - He responds to the feeling of being overwhelmed by the range of teachings, stressing kindness, patience, and choosing one thread to work with.
  3. 10:59 What energy body means - Rob defines the body in terms of felt sense, image, and idea, then shows how the energy body opens all three beyond conventional limits.
  4. 15:42 From samadhi to imaginal - He traces the term’s earlier use in samatha and emptiness practice, then explains how imaginal practice expands the body into a poetic and more elastic field.
  5. 22:01 Attunement and development - Rob clarifies that the energy body is more a poise of awareness than a thing, and gives examples of how its range deepens over time.
  6. 28:36 Grief, self, and mystery - Using a morning example of touching the body, he shows how bodily sensation can open into grief, compassion, narrative, and a more imaginal sense of self and death.
  7. 34:25 Aura, reification, and attention - He distinguishes energy body from aura language, warns against reifying experience, and emphasizes that the way of looking changes what appears.
  8. 45:27 Craving and navigation - Rob uses contraction and expansion as practical signs of craving or eros, tying the energy body to the second noble truth, relationship, and the roots of soul.
  9. 54:39 Unusual states and soulmaking - He answers questions about asymmetrical sensations and about art, movement, and the imaginal lattice, stressing that anything can become soul through participation.
