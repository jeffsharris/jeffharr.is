Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Energy Body: Instructions (2)

Dharma Seed 46:46 2015-08-08

What if the energy body is not a side topic, but the ground that lets samadhi, emotional work, and imaginal practice all connect? The talk moves from that central claim into a practical exploration of how to meet emotions on the midline with steadiness, delicacy, and enough vitality that attention does not get overwhelmed. From there, it turns to allowing, widening awareness, and occasionally letting images arise and be worked with by their effect on the body and heart. Near the end, the speaker distinguishes directing, energizing, and tuning attention, including tuning to qualities like compassion, love, and warmth.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Energy body as foundation - Burbea explains why the energy body will be central throughout the course and how it supports samadhi, metta, emptiness, and imaginal practice.
  2. 05:06 What emotions include - He defines emotion broadly as both mind and energy body, then places emotional practice in the larger aim of cultivating the heart and letting go of unhelpful habits.
  3. 11:29 Sensitivity and handling - The talk turns to growing sensitivity to subtle and intense emotions, and to learning how to hold difficult feeling without being overwhelmed.
  4. 15:17 Midline attention practice - Burbea introduces the second practice: placing steady, lightly held attention on emotional energy along the midline while keeping the whole body in the background.
  5. 19:57 Allowing and easing - He describes allowing as a practical shift in relationship to sensation, including how deep allowing can soften or dissolve difficulty, and how to check self-judgment.
  6. 25:41 Opening to images - The talk links emotional practice to imaginal practice, including the role of spontaneous images and deliberate invocation of loving figures.
  7. 31:21 Three aspects of attention - Burbea distinguishes directing, energizing, and tuning attention, showing how these capacities work in samadhi, emotion work, and imaginal practice.
  8. 42:33 Two practices, fluidly - He closes by restating the two principal practices, how to shift between them, and how images can be noticed for their effects on the energy body.
