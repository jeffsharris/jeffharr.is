Opening Talk for Metta and Emptiness (Level 1): Being on Retreat

Dharma Seed 1:34:04 2011-02-05

Rob Burbea opens a retreat on metta and emptiness by framing practice as a way of seeing existence differently, not as a project of becoming someone special. He moves from retreat logistics into a careful exploration of support, silence, community, and the subtle ways practice can create splits if kindness and emptiness are not fully inclusive. The talk ends in a call-and-response inquiry into the words emptiness, metta, kindness, and love, followed by a short settling meditation that treats receptivity itself as a form of kindness.

Chapters

  1. 00:24 Opening Welcome - Rob greets the group, names Catherine as co-teacher, and introduces the retreat with a light, grounded opening.
  2. 01:53 Freedom Through Seeing - He frames retreat practice as the possibility of freedom through seeing and sensing existence differently.
  3. 04:51 Two Wings Of Practice - He describes cultivation and investigation as the two wings of meditation, with metta and insight supporting one another.
  4. 05:57 Emptiness And Inclusion - The talk turns to emptiness as new ways of seeing and the importance of opening the whole being, not just one part of practice.
  5. 11:29 Retreat As Holding - Rob reflects on retreat rules, silence, and simplification as forms of support, while warning that every gift also has a shadow.
  6. 17:20 No Splitting - A longer exchange develops the theme that emptiness and metta both mean including everything, including the parts we usually reject.
  7. 25:01 Community And Form - The group is invited to see one another as a shared human field, while the retreat forms, silence, and structure are held lightly.
  8. 38:48 Practical Grounding - Rob and Catherine work through the schedule, room setup, and movement practice, preparing the room and bodies for inquiry.
  9. 54:46 Inquiry And Sitting - The evening turns into a guided inquiry where participants respond to emptiness and metta in their own words, then settle into a spacious body-based meditation.
