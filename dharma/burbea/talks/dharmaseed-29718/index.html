Jack Kornfield, Ruth King, Tara Brach, Thanissara: Mindfulness and Climate Action : 1

Dharma Seed 1:13:52 2014-10-05

How do you stay open to the scale of climate grief without collapsing into fear, guilt, or numbness? This conversation begins with a short grounding practice and then moves into a clear-eyed look at the climate crisis as both ecological damage and a symptom of separation, with references to the People’s Climate March, Joanna Macy, Rumi, and a few stories that point back to presence. From there the talk widens into questions of systemic injustice, collective action, and how practice can support sustained response rather than righteous burnout.

  5. 32:19 Dukkha and bodhisattva path - Jack Kornfield grounds the talk in the Buddha’s first noble truth and then shifts into bodhisattva vows and expansive responsibility.
  6. 42:46 Act from love - Jack draws on Merton, the Bhagavad Gita, Gary Snyder, and Maha Gosananda to stress non-attachment, love, and step-by-step compassion.
  7. 46:28 Questions on practice - The teachers respond to questions about anger, activism, and how inner practice and outer action can support each other.
  8. 56:51 Organizing together - Participants ask about systemic politics, good news, and practical networks, and the teachers name concrete movements and shared responsibility.
  9. 01:05:55 Closing blessings - The talk ends with a short blessing for Earth, a reminder to keep gathering, and an invitation to continue the series and the work.
