Emptiness and the Vastness of Awareness

Dharma Seed 1:35:22 2010-01-31

What if the solid world, the self, and even awareness itself are all just ways of looking that can be loosened? The talk moves from the retreat’s basic instruction—less suffering, less clinging—into a careful review of emptiness of self and then emptiness of phenomena, using the morning meditation as a practical base. It then widens into spacious awareness, the way experience can seem to arise and dissolve within it, before turning to Chittamatra or mind-only as a provisional step rather than a final truth. By the end, the emphasis is less on holding a view than on using it skillfully, trusting experience while refining understanding.

Chapters

  1. 01:14 Retreat context - He situates the talk as part of an ongoing retreat and frames the whole discussion in terms of reducing dukkha.
  2. 09:45 Dependence and solidity - He argues that what feels substantial is dependently arisen and that there are many entry points into insight.
  3. 12:14 Three characteristics - He reviews how seeing impermanence, unsatisfactoriness, and not-self loosens clinging and supports equanimity.
  4. 15:58 Fabrication and space - He revisits the morning practice, explains sankhara as fabrication, and introduces the sense of global awareness or open space.
  5. 22:55 Awareness as backdrop - He describes awareness as a holding space that seems unperturbed by experience, then names other traditions and flavors that people associate with it.
  6. 34:18 Flavors and conditioning - He qualifies the language of true nature, grounding different meditative flavors in dependent origination and experience.
  7. 44:55 Mind-only lens - He introduces Chittamatra and Yogacara as a provisional way of seeing experience as mind-only, using dream imagery and careful caveats.
  8. 59:28 Not the final truth - He warns against stopping at a beautiful awareness state, bringing in Nagarjuna and other texts to stress that the view remains provisional.
  9. 01:09:31 Questions and application - He answers questions about samadhi, practice pitfalls, and how to bring emptiness reflections into ordinary retreat life and daily frustration.
