Appropriate Response at the Tipping Point: Concern for the Planet as Dharma Practice

Dharma Seed 46:36 2012-06-14

Climate collapse, in this talk, is not treated as an abstract issue but as a moment that tests practice: do we fall into apathy, panic, or a steadier kind of response? The speaker begins with a front-page warning about a tipping point in global warming, then moves through his own swings between urgency and overwhelm before turning to concrete examples from the Dharma world, including questions about travel, sustainability, and institutional responsibility. He draws on Bhikkhu Bodhi’s warning about greed, arrogance, and ignorance, and on Joanna Macy’s sense of deep time, before leading a short guided exercise that asks listeners to imagine speaking from 75 years in the future.

Chapters

  1. 00:29 The climate warning - James opens with a front-page climate article and the recurring feeling that the planet is nearing a tipping point.
  2. 04:21 From outrage to overwhelm - He speaks candidly about his own shift from urgency and crusade to discouragement, and the mixed responses climate news can trigger.
  3. 08:42 Systems change and Dharma - A book by Bob Doppelt helps him frame ecological response as personal, organizational, and social change rooted in Dharma principles.
  4. 10:18 Institutions and responsibility - He tells a story about Dharma teachers weighing the carbon cost of travel and asking what sustainability requires of their communities.
  5. 13:32 Bhikkhu Bodhi at the cliff edge - James summarizes Bhikkhu Bodhi’s analysis of greed, arrogance, and ignorance, along with the need for immediate, long-term, and systemic action.
  6. 19:29 Joanna Macy and deep time - He turns to Joanna Macy’s reflections on hurry, consumption, and the industrial growth society, emphasizing a longer view of time.
  7. 25:24 Seventh-generation ethics - The talk widens into intergenerational responsibility, with the Iroquois seventh-generation ethic and Macy’s language of future beings.
  8. 27:20 Future self practice - James leads a guided contemplation from the standpoint of a being 75 years in the future, then invites short triad sharing and a quiet close.
