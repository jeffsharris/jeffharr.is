Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Meaning of Refuge

Dharma Seed 55:25 2008-12-28

What does it really mean to take refuge when money, relationships, comfort, and even our plans keep changing? The talk begins by stripping refuge away from ritual definition and asking what we actually rely on in life, then turns toward fear, impermanence, and the limits of worldly supports. From there it builds a case for refuge as practice: right view, the full eightfold path, the possibility of freedom in each moment, and the support of Sangha and admirable friends. The movement is quiet but exacting, ending with refuge as a way of taking responsible, courageous risks in the Dharma rather than hedging every bet.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 What refuge means - Burbea introduces the theme of refuge and shifts from ritual language to the deeper question of what refuge might mean in life and practice.
  2. 03:23 Ordinary refuges fail - He contrasts worldly refuges like money, home, career, relationships, food, sleep, and fantasy with the more reliable refuge the Buddha points to.
  3. 09:36 Fear and impermanence - The talk turns to fear as part of the path, with reflections on aging, sickness, death, loss, and the need to keep that awareness alive.
  4. 20:24 Awakening and practice - Burbea explains refuge in the Buddha as confidence in awakening, the possibility of developing those qualities ourselves, and the refusal of magical rescue.
  5. 24:12 The whole path matters - He expands refuge into the eightfold path, warning against over-relying on mindfulness alone and stressing the need to notice when practice is actually helpful.
  6. 30:54 Right view as refuge - Right view becomes central: seeing what leads to suffering, what eases it, and how our views shape choices, attention, and the whole direction of life.
  7. 38:34 Freedom is possible - He shows how suffering is added by the mind and heart, and how insight reveals that freedom is possible even in difficult moments.
  8. 44:17 Sangha and admirable friends - Burbea opens up the meaning of Sangha as realized beings, monastics, and the practicing community, emphasizing friendship with admirable people.
  9. 48:34 Courage to take risks - The talk closes by connecting refuge with direction, support, and the courage to take risks in generosity, mindfulness, love, and commitment to practice.
