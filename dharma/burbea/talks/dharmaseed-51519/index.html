Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Voice, Movement, and the Possibilities of Soul (1)

Dharma Seed 1:10:20 2018-06-24

What happens when movement, voice, and gesture are treated not as extras, but as ways the imaginal and the energy body can come alive? The talk begins by reframing sitting and walking practice around subtle and not-so-subtle movement, then moves into a series of guided experiments: loosening the body, a drunken walk, a fierce warrior expression, a delicate head tilt, a small smile, hissing, and finally paired exercises of witnessing and being witnessed. Across it all, the emphasis stays on sensitivity rather than performance, and on noticing how body, attention, and relationship condition one another.

Chapters

  1. 00:14 Opening the exploration - Burbea introduces the topic of movement, mudra, and the idea that image, body, and energy body can all be part of practice.
  2. 04:28 Why move this way - He explains the intention: to sense everyday movement, gesture, and voice as soul, and to open more channels for soul expression.
  3. 09:03 Finding your edge - The talk slows into a careful discussion of different levels of practice, from basic mindfulness to energy body and social self-consciousness.
  4. 13:34 First movement games - The group begins with loosening exercises, a drunken walk, and a warrior-like expression that brings in body, voice, throat, and eyes.
  5. 24:28 From gross to subtle - Burbea shifts back from the strong form to subtler sensing, showing how body, energy, and imaginal feeling can all be noticed at once.
  6. 31:11 Small smile and hissing - He demonstrates a very small smile and then a hiss, using tiny movements and vocalization to explore ripples in the whole being.
  7. 41:42 Pair witnessing - The practice turns to two-person work, where one person moves and the other witnesses, with attention kept on relational effects and comfort.
  8. 53:29 Balance and metta - The final section develops the balance of attention, includes a period of mutual metta, and closes with mover-and-witnesser rounds and integration.
