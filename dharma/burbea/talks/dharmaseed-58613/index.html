Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

On Practising with Images (Q & A)

Dharma Seed 1:10:33 2019-03-26

What happens when an image starts to include the self, and the self-sense itself begins to feel imaginal? The talk moves through practical questions about how to work with images that are vivid, flat, shifting, or surprisingly erotic, and keeps returning to the need to attune to what feels soulmaking rather than getting lost in story or trying to force a result. It also opens out into how art, beauty, grief, and relationship can all carry this imaginal quality, changing the way one sees oneself and others.

Chapters

  1. 00:57 A self seen as image - A participant describes an image of meeting another version of herself, and Rob begins to unpack what it means when the self becomes part of the imaginal field.
  2. 02:58 How image enters practice - He explains several ways imaginal quality can arise indirectly or directly, including how attention, resonance, and the self-sense can be drawn into the image.
  3. 06:27 Divinity, beauty, and culture - Rob expands on the sense of being image or angel, and reflects on how culture limits the way people understand self-worth, beauty, and divinity.
  4. 12:01 Repeated encounters matter - He emphasizes that this shift often needs repetition, post-meditative carryover, and patient exposure rather than a single striking experience.
  5. 18:33 Trusting the process - Rob responds to questions about bird-like selves and other imaginal forms, stressing trust, experimentation, and the fact that practice evolves through use.
  6. 24:12 Art, beauty, and violence - The conversation turns to beauty in art, including references to the Seven Stories and Rilke, and to the way art can open care, action, grief, and a wider field of feeling.
  7. 30:04 Boundaries and texture - He addresses boundary, dissolution, and the balance between vivid detail and what is soulmaking, trying to distinguish mere visual richness from living resonance.
  8. 43:01 Narrative and nectar - Using an image of two men on a porch, Rob distinguishes timeless iconic images from narrative drift and returns to the importance of lingering with what feels alive.
  9. 56:08 Relationship and desire - Questions about real people, shame, erotic charge, and multiple relational tones lead him to say that deep human connection already includes an imaginal dimension.
