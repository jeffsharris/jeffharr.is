Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Between Ikon and Eidos: Image & Hermeneutics in Meditation (Part 6 - Amitabha Buddha)

Dharma Seed 1:23:39 2018-01-14

In this talk, Rob Burbea returns to the role of ideas and images in soulmaking meditation, showing how a phrase can be held poetically until it opens real practice possibilities. He then turns to Amitabha Buddha, reading the name not only as limitless light but also as limitless appearance, and using that shift to explore imaginal perception, pure land practice, and the balance between emptiness and appearance. The talk moves through tantric deity yoga, Hakuin, and Kabbalistic readings of scripture to suggest that sacred text and sacred image can be lived as present, transfiguring ways of seeing rather than fixed doctrines.

Chapters

  1. 01:57 Poetic Hearing - He begins by showing how ideas can be received as seeds that gestate in the soul and open unexpected practice possibilities.
  2. 10:38 Amitabha Reconsidered - He introduces Amitabha through etymology, shifting from limitless light toward limitless appearance and image.
  3. 16:10 Consort And Wisdom - He uses Amitabha's consort and tantric imagery to speak about passion, transmutation, and imaginal perception that retains distinction.
  4. 22:17 Deity Yoga Stances - He outlines three possible relationships to a deity: supplication, harmonizing with qualities, and becoming or fusing with the deity.
  5. 27:49 Pure Land Here Now - He re-reads Amitabha's Pure Land as an orientation toward the mundus imaginalis here and now, shaped by humility and aspiration.
  6. 36:37 Balance With Akshobhya - He explores Amitabha in relation to Akshobhya, using the contrast between imaginal perception and the immutable or unfabricated.
  7. 46:20 Scripture As Living Land - He turns to Hakuin and then to Abraham Abulafia, showing how sacred texts can be heard as timeless and transfiguring rather than merely historical.
  8. 59:19 Dharmadhatu And World - He develops Dharmadhatu across Buddhist traditions and brings in D.T. Suzuki, Henri Corbin, and William Blake to link soul-state and perception.
  9. 01:12:49 Yearning Opens Gates - He closes with gematria, the phrase 'the one who yearns,' and the claim that devotion, longing, and poetic interpretation can open the Garden of Eden and the pure land in lived experience.
