Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Opening to Desire and the Imaginal (Q & A)

Dharma Seed 40:24 2017-03-29

How do you work with desire when it seems to open into something spacious, then tighten around an image, or disappear altogether? The talk moves through a series of questions about the energy body, imaginal practice, depression, fear of being flooded by images, and the way eros can show up either as strong feeling or as something very subtle. The speaker keeps returning to a practical thread: notice the fork in the road, learn when to stay with an image, when to rest in the body, and when to loosen back into a wider field. By the end, the emphasis is less on fixing a problem than on sensing how different conditions bring the whole field back to life.

Chapters

  1. 00:31 Contraction Around Image - A question about spacious energy-body practice leads into the problem of contraction when images arise.
  2. 01:57 Holding Both Experiences - Rob links the difficulty to Catherine McGee’s two-ness exercise and suggests learning to hold spaciousness and contraction together.
  3. 04:11 Images and Soulmaking - He explains that images can become alive imaginally and that the apparent problem may not lie where it first seems.
  4. 06:08 Desire, Depression, Reification - The talk turns to lack of desire, inner critic, depression, and how blocked or reified experience can interrupt eros and imaginal unfolding.
  5. 13:02 Fear Of Flooding - A question about opening awareness widely brings discussion of whether receptivity risks being overwhelmed by painful images.
  6. 17:27 Pain and Compassion - Rob distinguishes the imaginal realm from the realities of world pain and says the wider work requires emotional skill and compassion practice.
  7. 20:12 Opening To Desire - He returns to the day’s instruction, describing how opening to desire can lead into an imaginal realm of happiness rather than a single image.
  8. 27:53 Finding The Deeper Wanting - A long exchange about bodily pain and perfectionism explores how desire may point to ease, meaning, or a more beautiful aspect of a pattern.
  9. 33:59 Eros, Conditions, Subtlety - Rob closes by relating eros to the imaginal, noting that practice forks under different conditions and that mature practice becomes more subtle and sensitive.
