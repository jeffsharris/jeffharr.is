Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

A Hidden Treasure: The Relationship with the Hindrances

Dharma Seed 62:51 2019-12-19

How do the hindrances become part of jhana practice rather than a sign that practice has gone wrong? The talk moves from a brief correction about metta and energy-body awareness into a detailed, practical exploration of sloth, restlessness, desire, aversion, and doubt, with many concrete antidotes: widening awareness, using the breath differently, shifting to metta, and working with effort levels. It then opens into a subtler frame, where even deep concentration can still contain faint hindrances, and where patience, flexibility, and not taking anything personally become central. In the end, the talk turns from technique to insight, asking listeners to see hindrances as ordinary, workable, and sometimes even as a kind of treasure.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Framing the Talk - Rob sets up the retreat context, clarifies earlier points about metta and jhana, and says he wants to add sensitivity and attunement to the usual reasons for practice.
  2. 02:49 Hindrances and Jhana - He introduces the hindrances as central to jhana, citing sutta material to show they matter before and within deep concentration, not only in beginner practice.
  3. 05:35 Antidotes, Not Indifference - Rob distinguishes jhana practice from a purely observing stance and explains why practitioners should look for what feeds and what weakens a hindrance.
  4. 07:52 Sleepiness and Restlessness - He offers practical ways to work with sloth, torpor, restlessness, and worry through breath, body awareness, widened attention, and different degrees of energetic emphasis.
  5. 17:04 Sense Desire and Ill Will - Rob turns to sense desire, including sexual desire, then to ill will and aversion, showing how energy, metta, and self-compassion can transform these states.
  6. 22:17 Noise, Doubt, and Questions - He addresses irritation at external noises, the difference between doubt and genuine inquiry, and the usefulness of postponing some questions until outside formal practice.
  7. 27:39 Exchanging Self and Other - Rob introduces the practice of taking in suffering and offering happiness, framing it as a heart-opening way to meet hindrance and pain.
  8. 30:01 Subtle Hindrances - He returns to the spectrum of hindrances, describing subtle versions such as sinking and drifting, the role of effort, and how to stay with what is actually available.
  9. 42:59 Patience and Insight - Rob stresses patience, creativity, and not taking hindrances personally, then reframes them as gold dust that can be turned into treasure across meditation and life.
