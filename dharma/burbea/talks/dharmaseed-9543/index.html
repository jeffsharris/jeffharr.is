Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Three Characteristics

Dharma Seed 55:14 2010-01-23

Rob Burbea leads a guided meditation on the three characteristics, beginning with settling the body and moving through impermanence, reactivity, and not-self. The practice shifts from narrow attention on sensations to a wider field that includes the body, sounds, and the visual field, then into a gentler way of letting experience be. In the closing review, he emphasizes balancing samadhi and insight and warns against turning practice into a search for special experiences.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling In - He begins by collecting attention in the body and calming the mind enough to start the contemplations.
  2. 04:43 Impermanence in the Body - Attention is directed to the hands and face, noticing changing sensations and the fact of fluctuation itself.
  3. 10:09 Wider Field of Change - The contemplation expands to other body areas and then to the whole body, with a strong emphasis on flickering, pulsing impermanence.
  4. 20:24 Reactivity and Dukkha - He introduces pleasant, unpleasant, and neutral feeling tones, and asks listeners to notice aversion and craving as bodily contraction.
  5. 27:32 Letting Be - The emphasis shifts from careful noticing to allowing experience to be as it is, rather than forcing release.
  6. 29:40 Not-Self Practice - He guides a third contemplation in which sensations, sounds, thoughts, and even awareness are seen as just happening, not me or mine.
  7. 39:00 Seeing Without Clinging - The hand-and-eyes exercise is repeated to explore the subtle felt difference between identification and a lighter, more spacious way of seeing.
  8. 47:17 Balancing the Practice - He reviews the three characteristics work, encourages a flexible balance with samadhi, and cautions against hunting for experiences.
  9. 53:16 Two Ways of Not-Self - In response to a question, he contrasts self-inquiry with a more direct seeing that everything is simply happening, including awareness itself.
