Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Movement of Devotion (Part 2)

Dharma Seed 40:09 2016-07-29

In this talk, Rob Burbea explores prayer as more than supplication: as praise, blessing, devotion, surrender, and a way of sensing the world through the imaginal. He then turns to the felt relationship between body, energy body, and consciousness, offering careful experiments with posture, mudra, and subtle movement. The talk closes by asking when outer enactment helps and when a more inward devotion is the more alive practice.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Prayer beyond asking - Rob distinguishes supplication from a more open, humble form of prayer that does not tighten the self.
  2. 02:40 Praise and blessing - He broadens prayer into praise and blessing, describing how these qualities can become hard to put into words and remain rooted in the imaginal.
  3. 07:58 Widening perception - The talk traces how blessing can move from one object to body, world, and knowing itself, through the senses and a cosmopoetic way of seeing.
  4. 15:19 Flavors of devotion - He turns to the different intensities and mixtures of prayerfulness, devotion, love, compassion, and metta, emphasizing discernment over hierarchy.
  5. 18:54 Body and samadhi - Rob outlines the interrelationship of body, energy body, and psyche, including the role of pīti and how well-being can affect posture and feeling.
  6. 24:48 Subtle movement and mudra - He invites experimentation with uprightness, chin angle, hand posture, and spontaneous inner or outer movement as ways to tune practice.
  7. 30:29 Touching the earth - Using the Buddha-touching-earth image, he shows how mudra and posture can shift emotion, energy, attitude, and attention.
  8. 36:49 Inner or outer devotion - The closing section weighs physical enactment against inward bowing and surrender, asking which mode is more soulmaking in the moment.
