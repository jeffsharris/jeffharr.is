Guided Meditation - The Play of Appearances

Dharma Seed 37:22 2011-02-22

This guided meditation begins with settling the posture and letting awareness pervade the body, then moves into a gentle practice of receiving whatever arises. From there, the instruction broadens toward welcoming thoughts, emotions, and self-images as appearances in space, without making them into a problem. The meditation closes by emphasizing release, the felt peace of letting go, and a quiet sense that all things belong to awareness.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling the seat - The meditation opens by finding the seat, establishing posture, and letting awareness spread through the body and mind.
  2. 02:15 Receiving and softening - Instruction turns toward a tender, receptive awareness that holds experience with softness, warmth, and ease.
  3. 06:18 Appearances in space - Reactivity is reframed as part of the play of appearances, and the practice widens to include everything that arises.
  4. 11:03 Welcoming selfing - Thoughts, images, and definitions of self are allowed to appear without being taken as solid or problematic.
  5. 15:54 Bowing to experience - The talk deepens into allowing and bowing to the mysterious display of empty appearances, with tenderness and love.
  6. 20:27 Letting the heart release - Repeated invitations to let go point toward a simple release in which sensations, thoughts, and emotions can come and go.
  7. 26:24 Floating in space - Experience is described as insubstantial and belonging to space, with sensations and sounds held in silence.
  8. 32:47 Peace in all things - The meditation ends with sustained letting go and the repeated recognition that the ultimate nature of all things is peace.
