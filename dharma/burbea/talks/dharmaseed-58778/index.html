Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Sila and Soul (Part 5)

Dharma Seed 2:06:14 2019-06-13

Rob Burbea explores how moral values can become part of soulmaking rather than remaining abstract rules. He moves from climate action and protest, through crisis, conflict, guilt, and remorse, into a more imaginal sense of values as living realities that can grip the heart and shape action. The talk closes by sketching several meditation approaches for working directly with values, ideals, and the sense of participating in something larger than the personal self.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Values Become Alive - Rob opens by describing how moral values can be stirred by eros and then take on depth, dimensionality, and meaningfulness within soulmaking.
  2. 05:12 Climate Action and Protest - He turns to climate change, religiously framed struggle, and the etymology of protest as bearing witness for something rather than merely against something.
  3. 10:55 Crisis as Opportunity - Crisis, whether personal or collective, is presented as a doorway into courage, nobility, and the active realization of values, with Bonhoeffer and Frankl as examples.
  4. 16:31 Absence and Conflict - Using Hartmann, the talk argues that the absence of values and the presence of conflict can themselves matter because they make moral effort, discernment, and growth possible.
  5. 25:39 Plato and Divine Goodness - Rob contrasts world-escaping and world-participating strands in Plato, using them to clarify different ways virtue can relate to transcendence and divine goodness.
  6. 34:00 Virtue as Radiance - The talk deepens into Hartmann's account of moral values as things that grip us, and into examples of justice-centered lives that seem to carry a larger radiance or holiness.
  7. 46:03 Mundus Imaginalis - Rob brings in Corbin and Qadi Saeed Qumi to frame a three-tier cosmos, using the imaginal and intelligible realms to describe how values can be sensed as more than abstractions.
  8. 01:11:48 Remorse and Sensitivity - He then explores guilt, remorse, inner critic, and emotional training, arguing that a wider sensitivity to values can strengthen ethical action and rootedness.
  9. 01:35:37 Meditating on Ideals - The closing section moves toward practice: meditating on images, on imaginal figures, or directly on ideals themselves until values can be felt as a lived sphere of being.
