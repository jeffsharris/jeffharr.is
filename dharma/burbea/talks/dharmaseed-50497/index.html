Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Sensing with Soul (Part 5)

Dharma Seed 1:13:40 2017-12-25

How do you tell the difference between a busy mind and an imaginal opening? The talk keeps returning to that question through concrete examples: a creative project softened by another person's perception, a painful memory reframed in a shared field, and ordinary tenderness turning into an iconic sense of beauty and blessing. From there it widens into the claim that sensing with soul is not a technique to control, but a way of noticing what we value, what we receive from others, and how the energy body, humility, and attention help a life situation become imaginal rather than merely problematic.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the review - Rob begins by reviewing the overall intention of the talk and encourages experimentation, careful noticing, and patience rather than pressure to get it right.
  2. 03:41 Conditions for sensing - He names the main supports for sensing with soul, including humility, heart-softening, reduced fabrication, sensitivity, energy-body awareness, and different entry points into imaginal practice.
  3. 09:49 Grace and receptivity - The review turns to the limits of technique, the role of grace, and the idea that imaginal knowing often involves receiving something from beyond oneself.
  4. 14:51 A creative project opens - Rob tells a personal story about how Catherine’s imaginal perception helped loosen his anxiety and reopen a creative project in a more imaginal way.
  5. 20:19 Pain re-seen - He gives an example from retreat where a painful memory is reframed through an imaginal image, showing how shame can shift without erasing the original suffering.
  6. 25:02 Nakedness and blessing - A shared sense of tenderness with a friend becomes iconic and luminous, and Rob describes how attention, blessing, and touch can participate in the imaginal field.
  7. 36:34 What people transmit - Using Catherine’s niece and grandmother, he reflects on how people carry and communicate a particular refraction of the sacred, not just comfort or advice.
  8. 46:13 When fantasy becomes imaginal - Rob reframes a woman’s mixed feelings about art, literature, and professional pressure as a nexus of meaningful fantasies that can be worked with meditatively instead of dismissed as mere papanca.
  9. 01:04:46 Values and next steps - He closes by linking imaginal perception to values, suggesting that what we deeply value is always involved, and noting several themes that could be explored further in future talks.
