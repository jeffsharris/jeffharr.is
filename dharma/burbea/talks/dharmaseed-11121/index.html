Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Emptiness

Dharma Seed 60:52 2009-01-24

Rob Burbea offers an introduction to emptiness that is careful to separate it from blankness, depression, or a mere meditative state. Using the Heart Sutra, everyday examples, and repeated returns to dependent arising, he shows how the mind imputes solidity to selves, relationships, nations, time, and even perception itself. The talk keeps pointing back to practice: seeing things as empty is meant to reduce suffering, loosen fixation, and deepen compassion rather than become a philosophical position.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 What emptiness is not - He begins by clearing away the idea that emptiness means depression, flatness, or a blank state of mind.
  2. 03:00 Heart Sutra frame - The Heart Sutra and Prajnaparamita context are introduced to show that emptiness means phenomena lack inherent existence.
  3. 04:38 Everyday dependence - He shows how emptiness appears in ordinary life through grief, anger, culture, and the way relationships and identities get made solid.
  4. 14:07 Retreat and conditions - The talk explores how retreat practice, general anesthesia, countries, and social conventions reveal that things function conventionally without existing independently.
  5. 27:47 Mind imputes objects - Using the capital letter A and the Big Dipper, he explains how the mind constructs things from parts, context, and perception.
  6. 37:51 Nothing is exempt - He widens the lens to say that all phenomena, including time, awareness, space, atoms, and subatomic particles, are empty.
  7. 48:05 Practice over theory - Citing the Samadhi Raja Sutra and Tsongkhapa, he distinguishes passive blankness from the active contemplation that trains the mind in selflessness.
  8. 55:56 Compassion and liberation - He closes by drawing on Chandrakirti and Nagarjuna to show that emptiness deepens compassion and avoids both reification and nihilism.
