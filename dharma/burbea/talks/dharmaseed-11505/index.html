Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Psychodynamics in Meditation (Part 2)

Dharma Seed 59:27 2010-11-20

How do emotions stay stuck, and what lets them move again? The talk returns to a central idea: emotional life has an innate dynamism that can be blocked, and meditation can help unfreeze it by differentiating what is actually present, sustaining attention, widening the field, questioning assumptions, and resourcing difficulty with qualities like compassion, confidence, and generosity. From there it moves into detailed examples of self-doubt, yearning and despair, inner images, and personal story, asking how practice can include more than bare sensation without getting trapped in judgment or a fixed self-view.

Chapters

  1. 00:17 Reviewing the framework - Burbea revisits the premise that emotional life has an inherent liberating dynamism, then quickly reviews the earlier principles for unblocking it.
  2. 04:12 Differentiation and attention - He explains differentiation, sustaining attention, and broadening mindfulness so the emotional pattern can be seen with more precision and less overwhelm.
  3. 06:47 Questioning assumptions - The talk turns to inquiry, especially noticing self-views and assumptions that quietly organize difficult emotions into a tighter loop.
  4. 10:23 Why inquiry needs love - Burbea shows how questioning can become judgment if it lacks care, and how true inquiry needs courage, softness, and an honest willingness to look deeper.
  5. 17:22 Cultivating qualities - He argues that the path includes actively developing wholesome qualities like metta, compassion, and generosity rather than only staying with whatever is present.
  6. 20:18 Finding inner resources - A detailed case study shows self-doubt loosening as awareness widens, pain is felt directly, and compassion, confidence, peace, and strength emerge.
  7. 28:01 Yearning and hidden strength - Another example explores imprisonment, yearning, and despair, and then looks for the often-missed strength and excitement hidden inside the longing.
  8. 36:55 Working with imagery - Burbea describes a meditation image that reveals a split-off part of the psyche and suggests dialoguing with images in a way similar to active imagination.
  9. 47:31 Using story and seeing anew - He argues that personal story can be a resource for healing when held carefully, and that with enough inner fullness the same story can be seen from a freer, more loving place.
