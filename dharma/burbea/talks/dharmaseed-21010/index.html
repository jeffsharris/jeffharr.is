Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Mindfulness of Mind States

Dharma Seed 47:39 2005-11-05

What happens when a mind state starts to feel like the whole of reality? The talk moves through the third foundation of mindfulness by showing how emotions, moods, thoughts, body sensations, perception, and self-belief all shape experience, and how mindfulness can notice these pieces without collapsing them into “me.” It then shifts from simple observation to practical work: accommodating difficult states with steadiness, cultivating wholesome ones with right effort, and using spaciousness and kindness to see that mind states are changing, not fixed. The result is a quiet, detailed guide to meeting whatever is present without adding unnecessary suffering on top.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 From body to mind - Burbea situates the talk in the retreat context and introduces mindfulness of mind states as a step beyond body and feeling tone.
  2. 04:07 What mind states include - He defines mind states broadly, including emotion, mood, energy, brightness, spaciousness, and the body’s expressions of them.
  3. 10:11 Reading the whole constellation - The talk moves through thoughts, feeling tone, climate, and the way mind states color perception and sense contact.
  4. 15:00 Self-belief and view - Burbea warns against turning mind states into identity and discusses how assumptions about practice can intensify suffering.
  5. 20:09 Accommodating difficulty - He describes enlarging the container for grief, fear, and anger, with kindness, patience, courage, and confidence.
  6. 24:49 When to rest and return - The emphasis shifts to knowing when to work, when to step back, and how calm or metta change the usual approach.
  7. 30:18 Cultivating the wholesome - Burbea links the teaching to right effort, naming beautiful mind states and practical ways to support them.
  8. 35:50 Insight into change - He turns to impermanence, the supports behind mind states, and the effects of attention, spaciousness, and kindness.
  9. 43:25 Letting the talk settle - The final section returns to kindness as a central practice and invites listeners to take only what feels alive and useful.
