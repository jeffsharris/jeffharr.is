Preliminaries, Regarding Voice, Movement, and Gesture (Part 4)

Dharma Seed 1:59:41 2020-03-04

How do movement, gesture, and voice come alive when they are not copied from an idea, but guided from within by intention, felt soulfulness, and immediate feedback? The talk returns to exercise four, showing how blessing can be explored either through familiar forms or by letting the body, energy body, and sound lead into something more open, subtle, and less tied to meaning. It then widens into exercise five, using iconic moments such as Christ on the Via Dolorosa, the Buddha touching the earth, Rosa Parks refusing to move, and Abraham’s hospitality to the angels to show how an image can be embodied without becoming mere acting.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Blessing as lived intention - Rob returns to exercise four and invites listeners to begin from the intention to bless, rather than from a pre-planned idea of what blessing should look like.
  2. 12:44 Voice beyond meaning - He turns to mantra and the sound of speech itself, showing how vocalization can carry meaning while also opening a dimension of mystery beyond meaning.
  3. 20:23 Relational sensitivity - The talk centers on the witness role, subtle effects in the energy body, and practical responses to self-consciousness, fear of judgment, and blockage.
  4. 39:03 Imaginal dyad variations - Rob introduces the exercise with an actual partner or an imaginal figure, and explains why each form may support different kinds of subtlety and openness.
  5. 56:21 Christ and the earth - He uses Christ on the way to execution and the Buddha's earth-touching gesture as iconic moments that can be sensed from within rather than merely acted out.
  6. 01:11:22 Abraham's hospitality - The focus shifts to the soul-making poise of waiting, receptivity, and hospitality to angels before an image fully appears.
  7. 01:25:26 Rosa Parks' immovability - Rosa Parks becomes the central example of bodily courage, dignity, and ethical clarity, with Emmett Till and the bus boycott deepening the image.
  8. 01:47:56 Tantric language and Buddha nature - Rob briefly turns to a Sanskrit tantric formula and the Buddha's flexible self-designation as Tathagata to show how language can remain layered and alive.
  9. 01:56:08 Preliminaries that deepen - He closes by reflecting on preliminaries in Tibetan practice and on how foundational exercises can expand into something far from merely basic.
