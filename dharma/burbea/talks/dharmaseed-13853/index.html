Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Working with the Emotional Body (Instructions and Guided Meditation: Day One)

Dharma Seed 60:29 2011-07-30

Rob Burbea offers an introduction to working with emotions and mind states through the body, framing practice around the Pali term citta as heart-mind. He emphasizes not trying to get rid of feeling, but learning to meet it with interest, kindness, and enough attention that difficult states become workable rather than overwhelming. The talk moves from explanation into two guided practices, with instructions for sitting and walking meditation and a steady return to the body as the place where emotion can be known.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Heart-Mind Framing - Burbea introduces citta as heart-mind and sets out the basic questions of what we want from emotional practice.
  2. 04:33 What We Want - He names the aims of emotional work: fullness, openness, the ability to unknot difficulty, and growing confidence.
  3. 09:21 Mind States And Emotion - The talk distinguishes emotions from subtler mind states and explains why awareness of both levels matters.
  4. 15:09 Helpful Attention - He describes how attention and emotion can tip a seesaw, and why the way we attend can either help or worsen a state.
  5. 20:05 Body-Based Practice - Burbea leads a first guided sitting practice, inviting listeners to feel posture, bodily sensations, and the emotional state expressed in the body.
  6. 29:22 Allowing And Exploring - The instruction deepens into allowing, drawing close, noticing changing textures, and staying with whatever is present without demanding clarity.
  7. 40:19 Letting Go Of Demands - He addresses expectations directly, using the image of waves at the seaside to show how shifting experience can build trust.
  8. 45:14 Second Sitting - A shorter meditation repeats the central themes of ease, kindness, and checking in with the heart-state on the central line of the body.
  9. 54:51 Object And Walking Practice - The close of the talk clarifies the shift from being inside the emotion to seeing it as an object in awareness, then gives walking meditation instructions.
