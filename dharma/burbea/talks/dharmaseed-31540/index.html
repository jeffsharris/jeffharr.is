Theophany and Cosmopoesis: Instructions

Dharma Seed 1:19:37 2015-08-12

How does an image move from private meditation to a whole felt world? The talk tracks three broad avenues of practice—bare attention, emptiness practices that fabricate less, and a third set of imaginal practices that deliberately shape perception—then lingers on how a theophanic sense can be recognized, tuned to, and gently allowed to spread. Along the way it gives concrete examples: mantra heard as divine speech, silence felt as holiness, a poem becoming a way of looking, and even distraction turning into a doorway into a more sacred cosmos.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Reviewing the ground - Rob briefly recaps the role of image, emotional field, energy body, and perception in imaginal practice.
  2. 03:03 Soulfulness and holiness - He shows how resonance in image can mirror personal life, deepen soulfulness, and open toward holiness or divinity.
  3. 05:15 Cosmopoesis and theophany - He introduces cosmopoesis and theophany as ways an image can spread into the surrounding world and alter perception of reality.
  4. 09:39 Three avenues of practice - He lays out bare attention, practices that fabricate less, and skillful fabrication through imagination, tantra, and related methods.
  5. 15:33 How to steer practice - He explains how subtle views shape mindfulness, how anatta can be sustained as a way of looking, and how theophanic perception can be recognized and cultivated.
  6. 28:27 Examples from practice - He gives examples of tantric food practice, silence and holiness, erotic imagery, and the widening of sacred feeling into the whole environment.
  7. 40:55 Mantra and distraction - He turns to mantra as heard theophany and then shows how even a distracted musical thought can become a doorway into cosmic imagination.
  8. 47:15 Personhood and poetry - He explores how a loved person can appear as an angelic theophany and how a Hafiz line can become a living way of looking.
  9. 53:32 Breath, birdsong, and completion - He closes with breath as divine breath, birdsong as mantra, and a final summary of the steps of openness, recognition, tuning, assent, and letting the sense spread.
