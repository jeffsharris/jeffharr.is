Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Going Forwards

Dharma Seed 49:46 2020-01-08

Rob Burbea offers a grounded closing talk on how to leave retreat well: appreciating effort without measuring success, caring for the sensitive heart, and carrying practice into ordinary life. He frames practice as a set of streams and playgrounds—jhana, insight, Brahmavihara, emptiness, soulmaking, generosity, right speech—best approached with steady intention, playfulness, and clear curiosity about what one actually desires. The talk ends with a long dedication to the welfare of others and a reminder that practice ripples outward in ways we cannot fully know.

Chapters

  1. 00:30 Appreciation for effort - The talk begins by inviting appreciation for what people have invested in practice, apart from any judgment about results.
  2. 02:57 Caring for the heart - He turns to the transition off retreat, especially the need to care for the heart, relationships, and the way one shares practice with others.
  3. 07:24 Steady intention, playful practice - Rob suggests working in a delimited period with one practice, keeping intention steady while allowing experimentation and small creative variations.
  4. 10:02 Practice streams and mandala - He broadens the picture to different streams of Dharma—insight, samadhi, Brahmavihara, psychology, ethics, activism—and describes them as a coherent mandala.
  5. 13:36 Balancing the streams - The talk explores how one practice can support another, including balancing insight with samadhi or Brahmavihara and using groups or themed practice periods.
  6. 17:54 Withdrawal from entanglement - Using the Buddha’s language, he clarifies that jhana depends less on external rules than on not being entangled in hindrances, and he extends this to sex, orgasm, and diet.
  7. 21:00 Tools and the bookmark - He reminds listeners to keep experimenting with available tools, then uses the image of a bookmark in a book to show how a practiced capacity can remain available over time.
  8. 23:33 Desire, frameworks, and groups - Rob emphasizes that conceptual frameworks matter, then connects that point to desire, intention, and the different ways practices and groups can succeed or stall.
  9. 29:22 Group intentions clarified - He contrasts the needs of soulmaking, sangha, and other group forms, showing how unclear desire can distort a group’s purpose and outcome.
