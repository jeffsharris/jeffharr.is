True to Your Deepest Desires (Talk and Short Guided Meditation)

Dharma Seed 1:12:58 2019-12-26

How do you keep practicing when desire turns into comparison, self-judgment, and a sense of failure? The talk moves from the practical difficulties of questions on retreat to a larger inquiry into how the self gets built around practice, and how that can narrow or distort the path. From there, it turns toward the Four Bases of Power, the role of desire, and the possibility of relating to frustration without abandoning the longing that brought you here. A short guided meditation closes by inviting a listener to sense the beauty of that desire, the patience behind showing up, and the willingness itself as something to cherish.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the Question - He sets up the retreat context and the practical problem of questions, comparison, and psychological strain in a mixed group.
  2. 04:54 Culture and Self-View - He widens the lens to cultural conditioning, the modern sense of self, and why practice can feel so charged now.
  3. 08:57 You Can Do This - He reminds listeners that practice is possible, that dips do not mean failure, and that responsiveness matters more than panic.
  4. 15:13 View of Practice - He emphasizes how the self is constructed around practice, the need for a larger view, and the value of showing up without overpressure.
  5. 27:27 Radical Intention - He introduces exchanging self and other and then turns to the Four Bases of Power, reframing desire as part of liberation rather than a problem to hide.
  6. 32:13 Desire and Frustration - He explores desire in detail, arguing that what we learn about wanting, goals, and frustration may matter more than any particular jhāna result.
  7. 39:53 Sorting the Signals - He unpacks motives behind retreat practice and calls for a structural sense of what teachings matter most, not just the most detailed tips.
  8. 46:18 A Life Shaped by Wanting - Using his own life as a musician, he shows how deep desire can coexist with pain, humility, and long apprenticeship.
  9. 58:55 Desire Through Dukkha - He distinguishes deep longing from negative self-view, then frames desire as a burning coal that must be related to skillfully.
