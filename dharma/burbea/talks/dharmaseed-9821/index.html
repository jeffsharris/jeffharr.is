Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Breath Meditation (3rd Instructions)

Dharma Seed 41:30 2009-09-11

Rob Burbea frames breath meditation as an adaptable art shaped by changing mind states, not a fixed technique. He moves through common hindrances like dullness, restlessness, and thinking, then offers practical ways to respond through posture, breath, attention, and effort. The talk closes by widening the sense of the breath into a subtler energy field and inviting a lighter, more receptive way of practice.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Breath as an Art - He introduces breath meditation as a subtle practice with many possibilities and encourages taking only what is useful.
  2. 02:50 Kindness and Orientation - He emphasizes kindness as the context for practice and reframes struggle as a place where patience and steadiness are cultivated.
  3. 04:05 Working with Hindrances - He names the five hindrances, warns against taking them personally, and compares them to seeds that grow when hooked by the mind.
  4. 07:34 Responding in Practice - He describes meditation as responsive and playful, then gives specific ways to meet sloth, torpor, and restlessness with posture, breath, and space.
  5. 10:23 Adjusting Effort - He turns to the balance of effort, comparing practice to surfing or sailing and suggesting that too much pressure can tighten the mind.
  6. 14:14 Thought and Breath - He discusses relationship to thought, the possibility of cutting a thought short, and how breath can be experienced in different ways beyond the nostrils.
  7. 20:08 Guided Practice - He gives concise instructions to settle into posture, connect with kindness, open awareness through the body, and work with the breath in a helpful way.
  8. 22:31 Settling Into Space - A quiet stretch of practice follows, with the talk giving way to long silence and only brief closing cues.
