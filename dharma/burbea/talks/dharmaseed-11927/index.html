Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

A Question of Faith

Dharma Seed 60:10 2007-11-08

What do you actually put your faith in when life feels unstable, and what happens when the things you rely on start to break? The talk begins by probing faith as something hidden in ordinary choices, then moves through suffering, doubt, and the ways practice can either narrow into fear or open into confidence. It draws on stories of a prisoner choosing compassion, a Cambodian nun’s vow, and the Buddha’s encounter with aging, sickness, and death to show faith becoming less about belief and more about the lived possibility of transformation.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening the question - Burbea introduces faith as something to question rather than assume, and frames Dharma as a place for difficult inquiry rather than comfort.
  2. 04:18 The human predicament - He reflects on existential unease, childhood questions about life and death, and how those questions remain active beneath ordinary life.
  3. 11:06 What we trust - The talk turns to the practical shape of faith, asking what our choices, money, relationships, and attention reveal about what we rely on.
  4. 17:14 Faith in care - Burbea uses Viktor Frankl, a Cambodian nun, and a Catholic grandmother to show faith as devotion, compassion, and the nourishing of wholesome intention.
  5. 22:50 Cultivating the beautiful - He argues that practice should not only dismantle the negative, but also cultivate beauty, direction, and a grounded trust in heart qualities.
  6. 29:21 When forms break - Examples from his own life show how faith can survive disappointment, loss, and instability in teachers, identities, and practice forms.
  7. 40:04 Path of questions - He returns to the Buddha’s Four Noble Truths as a practical way of asking the right questions about suffering, its causes, and release.
  8. 45:54 What is possible - Burbea reframes Dharma faith as confidence in possibility, illustrated by the Buddha’s encounter with aging, sickness, death, and the search for the deathless.
  9. 50:25 Experience-based confidence - The talk closes by returning to practice as gradual verification: as one tastes more peace and loveliness, faith can become steadier and less shaken.
