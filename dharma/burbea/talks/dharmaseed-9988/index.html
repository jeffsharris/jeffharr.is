Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

About Metta Practice

Dharma Seed 67:45 2010-08-06

How does metta move from a simple wish for well-being into a practice that can soften the heart, steady intention, and change the way the world is seen? The talk begins by placing loving-kindness within the wider Buddhist path, then distinguishes it from conditional or romantic love, stressing its boundlessness, unconditionality, and quiet strength. From there it turns to nourishment, the slow power of repeated intention, and the way practice can loosen the sense of separateness and open into a more communal, less self-enclosed view. It closes with a long guided opening of the body and awareness, extending well-wishing from oneself to the people in the room.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Welcome to Retreat - Rob welcomes everyone to Gaia House and frames the gathering as a shared commitment to developing kindness.
  2. 01:37 Awakening and Suffering - He places metta within the Buddha’s promise of awakening and the diagnosis of suffering rooted in mistaken perception.
  3. 04:42 Two Wings of the Path - Rob presents insight and cultivation as the two wings of practice and positions metta within that larger framework.
  4. 06:28 What Metta Means - He defines loving-kindness as well-wishing, then distinguishes it from ordinary love, attachment, and polite sentiment.
  5. 11:16 Boundless and Strong - The talk explores metta’s boundlessness, unconditionality, and quiet strength, while also noting why it was often overlooked in early Western insight circles.
  6. 18:37 Nourishment and Skill - Rob describes metta as a learnable skill that nourishes the being, helps address harshness, and counters spiritual undernourishment.
  7. 29:00 Cultivation and Intention - He emphasizes cultivation practices, the power of intention, and the gradual effect of repetition, using the Buddha’s bucket image.
  8. 37:35 Bodhicitta and Widening - Rob turns to bodhicitta, Rilke, and Tikkun Olam to suggest a widening from self-healing toward a more expansive, world-healing orientation.
  9. 46:03 Shared Practice - The talk closes with a communal exercise and a long guided opening practice that moves from embodied awareness to self-wishing and then to the room as a field of mutual support.
