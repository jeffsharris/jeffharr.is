Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Voice, Movement, and the Possibilities of Soul (2)

Dharma Seed 53:23 2018-06-26

This talk is an extended set of movement and imaginal practice instructions, beginning with a practical choice about whether to stay in silence or keep exploring movement. Rob then leads a sequence of experiments with stomping, warrior-like posture, vocal hiss, subtle head movement, and pelvic rotation, each time asking what is helpful and what becomes perceptible in the body, emotions, energy, and image. Later he widens the frame to include the room, the earth, the stars, and finally a more explicit imaginal practice shaped by Catherine McGee’s framing of "sensing the soul."

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Choosing the practice - Rob opens by asking each person to notice what would actually be helpful now, whether silence and stillness or more work with movement.
  2. 04:59 Rooted movement - The group begins with contact, uprightness, and loosening movement, with an emphasis on sensing the body as a field of awareness.
  3. 08:47 Stomping and awareness - Rob introduces a vigorous stomping practice, first letting movement lead and then noticing how sensation, emotion, imagery, and energy respond.
  4. 13:36 Warrior in space - A more deliberate warrior image is explored, with the body facing the wall and the practice opening to the room as part of the movement.
  5. 18:43 Reciprocal space - The instruction deepens into a sense of intimate relationship between movement and the hall, with awareness of rhythm, sensitivity, and the broader space around the body.
  6. 24:27 Wider horizons - Rob invites practitioners to widen from the room to the world, the earth, and even the cosmos, treating movement as a relationship with something larger.
  7. 29:08 Voice and subtle motion - The practice shifts to a throat hiss and then to movement in response to voice, followed by a very small head movement meant to refine awareness.
  8. 36:32 Earth and stars - The head movement is placed between the molten core of the earth and the stars, then the body is explored as part of mysterious manifestation in the cosmos.
  9. 45:42 Imaginal relationship - After clearing the room, Rob ends with a quieter seated practice that calls up an image or presence and explores whether stillness or movement gives the fuller experience in relation to it.
