The Invitation of Otherness (Autonomy, Eros, and Intentionality)

Dharma Seed 1:32:05 2019-06-01

What does it mean for an image to be other, and yet intimate, autonomous, and responsive? The talk moves from two-ness and erotic tension into the autonomy of the self and the image, then into a careful distinction between narrowing practice around getting something and opening to what the image itself wants. Along the way, it turns to desire as part of self-knowledge, and to the way a fuller intention can let eros, healing, and soul-making unfold together rather than cancel each other out.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Imaginal relationship - He begins by situating the talk in earlier teaching on relationship with images and explains that the imaginal is less a thing than a relationship.
  2. 03:25 Two-ness and otherness - He develops the role of two-ness, showing how eros depends on difference and how otherness can deepen without becoming alienation.
  3. 06:24 Autonomy of the image - He turns to autonomy, explaining that the imaginal figure can feel both mine and not mine, with its own intelligence and will.
  4. 11:10 Essence and anchoring - Using Aslan and other examples, he shows how an image can open into essence, dimensionality, and a stable sense of being rooted without being frozen.
  5. 17:54 Autonomy of self - He clarifies that imaginal practice preserves the self’s freedom to choose, pause, refuse, or re-enter contact, even when the image feels powerful or demanding.
  6. 25:51 Eros and wisdom - Through examples of goddess, sexual energy, and divine nourishment, he shows how eros can be held skillfully without being reduced to possession or urgency.
  7. 39:11 Being loved back - He expands autonomy into reciprocity, drawing on anteros to show that the imaginal other may also return eros and that being received can be healing.
  8. 51:12 Desire and self-knowledge - He widens the discussion to desire itself, distinguishing culturally conditioned wishes from authentic soul desire and showing why knowing oneself includes knowing desire.
  9. 01:16:10 Fullness of intention - He closes by contrasting a narrow healing agenda with a fuller intention for soul-making, then uses a teaching story to show how eros can soften, become divine, and deepen into ensouling.
