Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Two Talks: 1- Mindfulness and Emptiness - The Foundations; 2 - The Ending of the World

Dharma Seed 1:14:07 2009-02-03

Rob Burbea begins with mindfulness as a way of seeing how suffering gets made out of fleeting sensations and mental labeling, then widens into emptiness as the deeper question of what is really here. From there he moves through dependent origination, mutual dependence, and the way clinging helps fabricate both self and world, using retreat examples, Buddhist texts, and a few Zen and Christian references. The talk ends with practical ways to work with fading, fear, and grasping without turning emptiness into aversion or an achievement.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Mindfulness and suffering - He starts from everyday experience and shows how careful attention exposes the mind's habit of making things seem solid and inherently awful or wonderful.
  2. 04:43 Fabrication as root - Using the retreat story and then the Buddha's 'end of the world' teaching, he names fabrication as the root issue and opens the move into emptiness.
  3. 09:05 Dualities arise together - He introduces dependent origination through dualities like short and long, showing that opposites depend on each other rather than existing on their own.
  4. 11:00 Context and clinging - The talk broadens to many paired experiences, from silence and noise to health and illness, and shows how clinging gives them force and meaning.
  5. 19:41 Practice without preferences - He brings in 'do not see fault anywhere' and Faith in Mind to frame practice as loosening preferences, grasping, and the urge to pick and choose.
  6. 28:48 What clinging means - Clinging is unpacked as push and pull, appropriation, identification, and delusion, all of which help build the felt world.
  7. 32:09 Objects fading away - He explains how perception can blur and fade as clinging loosens, and distinguishes meditative fading from ordinary functioning in the world.
  8. 43:24 Dependent arising in depth - Using the twelve links and the image of a web, he shows how less delusion and less clinging affect the whole system and lead toward non-fabrication.
  9. 51:49 Warnings and fear - He cautions against turning emptiness into a hidden strategy of aversion, and speaks to fear, safety, and the heart's need to know that nothing real is lost.
