Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Truthfulness and Authenticity

Dharma Seed 64:18 2009-11-02

What if the main obstacle to practice is not lack of understanding, but the ways we quietly drift from what we know is true? The talk moves from ordinary dishonesty and self-protection into the subtler habits of self-deception, inner criticism, and the ways perception is shaped by fear, craving, and mind state. Along the way, the speaker uses examples from Joshua Bell’s unnoticed metro performance, climate-change perception, Rumi, and the Bodhisattva path to ask how honesty, discernment, and wholehearted intention can actually be lived rather than merely affirmed.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the Theme - Rob greets the retreat and introduces truthfulness and authenticity as the morning's focus.
  2. 02:15 Speaking Truly - He looks at truthful speech, especially the Buddha's emphasis on what is true, helpful, timely, and non-harming.
  3. 08:25 Self-Deception and Bullshit - He turns to subtler forms of untruth, including fear-based withholding, social fitting-in, and Frankfurt's distinction between lying and bullshit.
  4. 13:35 Perception and Context - Using the Joshua Bell metro story, he shows how recognition and value depend on framing, attention, and conditions.
  5. 20:18 Slow Changes, Shifting Minds - He links truthfulness with climate-change perception, the placebo effect, and the way mind states and emotions color what seems real.
  6. 28:07 What Practice Is For - The talk moves into the malleability of perception, the question of trust, and the Buddha's paramitas as a basis for practice.
  7. 35:51 Deep Desire and Intention - Through Rumi and a discussion of the bodhisattva vow, he asks what truly drives us and how repeated renewal of intention matters more than ceremony alone.
  8. 44:06 The Inner Critic - He examines self-judgment, fraud feelings, and the inner critic and rebel as forces that can distort practice unless they are consciously met.
  9. 51:51 Honesty, Care, and Freedom - He closes by balancing anatta with self-care, asking what we really want and need, and returning to Rumi's image of truth leading toward deeper freedom.
