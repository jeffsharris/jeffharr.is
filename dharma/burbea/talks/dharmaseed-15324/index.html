Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

This Matter of Emptiness

Dharma Seed 50:33 2011-12-31

Rob Burbea explores emptiness as more than a philosophical idea, contrasting it with a simple “be with whatever is happening” understanding of mindfulness. He moves from Nagarjuna and the Buddha’s teachings to a practical account of how self, object, and suffering are fabricated through reactivity, mood, assumptions, and the way we look. The talk closes by reframing practice as learning to see this fabrication clearly, so that experience can be held with more freedom, groundlessness, and warmth.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Footstep and map - Burbea begins by distinguishing the immediate work of practice from the larger map, and explains why the talk will focus on the deeper dimensions of the path.
  2. 05:59 Ultimate truth - He introduces Nagarjuna and frames awakening as something that depends on realizing ultimate truth, not only settling into present-moment experience.
  3. 12:59 Self and all things - The talk widens from selflessness to the emptiness of all phenomena, with care around levels of teaching and the limits of a purely realist reading.
  4. 17:53 How experience is built - Burbea develops the practical insight that self and object are fabricated by reactivity, perspective, and the mind’s way of looking.
  5. 26:27 Wolf and rabbit - A visual demonstration with a wolf shadow and a bunny rabbit shows how fear, relief, and even mindfulness can miss the construction process itself.
  6. 34:12 Foam, mirage, magic - He turns to a sutta of similes—foam, bubble, mirage, plantain tree, magic show—to show that the aggregates and consciousness are empty of essence.
  7. 40:37 Realism and liberation - The talk names realism as the root delusion and reinterprets liberation as seeing the emptiness and dependent arising of things as they are.
  8. 44:05 Practice as seeing - In the closing movement, Burbea describes meditation as training ways of looking, so that fabrication loosens and a freer, less fixed relationship to experience becomes possible.
