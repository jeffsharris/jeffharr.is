Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Living in the Truth

Dharma Seed 66:18 2008-11-21

What if the question is not only how to end suffering, but how to live in a way that can actually see clearly? The talk moves from personal patterns and the inner critic, through the Buddha’s three characteristics, into questions of perception, clinging, and the different ways practice can point toward something more ultimate. Along the way it weighs intuition against analysis, and keeps returning to whether truth is something to defend, investigate, or quietly let transform the heart.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Truth and freedom - Burbea opens by linking truth with the release of suffering and framing Dharma as a lifelong passion for inquiry.
  2. 02:19 What truth means - He considers why the word truth can attract or trigger people, and narrows the Dharma question to what frees most deeply.
  3. 06:09 Mindfulness reveals patterns - He describes how mindfulness exposes thinking, body, emotion, suffering, and the distortions of assumption and self-view.
  4. 11:58 Personal truth - The talk turns to personal patterns, roles, beliefs, and the inner critic, with an emphasis on sincerity without over-focusing on the negative.
  5. 22:52 A quieter story - Using Basho's haiku, he shows how continuous attention can loosen the grip of personal narrative and make experience feel more immediate.
  6. 25:13 Universal truths - Burbea lays out impermanence, dukkha, and not-self, and discusses practice as a willingness to look directly at what is hard to face.
  7. 32:49 Views and perception - He examines competing models of practice, the pull between doing and non-doing, and how perception shifts can open the heart and alter what seems real.
  8. 45:01 Refining view - Drawing on Dzogchen and Nagarjuna, he argues for trusting experience while continually questioning perception and recognizing the limits of concepts.
  9. 59:12 Living unburdened - He closes by reframing Jesus' sayings as a teaching on living in truth, where rest, generosity, and freedom arise naturally.
