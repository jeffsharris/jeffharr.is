Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Catherine McGee, Chris Cullen, James Baraz, Thanissara: Mindfulness and Climate Action : 3

Dharma Seed 68:37 2014-10-19

What if climate action begins not with urgency alone, but with the way practice lets us meet fear, grief, and responsibility without hardening? The talk moves from an opening reminder of the global climate series into three linked reflections: one speaker explores the meeting place of the silence of practice and the fire of activism, another frames the climate crisis as a problem of reconnection using the fourfold spiral from Active Hope, and a third turns to the need for inspiring vision and a willingness to feel the pain of the crisis clearly. Along the way, the talk uses the Eightfold Path, the Buddha’s response to awakening, Bill McKibben’s Earth, and concrete questions about daily choices and political momentum to bring contemplation and action closer together.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening and Welcome - Thanissara welcomes listeners, introduces the series, and frames the conversation as an invitation to engage climate change in community.
  2. 04:25 Why This Matters - Lou Leonard outlines the climate context and the need for both systemic and individual responses, then hands to Catherine McGee.
  3. 09:54 Action and View - Catherine begins by asking where the silence of practice meets the passion of activism, and grounds action in intention, view, and an open system of relationship.
  4. 13:34 Bone-Jangling Reality - She shares a story of bringing climate data to an old Dharma teacher, using his shaken response to show how practice can hold painful truth without shutting down.
  5. 17:17 Silence and Fire - Catherine reflects on figures like Nelson Mandela and the possibility of combining depth, steadiness, and justice-oriented energy in action.
  6. 20:38 Reconnecting With the Earth - Chris takes up Joanna Macy and Chris Johnston's Active Hope as a four-part spiral: gratitude, honoring pain, seeing with new eyes, and going forth.
  7. 30:50 From Practice to Action - He turns to concrete steps, from home energy use and travel to public conversation and divestment, while stressing that action and contemplation support each other.
  8. 36:04 James on Inspiration - James speaks about holding an inspiring vision, how Bill McKibben's Earth changed his relationship to climate grief, and why climate work can be treated as Dharma practice.
  9. 39:53 Questions and Blessing - The discussion continues with audience questions about leverage and institutions, then closes with gratitude, a short announcement, and Catherine's blessing for all beings.
