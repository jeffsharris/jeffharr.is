Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Imaginal Practice: Doorways and Directions (Part 2)

Dharma Seed 62:22 2015-08-07

What if imagination is not a distraction from practice, but one of the ways we know the world, the self, and even the sacred? The talk moves from a quick recap of imaginal practice into a larger argument: that fantasy, mythos, and perception are already woven into ordinary life, and that Dharma can be understood as learning flexible “ways of looking” rather than fixed instructions. It then turns practical, shifting from philosophy to the retreat’s actual method: slowing down, working with the energy body, and treating the retreat as a place to develop skills and conceptual supports rather than chase experiences.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Recalling the framework - He briefly reviews the retreat’s earlier themes and says the most important issue is the conceptual framework brought to imaginal practice.
  2. 06:11 Why imagination matters - He sketches the cultural and philosophical background, arguing that imagination has been too narrowly understood and that it can open a larger sense of sacredness and reality.
  3. 13:52 Ways of knowing - He names imagination as a possible mode of knowing, introduces the mundus imaginalis, and points to a wider philosophy that can hold the imaginal seriously.
  4. 23:29 Key themes ahead - He lays out the retreat’s core terms—soulfulness, soulmaking, cosmopoesis, and the relation between imagination and practice—while distinguishing this work from therapy and related modalities.
  5. 32:35 Self and Dharma - He turns to emptiness, the malleability of self, the pervasion of fantasy in our lives, and how these insights may change what we mean by the Dharma.
  6. 42:48 Ways of looking - He reframes conventional Dharma injunctions as temporary modes of perception and uses this to explore flexibility, non-clinging, and the emptiness of appearances.
  7. 49:55 Learning the tools - He urges listeners to value understanding and durable practice skills over unusual experiences, and notes that familiar instructions like breath focus are often harder than they sound.
  8. 56:36 Energy body and pace - He explains that the first days will focus on the energy body, recommends keeping notes, and asks listeners to slow down gently to support subtler attention.
