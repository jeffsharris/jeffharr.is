The Beauty of Desire (Part 1)

Dharma Seed 59:52 2011-11-19

Rob Burbea opens a two-part inquiry into desire by questioning the usual Dharma assumption that desire simply causes suffering. He leads a guided exploration through deathbed reflection, body awareness, and the voice of the inner critic, then turns to examples showing how desire can be both limiting and life-giving. The talk stays tentative rather than conclusive, asking whether some desires may need protection and nourishment rather than immediate release.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the question - Rob introduces the two-part exploration and explains that he wants to question familiar assumptions rather than present conclusions.
  2. 04:26 The usual Dharma frame - He reviews the standard teaching that craving brings suffering and points to everyday examples of addiction, greed, and not getting what one wants.
  3. 09:24 Why letting go is hard - A retreat anecdote leads into the possibility that fear of desire, not just desire itself, may be shaping our practice.
  4. 12:32 Inquiry on the cushion - He guides a reflective exercise using kindness, a deathbed perspective, and questions about what one wanted from life and what one might regret.
  5. 20:15 What do we want? - The inquiry broadens into outward and inward desires, what we want to receive and give, and how the body and mind respond to those longings.
  6. 28:21 Inner critic and fear - Rob explores how self-judgment, fear of failure, and assumptions about deservingness can suppress desire or make it feel unsafe.
  7. 40:36 Desire as development - He reflects on how some desires may carve out depth, energy, and devotion over time, using his own search for a meditation text and jhana to show how longing can mature.
  8. 55:02 No quick conclusion - The talk closes by contrasting superficial and deep desires, recalling the Buddha’s wish to teach after awakening, and leaving the question open for the next talk.
