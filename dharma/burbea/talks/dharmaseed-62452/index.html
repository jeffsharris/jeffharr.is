Preliminaries, Regarding Voice, Movement, and Gesture (Part 1)

Dharma Seed 1:42:03 2020-03-01

What if the point of practice is not to arrive at calmness, but to open a much larger field of movement, desire, sound, and inquiry? The talk begins by framing these as preliminaries: simple exercises in movement, gesture, voice, and listening that may look small, but are meant to be developed and repeated. From there, the speaker widens into a deeper question about why we practice at all, how our fantasies of the path shape what we can hear, and why even apparently modest trainings can matter in the long run.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening the preliminaries - He explains why he is recording these talks now and frames them as practical preliminaries rather than a finished presentation.
  2. 01:56 Movement, voice, and development - He describes the exercises as centered on movement, gesture, and voice, and emphasizes that they should be developed carefully and organically.
  3. 07:27 Listening for instructions - He invites listeners to go back through earlier talks and extract hidden instructions and larger principles, rather than passively receiving teachings.
  4. 10:02 Why this focus matters - He connects movement, gesture, and voice to the capacities needed to 'fly' in practice, and to the possibility of hearing and reading more structurally.
  5. 19:54 Inertia and structure - He reflects on habits of listening and practice, the rarity of structural listening, and the value of seeing a whole conceptual framework before assessing details.
  6. 34:47 Desire and the path - Using jhana practice and then a retreat anecdote, he argues that desire, goals, and the fantasy of practice matter more deeply than isolated techniques.
  7. 51:08 What practice is for - He broadens the critique of narrow Dharma goals, brings in Einstein and Beethoven as contrasts to premature completion, and turns to the Four Noble Truths and the question of suffering.
  8. 01:15:02 Exercise one: movement - He introduces the first exercise, a movement practice that can be done physically or in imagination, with attention to posture, expansion, and the sensing of space.
  9. 01:28:56 Exercise two: voice - He outlines the second exercise, using extended vocal tones to explore resonance, whole-body emanation, mixed awareness, and sound filling the space.
