Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Tending the Flame

Dharma Seed 18:57 2016-08-03

The talk begins with the problem of leaving retreat: how to carry openness back into ordinary life without overwhelming yourself or the people around you. From there it moves into the need for Sangha, reading, conversation, and other forms of nourishment that keep the fire of practice alive after silence ends. It then turns to the retreat’s more philosophical edge—how to relate to the imaginal, realism, and the middle way by asking not what is fixedly true, but what is helpful, freeing, and soul-making. The talk closes by widening the frame further, showing how imaginal work, samadhi, metta, mindfulness, emptiness, and daily life can support each other across different periods of practice.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Leaving Retreat Gently - Burbea opens by speaking to the transition out of silence and the need to notice what the heart and soul may need after retreat.
  2. 02:29 Sharing With Care - He reflects on sharing retreat experiences with others, stressing sensitivity, timing, and attention to how one is received.
  3. 04:58 Sangha Keeps the Fire - The talk turns to the nourishment of Sangha, conversation, reading, and listening as essential support for practice after retreat.
  4. 08:32 The Imaginal Middle Way - He addresses questions around imaginal practice, realism, and the tension between what is real and not real.
  5. 10:51 Check the Soul-Making - Rather than fixed doctrine, he suggests testing a view by whether it brings aliveness, freedom, and beauty, while remaining wary of authority.
  6. 14:57 Practice Beyond Retreat - Burbea argues that these practices can be done in short periods and woven into ordinary life, not only on long retreats.
  7. 16:07 A Wider Practice Tapestry - He situates imaginal and cosmopoetic work within a larger set of mutually nourishing practices and closes by encouraging play, curiosity, and devotion to what the soul longs for.
