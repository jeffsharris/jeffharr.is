The End of Time (The Cessation of Perception and Feeling)

Dharma Seed 1:42:55 2020-01-07

What if the deepest freedom is not a special state you arrive at, but the letting go of the subtle structures that keep experience building itself? The talk moves from the unconditioned and the deathless into a close reading of jhāna, showing how insight can be used inside absorption to see fabrications, clinging, and even the faintest sense of subject, object, and time. Along the way, it pauses for Pali Canon passages, questions about emptiness and dependent origination, and a sustained argument that these are not separate topics but one spectrum of practice and understanding.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening context - Rob sets the frame for a late-retreat talk and says he wants to focus on the larger conceptual picture rather than technical details alone.
  2. 06:00 Unfabricated and emptiness - He introduces the unfabricated and explains why it matters alongside emptiness, then gives a brief distinction between dependent origination, emptiness, and lack of inherent existence.
  3. 16:19 Dependent origination - Using a Pali Canon story of the Buddha and Ananda, he argues that dependent origination is profound, not obvious, and that the mind’s way of looking is central to liberation.
  4. 23:54 What the unfabricated means - He clarifies that the unfabricated is not merely calm attention or nonthinking, but something much more radical, and notes how rare this framing is in Dharma teaching.
  5. 34:39 Beyond perception - Several sutta passages are read to show a state where all perception, sense contact, and ordinary time-space have ceased, including the language of consciousness without attribute.
  6. 46:14 Experience and knowledge - An aside on epistemology and ontology opens into the question of what meditation can legitimately show us about reality, and how philosophy and practice meet.
  7. 54:11 Jhana as insight - Rob reads passages where jhana is seen as fabricated, impermanent, and empty, then explains why stable absorption can become a strong basis for insight.
  8. 01:07:06 Sariputta’s discernment - A passage on Sāriputta shows jhana used with great skill in insight ways of looking, discerning the arising and fading of qualities without attraction or repulsion.
  9. 01:15:51 Subject, object, time - He develops a subtler account of fabrication in which subject, object, and time arise together, carrying intention, craving, and the basic momentum of samsara.
