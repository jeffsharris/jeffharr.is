The Sixth Jhana (The Realm of Infinite Consciousness)

Dharma Seed 1:19:46 2020-01-04

What changes when awareness turns back on itself and everything else falls away? The talk starts with the Buddha’s brief description of the realm of infinite consciousness, then slows down to unpack what that could mean in practice: noticing the subtle sense of knowing, tuning to it, and letting it strengthen until consciousness seems to know only itself. From there, the talk compares this state with the vastness of awareness, asks what such experiences really tell us, and keeps returning to the practical problem of how desire, intention, and training shape what becomes available. It ends by widening the frame again: these are not just rare altered states, but perceptions that can be cultivated, worked with, and understood in relation to dependent arising, emptiness, and ordinary life.

Chapters

  1. 05:51 Opening the Topic - The talk is introduced as a look at the sixth jhana, named here in the Buddha’s language as the realm of infinite consciousness.
  2. 06:32 Canonical Description - Rob reads the short canonical passage and treats it as an instruction to enter, stay with, and establish consciousness itself.
  3. 07:39 Awareness Knows Itself - He develops the core claim that awareness and consciousness are the same here, and that the key feature is awareness knowing awareness.
  4. 11:07 How Attention Settles - Subtle internal cues, repeated noticing, and tuning the mind toward the sense of knowing are presented as the way the state is stabilized and amplified.
  5. 17:46 Intensity and Release - He describes the realm as electric, self-sufficient, and transcendent, emphasizing its sense of escape from the phenomenal world.
  6. 20:15 Vastness Compared - Rob compares the sixth jhana with the vastness of awareness, distinguishing a state that excludes phenomena from one that includes them more fully.
  7. 26:52 After-Effects and Truth - The discussion turns to the after-effects on perception, the rarity of the states, and the caution that these are deep but still provisional truths.
  8. 41:40 Paths In and View - He outlines several ways into the realm, situates the formless attainments in the wider Buddhist map, and links them to release and dependent origination.
  9. 59:02 Training, Desire, and Use - The final stretch emphasizes practice as training, the importance of desire and coherent intention, and a brief tactical note on using imaginal or insight approaches when needed.
