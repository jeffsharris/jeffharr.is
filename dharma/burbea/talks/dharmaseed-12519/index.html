Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Fourth Instructions and Guided Metta Meditation

Dharma Seed 54:22 2011-02-09

How do you keep metta alive when the body is comfortable one moment, difficult the next, and sometimes feels like nothing at all is happening? The talk keeps returning to that practical question, moving from a review of the "three baskets" of practice into a flexible way of working with bodily well-being, pain, and the normal absence of feeling. It then opens into a guided meditation that widens awareness through the whole body, using light, warmth, and simple imagery to support a spacious, gentle metta practice.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Reviewing the practice - He reintroduces the three 'baskets' of metta practice and stresses that each approach is valid and healing.
  2. 08:17 Samadhi as spaciousness - He reframes concentration as a unification that is broad and balanced rather than tight or cramped.
  3. 12:59 Working with difficulty - He explains how to meet discomfort, pain, and blockage with flexibility, including imagination as a support for releasing contraction.
  4. 23:01 Whole-body metta - The practice expands from a focal point into the whole body, with metta expressed through touch, movement, and gentleness.
  5. 28:23 Boundless in steps - He places the practice in a larger arc: metta is boundless in aim, but approached gradually through suitable entry points.
  6. 30:09 Guided body meditation - He begins the sitting with contact, whole-body awareness, and a ray of light rising through the body, linking belly, chest, throat, and head.
  7. 41:05 Silent cultivation - Longer periods of silence are held within the same instructions to keep planting the intention of kindness and returning attention to the body.
  8. 50:24 Closing tone - He ends by emphasizing lightness, gentleness, and kindness in the effort itself, and invites the practice to remain soft and connected.
