Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Image of Ethics (Part 5)

Dharma Seed 2:11:02 2020-02-18

How do ethics stay alive once the talk has already unsettled the ground beneath ontology and epistemology? The speaker moves through Buddhadharma, emptiness, Abhidhamma, Nagarjuna, Augustine, Plato, Hartman, and modern science to ask not whether a view is simply true, but what it makes possible for care, virtue, and soulmaking. The talk keeps returning to a practical test: whether a framework deepens sensitivity, dimension, and responsibility, or flattens ethics into something mechanical or empty. It ends by widening the question to beauty, trained discernment, and whether ethics can be grounded in a sense of what is worthwhile and meaningful.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Framing the inquiry - He recaps the central aim of the series: to ask what our assumptions about reality and knowledge make possible for ethics and practice.
  2. 08:40 Buddhist views of value - He contrasts Pali Canon, Mahayana, and Vajrayana perspectives on virtue, karma, emptiness, and symbolic transgression.
  3. 13:40 Realism and its limits - Using Abhidhamma and Nagarjuna, he examines a realist picture of mind moments and why it can feel mechanically thin.
  4. 17:26 Ethics and modern reductionism - He pushes back on scientistic and conversational reductions of ethics, tracing how modern assumptions about reality have shaped the problem.
  5. 27:29 Ways of looking - He returns to emptiness as an open-ended practice of seeing suffering, self, and world, insisting that ethics remains built in.
  6. 33:39 Ontology for soulmaking - Through arithmetic, dependent origination, and soulmaking Dharma, he argues that emptiness is enough for liberation but not for every human domain.
  7. 40:48 Plato, Hartman, and hierarchy - He compares competing ontologies of value, asking how ideal realities, material reality, and human meaning relate.
  8. 01:02:19 Eros and Augustine - He turns from skepticism toward a more lived epistemology, then reads Augustine on divine illumination and the imprint of righteousness.
  9. 01:37:17 Beauty, expertise, and democracy - He broadens the discussion from ethics to beauty, art, and political judgment, wondering whether discernment should be trained rather than merely voted on.
