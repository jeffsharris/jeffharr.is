Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Energy Body (A Little Bit of What, Why, and How)

Dharma Seed 48:46 2018-06-23

What changes when the body is taken not as a set of sensations, but as a field of energy, texture, vibration, and image? The talk begins with a simple guided exploration of posture, breath, and open awareness, then widens into why this way of paying attention matters for samadhi, metta, insight, and imaginal practice. From there, it turns to the larger question of how concepts and images shape perception, and why staying flexible with them can open a much broader range of experience.

Chapters

  1. 01:04 Introducing the energy body - Burbea names the topic, explains why he is using the term, and frames it as a practical part of the retreat.
  2. 02:11 What it feels like - He defines the energy body narrowly as a way of attention that senses the body as vibration, texture, and field.
  3. 06:12 Posture, space, breath - A brief guided exploration uses posture, spacious awareness, and slow breathing to open the body as a field.
  4. 11:26 Light, metta, and emotion - He adds imagery of light and loving-kindness, then notes how contractions and emotions also show up in the energy body.
  5. 16:06 Why it matters - He explains how this way of sensing supports samadhi, metta, insight, and imaginal practice, and helps distinguish fixation from soulmaking.
  6. 20:56 Stillness and practice forks - Burbea recommends keeping the body still for now, then describes the fork between leaning toward energetic coherence or prioritizing the image.
  7. 25:46 A wider definition - He broadens the term to include felt sense, conceiving, and image, and argues for a phenomenological approach rather than a realist one.
  8. 39:30 Intention and range - He closes by stressing that the aim is not a fixed pleasant state, but an open-ended range of perception, intention, and energy.
