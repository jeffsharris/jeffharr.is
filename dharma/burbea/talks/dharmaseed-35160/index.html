Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Ending the Inner Critic (3)

Dharma Seed 35:12 2010-12-04

Rob Burbea continues his exploration of the inner critic by mapping it as a conditioned loop of self-view, selective attention, and interpretation. He then turns to several practical shifts: taking in beauty and ethics, aligning with deepest aspiration, seeing experience as a web of conditions rather than a fixed self, and loosening the authority we give to thought. The talk moves from diagnosis to practice and ends with a quiet confidence that these patterns can be weakened and, over time, ended.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Introducing the methods - Burbea sets up the next set of practices and distinguishes between working with the critic in the moment and training view when things are relatively stable.
  2. 03:02 The self-reinforcing loop - He explains how self-view narrows attention, which then feeds the same view again, describing this as dependent origination.
  3. 03:39 Shifting attention - The talk turns to practical ways of placing attention elsewhere and seeing more of the whole rather than only the evidence that confirms criticism.
  4. 04:47 Beauty and ethics - He encourages recollecting one’s own goodness, honoring ethical intention, and recognizing that culturally valued status is not the same as what is truly worth respect.
  5. 11:01 Deep aspiration - Using a story from Gaia House, he shows how asking what the heart most longs for can dissolve comparison and bring openness, strength, and joy.
  6. 15:11 Seeing conditions - Burbea shifts to the view that actions arise from conditions rather than a fixed self, using a small everyday mistake to show how self-blame can be replaced by understanding.
  7. 18:25 The web of causes - He lingers with the car-window story to show how much suffering comes from quick self-conclusion instead of seeing the larger web of causes and pressures.
  8. 25:01 Thought and gullibility - The final practice weakens the inner critic by loosening belief in thought, opening awareness to body, sound, and thoughts as passing phenomena.
  9. 31:44 Freedom is possible - He closes by emphasizing that freedom from the inner critic is achievable, sometimes gradually and sometimes suddenly, and that the pattern can be cut at the root.
