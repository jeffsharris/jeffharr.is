Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Beauty and the Buddha

Dharma Seed 1:32:44 2017-04-01

What if beauty is not a property of things at all, but something that arises in the relation between subject and object, and can be deepened by the way we look? The talk moves from the Pali Canon’s rather stark treatment of sense pleasure, through critiques of reductionist or tragic views of aesthetics, into an account of beauty as eros, resonance, imaginal perception, and eventually divinity and buddhanature. It ends by asking whether the aim of practice is really to reach a final truth, or to support an ever-expanding movement of soulmaking.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the Question - Burbea introduces beauty as the talk’s theme and says the material will unfold nonlinearly, tied to earlier retreat teachings.
  2. 02:54 Beauty in the Canon - He looks at the Pali Canon’s unusual use of beauty, especially its association with the mind and the fourth jhana rather than ordinary sense objects.
  3. 06:07 Sense Pleasures and Limits - The talk turns to the canon’s warnings about sense pleasure and the tension between worldly senses and the unfabricated.
  4. 08:54 Radiance and Vividness - He considers a friend’s claim that beauty arises from mindful, craving-free perception, then reframes radiance as vividness, alertness, and presence.
  5. 15:02 Tragedy or More - Keats’s negative capability and a tragic reading of art are used as a contrast, setting up Burbea’s critique of a singular, impermanence-centered aesthetic.
  6. 21:09 Eros and Resonance - Burbea develops beauty as eros, resonance, and participation between subject and object, linking it to imaginal experience and the widening of perception.
  7. 43:09 Emptiness and Dimensions - He argues that beauty is relational and empty of fixed essence, then critiques practices or teachings that flatten experience into one dimension or one taste.
  8. 01:00:04 Divinity and Buddha Nature - Beauty becomes an analogy for divinity and buddhanature, presented as open-ended, mysterious, and available through shifts in ways of looking.
  9. 01:17:30 Soulmaking Practice - He closes by describing how soulmaking, emptiness, and an expanded sense of the senses can dissolve dualism and orient practice toward limitless creation and discovery.
