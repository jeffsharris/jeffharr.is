Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

'The Holy Life' (Part 2)

Dharma Seed 1:28:16 2017-02-08

In this talk, Rob Burbea continues exploring eros in relation to the path, asking what kinds of awakening can actually support desire, imagination, and soulmaking. He contrasts deflationary models of practice with more expansive ones, moving through papancha, emptiness, Vajrayana imagery, and the way different views shape what feels possible on the path. The talk gradually widens from a Buddhist analysis of sense experience into a broader inquiry into psychology, culture, and the limits people place on eros.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the question - He frames the talk around eros, the path, and how they already include questions of awakening and the world.
  2. 05:05 Pleasure and reduction - He contrasts common teachings about sense pleasure and papancha with more nuanced ways of understanding what the mind is doing.
  3. 11:15 Papancha reconsidered - He revisits papancha as objectification and fabrication, then distinguishes different levels at which experience can be simplified or emptied.
  4. 17:34 Cooking and devotion - Using the Gaia House kitchen, he shows how ordinary activities can carry fantasy, beauty, and soulmaking beyond simple care.
  5. 22:49 Vajrayana and emptiness - He turns to Vajrayana mandala symbolism and the Heart Sutra to show different ways appearance, wisdom, and emptiness can be related.
  6. 29:40 Eros in the path - He develops the idea that the degree and kind of eros shape the kind of path a person can inhabit, with examples from lovers, food, and embodied practice.
  7. 47:43 History and deflation - He draws on Richard Tarnas, Nietzsche, William James, and Richard Rorty to show how sobriety, pragmatism, and deflation can narrow the available imagination.
  8. 01:02:23 Fear and limitation - With Douglas Fairbairn, he explores how fear of disappointment can create an anti-libidinal stance that dampens eros and soulmaking.
  9. 01:17:33 A larger path - He closes by arguing that more eros requires a larger vision of awakening, one that can keep opening into depth, divinity, and multidimensionality.
