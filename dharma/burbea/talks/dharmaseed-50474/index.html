Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Aspects of the Imaginal (Part 4)

Dharma Seed 1:22:09 2017-12-18

What if an imaginal perception is not a single thing, but a whole constellation of elements that can be entered from different doors? The talk moves through that field by tracing how attention, receptivity, eros, beauty, divinity, grace, reverence, humility, trust, and duty can each ignite the others, sometimes all at once and sometimes gradually, like dimmers turning up rather than a switch flipping on. Along the way, the speaker keeps returning to the practical question of how to sense these qualities without reducing them: how to stay open, how to tell the difference between contraction and opening, and how to let an image or insight actually reshape the life around it.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Imaginal constellation - Burbea frames imaginal perception as a lattice of interrelated elements that can be studied, tuned, and gently stirred.
  2. 05:51 Ignition and grace - He describes how one node can light up the whole field, and how receptivity matters as much as technique.
  3. 11:44 Switches and dials - The talk develops the sense that imaginal practice can shift suddenly or gradually, with attention acting like a set of adjustable dials.
  4. 19:36 Elements of the field - He names several key nodes in the constellation, including energy body, soulfulness, love, eros, and beauty.
  5. 27:54 Dimensionality and divinity - Burbea explores how imaginal depth shades into divinity, while warning against easy ontological claims and insisting on an epistemological middle way.
  6. 40:27 Grace, reverence, humility - He describes the sacred as gift, then turns to reverence, bowing, and humility as bodily and ethical orientations rather than self-effacing ideals.
  7. 50:30 Humility as image - Humility itself becomes an imaginal object that can expand, deepen, and be transformed through soulmaking rather than remaining a fixed virtue.
  8. 01:09:35 Trust in the image - A grain of trust can loosen reification, let puzzling images move, and reveal a deeper intelligence at work in the field.
  9. 01:15:04 Duty to consolidate - The talk closes with duty as the soul’s felt responsibility to bring new insight, oneness, or practice-state into ordinary life and not let it be forgotten.
