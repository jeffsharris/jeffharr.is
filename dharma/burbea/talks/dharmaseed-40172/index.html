Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Eros Unfettered (Part 2)

Dharma Seed 1:21:10 2017-01-24

In this second talk on eros, Rob Burbea develops the idea that erotic life is not just about a subject wanting an object, but about a larger soul-making movement in which self, other, world, and eros co-arise. He moves from basic distinctions between eros and metta into examples from imaginal practice, sexual and non-sexual desire, and cosmopoiesis, showing how perception can open into beauty, divinity, and a living world. The talk ends by widening that frame further, suggesting that eros can be understood as a cosmogonic force that helps make worlds.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Setting the Trajectory - He introduces the broader aims of the talk and reviews the thread connecting eros, the imaginal, and soul-making.
  2. 03:41 Dependent Co-Arising - He frames self, other, world, and eros as a fourfold constellation rather than a simple subject-object pair.
  3. 08:50 Images That Expand - An earlier example of goddess contact and bodily infusion shows how imaginal practice can deepen self and world.
  4. 14:07 Eros and Metta - He distinguishes eros from mettā with imagination, especially through eros's auto-erotic and dimensional character.
  5. 20:40 Desire and Interpretation - Using William Blake and a close look at craving, he shows how a way of looking can make desire harden or open.
  6. 29:52 Cosmopoiesis Opens - He turns to world-making, including Thomas Aquinas's line about beauty yearning to be seen and loved.
  7. 35:43 Examples in Practice - Several imaginal and erotic examples show how love can spread into body, matter, nature, and subtle energy.
  8. 46:42 Samadhi and Sacred Time - He describes the choice between absorption and staying with image, then brings in Corbin's idea of hierophantic time.
  9. 58:33 Expanding Sacredness - He recaps how eros can widen the sense of divinity, self, other, and world, while keeping the experience image-like rather than fixed.
