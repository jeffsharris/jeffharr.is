Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Seventh Jhana (The Realm of Nothingness)

Dharma Seed 1:26:23 2020-01-05

Rob Burbea explores the seventh jhana as the dimension of nothingness, starting with the Buddha’s description and then moving into the practical problems of how it is recognized, entered, and trusted. He emphasizes that this territory is often frightening unless it grows out of earlier steadier absorptions, and he keeps returning to the difference between mere absorption and liberating insight. The talk then widens into emptiness, perception, desire, heart-opening, and finally the imaginal possibilities that can open when perception becomes more malleable.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Introducing Nothingness - Burbea names the seventh jhana and frames it through the Buddha’s description of transcending infinite consciousness into nothingness.
  2. 01:52 Unexpected Openings - He describes how people may stumble into a void-like experience without a map, and why that can be confusing or frightening.
  3. 08:54 Stepping Stones - The talk turns to the earlier jhanas as stable bases that make the movement into nothingness less abrupt and less alarming.
  4. 15:53 Insight Ways of Looking - Burbea uses the Ananja Sappaya Sutta to show how seeing perceptions as just perceptions and as not peaceful can open the dimension of nothingness.
  5. 21:14 Empty of Self - He develops the sutta’s third contemplation, explaining how this is not just about personal self but about phenomenal self and fabricated things.
  6. 27:22 Confidence in the Dimension - The Buddha’s formula about practicing and frequently abiding becomes a way of explaining how confidence in nothingness is gradually established.
  7. 30:56 No Thingness and Fabrication - Burbea works through different subtle ways of looking at experience, insisting that the key payoff is insight into no-things rather than simple impermanence.
  8. 44:03 From Samadhi to Insight - He argues that insight is more important than the sheer degree of absorption and shows how this understanding changes the way one moves in the world.
  9. 56:22 Heart, Desire, and Limits - The talk broadens to metta, desire, and the power of concepts to either open or constrain what the heart can do.
