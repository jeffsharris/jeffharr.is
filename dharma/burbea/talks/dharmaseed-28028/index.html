Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Releasing the Self, Freeing its Demons (Part 2)

Dharma Seed 29:58 2014-10-05

What happens when an inner critic is met not as a solid enemy, but as one figure in a wider inner theater? The talk moves through retreat stories of comforting, devouring, erotic, and warrior-like images to show how engaging them with mindfulness and without literalizing can loosen the sense of a fixed self. It then widens further, suggesting that figures from art, myth, and public life can live in the psyche as real sources of resonance, challenge, and nourishment.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Picking Up the Thread - He situates the talk as a continuation and says he will return to the material in stages through the day.
  2. 00:47 Inner Critic, Inner Persons - Through a retreat example, he shows how the inner critic can open into a dialogue with multiple figures rather than a single fixed voice.
  3. 04:14 Mindfulness and Image - He distinguishes mindfulness with images from literalizing them, and explains how this both protects and vivifies the practice.
  4. 07:25 Loosening a Solid Self - He places image-work alongside emptiness and mystical dissolution as another way of softening belief in a singular self.
  5. 10:37 Images as Resources - A series of retreat stories shows images as sources of care, nourishment, power, and soul-level opening, including the grandpa, goddess, dragon, soldier, and mountain lion.
  6. 17:06 Figures in Culture - He turns to public figures and traditions, including the Thai forest Ajahns, Mandela, Einstein, Bohr, Hendrix, and Coltrane, to show how people can live in the psyche as images.
  7. 21:38 Wildness and Shame - He considers images that do not fit Buddhist ideals, including Dionysian energy and a story of sexual shame reframed as sacred rather than merely fallen.
  8. 26:06 Working Deliberately - He offers practical ways to engage images in meditation and notes that upset and samadhi can both make image-work more available.
