Closing Comments From Rob - Soulmaking and Sangha

Dharma Seed 18:53 2019-03-30

How do you leave a retreat where the heart has been opened, without spilling what feels precious? The talk moves from simple thanks and practical guidance for the days after retreat into a careful warning about sensitivity, enthusiasm, and the need to check the moment and the listener before sharing what has been touched. From there it turns toward the deeper question of how Soulmaking Dharma is meant to be taken up: as something to digest fully, develop, and only then weave into one’s own practice, in solitude and in Sangha.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening Thanks - Rob begins with gratitude and a brief sense of not yet knowing exactly what to say.
  2. 01:23 Leaving Retreat Carefully - He offers practical counsel for transitioning out of retreat, especially the heart’s sensitivity and the need for rest, space, or solitude.
  3. 03:58 Sharing What Is Precious - Rob warns that soul-touching experience cannot always be shared immediately and encourages checking the moment, the listener, and the conditions.
  4. 07:20 The Gift of Dana - He reflects on the moving dana ceremony and on how imaginal and soulmaking practice once felt difficult to speak about publicly.
  5. 11:43 Imaginal Lineage - Rob describes turning to a timeless imaginal lineage for support when teaching or when illness left him depleted.
  6. 13:28 Assimilation and Development - He encourages each person to take up the teachings according to their calling, while developing a solid base and noticing blind spots.
  7. 15:43 Sangha and Friendship - Rob closes by emphasizing study, mutual support, and the hard work of deep friendship within Sangha.
  8. 17:11 Final Thanks - He ends with repeated thanks and a brief, unfinished exchange as the talk comes to a close.
