Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Place of Calm

Dharma Seed 58:00 2012-11-01

What is calm for: relief from stress, clearer seeing, or something more unsettling? The talk starts with the familiar case for calming the mind, then steadily widens into samadhi as a whole field of well-being, energy, sensitivity, and unification—not just one-pointed concentration. From there it moves into the body’s contractions, clinging, nourishment, and the possibility that practice may be less about forcing stillness than about loosening entanglement and allowing perception to open in unexpected ways.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the question - He begins by noting the range of people and practices in the room and frames calm as a topic that may be useful in different ways for different listeners.
  2. 01:51 Why calm matters - Using the Buddha’s pond image, he describes calm as both relief from stress and a condition that allows clearer seeing.
  3. 04:13 What samadhi includes - He broadens samadhi beyond one-pointed focus into well-being, harmonization, energization, sensitivity, and subtle refinement of attention.
  4. 11:38 Nourishing practice - He turns to retreat life as part of practice, emphasizing kindness, gratitude, appreciation, and human connection as supports for samadhi.
  5. 15:38 Less entanglement - He explains that letting go of entanglement with phenomena allows the mind to gather and settle, and he warns against over-effort that turns practice tight and joyless.
  6. 18:55 Clinging in the body - He names clinging as bodily contraction and offers the body, especially tension and tightness, as a practical indicator for easing effort and releasing grasping.
  7. 24:11 Whole-body practice - He describes samadhi as including the whole body and suggests practices that begin from the subtle or energy body, or even make clinging itself the theme.
  8. 31:16 Reservoir and perception - He presents samadhi as a reservoir of well-being that empowers letting go, then moves into a deeper view in which practice fabricates and gradually unfabricates perception.
  9. 42:14 Questioning the image - He questions the modern ideal of calm as a final spiritual image, asks what gets lost in that framing, and ends by returning to retreat as a time for deepening rather than settling for a narrow ideal.
