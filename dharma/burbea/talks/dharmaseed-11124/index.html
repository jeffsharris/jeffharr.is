Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation on the Three Characteristics

Dharma Seed 52:52 2009-01-26

This guided meditation introduces three ways of working with experience: attending closely to impermanence, softening aversion or grasping, and seeing sensations, sounds, and thoughts as not-self. The practice moves through the hands, face, whole body, sound, sight, and thought, with brief pauses to let the sense of change become clearer. In the closing section, Rob also speaks about the unfamiliarity that can arise as letting go deepens, and how to meet fear by inclining toward what is peaceful, spacious, or warm.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Three approaches - Rob introduces the three linked modes of practice and sets the tone for a relaxed, balanced meditation posture.
  2. 01:20 Settling the mind - The meditation begins with calming and gathering awareness through breath, metta, or open awareness.
  3. 05:54 Impermanence in the hands - Attention moves into the hands, where the main emphasis is on noticing flicker, pulse, and change.
  4. 11:40 Face and whole body - The practice broadens to the face and then the whole body, still resting on the changing nature of sensation.
  5. 15:15 Sound and relation - Awareness opens to sound and then to the felt relationship with pleasant and unpleasant experience, including aversion and grasping.
  6. 26:39 Allowing experience - Rob reframes the practice as letting experience be, emphasizing allowing and non-interference.
  7. 30:04 Not-self in awareness - The third approach is introduced: seeing sensations and sounds as just happening, with less ownership and identification.
  8. 40:13 Walking through the day - The same three approaches are applied to movement and daily life, followed by a brief review of the methods.
  9. 47:55 Fear and familiarization - Rob speaks about the fear that can arise as consciousness opens, and suggests meeting it by trusting small doses of spaciousness, peace, or joy.
