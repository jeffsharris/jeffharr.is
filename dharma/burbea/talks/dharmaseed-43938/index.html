Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Eros in the Imaginal (Guided Meditation)

Dharma Seed 54:30 2017-03-27

How do you work with an image that is more than visual, more than personal, and more than a tool for metta? The talk first carefully sets out what counts as imaginal: something sensed in relationship, with resonance, mystery, autonomy, beauty, and even a quality of sacredness, rather than a merely imagined picture. It then moves into a long guided meditation that begins with the energy body, invites an image to arise or be chosen, and explores different kinds of contact—gazing, identifying, witnessing, and the possibility of erotic or other forms of communion—before ending with practical ways to enter imaginal work from dreams, emotion, the body, or the room itself.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 What Imaginal Means - Burbea defines imaginal practice as more than visualization, more than something purely internal, and more than something only visual.
  2. 10:01 Settling Into Practice - He invites the group into posture and stillness before opening attention to the body and the energy field.
  3. 11:12 Energy Body and Samadhi - The meditation begins with sensing the energy body, tending well-being, and using a light, caring mode of attention.
  4. 19:49 Letting An Image Form - An image is invited to arise, either from looseness, difficulty, or deliberate choice, and to be met in relationship.
  5. 23:41 Ways Of Relating - He explores several modes of contact with the image: as a dyad, by identifying with it, by witnessing it, or by noticing mutual gaze and exchange.
  6. 29:29 Eros And Transformation - The practice opens into being with, moving with, and potentially sexual contact, while staying rooted in sensitivity and non-hierarchy.
  7. 37:19 Primary Sense And Resonance - Attention turns to the image's dominant sense modality, its element, solidity, and the emotional and bodily resonance it evokes.
  8. 48:25 Choosing And Receiving Images - He closes by explaining that images may arrive or be chosen, and that practice can begin from dream images, people, the room, emotions, emptiness, or the energy body.
