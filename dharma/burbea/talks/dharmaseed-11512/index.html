Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Second Morning Instructions and Guided Meditation

Dharma Seed 46:19 2008-08-10

Rob Burbea offers a second-morning review of concentration practice, emphasizing whole-body awareness, sensitivity, steadiness, and working skillfully with discomfort. He then lays out a set of practical options for pain, restlessness, dullness, doubt, and craving, framing each as something to meet with experimentation rather than force. The talk closes with a long guided meditation that moves the breath through different points in the body, from the lower belly to the crown and feet, always returning to a whole-body field of awareness.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Reviewing the basics - Burbea recaps the central themes from the previous evening, especially whole-body awareness, experimenting with the breath, and balancing calm with energy.
  2. 02:29 Sensitivity and steadiness - He develops the paired qualities of sensitivity and steadiness, describing how attention can become more alive and less easily thrown off by ups and downs.
  3. 06:05 Working with discomfort - The talk turns to practical ways of meeting pain and constriction, including relaxing the rest of the body, enlarging awareness, and staying with comfort.
  4. 08:40 Breath as a resource - He explores using breath and imagination to soothe blocked areas, move energy through the body, and notice how often the most constricted place can open into the most pleasant.
  5. 11:18 Hindrances and discernment - Burbea addresses fear, tiredness, restlessness, doubt, greed, and aversion, and briefly invokes the Buddha’s pragmatic question about what is helpful and what leads to freedom.
  6. 16:32 Settling the posture - The guided meditation begins by settling into posture and reconnecting practice with goodwill, kindness, and a whole-body sense of sensitivity.
  7. 21:22 Breath through the body - He leads attention through breath at the lower belly, hara, solar plexus, heart center, throat, head, and crown, inviting the breath to be felt in whatever way is most supportive.
  8. 31:28 Down the body and feet - The meditation expands into breath moving down through the body, out through the feet, and into the back and spine, with repeated emphasis on what feels best right now.
  9. 40:28 Centering and spaciousness - The practice closes by centering awareness in the middle of the body, imagining the breath as arising from within, and then resting wherever feels most balanced and alive.
