The Rhythms of Soulmaking and Elements of the Imaginal (23 - 28)

Dharma Seed 40:00 2018-02-08

What if soulmaking does not arrive on command, but comes and goes with conditions, like any other practice state? The talk moves through that rhythm first, easing pressure and self-judgment, then widens into a practical map: a palette of practices that nourish the being, a middle way between forcing and neglect, and the sense that blocks can sometimes break open suddenly or shift only gradually. From there it turns to specific imaginal nodes—fullness of intention, duty, meaningfulness, echoing, values, and participation—showing how an image can carry both personal history and something larger than the self, even when the form fades. A few vivid examples, including Aslan and music arising from meditative stillness, help the talk circle back to beauty, nobility, and the possibility of participating in a much wider imaginal world.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening the frame - He introduces the talk as a brief set of general clarifications and sets up the themes he hopes to cover.
  2. 00:57 Self-judgment and conditions - He shows how inner criticism forms around whatever quality a community emphasizes, and how soulfulness, like any practice result, depends on conditions.
  3. 02:58 Pressure and the middle way - Using jhana as a parallel, he warns against forcing soulfulness and points toward a balanced attitude of nourishment without pressure.
  4. 04:50 The palette of practice - He places imaginal practice within a wider palette that includes energy body work, metta, emptiness, desire, and letting go.
  5. 06:15 Rhythms and blocks - He describes the stuttering, coming-and-going rhythm of soulmaking and the various walls it can meet, including sudden breakthroughs and gradual loosening.
  6. 12:45 Fullness of intention - He turns to the 23rd node and explains how intention deepens from self-development toward wanting soulmaking itself, or even doing practice for the divine.
  7. 17:12 Duty and its flavor - He revisits duty as a loaded concept, showing how it changes when paired with divinity or creation and why it needs careful discernment.
  8. 20:29 Meaning, echoes, and values - He moves through meaningfulness, infinite echoing, mirrored images, and the way values such as nobility and beauty can be sensed within imaginal practice.
  9. 29:18 Participation and participation - He closes with the 28th node, participation, stressing that we are not outside observers but participants in the imaginal world, the soul of the world, and the divine eros.
