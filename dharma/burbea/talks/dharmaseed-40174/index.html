Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Eros Unfettered (Part 4)

Dharma Seed 37:36 2017-01-26

In this talk, Rob Burbea explores eros as a force that opens perception, interpretation, and soulmaking rather than simple desire. He moves from Plato and Kabbalistic images of infinite meaning into Buddhist language, then turns to the ways eros can be narrowed by literalism, greed, or reification. The talk closes with a careful account of containment: how boundaries and non-acting can become a temenos, a sacred vessel in which eros deepens into imaginal and spiritual creativity.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Eros Opens Meaning - Burbea introduces eros as a force that can either support or block soulmaking, and frames it as something that opens interpretation, beauty, and depth.
  2. 02:32 Infinite Interpretation - He brings in Plato, Kabbalah, Sanford Drob, and Moses Chaim Rosato to show how eros can make the world and scripture feel inexhaustibly meaningful.
  3. 07:04 Buddha Nature Language - He translates the same opening into Buddhist terms, linking eros, divine seeing, conceptual creativity, and emptiness to the jhana of Buddha nature.
  4. 09:38 When Eros Narrows - Burbea describes how, without an imaginal dimension, eros can collapse into greed or horizontal pursuit, especially when nature is seen one-dimensionally.
  5. 12:29 Dream of the Door - He tells a dream of a carved door, music, rain forest, and stars to show how an image can carry beauty, reverence, and depth without needing to be fully unpacked.
  6. 17:17 Relationships And Limits - He applies the same pattern to nature, teacher-student devotion, and music, showing how eros fades when an image or relationship can no longer hold the soul's expansion.
  7. 24:10 Icon, Idol, Concept - Using Tibetan lineage, Henri Corbin, and Jean-Luc Marion, he distinguishes icon from idol and argues that the real danger is reification and identification, not expansion itself.
  8. 29:14 Temenos And Containment - Burbea explains how restraint and non-acting can create an alchemical vessel or temenos in which erotic tension is preserved and transformed into soulmaking.
  9. 35:36 James On Desire - He ends with William James on the extravagance of wants, using the quote to suggest that uneasiness and longing can point beyond present understanding.
