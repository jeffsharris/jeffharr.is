Between Ikon and Eidos: Image & Hermeneutics in Meditation (Part 8 - Talking with Trees)

Dharma Seed 1:55:38 2018-01-14

How do perception, speech, and love change when a tree is no longer just an object, but a being that can be met with reverence? The talk moves from body and earth as sacred texts, through examples of trees, stones, and imaginal figures, into a sustained inquiry into how ethical speech and attentive practice refine sensitivity. From there it broadens into questions of values, aesthetics, morality, and the way love can reveal a particular being’s hidden depth without reducing it to a concept or use. It ends by rereading Genesis as a call to leave what is no longer soul-making and to step into a wider dimensionality of life.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Body, earth, and icon - He revisits the earlier theme of sensing body and earth as sacred and icon-like, where perfection is a different order from health or appearance.
  2. 05:57 Suzuki and paradise - A D.T. Suzuki anecdote opens the possibility that ordinary things can be seen as already within paradise or the Pure Land.
  3. 08:07 How trees communicate - He turns to loving trees, asking how love is received through touch, speech, attention, and a willingness to experiment.
  4. 26:41 Speech and sensitivity - Right speech is presented as a way to refine the heart and body, increasing the subtlety needed for soul-sensing.
  5. 34:40 Reverence, culture, and doubt - He links reverence with wisdom, challenges cultural assumptions about nonhuman intelligence, and includes stories from Lakota and Franciscan traditions.
  6. 49:40 Love sees more deeply - Goethe and Hartmann are used to distinguish universal goodwill from personal love, which perceives uniqueness and hidden essence.
  7. 01:01:18 Eros, art, and knowing - Abulafia’s gematria leads into a wider discussion of reciprocity, artistic truth, and the participatory nature of sensing values.
  8. 01:23:09 Values and antinomies - He explores how moral and aesthetic values pull in opposing directions, making certainty difficult and keeping perception open-ended.
  9. 01:46:44 Loneliness and the call - The talk closes by linking ecological loss, commodification, and loneliness to the need to answer a perennial call beyond what is familiar.
