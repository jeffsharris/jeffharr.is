Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Movement of Devotion (Live and Shortened Version)

Dharma Seed 42:48 2016-07-29

The talk asks how practice changes when the aim is not tight concentration but a more open, flexible field of experience. It moves through a careful exploration of what supports imaginal practice: less clinging and fabrication, a blending of the senses, and a heart posture of receptivity, humility, and surrender. From there, it turns practical, showing how images, prayer, devotion, the energy body, and even simple shifts in posture can alter what becomes available in meditation.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Framing the retreat - Rob begins by situating the talk within a retreat where practice is expected to unfold differently for different people and at different stages.
  2. 02:27 Openness over concentration - He explains that the retreat is oriented less toward concentration as a goal and more toward openness of mind, perception, heart, and experiment.
  3. 05:29 Experimenting with view - Rob shows how eyes open or closed, and especially the way one relates to what is perceived, can either contract or open practice.
  4. 10:19 Flexibility and imaginal work - Drawing on Catherine McGee’s framing, he describes moving flexibly between emotion, samadhi, and imaginal practice while staying sensitive to the body.
  5. 13:29 What opens the imaginal - He names less fabrication and sensory blending as important conditions for imaginal opening, and explains these as effects of reduced clinging.
  6. 16:46 The heart's posture - Rob emphasizes receptivity, surrender, and humility as practical stances of the heart that allow imaginal experience to become alive and soulmaking.
  7. 19:47 Divinity and devotion - He uses illness, blessing, and Tibetan yidam practice to show how devotion, prayer, and tantric preliminaries can deepen imaginal contact.
  8. 25:19 Prayer, receiving, and union - Rob expands prayer into supplication, praise, and blessing, and describes how relationship with an imaginal figure can move through receiving, harmonizing, and union.
  9. 31:30 Body, movement, and discernment - He closes by showing how compassion, posture, mudra, walking meditation, and even prostration can shift energy and perception, then asks listeners to keep only what is useful.
