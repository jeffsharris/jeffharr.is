Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Working with the Emotional Body (Instructions: Day Five)

Dharma Seed 25:25 2011-08-03

Rob Burbea offers a practical talk on resourcing difficult emotions through awareness, the body, silence, and the emotional field. He moves from revisiting the spacious awareness practice into a wide set of supports: friendship, calm states, compassion, warmth, and imagination. The talk then turns to subtle ways difficulty is maintained, including judgment, contraction, isolation, and a lack of contact with our own strength and life force.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Reviewing Spacious Awareness - He revisits the previous day's practice of opening awareness and explains it as one option among several.
  2. 05:21 External and Inner Resources - The talk broadens into resources such as friends, calm heart states, and cultivated qualities like compassion and equanimity.
  3. 07:55 Space, Silence, and Body - He emphasizes unnoticed supports already present: silence, space, and the energy body holding experience.
  4. 10:43 Meeting Pain Directly - Burbea describes touching suffering more directly and noticing when compassion arises but is passed over too quickly.
  5. 13:19 Warmth Around Difficulty - He offers images and questions for surrounding pain with warmth, and gently inquiring into what blocks that movement.
  6. 16:24 Allowing and Softening - He shows how allowing creates space, and how softening the body and belly can soften the heart.
  7. 18:40 Not Alone in Suffering - The teaching turns to self-view and isolation, including the suggestion to recognize that others share the same kind of suffering.
  8. 21:37 Strength, Power, Desire - He ends by reclaiming strength and power as resources, distinguishing them from anger and linking them to life force and even desire.
