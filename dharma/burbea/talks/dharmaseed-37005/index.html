Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Creating the Path (Q & A)

Dharma Seed 43:20 2016-07-31

In this Q&A, Rob Burbea explores how imaginal practice can make the world feel sacred without collapsing into either rigid metaphysics or mere self-projection. He moves from Tara and other forms, to art and dependent arising, to sexuality, eros, and the ways an image can retain autonomy while still being part of practice. The talk closes with a sober reflection on risks, path-specific dangers, and the question of what one most deeply wants from Dharma life.

Chapters

  1. 01:19 Imaginal forms - Rob weighs different ways of understanding Tara and other divine figures, from an objective imaginal realm to the role of mind and culture in perception.
  2. 04:54 Art and dependence - He uses Beethoven and art theory to show that aesthetic response arises dependently from the work, the listener, and the surrounding culture.
  3. 06:37 Practice and sacredness - The discussion turns to how practice changes perception, including seeing Tara in ordinary or even difficult objects, and to a practical way of judging whether something is truly wholesome.
  4. 14:26 Cosmopoesis - Rob explains how imaginal perception can spill over into the world so that the whole cosmos is seen as light, fire, music, or Tara's presence.
  5. 18:49 Eros and otherness - He distinguishes oneness experiences from an imaginal mode in which a figure retains character, otherness, and erotic charge without collapsing into merging.
  6. 23:33 Texture and autonomy - A listener asks about kinesthetic experience, and Rob clarifies that image may be nonvisual, may hold a subtle self-sense, and can feel autonomous without being separate.
  7. 29:17 Emptiness and fluidity - He brings the teaching back to dependent arising and emptiness, emphasizing that self and image have no inherent existence and can shift fluidly from moment to moment.
  8. 32:37 Paths and dangers - A question about Theravada and Mahayana leads into a wider reflection on spiritual ambition, fear about outcomes, and the specific dangers of each path, including being swept away or reifying experience.
  9. 40:50 What matters most - In the closing movement, Rob asks whether freedom is really the deepest value for each person, and invites an honest look at sacredness, beauty, art, and authenticity.
