Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Sensing with Soul (Part 7)

Dharma Seed 50:37 2017-12-27

What counts as knowing, and why do we trust some kinds of experience while shrinking back from others? The talk moves through that question by comparing ordinary facts, values like beauty and goodness, inner images, and the harder case of sensing soul in the world around us. From a tree’s love, to the Smurfs and Beethoven, to a black cat in a dark basement, the speaker keeps testing where contemporary culture draws the line between imagination, perception, and reality. The talk ends by suggesting that a middle way, and eventually the idea of participation, may open a less cramped way of understanding truth and soul-making.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening the epistemic question - He returns to the talk’s opening concern: what counts as knowing when the subject is soul-sensing and value.
  2. 03:50 Values and modern skepticism - He contrasts material facts with beauty, goodness, and love, arguing that modern culture often grants values a lesser truth status.
  3. 09:01 Inner images, outer knowing - He distinguishes intra-psychic images, which many people now validate, from the harder question of extra-psychic soul-sensing.
  4. 13:30 Love, beauty, and judgment - Using the tree example, a mother-son story, and the Smurfs versus Beethoven, he asks how we know love and beauty at all.
  5. 19:23 ESP and imaginal contrast - He separates matter-of-fact ESP-like information from imaginal perceptions that carry depth, beauty, and meaningfulness.
  6. 27:14 Middle way in practice - He describes the imaginal middle way as a stance that preserves freedom, wonder, and soulmaking, while literalizing tends to diminish the experience.
  7. 32:56 Black cat metaphor - A basement-and-black-cat image illustrates how experience can make what once seemed speculative feel worthy of conceptual framing.
  8. 36:23 Culture and hidden assumptions - He broadens the inquiry into how cultures rank forms of knowing, using autism, color-blindness, and Richard Rorty to question default materialism.
  9. 46:51 Participation and soulmaking - He closes with participation as a possible way beyond subject/object dualism, where truth, reality, and soul are mutually discovered and made.
