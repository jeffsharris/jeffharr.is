Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Soul Making Dharma (Q & A)

Dharma Seed 41:26 2018-06-28

What if soul is not a fixed thing but the organ of perceiving soulfully, where experience is both created and discovered at once? In this Q&A, the talk moves through that tension carefully: the looseness of concepts, the role of Logos, the way awakening can be understood as expanding rather than ending, and why language matters for noticing what practice is already doing. It then turns to the pain of soullessness, the healing power of imaginal work, and the delicate balance of effort, receptivity, grace, and energy in practice.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the Questions - Rob gathers the retreat questions and orients the discussion toward the foundations of Soulmaking Dharma and how it relates to awakening.
  2. 09:58 What Soul Means - He offers a loose definition of soul as psyche and as the organ of soulful perception, emphasizing openness over fixed terminology.
  3. 11:15 Create and Discover - Rob explains the create/discover formulation as a useful balance point that names participation without reducing experience to either fabrication or mere finding.
  4. 14:03 Concepts That Stay Loose - He widens the discussion of Logos and related concepts, stressing that these terms need elasticity if they are to remain fruitful in practice.
  5. 16:33 Awakening Expands - Rob shows how different people and traditions use 'awakening' in very different ways, from courage before meaninglessness to subtle meditative opening.
  6. 20:21 Dukkha of Soullessness - He brings the teaching into a broader psychological and cultural frame, including how soulless environments and lack of meaning can be a real form of suffering.
  7. 28:31 Logos and Devotion - Rob clarifies that even the barest perception involves concept, and he connects imagery, devotion, and love of the path to natural soul-making.
  8. 31:50 Language and Practice - He argues that naming inner processes helps practitioners see what is already happening, then describes practice as a delicate modulation of effort, grace, and receptivity.
  9. 37:29 Energy and Caution - Rob answers a final question about the energy body, noting that most people need more harmonized energy rather than less, while acknowledging possible imbalances.
