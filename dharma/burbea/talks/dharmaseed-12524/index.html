Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Second Instructions and Guided Metta Meditation

Dharma Seed 54:33 2011-02-07

How do you keep metta alive without turning it heavy, rigid, or forced? The talk moves through a practical answer: treat the practice like sailing, adjusting to the conditions; when things are smooth, stay with the warmth; when difficulty is present, include it with kindness; and when it is too much, know when to set the formal practice down and meet experience differently. A guided metta practice follows, using posture, bodily sensitivity, phrases, visualization, and the felt sense of warmth to let kindness gather around the whole body and then toward oneself and another person.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Responsive metta - Rob introduces the talk as a further look at metta and emphasizes responsiveness, lightness, and playfulness rather than heavy effort.
  2. 01:29 The sailing analogy - He uses sailing to show how practice changes with conditions, from smooth cruising to adjusting the sails as the wind shifts.
  3. 04:47 Working with difficulty - He names hindrances, restlessness, doubt, and sleepiness, then explains when to stay with metta and when to step back and meet suffering more directly.
  4. 10:30 Mindfulness with warmth - Rob points ahead to Catherine McGee's teaching and clarifies that mindfulness includes acceptance and kindness around hard experience, not just bare attention.
  5. 12:21 Phrases and resonance - He explains flexible use of phrases, the varying flavors of metta, and how words can echo in the body and be repeated only as long as they remain alive.
  6. 19:02 Allowing well-being - He explores the subtler possibility of drawing out or allowing bodily well-being, and how attention can choose not to get trapped by difficulty.
  7. 26:23 Self and easiest person - Rob distinguishes the narrative self from a simpler bodily sense of self, then prepares the group for a guided practice starting where metta feels easiest.
  8. 33:23 Guided metta practice - A long guided meditation settles posture, opens awareness through the body, and moves from self-metta to another person with phrases, imagery, and kinesthetic warmth.
  9. 48:29 Closing the practice - The guided practice continues through images and phrases until the meditation closes quietly at the end of the sit.
