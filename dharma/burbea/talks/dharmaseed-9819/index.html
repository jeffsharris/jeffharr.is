Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Self and Its Search for Security

Dharma Seed 61:39 2009-09-11

What if the drive for security is quietly turning money, self-image, and even the future into sources of worry? The talk starts with the sense of a vulnerable self that reaches for ground, then turns to money as a revealing test case: how fear, scarcity, respect, and survival get tangled together, and how a different relationship to resources might be possible. It moves from social examples and a Harlem fundraising story into a closer inquiry into self-view itself, showing how the self is built and reinforced through memory, mood, and reactivity. By the end, the emphasis has shifted from trying to shore up a fixed self to cultivating inner qualities that can actually be trusted.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the Theme - Burbea begins by naming the talk’s theme and sketching the basic sense of self that feels vulnerable in a changing world.
  2. 03:03 Groundlessness and Money - He introduces David Loy’s framing of groundlessness and the ways the modern self tries to shore itself up through money, fame, love, and power.
  3. 07:58 Money as Flow - Using Lynn Twist’s language, he describes money as something that can carry fear or love, and can either harden self-concern or support transformation.
  4. 12:59 Scarcity and Distortion - He looks at money as a measure of worth and security, and widens the lens to collective patterns that normalize fear, imbalance, and short-term thinking.
  5. 21:44 Frog, Flood, and Stewardship - The boiling-frog analogy and the Harlem church-basement story both point toward hidden habits of fear and a different, more generous relation to resources.
  6. 29:29 Wise Security - Burbea turns from accumulation to the Buddha’s idea of security rooted in loving kindness, compassion, generosity, equanimity, mindfulness, and meditation.
  7. 36:13 Self-View and Story - He examines how self-image depends on other people’s reactions, on chosen memories, and on the story we tell about our lives, illustrated by a dream of suddenly seeing that none of it is true.
  8. 45:29 How Self Is Built - The talk deepens into the mechanics of self-view, showing how present mood, cultural values, and reactivity reinforce the sense of a solid person over time.
  9. 53:27 Putting It Down - He distinguishes skillful self-views from the deeper insight that all selfing is fabricated, ending with the sense that noticing this rise and fall can open into a freer way of being.
