Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Energy Body: Instructions (1)

Dharma Seed 1:34:14 2015-08-07

Rob Burbea introduces the retreat’s central practice as working with the energy body: a whole-body field of felt experience that can support samadhi, emotional skillfulness, and imaginal practice. He distinguishes sensation-based body awareness from more conceptual body contemplation, then spends most of the talk surveying practical ways to sense, shape, and play with the energy body through attention, breath, and imagery. The talk ends with concrete advice for working with pain, discomfort, and pleasantness, and with a reminder that the practice is meant to be light, experimental, and gentle.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Setting the course - Burbea introduces the retreat instructions and explains why there is so much material to absorb slowly and in order.
  2. 02:16 Three body modes - He distinguishes sensation awareness, conceptual contemplation of the body, and the more direct work of energy-body sensitivity.
  3. 09:37 What energy body means - He defines the energy body as a phenomenological way of sensing bodily experience, not as a metaphysical belief.
  4. 14:37 Samadhi and well-being - He describes samadhi as whole-body awareness that becomes bright, restful, harmonious, and useful for practice.
  5. 23:15 Playful practice - He encourages experimenting without a right-or-wrong attitude and then begins the guided meditation with posture and body scanning.
  6. 29:34 Opening the field - The meditation expands awareness into a space slightly larger than the body and asks listeners to sense its tone, texture, and vibration.
  7. 34:05 Breath and centers - He explores breath-energy centered in the heart, solar plexus, hara, and head, emphasizing comfort and direct feeling over doctrine.
  8. 54:43 Lines and light - The practice shifts to vertical and radiating lines of energy, then to a cloud of light and a body of light as further imaginal options.
  9. 01:07:16 Survey of possibilities - He reviews the meditation as a set of options, including metta and other figures, while keeping direct felt sensing primary.
