Counting Within the Breath (Guided Meditation)

Dharma Seed 48:15 2019-12-17

What changes when you count not before the breath or after it, but inside the breath itself? The talk introduces a guided practice that matches the count to the length of the in-breath and out-breath, while keeping whole-body awareness open and alive, and then shifts into the details of how to make the practice comfortable, bright, and workable. After the guided section, the speaker explains why this method can steady attention, counter low energy, and even support the approach to first jhana, before giving practical retreat guidance on posture, movement, and interviews.

Chapters

  1. 01:45 Counting Within Breath - Rob introduces the uncommon method of counting inside each breath and guides the group through the practice with whole-body awareness.
  2. 29:27 Settling After Practice - He checks in on how the exercise felt and notes that it may be awkward or helpful in different ways for different people.
  3. 30:31 Why It Helps - Rob explains that the practice can counter low energy and provide more support for attention, especially at the start of retreat.
  4. 31:57 Attention and Jhana - He describes how the practice can be developed and says it may bring the mind close to first jhana if it is carefully cultivated.
  5. 33:48 Posture and Comfort - Rob emphasizes that an upright, comfortable posture matters and points people to a posture session for further help.
  6. 35:48 Movement and Energy - He recommends balancing sitting with walking, standing, gentle movement, and time outside to support the body and the retreat energy.
  7. 37:21 Interviews and Practice Focus - Rob explains how interviews will work and asks participants to bring questions mainly about their meditation practice and jhana work.
  8. 40:10 Practical Questions - He closes with guidance on pace, room use, water, chairs, standing meditation, and other retreat logistics before ending for the evening.
