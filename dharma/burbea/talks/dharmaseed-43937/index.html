Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Eros and Equanimity in the Movements of Soul

Dharma Seed 1:29:23 2017-03-27

How does desire deepen practice without collapsing into grasping, realism, or imbalance? The talk develops a framework in which eros, imaginal perception, and concepts mutually fertilize one another, widening experience when they are not blocked by rigid views, aversion, or a refusal of image. It then turns to the tension between eros and equanimity, asking how the movement of desire can remain balanced, spacious, and sustainable. Near the end, the speaker returns to the key middle way of seeing image as image, and shows how that stance can open both potency and trust in practice.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the Terms - Burbea begins by explaining why soul and soul-making resist fixed definition and why that resistance may be appropriate.
  2. 02:51 What Soul-Making Means - He offers a working definition of soul-making as a dynamic relationship with experience that expands sensing, conceiving, and imaginal ways of knowing.
  3. 10:08 The Shape of Eros - He describes eros as desire for more intimacy and connection, with qualities like juiciness, arousal, beauty, and an attraction that carries longing for more.
  4. 24:51 Eros-Psyche-Logos - Burbea lays out the three-part dynamic of eros, imaginal perception, and concepts, showing how they can deepen and enrich one another.
  5. 33:45 Otherness and Beyondness - He distinguishes eros from metta, then argues that eros depends on twoness and creates more otherness without dismissing present appearance.
  6. 43:50 Self, World, and Eros - The imaginal field widens to include the self and the world, and he shows how attention can either support or block that expansion.
  7. 57:34 Staying Balanced - Using a clinical example, Burbea distinguishes intensity from potency and shows how opening attention can keep eros sustainable and less constricted.
  8. 01:10:02 Eros and Equanimity - He compares eros with equanimity, noting that equanimity quiets fabrication while eros can support skillful fabrication grounded in emptiness.
  9. 01:17:44 Seeing Image as Image - Burbea closes with the middle way between real and unreal, including Mother Teresa as a tentative example, and ends by stressing trust in the imaginal without reifying it.
