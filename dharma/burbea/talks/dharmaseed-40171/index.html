Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Eros Unfettered (Part 1)

Dharma Seed 1:36:17 2017-01-23

Rob Burbea explores eros as a soulmaking force, asking why erotic imagery and attraction are so central in imaginal practice. He develops eros as desire for contact with the beloved other, then widens the frame to include beauty, otherness, longing, and the way image, self, and world are co-fabricated. The talk moves from conceptual clarification to long, concrete examples, including subtle and overt erotic images, before returning to the practical question of how trust, guilt, or reduction shape what the image can become.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the frame - Rob introduces the talk as a careful unfolding of eros and the soulmaking dynamic, with an emphasis on both theory and practical consequence.
  2. 17:22 Why eros appears - He explains why eros naturally arises in imaginal practice, linking it to beauty, otherness, and the broader range of images that a practice can hold.
  3. 24:52 Desire for contact - Eros is reframed as a movement toward contact, intimacy, and touch, with Pothos and Himeros used to describe longing and ongoing movement.
  4. 44:57 Soul loves soul-making - He develops the mutual fertilizing of eros, psyche, and logos, arguing that the soul wants more soul and that image, idea, self, and world expand together.
  5. 59:17 Subtle erotic images - A series of gentler examples shows that erotic imagery can be tender, luminous, and non-escalatory, not limited to overt sexuality.
  6. 01:03:01 Samadhi and transformation - He describes how movie residue, prison imagery, the tiger, and deeper samadhi shift into a more luminous imaginal scene, showing the link between mind state and image.
  7. 01:18:40 The dark goddess rite - A long imaginal narrative on a raft at sea explores ritual, blood, devotion, dismemberment, and the wide range of erotic and sacred image-forms.
  8. 01:33:33 Trust or collapse - He closes by showing how trust, guilt, or reduction change the fate of the image and whether the soulmaking process opens or folds.
