How To Be an Earthling

Dharma Seed 26:51 2014-04-20

Wes Nisker offers a humorous Earth Day talk that moves from comic songs and playful scripture mashups into a serious reflection on ecological crisis and our place within evolution. He uses Gary Snyder, John Muir, Darwin, and his own satire to question human exceptionalism and suggest a more grounded, interdependent story. The talk closes with a simple practice of presence—here, now—paired with a call to care for the planet in ordinary, practical ways.

Chapters

  1. 00:05 Ecological pledge - Wes opens with Gary Snyder’s ecological Pledge of Allegiance to frame the talk around belonging to the earth and all beings.
  2. 01:26 John Muir and wonder - He honors John Muir and reads a short poem on the earth’s continuous cycles of sunrise, sunset, and turning.
  3. 02:45 Crisis and comic relief - A song about modern anxieties turns environmental dread and media saturation into a shared, playful release.
  4. 07:06 Liberation and Passover - Wes imagines Moses, Jesus, and Buddha in contemporary terms, linking spiritual renewal with liberation from greed and bondage.
  5. 09:11 Earth Day and Gaia - He praises Earth Day as a universal holy day and invokes Gaia, Pachamama, and the planet’s gravity as forms of care and support.
  6. 10:54 Evolution as creation myth - Wes contrasts the Holocene and Anthropocene, then asks listeners to take evolution seriously as a new story of who we are.
  7. 14:36 Made of earth - He emphasizes that our bodies are literally made from earth and shared life, and urges simpler living and practical protection of the planet.
  8. 17:46 Enough stuff - Kevin Griffin’s song satirizes consumer excess and names the feeling that we already have more than enough.
  9. 23:57 Here and now - The closing teaching becomes a call-and-response on presence, reducing the message to a simple instruction: be here now.
