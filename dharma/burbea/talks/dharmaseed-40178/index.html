Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

In Love with the Way: Images of Path and of Self

Dharma Seed 1:34:34 2017-02-10

Rob Burbea explores how practitioners image the path, awakening, and themselves in relation to tradition. He contrasts the usual medical-patient and religious fantasies of practice with the scientific researcher and artist fantasies, then opens the question of what is most authentic, fertile, and soulmaking. The talk moves toward a gentler inquiry: noticing where fantasy is alive, where it collapses into ego-measurement, and how different areas of practice may call for different ways of engaging.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the inquiry - Burbea explains why he wants to slow down and revisit a point about Eros for the path rather than rush past it.
  2. 02:37 Eros and path fantasy - He introduces the question of being in love with practice and suggests that the path is always carried by images, desire, and a sense of more to discover.
  3. 09:57 Medical model of practice - The Four Noble Truths are read through a medical diagnosis-and-cure model, showing how Dharma can be imagined as treatment for suffering.
  4. 17:12 Bones need flesh - Burbea argues that the Four Noble Truths are only a skeleton unless they are fleshed out by psyche, logos, image, and fantasy.
  5. 25:04 Researcher fantasy - Using science and figures like Galileo, Newton, Einstein, and Planck, he presents a path-image grounded in revision, openness, and new discoveries.
  6. 37:59 Artist fantasy - He turns to the path as art, beginning with creativity and then widening into a sense of participation in divine or Buddha-nature creativity.
  7. 47:50 Permission and scope - The talk explores how these fantasies overlap, how much permission we give them, and how they can expand or contract depending on the situation.
  8. 01:07:17 Five areas of practice - Burbea applies the inquiry to emptiness, imaginal practice, samadhi, metta, and ethics, asking what each area most truly calls forth.
  9. 01:28:14 Open-ended path - He closes by inviting listeners to test these fantasies, notice what is authentic or fertile, and consider whether the path itself may be open-ended.
