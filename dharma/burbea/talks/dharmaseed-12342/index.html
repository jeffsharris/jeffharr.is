Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

God and the Ultimate Truth

Dharma Seed 57:49 2007-03-17

Rob Burbea explores the idea of God through a Dharma lens, moving from belief and devotion into questions of fear, compassion, and what actually shapes a life. He compares personal and impersonal forms of God, brings in figures like Krishnamurti, Mother Teresa, Thomas Keating, and St. John of the Cross, and keeps returning to the practical test of whether a view opens the heart or not. The talk gradually shifts toward emptiness, silence, and the possibility that what is most ultimate may be beyond the usual opposites of belief and non-belief.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the question - Burbea introduces the topic of God carefully, naming the wide range of reactions it can bring and framing the talk as an exploration rather than an argument.
  2. 03:38 Devotion and belief - He distinguishes devotion from ritual, then contrasts belief-based approaches with Krishnamurti’s refusal of assumptions and reliance on attention.
  3. 08:40 Images of God - The talk turns to common images of God, fear of judgment, and how strong beliefs can still be transformed into compassion, using Mother Teresa as an example.
  4. 12:57 What lies beneath belief - Burbea asks what beliefs are rooted in fear, experience, or inheritance, and considers whether non-belief can hide its own assumptions or nihilism.
  5. 17:24 Bodhisattvas and practice - He moves through bodhisattva imagery, Tibetan devotional practice, and the Buddha’s line that what one dwells on is what one becomes.
  6. 21:36 Surrender and discernment - Using Gethsemane and Thomas Keating, he explores surrender, the danger of self-centered spirituality, and the question of what one is really oriented toward.
  7. 26:45 The heart as divine - The Buddha’s kilesas and brahmaviharas are used to recast divinity as qualities of the heart, which can deepen into a sense of being held by love itself.
  8. 31:14 Mystical perception - A restaurant story and a meditation on perception lead into dependent conditions, emptiness, and the warning that emptiness is not nihilism.
  9. 36:22 Silence and letting go - The talk follows Christian mystical language from “get attached to it” through Mother Teresa, Bernadette Roberts, and St. John of the Cross, toward silence, dispassion, and equanimity.
