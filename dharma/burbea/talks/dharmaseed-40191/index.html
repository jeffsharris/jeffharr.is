Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Way of Non-Clinging (Part 2)

Dharma Seed 1:14:05 2017-01-16

What if the aim of practice is not to get rid of craving once and for all, but to see how craving, clinging, self, and world are being fabricated in each moment? The talk moves from ordinary meditation experience into a finer-grained exploration of “ways of looking,” showing how releasing contraction can thin out dukkha, loosen self-view, and even change how objects, time, and perception appear. It then widens into Mahayana and Vajrayana territory, where emptiness, appearance, and the role of practice-tools like dependent origination are held together without reducing the path to a simple instruction to “just let go.”

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the question - Rob frames non-clinging as a deeper question about how suffering is made, and suggests approaching it directly in practice rather than only in moments of obvious craving.
  2. 05:03 Craving and subtle dukkha - He describes how grasping shows up as bodily and energetic contraction, and how releasing it brings calm, softness, and a more precise sense of dukkha.
  3. 10:47 Self, world, and fabrication - The talk turns to how craving fabricates self and world together, and how reducing clinging makes appearances lighter, blurrier, and less solid.
  4. 18:04 Ways of looking - Burbea defines a way of looking as the combined mode of perceiving, relating, and conceiving that shapes experience at every moment.
  5. 24:44 Spectrum of fabrication - Using dependent origination and papancha, he maps experience as a spectrum from dense obsession to increasingly less fabricated states.
  6. 30:56 Impermanence and the map - He compares impermanence practice with other views, then sets out the Four Noble Truths and dependent origination as a practical map rather than a final theory.
  7. 38:49 Empty appearances - A castle-and-tool analogy leads into a sustained argument that appearances are empty, dependent on mind and clinging, and that letting go needs a guiding way of looking.
  8. 46:35 Perception and Mahayana - The talk enters classical Buddhist puzzles about perception in an arahant or Buddha, and then a Mahayana discussion of emptiness, compassion, and appearance.
  9. 54:52 Progressive letting go - He concludes by showing how non-clinging develops through subtler forms of clinging, like a ladder or raft, and warns against treating simple letting go as the whole of the Dharma.
