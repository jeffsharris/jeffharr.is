Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Sila and Soul (Part 9)

Dharma Seed 1:10:03 2019-06-17

Rob Burbea explores ethics through the contrast between utilitarian consequence-calculation and a richer, virtue-based sense of moral life. He then turns to climate change and species loss, asking whether these call for a higher moral sensibility than concern with immediate suffering alone, and whether soulmaking belongs in that same register. The talk closes by returning to Hartmann’s idea that the higher values may be ‘useless’ in a practical sense while still being what gives life meaning, beauty, and depth.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Ethical frameworks - Rob begins by contrasting utilitarianism with other moral approaches and naming the questions that make any ethical system difficult to apply.
  2. 02:47 Limits of utility - He uses climate activism and the measurement problem to show how simple calculations of benefit and harm become unstable in practice.
  3. 07:26 Virtue and efficacy - The talk shifts toward virtue ethics, stressing that intention, character, and practical effectiveness both matter.
  4. 10:19 Higher and lower values - He develops Hartmann’s idea of a hierarchy of values, where moral worth depends on more than the value itself and includes the state of the whole being.
  5. 17:17 Fear and moral depth - Rob looks at how fear, habit, effort, and the quality of one’s consciousness can lower or elevate the same action, and how this becomes a question of sensibility.
  6. 27:50 Species loss and care - He turns to biodiversity, polar bears, and species extinction, asking whether these concerns require a larger moral perception than suffering-based appeals usually reach.
  7. 39:24 Meaning and intention - The talk widens to the relationship between Dharma, meaningfulness, and the different strands of our motivation, including the wish to reduce suffering and the love of value itself.
  8. 46:36 Useless values - Drawing on Cicovacki and Hartmann, he argues that the higher values may be practically useless while still conferring meaning, fulfillment, and nobility on life.
  9. 56:56 Foundations and beyondness - He closes by relating climate collapse to both the material foundations of life and the call for a wider, more subtle moral sensibility, before returning to soulmaking as something loved for its own sake.
