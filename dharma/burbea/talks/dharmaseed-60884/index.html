Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Energy Body and the Whole-body breath (Instructions and Guided Meditation)

Dharma Seed 52:32 2019-12-18

Rob Burbea begins with a short explanation of why this retreat will focus on whole-body breath and the energy body as supports for samadhi. He then leads a long guided meditation, moving from posture and whole-body awareness into the felt qualities of breath, including expansion, energization, relaxation, and subtle energy currents. In the closing talk, he returns to the same theme in practical terms: experiment rather than default, and let attention become lighter, more responsive, and more subtle both on and off the cushion.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the approach - He explains the retreat context and sets up the session as a guided meditation followed by teaching.
  2. 02:41 Whole body and energy - He defines what he means by whole body and energy body, distinguishing subtle felt texture from ordinary bodily sensation.
  3. 05:06 A teaching story - He recalls being taught this approach and then being asked, in a Mahasi context, what he was actually attending to.
  4. 12:04 Posture and presence - The guided meditation begins with careful posture, then expands awareness into the whole body as a living field.
  5. 18:46 Breath in the whole body - He invites the longest comfortable breath and asks listeners to feel expansion, contraction, energization, and relaxation throughout the whole space.
  6. 27:05 Solar plexus play - The practice opens to imagination and subtle currents around the solar plexus, with emphasis on experimentation rather than certainty.
  7. 34:02 What feels best - He encourages trying different breath lengths and textures to notice which supports the most ease, clarity, and pleasure in the body.
  8. 40:17 Review and principle - After the meditation, he recaps the main instructions and reframes samadhi as a path of refinement, subtlety, and responsiveness.
  9. 47:59 Daily life practice - He closes by extending whole-body awareness into ordinary activity and linking it with appreciation, nourishment, and mudita.
