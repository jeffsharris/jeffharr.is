Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Q & A, and Short Talk

Dharma Seed 1:10:08 2019-12-24

How do you work with jhāna when the mind keeps wanting either to probe, to open, or to pull back? The talk moves through a series of questions about pīti, peacefulness, subtle hindrances, and the different “bandwidths” of concentration, using images like tuning a radio, drinking from a spring, and not snatching at pleasant experience. It also widens out into practical guidance on retreat life: how openness, connection, and a steady intention can support samadhi without becoming rigid or entangled.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the questions - Rob invites questions and sets the tone for a practical discussion of jhāna and related details.
  2. 03:03 Tuning attention - He explains the difference between probing, opening, and listening for a quality in the energy body.
  3. 06:39 Receiver and refinement - The talk turns to the radio receiver metaphor and the way refinement signals a shift in jhāna.
  4. 09:17 Enjoyment before control - Rob reframes practice as maximizing enjoyment, and answers concerns about using insight methods with jhāna.
  5. 15:35 Not snatching at pīti - He warns against grabbing at pleasure too quickly and recommends a middle ground between forcing and shutting down.
  6. 20:19 Delighting in happiness - Drawing on Buddha similes, he says happiness itself does important work and may matter more than technical milestones.
  7. 26:14 Daily life and familiarity - Questions about mastery, retreat conditions, and moving between jhānas lead into a discussion of context and repetition.
  8. 32:18 First jhāna in context - He normalizes changing relationships to the first jhāna and distinguishes true jhanic equanimity from a drier neutral state.
  9. 42:24 Openness and restraint - Rob addresses pīti, fear of deepening, eye contact, and the way open-heartedness and clear intention support samadhi.
