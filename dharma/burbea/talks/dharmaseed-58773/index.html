Opening the Sense of the Energy Body

Dharma Seed 1:17:25 2019-05-27

What happens if the energy body is not a fixed thing but a way of sensing that can be opened, widened, and even re-imagined? The talk moves from a practical definition of energy body awareness—felt sense, image, and idea—into a series of adjustments: experimenting rather than fixating, bringing the whole body into contact with the object or other, and noticing how the energy field can become an organ of perception with a more synesthetic quality. It then connects this opening to breath, generosity, devotion, samadhi, and dyadic practice, including the possibility that a shift in one person’s perception can change the whole field.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Framing the inquiry - Rob situates the talk within a larger series on sensing the soul of practice and introduces the energy body as this session's focus.
  2. 02:46 Three aspects of energy - He defines the energy body through felt sense, image, and idea, emphasizing that they overlap but that felt sense is experientially primary.
  3. 09:43 Experimenting with form - Using the story of a student over-literalizing a visualization, he points to the need to test instructions by their effects rather than by visual detail alone.
  4. 14:05 A nose for essentials - He broadens the point into a general Dharma skill: developing discernment for what is crucial, what is secondary, and what actually bears fruit.
  5. 16:57 Whole-body contact - Rob explains how to bring the whole energy body into contact with an object or other person, including the idea that the body itself can become an organ of perception.
  6. 25:31 Opening the field - He describes the range of subtle-body experience, the limits imposed by cultural assumptions, and the way relationship can become a shared energetic field.
  7. 33:38 Breath, generosity, and opening - He turns to the Anapanasati Sutta and then to generosity, arguing that long breath, openness, and radical giving can all open the energy body and support samadhi and freedom.
  8. 48:07 Capacity and path - The talk shifts to trainable habits, soul styles, devotion, and the possibility that different Dharma paths fit different capacities and openings.
  9. 59:52 Energy as imaginal - Rob concludes by showing how the energy body can be sensed as imaginal or poetic, with examples from dyad practice, eros, and the subtle interdependence of experience between two people.
