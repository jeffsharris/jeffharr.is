Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Energy, Image, Emotion (Part 1)

Dharma Seed 63:23 2015-08-07

Rob Burbea explores how emotions, energy, image, and view interrelate in imaginal practice, with an emphasis on the felt life of the energy body. He distinguishes between cultivating calm and pleasure, and simply attending to emotions as they arise, then lays out a larger framework for working with emotions: feeling them, tolerating them, cultivating wholesome qualities, letting go of what is unfruitful, and understanding how they are conditioned. The talk moves into practical guidance on attention, allowing, and the use of helpful images, including several vivid retreat examples where imaginal figures transform pain and shift the energy body.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the Nexus - Burbea introduces the talk as an exploration of the nexus between emotion, energy, image, and view, and briefly recaps the energy body emphasis of the retreat.
  2. 10:41 Why Train the Energy Body - He explains the energy body as an experience rather than a belief, and describes how cultivating it supports samadhi, emotional sensitivity, and imaginal practice.
  3. 20:38 Two Practice Directions - He distinguishes between tending toward well-being in samadhi and simply attending to emotions as they show themselves in the energy body.
  4. 23:25 Five Aims With Emotion - Burbea lays out five broad aims in emotional practice: sensitivity, capacity to bear, cultivation of wholesome qualities, release of what is unfruitful, inclusion in imaginal practice, and understanding conditioning.
  5. 29:51 Working With Attention - He turns to the practicalities of sustaining delicate attention, widening awareness, balancing mindfulness against emotion, and recognizing that the relationship to the emotion matters as much as the emotion itself.
  6. 41:05 Helpful Images in Practice - Burbea explains how deliberate or spontaneous images can support difficult emotion, and reads a retreatant's account of pain transformed into ecstatic pleasure through an image of being kissed.
  7. 50:46 Lion, Divinity, and Energy - He offers further examples, including a lion healing liver pain and a figure of Ramana Maharshi arising from a sense of divinity, showing how images can open and sometimes need smoothing.
  8. 55:40 Options and Otherness - He closes by summarizing options for difficult energy, then reflects on how imaginal figures can be deliberately engaged and how their felt Otherness gives images vitality and depth.
