Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Refining the Metta Practice (Question and Answer Session 1)

Dharma Seed 62:40 2011-02-08

How do you keep metta alive when you are walking, when the body is busy, or when pain and tension seem to pull the mind off course? The talk works through practical ways of refining the practice: using a visual field, centering in the heart, choosing an easier object, adjusting phrases, and letting the practice be both more local and more spacious as needed. From there it opens into a broader view of why metta matters at all: not just as a feeling to generate, but as a way of building enough inner nourishment for letting go, and as a path into insight about how perception is shaped by mind state.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the inquiry - The session begins with a practical question about difficulty doing metta while walking, and Rob starts by exploring attention, body sense, and visual imagination.
  2. 06:25 Walking with a friend - He develops Catherine McGee’s advice to imagine walking side by side with a friend, adjusting pace and posture so metta can remain workable in motion.
  3. 11:03 Cultivation and insight - Rob frames practice as having two wings, cultivation and insight, and explains why loving-kindness and other heart practices create the nourishment needed to let go.
  4. 14:07 Mindfulness and metta - The talk turns to how mindfulness serves insight, how metta can also lead to insight, and why different practices are suited to different aims.
  5. 19:52 Range, space, and choice - He describes metta as restfulness and resource, then introduces the idea that awareness can work either in a smaller bubble or in a more open field.
  6. 26:11 Words, tone, and deepening - Rob explains how different words and phrases change the quality of metta, and how repeated practice can make the field feel more open, unified, and less made by the practitioner.
  7. 33:53 Metta and perception - He balances a psychological and a more imaginal view of metta, then uses the talk to point toward the malleability and emptiness of perception.
  8. 39:56 Entering metta in daily life - The conversation shifts to metta as a shared field rather than something one gives, and to choosing the easier object first, with the campfire image for building momentum.
  9. 49:50 Pain, aversion, and fire - The final stretch works with chest pain and difficult bodily experience, showing how different phrases, spaciousness, and a gentler response can avoid pouring gasoline on the fire.
