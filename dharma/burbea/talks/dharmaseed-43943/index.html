Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Longing, Vulnerability, Anteros

Dharma Seed 26:39 2017-04-01

What happens when longing is not treated as a problem to overcome, but as a doorway into relation? The talk moves from a difficult book-writing experience, through imaginal practice and the figure of Anteros, into a wider exploration of how devotion can answer us back, especially when vulnerability, grief, and not-knowing are included. It closes by asking how ordinary things, even tangerines, can become holy through the way we look, bless, and participate in their meaning.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the theme - He introduces longing, devotion, and vulnerability as a shared field where humanity and divinity meet.
  2. 02:02 Book-writing as doorway - A difficult book project becomes a case study in meeting burden through imaginal practice instead of bypassing it.
  3. 05:50 Anteros and reciprocity - He names Anteros as reciprocal eros and emphasizes that we are loved by what we love.
  4. 07:24 Anxiety into image - A more recent project, its anxiety, and the single-mother image show how staying with loss can reveal the soul of the work.
  5. 14:59 Deity and vulnerability - He widens the teaching through devotional relation to Jesus, the Buddha, Kuan Yin, and Avalokiteshvara.
  6. 17:07 Alchemy of desire - Longing, devotion, and eros are described as becoming transubstantiated in a mutual relation between self and beloved.
  7. 20:06 Devotion and image - Devotion is reframed as oriented to image, soul, and an imaginal dimension rather than flat material reality alone.
  8. 24:23 Sacredness made visible - He closes by saying eros needs twoness and by using tangerines as an example of ordinary things becoming holy.
