Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Sensing Divinity

Dharma Seed 51:20 2016-08-01

Can divinity be sensed both as a universal oneness and as something alive in the particulars of a self, another person, or even desire itself? The talk moves between those two modes, tracing how meditation, emptiness, metta, and imaginal practice can cool clinging while also re-enchanting personhood rather than erasing it. It then turns into a guided paired exercise: settling in the body, offering well-wishing, receiving it, and looking for the brightness and mystery of consciousness in the other’s eyes.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Enchantment and divinity - Rob introduces the retreat's use of enchantment and divinity, and says he will focus on how these meanings shape the path.
  2. 01:51 Universal oneness - He describes a universal sense of divinity that can appear as love, awareness, silence, peace, or oneness, often through deep practice.
  3. 04:02 Desire and equanimity - Using Thomas Merton and Catherine McGee as context, he shows how universal divinity can cool desire and widen the space around life.
  4. 06:50 Personal and imaginal - He turns to a more personal divinity in and through figures, objects, the self, and the imaginal, emphasizing that the two modes overlap.
  5. 11:02 Re-enchanting the self - Rob asks what it would mean to know one's own divinity and how emptiness, metta, and imaginal practice can support that seeing.
  6. 18:32 Perfection and surrender - He reflects on Neem Karoli Baba and Thomas Merton to show how surrender, poetry, and universal perception can reveal a deeper perfection without bypassing suffering.
  7. 25:27 Buddha nature and tantra - Rob moves through Tantra, Judaism, and Buddha nature to suggest that desire, anger, action, and consciousness itself can be experienced as blessed or divine.
  8. 33:36 Divine fire in particulars - He settles the teaching into the claim that personhood need not be erased but can be inflamed and perceived as divine in its unique details.
  9. 40:10 Metta and the gaze - The talk becomes a guided pair practice: giving and receiving metta, then sensing the consciousness and radiance in another person's eyes before closing with blessing and care.
