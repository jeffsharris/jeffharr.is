Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Ayya Anandabodhi, Rev. angel Kyodo williams, Susie Harrington, Thanissara: Mindfulness and Climate Action : 2

Dharma Seed 69:28 2014-10-12

What does practice ask of us when the climate crisis is no longer abstract, but a room that is already burning? This conversation moves from a grounding breath into a frank exchange about climate action, social justice, and the Buddhist roots of greed, hatred, and delusion in ecological harm. The speakers keep returning to the same pressure point: overwhelm, grief, and fear can either shut us down or be turned into clarity, compassion, and concrete action. Along the way, they move between personal responsibility, systemic critique, and practical ways to join others without retreating into isolation.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the conversation - Thanissara introduces the panel and frames climate change as a Dharma and justice issue, with a brief grounding breath for everyone joining the call.
  2. 03:34 Climate as practice - Ayya Anandabodhi speaks about climate change as an ethical and spiritual matter, naming fear, overwhelm, and the need for right action.
  3. 09:32 Interbeing and the poisons - Ayya develops the teaching of interconnection and links ecological crisis to greed, hatred, and delusion, urging practitioners not to hide behind impermanence.
  4. 18:40 Justice and systemic harm - Reverend Angel Kyodo Williams expands the lens to colonialism, hyper-capitalism, racism, and the need for sangha to become a deeper form of collective responsibility.
  5. 32:00 Not looking away - Susie Harrington reflects on truth, grief, extraction, and the courage to face what is happening without turning away, including examples from the Alberta tar sands.
  6. 39:37 From overwhelm to action - The teachers explore overwhelm as a form of collapse, then point toward compassion, nature, local action, and community resources as ways through it.
  7. 46:38 Questions and next steps - The discussion turns to breakout groups, an upcoming training, and questions about how to speak about climate change in resistant settings.
  8. 56:51 Facing resistance - Ayya and Reverend Angel answer questions about engaging people non-threateningly, finding the right community, and speaking from a truthful place.
  9. 01:04:34 Closing blessings - The call ends with announcements, final encouragement, and a blessing that returns attention to the body, the earth, and shared good intention.
