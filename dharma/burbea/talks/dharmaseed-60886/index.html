The Eighth Jhana (The Realm of Neither Perception Nor Non-Perception)

Dharma Seed 69:47 2020-01-06

What if the deepest meditation is not a blank space, but a very subtle refusal to land on any object at all? The talk works through the eighth jhana as a state where even nothingness is no longer taken up, and then turns that into a wider question about what perception means, whether it is labeling or the fabrication of experience itself. From there, the talk moves outward into a practical investigation of insight, dependent arising, and the danger of confusing genuine release with aversion or a rigid idea of how practice should unfold.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Entering the eighth jhana - Burbea introduces the move beyond the realm of nothingness into neither perception nor non-perception and reads the canonical framing of the state.
  2. 01:58 What perception means - He begins redefining perception, arguing that the key issue is not labeling but how an object is constituted for consciousness.
  3. 04:04 Perception as fabrication - The talk develops the central interpretive move: perception, experience, appearance, and object are treated as closely related terms for fabrication.
  4. 10:15 Nothingness as last object - He contrasts the realm of nothingness with the eighth jhana, where even the subtle object of nothingness is no longer being landed on.
  5. 14:45 Non-landing and release - Using Hitchhiker's Guide and a sutta analogy about sunlight, he describes the felt sense of not landing, being unhooked, and the liberation that comes with it.
  6. 27:50 Refinement through the jhanas - He situates the eighth jhana within a spectrum of increasing refinement, then offers practice suggestions for moving into it through subtle attention and maturation.
  7. 34:42 Two paths into the state - He turns to the Anupada Sutta and outlines several ways the state may open, including insight reflections, a subtle question, or tuning to a hidden level of mind.
  8. 46:43 Insight without aversion - Burbea warns that genuine disenchantment differs from repulsion, and he contrasts liberating insight with practice that has become agitated or aversive.
  9. 59:28 Emptiness and intimacy - He closes by showing how fabrication, dependent arising, and emptiness can lead not to dualism but to a more intimate, tender, and wonder-filled relation to experience.
