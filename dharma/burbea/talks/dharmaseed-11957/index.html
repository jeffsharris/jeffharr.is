Guided Meditation - Directing Love Towards dharmas

Dharma Seed 38:49 2008-02-10

This guided meditation begins by settling the body and then turning attention toward whatever is most present, whether sensation, emotion, thought, or mood. From there, the practice keeps returning to a simple movement: bathing experience in kindness, compassion, and complete welcoming, even when what is there is resistance, numbness, boredom, fear, or peace. Midway through, the emphasis broadens to include not only experience itself, but also the intention of kindness and the relationship we have to what arises. The meditation closes by asking whether anything exists independent of that relationship, leaving the listener with a quiet question about how experience is made and met.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling the body - The meditation begins with posture, openness, and softening into the body and the field of bodily sensation.
  2. 02:03 Meeting what stands out - Attention turns to whatever is prominent in experience, whether sensation, emotion, thought, or mind state.
  3. 06:27 Bathing experience in kindness - Rob introduces the core practice of directing lovingkindness and compassion toward whatever is present, including complete welcoming and radical acceptance.
  4. 12:23 Letting precision soften - The emphasis shifts from careful noticing to spacious kindness, repeating the invitation to hold each experience without trying to exclude anything.
  5. 20:12 Nothing is exempt - After a brief stretch of referenced names, the guidance expands to include resistance, numbness, boredom, fear, restlessness, peace, and happiness alike.
  6. 26:04 Including self and intention - The practice widens further to include the image of oneself, then even the kindness itself and the intention toward kindness.
  7. 34:26 Relationship and inquiry - The meditation closes with a repeated question about whether anything exists independent of our relationship to it.
