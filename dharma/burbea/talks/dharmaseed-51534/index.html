Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Spreading of Five Wings

Dharma Seed 14:53 2018-06-28

What if practice is less about fixing experience than widening its range? The talk gathers five directions of possible expansion: the elements in the constellation, the play of self/other/world/eros, the eros-psyche-logos dynamic, the spectrum from dark or difficult material to light and pure, and the texture of the energy body from subtle to dense. It keeps returning to a simple inquiry: where is the range open, where is it narrowed, and what habits or fears may be shaping that narrowing?

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Why Expansion Matters - Burbea frames soulmaking as something organic that tends to widen into new domains and asks what the point of the practice is in terms of opening sacredness.
  2. 01:55 The Five Domains - He introduces the five possible ranges and begins with the elements of the constellation or lattice, emphasizing how different nodes may feel vivid, cloudy, or still unilluminated.
  3. 04:00 Self, Other, World - The second range concerns how self, other, and world infuse one another in experience, and he asks whether one of these tends to dominate or remain undeveloped.
  4. 06:03 Eros and Logos - He adds eros explicitly and then turns to the eros-psyche-logos dynamic, suggesting reflection on how much of the intensity range is available and whether subtle eros is being overlooked.
  5. 09:11 Dark and Light - The talk widens to the spectrum from darkness, violence, and confusion to what seems pure, light, and angelic, with attention to personal tendency and openness.
  6. 11:20 Energy Body Texture - Burbea closes the list with the energy body, from luminous and gossamer to dense and solid, inviting listeners to notice what feels natural and what may have been closed off.
  7. 14:16 Imaginal Self - He ends with a clarification that the self can become imaginal without appearing as an image-figure, and that images may ask something of us in life and practice.
