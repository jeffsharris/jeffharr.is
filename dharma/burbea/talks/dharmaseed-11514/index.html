Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Talk Two: Understanding the Heart

Dharma Seed 65:52 2008-08-09

The talk begins with the question of why we understand so many things, yet not the one that matters most: the mind-heart, or citta. From there, it lays out a broad map of practice—mindfulness, cultivation, and insight—before narrowing to samatha as a way of building steadiness, well-being, and a more reliable basis for letting go. The speaker keeps returning to concrete questions: how generosity, ethics, sense restraint, and careful work with the breath shape the heart, and how a gentler, more spacious attention can meet hindrances, pain, and emotion without getting pulled around by them. The talk closes by contrasting fleeting happiness with the quieter, steadier joy that can deepen over time, and by asking for patience with a gradual path.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Understanding the heart - He opens with Ajahn Chah and sets out citta, the mind-heart, as what practice is really about caring for.
  2. 02:44 Three strands of practice - He outlines mindfulness, cultivation, and insight as a broad map of the path, with samatha included in cultivation.
  3. 05:42 Happiness as foundation - He argues that happiness is not a distraction but an essential support for practice and for the ability to let go.
  4. 08:28 Generosity and ethics - He develops generosity and moral restraint as ways of nourishing self-respect, warmth, and a less burdened heart.
  5. 15:11 Guarding the senses - He reframes sense restraint as a careful, kind orientation to sight and hearing that protects the heart from hunger and distraction.
  6. 19:02 Progress and the path - He challenges a flattened 'just be here' view and describes real progress as greater subtlety, freedom, and well-being over time.
  7. 25:04 Working with hindrances - He names sleepiness, restlessness, and the other hindrances, then offers practical ways to work with breath, posture, and spaciousness.
  8. 30:26 Not taking them personally - He explains how to meet hindrances, pain, and impulses without identifying with them or letting them grow into a whole story.
  9. 40:01 Sensitivity and emotion - He emphasizes play, patience, steadiness, and sensitivity, then explores whether emotions are being held or freshly fabricated in the moment.
