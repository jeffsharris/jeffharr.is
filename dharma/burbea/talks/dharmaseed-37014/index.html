Neither 'Is' nor 'Is Not': Of Emptiness and the Imaginal (Q & A)

Dharma Seed 32:11 2016-07-30

What happens when truth, emptiness, and image pull in different directions? The talk moves from an ethical question about when life demands a plain response into examples like climate change, art, and personal practice, showing how emptiness need not mean indifference but can support care, responsibility, and re-enchantment. In the second half, the conversation turns to soul-making and imaginal practice: how images can serve growth, or be held as primary presences, and how an image can become lively enough to seem to spread into the world.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Truth Demands Response - Rob begins with the idea that truth can require action, using climate change and meditation as examples of when to yield to reality rather than fantasy.
  2. 02:48 Ethics And Commitment - He gives a personal example of dropping Zen practice after learning about wartime complicity, and clarifies that imaginal relationship is a conscious entering into, not a fixed stance.
  3. 04:56 Climate Change And Fantasy - The discussion broadens into how facts gather fantasies around them, including fantasies of the meditator and activist, while emptiness still allows care and embodied response.
  4. 10:19 Art Into Emptiness - Rob points to art and poetry as a way in for people who relate more easily through aesthetic or symbolic meaning, then links that to freeing things from rigid ideas of real and not real.
  5. 14:19 Ways Of Looking - He states the emptiness teaching more directly: there is no fixed thing behind appearances, only ways of looking, and the loss of care is a sign that the understanding is off.
  6. 17:15 Plural Souls - A question about soul-making leads into a wider account of multiple inner voices, with comedy and ethical action both named as possible and sometimes contrasting forms of nourishment.
  7. 25:16 Images And Divinity - Rob explains that 'imaginal' already carries a sense of divinity, and distinguishes using images to cultivate qualities from serving the image as a primary presence.
  8. 30:03 Cosmopoiesis - He clarifies cosmopoiesis as an image becoming alive and spreading into the world, using Tara as the example, and closes by encouraging the listener to trust what is working.
