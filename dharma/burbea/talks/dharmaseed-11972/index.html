Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

What is Insight?

Dharma Seed 59:58 2007-12-29

Insight here is not treated as a single flash, but as a way of seeing that reduces suffering. The talk moves from a broad map of insight, through personal and universal forms of understanding, into deliberate practices with impermanence, dukkha, and not-self, and finally toward the very deep end of the path where clinging itself fades. Along the way, the speaker keeps asking a practical question: does what we see actually change how we live, relate, and let go?

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Setting the frame - Rob introduces the talk as an overview of insight and names the different levels and paths he plans to cover.
  2. 03:14 What insight means - He defines insight as any seeing or understanding that reduces suffering, then gives everyday examples of how that works.
  3. 10:02 Personal and story - The talk turns to personal insight: knowing patterns, strengths, weaknesses, and the stories we tell about ourselves.
  4. 14:07 Universal truths - He moves from personal tendencies to universal realities like impermanence, ethics, generosity, and kindness as things that support well-being.
  5. 20:12 Insighт deepens - Rob describes how repeated seeing can make insights unshakable, and how they often need to be acted on and consolidated in life.
  6. 26:46 Using insight as a lens - He distinguishes insight as a result from insight as a deliberate mode of seeing, then uses the Four Noble Truths to ask how we add to suffering.
  7. 35:11 Three characteristics - The talk focuses on impermanence, dukkha, and not-self as practices that can be deliberately taken up and tested in experience.
  8. 43:47 Fading and release - Rob explores how letting go reduces identification and how experience itself can brighten, then fade, as clinging loosens.
  9. 52:24 Dependent arising - He closes with the Satipaṭṭhāna refrain, the Buddha's teaching on tearing down the aggregates, and a middle-way view beyond existence and nonexistence.
