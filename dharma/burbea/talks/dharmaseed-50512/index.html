Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Trusting in Soulmaking (Q & A)

Dharma Seed 34:27 2018-02-04

What if the path is less about getting a practice exactly right and more about learning how soulmaking actually moves through the whole person? In this Q&A, the talk ranges from chanting, movement, and language as sparks for the energy body to the way imaginal perception can shift between embodiment, two-ness, and oneness. It then turns to the harder questions of how this fits with Buddhadharma, why some concepts need fuzzy edges to stay alive, and how to trust a felt compass even when clarity is still emerging.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the field - Rob frames soulmaking as a wide path that can include the whole of practice, and invites questions from any part of it.
  2. 02:05 Language and energy - A participant describes how chanting and moving opened the energy body, and Rob explores how language, sound, and movement can become entry points.
  3. 04:46 What can spark soulmaking - He develops the image of a spark or seed, showing how poetry, concepts, music, and other forms can ignite practice.
  4. 09:02 Broadening beyond one doorway - Rob says soulmaking can begin through one mode and then spread into other sensitivities without forcing anything.
  5. 10:37 Two-ness in embodiment - He responds to questions about imaginal embodiment, explaining the movement between identification and two-ness, and its role in relational, even erotic, imaginal experience.
  6. 17:54 Trusting and refining - He offers the phrase about trusting experience while refining view as a practical way to navigate subtle states.
  7. 19:00 Emptiness and imagination - A question about form and emptiness leads into a discussion of where soulmaking sits in relation to Buddhadharma and vajrayana-style imagination.
  8. 23:20 Why it stays open - Rob explains why he avoids overly fixed formulations, then turns to the question of how to speak about soulmaking to others without forcing premature clarity.
  9. 30:43 Clarity with fuzzy edges - He ends by saying precision is valuable, but concepts need some indeterminacy to remain fertile, and by affirming the soul's instinct for soulmaking.
