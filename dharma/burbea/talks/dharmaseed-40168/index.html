Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Doubt and Discernment (Part 1)

Dharma Seed 1:19:57 2017-02-05

How do you tell the difference between eros, craving, and the imagination’s pull into fantasy? The talk starts with the common doubts that arise around erotic imaginal practice — whether it is escapism, wish fulfillment, or a closing down of life — and keeps turning those questions back into inquiry rather than belief. From there it moves into a careful discernment of what images ask of us, how they shape engagement, and why the path may involve risk, subtlety, and a different sense of reality than flat, materialist assumptions allow. It closes by contrasting a path of oneness and non-engagement with the open-ended, infinitely creative possibilities of the erotic imaginal.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the doubt - He names the normal doubt that arises around erotic imaginal practice and proposes turning uncertainty into inquiry rather than paralysis.
  2. 03:30 Image asks for translation - He returns to earlier work on the imaginal and explains how images often carry a sense of duty and ask to be translated into life.
  3. 06:44 Non-engagement or depth - He questions the image of renunciation and non-engagement, asking whether imaginal practice really withdraws from life or deepens contact with it.
  4. 09:10 Fleeing reality revisited - He reframes the accusation of fleeing reality by asking what is meant by reality and whether dismissing the imaginal may itself be a kind of avoidance.
  5. 11:57 Eros versus craving - He introduces a sharper discernment between eros and craving, showing how desire can either narrow the heart or open and energize the whole being.
  6. 19:03 Fire, risk, and unbinding - He explores how eros is like fire: useful and dangerous, linked to Buddhist fire imagery, the Buddha’s reversal of it, and the meaning of Nibbana as unbinding.
  7. 25:59 Doubt, suffering, and path - He places the work in a wider mythic and practical frame, using Dionysus, Vajrayana, Mahasi-style retreat hardship, and Ajahn Chah’s tears to ask what kinds of risk and suffering a path can hold.
  8. 32:45 Personal movement in practice - He shares his own gradual movement from caution toward trust in erotic imaginal work, including dreams, ambivalence, and the slow opening of relationship with image and nature.
  9. 49:01 Living relationship and infinity - He ends by distinguishing imaginal practice from ordinary fantasy through sensitivity, logos, intentionality, and the theater of image, then contrasts a finite path of oneness with the infinite creative possibilities of eros and imaginal relating.
