Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Talk One: Introduction to the Art of Concentration (Samatha Meditation)

Dharma Seed 45:03 2008-08-08

What if concentration is not a narrow technique, but a way of letting the mind and body settle into a more pleasant, workable home? The talk opens by framing samatha as a retreat into a quieter stream of practice, then moves into the real task: playing with the breath, the body, attention, and perception so that calm can grow into joy. Along the way, the speaker keeps widening the frame with stories of the Buddha, emphasis on generosity, silence, and appreciation, and a steady warning against making practice tight, self-preoccupied, or merely dutiful.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the retreat - Rob welcomes the group, describes retreat as stepping into a contemplative stream, and names samatha as the focus.
  2. 03:05 Why samatha matters - He defines samatha and samadhi, explains concentration as collectedness and well-being, and notes the Buddha's strong emphasis on it.
  3. 07:11 The art of practice - He distinguishes this retreat from his usual insight teaching and says the work will be approached as an art, not a rigid procedure.
  4. 10:41 Play and patience - Rob offers two key attitudes for the retreat: a spirit of play with the breath and patience with a gradual, non-linear process.
  5. 15:01 Breath and body energy - He explains that the practice involves subtle manipulation of breath, attention, and perception, aimed at a more comfortable, pleasurable body-breath relationship.
  6. 21:39 Pleasant abiding - Rob expands samadhi beyond fixation on an object, describing it as letting go, refined perception, and a pleasant abiding that opens consciousness.
  7. 23:44 Practice for all beings - Drawing on Ajahn Buddhadasa, he reframes meditation as generosity and public health, not private self-improvement.
  8. 29:33 Joy, gratitude, and enough - He turns to climate change, consumer appetite, and inner sufficiency as reasons to cultivate appreciation, joy, and a reservoir of well-being.
  9. 36:52 Retreat discipline and silence - Rob closes with practical guidance on nature walks, exercise, group interviews, silence, phone use, and precepts as supports for trust and openness.
