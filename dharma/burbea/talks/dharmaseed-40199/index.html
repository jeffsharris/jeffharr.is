Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Wisdom, Art, Balance (Part 5)

Dharma Seed 25:10 2017-02-01

How do you keep imaginal practice from tipping into one-sided intensity? The talk works through balance as a practical art: noticing where attention gets captured, redressing the emphasis, and finding a steadier field in which heart, energy body, image, and desire can support one another. It moves from a concrete example of overexcited heart-energy into a broader map of sexual energy, erotic tension, participation, and the different ways practice can be steered toward less fabrication or toward skillful imaginal making. In the end, the conceptual framework itself is treated as part of the vessel that can hold and guide the work.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Balance in Practice - He frames the talk around balance, sustainability, and fertility in imaginal practice, using attention as the main lever.
  2. 01:31 Heart and Image - A case example shows how attention can overconcentrate in the heart and energy body, crowding out the imaginal dimension.
  3. 04:31 Intensity and Potency - He names the difference between something feeling intense and something actually being transformative or soulmaking.
  4. 06:17 Sexual Energy - The talk turns to sexual arousal, suggesting a wider, whole-body attention that can let energy spread and transform into bliss.
  5. 10:24 Union or Participation - He describes two possible directions in erotic imaginal practice: dissolving into oneness, or sustaining two-ness and participation.
  6. 15:55 Shifting the Emphasis - He generalizes the balancing move across desire, image, energy body, emotional tone, and the three centers of belly, heart, and head.
  7. 21:23 Degrees of Fabrication - He revisits the distinction between bare attention, a middle mode, and the deliberate fabrication of imaginal practice.
  8. 23:16 Framework as Vessel - The closing section emphasizes conceptual framework as a container that offers orientation, trust, stability, and guidance.
