Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation on Gratitude and Compassion

Dharma Seed 47:40 2008-02-09

What happens when gratitude is not just a feeling but a way of seeing the whole web of support behind a life? The guided meditation moves from settling the body into a long sequence of recollections: benefactors, teachers, parents, friends, difficult people, strangers, animals, plants, and the wider ecosystem, each time pairing gratitude with an awareness of suffering and a wish for peace. By the end, the practice expands into a sense of the body radiating compassion in all directions, until everything is held in that tenderness.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling the Body - The meditation begins by finding ease, uprightness, and an open sensitivity to bodily experience.
  2. 01:48 Benefactor and Compassion - A benefactor is brought to mind, first in gratitude for their kindness and then in compassion for their suffering.
  3. 05:08 Many Who Have Helped - The practice widens to include other kind people, spiritual teachers, and school teachers, with appreciation and compassion for each.
  4. 15:00 Parents and Early Care - The heart turns toward parents and the care they provided, alongside sensitivity to the complexity and pain in those relationships.
  5. 19:06 Friends and Intimacy - Friends and partners are recollected, including the gifts of companionship and intimacy, and their own struggles are held in compassion.
  6. 23:04 Unknown Benefactors - The meditation expands to unknown inventors, scientists, artists, and workers whose efforts shape daily life.
  7. 29:35 Trade and Difficult People - The web of cooperation and exchange is reflected on, then the practice turns toward difficult people and the growth they can evoke.
  8. 36:28 Animals and Ecosystem - Compassion opens to animals, plants, and the fragile interconnectedness of the ecosystem, ending with a spacious radiating of compassion.
  9. 45:10 Practice Options and Close - Burbea offers practical ways to include all beings in compassion and suggests experimenting with categories, directions, or bodily radiance.
