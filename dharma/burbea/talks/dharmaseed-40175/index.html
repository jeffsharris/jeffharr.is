Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Eros Unfettered (Part 5 - Otherness and the Art of Disappearing)

Dharma Seed 1:12:48 2017-01-27

Can eros really be a movement toward union, or does it preserve otherness and keep relationship alive? In this talk, the speaker revisits that question, contrasting soulmaking eros with older ideas of dissolution and then tracing how meditative oneness, imaginal practice, and relationship can coexist without collapsing into final merger. The talk then widens into a sustained reflection on participation: how images, experience, and Dharma may be both discovered and shaped through the way of looking, leaving the listener with a sense of an endless, re-enchanted path.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Eros and otherness - Burbea redefines eros against older union-centered accounts and says it preserves two-ness rather than dissolving it.
  2. 02:02 Oneness and its limits - He examines longing for oneness, distinguishes temporary meditative unions from permanent dissolution, and questions aversion hidden inside transcendence-seeking.
  3. 06:45 Meditative dissolution - He describes jhana, brahmavihara, cessation, and tantric becoming as real but temporary openings that do not erase distinction.
  4. 10:52 Eros keeps tension - The talk develops the idea that erotic tension arises from desire for the other, from the object's increasing richness, and from the fact that more always remains.
  5. 15:38 The birth of us - Burbea describes how eros can constellate a third term, the 'us,' along with child, project, or shared world as new erotic objects.
  6. 23:18 Not the art of disappearing - He contrasts soulmaking eros with the art of disappearing, saying that valuable experiences of no-self or emptiness are not the same as eros.
  7. 27:28 Participation and sacredness - The talk moves into participation: images and eros can be granted sacredness and autonomy, or discovered as such, depending on how they are seen.
  8. 40:59 Ways of knowing - He broadens the discussion into epistemology, bringing in the mundus imaginalis, spiritual sensation, Sufism, and John Archibald Wheeler's participatory universe.
  9. 57:05 Endless Dharma - Burbea returns to Plato and the Buddha to suggest that emptiness, imaginal practice, Mahayana vastness, bodhisattva service, and soulmaking can each open an endless Dharma.
