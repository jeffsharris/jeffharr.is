Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Union of Appearance and Emptiness

Dharma Seed 64:15 2010-02-15

What happens when emptiness is not treated as a theory but as something that changes how solid the world feels? The talk begins with everyday craving and the way thought, memory, association, and mood build up the sense that things really exist, then moves into how mindfulness and equanimity can first sharpen and later dissolve that solidity. From there it opens into the harder questions: what the tradition means by cessation, why this can sound nihilistic, and how the middle way tries to hold both emptiness and causal life without collapsing into either extreme. The talk closes by returning to the union of appearances and emptiness, and to the claim that seeing that deeply makes care, ethics, and reverence more alive, not less.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the spiral - Burbea introduces the evening's thread and returns to the relation between emptiness, equanimity, and experience.
  2. 02:11 How solidity is built - He uses the example of ex-Benedict to show how thinking, memory, association, desire, and social context make things seem substantial.
  3. 05:15 Mindfulness and fading - Mindfulness first sharpens perception, then deepens into equanimity, where the apparent solidity of experience begins to dissolve.
  4. 12:02 What does fading mean? - He asks what we want the teaching to mean and places the discussion inside differing human tendencies toward transcendence, realism, or fear of nihilism.
  5. 17:49 Canon and cessation - Burbea draws on the Pali Canon to describe cessation as more than neutrality and to frame emptiness through the three characteristics.
  6. 23:34 No rebirth of appearances - He explores the implications of deep fading, including the Buddha's refusal to answer postmortem questions and the sense of bowing, reverence, and care that can arise.
  7. 31:47 Theravada and Mahayana - The talk moves through post-Buddha philosophical developments, including Nagarjuna, Chandrakirti, Shantarakshita, and Mipham, toward the union of appearances and emptiness.
  8. 44:32 The razor's edge - Burbea works through the middle way beyond existence and non-existence, warns against both nihilism and subtle reification, and uses the Heart Sutra and Tsongkhapa to rebalance the view.
  9. 59:07 Freedom with cause and effect - He closes by insisting that seeing emptiness more deeply increases freedom, ethics, and reverence for life rather than diminishing them.
