Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Fifth Instructions and Guided Metta Meditation

Dharma Seed 43:37 2011-02-10

How do you widen metta beyond the people the heart already likes? The talk works through the move from easy categories to the neutral person, noting boredom, drift, and the need for extra care, then reframes the practice through shared humanity, vulnerability, mortality, and ordinary joy. It then settles into a guided meditation that begins with the body, uses simple phrases, and gradually invites a neutral person into the field of warm well-wishing without forcing the heart.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Introducing the neutral person - Burbea explains how the neutral person enters metta practice and why this category often feels hard to notice at first.
  2. 03:26 Shared humanity - He develops reflections on common desires, suffering, mortality, and vulnerability as ways of widening the heart.
  3. 08:50 Phrases and flexibility - He discusses how metta can be carried in ordinary life, and how repeated phrases can stay alive by shifting in meaning.
  4. 12:13 Stretching the heart - Burbea contrasts the narrative self with the simpler felt sense of a being, and speaks about balancing ease with deliberate stretching.
  5. 15:46 Self, friend, and shadow - He turns to the difficulty of self-metta, warning against getting stuck in self-focus while also affirming its healing value.
  6. 18:12 Settling into practice - The guided meditation begins with posture, body awareness, and gently sustained metta phrases that open into the whole body.
  7. 27:17 Neutral person meditation - He invites the image of a neutral person and offers reflections and phrases that include their difficulties, joys, and wish for peace.
  8. 39:41 Closing and return - The practice softens into a final sense of warmth, simple pleasures, and a return to the body with care.
