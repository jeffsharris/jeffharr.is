Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Instructions: Letting Go

Dharma Seed 27:27 2017-03-28

Letting go is presented here not as a single move, but as one thread among several: nourishment, emotional care, imaginal practice, and renunciation all have to be woven together. The talk then lays out four ways of working with desire and craving: return to what nourishes, look beneath desire to the feeling underneath, give craving space until it peaks and passes, and reflect on whether the object will really satisfy. In the later part, the speaker turns to impermanence and then to a practical exercise with mealtimes and the lunch queue, using food to notice craving, fear of scarcity, and the difference between raw sensation and the mind’s story.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Expanding the path - He introduces the retreat’s larger frame: practice can include samadhi, emotional care, and imaginal work, and the art is learning how to move among these threads.
  2. 02:58 Renunciation as a thread - He adds renunciation to the picture and distinguishes between trusting desire at times and deliberately cooling things down by letting them go.
  3. 06:20 Return to nourishment - The first way of working with desire is to come back to what nourishes, such as loving-kindness and samadhi, especially when grasping comes from a low reservoir.
  4. 11:23 Care for hidden pain - He suggests that desire often covers loneliness, grief, boredom, or heartache, and that turning toward those emotions with care can soften the pull.
  5. 13:42 Give craving space - The third possibility is to notice craving as dukkha, feel its pressure in the body, and create enough space for it to rise and subside without being acted on.
  6. 17:37 Reflect on desire - He offers reflective practices: asking whether the object is really wanted, remembering how often satisfaction has been partial, and seeing how impermanence changes the picture.
  7. 19:50 Impermanence and dukkha - He broadens the view with impermanence and a temporary dukkha-based way of looking, using them as contemplative gears that support letting go.
  8. 24:06 Eating as practice - He ends with a practical exercise for lunch: watch craving in the queue, notice fear and taking, and pay close attention to taste, smell, and the gap between sensation and story.
