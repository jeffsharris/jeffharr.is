Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Way of Image

Dharma Seed 1:33:09 2019-05-25

What counts as an image, and when does it become genuinely imaginal? The talk starts by laying out the foundations of Soulmaking Dharma: how ordinary Dharma capacities like mindfulness, metta, samatha, and emotional skill can become roots in their own right, and how clear definitions matter without freezing the practice into something rigid. From there it moves into distinctions that protect the imaginal from being confused with shamanism, energy healing, or ESP, then opens out into many examples of non-visual imagery: compassion sensed through chant, music as a god with a claim on life, a dream of dying soon, a chest opening like a Torah ark, and angelic ministration at the threshold of death.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening frame - Rob sets out the aims of the series and explains that this talk will introduce the terrain of imaginal practice, its roots, and its relation to broader Dharma.
  2. 06:57 Foundations become roots - He shows how ordinary Dharma capacities like mindfulness, metta, samatha, and emotional skill are not only prerequisites but can be drawn into soulmaking themselves.
  3. 11:40 What soul means - Rob offers a working definition of soul as the part that senses in soul-making ways and wants soul-making, while holding the definition lightly enough for it to grow.
  4. 17:32 Dyad and boundaries - The talk expands from individual practice to the idea of a 'soul of the dyad,' then carefully distinguishes imaginal practice from shamanism, channeling, energy healing, and related fields.
  5. 24:21 Imaginal middle way - He clarifies that image and imaginal are not just private inner pictures, and introduces the middle way between literal belief and dismissal.
  6. 31:32 Three kinds of image - Rob distinguishes fixated images, mindful observation of images, and images that become genuinely imaginal, then gives examples showing that image is not necessarily visual.
  7. 44:30 Music as a god - A long personal example shows grief about leaving music deepen into reverence, surrender, and a sense of music as an autonomous, soul-level presence.
  8. 01:00:46 Death, sacredness, land - He turns to dream fragments, an imaginal sense of dying, and a car ride in which the chest, the Torah ark, life and death, and the land itself take on sacred resonance.
  9. 01:09:10 Soft edges and refinement - The closing section returns to non-visual compassion and angels, then broadens into teaching about spectra, dimmer switches, trust, refining view, and the need for lightness around labels.
