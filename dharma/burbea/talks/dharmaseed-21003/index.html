All In This Together

Dharma Seed 57:20 2013-10-07

Catherine McGee begins from Earth Care Week and uses it to open a wider question: how inner practice and outer responsibility belong to the same field. She moves through environmental examples, the experience of loneliness and resonance on retreat, and teachings on non-separation, showing how clear seeing can lead to compassionate but unforced action. The talk closes by widening into bodhisattva aspiration, with a strong emphasis on community, humility, and responding appropriately in changing conditions.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Earth Care Frame - Catherine opens by situating the talk in Earth Care Week and naming the larger environmental context behind the retreat theme.
  2. 04:14 Inner and Outer - She asks how Dharma practice meets overwhelming global suffering without collapsing into guilt, urgency, or helplessness.
  3. 10:06 We Are the Environment - Using John Francis as an example, she shows how restraint, silence, and sustained attention can become a direct response to ecological harm.
  4. 13:41 No Separation - She deepens the teaching that inner and outer are not truly separate, and that action affects the whole even when it seems small.
  5. 20:10 Resonance and Loneliness - A story from retreat about a bucket and the need to be seen opens into the heart-mind's longing for resonance and its capacity to turn back toward itself.
  6. 30:52 Emptying Out - She turns to the Buddha as 'awake' and to emptiness as the clearing of greed, hate, and confusion so responsiveness can arise without strain.
  7. 37:23 Who Hears? - Kuan Yin and the question 'who hears?' guide the talk into compassionate listening that can hold both inner cries and the cries of the world.
  8. 43:58 Community Awakening - She weaves in Thich Nhat Hanh, Siddhartha, and Zen teaching to describe awakening as collective, intimate, and responsive to changing conditions.
  9. 53:58 Bodhisattva Vow - The talk closes with Shantideva's aspiration to be medicine, shelter, and sustenance for beings until freedom, ending in shared sitting.
