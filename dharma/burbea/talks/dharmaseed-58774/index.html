Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Sila and Soul (Part 1)

Dharma Seed 1:45:52 2019-06-09

Rob Burbea opens this first part of a talk on sīla by asking what ethics means in a modern Buddhist and Soulmaking context, and why the subject is necessarily unfinished. He contrasts inherited moral frameworks from Abrahamic religion, Western modernity, and Buddhism, then widens the frame to include climate, globalization, democracy, and the loss of moral education. In the second half, he turns toward Soulmaking Dharma, arguing that imaginal practice can deepen ethical sensibility rather than bypass it, while also acknowledging the real contradictions of moral life.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening the subject - Burbea introduces ethics as an endless field of inquiry and says the talk will only be an incomplete beginning.
  2. 03:28 Buddhist and Western frames - He contrasts the Buddhist framing of skillful and unskillful action with Abrahamic good-and-evil morality and the effect of self-judgment.
  3. 09:16 Precepts and simplification - He explains how sila and the five precepts are meant to calm and simplify the mind rather than intensify moral worry.
  4. 13:38 Culture, guilt, and character - Using Thich Nhat Hanh and then wider cultural analysis, he traces the mix of inherited morality, individualism, inner critic, and Dharma archetypes.
  5. 24:33 MacIntyre and modernity - Drawing on Alasdair MacIntyre, he describes the narrowing and fragmentation of morality through modern philosophy, law, and postmodern relativism.
  6. 37:30 Rorty, democracy, and power - He critiques Richard Rorty’s pragmatist emphasis on conversation, then examines democracy, manipulation, and the instability of shared values.
  7. 54:33 Global ethics and moral education - Burbea turns to globalization, capitalism, climate, and the loss of moral education, arguing that ethics now has global consequences and needs rethinking.
  8. 01:17:58 Vinaya and meditation - He uses vinaya as an example of moral education in the Buddhist tradition and links ethics directly to the settling needed for jhana.
  9. 01:36:46 Soulmaking and moral life - He closes by showing how imaginal practice, values, and the polytheistic psyche can enrich ethical life while still leaving it complex and conflicted.
