Ayya Santacitta, Ayya Santussika, David Loy, Thanissara: Mindfulness and Climate Action : 5

Dharma Seed 68:40 2014-11-02

This episode closes a five-part series on mindfulness, Dharma, and climate action with a practical question: how can Buddhist practice become collective response? David Loy frames the climate crisis as a challenge to reexamine Buddhism itself, while Thanissara and Ayya Santussika move the conversation toward organizing, public pressure, and faith-based networks. The talk stays grounded in both inner practice and concrete action, ending with reflections on joy, courage, and continuing the work together.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the final call - Thanissara opens the last session of the series, notes Bhikkhu Bodhi’s absence, and names the talk’s focus on turning reflection into action.
  2. 01:24 What can we actually do? - Lu introduces the central question and points to hope through existing signs of change, especially renewable energy and political engagement.
  3. 06:15 Reexamining Buddhist practice - David Loy argues that Buddhism needs to clarify what awakening means in a climate crisis, challenging both transcendence-as-escape and purely psychological inwardness.
  4. 13:32 The bodhisattva path - Loy develops the bodhisattva path as a double practice of meditation and engagement, linking wisdom, compassion, and the willingness to act without certainty of results.
  5. 20:31 Building a Buddhist movement - Ayya Santussika describes how Buddhists can organize locally and nationally, drawing on the People's Climate March, faith coalitions, divestment, and voter engagement.
  6. 34:41 Precepts, urgency, and earth - The discussion turns to ethical responsibility, the first two precepts, climate deadlines, fossil-fuel dependence, and the Buddha touching the earth as a model for fearless speech and action.
  7. 47:20 Questions from the sangha - Listeners respond with gratitude, questions about resources, and stories of being moved into new forms of leadership, including local teaching and voting.
  8. 58:07 Joy, continuity, blessing - The closing section returns to joy as a condition for sustained action, outlines plans for monthly follow-up calls, and ends with a Buddhist blessing.
