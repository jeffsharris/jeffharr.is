The Love and Demands of the Imaginal (Part 1)

Dharma Seed 62:18 2015-08-11

What if an image is not a metaphor but a living presence that can love, demand, and stretch us beyond the ordinary self? The talk moves through examples of healing, erotic charge, creative work, and shame to ask how imaginal figures shape practice when they are seen as real in a non-literal, dependent-arising way. It keeps returning to the question of discernment: when is an image asking for action, when is it asking only to be witnessed, and what happens when we honor its autonomy without making it concrete?

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Image, not metaphor - He begins by distinguishing image from ordinary metaphor, stressing aliveness, autonomy, and richness.
  2. 02:25 Love and demand - He opens the themes of love, necessity, and demand, and shows how they arise through a way of looking.
  3. 04:27 Middle way, not literalism - He places imaginal practice between scientific proof and nihilistic dismissal, comparing it more to art than to propositions.
  4. 08:30 Healing old wounds - Through a retreatant's story, he shows an image working directly with early trauma and bodily memory.
  5. 11:37 Authority and holiness - He turns to Thich Nhat Hanh, then to the tramp and Christ, to show how images can carry authority, outcast-ness, and hidden sanctity.
  6. 17:22 Compassion and eros - A guided compassion practice unexpectedly opens into erotic imaginal experience, revealing how self and image can interpenetrate.
  7. 22:47 Being stretched - He discusses how imaginal figures can demand a trial, and why liquidity and emptiness are needed to keep the view non-literal.
  8. 28:11 What is healing? - He pauses on healing, then moves through Aphrodite-like beauty and the gradual widening of sacredness through witnessing.
  9. 34:57 Sculptor and Coltrane - He uses a master sculptor and John Coltrane as examples of demanding, loving presences that shape creative work.
