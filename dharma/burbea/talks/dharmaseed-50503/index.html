What is Awakening? (Part 4)

Dharma Seed 1:37:39 2018-01-08

What if awakening is not a single breakthrough, but a widening process in which fabrication, perception, eros, and ideas keep opening and being reworked? The talk moves from a phenomenological inquiry into fabrication and ways of looking, to a critique of the myth of sudden, total enlightenment, and then into the soulmaking paradigm, where awakening includes deconstruction, reconstruction, and the ongoing creation of more spacious images and concepts. Along the way, it questions fixed ideas of liberation, brings in philosophy and theology, and ends by reading Genesis as a call to leave stagnation and keep moving into new territory.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Fabrication and looking - He begins by revisiting the double question of fabrication and ways of looking as a practical, phenomenological way into experience.
  2. 03:30 A possible awakening arc - He outlines a developmental arc through personality, oneness, emptiness, and the non-duality of fabrication and the unfabricated.
  3. 07:15 Why linear maps fail - He questions whether that arc is actually attractive or true, then qualifies it by describing realization as non-linear and leaping.
  4. 10:05 Soulmaking joins in - He weaves in soulmaking and the erotic imaginal, showing how eros and image broaden the earlier emptiness-based path.
  5. 16:47 The myth of sudden awakening - Using the Buddha’s awakening story, he examines the appeal of sudden, total enlightenment and why it can mislead.
  6. 19:55 Freedom in wider domains - He argues that freedom may open in sexuality, intellect, creativity, and public courage without being captured by narrow spiritual ideals.
  7. 25:38 Concepts must be rebuilt - He develops the role of ideas in practice, bringing in Bernstein, Habermas, Popper, and Jung to argue that deconstruction must be followed by reconstruction.
  8. 39:45 Idols and icons - He contrasts rigid idols with living icons, suggesting that awakening images must remain soft, dimensional, and revisable.
  9. 54:02 Go from here - He returns to the question of who awakens, links awakening with cosmology and desire, and ends with Genesis as a call to keep moving from fixation into new soulmaking territory.
