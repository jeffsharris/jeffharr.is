Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Aspects of Mindfulness (Part 1)

Dharma Seed 27:24 2006-04-23

What is mindfulness, really: a way of being present, or a tool for seeing how the mind gets caught? The talk moves from a broad framing of the Buddhist path into a careful unpacking of attention, self-inquiry, and the places we most often suffer—body, feeling, thought, intention, and habitual reaction. It then becomes practical, contrasting bare attention with clear comprehension and emphasizing that meditation is less about eliminating thought than about repeatedly noticing drift and returning without judgment.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the theme - Burbea welcomes the retreat and introduces mindfulness as the theme for the day within the insight tradition.
  2. 03:43 Mindfulness and awareness - He explains mindfulness as attention and self-awareness, and why that capacity matters for practice.
  3. 06:57 What to attend to - The talk turns to the Buddha's emphasis on body, feeling, mind, behavior, and intention as the areas where suffering is most clearly met.
  4. 11:55 Presence, not emptiness - Burbea clarifies that mindfulness is not about getting rid of thought, but about knowing what is present in mind, body, and sense experience.
  5. 15:50 Holding one thing in mind - He describes mindfulness as keeping an object in mind for investigation and for calming the heart, with a brief aside on human skill and what is worth cultivating.
  6. 19:06 Bare attention and clarity - Burbea explains two classic dimensions of mindfulness: direct, non-conceptual contact with experience and clear comprehension of what one is doing.
  7. 22:47 Practice and repetition - He stresses that mindfulness is a practice, and that noticing distraction and returning without judgment is itself the training.
  8. 26:24 Patience with wandering - The closing section reassures listeners that a wandering mind is normal, and that repeatedly bringing it back is how the mind becomes stronger and brighter.
