Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

At Home in the Universe

Dharma Seed 1:15:09 2011-08-02

Rob Burbea explores what it might mean to feel at home in existence, starting from modern alienation and moving through self-relationship, community, the body, the earth, and the mind. He then widens the frame into death, big-picture views, and a participatory universe, using both Dharma and modern physics to question the sense that reality is separate from the way we see it. The talk closes with a quiet but radical claim: that non-separateness and emptiness can make home possible even in what seems difficult or impure.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Alienation Everywhere - He begins by tracing a long Western history of alienation and names the talk’s central question: whether practice can lead to a deep sense of belonging.
  2. 04:16 Home With Experience - He turns to the four foundations of mindfulness and the way relationship to feelings, thoughts, and the body can heal disconnection.
  3. 07:59 Sangha and Trust - The talk emphasizes friendship, therapy, teaching, and community as conditions that allow shame and woundedness to be met safely.
  4. 13:52 Body and Elements - He develops the theme of bodily at-homeness through the body as nature, the five elements, and the image of bowing to the earth at dawn.
  5. 19:55 Earth, Convenience, Pilgrimage - He contrasts consumer comfort with pilgrim-like movement through life and asks what values shape our sense of the earth as home.
  6. 27:34 Identity and Social Pressure - He looks closely at modern individualism, identity, self-measurement, and social anxiety, suggesting a more spacious way of understanding personality.
  7. 39:25 Death and Big Picture - He broadens the frame to mortality and existential suffering, arguing that practice needs both meditative precision and a larger view of existence.
  8. 49:15 Mystical Home - He describes a mystical sense of home, drawing on Jesus and the Buddha to suggest that alienation can be met by a deeper dimension of reality.
  9. 59:52 Space, Physics, Emptiness - He closes with the opening of space, then moves into modern physics, Pooh, and a sutra passage to show how observation, non-separation, and emptiness reshape what is taken to be real.
