Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Wisdom, Art, Balance (Part 4)

Dharma Seed 1:18:51 2017-01-31

What if the problem is not desire itself, but the way it gets flattened into something only personal, only literal, or only to be let go? The talk moves through cases of unavailable love, shame, grief, and devotional longing to show how opening to the imaginal can enlarge self, other, and world instead of contracting them. It then turns to balance: not suppressing eros, but learning how to include the self in the field, let the image become more dimensional, and sometimes perceive desire as already happening in a sacred time.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Eros and balance - Burbea frames the talk as a question of how to work with eros so it becomes fertile and soulmaking rather than merely alternating between fire and steadiness.
  2. 02:16 The unreachable beloved - He offers a relational example in which painful attraction can be entered imaginally, enlarging the other beyond a flat, concrete reality.
  3. 07:34 Beyond 'let go' - He questions the simplicity of the usual advice to let go and suggests that, in complex situations, opening to the image may be more fruitful.
  4. 16:11 A transforming story - A long student example shows frustration, inferiority, and longing opening into vivid animal-erotic imagery, connection, and holiness.
  5. 22:49 Include the self - He argues that balance in the soul-making dynamic requires seeing not only the beloved other but also the beauty and eros of the self.
  6. 28:13 Forest and benefactors - A retreatant’s account moves from grief with trees and cold air into specific imaginal love from several benefactors, showing pain opening into care.
  7. 36:07 Love in the imaginal - Burbea draws out the lesson that imaginal perception already contains love and that love can flow both from the image and back toward the practitioner.
  8. 47:06 A balanced constellation - He broadens the teaching to include object, self, world, and eros, warning against reification and showing how multidimensionality loosens fixation.
  9. 01:04:49 Desire, divinity, and time - Using Blake and Henri Corbin, he explores desire as divine influx, desire as already happening in sacred time, and then contrasts this with an anatta reading that makes desire fade.
