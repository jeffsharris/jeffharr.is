Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Samadhi in the Practice of Emptiness

Dharma Seed 44:40 2009-01-24

What if samadhi is less about microscopic focus and more about a gathered, unified mind that feels spacious, steady, and workable? The talk moves from correcting common ideas about concentration to showing how samadhi supports emptiness practice: it cushions fear, steadies investigation, and keeps insight from tipping into dryness or nihilism. Along the way, the speaker treats meditation as a learnable skill shaped by happiness, patience, playfulness, and whole-body awareness, with breath and metta as the main ways in.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 What Samadhi Means - He defines samadhi, contrasts it with narrow concentration, and argues that it is a gathered, unified, spacious quality of mind and body.
  2. 03:39 Why It Matters - Using a Buddha simile and the retreat context, he explains why samadhi strongly supports awakening and should be cultivated alongside insight and metta.
  3. 09:49 Climate for Inquiry - He describes samadhi as the emotional and cognitive atmosphere that lets emptiness work go deeper without becoming frightening or nihilistic.
  4. 11:57 Beyond Tight Focusing - He re-frames mindfulness as noticing when the mind has wandered and emphasizes the larger picture, rather than treating every lapse as failure.
  5. 18:57 A Learnable Skill - Samadhi is presented as a skill that develops through patience, happiness, kindness, experimentation, and the willingness to learn.
  6. 21:48 Working With Hindrances - He names common obstacles like dullness, restlessness, doubt, judgment, and impatience, and shows how to meet them without taking them personally.
  7. 28:33 Body and Tightness - The talk turns to whole-body awareness, subtle contraction, and how relaxing the body can release tightness in the practice.
  8. 32:42 Subtle Body and Insight - He develops the idea of subtle body energy, explaining how insight and emptiness can help unblock it and how samadhi and insight reinforce each other.
  9. 39:23 Practical Guidance - He closes by outlining the retreat plan, clarifying what he means by samadhi practice, and encouraging people to find the approach that actually works.
