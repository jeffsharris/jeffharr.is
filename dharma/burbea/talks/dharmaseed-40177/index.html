From Mindfulness to Divinity: Towards the Tracing of a Phenomenology of Soul (Part 2)

Dharma Seed 1:41:55 2017-02-20

What happens when mindfulness keeps widening until it touches eros, imagination, and the sense that anything perceived is already fabricated? The talk traces a path from dependent origination and emptiness into states of less fabrication, then into the way divinity, soulmaking, and the imaginal can arise as lived experience rather than abstract belief. From there it turns to the self, the beloved, and the world as participating in a double movement: toward transcendence and toward the rich particularity of manifestation.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Phenomenology of practice - Burbea frames meditation as an inquiry into how ways of looking fabricate experience and how practice can gradually lessen fabrication.
  2. 04:26 Stages of divinity - He describes different kinds of oneness and divinity, from universal love and consciousness to the unfabricated and emptiness.
  3. 09:31 Opening the imaginal - Seeing the emptiness of all things loosens the real/unreal split and creates room to explore the imaginal without crude reduction.
  4. 14:19 Eros and soulmaking - He turns to eros, fantasy, and soulmaking as active forces in ordinary life and as a practical guide for working with blocked or limited practice.
  5. 25:16 The self as image - Using Corbin, Panagiotis Nellas, and Meister Eckhart, he develops the image of the self as a divine image or theophany rather than a flat identity.
  6. 33:15 Beauty and divine mind - Aquinas and the phrase 'my soul doth magnify the Lord' are used to show how beauty, soul, and divinity arise together in perception.
  7. 48:57 Two movements of eros - Burbea contrasts eros toward the unfabricated with eros toward soulmaking fabrication, and names the distortions or near enemies of each movement.
  8. 01:04:57 Dependent origination and matter - A philosophical question about matter leads into dependent origination, subject-object-time, and the claim that these teachings are not temporal explanations.
  9. 01:19:17 Eternity and time - He returns to the idea that phenomenology can arrive at what sounds like metaphysics: eternity, divine mind, and the productions of time participating in one another.
