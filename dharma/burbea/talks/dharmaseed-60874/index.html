Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Q & A

Dharma Seed 1:27:48 2019-12-27

In this Q&A, Rob Burbea works through how to relate to ringing in the ears, first as something not to fight, then as a possible object for concentration or insight. From there he turns to subtlety, pīti, and the role of primary and secondary nimittas, before widening the discussion to guilt around happiness, service, and the need for nourishment in practice. The talk closes with a broader frame: jhana as a way to open the whole range of experience, and with a practical exercise for tracing hindrances before they harden into papañca.

Chapters

  1. 01:42 Ringing and aversion - He lays out several ways to relate to ringing in the ears, emphasizing not fighting it and watching the subtle aversion that can amplify it.
  2. 05:22 Sound as concentration - He explains how the sound can become a refined concentration object, including the role of the highest pitch, the body in the background, and pīti.
  3. 09:34 Sound as insight - He shifts to using the sound as a backdrop that highlights impermanence, while warning against taking it as an ultimate or cosmic truth.
  4. 14:42 Subtlety and refinement - He untangles subtlety, intensity, and refinement, and reframes the practical question as how to support the experience and where the practice is going.
  5. 25:01 Questions from practice - He responds to retreat questions about confusion in practice and bodily movements in jhana, normalizing them as part of energetic opening.
  6. 33:10 Joy and service - He addresses guilt around happiness, argues that suffering arises from many conditions, and presents jhana as nourishment for broader service in the world.
  7. 48:14 Perception and emptiness - He explains how perceiving everything through a nimitta-like quality points toward dependent origination and emptiness rather than a fixed altered state.
  8. 53:07 The bandwidth of happiness - He uses the library analogy to stress knowing the whole range of sukha, from bubbly joy to serenity, and letting that range work on the being.
  9. 01:10:08 Hindrances and spaciousness - He offers a light exercise of tracing circling thoughts back to hindrances, then closes by widening the frame to emotional range, mystery, and what practice is for.
