Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Doubt and Discernment (Part 2)

Dharma Seed 1:20:06 2017-02-06

Rob Burbea continues a close examination of doubt, asking how it can become a workable instrument of discernment rather than a source of paralysis. He contrasts eros and craving, then extends the same kind of discrimination to rest, metta, imaginal practice, and jhana, showing how each can be present in ways that are either soulmaking or merely comforting. The talk keeps returning to emptiness and dependent origination as a basis for letting images, longing, and practice be taken seriously without being reified.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Practice as preparation - He opens by naming the skills that make this investigation possible: mindfulness, energy body work, emotion, and insight into emptiness.
  2. 02:07 Eros or craving - He develops the central distinction between eros and craving, emphasizing how eros opens the energy body and craving contracts it.
  3. 05:46 Wise effort - Using the Buddha’s four bases of success and examples from practice, he shows how desire and effort can be handled without either strain or passivity.
  4. 09:58 Questioning and experiment - He encourages daring, careful experimentation, and notes that eros and craving can become tangled without that meaning the whole practice should be discarded.
  5. 14:08 Imaginal autonomy - He turns to Piaget, then offers a story of an imaginal figure acting with its own intelligence, to show why the imaginal is not just wish fulfillment.
  6. 19:09 Not realism, but relation - With James Hillman and many examples, he argues that the main danger is identification, realism, and objectification, not the mere presence of strong images.
  7. 32:27 Emptiness as basis - He places emptiness at the foundation of the path and reframes the issue as one of efficacy and potency rather than reality claims.
  8. 47:05 Rest and jhana - He distinguishes shutting down from resting in living relationship, then compares imaginal aliveness with states of less fabrication and jhana.
  9. 01:01:37 Longing and devotion - He closes by asking where the imaginal is alive in our lives, and by treating longing as a clue to both personal desire and what we are called to serve.
