Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

A Rite of Angels

Dharma Seed 64:28 2019-03-24

How do you practice when the mind keeps measuring what’s missing? The talk begins with a quiet return to gratitude: Buddha, Dharma, Sangha, the lineage behind practice, and the many supports that have carried a person to the cushion. It then turns into a more elaborate retreat ritual of walking, watching, chanting, and blessing, where others are met first with welcoming and metta, then increasingly as unique beings, and finally as angel or Buddha nature. The movement is from self-criticism toward a more expansive way of seeing: body, voice, soul, limitation, and potential held together in one field of practice.

Chapters

  1. 01:02 Opening the ritual - He introduces the Triple Gem, lights candles, and frames the evening as a practice of time and the timeless.
  2. 02:15 Resting on support - The talk turns into a reflective practice on how practice rests on lineage, teachers, and one’s own long effort.
  3. 11:42 Setting up the circle - He explains the walking-circle exercise and the basic intentions of welcoming and metta.
  4. 19:19 Seeing the walker - The practice expands into sensing another person’s dukkha, uniqueness, and possible angelic or Buddha-nature presence.
  5. 27:00 Chant and form - He teaches the chant, linking OM AH HUNG to body, speech, and mind, and AH MI THA to limitless emptiness.
  6. 36:40 Blessing and resonance - As the chanting deepens, he invites the room, earth, night, and community into a field of blessing.
  7. 46:04 Angel gaze - The chant continues through themes of mercy, soul, limitation transformed, and the patient gaze of angels.
  8. 59:54 Closing vision - He closes by naming the possibility of perceiving oneself and others as angelic or Buddha nature, and of both receiving and giving blessing.
