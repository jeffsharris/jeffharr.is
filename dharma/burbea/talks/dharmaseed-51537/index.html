Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Of Hunger and Humility

Dharma Seed 16:01 2018-06-29

How do you keep a practice alive without forcing it? The talk moves from gratitude for the retreat into a careful picture of soulmaking as something natural that grows when it is not blocked by narrow ideas, limited energy, or relational carelessness. It then turns to the practical tension at the center of the teaching: stay hungry for what opens the path, but meet that hunger with discernment, humility, and attention to the vessel that can hold it.

Chapters

  1. 00:13 Opening Gratitude - Burbea thanks the retreat participants and notes that different people will have related to the material in different ways.
  2. 02:05 Natural Soulmaking - He describes soulmaking as something the soul wants and does naturally when it is not blocked.
  3. 05:17 Ranges And Openings - The talk turns to the earlier discussion of five ranges and the idea that growth can open in distinct domains without being forced.
  4. 08:04 Careful Discernment - He stresses that practice is often diverted into familiar or narrower forms, so attention and discrimination matter.
  5. 10:00 Beauty And Desire - Burbea broadens soulmaking beyond any single category, linking it with beauty, love, imagination, and desire.
  6. 11:57 The Relational Vessel - He turns to the importance of the relational field, the temenos, and the fragile vessel that needs care in practice with others.
  7. 14:01 Insatiable Yet Careful - The closing counsel frames the path as open-ended investigation: bring creativity and humility, and hold hunger together with careful discernment.
  8. 15:15 Hunger And Humility - He lands on the image of two wings—fierce appetite and exquisite humility—before closing the talk.
