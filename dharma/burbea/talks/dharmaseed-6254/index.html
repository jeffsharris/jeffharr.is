Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

What is ‘Insight’?

Dharma Seed 64:04 2009-05-03

What is insight, really: a passing understanding, a useful way of looking, or something that can cut deeper into suffering itself? The talk starts with a simple map and then moves through personal, universal, and ultimate insight, showing how each can lessen dukkha and how some insights need to be used and repeated until they become part of the way we see. It then turns to the three characteristics—impermanence, dukkha, and not-self—as deliberate lenses, before opening into dependent origination, emptiness, and the middle way beyond the habit of making things either solidly real or unreal.

Chapters

  1. 00:14 Opening the map - Burbea frames the talk as an overview of insight and invites listeners to notice how they relate to ideas, goals, and the larger picture.
  2. 03:36 What insight means - He defines insight as any seeing that lessens suffering, and distinguishes between personal insight and the patterns, strengths, and stories that shape our lives.
  3. 12:33 Universal truths - The talk turns to truths that apply to everyone, including impermanence, generosity, kindness, precepts, and the way repeated understanding becomes stable.
  4. 15:53 Relative and ultimate - Burbea contrasts the relative world of body, mind, and relationships with ultimate insight, and then explains how sila and samadhi support insight arising.
  5. 20:49 Using and consolidating - He describes two modes: insights that arise naturally in practice, and insights that are deliberately reinforced by choosing and seeing from their perspective.
  6. 26:09 Learning ways of seeing - The talk argues that mindfulness alone is often not enough, because liberation depends on developing skillful modes of seeing that weaken greed, aversion, and delusion.
  7. 31:25 Three characteristics - Using the Four Noble Truths and the three characteristics, he shows how practitioners can investigate suffering through impermanence, dukkha, and not-self as active lenses.
  8. 42:19 Beyond fabrication - Burbea moves from viewing phenomena as unsatisfactory to seeing how push-pull and identification construct experience, leading toward the unfabricated and the unborn.
  9. 52:52 Middle way and emptiness - He closes by tying arising and passing away to dependent origination and the middle way, avoiding both nihilism and reification.
