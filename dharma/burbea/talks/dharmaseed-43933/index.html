Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Awakening and Soulmaking (Q & A)

Dharma Seed 38:17 2017-03-31

Can awakening still be possible when long sits, frequent retreats, or a fixed practice style are not available? The talk moves from that practical worry into a widening view of meditation as flexible ways of looking, where insight can arise in walking, in nature, in relationship, and even outside formal practice. From there it turns to jhana, imaginal practice, eros, and devotion, asking how these threads can open both samadhi and a more expansive soulmaking path without reducing any of them to a single formula.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the question - A listener describes the limits of her current practice and asks whether awakening is still possible without long sitting or retreat.
  2. 05:55 Beyond the cushion - Rob widens the meaning of meditation and argues that realization is not confined to one posture, one retreat format, or one body condition.
  3. 09:34 Jhana without dogma - He challenges the idea that jhana is a required ladder to insight and speaks cautiously about teacher authority and fixed path models.
  4. 13:52 The unfabricated nearby - Rob says the perfume of the unfabricated can appear in ordinary life and may bring both faith and liberation in small ways.
  5. 15:52 Image into samadhi - A question about a recurring imaginal image leads into a discussion of how image, beauty, and energy-body resonance can tip into jhana or samadhi.
  6. 24:35 Body and image - Another listener asks whether the body can be primary, and Rob treats body, image, and experience as different but fertile starting points.
  7. 28:30 No single framework - He uses emptiness to argue that science, body-based inquiry, or any other framework can be fruitful without being ultimate.
  8. 30:07 Eros and devotion - Rob develops eros as a method of inquiry, links it with devotion and bhakti, and describes soulmaking as a process that expands and sacralizes experience.
  9. 37:19 Closing exchange - The talk ends lightly with a listener's question about bare attention, followed by a brief, quiet close.
