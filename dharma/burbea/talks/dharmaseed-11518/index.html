Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Third Morning Instructions

Dharma Seed 18:42 2008-08-11

Rob Burbea offers practical instructions for working with concentration when the meditation goes through ups and downs. He emphasizes attitude, appreciation, patience, and contentment, then turns to how to meet thoughts, subtle restlessness, boredom, and the balance between too much effort and too little. The talk closes by opening into a more refined sense of breath energy in and around the body, leading into a sitting practice.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Expect the waves - He begins by normalizing the ups and downs of practice and stressing that an expectant, caring attitude matters from the start.
  2. 02:50 Appreciation and patience - The talk turns to appreciating practice, being patient with slow understanding, and staying content with what is pleasant.
  3. 04:19 Working with thoughts - He describes renouncing unhelpful thoughts, seeing their limited reward, and noticing how subtle curiosity can feed the mind's hunger.
  4. 07:10 Restlessness and boredom - Burbea explores the faint stirrings that lead away from the breath, and how boredom often appears when sensitivity to the present moment drops.
  5. 11:18 Lightness of attention - He discusses the right degree of effort, using lightness in the body and attention to help the mind stay settled without strain.
  6. 15:25 Breath energy field - The final section opens into a more porous sense of body and breath energy all around and within the body, ending with instructions to sit.
