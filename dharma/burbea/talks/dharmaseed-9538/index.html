Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

A Wise Relationship To Practice

Dharma Seed 1:17:55 2010-01-28

How do you relate to practice: as kindness, as measurement, or as a way of proving yourself? The talk follows that question into effort, showing how aspiration, confidence, joy, and rest can support practice when they are balanced, and how the inner critic can quietly distort the whole path. It then turns to progress, difficulty, and emptiness, asking how to keep questioning without slipping into self-judgment, complacency, or fear.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the question - Rob frames the retreat as a set of talks and asks how we relate to practice as a whole.
  2. 04:20 Practice as kindness - He emphasizes the beauty and nobility of practice, and warns against turning it into self-criticism or measurement.
  3. 06:43 What effort means - He begins unpacking effort in practice, arguing for a wise relationship that does not split practice off from life.
  4. 08:54 Aspiration and balance - Using Shantideva, he describes healthy effort as resting on aspiration, confidence, joy, and rest.
  5. 13:37 Confidence and progress - He discusses how confidence grows through sense-making and real signs of progress, including reduced suffering and self-sense.
  6. 17:38 Deepening without strain - He expands the discussion to progress, development, and the need to trust experience while staying open to further refinement.
  7. 30:17 Difficulty as fertilizer - He explores how suffering, discomfort, and even doubt can become material for awakening when met without self-judgment.
  8. 46:36 Keeping practice alive - He turns to practical ways of nourishing practice over time: joy, courage, playfulness, risk-taking, and meeting fear as self softens.
  9. 01:07:36 Anatta as a way of looking - He clarifies that anatta is less about labeling than about a different relationship to experience that brings relief and less clinging.
