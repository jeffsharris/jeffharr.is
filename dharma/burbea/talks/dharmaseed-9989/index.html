Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Selflessness and a Life of Love

Dharma Seed 68:49 2010-08-10

Rob Burbea reflects on mortality, apathy, and the question of how to live a life of love. He moves through social and historical examples of responsibility and care, then turns toward emptiness as a way of loosening fixed self-view and allowing love to become steadier and less fearful. The talk ends by showing how metta, spaciousness, and the perception of emptiness can support a more radical and enduring compassion.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Mortality and the question - He begins with a walk through a graveyard and uses mortality to ask how we want to live.
  2. 05:38 Against docility - He explores apathy, social passivity, and the danger of becoming spiritually or politically harmless.
  3. 11:26 Radical teachers of love - He draws on Jesus and Shantideva, along with Frankl and others, to show the depth of human freedom and self-transcending love.
  4. 16:49 Responsibility in the world - Using Rwanda, climate, and public life, he looks at fear, futility, and the challenge of not giving up.
  5. 23:42 Hope, long view, and urgency - He brings in Adrienne Rich, the abolitionist movement, and Paul Hawken to show that patient collective action can outlast despair.
  6. 31:09 Emptiness and perception - The talk turns to emptiness, anger, and the way states of mind shape how self, other, and world appear.
  7. 41:23 Loosening self and other - He shows how metta softens judgment, how perception becomes more malleable, and how the self can be seen as unfindable.
  8. 52:38 Immeasurable heart - Through the salt-crystal simile, purified perception, and divine or luminous appearance, he links spaciousness to a heart that can hold suffering without sinking.
  9. 01:01:47 Radical love, quietly possible - He closes with Rumi and Shantideva, emphasizing that emptiness can free love into something more stable, generous, and real.
