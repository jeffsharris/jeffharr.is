Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

First Samatha Instructions and Guided Meditation on the Breath

Dharma Seed 42:54 2007-03-31

What if the real work of breath practice is not how many breaths you can count, but how alive and kind the mind becomes while it keeps returning? The talk sets samatha inside a larger view of the path: cultivating calm, patience, non-judgment, and a bright attentiveness that can support insight. A guided meditation then moves through posture, relaxing tension, and sensing the breath in the whole body, gradually shifting from long, slow breathing to a more subtle search for what feels most comfortable and enjoyable. By the end, the emphasis is on making the attention clear, spacious, and sensitive enough to include even discomfort without tightening around it.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 The bigger picture - Burbea frames samatha as part of a balanced path of cultivation and insight, then briefly clarifies vipassana as clear seeing that leads to freedom.
  2. 02:26 Why practice with breath - He returns to the question of why the breath is being used at all, explaining that the breath supports calmness, brightness, and a mind fit for investigation.
  3. 04:44 Patience over self-judgment - The teaching turns to the repeated wandering of the mind as normal practice, inviting patience, non-judgment, and self-kindness instead of discouragement.
  4. 10:06 Quality, not counting - He distinguishes applied and sustained attention, then warns against turning meditation into a numbers game, urging vivid, momentary quality of attention.
  5. 12:22 Settling into the body - The guided meditation begins with posture, relaxing the face, throat, shoulders, and belly, then sensing the whole body and the breath in a spacious way.
  6. 25:38 Whole-body breathing - Attention is broadened to the body as a whole, with the in-breath felt as expansion and energizing and the out-breath as natural relaxation and letting go.
  7. 34:14 Adjusting the breath - He invites a more intimate exploration of where the breath is felt, then encourages finding the most comfortable and enjoyable way to breathe right now.
  8. 39:29 Bright and alive - In the closing minutes, the instruction is to keep the awareness bright, sensitive, and receptive to whatever the breath and body are doing.
