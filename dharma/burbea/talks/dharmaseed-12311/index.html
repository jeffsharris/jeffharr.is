Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Jhana Practice

Dharma Seed 1:12:31 2007-04-02

Rob Burbea offers a wide-ranging introduction to jhana practice, beginning with the place of samatha within the larger field of Dharma and then moving through the eight absorptions as a map of deepening calm, pleasure, stillness, and spaciousness. He emphasizes that the point is not attainment for its own sake, but the fruits of practice: less clinging, more confidence, insight, and a quieter relation to self and experience. The talk also turns practical and candid about pitfalls, including attachment, self-inflation, and the backlash of hindrances as practice deepens.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Practice in broad view - He places jhana within the wider landscape of practice, distinguishing being with experience, looking for freedom, and cultivating wholesome qualities.
  2. 02:40 Why jhana matters - He narrows the focus to calm and explains why this retreat is turning toward the deeper possibilities of samatha.
  3. 04:39 What jhana means - He defines jhana as meditative absorption, sketches the eight states, and invites listeners to notice their own reactions to hearing about unfamiliar depths.
  4. 08:11 First two absorptions - He describes the first jhana through pīti and the second through fuller happiness, using the Buddha's language of suffusing the whole body.
  5. 12:31 From contentment to space - He moves through the third, fourth, fifth, sixth, and seventh jhanas, from peacefulness and stillness into infinite space, consciousness, and nothingness.
  6. 17:13 Fruits of the states - He explains the insights that can arise in each absorption, especially wishlessness, equanimity, oneness, and relief from taking things as solid.
  7. 24:52 What counts as jhana - He argues that the real test is fruit, not labels or technical purity, and compares jhana to a tree known by what it bears.
  8. 30:20 Confidence and insight - He describes how these states strengthen trust in the Dharma, support spontaneous insight, and soften fear and fixation.
  9. 39:40 Pitfalls, questions, and conditions - He addresses attachment, the role of conditions, the rarity of samatha retreats, the backlash of hindrances, and questions about realism for lay practice.
