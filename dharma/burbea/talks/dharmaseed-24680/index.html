Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Meditation: Opening to Aliveness

Dharma Seed 26:50 2014-09-17

Beginning with posture and breath, the meditation settles into a simple inquiry: what most matters right now? The talk then moves through body scanning, softening the face, shoulders, heart, belly, pelvis, legs, and feet, so attention can rest in aliveness rather than in thought. From there, sounds, sensations, and feelings are included as part of the same field, with repeated invitations to notice when the mind has wandered and start fresh. It ends by returning to the sense of being earth, awake and animated in the present moment.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling the Body - The meditation opens with posture, balance, and a few deliberate breaths to establish alertness and ease.
  2. 01:56 Intention and Aum - Attention moves to the heart, where she asks about sincere intention and introduces a brief chanting of Aum.
  3. 05:02 Softening the Face - The practice turns to the face, jaw, mouth, and shoulders, using relaxation to invite receptivity rather than force.
  4. 07:06 Heart and Belly - Awareness widens through the chest and belly, with the reminder that smiling into experience is a way of making space for it.
  5. 10:58 Pelvis and Earth Body - The scan continues through the pelvic region, legs, and feet, opening into the sense of the body as a living field and the earth beneath it.
  6. 14:08 Sound and Presence - Sound is folded into the practice, and the talk points to an alert stillness in the background of hearing and sensing.
  7. 17:29 Returning Fresh - She names the ordinary drift into story and offers a simple, nonjudgmental return to the senses and the breath.
  8. 20:59 Deepening Into Silence - The closing sections revisit relaxing more fully, sinking into the earth-body, and resting in the vast awake silence that remains aware.
