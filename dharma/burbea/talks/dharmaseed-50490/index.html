Dukkha and Soulmaking (Part 6)

Dharma Seed 1:33:06 2018-01-02

How does dukkha change when it is not treated as a problem to be solved, but sensed until it can open into image? The talk moves from the basic distinction between pain and the extra “second arrows” of contraction, into a fuller account of imaginal perception, soulmaking, and the role of intention, assent, and receptivity in that process. It then turns practical again: the speaker explores when emptiness or acceptance are enough, when healing image or action is called for, and how illness, mortality, and social suffering can each become places of depth rather than fixed meaning.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Dukkha Meets Image - The talk begins by showing how intimate contact with suffering can allow an image to arise from within the difficulty itself.
  2. 02:26 Sensing With Soul - He clarifies how imaginal perception softens contraction, brings space, and can suggest wiser bearing and response without avoiding pain.
  3. 06:20 Tool Among Other Tools - Soulmaking is placed alongside emptiness, metta, and letting go, with a caution that it is not for every situation or every stage of practice.
  4. 14:31 Second Arrow And Intention - Using the Buddha’s two arrows, he distinguishes unavoidable pain from added suffering, then asks whether the intention for imaginal practice must be larger than relief alone.
  5. 25:37 Perfection, Gift, And Assent - He argues that a sense of perfection can become shallow if detached from the felt reality of suffering, and that soulmaking requires both what is given and our assent.
  6. 40:10 Mundus Imaginalis Opens - The talk broadens into the imaginal realm, timeless perspective, and the claim that soulmaking can sacralize life, illness, and death without denying their difficulty.
  7. 57:00 Illness As Threshold - A detailed personal account of cancer and osteoporosis shows emptiness practice making experience liquid enough for humility, eternality, and theophany to emerge.
  8. 01:12:02 Healing And Action - He turns to Tikkun Olam, sacred perception, and the possibility that some suffering asks not only to be seen differently but also to be changed in the world.
  9. 01:26:37 Image, Fantasy, And Freedom - Near the end, he distinguishes fantasy, mindful observation, and fully imaginal perception, ending with the reminder that sometimes the same situation must be entered, not escaped.
