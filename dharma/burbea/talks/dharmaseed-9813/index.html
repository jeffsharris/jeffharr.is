The Space of Awareness - Welcoming (4th Guided Meditation)

Dharma Seed 40:31 2009-09-12

What changes when the first move is not to fix experience, but to welcome it completely? The talk begins with posture, settling, and a simple wish for kindness, then turns into a sustained guided practice of opening to whatever is most present—body sensation, mood, sound, resistance, or ease. As it continues, the emphasis widens from a single prominent feeling to the whole field of awareness, including subtle tension, the image of self, and even the fading of experience itself, with repeated reminders to soften, allow, and let things be.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling the posture - The meditation begins with finding a balanced posture that holds wakefulness and softness together.
  2. 01:17 Setting kindness - Rob invites a clear aspiration and asks listeners to notice what is most prominent right now.
  3. 02:37 Welcoming what arises - Whatever appears—body sensation, mood, emotion, discomfort, or something pleasant—is met with complete welcoming.
  4. 04:21 Opening the whole body - The practice expands to include the whole body and whole mind as a field of total acceptance.
  5. 06:11 Including sounds and space - Awareness widens further to near and distant sounds and to the sense of vast space for everything to be allowed.
  6. 08:31 Resting in allowance - The emphasis shifts to sitting back into total welcoming, regardless of whether experience is pleasant, unpleasant, or neutral.
  7. 14:12 Returning to welcoming - After a brief acknowledgment of related practices, the instruction returns to repeatedly softening into openness and letting phenomena arise, stay, and disappear.
  8. 27:57 Working with resistance - Rob points to subtle aversion, clinging, and tension in the body or in the felt space of experience, and shows how to relax them.
  9. 34:22 Welcoming subtlety - The meditation becomes increasingly refined, including the image of the self, the welcoming itself, and the fading or blurring of boundaries, ending in repeated allowing.
