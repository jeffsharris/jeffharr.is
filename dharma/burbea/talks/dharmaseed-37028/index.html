Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

From Inertia, Towards the Solutio (A Fragment)

Dharma Seed 7:43 2016-07-31

How do we loosen the mind’s inertia enough for perception to become more fluid, more creative, and less locked into habit? The talk moves from the basic support of inhabiting the energy body toward opening the senses, especially in walking and standing meditation, where a different awareness of the elements and the landscape can be invited. Rather than forcing a special state, the emphasis is on softening the gaze and attitude so that a more prayerful, sacred way of relating can emerge and be gently supported.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the senses - The talk begins by extending the retreat’s instruction to stay in the energy body into a broader openness of the senses.
  2. 01:10 Habit and inertia - Burbea names the mind as habitual and describes the repeated ways we see, think, and perceive.
  3. 02:21 Softening perception - He explores how a slight softening in gaze, attitude, and heart can loosen rigid perception and allow more fluid seeing.
  4. 03:31 Walking prayerfully - The teaching turns to walking meditation, sacred landscape, and the possibility of moving with a prayerful quality.
  5. 04:49 Body and self-consciousness - He notes the inertia of body and culture, including self-consciousness about movement practices, and encourages attention to that resistance.
  6. 06:05 From loosening to creation - Burbea emphasizes that only after loosening do we notice how locked in we were, and that this openness makes room for sculpting and creativity.
  7. 07:27 Returning to practice - He closes by inviting listeners to notice what calls them in the time before the next meeting.
