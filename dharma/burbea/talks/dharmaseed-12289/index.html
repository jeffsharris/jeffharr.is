Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Equanimity in Compassion

Dharma Seed 1:13:36 2007-02-08

Rob Burbea explores equanimity as the fourth Brahma Vihara, focusing first on its role within compassion and then widening the frame to samadhi, dependent origination, emptiness, and the eight worldly conditions. He distinguishes equanimity from indifference, showing how steadiness can remain tender, spacious, and responsive without being overwhelmed by suffering. The talk moves from practice instructions into a wide reflection on relationship, responsibility, and the way wisdom and compassion deepen together.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 The Brahma Viharas - Burbea introduces equanimity as the fourth sublime abiding and contrasts it with a broader, more life-based sense of joy.
  2. 03:21 What Equanimity Means - He defines equanimity as steadiness and balance, both in relation to all things and as a support within compassion.
  3. 06:09 Compassion and Balance - The talk turns to the moving balance inside compassion, including empathy, the wish to help, and the need for sustainability.
  4. 11:45 Samadhi and Spaciousness - He explains how concentration deepens equanimity and how compassion can shift from a self doing something to a spacious field holding suffering.
  5. 19:48 Needs and Conditions - Burbea explores the messiness of care, unmet needs, and relationship strain, then uses dependent origination to loosen the sense of individual control.
  6. 29:33 Karma and Non-Separation - He reads traditional karma reflections, then shows how emptiness and anatta can open love rather than lead to indifference.
  7. 42:18 All Conditions - The teaching broadens to the Buddha’s framing of equanimity amid all conditions and the eight worldly winds of praise and blame, gain and loss.
  8. 52:16 Subtle Letting Go - He describes how equanimity deepens through releasing grasping, dropping future-thinking, and seeing experience as fabricated and momentary.
  9. 01:01:41 Questions and Integration - The final section answers practice questions and closes with practical guidance for integrating retreat into ordinary life.
