Guided Meditation: The Vastness of Awareness

Dharma Seed 48:22 2010-01-31

This guided meditation begins by settling the body and gathering attention, then gradually opens awareness to sound, silence, and the sense of spaciousness itself. Rob Burbea uses repeated imagery—open sky, ocean, waves, reflections, and fireflies—to invite a felt sense that appearances arise and fade within an unshakable field of awareness. The closing turns to how to practice this in walking, standing, and with eyes open or closed.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling In - The meditation opens with posture, centering, and calming body and mind.
  2. 01:19 Body and Stillness - Attention is gathered in the body, including the life and stillness already present there.
  3. 04:11 Opening to Sound - Awareness widens from bodily stillness to listening, sound, and the space around sound.
  4. 05:50 Silence as Ground - The practice turns toward silence between sounds and the sense of sounds arising from and returning to silence.
  5. 09:57 Sky of Awareness - Awareness is imagined as an open sky in which sensations, thoughts, and images appear and disappear.
  6. 14:15 Letting Everything Belong - The instruction softens into non-struggle, non-control, and allowing every appearance to belong to awareness.
  7. 18:43 Waves and Reflections - More images—waves, a lake, floating, dissolving—emphasize experience as insubstantial and free within awareness.
  8. 24:31 Repeated Refrain - The meditation keeps returning to letting all experience arise and fade, seen again and again as impressions in awareness.
  9. 36:06 Pervading Practice - The talk concludes by pointing to the stillness, purity, and peace of awareness, then suggests bringing the same view into walking, standing, and open- or closed-eye practice.
