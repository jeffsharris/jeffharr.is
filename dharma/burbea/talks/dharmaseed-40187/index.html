Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

'The Holy Life' (Part 1)

Dharma Seed 59:23 2017-02-07

Rob Burbea explores the early Buddhist path as having a clear transcendent thrust: not just calmer experience, but a movement beyond sense experience, fabrication, and the world. He tracks this through repeated Pali Canon passages, then asks what it means for eros, soul-making, and the meaning of the “holy life.” The talk closes by setting early Buddhism alongside later Mahayana and modern attitudes, asking how each orients us toward transcendence, immanence, and the world.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Eros and the path - Burbea opens by asking where eros is directed in practice and whether the path itself can be understood as a form of soul-making.
  2. 02:38 Beyond the senses - He reads passages from the Samyutta Nikaya that describe liberation as a dimension where the sense bases cease and dukkha ends.
  3. 05:36 Beyond ordinary perception - A Majjhima Nikaya passage is used to show that even refined jhana states are still not the final goal, which lies beyond the whole field of perception.
  4. 08:06 The unfabricated - He turns to the Udana and a strong instruction to 'smash' the aggregates, interpreting it as meditative unfabrication rather than literal destruction.
  5. 10:54 Consciousness without attribute - Burbea gathers more Pali passages that describe an objectless, attribute-free mode beyond the six senses, beyond the immaterial realms, and beyond concentration alone.
  6. 15:24 No coming, no going - Another cluster of verses points to a dimension without arising or passing away, which he presents as utterly beyond time, process, and objecthood.
  7. 19:15 Via negativa and longing - He broadens the discussion into apophatic language and then brings in Gregory of Nyssa to suggest that human longing may point beyond the limited world.
  8. 24:06 The soul's more - Returning to eros and soul-making, he distinguishes ordinary accumulation from a deeper 'more' that seeks the transcendent and maps onto jhana and nibbana.
  9. 29:11 The holy life - Burbea explains how the phrase 'the holy life' functions in the Pali Canon, then contrasts the early Buddhist renunciate aim with the Mahayana Bodhisattva ideal.
