Opening, Tuning, and Relating to the Imaginal: Instructions (1)

Dharma Seed 42:04 2015-08-10

What if imaginal practice works best when perception loosens, rather than when images are made too solid? The talk begins by setting imaginal work in context and then argues for a quieter, more meditative approach centered on the energy body, with images arising from samadhi, emptiness, emotion, deliberate invocation, daydreaming, or even the world itself as image. Along the way, the speaker keeps returning to a practical caution: do not literalize the image, and pay attention to its intelligence, its pull, and the subtle shifts in attention that let it become vivid without becoming fixed.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Practice context - The talk begins by framing the retreat’s approach to imaginal practice and why it differs from more concretizing methods.
  2. 04:03 Energy body emphasis - He explains why the retreat leans toward subtle meditative work with the energy body rather than spending most of the time with images themselves.
  3. 09:18 Seven entry points - He introduces a practical list of ways images can be elicited or received, beginning with samadhi and energy-body attention.
  4. 13:25 Liquidity and emptiness - Using an alchemical maxim about everything becoming liquid, he links insubstantial perception, emptiness practice, and imaginal fertility.
  5. 20:28 Emotion as doorway - He shows how emotional upset, low-energy contraction, and the inner critic can all constellate images that carry an intelligence of their own.
  6. 25:47 Deliberate imagining - He continues through deliberate invocation of figures such as Jesus, Kuan Yin, John Coltrane, Nelson Mandela, and Etty Hillesum, including the role of intention and metta.
  7. 30:49 Places and daydreams - He turns to imaginative spaces, narrative journeys, and the way ordinary daydreaming can sometimes become meaningful practice.
  8. 36:25 The world as image - The talk closes by widening imaginal practice to perception itself, including seeing the world as image and noticing where mythos is already alive in life.
