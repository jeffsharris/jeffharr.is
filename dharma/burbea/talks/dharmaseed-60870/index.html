Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

Jhanas and Insight

Dharma Seed 1:36:13 2020-01-02

Rob Burbea explores the relationship between jhana and insight as a single continuum rather than a simple two-step path. He questions common models of concentration and awakening, then develops a view in which perception, selfing, clinging, and dukkha all vary together across a spectrum of fabrication. The talk moves from familiar Buddhist formulas to emptiness, dependent origination, and the practical after-effects of meditative states on ordinary perception.

Chapters

  1. 00:04 Framing the Question - Rob introduces the talk as an inquiry into how jhanas and insight relate, and explains why the question takes repeated reflection to understand.
  2. 06:00 Questioning the Usual Formula - He challenges the common idea that jhana or concentration must come first and insight second, reading the usual sequence as a contextual teaching device rather than a literal recipe.
  3. 10:56 Competing Models of Awakening - Rob lays out several traditional ways of understanding awakening, including reducing papanca, greed, aversion, and delusion, and asks where jhana fits in each model.
  4. 20:08 Emptiness and Ways of Looking - He shifts to emptiness as a deep embodied knowing, and explains insight practice as experimenting with ways of looking that change how phenomena, self, and time are experienced.
  5. 34:39 One Spectrum of Fabrication - Rob argues that jhana, dukkha, self-sense, and object-sense all lie on one continuum, so that less fabrication in one dimension tends to affect the others.
  6. 41:25 Practice, Agency, and Caution - A retreat note complicates the picture by showing that some forms of practice can restore agency and psychological balance, reminding listeners that not every healing comes from jhana alone.
  7. 51:48 After-Effects on Perception - He introduces the idea that meditative states leave lingering effects on perception, illustrated with a participant’s account of seeing the world as radiant and joyful.
  8. 58:51 Dependent Origination in Practice - Rob uses metta, equanimity, and the language of lawfulness to show how changing the way attention meets experience alters what is fabricated and what fades.
  9. 01:09:21 Jhana and Insight Both Ways - He maps the twelve links of dependent origination onto his spectrum of fabrication, then shows how specific jhanas and specific insight views can lead into one another, ending with a warning about getting fixed in any view as ultimate.
