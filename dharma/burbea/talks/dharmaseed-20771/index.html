Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

The Broader Context of Mindfulness

Dharma Seed 57:25 2013-09-23

What does mindfulness become when it is more than bare attention? The talk moves from a critique of “McMindfulness” and the easy commercialization of attention practices into stories from hiking, panic, email, military training, and public action, showing mindfulness as something that can pause reactivity, gather wisdom, and lead to wiser conduct. Rather than treating it as a passive witnessing practice, the speaker keeps returning to how clarity, equanimity, and restraint support speech, ethics, and compassionate intervention in the world.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Mindfulness Beyond Attention - Mark introduces the talk by responding to the popularized, decontextualized view of mindfulness as bare attention.
  2. 03:35 Rooted in Tradition - He situates mindfulness inside the larger Buddhist training and introduces his mountain stories as a way to explore it.
  3. 10:01 Clarity in Action - A night hike and a panic attack on the mountain become examples of how presence, breath, and groundedness can help in crisis.
  4. 15:03 Path, Ethics, Wisdom - He places mindfulness within ethics, meditation, and wisdom, and shows how it reveals karma, self-talk, and the causes of suffering.
  5. 19:10 Unwise Attention - Mark explores how attention can be used manipulatively, including in advertising, status-seeking, and military contexts.
  6. 26:59 Qualities of Presence - The group reflects on what arises with mindfulness: safety, joy, cohesion, intuition, less delusion, and a lighter heart.
  7. 33:03 From Awareness to Conduct - He argues that mindfulness gathers wholesome qualities and then moves outward as speech, conduct, and non-harming action.
  8. 43:14 Stories of Intervention - A second camping story, the Buddha stopping a war, and Christopher Titmuss confronting a political leader all illustrate mindful action in the world.
  9. 54:31 Practice and Climate - Mark ends with reflections on how mindfulness informs action, including the climate crisis and Earth Care Week.
