Rob Burbea Dharma Talks

This Retreat

Dharma Seed 61:51 2019-03-23

How do you begin a retreat by making room for the whole person, including illness, grief, and uncertainty? The talk opens as a communal welcome, then turns into a frank health update and a collective pause, asking listeners to tend their own hearts and bodies in the presence of mortality. From there it moves into paired sharing, a compassion chant, and an extended practice of moving through the room while sensing the body, the land, and other people as blessed, angelic, or alive with Buddha nature. The talk closes by returning to stillness and framing practice as a way of seeing oneself and others more openly, poetically, and sacredly.

Chapters

  1. 01:27 Welcoming the whole person - The talk opens by greeting the group in their particularities and framing Soulmaking Dharma as a practice that can hold all of who people are.
  2. 11:19 A retreat shaped together - Matthew explains that the retreat was designed as a shared, participant-led process and emphasizes humility, discernment, and long-term practice.
  3. 18:26 Health, uncertainty, patience - Rob’s health and the practical effects of treatment are named openly, along with the need for flexibility, patience, and forgiveness.
  4. 21:29 Meeting mortality as practice - The diagnosis and possible dying are brought into the field of practice, with grief, love, and death framed as part of soul-making.
  5. 28:21 Compassion chant - Matthew invites the room into Om Mani Padme Hum as a shared devotional response and a way of being together with Rob.
  6. 33:34 Witnessing in small groups - Participants form triads to speak briefly about arriving, with the emphasis on listening, presence, and allowing what is here to be voiced.
  7. 42:12 Movement and blessing - A guided moving practice unfolds in the hall, shifting from embodied awareness to blessing each other, the room, and the wider field.
  8. 01:00:08 Stillness and closing - The talk settles into stillness and ends by naming the room, the land, and everyone present as alive with sacredness and blessing.
