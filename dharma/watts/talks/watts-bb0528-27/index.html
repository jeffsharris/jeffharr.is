Alan Watts

Love of Waters

KPFA Archive 26:51 1960-06-10

This talk begins as a personal meditation on the sea, moving from sensory description to a larger claim that waves offer a kind of timeless time. It then turns sharper and more philosophical, using the gull, the crab, and the fish to argue that life is inseparable from eating, being eaten, and transformation. From there, the speaker questions human self-consciousness, contrasts isolated abstraction with living relation, and returns to the ocean as a place where the world feels joined rather than split apart. The closing image folds the whole meditation inward: the knocking at the edge of space turns out to be the speaker’s own heart.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening Love of the Sea - Watts begins with a direct confession of his attachment to water, sea air, and the first sensory impression of the ocean.
  2. 01:01 Timeless Waves - He contrasts wave-rhythm with clock time, then opens the sea into a mythic image of endless creation and dissolution.
  3. 02:23 Sausalito from the Water - The talk turns observational as he describes living on a ferry boat, where docks, boats, junk, and light are all transformed by the seascape.
  4. 05:13 The Ocean’s Paradox - Watts dwells on the sea’s beauty and its hidden struggle, moving from driftwood and light to the hard fact of eating and being eaten.
  5. 06:54 Crab, Gull, and Human Fear - A gull and crab become a compact drama of appetite and pain, which Watts expands into a critique of human dread of mortality.
  6. 09:47 Objecting to Life - He argues that the reflective human mind turns life into a problem, and that objecting to pain is itself part of pain.
  7. 12:05 Self-Consciousness and the Fall - Watts develops his central claim that the real error is mistaking the commentator for the whole self, using the hand and finger-thumb analogy.
  8. 15:22 Part and Whole - He extends the argument through images of orange, blood, and living context, warning against the severing power of abstraction.
  9. 17:34 Back to the Heart - The final movement returns to the ocean as a place where roots can be felt again, ending with the discovery that the supposed knocking beyond consciousness was his own heartbeat.
