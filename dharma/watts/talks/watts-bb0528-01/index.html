Alan Watts

The Future of Christian Philosophy IV

KPFA Archive 28:42 1960-01-08

Alan Watts opens by returning to a listener’s objection and uses it to sharpen his distinction between intelligence as pattern and intelligence as mechanism. From there he moves into a larger argument about why the universe, the human being, and Christian language about God cannot be understood well if they are forced into a purely mechanical model. The final stretch turns to freedom, sin, and divine judgment, asking whether Christianity can still speak meaningfully about responsibility without collapsing into legalism or determinism.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 A listener’s objection - Watts begins by revisiting a question about intelligence and clarifying the problem that set the talk in motion.
  2. 00:54 Pattern versus mechanism - He distinguishes intelligence as organic pattern from intelligence as mechanical structure and argues that mind cannot be reduced to mind-stuff or parts.
  3. 03:56 Nature grows from within - Watts contrasts constructed machines with natural growth, using development in crystals and organisms to show pattern emerging from inside outward.
  4. 07:30 God as total pattern - He extends the argument to the universe as an organic whole and suggests that the symbol God only works if it names that living unity.
  5. 08:48 The human image - Watts turns to the Christian claim that man is made in God’s image and frames the central problem as freedom versus determinism.
  6. 13:03 Outside and inside views - He separates the objective study of man from the lived inner experience of thought and feeling, citing Teilhard de Chardin to support the distinction.
  7. 15:34 Responsibility under pressure - Watts explores the conflict between psychological determinism and moral responsibility, then shows how a punitive view of sin threatens the idea of freedom.
  8. 19:56 Judgment and freedom - He argues that if sin is treated as legal offense and God as judge, Christianity stops speaking about freedom, and he closes by proposing this as the next problem to explore.
