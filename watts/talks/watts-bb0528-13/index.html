Alan Watts

Paul Tillich on The Eternal Now

KPFA Archive 28:20 1960-03-25

Alan Watts frames this episode as a brief experiment in sharing another voice, then turns the program over to Paul Tillich’s "The Eternal Now." The talk moves from human anxiety before death, through the theological problem of time, into a fuller argument about the past, forgiveness, and the difficulty of resting in the present. By the end, Tillich’s language of the eternal is used not as abstraction but as a way to name courage, release, and presence.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Why this reading - Watts explains why he is stepping back from his usual format and introducing a reading from Paul Tillich.
  2. 01:50 The Eternal Now begins - Tillich opens with mortality and the pressure of endings as the entry point into the question of time.
  3. 03:46 Judging time from eternity - Augustine and prayer are used to suggest that time can only be understood from a standpoint related to the eternal.
  4. 04:50 Past, present, future - The talk lays out the three modes of time and shows how the future shapes anxiety, hope, and our sense of the end.
  5. 07:17 Denial and endless time - Tillich examines common evasions of death, including the fantasy of endless future, and rejects it as a false image of eternity.
  6. 10:12 Christian time and eternity - Christian language, especially Alpha and Omega and the fourth gospel, is used to distinguish eternity from mere endless duration.
  7. 14:18 The burden of the past - The argument turns to how the past remains active in the present, in both personal life and national history, and how repentance can break its power.
  8. 18:08 Blessing, curse, forgiveness - Tillich deepens the contrast between blessing and curse, then identifies forgiveness as the power that can change the meaning of the past.
  9. 20:45 The problem of presence - The present is shown as the point where past and future meet, yet also the hardest thing to hold without losing contact with the eternal.
