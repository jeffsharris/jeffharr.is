Alan Watts

The Future of Christian Philosophy VI

KPFA Archive 27:59 1960-01-22

Alan Watts closes this talk by turning from church machinery to the question of what faith is for. He argues that Christianity has confused spiritual life with fundraising, self-defense, and historical clinging, and he uses Jesus’ own sayings to press for a faith that releases rather than protects. The last movement widens the point: religion matters when it heals the split between inner and outer life, not when it props up institutions.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Why return to the topic - Watts explains why this will be the final program on the subject and resets the question of what the future of Christian philosophy is for.
  2. 01:52 Church as administration - He describes church life as dominated by fundraising, canvassing, and institutional self-preservation rather than spiritual depth.
  3. 04:10 Attraction and overreach - Watts contrasts a pastor who teaches deeply with churches that try to lure people through social activity, youth programming, and entertainment.
  4. 07:01 Defending the faith - He argues that proselytizing and claims of exclusive rightness reveal insecurity, and he pauses over the irony of religions acting as if God needs protection.
  5. 09:01 Faith versus clinging - Watts develops his central distinction between real faith and anxious attachment, then turns to Jesus as a figure who does not preserve himself by worldly success.
  6. 13:41 Death, resurrection, and release - Using the grain of wheat, Mary Magdalene, and the empty tomb, he reads the resurrection tradition as a refusal of clinging to the past or to sacred objects.
  7. 18:26 Dead rock, living flesh - He criticizes frozen doctrine and the imagery of hiding in the 'Rock of Ages,' then shifts toward a Zen-like seeing that does not overstate what is present.
  8. 24:36 Wholeness in daily life - Watts argues that religion is not made by attaching church purposes to ordinary acts, but by a transformed awareness in which life becomes whole and holy.
  9. 28:05 Healing the split - He ends by saying religion’s main work is to heal the profound division in modern consciousness, for Christianity or any other faith.
