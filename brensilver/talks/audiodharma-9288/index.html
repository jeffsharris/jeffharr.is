Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: The Human Condition

AudioDharma 09:34 2018-09-19

Matthew Brensilver reflects on how hard it can be to hold the full range of being human: grief, joy, climate anxiety, small pleasures, and the sense that they don’t neatly fit together. He then describes a meditation-related experience in which tears, laughter, and a surprising underlying peace arose at once, suggesting a more spacious way of meeting suffering. The talk ends with a quiet recognition that the human condition is larger, stranger, and more workable than it first appears.

Chapters

  1. 00:11 Being Human - He opens with the basic confusion of being human and the challenge of holding joy, sorrow, suffering, and progress together.
  2. 01:18 One Moment at a Time - He turns to Buddhist psychology and the idea that experience arrives one mind moment at a time, even when life feels internally divided.
  3. 02:27 What Happens in the News - He describes how news and climate concerns can dominate consciousness and make everything else seem secondary.
  4. 02:43 A Practice Setting - He introduces an unfamiliar breathing practice in a dark room, setting up the experience that follows.
  5. 03:35 Tears and Compassion - Hearing a young woman crying nearby, he feels tears of compassion that also touch unfinished places in his own heart.
  6. 04:57 Laughter in Grief - Alongside sadness, he notices unexpected laughter and a sense of the cosmic joke, a wider perspective on pain.
  7. 06:45 Peace Underneath - He describes a third simultaneous layer: a deep, steady peace that seemed to hold the tears and laughter together.
  8. 08:03 The Human Condition - He draws the experience together as a full acknowledgment of the human condition and a quiet acceptance of how much it asks of us.
