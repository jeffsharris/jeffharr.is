Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided meditation: Affect and the Lure of Thought

AudioDharma 27:46 2024-12-11

Matthew Brensilver leads a guided meditation on meeting affect directly rather than using thought to manage discomfort. He contrasts a gentle settling into practice with the more decisive attitude of “starting on a dime,” then repeatedly returns to renunciation as a willingness to feel what is present. The meditation emphasizes body-based attention, equanimity with emotional activation, and a quiet release of urgency and self-concern.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Settling into practice - He begins by describing a gentle way of entering meditation and how the act of getting in can matter as much as where one arrives.
  2. 00:40 Start on a dime - Using Shinzen Young’s advice, he contrasts soft settling with a more immediate, decisive turn away from distraction.
  3. 01:43 Putting thoughts down - He names the sense of incompletion that can make thoughts feel urgent, and invites the listener to set them aside anyway.
  4. 04:30 Renunciation as feeling - Renunciation is framed not as suppression but as the willingness to feel the egoic wound, uncertainty, and ambiguity without using thought as a pacifier.
  5. 06:36 Body and emotion - The guidance shifts toward breathing, bodily awareness, and tending carefully to emotional circuits that insist thinking is necessary.
  6. 15:51 Equanimity and urgency - He explains how meeting emotional activation with equanimity can make everything feel less urgent.
  7. 17:54 This is your life - The talk turns toward presence in ordinary life, emphasizing that practice is not postponed until later and that this moment is already the whole field.
  8. 23:36 Release of self - He closes by pointing to a deepening intimacy with experience that requires loosening self-clinging and resting in what he calls the wondrous dark.
