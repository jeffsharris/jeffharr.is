Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Breath, Pleasure, Opening

AudioDharma 29:08 2024-09-11

This guided meditation begins with a gentle invitation to soften the face and notice a simple, almost hidden smile. From there, Matthew Brensilver develops a practice of allowing pleasure to be present alongside difficulty, using the breath as a place to meet aliveness without forcing anything to disappear. The talk gradually widens into open awareness, then loosens even the instructions themselves so the practice can rest in sound, sensation, and trust.

Chapters

  1. 00:10 Settling with a Smile - The meditation begins by inviting a barely noticeable smile and letting that ease spread through the face and body.
  2. 01:40 Joy with Difficulty - He frames the practice as making room for joy without denying suffering, especially the mixed and ambiguous nature of experience.
  3. 03:56 Relaxing and Receiving - Attention is guided to release tension where possible and rest on something soothing or pleasantly subtle in the background.
  4. 08:13 Breath and Pleasure - Pleasant feeling is invited to mingle with breathing, with the breath understood as a living rhythm of receiving and release.
  5. 12:57 An Anchor Everywhere - The practice shifts from a narrow breath anchor toward a wider field in which awareness includes everything.
  6. 14:45 Opening to Awareness - He emphasizes waking up to arising and passing experience without building a fixed viewpoint, ending in trust in awareness itself.
  7. 20:41 Letting Instructions Go - The close loosens the guidance itself, noting the pull to make meaning and inviting the listener to let voice and instruction become simple sound.
