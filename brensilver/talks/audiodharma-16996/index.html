Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Unseduced by the Mirage of the Future

AudioDharma 30:53 2022-09-27

Matthew Brensilver offers a gentle guided meditation centered on loosening the habit of performing in practice and meeting experience without trying to prove anything. The meditation moves from settling the body and attention, to relaxing self-conscious effort, to seeing how the future can seduce the mind into anxiety and projection. He returns again and again to simple phrases of enoughness, encouraging a willingness to rest in what is already present.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and settling - The meditation opens with a brief welcome and a soft invitation to begin sitting simply and naturally.
  2. 01:19 Natural beginning - He asks listeners to notice what they think meditation is supposed to look like and to loosen that inherited script.
  3. 04:49 No need to prove - Matthew names the urge to perform for oneself, a teacher, or even the Buddha, and gently releases that pressure.
  4. 07:06 Attending lightly - He suggests using breath, body, sound, and closed-eye darkness as anchors while avoiding a brittle, achievement-oriented attention.
  5. 12:09 Time and projection - The practice turns toward how we imagine trajectories, outcomes, and identities, and how that can sandwich us inside time.
  6. 18:49 Unseduced by future - He directly names the mirage of the future and points to the gap between wanting and actually arriving.
  7. 19:58 Enoughness and surrender - The meditation settles into phrases of safety and sufficiency, with a quiet surrender into what is here.
  8. 25:57 Opening to phenomena - He closes by encouraging a steady openness to pleasant and unpleasant experience without spinning outward, resting back in awareness as the bell rings.
