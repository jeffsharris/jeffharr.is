Guided Meditation: Clinging and Space (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 60:37 2018-06-14

This guided meditation begins with settling the body, scanning for how the retreat moment feels, and reassuring the mind that the conditions for freedom are already here. It then turns toward attention, letting go of what is not ours, and meeting craving, aversion, and imperfection with steadiness rather than resistance. In the later sections, Matthew opens out into compassion and nonviolence, drawing on Buddha teachings, Martin Luther King Jr., Shinzen Young, and the Dalai Lama to show how softening the heart can change how we meet experience and one another.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling In - The meditation begins by relaxing into the breath, scanning the body and mind, and orienting to the retreat container.
  2. 05:11 Finding the Anchor - Matthew invites attention to stabilize on the breath and rest in one place instead of flitting across the mind's screen.
  3. 12:35 Letting Go - He emphasizes that even one breath of presence matters and links wakefulness with a radical inner nonviolence.
  4. 18:29 Craving and Aversion - A Buddha teaching frames the problem as not pleasure or pain themselves, but craving and aversion, which complicate experience.
  5. 29:02 More Space Than Expected - The talk opens into the sense that the mind has more room than it assumes, and that guards around the senses can stand down.
  6. 39:58 Body and Thought - Matthew traces how ruminative thought is powered by bodily activation and how peace with the body changes the mind's story.
  7. 46:18 Nonviolence and Love - He distinguishes acceptance from passivity, then turns to hatred, Martin Luther King Jr., and the need to meet our own experience with love.
  8. 52:37 Silence and Integration - The closing moves through Shinzen Young and the Dalai Lama, then returns to retreat practice, shared silence, and noticing where the mind defaults.
