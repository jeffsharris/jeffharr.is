Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Relaxing and Falling Awake

AudioDharma 29:49 2024-06-26

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation on settling the body and mind without forcing ease. The practice moves from posture and releasing the day, through soft attention to breath and sound, toward a gentler understanding of relaxation as relinquishment rather than control. He returns repeatedly to the idea that tension is bound up with grasping, and that waking up may happen in a way closer to falling asleep than to trying harder.

Chapters

  1. 00:29 Settling Into Posture - He begins by inviting a balanced posture that tilts toward alertness or relaxation depending on what is needed.
  2. 01:51 Laying Down the Day - The guidance turns toward whatever is still entangling from the day or week, with encouragement to feel and bless it without getting more caught.
  3. 04:19 Blessing and Release - He emphasizes giving blessing to what is present so it can be set down rather than carried forward.
  4. 06:17 Breath and Sound - Attention opens to the ends of exhalations and to very faint sounds, including the edge between hearing and silence.
  5. 11:05 Tension and Holding - The talk names greed, hatred, selfing, and duality as forms of tension, and contrasts them with the limits of willful relaxation.
  6. 15:37 Relinquishing Control - He describes a faith-filled softening in which the mind stops trying to engineer experience and lets the moment do some of the work.
  7. 21:07 Relaxing as Exhale - The meditation continues with a quieter settling around the felt sense of relaxing, leading into a brief reflective turn on the practice itself.
  8. 24:30 Falling Awake - He closes by contrasting commanded relaxation with genuine relinquishment, comparing it to falling asleep and suggesting that awakening can happen from openness.
