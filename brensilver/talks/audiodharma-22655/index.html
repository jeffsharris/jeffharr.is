Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: All Phenomena are Friendly

AudioDharma 29:58 2025-03-31

Matthew Brensilver leads a guided meditation that steadily loosens the pressure of self-judgment and future-minded striving. He invites listeners to set down the habit of making the self into an object, rest in the breath, and become less intimidated by uncertainty. The practice gradually opens into a quieter Dharma perspective: experience can be met more softly, even when it hurts.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Arrival and settling - A brief welcome and orientation into practice, with an invitation to find a posture that balances alertness and relaxation.
  2. 00:53 Letting go of selfing - The meditation turns toward dropping the habit of assessing oneself and forgetting familiar stories about who we are.
  3. 02:49 Breath and fresh space - Guidance opens into breathing and bodily ease, encouraging a simpler relationship to the present moment.
  4. 07:26 Pressure to perform - Matthew explores how self-concern accumulates and how relaxing can soften the drive to prove, perform, or become.
  5. 11:55 Uncertainty and release - The talk names anicca and the burden of trying to manage every future self, then encourages resting in the open space of this breath.
  6. 17:42 Precision without tension - The meditation becomes more specific about the breath, with attention to beginnings, endings, and the pause of release.
  7. 23:19 All phenomena friendly - The central phrase is repeated and then clarified as a Dharma view: everything can be met as friendly, even when some experiences hurt.
