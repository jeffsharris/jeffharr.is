Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Mortality - Futurelessness & Samadhi (2/5)

AudioDharma 13:57 2023-04-25

Matthew Brensilver explores mortality as a practical contemplation rather than a grim abstraction, asking how death can reveal the mind’s constant orientation toward the future. He moves from William James and the Buddha to a contemporary scientific framing, then back to lived practice: settling the heart, pruning counterfactual thinking, and resting in what is here now. The talk ends by linking deep surrender with the ordinary task of shaping a Dharma life.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Death and practice - He introduces the week’s theme: how death can inform life and why mortality should neither be over-read nor ignored.
  2. 01:20 The pull of the future - He turns to the role of future-thinking in inner life, describing the mind’s effort to predict an uncertain world.
  3. 02:52 A trembling world - With a Buddha reference, he emphasizes impermanence and the way even memory often serves the need to control what comes next.
  4. 04:22 Futurelessness - He develops the idea that death invites contemplation of a moment without a future, where vigilance and strategizing fall away.
  5. 06:02 Only this moment - He frames mortality as a direct taste of no future, suggesting that if this were all we had, awareness would matter most.
  6. 07:48 Science and samadhi - He brings in Helen Slater’s account of predictive processing to echo the Dharma view that meditation can reduce future-thinking and soften self-boundaries.
  7. 10:09 Sit like dying - He recalls an early instruction to sit like a dying person and hears it as sitting without a future, with nothing left to do but be awake.
  8. 12:15 How to live - He closes by saying mortality can settle the mind, clarify the life one wants to lead, and bring surrender and Dharma organization into conversation.
