Guided Meditation: Surrendering the Territory of Me

Dharma Seed 38:59 2025-04-18

This guided meditation begins with simple sensory attention and gradually widens into a softer relationship with body, sound, and awareness. Matthew Brensilver repeatedly returns to the felt sense of self as a territory we patrol, then invites a gentler letting go of that guarding and a more intimate experience of spaciousness. The closing instructions emphasize non-preference: meeting contraction and ease alike without making them a problem.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling on sound - Attention opens with the faintest sound and the edge between hearing and silence.
  2. 01:16 Soft gaze inward - The practice shifts to resting visual attention in the darkness or brightness behind closed eyes.
  3. 03:09 Weight and ease - Instructions return again and again to grounded bodily sensation, heaviness, and simple restful alertness.
  4. 07:48 The guarded territory - Matthew names the sense of self as a protected boundary and invites recognition of the effort to patrol it.
  5. 12:39 Lay down your arms - The defense of self and world is softened, and the practice turns toward relaxing resistance.
  6. 15:39 Contracting and opening - He points to self as a contractive force and then to the expansive side where self and not-self are made of the same elements.
  7. 20:21 Life happening - The meditation broadens into allowing life to be experienced as happening, without centering everything on 'me.'
  8. 27:13 No preference - The talk turns toward loving preferences and control without taking them seriously, and toward peace that does not depend on phenomena.
  9. 36:49 Closing reflection - Matthew briefly cites Shinzen Young on non-preference, then ends by inviting an intuitive sense of self as a defended boundary.
