Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Maturing our autobiography

AudioDharma 13:23 2019-03-20

Matthew Brensilver explores how mindfulness helps us step out of self-reinforcing stories without dismissing the value of narrative altogether. He argues that practice can mature our autobiography: making our life story more coherent, less rigid, and gentler toward what was once too painful or confusing to hold. The talk moves from neuroimaging and the limits of rumination into a quieter account of remembering, reinterpreting, and blessing the past with Dharma.

Chapters

  1. 00:09 Stepping out of narrative - He opens with mindfulness as a way of disengaging from ruminative, self-relevant story and returning to direct experience.
  2. 01:43 Why stories limit - Using a quotation about storytelling animals, he shows how identity can become claustrophobic when we mistake thought for reality.
  3. 03:02 Space around thought - He contrasts identification with thoughts and feelings with the relief of knowing them as experiences that can be held at a distance.
  4. 04:39 Maturing the story - The talk turns to the redemptive side of story, suggesting that practice can make our self-narrative more rich, coherent, and nuanced.
  5. 06:07 A jagged autobiography - He describes how practice brings up unfinished or undigested experiences, and how simplistic judgments can shape the way we tell our lives.
  6. 08:16 Memory in practice - Bringing past experience into stillness, he says meditation lets old pain and grief arise now, where they can begin to lose some of their charge.
  7. 09:56 Remembering with Dharma - He links memory, forgiveness, and reinterpretation, suggesting that practice helps us bless the past with wisdom and love.
  8. 11:42 Putting the story down - The close emphasizes integration: the past feels more like the past, life feels less ambush-prone, and the autobiography can finally be set down more safely.
