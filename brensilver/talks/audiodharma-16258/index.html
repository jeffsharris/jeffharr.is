Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Compassion

AudioDharma 30:53 2022-05-11

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation on compassion that begins with making space for the full range of human joy and sorrow, then settles into a steadier confidence in taking one’s seat amid ordinary imperfection. He gradually turns toward the suffering of someone cared about, first allowing grief to be felt and then shifting into warmth, blessing, and the wish that suffering be eased. The meditation closes by loosening the boundary between self and other and resting in a more open, distinction-less compassion.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Arriving and settling - A brief opening welcomes listeners into stillness and invites them to sit together.
  2. 01:25 Space for human life - The practice opens the heart to joys and sorrows, and to the sense that no life or mind is perfectly complete.
  3. 05:24 Relaxing control - He emphasizes letting go of control and sensing how love becomes more necessary when experience is allowed to be as it is.
  4. 08:24 Blessing the moment - The instruction deepens into blessing the intensity of feeling with wisdom and love, rather than trying to solve the whole human predicament.
  5. 14:26 Seeing another's pain - The meditation turns explicitly toward someone who is suffering, making their situation vivid and allowing grief to be part of the practice.
  6. 18:39 From grief to warmth - Grief is acknowledged but not held as the endpoint; the heart moves into love, compassion, and the wish for relief and peace.
  7. 24:01 Offering care - The practice rests in the attitude of offering blessings and love without insisting that suffering immediately stop.
  8. 27:24 Open compassion - In the closing minutes, the distinction between self and other is loosened and compassion is allowed to simply dwell as openness.
