Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Talk: Whys and Hows of Effort - Energy & Bhava Tanha

AudioDharma 50:11 2025-03-18

Matthew Brensilver reflects on effort as something steadied by trust, patience, and a clear sense of why we practice. He moves from the hidden way goodness accrues, to motivation rooted in benefit for others, to the tension between wholesome effort and craving for becoming. The talk closes by warning against spiritual striving and inviting a more receptive, willing relationship to practice.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Listening and trust - He opens by reflecting on how to listen to Dharma talks selectively, then turns to effort, patience, and trust as the mood that lets practice take root.
  2. 05:44 Practice that simply continues - Using Sharon Salzberg and Nietzsche, he describes practice as something we keep doing from faith in the path, even when results are not obvious.
  3. 12:21 Why make the effort - He asks what actually sustains effort, shifts from self-centered motivation to concern for others, and points to the way healing and love spill beyond the self.
  4. 17:00 Precious human life - He introduces the Tibetan contemplations, beginning with the preciousness and strangeness of being born at all as a source of reverence and motivation.
  5. 19:00 Against the stream - A long aside on cosmology gives way to the Buddhist point that conditioning pushes us toward habit, while practice asks for a critical stance and real energy to let go.
  6. 32:07 Open palm of release - He explores how effort becomes the energy to listen to our own wisdom, then turns toward receptivity, surrender, and willingness rather than willfulness.
  7. 40:08 Non-gaining practice - Suzuki Roshi's teaching on non-gaining practice frames the danger of spiritual ambition and the subtle forms of craving that hide inside progress.
  8. 45:55 Your own path - He ends with a brief silence and a closing reminder to take what is useful, leave the rest, and let the path feel like one's own even within the Buddhist tradition.
