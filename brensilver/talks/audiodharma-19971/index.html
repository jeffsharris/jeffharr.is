Dharmette: Emerging From Doubt

AudioDharma 13:06 2024-03-12

Matthew Brensilver explores doubt as a kind of disorientation: the mind keeps reaching for certainty, but the more it grasps for answers, the more it can get tangled in thought, comparison, and self-conscious practice. He then turns toward faith—not as belief in a theory, but as a growing trust in direct experience, patience, and what is actually trustworthy. The talk closes with a practical encouragement to stay open amid intensity and let the path unfold without demanding impossible certainty.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Opening and Theme - After a brief opening, the talk settles into the theme of faith and doubt.
  2. 00:57 Doubt as Disorientation - Doubt is framed as being lost without a map, with the mind hungry for data but unable to find trustworthy guidance.
  3. 03:01 Narratives and Rumination - He describes how doubt makes the mind bounce between incompatible stories, over-trust others, and treat thoughts as the most important evidence.
  4. 05:01 What Practice Cannot Do - The talk shifts to the limits of practice, especially the wish that meditation could protect us from change, pain, and the First Noble Truth.
  5. 07:38 Precision and Letting Go - A brief Aristotle reference supports the idea that wisdom does not demand more exactness than a situation can offer, and that certainty often cannot be forced.
  6. 10:04 Trusting Experience - Drawing on Sharon Salzberg, faith is described as learning to trust one's own deepest experience, which brings patience and independence.
  7. 10:59 Faith as a Hinge - Ajahn Sucitto's image of faith as a hinge suggests a point of no return, after which practice no longer feels like a repeated choice.
  8. 12:15 Closing Encouragement - The talk ends with an invitation to stay open, poised amid intensity, and entrust the heart to what is trustworthy.
