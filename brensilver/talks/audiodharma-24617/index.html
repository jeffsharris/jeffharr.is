Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Metta meditation

AudioDharma 3:35:11 2026-01-17

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet introduction to metta as a flexible heart practice rather than a feeling to force. He emphasizes that the Dharma does not ask us to pretend, and that loving-kindness may take different forms depending on the moment: compassion, joy, equanimity, forgiveness, gratitude, or simply a simpler phrase that can be held with sincerity. The talk moves from this framing into guided practice, then into questions about bodily constriction, deservingness, grief, and how love and sorrow are intertwined.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening frame - Matthew introduces the session and sets up metta as an improvisational practice that should not require pretending.
  2. 02:07 Finding the right love - He names several forms of heart practice and warns against measuring practice only by the intensity of feeling.
  3. 05:57 Measureless awareness - The talk turns to the Brahma-viharas, concentration, and purification, including a reference to Ajahn Sucitto on moving out of the gridlock of self.
  4. 12:00 Guided metta practice - Matthew leads a long sitting practice, inviting attention into the body, the heart center, and whatever words or images can orient the mind toward goodness.
  5. 40:31 Practice in the world - In a later section of questions, he returns to the four Brahma-viharas as different kinds of love that may be relevant while moving through the world.
  6. 50:20 Constriction and worthiness - He answers a question about bodily constriction and the feeling of being undeserving, pointing toward a practice that steps outside the deserving versus undeserving debate.
  7. 54:42 Love and grief - The final discussion takes up the tension between personal ease and world suffering, and Matthew argues that grief is an expression of love rather than a defect in it.
  8. 01:00:54 Closing instructions - He settles the group back into quiet practice and closes with practical guidance for walking, stretching, and letting different forms of love become available.
