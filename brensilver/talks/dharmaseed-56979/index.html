Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

The Story of Jumping Mouse (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 58:32 2019-06-18

Matthew Brensilver uses the story of Jumping Mouse as a frame for an evening retreat talk on the spiritual path: hearing a distant call, leaving comfort, and learning to trust disorientation. He moves through the mouse’s encounters with raccoon, frog, buffalo, wolf, and eagle to explore curiosity, humility, renunciation, refuge, and surrender. The talk also draws on other teachers and texts to show how practice can exceed what we thought we were signing up for.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the retreat - Matthew sets the tone for the retreat and explains why he chose to begin with a bedtime story.
  2. 01:51 Why stories matter - He introduces Jumping Mouse, reflects on story and metaphor in Buddhism, and invites listeners to see themselves in the characters.
  3. 06:28 Hearing the river - The first part of the story becomes a teaching on doubt, curiosity, courage, and the first movement toward practice.
  4. 11:40 Walking with raccoon - Matthew interprets the journey to the river as a path that disorients us and cannot simply be used to improve the old self.
  5. 18:10 River and mountain - He expands the story through the metta chant, the frog’s instruction, the sacred mountains, and the warning not to get stuck in a merely comfortable place.
  6. 30:19 Choosing the wider path - The later journey turns toward deeper renunciation, including the buffalo and the loss of an eye as part of a widening of vision.
  7. 40:49 Views and surrender - Matthew shifts into teachings on perception, letting go of views, renunciation, and the way the Dharma asks for more than adding practice onto ordinary life.
  8. 54:39 Refuge and closing - He closes with Mahasi Sayadaw and Bhikkhu Bodhi on openness, refuge, and how small moments of clarity carry larger consequences.
