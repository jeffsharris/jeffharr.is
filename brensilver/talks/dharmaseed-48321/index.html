Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Matthew Brensilver, Sylvia Boorstein: Day 1: Morning Practice Session (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 3:29:05 2018-01-13

This retreat opening moves from love and mindfulness into a clear description of why practice matters at all: life is intense, thought makes it seem more urgent than it is, and retreat gives us a chance to meet experience more directly. Matthew Brensilver and Sylvia Boorstein alternate between teaching, short guided periods, and conversation, returning again and again to softness, equanimity, and a truce with imperfection. The talk includes references to Daniel Kahneman, After Life, Martin Luther King Jr., the Metta Sutta, and the Foundations of Mindfulness, each used to steady the practical arc of the morning rather than decorate it.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening to practice - The talk begins by framing the weekend as a practice of love joined with wisdom, and of awareness joined with openheartedness.
  2. 10:17 A moment to live forever - Matthew uses After Life and Daniel Kahneman to show how narrative mind exaggerates urgency and how any moment can become a place of depth.
  3. 14:09 Equanimity and attention - The teaching turns toward nonviolence, narrative thinking, and the shift from controlling experience to resting with what is actually here.
  4. 44:51 Softening into the body - A long guided stretch and sit emphasizes relaxing the face, shoulders, belly, and breath, and noticing how thoughts and sensations rise and pass.
  5. 01:26:51 Walking with goodwill - The Metta Sutta becomes a guide for walking meditation, recollection, and making a truce with imperfection as the body moves.
  6. 01:42:36 Dukkha and hardening - Sylvia and Matthew talk through suffering, impermanence, and the way the mind adds extra pain through resistance, bitterness, and longing for completion.
  7. 02:31:54 Not to control life - The dialogue deepens around surrender, watchtower vigilance, and the shift from trying to manage experience to being changed by it.
  8. 03:01:43 Practice, food, and closing - They end with guidance on the day’s rhythm, food, questions, and the reminder that practice is ordinary, tender, and ongoing.
