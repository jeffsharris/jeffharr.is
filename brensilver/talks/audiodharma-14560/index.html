Guided Meditation: Clinging Complicates Everything

AudioDharma 29:58 2021-11-17

Matthew Brensilver leads a guided meditation on how clinging tightens experience and makes the mind feel complicated. He begins with breath and body cues for smoothing out energy, then turns toward greed, aversion, and the ways practice can become simpler when there is less grasping. The later part of the talk points toward resting more fully in the moment, with a brief Ajahn Chah reference shaping the theme of simplicity and letting go.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Settling In - A brief welcome and set-up leads into sitting together and preparing the body-mind for practice.
  2. 02:54 Balancing Energy - He uses a yoga-style instruction to soften what is heavy and enliven what is dull, working with the breath to smooth the body-mind.
  3. 05:50 Breath Through the Body - The guidance continues through fatigue, numbness, and whole-body breathing, emphasizing steadiness and integration.
  4. 09:27 The Cost of Clinging - He names clinging as agitating and complicating, and points to practice as training in non-clinging rather than reinforcing old habits.
  5. 12:15 Equanimity and Ease - The meditation turns to meeting longing and aversion with equanimity, showing how the moment becomes simpler when grasping loosens.
  6. 14:13 Resting in Simplicity - After a long stretch with unclear audio, the talk returns to the felt simplicity of practice and the quiet holiness of just resting there.
  7. 22:12 Sacred Enough - A brief closing reflection, likely echoing Ajahn Chah, points to the Dharma coursing through ordinary experience and resting as enough.
