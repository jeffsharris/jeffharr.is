Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Compassion & Equanimity

AudioDharma 15:51 2023-12-15

Matthew Brensilver explores compassion and equanimity as a paired practice rather than separate ideals. He moves from the Buddha’s nuance and resistance to fundamentalism, into the willingness to feel suffering vividly, then into a quiet kind of love that can rest without collapsing into apathy or blame. The talk closes by describing an alternating rhythm of love and rest, where each supports the other.

Chapters

  1. 00:55 Teaching with nuance - He opens by reflecting on how spiritual teachings can be misread and why the Buddha’s path resists fundamentalism and overconfidence.
  2. 02:53 Compassion and suffering - He introduces compassion and explains how practice involves staying present with dukkha rather than deflecting into blame or avoidance.
  3. 05:26 Why we help - Opening to suffering reveals the shared wish to be happy and naturally turns toward action on behalf of others.
  4. 06:32 From grief to love - He brings in Marx and then a compassion instruction attributed to Matthieu Ricard to show how compassion can deepen into love.
  5. 08:18 When compassion tires - He names the danger of compassion becoming exhausting when it is not held in balance.
  6. 09:06 Equanimity as resting - Using Michelle McDonald’s flower image, he describes equanimity as the heart’s healthy closing: not apathy, but a refined relationship to helplessness.
  7. 12:36 Quiet love and rest - He explains equanimity as a quiet, open love that reduces reactivity, purifies compassion, and makes rest possible when no action is needed.
