Guided Meditation: What Does it Take to Land Here? (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 61:13 2026-01-19

This guided meditation begins with a brief retreat ritual of surrendering phones and settling into shared silence. Matthew Brensilver then leads a practice centered on letting go safely: meeting fear with awareness, trusting the support of the ground, and allowing the breath to signal ease to the body and mind. In the second half, he shifts to walking meditation, emphasizing simplicity, rhythm, and the felt sense of experience over discursive thinking. The talk closes with practical guidance for where to walk at Spirit Rock and how to adapt the practice if walking is difficult.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening Ceremony - Matthew welcomes the retreat and explains the first-morning phone surrender as a symbolic release into practice.
  2. 02:20 Settling Into Silence - After the phone ceremony and bells, the group settles into shared stillness before instructions begin.
  3. 08:31 Letting Go Safely - He asks what needs blessing with awareness so that letting go can feel less threatening and more possible.
  4. 15:30 Honest Practice - Matthew emphasizes not pretending, trusting the Dharma to hold the fullness of life, and breathing with awareness.
  5. 23:44 Breath and Fear - The guidance turns toward feeling how thought can arise from fear and using the breath to soothe agitation.
  6. 34:09 Deepening Into Rest - Longer stretches of quiet are framed as practice in simplicity, acceptance, and a calmer relationship to the mind.
  7. 49:32 Walking Meditation - Matthew introduces walking meditation as a simple rhythm of standing, stepping, turning, and sensing the body directly.
  8. 57:33 Where to Walk - He gives practical directions for the courtyard and meadow, and offers adaptations for people with mobility limits.
