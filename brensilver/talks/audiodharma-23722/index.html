Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Uprooting vs Allowing

AudioDharma 16:04 2025-08-27

Matthew Brensilver answers a question about when to allow experience and when to actively work with unwholesome mind states. He emphasizes that practice is not a fixed hierarchy of techniques, but a matter of discernment, training, and what actually reduces suffering. The talk moves from restraint and equanimity to more active cognitive methods, then into listening for the deeper need beneath jealousy, anger, fear, and self-protection.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 The question of technique - Brensilver introduces the listener's question about open awareness versus directed practice in daily life.
  2. 01:43 Don't fetishize technique - He reframes the issue around suffering and skillful means rather than fixed method or hierarchy.
  3. 02:30 Training before crisis - He describes how practice gradually prevents unwholesome states and makes restraint and letting go more natural.
  4. 04:23 Equanimity and aversion - He warns against mistaking subtle aversion for equanimity and distinguishes genuine curiosity from spiritual bypassing.
  5. 05:58 Marinating versus being with - He clarifies when simple presence is helpful and when it becomes a kind of drowning in unwholesomeness.
  6. 07:49 Active redirection - Using a sutta image of a carpenter removing a peg, he shows how deliberate attention-shifting and reflection can uproot harmful states.
  7. 10:12 Listen for the signal - He turns toward a more investigative approach, asking what information or wounded need may be hidden inside the feeling.
  8. 12:36 Self, fear, and peace - He points to selfing and fear as common roots of clinging, then closes by saying that peace and freedom are the real aim.
