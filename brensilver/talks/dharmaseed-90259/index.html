Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: dropping secret agendas (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 47:13 2025-05-13

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation on loosening hidden striving: the wish to find, fix, control, or curate experience. He moves from relaxing the body and letting the breath, sounds, and awareness arrive on their own, toward a quieter look at how grasping and aversion shape attention and the sense of self. In the final section he turns to walking meditation as a way to carry steadiness, release, and beginning again into daily movement.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Settle and relax - The meditation begins with simple permission to relax and not go looking for anything special.
  2. 02:28 Hidden agendas - Brensilver names the subtle drives beneath practice—wanting to make things happen, make them stay, or control the shape of attention.
  3. 05:57 Wanting and attention - He describes how unnoticed grasping and aversion sponsor meditation and pull attention into the drama of gain and loss.
  4. 10:48 Let the self loosen - The talk turns to the meditator role itself, inviting the listener to let down the endless curation of self and receive the body, breath, and soundscape more simply.
  5. 18:12 Surrendering words - When difficult emotions and old fears arise, he points to the impulse to solve sorrow with words and suggests briefly releasing that habit.
  6. 19:22 Practice in the hall - The middle of the talk settles into extended guidance for sitting practice, emphasizing receptivity, awareness, and staying with experience as it is.
  7. 39:47 First and second arrows - Near the end he distinguishes unavoidable pain from the extra suffering added by resistance, while also acknowledging grief that cannot be dissolved.
  8. 42:21 Walking as training - He closes by explaining walking meditation as training in continuity, letting go at each turn, and carrying calm alertness into ordinary movement.
