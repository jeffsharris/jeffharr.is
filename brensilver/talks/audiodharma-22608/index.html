Dharmette: Question about Etiquette Approaching Teachers

AudioDharma 16:47 2025-03-26

Matthew Brensilver responds to a listener’s question about etiquette in approaching meditation teachers by shifting the focus from rules to relationship. He frames teaching as service and devotion, then explores projection, transference, and the ways teachers and students can use contact to see the mind more clearly. The talk closes with a careful distinction between wanting to be loved and wanting to be understood, and with sincerity as the heart of genuine Dharma exchange.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Question and framing - Matthew introduces the question about approaching teachers and uses a comic reference to open the issue of approval and need.
  2. 02:22 Teaching as service - He suggests teachers want very little from students, and describes teaching as a practice of compassion, devotion, and care for the Dharma.
  3. 04:25 No fixed etiquette - Matthew argues that teachers should meet students flexibly and create unpressured space rather than impose a code of conduct.
  4. 06:36 Projection and transference - He turns to how teacher relationships activate old patterns, including psychoanalytic ideas of projection and transference.
  5. 09:00 Relationship as healing - Matthew says these dynamics can help reveal need and longing, and that spiritual maturity includes tolerating ambivalence.
  6. 10:56 What teachers want - He identifies sincerity as the main quality teachers look for, describing it as willingness to examine the questions that support a life.
  7. 13:39 Loved or understood - Matthew distinguishes the need to be loved from the wish to be understood, and says he is most moved when student and teacher meet in open Dharma rather than role.
