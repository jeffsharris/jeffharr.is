Guided compassion practice: grieving as a factor of growth

Dharma Seed 41:03 2022-03-06

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided compassion practice that moves from settling the body into a reflection on grief as part of spiritual growth. He frames grief not as a failure of practice, but as a natural companion to love, ethical maturity, and a deeper willingness to care. The meditation then expands from holding one suffering person to radiating compassion more broadly, with repeated phrases and a closing blessing for all beings.

Chapters

  1. 00:30 Settling In - The practice begins by softening into posture and breath, inviting the body to become steady and present.
  2. 02:01 Growth and Grief - Matthew introduces the idea that spiritual and moral growth often includes letting go, and that grief can be part of how compassion matures.
  3. 07:09 Waking Up to Loss - He connects grief with the three characteristics, pointing to impermanence, instability, and the inability to find a final home in self or experience.
  4. 09:25 Dharma and Generativity - The teaching shifts toward how Dharma does not remove pain but can protect the heart and make grief more generative for love.
  5. 11:36 Compassion as Action - He says compassion is not only a feeling; it includes willingness to act for peace and relief, while also honoring the simplicity of just caring right now.
  6. 14:44 Taking in Suffering - A formal contemplation invites one suffering person to mind, to bear their pain, and then to send love wholeheartedly toward them.
  7. 20:27 Grief and Karuna - The practice deepens into repeated phrases of blessing, alongside a reflection on unbearable suffering and the mix of grief and love that becomes compassion.
  8. 26:05 Heart Practice - Matthew explores the idea that what feels like pain may actually be the heart, using words, images, and reverberation in the body as ways of recognizing care.
  9. 34:17 Boundless Well-Wishing - He closes with a broader image of measureless compassion for the suffering world and repeated wishes that all beings be held, eased, and supported.
