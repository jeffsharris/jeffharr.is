Matthew Brensilver, Sylvia Boorstein: Day 1: Afternoon Practice Session (part 2) (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 67:30 2018-01-13

Sylvia Boorstein offers a practical talk and guided sitting on how to meet ordinary afflictive states without self-criticism. Using humor, poems, and simple breath instructions, she turns annoyance, sleepiness, restlessness, and doubt into occasions for steadier attention. The talk gradually widens from personal discomfort to compassion, civic engagement, and the ordinary courage of continuing to practice.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the weather - She begins with the disappointing weather and uses it to point toward the basic human habit of meeting what we did not want.
  2. 02:47 Annoyance and humor - A Billy Collins poem about a barking dog becomes a playful way to look at irritation and the mind's tendency to get stuck.
  3. 05:51 Naming the trouble - She names everyday afflictive states like anger, regret, and distress, and reframes them as workable moments rather than failures.
  4. 09:25 Mindfulness as truce - The talk shifts toward making a truce with imperfection, noticing what is present, and choosing a helpful response rather than forcing ease.
  5. 13:33 Breath as antidote - She introduces a guided practice with different breathing instructions to meet sleepiness, restlessness, and doubt with precise attention.
  6. 21:21 Awakening attention - The sitting continues with noticing the breath at its beginnings and endings, then using deeper breathing and a smile to enliven the body.
  7. 33:43 Resting in awareness - She invites a quieter phase of practice, with eyes open to the afternoon light or closed in stillness, and suggests simple self-reminders like 'I'm here, I'm awake.'
  8. 50:29 Compassion and courage - In the closing reflections, she describes spacious awareness, tells a story about a lifelong marcher, and argues that practice supports greater courage and goodwill rather than withdrawal.
  9. 01:05:04 Practice and civic life - She ends by linking meditation with voting and public engagement, then closes gently as the session moves toward dinner.
