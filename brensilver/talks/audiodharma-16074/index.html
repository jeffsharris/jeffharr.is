Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Lightly Guided Meditation

AudioDharma 29:56 2022-04-12

Matthew Brensilver offers a lightly guided meditation centered on breathing softly with the body, especially the exhale, as a way to settle intensity without trying to solve life’s larger problems. The practice moves from posture and full breaths into a patient attention to belly, face, jaw, and a general smoothing of bodily density. In the latter part of the talk, he reflects briefly on how emotional activation can drive thought, then returns to extended silent practice with very occasional reminders.

Chapters

  1. 00:25 Settling In - The talk opens with a brief welcome and an invitation to practice together.
  2. 00:55 Full Breaths - Guidance begins with deep breathing and noticing a wave of relaxation on the exhale.
  3. 02:42 Soften the Body - The attention widens to the belly, face, eyes, and jaw, encouraging a softer body.
  4. 03:44 Breath Rhythm - A short sequence of breath cues helps settle into a steady rhythm of inhaling and exhaling.
  5. 04:50 Smooth the Energy - Brensilver describes how breath can smooth out the energy of body and mind and ease a sense of density.
  6. 07:56 Just for Now - He clarifies that the practice is not meant to resolve life’s complications, but to bring some tranquility and brightness right now.
  7. 09:52 Long Silent Practice - The talk moves into extended silent sitting with occasional exhale reminders, allowing the practice to deepen.
  8. 14:55 Emotion and Thought - He reflects on how emotional circuits and bodily density can fuel thinking, and how softness can make thought feel less urgent.
  9. 17:24 Lovingly Working With Density - The remainder returns to spacious, quiet practice, inviting loving awareness, patience, and ongoing smoothing of bodily intensity.
