Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Relaxing all clinging

AudioDharma 27:07 2025-10-15

This guided meditation explores relaxation as a form of surrender rather than mere physical softening. Matthew Brensilver repeatedly returns to letting go of control, stepping out of narrow identifications, and allowing the mind to rest without feeding familiar grooves of worry and selfing. The practice moves toward tenderness, forgiveness, and trust in the goodness already present, ending in a quieter sense of awareness less dominated by self.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling into surrender - The meditation opens by defining relaxation as surrender and letting go of the agenda to control.
  2. 00:50 Releasing the familiar - The practice turns toward how much of experience can relax, release, and step out of habitual self-protection.
  3. 04:37 Fatigue with old patterns - He points to becoming tired of the same worries and stories, and to attuning to silence instead of feeding the mind.
  4. 09:06 Longing to rest - The guidance frames relaxation as a deep, natural desire to get cozy and release after a long day or a long life.
  5. 10:58 Stepping out of identification - The talk emphasizes that real ease comes from disidentifying from thought, feeling, and perception and leaving compression behind.
  6. 14:40 Forgiveness and kindness - The practice softens into deep forgiveness and kindness for conditioning and for the mind’s habitual ways of finding bearings.
  7. 17:22 Trusting goodness - He encourages relaxing because seeds of goodness are already here, and the sense of self becomes less solid in awareness.
  8. 22:10 Quiet ending - The meditation closes in a quieter, more spacious awareness, followed by the bell.
