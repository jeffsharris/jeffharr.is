Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Tranquilizing side of the 7 Factors: Agitation and temporal density.

Dharma Seed 48:46 2021-09-30

Matthew Brensilver explores the tranquilizing side of the seven factors of awakening, moving from the charged beauty of loving-kindness into the quieter liberation of peace, samadhi, and equanimity. He argues that agitation thins and time feels less dense when the mind stops compulsively predicting, interpreting, and securing itself. The talk blends personal story, poetry, and references to several teachers to show why letting go can feel less like producing a state and more like surrendering into rest.

Chapters

  1. 00:30 Kindness Overflowing - Matthew opens with a retreat story about a 'tunnel of kindness' that became intensely beautiful and emotionally charged.
  2. 04:44 Why Peace Matters - He turns from exuberance to the claim that peace is the only thing that does not fatigue the heart, and frames the tranquilizing factors as a different arc of practice.
  3. 09:43 Road Before the Bend - A Fernando Pessoa poem supports his argument that practice asks us to stay with what is here rather than lean toward what comes next.
  4. 12:13 Agitation And Time - He explains how agitation, vulnerability, and the mind's predictive habit create a sense of temporal density and confinement.
  5. 20:11 Animal Vigilance - Drawing on evolutionary language and everyday examples, he shows how the mind keeps testing safety, security, and self-boundaries.
  6. 27:20 Equanimity Softens - Matthew describes equanimity as a release from vigilance and compulsive meaning-making, and as a way time begins to thin out.
  7. 32:47 Samadhi As Letting Go - Using Ajahn Sucitto and the Buddha, he contrasts pleasure with the stable joy of letting go and presents samadhi as refined, healing enjoyment.
  8. 40:34 Settling Into Refuge - The talk reaches a personal turning point in settledness, then opens into refuge in Buddha, Dhamma, and Sangha as a lived sense of protection and rest.
