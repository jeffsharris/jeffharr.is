Guided Meditation: don't wait to let go

AudioDharma 28:27 2025-08-27

This guided meditation begins with posture, settling, and a simple bodily yielding to gravity and breath. Matthew then turns toward impermanence, sufficiency, and the instruction not to delay letting go, before opening into a brief reflection on loving-kindness and the widening that can come when there is more space around grief. The final minutes include a personal remembrance of Kathleen Haviland, which grounds the talk in loss, relationship, and the practical compassion of practice.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling the body - He begins by asking listeners to find posture, suppleness, and strength, moving into a quiet settling of attention.
  2. 02:41 Yielding to gravity - The practice emphasizes giving body and mind to gravity and the earth while exploring breathing that supports ease and aliveness.
  3. 06:23 Finitude and enough - Matthew reflects on impermanence, suggesting that seeing finitude clearly can make each breath feel sufficient.
  4. 12:16 Do not wait - The central refrain frames letting go as something to do now, with surrender changing how everything is seen.
  5. 13:08 Resting after surrender - A long stretch of quiet practice follows, holding the field opened by the instruction to release and return to breathing.
  6. 22:25 Practice loving - He notes that love is needed because the human condition can feel too much, and invites a simple abiding in loving attention.
  7. 23:44 More space around pain - The meditation turns toward the body-mind as a place where experience can be felt more directly, with less control and more room.
  8. 25:59 Remembering Kathleen - Matthew briefly remembers a friend who died that weekend, using the memory to speak about grief, claustrophobia, and the relief of more space.
