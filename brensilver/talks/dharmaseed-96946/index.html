Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Equanimity as Love in the Face of Helplessness (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 39:06 2026-05-06

Matthew Brensilver explores equanimity not as detachment, but as a kind of love that can remain present when nothing can be fixed. Drawing on Issa, Thich Nhat Hanh, Ajahn Sucitto, and Ajahn Chah, he moves from grief and helplessness into a practical understanding of compassion, rest, and the way love can be purified when it stops insisting on control. The talk then settles into a meditation on breathing and the rhythm of expenditure and rest, returning to the idea that equanimity helps compassion become steadier, less compulsive, and more spacious.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Loss and the question of love - Matthew opens with loss, dukkha, and the uneasy question of whether love must include pain.
  2. 04:09 True love and koans - He brings in Thich Nhat Hanh and frames the questions of love and obligation as koans rather than problems to solve.
  3. 05:30 Compassion and helplessness - Compassion and equanimity are introduced as two wings, with equanimity described as love in the face of helplessness and lack of power.
  4. 08:58 Holding what cannot be fixed - An Ajahn Sucitto story about illness and decline illustrates how presence, touch, and metta remain possible when words and repair no longer work.
  5. 14:21 Already-broken teacup - Ajahn Chah's broken teacup image points to a heart that does not shatter with impermanence because it already sees breaking as the nature of things.
  6. 18:42 Practice in the body - After a short settling, the talk turns toward sensing love in the body as either compassion or equanimity, depending on what the moment allows.
  7. 25:25 Deep time and letting go - Equanimity widens the view to space and deep time, loosening the small, urgent perspective of the self.
  8. 31:07 Rest that restores love - The closing teaching returns to the main theme: equanimity restores energy, purifies compassion, and leaves fewer strings attached to caring.
