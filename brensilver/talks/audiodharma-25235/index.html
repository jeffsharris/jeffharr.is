Dharmette: Ajahn Chah's Advice to Woman on Deathbed

AudioDharma 14:00 2026-04-08

Matthew Brensilver begins by naming his own difficulty finding something to say in a time marked by war, anger, and helplessness, then turns to a remembered teaching from Ajahn Chah as a steadier response. The core of the talk is a deathbed instruction on letting go, seeing body and mind as not-self, and taking awareness and the breath as refuge. Matthew closes by reflecting that these words do not answer everything, but they can still matter as a necessary practice and a kind of love without control.

Chapters

  1. 00:11 Searching for a talk - Matthew describes struggling to find an apt subject and the emotional weight of current events that made ordinary inspiration difficult.
  2. 02:53 Turning to a master - He explains why he chose to read Ajahn Chah’s words as a response to being stuck and overwhelmed.
  3. 03:57 Letting body and world go - The teaching begins with impermanence, not-self, and the instruction to see conditioned things clearly and release them.
  4. 06:06 Awareness as refuge - Ajahn Chah shifts toward practice: take awareness and the breath as refuge, and refine attention until only wakeful awareness remains.
  5. 07:57 No self, no attachment - The teaching widens to family, future, and the world, pointing toward disenchantment and the sobering recognition that nothing can be held as mine.
  6. 09:52 A gift of Dhamma - Ajahn Chah frames the teaching as a gift that can be shared, ending with the claim that Dhamma does not run out.
  7. 11:41 What words cannot do - Matthew reflects on William James, the limits of consolation, and why the instruction to drop the world can still be necessary and even loving.
