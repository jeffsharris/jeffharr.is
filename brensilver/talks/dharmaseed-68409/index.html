Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Reflections and Guided Heart Practice

Dharma Seed 36:34 2021-05-20

Matthew Brensilver opens with a brief communal reflection on the brahma-viharas and then turns toward why loving-kindness matters in practice: not as a pleasant mood, but as a softening of the heart. He connects this to a Buddha story about the “abodes of Brahma,” then reads Raymond Carver’s “Late Fragment” to widen the question of what it means to feel beloved on the earth. The second half becomes a guided loving-kindness practice centered on receiving kindness, first from imagined benefactors and then, if it feels right, extending it outward.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening the Heart Practice - Matthew introduces the evening’s loving-kindness period and names the heart practices that will guide it.
  2. 00:33 Community and Receiving - The talk begins with a shared orientation to community, slowing down together, and letting kindness be present among practitioners.
  3. 05:06 Kindness Over Attention - He explains why softening the heart can matter more than simply trying to stay attentive, and how that changes the sense of practice.
  4. 09:01 Brahma-viharas Explained - Matthew tells the Buddha story of the Brahma-viharas as the abode of Brahma and links them to a free heart.
  5. 11:35 Freedom and Tenderness - He contrasts Buddhist freedom with the wish for a pleasant life all the time and points toward a heart that is tender and responsive.
  6. 13:51 Raymond Carver's Question - A short poem by Raymond Carver deepens the teaching by naming the wish to feel oneself beloved on the earth.
  7. 16:29 Metta as Free Heart - Matthew defines loving-kindness as friendliness and freedom, and emphasizes that practice includes both feeling it and not feeling it.
  8. 20:03 Settling and Imagining - The guided practice begins with posture, ease, and an imagined circle of beings who love and encourage you.
  9. 24:31 Receiving Kindness - He invites listeners to hear phrases of goodwill, let kindness fill the body, and stay with whatever response arises.
