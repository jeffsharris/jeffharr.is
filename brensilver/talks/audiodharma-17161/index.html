Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Affect and Motivation

AudioDharma 53:24 2022-10-26

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation that begins with posture, breath, and a felt sense of the body as a constellation of sensations rather than a fixed outline. He then turns toward affect and motivation, encouraging close attention to the body’s signals of wanting, aversion, and urgency without preferring spaciousness over solidification. In the later teaching, he links clinging to these affective circuits, points to how narratives try to explain experience, and returns to the value of staying with practice, equanimity, and the pace of awareness.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling into posture - The talk opens with a brief orientation to upright, grounded presence, balancing sky and earth.
  2. 02:30 Breath and relaxation - He invites a few breaths that support relaxation and begin to soften the body.
  3. 03:58 Body as sensation - Attention shifts from body image to the changing field of sensations, with the body felt as porous and spacious.
  4. 08:51 Softening the heart - The repeated invitation to let the breath reach the heart deepens the sense of openness and pervasiveness.
  5. 12:29 No preference for spaciousness - He emphasizes that mindfulness and love should dissolve solidity without clinging to openness as a better state.
  6. 20:57 Affect and motivation - The teaching moves into urges, pressure, liking and disliking, showing how emotional movement arises in the body.
  7. 26:52 Knowing and letting go - He explores how knowing is suffused by wanting, and repeatedly returns to gentle, non-grasping awareness.
  8. 35:31 Clinging and narrative - Clinging is linked to affective circuits, and he describes how stories about self and life grow from bodily feeling.
  9. 46:23 Practice rhythms and closing - The talk ends with guidance to stay close, work at the pace of awareness, meet boredom with equanimity, and then transitions into retreat announcements.
