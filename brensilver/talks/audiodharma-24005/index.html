Dharmette: PR Campaign for Non-Hatred

AudioDharma 16:34 2025-10-15

Matthew Brensilver reflects on what part of the Dharma could have public reach in a time shaped by anger and polarization. He argues that non-hatred may be the most realistic and useful teaching to broadcast, distinguishing it from love while grounding it in bodily, relational, and canonical evidence. The talk closes with a William Stafford poem that deepens the call for mutual recognition and clear signals in dark times.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Opening Context - Matthew greets the group and situates the talk in a broader question about where the Dharma can be useful now.
  2. 00:59 Dharma And Public Reach - He reflects on the limits of Dharma dissemination and asks what teaching could genuinely scale without becoming esoteric.
  3. 03:49 Choosing Non-Hatred - He arrives at non-hatred as the teaching he would feature in a public campaign, rather than love or other more demanding ideals.
  4. 05:19 The Cost Of Hatred - He describes how hatred distorts perception, blocks empathy, and harms both individuals and communities, with Baldwin and the Buddha as supporting voices.
  5. 09:03 Non-Hatred In Practice - Matthew distinguishes non-hatred from love and argues that releasing hatred does not require giving up ethical commitments.
  6. 10:55 Guard The Senses - He turns to practical cultivation, linking the Buddha's advice to guard the senses with the work of protecting attention and intention.
  7. 12:44 Stafford's Warning - A William Stafford poem is read as a companion text on knowing one another, avoiding small betrayals, and keeping signals clear in dark times.
