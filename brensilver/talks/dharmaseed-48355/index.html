Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Matthew Brensilver, Sylvia Boorstein: Day 2: Afternoon Practice Session (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 1:37:25 2018-01-14

This afternoon practice session moves from a simple story about family irritation into a wide-ranging reflection on attention, suffering, and the way practice changes how life is seen. Sylvia Boorstein uses stories from ordinary life, death, and retreat practice to show how clarity can cut through editorializing and reveal what is actually happening. The talk closes by returning to loving-kindness in movement, with walking practice turned toward wishing well for everyone encountered.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Practice as attention - She begins by framing all postures as meditation and uses a story to show how purposeful attention changes what the mind makes of a moment.
  2. 08:36 Widening the field - The talk shifts from a private practice story toward metta, including the idea of meditating on behalf of others and resting in shared goodwill.
  3. 22:57 Retreat and first tears - After a long stretch of guided sitting and silence, she reflects on being moved by retreat and on how practice often begins quietly rather than dramatically.
  4. 44:51 Inching into Dharma - She describes her first contact with practice through friends and explains how Dharma slowly begins to claim more of life than we expect.
  5. 55:22 Perspective and mortality - A teaching story about two pockets, along with reflections on mortality and multi-generational responsibility, widens the frame from personal problems to ethical life.
  6. 01:06:56 Clarity and the heart - The conversation turns to enlightenment, clear seeing, and the link between understanding, kindness, and the possibility of living with less confusion.
  7. 01:18:57 Renunciation and dying - Stories of Mother Teresa, a dying friend, and a teacher’s advice on enjoyment are used to show renunciation as not wanting reality to be other than it is.
  8. 01:29:55 The path in practice - She summarizes the Four Noble Truths and the Eightfold Path as a mutually reinforcing circle, emphasizing wise effort and self-compassion.
  9. 01:35:08 Walking with goodwill - The session ends with instructions for counterclockwise walking and a direct invitation to notice others and wish them well.
