Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Attention and Love

AudioDharma 30:21 2024-12-11

Matthew Brensilver leads a quiet guided meditation that begins with settling the body, moving attention through chest, belly, breath, and earthiness. The practice gradually opens into a reflection on how attention reveals love and care, then turns toward the difficulty of hatred and the choice to meet it with breathing and affection. The close is sparse and intimate, with repeated pointers to simply feel love and allow it to be known.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and Settling - After a brief welcome, the talk moves into silence and the first instructions for settling into practice.
  2. 02:18 Arriving in the Body - Attention is invited to settle as if for the first time, preparing for a more embodied practice.
  3. 04:01 Chest, Breath, Belly - The meditation moves through the heart center, the lungs, and then down into the belly as a place of groundedness.
  4. 08:24 Earth and Rootedness - Breath is used to connect more deeply with the earth element and the steadiness of being rooted.
  5. 11:44 Attention Reveals Love - The teaching shifts from body awareness to the idea that careful attention helps reveal what we love and care about.
  6. 15:09 Patience with Longing - Rather than forcing answers, the practice emphasizes patience and letting the poignancy of being born arise on its own.
  7. 17:23 Heart Energy in the Body - From groundedness, the heart energy is invited to fill the body, with emphasis on tenderness, flexibility, and wishing ourselves well.
  8. 21:43 Care and the Wider Field - The reflection broadens into ways attention can express care for one’s life, with the meditation holding space for what arises.
  9. 26:59 Love over Hatred - A poetic sequence reframes hatred as something brittle and unnecessary, ending with the invitation to breathe and feel love directly.
