Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: The Clouds are Sky Too

AudioDharma 43:02 2017-08-19

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation centered on relaxing effort and resting with awareness rather than clinging to the meditation object. The practice moves from settling the body and mind into a direct inquiry into present-moment experience, including a repeated reflection on whether anything is really being waited for. As the meditation unfolds, he uses the sky-and-clouds image, then turns it inside out, inviting a softer sense that experience itself can be held with permission and ease.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling with awareness - The meditation opens by emphasizing care for awareness itself and easing the habit of over-focusing on the object.
  2. 05:49 Not waiting anymore - He repeatedly asks what it would be like to be alive without waiting for anything, loosening the future-oriented mind.
  3. 10:09 Safety in the present - The talk turns toward the ways the present can feel unsafe and how stillness and community support can help release into it.
  4. 15:41 Permission to arise - He invites complete permission for experience to be as it is, while noting that action and change still have their place.
  5. 25:34 Radical acceptance - The practice deepens into not taking a stand for or against experience, including even preferences and preferences about preferences.
  6. 31:54 Sky and clouds - Using the familiar metaphor of awareness as sky and thoughts or feelings as clouds, he asks what happens if the distinction is not so fixed.
  7. 37:55 Rumi's ocean image - A repeated Rumi refrain about diving into the ocean reinforces surrender, disappearance of effort, and the sea of experience.
  8. 41:00 Putting down effort - The closing notes point to what may stop when meditation stops: extra efforting, strain, and the habit of doing more than needed.
