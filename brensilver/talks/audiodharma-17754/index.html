Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Dharma & Grief

AudioDharma 14:28 2023-02-16

Matthew Brensilver reflects on grief as a core part of Dharma rather than a feeling to bypass. He moves from the Buddha’s encounter with mortality to the ways grief shows up in letting go, ethical growth, and widening compassion. The talk closes by suggesting that grief can be fluid and transformative, even “pregnant with bodhicitta.”

Chapters

  1. 00:17 Buddha and mortality - Brensilver frames the talk with the Buddha’s encounter with aging, sickness, death, and the grief that can awaken the spiritual search.
  2. 01:54 What grief means - He questions the idea that grief is simply afflictive, and suggests it is tied to love, sensitivity, and moral awareness.
  3. 04:01 Grief in change - The talk turns to Dharma practice itself, describing letting go and inner change as experiences that often feel like grief.
  4. 05:56 Suffering and empathy - He links grief to comprehending suffering, ethical maturation, and the widening circle of empathy.
  5. 07:26 Illusions and mortality - Brensilver briefly draws on Shelley Taylor, Metzinger, and Nietzsche to show why people cling to comforting illusions and avoid mortality.
  6. 09:35 Grief into practice - He argues that Dharma can condition the heart toward truth, motivating practice, compassion, and real growth rather than hopelessness.
  7. 11:27 Fluid grief - Using James Baldwin, he distinguishes transformative grief from grief that becomes stagnation or a substitute for change.
  8. 12:52 Pregnant with bodhicitta - The talk closes with Pema Chodron’s image of grief as carrying awakening within it, transmuting suffering into wisdom and love.
