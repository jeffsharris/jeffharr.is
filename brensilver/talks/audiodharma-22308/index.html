Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Equanimity 2

AudioDharma 29:41 2025-02-16

Matthew Brensilver explores equanimity as a way of meeting experience without turning every sensation into a problem to solve. He traces how the mind reads events through self-relevance, how pleasant and unpleasant feelings trigger strategy and control, and why relaxing the impulse to manage each moment can reveal a simpler flow of arising and passing. The talk closes with a repeated settling into letting go, making it especially useful for listeners interested in how equanimity works in practice, not as indifference but as a softer relationship to feeling.

Chapters

  1. 03:34 Self-Relevance - Brensilver introduces how phenomena are evaluated through their significance to the project of the self.
  2. 05:37 Pleasant and Unpleasant - He describes how experiences feel pleasant when they support the project of life and unpleasant when they threaten it.
  3. 07:02 Alarm and Strategy - The talk turns to how alarm makes the present into a problem, pushing the mind toward control and disengagement from direct feeling.
  4. 09:24 What Equanimity Means - He reframes equanimity as dissolving the need to solve everything, allowing experience to be seen as arising and passing.
  5. 13:18 Desperation Around Pain - Brensilver names the panic that often arises with unpleasantness and distinguishes bodily pain from the larger prediction of ruin.
  6. 16:01 Relaxing the Bracing - He invites a relaxation of judgment, contraction, and the urgency to govern life moment by moment.
  7. 17:26 Letting Go Practice - The talk closes with a sustained repetition of letting go, emphasizing the felt release rather than a conceptual conclusion.
