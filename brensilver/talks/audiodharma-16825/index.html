Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Dharma as Existential Therapy

AudioDharma 14:00 2022-08-19

Matthew Brensilver frames Dharma practice as a kind of existential therapy: a way of meeting death, impermanence, freedom, isolation, and responsibility without flinching. He moves from stark quotations about mortality into a quiet case for practicing with groundlessness, building meaning from within lived experience, and becoming more ethically responsive to others. The talk stays practical and grounded, with literary and Buddhist references used to clarify the work of waking up.

Chapters

  1. 00:25 Facing Mortality - Matthew opens with Russell and a Zen-style reminder to put mortality and urgency at the center of practice.
  2. 01:58 Dharma as Therapy - He names Dharma as attention training, cognitive therapy, exposure therapy, and finally existential therapy.
  3. 04:18 Learning Groundlessness - He describes anicca as something the heart acclimatizes to through bearable doses of impermanence and loss.
  4. 06:34 Meaning Is Made - Using Yalom and the Buddha, he argues that the world is contingent and that meaning has to be built rather than found.
  5. 08:20 Building a Life - He turns to Philip Appleman and Leonard Cohen to suggest that practice helps us get straight with ourselves and spend our freedom well.
  6. 10:05 Alone, Yet Responsible - Matthew reflects on aloneness, then broadens the question to what we owe others across distance and time.
  7. 11:57 Do Better - He closes with Maya Angelou’s ethic of responding as we learn, followed by brief thanks and farewells.
