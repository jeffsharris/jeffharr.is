Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Day 6: Sitting with Instructions (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 51:52 2018-11-21

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet instruction for sitting with honesty rather than performance. He moves from noticing urgency and defining the edge of attention, into a longer reflection on impermanence, where the present moment is not something to freeze but something to trust as it changes. The talk then turns toward mettā practice and closes with a practical reminder about continuity of awareness in ordinary life.

Chapters

  1. 01:16 Starting the sit - He begins with a gentle body-and-mind scan and points to places where urgency is present.
  2. 01:51 Not pretending - The teaching emphasizes that meditation does not require forcing acceptance, and that this moment can be met as imperfect yet good enough.
  3. 07:31 Drawing the boundary - He explains the practical difference between the area of focus and distraction, and why it helps to know exactly where attention is being placed.
  4. 15:24 Urge and tension - The talk turns to how the mind leans toward the next breath, the next insight, or the future, and how that subtle reaching can be released.
  5. 23:25 No banks on this river - Using the image of a river without banks, he describes surrendering into change and the flow of anicca rather than freezing the moment.
  6. 32:44 Offering experience - He shifts from trying to get something from practice to offering oneself to experience and trusting the logic of Dharma.
  7. 37:43 Turning to mettā - The talk moves into loving-kindness, with repeated phrases of goodwill and a closing reflection on what changes when the bell ends the sit.
  8. 48:16 Continuity off the cushion - He ends by comparing continuity of awareness to not skipping chapters in a book and then gives retreat announcements.
