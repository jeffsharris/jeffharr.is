Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Practice Note: Waiting for Nothing

AudioDharma 02:47 2016-05-18

Matthew Brensilver offers a brief practice note on the difference between habitually waiting for something and the more radical stance of “waiting for nothing.” He frames meditation as resting at the edge of the present moment, willing to live without leaning toward a future payoff. The talk ends with a compact instruction for walking, sitting, and exploring this attitude directly in practice.

Chapters

  1. 00:10 Small theme - He introduces a small theme for practice and turns toward the way attention often leans into the future.
  2. 00:29 Waiting for something - He names the ordinary habit of waiting for something to happen and seeking redemption ahead of us.
  3. 01:04 Waiting for nothing - He contrasts that habit with meditation as waiting for nothing and resting on the edge of the present.
  4. 01:22 Living without a future - He describes a willingness to live as if there were no future, not as a mortality teaching but as a stance of surrender.
  5. 01:47 Radical mindfulness - He calls this a radical form of mindfulness and points to the spirit of the practice.
  6. 02:04 The invitation - He asks how to sit and walk in the strange place where waiting is present but nothing is being waited for.
  7. 02:30 Practice instruction - He closes by naming the instruction as deliberately cryptic and inviting listeners to explore it for themselves.
