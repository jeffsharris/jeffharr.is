Guided Meditation: Lightly Guided Meditation

AudioDharma 30:36 2024-03-14

Matthew Brensilver offers a lightly guided meditation that keeps returning to receptive listening: allowing experience to be received before it hardens into resistance or identification. The talk moves from settling the body, to noticing the quiet agenda of “me” in meditation, to softening around wanting, control, and the effort to make something happen. Near the end, he frames practice in terms of faith — the possibility that something can hold us even when control is released.

Chapters

  1. 00:30 Settling In - The meditation begins with posture and a simple invitation to become receptive to Dharma and to one’s own wisdom.
  2. 01:30 Deep Listening - Matthew introduces the idea that what is not received in awareness becomes identification, urging a listening that does not turn into resistance.
  3. 05:52 The Project of Me - Awareness notices the subtle tension of trying to make meditation happen and the transactional mind that issues demands to the moment.
  4. 10:01 Wanting and Agenda - The practice turns toward background wanting, bargaining with the present, and the way knowing becomes entangled with those agendas.
  5. 14:12 Trusting Awareness - He points toward a confidence that remains after effort and strategy dissolve, encouraging trust in awareness that does not depend on the self.
  6. 21:20 Control or Connection - Using Michelle McDonald’s question, he explores whether practice is driven by control or connection and suggests that release is possible.
  7. 25:47 Faith in Letting Go - The meditation closes with repeated reassurance that something can hold us beyond control, ending with a quiet emphasis on release and willingness.
