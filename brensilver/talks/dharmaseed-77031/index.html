Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

The Three Characteristics and Love

Dharma Seed 44:49 2023-03-31

Matthew Brensilver reflects on how retreat can make the heart more porous, so that insight does not stay abstract but becomes a felt opening to love. He moves from the three characteristics—dukkha, impermanence, and not-self—toward the way these teachings soften defensiveness, grief, and self-clinging. The talk gradually lands in a quiet claim: when ownership loosens, what remains is a very ordinary, stable kind of love.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the Retreat Mind - He begins by noticing how retreat impressions fade and asking what is actually real about experience.
  2. 01:46 Retreat as Recollection - The talk develops the sense that retreat is not artificial but a remembering of something native to the heart.
  3. 05:07 Love and Receptivity - He uses Plato, falling in love, and the atmosphere of retreat to show how Dharma opens the heart and makes it more receptive.
  4. 11:48 Wisdom With Love - Bringing in Roxanne and Pascal, he argues that wisdom and love belong together and that the Buddha cared about what leads to freedom.
  5. 15:07 Three Characteristics - He turns to Bhante Gunaratana and unpacks how impermanence, unsatisfactoriness, and not-self can be directly transformative.
  6. 18:14 Difficulties as Food - Using Ajahn Sucitto, Lao Tzu, and a story from his social work days, he reframes problems as material to digest rather than enemies to fight.
  7. 24:34 Grief and Change - He deepens the discussion of separateness, loss, and anicca, showing how grief can become a more radical love.
  8. 29:59 Non-Self and Ownership - Norman Fisher, Jennifer Beer, and a scene from The Wire help him explore self-clinging, defensiveness, and the freedom of non-ownership.
  9. 39:05 Letting Go - He closes by linking self-acceptance, surrender, and reverence, ending with a simple emphasis on love and release.
