Guided Meditation: The Breath and Awareness don't depend on you

AudioDharma 30:01 2024-05-08

Matthew Brensilver leads a quiet guided meditation that begins with settling the body and reframing the breath as something that does not depend on the usual sense of “me.” From there he turns toward awareness itself, emphasizing that it also is not owned or controlled, and uses the language of emptiness to soften selfing. The back half of the talk lingers in long repetitions and then opens into an image of egoic “proprioception,” showing how a sense of self is continually maintained until awareness becomes less identified and more spacious.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling In - Opening remarks, a brief schedule note, and an invitation to arrive and practice together.
  2. 01:15 Breath Beyond Self - The meditation begins by emphasizing that breathing does not depend on the constructed sense of me or on effortful control.
  3. 03:27 Awareness Receives - The teaching shifts from the breath to awareness, pointing out that attention and knowing also do not depend on personal will.
  4. 05:22 Emptiness Repeated - A long contemplative stretch returns again and again to emptiness, letting the phrase do the work of loosening fixation.
  5. 11:07 Ego's Bureaucracy - Matthew cites Trungpa Rinpoche to describe insight as stepping out of the bureaucracy and micromanagement of ego.
  6. 13:08 Same Stuff, Rising - He expands on self-related phenomena as part of the same flux as everything else, marked by the openness of awareness and the effortlessness of breathing.
  7. 17:42 Egoic Proprioception - Using the body’s balance sense as a comparison, he describes how the ego keeps tabs on itself and continually re-creates a sense of place.
  8. 20:22 Less and Less Me - The closing movement suggests that, as sensitivity grows, the self stops feeling like a home base and other modes of awareness come online.
