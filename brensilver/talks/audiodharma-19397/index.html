Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Not updating your autobiography

AudioDharma 29:36 2023-12-11

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation and reflection on not turning each moment into a continuation of the personal story. He repeatedly returns to the body, breath, and sensory precision, then widens into equanimity, non-clinging, and a kind of futurelessness that lets this moment be enough. The talk moves gently between instruction and reflection, emphasizing surrender without erasing memory or life history.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Settling In - Matthew welcomes the group and begins the meditation with simple settling instructions.
  2. 02:37 Body and Breath - He orients attention to the felt sense of the body and breath, not trying to make pain pleasant but to include it more fully.
  3. 06:19 Awareness Spreads - The practice continues with repeated invitations to sit and let awareness fill out the body, soothing hot spots where possible.
  4. 10:20 Not the Next Chapter - He turns to the main reflection: practice is not a continuation of the autobiography, but a surrender of that story-making.
  5. 12:09 Letting Go of Story - He names the many ways we keep refreshing identity through likes, dislikes, plans, and memories, and asks for more precise attention to present sensation.
  6. 19:07 Equanimity and Non-clinging - He reflects on how experience is always mixed, and how equanimity means not needing to sort everything into success or failure.
  7. 21:17 Self as Hologram - He suggests the sense of self can freeze through clinging, like a hologram cast by spotlights, and that non-clinging loosens it.
  8. 26:58 Futurelessness - The closing reflection invites surrender into a sense of futurelessness, a different way of learning that does not leverage the present for later.
