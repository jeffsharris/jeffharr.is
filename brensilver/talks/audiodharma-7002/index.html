Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Feedback & the Architecture of Self

AudioDharma 13:20 2016-09-14

Matthew Brensilver frames the talk around Shinzen Young’s two maxims: don’t fight with yourself, and take feedback. From there he explores how criticism and misunderstanding can reveal the hidden architecture of self, showing how defensiveness protects self-view and how practice can gradually make us less brittle. He closes by linking this inner work to becoming safer, more open company for others.

Chapters

  1. 00:31 Two Maxims - Matthew introduces Shinzen Young’s two simple guidelines and uses them as the frame for the talk.
  2. 01:35 Learning From Feedback - He distinguishes concrete feedback from metaphor and argues that ordinary experience can teach us if we are willing to receive it.
  3. 02:58 Delusion and Self-Image - He connects feedback to seeing the blind spots of the mind and to loosening the habit of assuming we already know.
  4. 05:07 Seeing Self-Clinging - Matthew explains how feedback makes the structure of self-view visible, especially where defensiveness protects identity.
  5. 07:10 Becoming Unoffendable - He describes a Dharma practice of working through raw places in ego so that criticism and misapprehension do not automatically trigger pain.
  6. 09:30 Tolerating Disorientation - The talk turns to the instability that arises when self-view is challenged and the need to stay with that disorientation without scrambling to re-solidify.
  7. 10:51 Safer For Others - He broadens the practice outward, describing how non-defensiveness and willingness to be wrong can make us more trustworthy and refuge-like for others.
