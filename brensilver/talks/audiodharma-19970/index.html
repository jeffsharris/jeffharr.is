Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Attention and Sustaining Openness

AudioDharma 30:01 2024-03-12

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation centered on two linked skills: training attention and sustaining openness when intensity arises. The talk repeatedly returns to how awareness narrows into problem-solving and how practice can gently re-open the field, with attention to the body, breath, soundscape, and a sense of okayness. Rather than building a conceptual map, it keeps pointing back to direct experience, patience, and relinquishment as ways to meet ordinary and subtle stress.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Practice frame - Brensilver introduces the distinction between focusing attention and staying open, and explains why the practice looks at small moments of collapse.
  2. 02:50 Settle into posture - He invites the sit, encourages a simple posture, and points toward trusting enough goodness to begin.
  3. 05:13 Intensity and collapse - The meditation turns toward how life’s intensity narrows awareness into problem-solving, and how to notice that movement gently.
  4. 09:38 Reverence for dukkha - He names the First Noble Truth and explores how attention to breath, body, and sound can make the mind less brittle.
  5. 13:43 Good enough awareness - The guidance emphasizes patience, forgiveness, and a quieter relation to the urge to optimize or fix the moment.
  6. 19:47 Re-establish openness - He suggests returning to okayness, letting energy unwind, and rebuilding the open field before leaning again on an anchor.
  7. 26:31 Not solving - The closing reflection points to the simple relief of not solving everything and resting in awareness instead.
