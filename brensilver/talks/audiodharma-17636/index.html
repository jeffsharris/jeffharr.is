Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Five Spiritual Faculties - Mindfulness (3 of 5)

AudioDharma 14:30 2023-01-25

Matthew Brensilver uses this short dharmette to unpack what mindfulness is, and just as importantly, what it is not. After noting how the word can get overloaded or diluted, he turns to the Satipatthana Sutta and sketches mindfulness as the opposite of many common features of distress: rumination, fragmentation, avoidance, rigid self-stories, and moralism. The talk stays close to lived experience, ending with mindfulness as a more spacious, stable, less self-centered way of meeting what arises.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Opening the topic - He begins by greeting the group and naming mindfulness as the final topic in a short sequence on the spiritual faculties.
  2. 00:46 A loaded word - Using Nietzsche’s image of words as pockets, he explains how mindfulness can gather many meanings and be stretched in unhelpful ways.
  3. 02:16 Satipatthana baseline - He turns to the classical definition of right mindfulness and frames it as something to understand through practice, not just definition.
  4. 03:33 How distress feels - He sketches shared features of distress: thoughts that feel absolute, rumination, narrowing of experience, and unstable attention.
  5. 05:19 What suffering does - The talk moves through more markers of distress, including arousal shifts, avoidance, low tolerance for pain, personalization, and rigid models of self and world.
  6. 08:38 Less fusion, more space - He describes mindfulness as reduced fusion with thought, de-reification, and a less intimidating inner world, linking this to equanimity.
  7. 10:07 Stability and tolerance - Mindfulness is presented as non-interference, attentional stability, and a growing capacity to stay present with charged experience.
  8. 12:29 Less self, more wakefulness - He closes with mindfulness as a relaxed, hypo-egoic openness in which the self is less central and awareness remains awake.
