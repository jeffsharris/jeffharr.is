Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Morning Instructions

AudioDharma 40:03 2019-10-05

Matthew Brensilver offers a grounded morning instruction centered on paying attention to life as it is, with care rather than ambition. He moves from meeting hindrances and “Mara” directly, to sensing the body as a changing field, to the Buddha’s teaching on conditionality as the basis for a more natural love. The talk closes by noticing subtle efforting in meditation and returning to curiosity without agenda.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 This Life, Attended To - He opens by framing practice as honoring life through attention and a simple willingness to be present.
  2. 02:27 Meeting Hindrance - The instruction turns to relaxing bodily effort and noticing greed, aversion, restlessness, dullness, doubt, and bewilderment without adding drama.
  3. 04:39 Directness Over Struggle - He emphasizes not meeting difficulty with force, but with a clear, non-reactive awareness that can simply say, 'I see you.'
  4. 07:53 Seeing Conditionality - Using the Buddha’s teaching on cause and effect, he links insight into conditions with the softening of self-harshness and hatred.
  5. 10:48 Body As Flow - The talk moves into holding the body in tenderness, noticing physicality, emotionality, and the constant shifting of sensation.
  6. 14:27 Image And Sensation - He distinguishes between imagining the body and directly feeling sensation, encouraging awareness to stay close to the changing experience itself.
  7. 18:17 No Hidden Agenda - He ends by inviting a periodic check for subtle striving, and a return to curiosity that is not trying to manufacture experience.
