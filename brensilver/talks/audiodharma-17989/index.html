Guided Meditation: Not reading the tea leaves of the body

AudioDharma 37:13 2023-03-18

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation centered on meeting body experience without turning it into a puzzle to solve. He repeatedly returns to the idea that sensations, emotions, and alarms can be received as data, then gently released, rather than interpreted as signs of what will happen next. Near the end, he brings in the Mula Sutta to frame each experience as a doorway to Dharma, with liberation rather than self-analysis as the orientation.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening into welcome - The talk begins with a broad, reassuring opening that evokes many inner worlds and the Dharma field as always available.
  2. 02:43 Receiving the body - After a stretch of silence, the instruction turns toward simply receiving the body as it is, without trying to fix it.
  3. 05:09 Not solving problems - Matthew warns against treating life as a riddle to solve and invites problems to dissolve in awareness, embodiment, and love.
  4. 07:16 Past and future pressure - He describes how the mind writes an autobiography of each moment and gets squeezed between memory and prediction.
  5. 14:38 False alarms - In the safety of the practice setting, body and mind alarms are asked to be held lightly, just for now, as false alarms.
  6. 17:01 This moment only - The meditation narrows into repeated invitations to place all hope into the present and stop reading the tea leaves of bodily sensation.
  7. 23:31 Letting go repeatedly - A long series of release instructions emphasizes that sensation, feeling, and notion can all mean the same thing here: let go.
  8. 28:53 Liberation in the sutta - Matthew cites the Mula Sutta to say that the core of all phenomena is liberation and that every wall can become a door to Dharma.
  9. 33:44 Renouncing words - The close returns to renouncing the urge to build the world out of words and staying intimate with body and feeling, neither heaven nor hell.
