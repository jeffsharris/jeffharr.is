Lightly Guided Meditation

AudioDharma 26:48 2026-04-08

Matthew Brensilver offers a lightly guided meditation on opening to this moment by softening obvious tension and the subtler habits of control. The talk moves from surrender and the release of dividing lines, toward a steadier sense of urgency that is not driven by desperation. It closes with a quiet inquiry into awareness, vulnerability, and what can actually be harmed.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Opening to this moment - He begins by asking what it means to open to the present without immediately tightening around it.
  2. 01:19 Putting down control - The guidance turns toward releasing grasping, aversion, and the subtler effort to make life happen.
  3. 05:39 Breath and bracing - He invites the breath to signal safety to the deeper mind and notices the subconscious lines that separate self from experience.
  4. 07:51 Urgency without strain - The meditation develops a careful sense of impermanence and urgency that does not collapse into self-pressure.
  5. 13:50 Awake, not deliberate - He distinguishes vivid awareness from technique and points to non-delusion as something deeper than method.
  6. 17:48 Awareness and vulnerability - The talk settles into the possibility that awareness does not shield us from life, but can change how it is met.
  7. 23:42 What can be harmed - It ends with a brief, open inquiry into harm, fragility, and the meeting of one gust of wind with another.
