Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Much Becomes Incidental

AudioDharma 28:16 2025-04-02

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet guided meditation centered on listening to silence and letting the usual mental inventory become incidental. The practice repeatedly returns to receiving the moment without project-making, self-management, or the need to solve experience. As the talk unfolds, surrender is framed not as passivity but as a temporary release of control in which even difficulty can be held more lightly.

Chapters

  1. 00:13 Listening to silence - The meditation begins by inviting attention to silence itself, even when there is noise around it.
  2. 01:58 What becomes incidental - Pleasant and unpleasant feeling, autobiography, and fixed ideas are allowed to recede for a little while.
  3. 03:43 Receiving the moment - The teaching settles into the simple stance that receiving the present moment is enough.
  4. 06:41 No projects, no building - Brensilver emphasizes dropping the impulse to build, manage, or improve experience.
  5. 08:29 Surrender and love - Surrender is described as the dissolution of volitional energy, with love named as the one thing worth building.
  6. 09:27 Receiving space - The practice widens into a spacious resting in which space seems to receive space.
  7. 16:54 A new chance to let go - The talk returns to the ordinary urge to medicate feelings and thought, and frames the session as another opportunity to release control.
  8. 20:18 Emerging unchanged - The closing notes suggest that life will still be there afterward, the same and yet slightly different after practice.
