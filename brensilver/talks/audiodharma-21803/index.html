Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Letting the Dharma Soak into You

AudioDharma 29:12 2024-11-27

This guided meditation invites a gentle, receptive relationship with practice rather than an effort to force it. Matthew Brensilver emphasizes settling into the body, meeting agitation with patience and quiet love, and tending tenderly to any sense of distance from the Dharma. The talk later turns to recollecting Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha as a way of re-entering sensitivity, urgency, and trust, before ending in a simple offering of the heart with no strings attached.

Chapters

  1. 02:16 Settling In - The meditation opens by finding some sweetness in sangha, technology, and lineage, then asks for a gradual settling of attention.
  2. 05:18 Body and Earth - Attention moves into the body on the earth, with repeated emphasis on gravity as a kind of embrace.
  3. 06:50 Meeting Unsettledness - Agitation, tension, and numbness are met with patience, tolerance, awareness, and quiet love.
  4. 08:56 When Dharma Feels Distant - Matthew names the felt remoteness of practice and invites listeners not to force connection, but to tend the sense of separation gently.
  5. 14:03 Recalling the Triple Gem - Recollecting Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha is presented as a way to remember, soften, and enter greater sensitivity and care.
  6. 16:23 Courage To Keep Looking - The meditation deepens into the courage of meeting this moment as it is, staying open to surprise rather than assuming we already know it.
  7. 23:57 Offering Without Strings - The close releases expectation and simply offers the heart to the Dharma, without insisting on any result.
