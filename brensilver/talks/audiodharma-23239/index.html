Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Meditating on June 19

AudioDharma 43:40 2025-06-19

This guided meditation moves gently between breath, body, and awareness, then opens into a quiet reflection on love, freedom, and nonviolence. Matthew Brensilver names Juneteenth and uses it to frame the practice as staying close to love, feeling the intensity of being human, and sensing the innocence of the wish for peace. Later, he points to the image of peace at every sense door—body, eye, ear, and mind—as a way of understanding awakening as a soft, loving openness.

Chapters

  1. 02:30 Finding the Anchor - The guidance begins by exploring different possible anchors, from the whole field of awareness to the breath and the felt body.
  2. 04:42 Staying with Awareness - The instruction settles into repeated attention to awareness itself and the way the mind colors each moment.
  3. 07:47 Juneteenth and Freedom - Matthew names Juneteenth and reflects on cruelty, freedom, and the need to remain close to love.
  4. 12:13 Peace at the Senses - He draws on a traditional Buddhist description of enlightenment as peace at the body, eye, ear, and mind doors.
  5. 15:53 Stopping the Fight - The practice turns toward a felt release from self-conflict and conflict with the world, toward radical nonviolence.
  6. 17:19 Settling in Silence - A long stretch of quiet holding follows, giving space for the practice to deepen without much verbal instruction.
