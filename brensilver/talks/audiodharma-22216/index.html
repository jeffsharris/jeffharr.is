Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Remembering Ann Buck

AudioDharma 15:31 2025-02-05

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet remembrance of Ann Buck, beginning with a morning scene, a Dylan lyric, and the feeling of mortality that opened the talk. From there he moves through memories of Ann’s practice life, her fierceness and warmth, and the way her presence made Dharma feel ordinary and real. The talk ends by widening from personal grief to a meditation on how the living might honor the dead through memory, steadiness, and continued love.

Chapters

  1. 00:09 Morning, Dylan, mortality - Matthew begins with a morning stoplight, a Dylan lyric, and the mood of mortality that frames the rest of the talk.
  2. 01:14 Ann’s passing and example - He introduces Ann Buck’s death, her long practice, and the strange coexistence of tragedy, humor, and perspective.
  3. 03:10 Life in the ordinary - A sequence of small, vivid moments shows how practice meets daily life, from coffee to a bright pink bike.
  4. 05:01 Cling, then let go - Matthew recalls retreat memories with Ann and a shared recognition of attachment and release.
  5. 06:40 Her direct kindness - He describes Ann’s fierce clarity, including her strong encouragement to love oneself, and the trust that came with her words.
  6. 08:13 Dharma through conversation - Their phone calls and shared conversations are presented as naturally infused with Dharma and grounded support.
  7. 10:30 Mind, aging, and refuge - Matthew reflects on Ann’s practice lineage and the steadiness of a mind described as empty, radiant, and responsive.
  8. 12:50 What the dead might want - He closes with a James Fenton poem that turns grief toward memory, generosity, and continued life.
