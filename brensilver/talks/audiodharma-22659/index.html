Guided Meditation: Reverence & Renunciation

AudioDharma 30:29 2025-04-01

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation that frames practice as both reverence and renunciation. He invites a posture of sanctity without strain, then guides attention to the breath as a way of putting down multitasking, worry, story-making, and clinging. The talk widens into a quiet reflection on lineage, sincerity, and the way letting go can simplify experience and make breathing more intimate.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling In - A brief welcome and opening transition into shared practice.
  2. 00:55 Posture as Reverence - He frames posture as an open heart toward the world, with sanctity and relaxation held together.
  3. 03:24 Breath and Softening - The practice moves into the belly breath, encouraging a fuller and slower rhythm.
  4. 05:00 Sacred Lineage - He reminds listeners that even familiar meditation is part of a larger lineage of wisdom and compassion.
  5. 09:55 What to Put Down - Renunciation is defined as a simple, non-moralistic recognition of what can be set aside for now.
  6. 13:54 Story and Hope - He points to the tendency to proliferate around sense contact and to postpone life into the future.
  7. 18:20 Clinging Simplifies - The talk turns to the false promises of clinging and the relief that comes when grasping is released.
  8. 24:16 Resolution and Bells - He names samadhi as a momentary resolution of ambivalence, then the meditation closes in silence with bells.
