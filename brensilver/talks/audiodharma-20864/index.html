Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Sitting in the Bones

AudioDharma 27:35 2024-07-17

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation centered on settling attention into the body, especially the felt sense of the bones, heaviness, and gravity. The practice moves from fuller breathing and relaxation into a steadier, mountain-like groundedness, then broadens into the freedom to let experience arise and pass without strain. In the closing, he brings in loving-kindness as a quiet ethical and emotional dimension of the practice.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Settling the Breath - Fuller breathing is used to soften the belly and signal the body that it can relax.
  2. 02:30 Sitting in the Bones - The instruction turns to feeling the body as grounded and heavy, with attention dropping below the head.
  3. 04:11 Gravity and Earth - He emphasizes the downward pull of gravity and the sense of the earth offering support.
  4. 06:01 Attention Downward - When awareness drifts upward, the instruction is to pour it back into the body and let it seem to arise from the belly.
  5. 09:34 Mountain Stability - The practice is framed as growing rootedness, with a brief reference to Chögyam Trungpa's mountain image.
  6. 11:01 Stop Doing So Much - Relaxation is presented as a release from strain and self-effort, trusting awareness to remain awake without constant doing.
  7. 17:28 The Self Can Relax - The sense of a contracted 'me' is invited to loosen, making room for a wider, less possessive awareness.
  8. 23:11 From Rest to Metta - Steadiness is linked with love, and the talk ends by folding loving-kindness into the practice as something we may need even when we do not feel inclined toward it.
