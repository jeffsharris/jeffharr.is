Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

The Circuitous Journey to Presence through Past and Future

Dharma Seed 46:33 2025-09-19

Matthew Brensilver explores why “be present” is not a simple instruction but the fruit of a long process of seeing through memory, anticipation, and the habits that organize selfhood. He moves from humor and personal recollection into a careful argument about pain, grief, and the way mindfulness makes the past less personal and the future less compelling. The talk closes by returning to time, fear, and freedom, with a poetic coda from Bob Dylan.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the Question - Matthew begins by framing the talk as an inquiry into whether Dharma teachings actually hold up in lived experience.
  2. 03:43 Presence and the Past - He jokes with familiar retreat language about happiness and presence, then asks what the present really is and why it seems to exclude memory and anticipation.
  3. 09:59 Change and Training - Using Suzuki Roshi and Thich Nhat Hanh, he argues that presence is not a mere decision but the result of gradual training and a clearer relation to change.
  4. 13:38 Past as Refuge and Burden - Matthew describes how past and future can feel like wounds that make resting impossible, and how forcing attention can become a kind of self-harshness.
  5. 20:51 Listening to Sorrow - A Leonard Cohen poem and Andrea Gibson’s line about a hiding place lead him into the patient work of holding pain without prematurely explaining it.
  6. 26:30 Making Memory Legible - He turns to self-modeling and mindfulness, describing how painful memory becomes less opaque and less identified with ‘me’ when it is met with clarity and love.
  7. 32:19 The Urge to Fill Time - With Zadie Smith and bhava-tanha, he explores the craving to become, the pressure to stay busy, and the fear that lies beneath the need to fill time.
  8. 39:22 Futurelessness and Freedom - Matthew brings in Anil Seth and then returns to refuge, uncertainty, and death, ending with Dylan’s image of a futureless heart that is still light and free.
