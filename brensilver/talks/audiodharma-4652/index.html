In The Gap

AudioDharma 03:03 2014-03-12

Matthew Brensilver invites listeners to pay attention to the gaps between formal moments of practice, where habit and suffering often show themselves most clearly. He also reframes mindfulness as a matter of minimal effort rather than strain, suggesting that a little less forcing can turn spaciness into spaciousness. The talk is brief and steady, with a practical emphasis on continuity, ease, and the ordinary transitions of daily life.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Noticing the gaps - The talk begins by pointing to the gaps and transitions where attention often drops away and habits reveal themselves.
  2. 00:35 Ordinary transitions - Brensilver describes how the mind defaults in small movements like getting up and walking away from practice.
  3. 01:03 Habit hides there - He emphasizes that suffering and habitual patterns often appear most clearly in the in-between moments.
  4. 01:26 The cost of strain - The talk turns to the feeling that continuous mindfulness can be tiring and that the mind may want unconsciousness as relief.
  5. 02:05 Less effort needed - He reframes mindfulness as using only the minimum effort required, without adding unnecessary strain.
  6. 02:34 From spaciness to space - The talk closes by suggesting that a small shift in effort can transform spaciness into a sense of spaciousness.
