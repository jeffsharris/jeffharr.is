Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Arriving and Opening

AudioDharma 26:58 2025-03-26

Matthew Brensilver offers a gentle guided meditation centered on arriving, softening self-conscious effort, and letting the body be as it is. The practice moves from settling posture and attention, to checking for places that need patience or care, to resting with the breath and the felt sense of the body. He closes by framing the practice in terms of relinquishment, true nourishment, and a quiet metta-like response to agitation.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Arriving Gently - He begins by encouraging an unhurried arrival, without forcing posture or shaping attention.
  2. 01:52 Sensing What Needs Care - The practice widens to notice anything in the body or mind that may need awareness, patience, or softening.
  3. 05:02 Relaxing Self-Image - He points toward body sensations that hurt, and invites a release of self-conscious tension and fixed ideas about the body.
  4. 07:55 Breathing With Safety - The guidance settles on breathing with whatever alarm or resistance arises, staying close to the beginning and ending of each breath.
  5. 12:45 Relinquishment and Nourishment - He turns to the Buddha's teaching that peace comes through relinquishment and Ajahn Chah's image of 'food for the heart.'
  6. 13:53 Extended Silent Practice - A long stretch of quiet practice follows, with the meditation continuing in silence.
  7. 20:17 Meeting Agitation - He returns to naming restlessness, finding something stable, and responding to ill-will with a brief, direct metta.
