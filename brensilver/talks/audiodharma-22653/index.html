Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Humor in the Dharma

AudioDharma 15:43 2025-03-31

Matthew Brensilver offers a short Dharma talk on humor and playfulness as part of practice, arguing that seriousness is not the same as sincerity and that insight can make room for laughter. He moves through personal stories, references to Thai Forest teachers, and quotes from George Saunders, Freud, Nietzsche, and Winnicott to show how humor can reveal truth, loosen self-consciousness, and deepen friendship. The talk closes with an account of crying and laughing at once, suggesting that grief, joy, and peace can co-occur without canceling each other out.

Chapters

  1. 00:11 A playful fake list - He introduces the idea of a personal, unofficial list of Dharma factors, setting up humor and playfulness as the first theme.
  2. 01:28 Captain Buzzkill - A story about being called out for dampening excitement leads into his claim that Dharma can become overly serious when ego takes over.
  3. 03:54 Seriousness and lightness - He argues that practice can make experience more spacious and less self-bound, which opens the door to humor.
  4. 06:08 Truth told quickly - George Saunders and Freud are used to suggest that humor and shared laughter can reveal truth and deeper common ground.
  5. 07:37 Learning to play - Nietzsche’s idea of mature play frames Dharma as a way of recovering ease, non-vigilance, and less self-consciousness.
  6. 09:02 Therapy and play - Winnicott’s account of therapy as shared play is used to widen the point: healing often means moving from rigidity into playfulness.
  7. 10:38 Playing with self - He explores how practice can loosen the conventions of self, letting humor meet shame, pain, and defensiveness without denial.
  8. 12:43 Crying and laughing - A breathing-workshop story brings the talk to a close with crying, laughter, and peace coexisting in one porous experience.
