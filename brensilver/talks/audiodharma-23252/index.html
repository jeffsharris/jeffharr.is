Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Samadhi

AudioDharma 52:16 2025-06-20

Matthew Brensilver explores samadhi as something that cannot be forced, but arises from causes and conditions: steadiness, letting go, and a quieted relationship to experience. He moves from attention and classical Buddhist categories into a practical account of how concentration softens fragmentation, reduces strategizing, and makes the mind more receptive to learning. Along the way he contrasts samadhi with craving, self-management, and the urge to keep narrating and controlling life.

Chapters

  1. 00:30 Opening the Theme - He begins by distinguishing the part of us that wants instructions from the fact that samadhi cannot simply be commanded into existence.
  2. 05:56 Attention and Will - Using William James and the Mula Sutta, he frames attention as central to agency and experience, then introduces samadhi as deeper than mere focus.
  3. 10:56 How Attention Works - He describes alerting, orienting, and executive attention, and uses images like spotlighting and vitakka-vicara to show how practice steadies the mind.
  4. 15:08 Mindfulness and Samadhi - Drawing on Shinzen Young, he argues that mindfulness and concentration are not separate tracks but overlapping skills that support each other.
  5. 17:39 One Path, Not Two - He responds to the common split between mindfulness and concentration, then turns to the feeling of being startled, fragmented, or pulled into strategy.
  6. 24:58 Craving and Relief - With the Cartesian theater as a foil, he examines how craving for pleasure, relief, or identity keeps the mind from settling and why samadhi can feel healing.
  7. 34:15 Objects and Conditions - He broadens the discussion to different meditation objects and explains that supportive conditions, including tranquility and renunciation, matter more than finding the perfect target.
  8. 40:20 Letting Go of Meaning - He connects precepts, safety behaviors, and hypothesis testing to the mind's need to manage experience, then asks whether we can safely let go of meaning.
  9. 45:14 Silence, Faith, and Enough - He closes by describing the faith needed to rest in silence, the sense of being fully nourished by this moment, and the relief of a mind that is open and ready to learn.
