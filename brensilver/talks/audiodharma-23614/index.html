Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Lightly Guided Meditation

AudioDharma 25:53 2025-08-13

Matthew Brensilver offers a lightly guided meditation that begins with warmth and goodwill, then narrows attention to a simple resting place in the body, breath, and sound. He returns again and again to the ordinariness of goodness, trust, and ease, suggesting that practice can make us less intimidated by the future and less burdened by the past. Near the end, he turns toward the relation between care and rest, pointing to the need for cycles of devotion and recovery rather than constant strain.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening goodwill - He begins by sensing the field of shared goodwill and the wish for less suffering.
  2. 00:57 Settling posture - The guidance invites a simple posture and a felt sense of sweetness and sangha in the body.
  3. 02:33 Goodness as practice - He frames Dharma as a gradual sensitization to goodness that brightens over time.
  4. 04:35 Trust and security - The talk turns toward trusting goodness, feeling less intimidated by the future, and resting more naturally.
  5. 06:28 Clear attention - He describes setting a gentle boundary around the meditation object and not making distractions into an enemy.
  6. 07:22 Resting now - The instruction deepens into resting with breathing, body, and sound, allowing the present moment to be enough.
  7. 11:08 Releasing memory - He speaks about a present that is easier to inhabit when the past and future are no longer pressing for analysis.
  8. 17:24 Care and rest - The closing reflection asks for cycles of love, devotion, and rest rather than constant caregiving strain.
