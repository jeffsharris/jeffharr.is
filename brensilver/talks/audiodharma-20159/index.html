Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Aperture, Vividness, Stability, Effort

AudioDharma 29:56 2024-04-10

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation built around four practical dimensions of practice: intention, attention, vividness, stability, and effort. He repeatedly returns to the sense that meditation is not a workspace for solving problems, but a period of putting down grasping and letting the moment be enough. The sit then moves from a more concrete anchor to a broader, more open awareness, with guidance on how to adjust effort without strain.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening frame - Brensilver introduces the sit and frames the talk around a more concrete set of instructions than usual.
  2. 01:19 Intention to release - He emphasizes putting down greed and distress, and letting go of the urge to use meditation for problem-solving.
  3. 02:28 Attention and aperture - The guidance turns to where attention rests, including the metaphor of narrowing or widening the aperture of awareness.
  4. 04:34 Vividness and stability - He describes cultivating clear, vivid contact with experience while keeping attention steady.
  5. 06:01 Effort in balance - Brensilver explores the difference between sustainable effort and effort driven by clinging or control.
  6. 07:42 Settling into the sit - The guided portion begins by reassuring listeners that this period need not solve anything, and by softening the drive to optimize.
  7. 12:40 Anchor and soundscape - He invites attention to the breath, body, or soundscape, and stresses a gentle, sustainable way of staying with the object.
  8. 17:17 Returning from mind wandering - The meditation widens to include the mind’s activity, with instruction on noticing when agency collapses into thought and then reestablishing intimacy with the anchor.
  9. 26:46 Open awareness - The practice shifts from a concrete anchor to a more spacious noticing where the whole field of experience can be included.
