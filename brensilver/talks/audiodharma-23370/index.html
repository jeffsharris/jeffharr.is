Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Forgive all phenomena

AudioDharma 29:01 2025-07-09

Matthew Brensilver offers a gentle guided meditation that begins with settling the body and taking up a careful, relaxed posture. He then turns to the felt mechanics of craving and the habit of always reaching for the next thing, before shifting into the central instruction to “forgive all phenomena” — including pleasantness and pain — and trust the quieting that follows. The talk closes with a more spacious, effortless resting and a few bell tones.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Opening Into Practice - A brief welcome and invitation to begin the meditation together.
  2. 00:57 Settling the Body - He guides a gentle way of arriving, taking time with posture and easing into practice without force.
  3. 04:08 Bored With Samsara - The talk reflects on weariness with praise, gain, and the effort to keep everything together.
  4. 06:54 The Pull of Next - Craving is described as the repeated movement toward the next thing, even when dressed up as progress.
  5. 08:49 Just This Breath - Attention narrows to simple awareness of the breath and the immediacy of this moment.
  6. 12:49 A Few Conscious Breaths - He emphasizes how quickly the mind can shift, even after only a few mindful breaths.
  7. 19:38 Forgive All Phenomena - The core instruction is to forgive every experience, including both pleasure and pain, and then let go of extra effort.
  8. 21:24 Resting In Ease - The meditation closes in a quieter, more open resting, ending with bell sounds.
