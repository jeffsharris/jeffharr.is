Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Reflection on Mortality: The Poignancy of Being Alive

AudioDharma 38:24 2014-02-16

Matthew Brensilver reflects on mortality as a way of seeing more clearly what a human life is for. He moves from a cemetery image and the discomfort of death contemplation into Buddhist teachings, psychology, and personal stories that show both the burden of denial and the possibility of tenderness, freedom, and kindness. The talk ends with a quiet invitation to live so fully that dying would not feel like a shock.

Chapters

  1. 00:13 A Cemetery Reminder - Matthew begins with a small but vivid encounter near a veterans cemetery and uses it to open the theme of mortality.
  2. 01:49 Mindfulness of Death - He invites listeners to notice their reactions to the topic and introduces mortality as something to be included in practice, not avoided.
  3. 04:49 Practicing the Last Year - He describes Stephen Levine’s year-to-live practice and a conversation with a Holocaust survivor that brings humility to the exercise.
  4. 09:01 Denial and Humility - Matthew reflects on why mortality feels distant even when it is always near, weaving in Freud and his own lineage of sensitivity around death.
  5. 14:08 Walking With Death - He cites Larry Rosenberg and then turns to the idea that death is not at the end of life but present throughout it.
  6. 18:07 Mortality Salience - Drawing on Terror Management Theory, he looks at how fear of death can harden identity, prejudice, and defensive behavior.
  7. 21:39 Kindness Under Groundlessness - Using Pema Chödrön and Mary Oliver, he shows how shared fragility can soften the heart and clarify what matters.
  8. 25:22 Safety in Change - Matthew returns to the Buddha’s search for safety amid impermanence and connects it to meditation as a practice of letting go.
  9. 31:14 The Phantom Self - He closes by linking death fear to self-view, then softens the talk with a poem by Nyogen Senzaki and a final blessing for open-hearted living.
