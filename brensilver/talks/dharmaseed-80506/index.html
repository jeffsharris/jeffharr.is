Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Metta Practice

Dharma Seed 38:31 2023-10-08

Matthew Brensilver presents metta as something more than a pleasant mood: a capacity that makes insight practice bearable, especially when the heart meets the full intensity of suffering. He moves through the four brahmaviharas as four distinct kinds of love, then explains how metta can cultivate warmth, steady the mind, or bring buried grief and fear to the surface for healing. The talk closes with a simple practice of repeating phrases, listening for their effect, and widening the field of care to others without denying war, oppression, or loss.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Love and Insight - Metta is introduced as essential to opening to the human condition without overwhelm, not as an optional extra to insight practice.
  2. 02:28 Four Species of Love - The talk reframes the brahmaviharas as metta, compassion, sympathetic joy, and equanimity, each meeting a different condition of life.
  3. 06:33 Why Love Matters - Matthew explains why wisdom is incomplete without love and how metta can function as cultivation, concentration, or purification.
  4. 10:56 Meeting What Hurts - Metta is described as a practice that can draw grief, rage, and confusion into view and hold them with kindness rather than avoidance.
  5. 12:45 Settling the Body - The instruction turns practical, emphasizing a sustainable posture, relaxation, and a quiet refusal to perform practice for anyone.
  6. 16:55 Remembering Goodness - Listeners are guided to bring to mind an image of goodness, sense the heart center, and let warmth soften clinging and self-harshness.
  7. 25:32 Abiding in Kindness - The teaching deepens into a felt sense that kindness makes it safe to let go and reminds the mind that aversion and fear cannot truly protect it.
  8. 31:33 Extending the Wish - The phrases widen from oneself to others, turning personal longing into a more universal wish for happiness, safety, and peace.
  9. 37:04 A Sublime Abiding - The close acknowledges suffering in the world while affirming that this species of love is not diminished by it and can be a noble dwelling of mind.
