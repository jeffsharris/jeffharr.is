Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Reflection & Meditation: Grief & Compassion

Dharma Seed 30:54 2020-10-08

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet reflection on grief as part of loving well, moving from metta into compassion, impermanence, and the moral work of not hardening around pain. He speaks personally and also broadly about social suffering, then grounds the talk in a guided phrase practice and a reading of Hala Alyan’s poem "Spoiler." The ending returns to letting go as a form of freedom, not denial.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening Into Practice - After a brief preface about settling in, Matthew invites listeners to stay with stillness or adjust as needed.
  2. 01:36 Metta Meets Suffering - He frames metta as inseparable from compassion, saying that love becomes compassion when it meets suffering.
  3. 03:02 Groundlessness and Grief - The talk widens to the instability of life in 2020 and the need to let grief be part of maturity rather than something avoided.
  4. 05:15 Why We Grieve - Matthew describes grief as part of opening to dukkha, with personal and societal consequences when grief is refused.
  5. 08:44 Softening the Heart - He emphasizes patience, generosity, and the willingness to be softened by pain rather than hardened by it.
  6. 13:42 Generative Grief Practice - The teaching turns practical as he names grief as catalytic and leads a silent lovingkindness-style contemplation for all beings.
  7. 22:38 Reading Hala Alyan - Matthew introduces and reads Hala Alyan’s poem "Spoiler," using its images of illness, loss, and the tide as a mirror for impermanence.
  8. 27:46 Letting Go Freely - He returns to the poem’s insight that holding on clings to illusion and that love after letting go can be radical, beautiful, and free.
