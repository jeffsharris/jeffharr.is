Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Rivers are just rivers

AudioDharma 16:07 2025-11-19

Matthew Brensilver uses Dogen’s image of mountains and rivers to trace a practice arc from ordinary certainty, through destabilizing insight, to a more mature ordinariness. He moves from the mind’s assumptions and a brief neuroscience aside into meditation as a kind of reversal of our usual attentional habits, where the self feels less fixed and experience more fluid. The talk ends by softening the apparent paradox: awakening does not replace ordinary life so much as make love and intimacy more visible within it.

Chapters

  1. 00:52 Opening the Theme - He introduces the famous mountains-and-rivers passage and frames the talk around a developmental arc in practice.
  2. 02:05 Ordinary Certainty - The talk begins with the assumed solidity of life, where our conditioning and identity feel unquestioned and fixed.
  3. 03:56 Looking Inward - He turns from the external metaphor to mind itself, using the Dhammapada and a brief neuroscience reference to explore self-world boundaries.
  4. 05:32 Meditation Reverses Habit - Matthew describes meditation as a reversal of ordinary sensory suppression, inviting the practitioner to enter experience with beginner’s mind and high sensitivity.
  5. 07:36 Fluid Perception - He develops the language of emptiness as non-solidification, where perception becomes less rigid and the world feels more spacious and alive.
  6. 09:28 The Weird Phase - He names the disorienting middle territory of insight practice, when the self feels loosened and reality can seem unfamiliar or even alien.
  7. 12:07 New Baselines - He reflects on how practice stabilizes over time, so fluidity becomes less jarring and the path is understood with more sobriety and less fixation.
  8. 14:35 Love Remains - The talk closes by saying that when mountains are just mountains again, love becomes more prominent and ordinary life is seen as slightly changed, not replaced.
