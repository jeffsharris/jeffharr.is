Guided Meditation: Settling into the Pleasure of the Breath

AudioDharma 49:46 2019-05-13

Matthew Brensilver leads a guided meditation that begins with settling the body and sensing the breath as a source of quiet pleasure. The practice then widens into a way of using that pleasure to signal safety, soften the heart, and stay with experience without sorting it into good and bad. In the latter part of the talk he turns to walking meditation, describing it as a bridge into daily life and a place to work gently with boredom, continuity, and attention.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling the body - The meditation opens by gathering awareness into the whole body and finding a posture that balances ease with alertness.
  2. 02:36 Pleasure in the breath - He invites attention to the subtle satisfactions in inhaling and exhaling, letting the whole body receive and be soothed by them.
  3. 07:10 Letting awareness flood - The practice emphasizes arriving, resting in the breath cycle, and allowing the body to feel held and supported.
  4. 11:32 Safety and softening - He speaks of signaling to the deep mind that it is safe to relax and letting experience soften the heart rather than being managed.
  5. 19:27 Whole-body presence - The instructions return to sensing the whole body breathing and opening wholeheartedly to what is here.
  6. 29:35 Knowing the breath - A brief instruction anchors attention in direct, simple awareness of breathing in and breathing out.
  7. 33:18 Walking as continuity - The talk shifts to how sitting and walking support each other and how practice can be carried through the day.
  8. 40:29 Working with boredom - He explores boredom, narrowed attention, and small mental notes as ways to stay intimate with sensation while walking.
  9. 45:00 Practical retreat notes - The closing minutes cover alternatives for injury, retreat logistics, and a few gentle reminders about not drawing early conclusions.
