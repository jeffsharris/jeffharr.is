Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Thought as the exhaust from engine of affect

AudioDharma 29:12 2023-02-15

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation built around attunement to present-moment experience, especially the way feeling gives rise to interpretation and mental proliferation. He invites listeners to notice the body’s surges without over-reading them, then repeatedly returns to a single instruction: soften. The practice gradually widens into resting with unpleasantness, letting the Dharma saturate experience, and allowing felt urgency to be understood more gently.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Arriving Together - A brief welcome settles into shared sitting and the basic invitation to attune to the present.
  2. 01:41 Attunement and Empathy - The meditation frames attention as a kind of self-directed empathy, like clarifying the inner landscape of another person.
  3. 05:21 Feeling Becomes Meaning - Brensilver describes affect as a moving pool of energy that quickly invites interpretation, desire, and aversion.
  4. 08:55 Reading the Body - He names the habit of reading bodily signals and turning them into conclusions, while noting how tiring that can be.
  5. 11:26 Not Over-Interpreting - The practice shifts toward meeting bodily energies as they surge and collapse without making them mean too much.
  6. 14:04 Soften - A long repetition of the instruction to soften establishes the central response to feeling and urgency.
  7. 20:29 Resting in Unpleasantness - He turns to abiding with discomfort, letting the Dharma saturate experience and loosen the grip of ego.
  8. 23:01 Keeping Connection - The close emphasizes staying empathically connected to experience and the richness of feeling as the meditation winds down.
