Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Everything is Passing

AudioDharma 29:38 2025-08-20

Matthew Brensilver leads a guided meditation centered on impermanence, beginning with settling, breath, and a gentle invocation of the whole history of practice. He gradually widens the frame to include reverence, loving-kindness, and forgiveness of conditioning, then turns to a clear reflection on anicca: not just that things pass, but that everything is always passing, including the sense of the observer. The practice closes by softening into the felt experience of breathing and the changing nature of awareness itself.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Arriving and settling - The meditation opens with welcome, a call toward stillness, and the first instructions for arriving in the body and breath.
  2. 02:00 Calling in practice - He invites a deep breath as a way of summoning all the moments of Dharma practice and the qualities they have cultivated.
  3. 04:30 Reverence and devotion - The practice shifts into reverence for the path, goodness, and love, with a sense of bowing toward what matters most.
  4. 07:25 Loving-kindness and care - He broadens into metta and a shared field of care, grounding the practice in kindness for all beings.
  5. 10:23 Understanding wandering - Attention’s movement away from the present is reframed as a kind of innocent, naive search for happiness rather than something to condemn.
  6. 14:28 Forgiving conditioning - The meditation turns toward forgiveness, softening judgment around conditioning and returning to breath.
  7. 18:17 The flow of anicca - Matthew develops the central teaching that everything is always passing, including the apparent stability of experience and the meditator’s sense of self.
  8. 25:06 Melting into breathing - The talk closes by letting the idea of a solid observer dissolve into the changing flow of breathing and direct experience.
