Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Effort as Not-Self

AudioDharma 28:15 2024-09-12

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet guided meditation on effort as a kind of not-self practice: instead of pushing harder, the instruction is to stop what can be stopped and receive the breath more openly. The talk explores how meditation can crystallize a sense of a separate doer, and then gently redirects attention toward a deeper, less anxious source of practice. After the instruction, the guidance settles into extended periods of silence punctuated by reminders to be seated and to avoid harsh, moralistic self-talk.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling and Stopping - The opening invites a first minute of doing nothing and points toward noticing how much can simply be stopped.
  2. 00:43 Freedom Already Here - The guidance frames meditation as trusting that goodness and freedom are already present, rather than being forced into being by self-effort.
  3. 02:36 How Effort Becomes Self - Brensilver explains how intention and attention can harden into a sense of an inner meditator pulling the strings.
  4. 05:06 Relaxing the Impulse - The practice turns toward relaxing the urge to do and learning to trust what is less forced and more receptive.
  5. 09:36 Receiving the Breath - A longer stretch of silent practice emphasizes a different mode of attention: openly receiving the breath without congealing a separate self around it.
  6. 14:15 Choosing the Right Counsel - The closing instruction cautions against impatient or harsh inner voices and encourages drawing practice direction from something deeper, more embodied, and less compulsive.
