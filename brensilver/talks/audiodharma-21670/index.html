Guided Meditation: Soothing, Space, Love

AudioDharma 28:12 2024-11-06

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation that begins with gentle self-care and bodily soothing, then gradually opens into spacious awareness. From there, he turns toward the sense of love as something subtle and already present, not something to manufacture. The practice closes by returning whatever arises — even fear — back into that larger field of love.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Arrival and Intention - The meditation opens with a brief welcome and an invitation to remember what matters in the heart.
  2. 01:09 Soothing the Body - Attention settles into simple, pleasant physical contact and a felt sense of care for the body.
  3. 04:24 Breath and Unfreezing - The breath is used to enliven the body and soften what feels braced or frozen.
  4. 08:47 Recognizing Vastness - The mind is invited to rest in openness and recognize spaciousness already present.
  5. 11:52 Space Without Struggle - He emphasizes that vastness does not need to be created and that solidity or density can be allowed without fighting it.
  6. 15:37 Love and Poignancy - The teaching turns toward a subtle sense of love and the tenderness of meeting life closely.
  7. 17:28 Returning to Love - Whatever arises, including fear, is received and offered back into love.
