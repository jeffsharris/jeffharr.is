Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Difficult for the Self to Understand what it can't do

AudioDharma 27:00 2026-04-29

This guided meditation begins with simple orientation to body, breath, and a warm attitude of love, then settles into a steady practice of resting attention without overworking it. Matthew Brensilver emphasizes the difference between what we can intentionally do and what belongs to awareness itself, repeatedly returning to the question of “what is your job” and what is not. The talk moves toward a quiet humility: attention can be directed, but awareness and the unfolding of Dharma cannot be forced or owned by the self.

Chapters

  1. 00:11 Arrive in the body - The meditation opens by inviting simple contact with body, breath, and love.
  2. 00:50 A relaxed attention - The instruction shifts toward a gentle, workable way of pointing attention without strain.
  3. 02:04 Hope, fear, and re-dedication - Brensilver names the pull of hope and fear and returns again and again to what's actually here.
  4. 03:24 What is your job - He asks listeners to sort out what they can do from what was never theirs to control.
  5. 07:54 Staying with the step - The guided practice continues with repeated cues to stay present while not letting attention's doingness obscure awareness.
  6. 15:23 The self and the path - He reflects on how intentional practice can create the illusion that the self is orchestrating the Dharma path.
  7. 16:43 Awareness larger than story - The talk opens into the sense that the self's plot is too small to describe what is actually happening.
  8. 23:13 Instruction and limitation - He closes by acknowledging that instruction implies doing, while reminding listeners not to let action eclipse what cannot be done.
