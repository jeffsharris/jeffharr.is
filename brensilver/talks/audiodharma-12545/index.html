Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Wednesday Morning Instructions: Clarifying Body

AudioDharma 51:13 2020-12-09

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet guided body meditation that begins with settling into surrounding silence and then turns toward gravity, exhalation, and the changing texture of sensation. The talk gradually reframes the body as a shifting field rather than a solid object, encouraging a truce with imperfection, a softer relationship to wanting to fix experience, and a steadier equanimity. In the closing minutes, he narrows the instruction to “protecting nothing” and carrying a thread of Dharma into the day through non-distractedness, tranquility, or mindfulness.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling Into Silence - The practice begins by noticing inner and outer noise and resting in the silence that surrounds it.
  2. 03:59 Gravity and Exhalation - Attention turns to bodily soothing through the downward pull of gravity and the ripple of sensation with the breath.
  3. 09:07 Whole-Body Awareness - The body is held as a whole constellation of pleasant, unpleasant, dense, and airy sensations without quarrel.
  4. 11:44 Unifying Attention - Matthew describes how whole-body awareness steadies attention, reduces fragmentation, and deepens the sense of soothing.
  5. 15:14 Clarifying Body Experience - The talk develops the idea of higher-resolution perception, where the body is seen as change rather than a solid thing.
  6. 26:05 Truce With Imperfection - He links body awareness with equanimity, a truce with samsara, and letting sensations unfold in their own course.
  7. 36:14 Dharma Mind - The teaching shifts to the sense that background assumptions meditate us, and that we nurture wholesomeness with a Dharma mind.
  8. 40:25 Protecting Nothing - In the closing guidance, listeners are asked to brace against nothing, stay still, and let the moment move through awareness.
  9. 48:14 Carrying Practice Forward - The talk ends by encouraging a simple thread to the Dharma through the day, whether as mindfulness, non-distractedness, tranquility, or equanimity.
