Dharmette: Very brief overview of Shinzen Young’s approach

AudioDharma 15:37 2026-04-15

Matthew Brensilver gives a concise overview of Shinzen Young’s approach to mindfulness, treating it less as a vague quality than as a workable training map. He breaks the method into three interlocking skills—concentration, sensory clarity, and equanimity—then shows how that framework leads to three aims for practice, three sensory domains, and three basic modes of meditation. The talk is brief, practical, and reflective, with a few comments on why this system has stayed influential in his own teaching.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the sketch - Matthew names Shinzen Young as an important long-time teacher and frames the talk as a brief overview rather than a full treatment.
  2. 01:24 What mindfulness means - He notes that mindfulness is contested as a term and situates Shinzen’s approach as one useful definition among others.
  3. 02:13 Three skills together - Matthew lays out Shinzen’s definition of mindfulness as concentration, sensory clarity, and equanimity working together, then explains why concentration is not separate from mindfulness.
  4. 03:50 The three skills unpacked - He gives a fuller explanation of each skill: focused attention, clearer perception of subtle experience, and allowing experience without push or pull.
  5. 07:36 Why practice - Matthew presents Shinzen’s three reasons for meditation—appreciate, transcend, and improve self and world—and expands them into fuller experience, self-transcendence, and deeper service.
  6. 10:22 Where to practice - He describes Shinzen’s map of visual, auditory, and somatic experience, including their restful or internal dimensions.
  7. 12:23 Three practice modes - Matthew closes with Shinzen’s three ways of cultivating mindfulness: noting sensory events, doing nothing by releasing control, and nurturing positive states.
  8. 15:20 Closing invitation - He ends by inviting listeners to take what is useful and leave the rest, keeping the presentation modest and open-ended.
