Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Relaxing into the Moment

AudioDharma 29:21 2024-09-09

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation centered on relaxing into the moment with honesty rather than trying to fix experience. The practice moves from settling the breath and body to meeting thoughts and worries with less struggle, then widens into a steadier sense of refuge and trust. By the end, the emphasis is on honoring the moment as a form of healing in itself.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Honesty With the Moment - The meditation opens by framing practice as a truthful meeting with experience, rather than pretending or controlling it.
  2. 01:04 Settling With Breath - Instruction turns to fuller breathing and repeated reassurance that it is safe to be here, just as things are.
  3. 03:31 Relaxing the Problem Habit - The talk loosens the urge to treat the present as something to solve and encourages a softer trust in whatever is arising.
  4. 06:24 Body as Refuge - Awareness expands through the front, back, and lower body, using the breath to animate and unify the whole body.
  5. 08:56 Soothing and Energizing - Breath is presented as a way to enliven what is numb and calm what is overactivated, bringing the body into greater balance.
  6. 12:00 Dissolving Worry - The guidance contrasts thinking with breathing awareness, suggesting that worries can loosen when met from tranquility and surrender.
  7. 17:07 Finding Refuge - Attention returns to the most vivid place of breathing and to a sense that thought is not the only important thing in experience.
  8. 25:35 What Truly Matters - The meditation closes by reflecting that honoring the moment leaves a durable imprint, refining attention and resting the heart from struggle.
