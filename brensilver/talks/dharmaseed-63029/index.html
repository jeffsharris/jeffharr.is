Guided Meditation: Settling into the Body

Dharma Seed 40:14 2020-10-03

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation that moves gently from settling the body to trusting the breath as a stable cue. He emphasizes that resting is not negligence, but a way of loosening compulsive worry without losing care or ethics. The practice widens into a shared retreat intention, a softening into gravity and nature, and a patient trust in the Dharma’s logic.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening Instructions - He introduces the guided practice and offers a first-day retreat reminder not to rush into conclusions.
  2. 01:37 Taking Cues From Body - The attention is redirected to posture, breathing, and the body’s simple rhythms as the basis for practice.
  3. 04:01 It Is Okay To Rest - He directly addresses the fear that resting means neglect, and reassures that letting go of compulsive worry can deepen care.
  4. 05:10 Shared Intention - The practice turns toward the group’s collective intention for wisdom, harmony, courage, and non-harming.
  5. 08:03 Gravity And Release - Attention settles into the downward pull of gravity, allowing strain and control to drain into the earth.
  6. 11:20 Natural Breathing - He encourages a non-performative way of meditating, simply following the natural sensations and rhythm of breathing.
  7. 17:30 Porous To Breath - The meditation deepens into resting with the breath as it balances craving, aversion, dullness, and restlessness.
  8. 27:58 Innocent Mind Movements - He broadens the view to include hope, fear, pleasure, pain, past, and future as understandable movements of mind, to be met with non-violence.
  9. 29:58 Trusting The Dharma - The closing movement offers attention to the grounded body and breath, ending with a sense that practice is less a task than a trust in Dharma’s logic.
