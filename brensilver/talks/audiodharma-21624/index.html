Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Giving Love the Last Word

AudioDharma 13:11 2024-10-30

Matthew Brensilver opens with a philosophical analogy about fever and uses it to frame anger as a protective response rather than something to suppress or glorify. From there he returns to a refrain: anger may be the first word, but love should be the last, even in fear, conflict, and uncertainty. The talk moves toward nonself, the limits of control and domination, and a closing ethic of blessing others through everyday contact.

Chapters

  1. 00:19 Fever and Anger - He begins with an analogy about fever to show how anger can be a sign that something valuable is under threat.
  2. 02:25 Love the Last Word - Matthew develops the central refrain that anger may arise first, but love must be the final orientation toward suffering.
  3. 05:23 The Koan of Love - He reflects on love as an open question, then turns to Ajahn Sucitto’s teaching on domination and the heart’s response to uncertainty.
  4. 08:12 Not Knowing - The talk pauses on the usefulness of uncertainty, suggesting that wisdom may depend on comfort with not knowing.
  5. 09:16 Releasing Ownership - He broadens the practice to letting go of 'I, me, mine' and noticing the instability of possession, identity, and control.
  6. 10:24 Relief and Openness - Matthew describes the relief that can come with nonself, while preserving the reality of suffering in a new way.
  7. 11:43 Every Contact Matters - He closes with Shantideva’s aspiration that no encounter be insignificant, ending on the wish to bless the world.
  8. 12:39 Closing Blessing - He offers the talk for consideration and leaves with a simple sign-off.
