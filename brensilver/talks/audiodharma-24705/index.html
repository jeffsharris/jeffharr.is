Guided Meditation: Sitting as if already dead

AudioDharma 28:22 2026-01-28

This guided meditation uses the image of sitting "as if already dead" to loosen concern, self-protection, and future bargaining, while keeping the mind very awake. Matthew Brensilver repeatedly returns to softening the body, relaxing the heart, and resting in a way that lets love remain when fear and hatred lose their footing. The practice gradually widens into loving-kindness and a simple instruction to slow down, be still, and let the moment do some of the work.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Settling and relaxing - The meditation begins by relaxing whatever can be relaxed and finding a breath that soothes the skin.
  2. 01:00 Softening the heart - The guidance turns toward subtly relaxing the heart and attuning to the body's quieter soothing effects.
  3. 02:40 Space from samsara - Brensilver invites a little distance from old habits and the ways we come into practice, emphasizing being softened by the moment.
  4. 05:32 Not bartering with future - He encourages not negotiating with the future for now, making room for present-moment attention instead of worry.
  5. 08:20 Already dead, awake - The core framing arrives: sit as if already dead, not extrapolating the instruction into life planning, but staying vividly awake.
  6. 12:57 Love without fear - In the imagined afterlife of no fear, self-identity and hatred loosen, leaving rest and love as the available conditions.
  7. 19:34 Mettā made vast - Loving-kindness is described as making the mind vast and measureless, with no other side to this love.
  8. 25:25 Hatred is not final - The closing reflection says hatred is never the last word, and ends with a quiet instruction to slow down and be still.
