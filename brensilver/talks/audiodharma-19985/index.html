Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Faith in Love (3 of 5)

AudioDharma 14:33 2024-03-13

Matthew Brensilver reflects on a kind of faith that is not certainty, but trust in love as the deeper response to suffering, aversion, and fear. He opens with a tender poem by Louise Cook, then turns to the psychology of hatred, the necessity of open-heartedness, and the way practice can reveal that deep happiness depends on love. In the latter half, he uses dependent arising and a reference to Martha Nussbaum to show how following emotion all the way through can lead not to hate, but to kindness, compassion, joy, and equanimity.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Opening Poem - A poem attributed to Louise Cook sets a tender, bittersweet tone for the talk and frames love as vulnerable and enduring.
  2. 01:07 Faith and Doubt - Matthew returns to the series theme of faith and doubt, then introduces faith in love as the new focus.
  3. 02:02 Love Against Aversion - He contrasts love with the deep evolutionary and psychological force of aggression and hate, arguing that a life with purpose must involve love.
  4. 04:17 Open-Hearted Happiness - The talk develops the idea that deep happiness depends on open-heartedness, not closed-off pleasure or excitement.
  5. 06:20 Anger’s Dead End - Matthew acknowledges the useful signals anger can carry, while emphasizing the repeated experience of its dead end and the regret it leaves behind.
  6. 07:59 Dependent Arising - Using paticca-samuppada, he argues that seeing conditionality clearly is incompatible with hatred and asks listeners to keep following the chain of causation.
  7. 10:27 Emotion and Need - A reference to Martha Nussbaum helps him describe emotion as a sign of incompleteness, need, and innocence rather than failure.
  8. 11:46 Following to Love - He closes by tracing emotional intensity to its dissolution, suggesting that careful investigation ends in the Brahma-viharas and leaves a remembered shadow of love.
