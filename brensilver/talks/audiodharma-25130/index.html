Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Dedicating our practice

AudioDharma 27:56 2026-03-25

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet guided meditation on arriving, softening, and dedicating practice to the welfare of self and others. The instruction moves from settling body and mind, to letting go of bracing and defensiveness, to reflecting on love, finitude, and the seriousness of practice without strain. The tone stays steady and unforced, with long stretches of repeated phrases that support breathing and attention.

Chapters

  1. 00:30 Arriving Here - The meditation opens by asking what it means for body, mind, and heart to arrive in this moment.
  2. 02:01 Putting It Down - The instruction turns toward releasing bracing and defensiveness so experience can be met more directly.
  3. 03:20 Dedicating Practice - Brensilver invites reflection on whom we practice for and on the seriousness of practice in light of birth and death.
  4. 10:29 Love and Finitude - The talk explores how love may be larger than our power while still remaining steady and strong.
  5. 12:16 Quiet Repetition - A long guided stretch uses repeated phrases to let love gather in the background while breathing and honoring the path.
  6. 19:44 Seeing Differently - The meditation closes by linking love, finitude, and the way the world looks when they come into clearer view.
