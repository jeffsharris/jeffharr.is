Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

JoAnna Hardy, Matthew Brensilver, Vinny Ferraro: Where You At, Where You Going

Dharma Seed 55:30 2016-10-01

This talk uses the Eightfold Path as a quiet map for noticing how people come to practice through different doorways: ethics, meditation, or wisdom. Matthew Brensilver and the other speakers move from personal stories of harm, shame, and service into a more balanced view of practice, emphasizing that the path is meant to stretch our strongest habits and invite the less-developed parts of ourselves. The conversation ends by turning that reflection into a practical exercise: name your strengths, notice your edge, and choose what to work with over time.

Chapters

  1. 00:51 Eightfold Path Frame - The talk opens by naming the Eightfold Path as a classic, practical teaching with multiple entry points.
  2. 02:40 Three Doorways - The speakers describe the three trainings—ethics, concentration, and wisdom—and how different people may enter through each one.
  3. 05:36 Ethics and Turning Around - A personal story of homelessness, theft, and confusion becomes a story of finding precepts and using sila to begin changing life.
  4. 11:43 Precepts As Mirror - The talk deepens into how ethical conduct is not about outside judgment but about seeing the effects of action, reducing shame, and supporting meditation.
  5. 18:33 Friendship And Directness - JoAnna Hardy is named as a spiritual friend whose direct kindness models how truth and care can live together.
  6. 22:28 Taking Refuge In Samadhi - Matthew Brensilver joins in and speaks about samadhi as a real refuge that steadies the nervous system and helps us digest experience.
  7. 26:46 Dropping Into Experience - The discussion turns toward settling beneath the story of our life and seeing neurotic patterns more clearly through quiet and stability.
  8. 33:28 Find Your Edge - The speakers invite listeners to notice which strand of practice comes naturally and which one needs deliberate cultivation over the next year.
  9. 37:58 Dyads, Silence, And Closing - The room shifts into paired sharing, brief reflection, and a closing reminder to hold the container, stay quiet, and finish the retreat strongly.
