Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Beginning with Goodness

AudioDharma 29:57 2023-01-24

This guided meditation begins with the suggestion that practice can start from a felt sense of goodness already present in experience. Matthew Brensilver then repeatedly returns to the breath as a place of receiving, releasing, and settling into care without strain. The closing section brings the meditation into a larger view, suggesting that the sacred does not need to be manufactured and can be recognized in ordinary life.

Chapters

  1. 01:24 Beginning with goodness - The meditation opens by framing Dharma as growing more sensitive to goodness in oneself, others, and the path.
  2. 04:38 Settling in the body - Listeners are invited to sense goodness running through the body and to use that as a support when the mind tightens.
  3. 10:54 Breath as nourishment - The practice turns toward the felt relief of breathing in and out, with attention to the body being nourished and released.
  4. 18:09 Relaxing vigilance - Matthew names the habit of hedging against the present and encourages a gentler trust in what is happening now.
  5. 19:55 Devoting ourselves here - The focus narrows to the breathing body, with an emphasis on clarity, precision, and presence without tension.
  6. 27:33 The sacred already present - The closing quotation reinforces the teaching that the sacred need not be manufactured, but can be recognized in ordinary experience.
