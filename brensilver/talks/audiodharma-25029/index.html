Dharmette: Meditation is so many things

AudioDharma 15:06 2026-03-11

Matthew Brensilver reflects on meditation as something much larger than attention training: a way of resting, digesting pain, cultivating gratitude, and learning to trust experience as it changes. He moves through peace, suffering, goodness, samadhi, letting go, and the question of what it means to arrive in one’s life. The talk stays practical and restrained, while widening the frame from daily practice to mortality and completion.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Meditation has many facets - He opens by resisting any reduction of meditation to a single function and argues that there are many ways the practice can help.
  2. 01:57 Deep rest and wakefulness - Meditation is described as a true rest that is still awake, unlike the numbing rest of distraction.
  3. 03:18 Peace, pain, gratitude - He connects tranquility with digesting greed, hate, and delusion, then distinguishes genuine gratitude from a version that avoids suffering and death.
  4. 05:31 Earth from space - A passage from Samantha Harvey's Orbital becomes a way to think about finitude, perspective, and the world as both fragile and enough.
  5. 06:50 Knowing ourselves clearly - Meditation is presented as familiarization with our actual experience, cutting through self-curation and the question of whether we truly want to understand ourselves.
  6. 08:21 Suffering and goodness - Using Yuval Harari's remarks on suffering, he contrasts agitation with peace and then describes meditation as cultivating many forms of goodness.
  7. 10:26 Samadhi and letting go - He explains samadhi as a unification of mind that makes subtle pleasure more satisfying and also reveals burdens we had not recognized.
  8. 12:23 Past, mortality, completion - The final movement turns to how meditation reworks the past, loosens identification, and raises the question of what it would mean for a life to feel complete.
