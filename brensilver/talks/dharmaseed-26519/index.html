Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Self-Love and Selflessness - Part 1

Dharma Seed 58:39 2015-02-23

Matthew Brensilver explores the relationship between self-love and selflessness, arguing that the path is not a battle between them but a gradual deepening in which kindness, acceptance, and insight support one another. He begins with a quote from Shinzen Young, then moves through sutta-based framing, the Buddha’s silence with Vacchagotta, and practical reflections on how self-clinging, shame, and egoic effort create strain. The talk later turns toward embodied practice, the softening of self-story, and the possibility that awareness can be known without being turned into another identity.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Insight and service - Matthew opens with Shinzen Young to frame practice as both liberating insight and a life of goodness and service.
  2. 02:52 No conflict - He argues that self-development and selflessness are not enemies, but part of a balanced path.
  3. 05:29 From harshness to kindness - The talk reframes the problem as self-clinging rather than self-love, and places self-kindness on a continuum toward selflessness.
  4. 09:01 Experience, not theory - Matthew resists metaphysical debate by using the Buddha’s silence to show that self and not-self are experiences, not fixed things.
  5. 14:48 Humbling the ego - He draws on Freud and Jack Engler to describe the de-centering of self and the limits of spiritual language that turns ego into an enemy.
  6. 19:29 Healing the fragments - Practice is presented as a way of making everything relevant, digesting pain, and gathering scattered parts of experience into more wholeness.
  7. 27:55 Love, security, and illusion - With Bhikkhu Bodhi, Ajahn Sumedho, and Rumi, he shows how self-love can loosen self-clinging while egoic security remains burdensome.
  8. 36:16 Awareness and not-self - The teaching turns toward how awareness can feel like 'me' until mindfulness reveals experience as arising and passing, without needing to become an ultimate self.
  9. 47:57 Questions and closing - In discussion, he clarifies that what is known is not self, then closes with a softer sense of story, energy freed from self-hatred, and more capacity to serve.
