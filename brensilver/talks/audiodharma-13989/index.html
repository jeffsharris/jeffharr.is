Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Between Suppression and Entanglement

AudioDharma 30:49 2021-08-10

Matthew Brensilver leads a quiet guided meditation that moves from settling the body into sensing companionship and refuge. He emphasizes softening self-conscious tension, resting in the goodwill of Sangha, Buddha, and Dharma, and letting awareness bless experience without forcing it. The practice then opens into a spacious release of struggle, ending with a middle way he names as neither suppression nor entanglement.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening Welcome - The meditation begins with a brief welcome and an invitation to settle in together.
  2. 00:39 Sustainable Posture - Attention turns to finding a posture that can be maintained without strain and easing into stillness.
  3. 02:02 Presence and Softening - He invites sensing the presence of others, softening the face, and relaxing the felt grip of self-consciousness.
  4. 04:26 Relaxing the Body - The practice widens to the shoulders, belly, and hands, with repeated cues to release tension and defensiveness.
  5. 08:08 Undefended Refuge - He frames sitting undefended as dignified, then points to the goodwill of Sangha, Buddha, and Dharma as support for making peace with this moment.
  6. 12:09 Blessing Experience - The instruction shifts toward gently blessing experience with awareness instead of trying to optimize or fix it.
  7. 19:35 Releasing Strife - A long spacious section invites giving up struggle and letting solidity become more like space.
  8. 25:41 Middle Way Closing - The meditation closes by breathing awareness into the stories that arise and resting somewhere between suppression and entanglement.
