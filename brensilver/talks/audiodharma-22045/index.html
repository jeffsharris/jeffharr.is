Guided Meditation: Putting Down the World

AudioDharma 28:22 2025-01-08

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation on what it means to “put down the world” without turning away from it. The practice moves from settling the body and mind, to resting without bartering for outcomes, to meeting experience directly through breath, sound, and sensation. In the final movement, he returns to the paradox that letting go can deepen love, care, and a more workable response to life.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening reflection - Brensilver opens with a brief reflection on practice as a radical surrender, putting the world down without abandoning care.
  2. 02:50 Settling the posture - The instruction turns to posture, alertness, brightness, and a non-tensed way of arriving.
  3. 04:03 Putting the world down - He names greed and distress with respect to the world, and explores the fear that letting go means neglecting what matters.
  4. 06:40 Autobiography and care - The talk deepens into the density of self-story, time, and compulsive care, pointing toward a deeper love beneath them.
  5. 07:59 Simple immediacy - Attention is guided toward the present-moment texture of sound, sensation, and breath, with less effort and less making.
  6. 11:58 No bartering - He cautions against using practice as a means to get somewhere, or as a buffer from intimacy with life.
  7. 15:08 Falling into the present - The meditation widens into resting without trading the moment away, and repeatedly returns to presence as something that can be entered rather than achieved.
  8. 22:11 Love after letting go - In the closing teaching, he returns to the idea that laying down the world can deepen love, reduce hatred, and make ordinary helpfulness more natural.
