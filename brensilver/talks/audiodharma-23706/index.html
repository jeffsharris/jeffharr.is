Guided Meditation: Clinging is complicated, letting go simplifies

AudioDharma 30:02 2025-08-18

Matthew Brensilver offers a quietly structured guided meditation on how clinging complicates experience and letting go can simplify it. He repeatedly invites the listener to rest in the body, notice hope, fear, and thinking without finishing their stories, and soften into the breath. The practice gradually shifts toward gentleness and warmth, with an emphasis on meeting the mind’s movements without neglecting what matters.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Opening and posture - A brief welcome and invitation to settle the body and gather attention for practice.
  2. 02:05 Taking up practice slowly - He contrasts a gradual inner settling with the possibility of putting everything down at once.
  3. 04:12 Laying down complexity - The talk frames radical simplification as something that can support deeper love and service.
  4. 07:50 Hope, fear, and the body - Rather than following thought stories, the practice turns toward the bodily impact of hope, fear, and memory.
  5. 11:16 Breath as release - Breathing is presented as a quiet invitation to let go into the present moment.
  6. 16:49 Clinging and stickiness - He describes how auditory thought can feel sticky when there is little equanimity with feeling.
  7. 21:26 Gentle warmth - The closing section emphasizes the innocence of the mind and meeting it with warmth, simplicity, and care.
