Guided Meditation: Faith in Awareness

AudioDharma 30:18 2022-09-28

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation centered on trust in awareness rather than trying to engineer experience. The practice moves from settling posture and sensing what is present, to recognizing that even ordinary stillness is both familiar and fresh, to relaxing the search for signs of progress. He closes by emphasizing that the seeds of awakening are already here and that the practitioner’s work can become simpler: listen carefully, attune, and rest.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Entering the sit - Brensilver welcomes the group, frames the morning session as an exploration of technique, and invites everyone to settle into posture.
  2. 01:06 Familiar and fresh - He turns attention toward the immediate feel of sitting, noticing how stillness and silence are both well known and newly alive.
  3. 03:17 Meeting this moment - The instruction opens into curiosity about what this day brings, including whatever insight may be latent in the present body and mind.
  4. 06:23 Dharma posture - He describes a posture of awareness and heart that can be cultivated no matter what phenomena arise, serving long-term welfare and happiness.
  5. 12:29 No need for signs - The talk addresses the habit of looking for proof of progress and suggests that healing and awakening often unfold in ways we cannot directly see.
  6. 14:43 Faith and trust - Brensilver emphasizes settling back into faith in awareness and trusting that the Dharma is working through us, simplifying the practitioner’s role.
  7. 21:32 Already enough - He underscores the relief of not having to engineer the moment and points to the seeds of awakening as already present.
  8. 28:24 Listen and rest - The meditation closes by asking for careful listening, attunement to wisdom and compassion, and a final resting back into the practice.
