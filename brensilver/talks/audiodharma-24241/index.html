Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Ease Makes Letting Go Safer

AudioDharma 26:12 2025-11-19

This guided meditation invites a gentler relationship with agitation by finding a breath that carries ease into the body. Matthew Brensilver emphasizes that awareness does not solve every problem, but it can change how problems are carried, making letting go feel safer when there is some stability and warmth present. The practice moves gradually from settling the body to resting in a subtle sense of care, simplicity, and enoughness.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Settling the breath - The practice begins by finding a way of breathing that signals ease to the body.
  2. 01:11 Rest with ease - Rather than trying to remove agitation, the instruction is to rest attention in any place that feels soothed.
  3. 02:33 Care for the present - The talk links confidence about the future with taking very good care of what is here now.
  4. 04:50 Awareness changes carrying - Agitation is named as something that wants solving, but awareness is presented as dissolving the weight of experience rather than fixing every problem.
  5. 08:42 Wide world, simple attention - The meditation widens the frame to the vastness of the world while keeping attention simple, relaxed, and buoyed by care.
  6. 14:22 Letting go feels safer - The instruction turns toward the felt sense that letting go can become safer when ease, warmth, and stability are present.
  7. 20:29 Warmth and abiding - The closing settles into subtle warmth, a tiny smile, and the sense that simply abiding is enough.
