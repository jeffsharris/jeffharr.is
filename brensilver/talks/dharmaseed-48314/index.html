Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Matthew Brensilver, Sylvia Boorstein: Opening Night: Evening Practice Session (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 1:10:39 2018-01-12

This opening-night retreat talk sets a quiet frame for the weekend: a shared container of silence, care, and practical training. Sylvia Boorstein introduces the teaching team, then the talk moves into simple practices of blessing, warm-hearted intention, and meeting experience fully and as a friend. Matthew Brensilver later widens the frame to refuge, the precepts, and a steadying phrase—“tranquil and alert”—as portable supports for retreat and daily life.

Chapters

  1. 00:31 Welcoming the retreat - Sylvia introduces the teachers and assistants, names the retreat context, and sets a warm, communal tone.
  2. 03:51 Retreat instructions - Matthew describes the retreat as intentionally less structured and emphasizes the same basic instruction for beginners and long-time practitioners alike.
  3. 08:03 Seeing the people here - The room is invited to notice one another, remember why each person came, and offer brief blessings before practice begins.
  4. 16:58 Meet it as a friend - A short guided practice introduces two phrases for the breath: meeting this moment fully, and meeting it as a friend.
  5. 39:15 Skillful response - Matthew uses a Buddha story about disappointment to ask whether strong reactions would actually help, then broadens into bhavana as cultivation and the wider path of training.
  6. 45:37 Collective refuge - He frames the retreat as a shared art project and a refuge in which people can become safe for others and welcome the whole of experience.
  7. 50:38 Precepts and safety - The talk turns to non-harming and the five precepts, including the line that they are a cause of happiness, as conditions that support clarity and trust.
  8. 01:01:38 Portable practice phrases - Matthew offers Sharon Salzberg’s instruction, ‘meet this moment fully, meet it as a friend,’ as a simple way to steady ordinary activity throughout the retreat.
  9. 01:06:26 Tranquil and alert - He closes with the story of Barry, Massachusetts and the motto ‘tranquil and alert,’ turning it into a personal invocation for difficult moments.
