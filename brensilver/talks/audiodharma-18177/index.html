Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Not Locating Oneself

AudioDharma 30:09 2023-04-25

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation organized around not re-establishing the usual sense of self. He invites a gentle stance of not knowing, then balances attention to the meditation object with attention to how attention is being held. The talk gradually settles into a quiet instruction to let self-references dissolve into the openness of the present moment.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and welcome - A brief welcome and settling-in to begin the meditation.
  2. 00:30 Settling without knowing - The instruction shifts toward a gentle openness that does not fit the moment into existing ideas.
  3. 01:20 Not locating a self - Matthew names the habit of taking residence in familiar self-reference and introduces the central instruction not to locate oneself.
  4. 03:30 Opening practice - The meditation emphasizes releasing familiar reference points like who, what, and where I am, and opening in a new way.
  5. 08:09 What and how - He brings in Gil Fronsdal’s distinction between what we attend to and how we attend, suggesting a balance between the two.
  6. 09:36 Sustained guidance - The talk enters a long stretch of quiet guided practice, with the instruction carried mainly through silence and simple reminders.
  7. 18:29 Care and openness - He names a reciprocal sense of care between practitioner and moment, then returns to letting self-references dissolve.
  8. 22:10 Closing silence - The meditation continues in silence after the main instruction, allowing the open stance to settle.
