Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Talk: Guided Meditation: Insight Into Not-self

AudioDharma 52:29 2018-03-24

Matthew Brensilver explores not-self as a lived practice rather than a philosophy, beginning with how we freeze loved ones, and ourselves, into familiar concepts. He moves through mental noting, the burden of selfing, and how clinging, fear, and self-protection make experience feel dense and brittle. The talk gradually opens into a calmer view in which impermanence loosens identification, and not-self becomes closely tied to love, freedom, and care for others.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Seeing Beyond Concepts - The talk opens with the way familiarity can stop us from really seeing another person, especially people we love most.
  2. 03:36 Noting and Self Assumptions - Matthew connects selfing to meditation practice, explaining why self feels like a note that never quite lands and why it cannot serve as refuge.
  3. 09:09 Not-Self as Practice - He uses Thanissaro Bhikkhu and a study on predicted change to argue that not-self is not a new view, but a way of letting go of self-conceptions.
  4. 13:39 Ego, Shame, and Comparison - Drawing on Jack Engler and related teachers, he distinguishes healthy psychological development from spiritual anti-ego language, then turns to clinging as self-investment.
  5. 17:59 Curating the Self - A long stretch of examples, including a story about the New York Times, shows how much effort goes into protecting and performing a self-image.
  6. 24:57 Thought and the Sense of I - The talk shifts toward subtler forms of selfing in thought, asking where the thinker is and how the felt center of experience gets built.
  7. 32:29 Narrative Gravity and Fear - With Dan Dennett and Andy Olinsky, Matthew describes self as a narrative center shaped by grasping and traces self-construction back to fear and mortality.
  8. 41:37 Open Air of Change - Derek Parfit's reflections on identity lead into impermanence as the doorway to not-self, with the river-and-bank image and the softening of the 'I am' sense.
  9. 46:53 Not-Self and Love - The closing section emphasizes that when clinging loosens, energy is freed for courage, compassion, and a more natural kind of service.
