Guided Meditation: Letting the Moment Soften You

AudioDharma 30:21 2023-12-13

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation centered on the felt sense of softening rather than forcing concentration. He moves through breath, body, thought, emotion, and sound as different ways the moment can be met, with repeated encouragement to meet experience with blessing, awareness, and release. The practice ends by framing this softening as rehearsal for freedom: not pretending discomfort is pleasant, but learning to let go again and again.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Arriving - A brief opening welcomes listeners and settles into the practice.
  2. 01:13 Entering the moment - He describes different ways of coming into presence, whether gradually or all at once.
  3. 02:20 Finding the breath - The meditation turns to the breath as a way into immediacy, sometimes through effort and sometimes by simple availability.
  4. 03:39 Meeting the body - Listeners are invited to sense what hurts, what needs care, and how thought, anxiety, and emotion can be met with blessing.
  5. 09:06 Softened by phenomena - He broadens the practice to include all phenomena, treating thinking, sensation, and breath as conditions that can soften the heart.
  6. 11:17 When suppleness is lost - The talk acknowledges how identification and self-protective reactivity harden the heart, and how softness can return after contraction.
  7. 18:18 Letting go in practice - He lingers on the movement from clinging to release, including the way discomfort and relief can coexist in softening.
  8. 26:02 Rehearsing freedom - The closing frames practice as repeated rehearsal of freedom, like an athlete practicing a shot, without needing to force a result.
