Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Understanding, Forgiveness & Unconsciousness

AudioDharma 14:24 2026-03-04

Matthew Brensilver responds to a question about remorse after having done serious harm, especially the pain of not being able to make a coherent story about how it happened. He explores the limits of self-understanding, the role of unconscious forces, and how forgiveness can coexist with grief even when full explanation never arrives. The talk moves toward a gentler view of divided motivation, not-self, and the possibility of taking responsibility without disowning the parts of ourselves we do not fully know.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 The Question of Remorse - Matthew introduces the anonymous question about past harm, disbelief, self-judgment, and the wish to understand what happened.
  2. 02:09 Witnessing Without Explanation - He distinguishes understanding from bearing witness, suggesting that grief and remorse may not require a complete account of the past.
  3. 04:04 Seeking Forgiveness - The talk turns to forgiveness, including the possibility of not being forgiven, and to the patience practice asks of prolonged grief.
  4. 05:55 Reasons and Causes - Matthew contrasts the reasons we can tell with the deeper causes we cannot directly see, arguing that conscious stories never capture everything.
  5. 08:34 The Unconscious at Work - He describes unconscious forces as disruptive but not simply bad, and as evidence that we are more mysterious to ourselves than we assume.
  6. 09:44 Divided Selves - Drawing on Freud and Adam Phillips, he develops the themes of ambivalence, mixed motives, and the many versions of self that live within one life.
  7. 12:04 Pleasure and Renunciation - Matthew reflects on how Buddhist practice matures our relationship to pleasure, fidelity, renunciation, and the unlived lives that shadow us.
  8. 13:34 Love and Heartbreak - He closes by returning to heartbreak as something that can still be dedicated to love.
