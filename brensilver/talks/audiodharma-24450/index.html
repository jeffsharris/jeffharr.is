Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Lightly Guided Meditation

AudioDharma 25:39 2025-12-17

Matthew Brensilver offers a lightly guided meditation on relaxing the body and loosening control so experience can be met more simply. The talk moves from basic instructions to a reflection on equanimity, non-proliferation, and how unpleasant experience can be allowed to end without turning into a story. It closes with a gentler, more embodied image of the moment as something that can soften the body-mind and strengthen the case for love.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Settling the body - The meditation begins by inviting relaxation in the body and a loosening of control.
  2. 01:00 Putting down identity - The guidance turns toward forgetting, even briefly, who we think we are and releasing habitual reactions.
  3. 02:02 Letting experience be - The talk deepens into allowing pleasant and unpleasant sensations to be exactly what they are, without adding extra interpretation.
  4. 07:10 Equanimity and non-proliferation - Brensilver describes equanimity as what lets experience stop at itself instead of becoming a problem or command.
  5. 09:37 Very alive to this moment - The repeated phrase emphasizes a vivid, present awareness that does not need to elaborate.
  6. 11:03 Softening into love - A closing image of the moment as a skilled healer frames discomfort as something that can support tenderness and courage.
  7. 12:39 Closing bell meditation - The remainder is quiet closing time marked by bells and space for the practice to settle.
