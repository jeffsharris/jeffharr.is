Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Insight & Love

AudioDharma 15:17 2026-04-29

Matthew Brensilver explores how insight and love are not separate paths, but mutually reinforcing responses to lived experience. He argues that when impermanence, unsatisfactoriness, and not-self are felt deeply rather than understood abstractly, they soften the heart and make care, non-violence, and forgiveness more natural. The talk moves from a question about wisdom and compassion into a grounded reflection on grief, selfing, and the relief of non-ownership.

Chapters

  1. 00:17 Insight and love - Matthew opens by asking whether awareness and love converge, and suggests they do when the three characteristics are felt with emotional force.
  2. 02:01 Wisdom from ordinariness - He describes mindfulness as the training that lets ordinary experience become wisdom, with care and non-violence arising as a by-product.
  3. 03:27 Gunaratana on insight - He cites Bhante Gunaratana to describe the searing recognition of impermanence, suffering, and no-self that ends craving.
  4. 04:55 Heartbreak before freedom - The talk turns to the non-linear side of practice, where the Dharma may first break the heart before it feels liberating.
  5. 06:23 Dukkha softens the heart - Matthew says suffering can make hatred less tenable and awaken a desire to help within the helplessness of being human.
  6. 08:00 Facing impermanence - He reflects on how acknowledging change, including through the five remembrances, helps the heart catch up with anicca and grieve more honestly.
  7. 10:38 Letting self relax - The teaching shifts to anatta, showing how self-view feeds fear, envy, and defensiveness, and how loosening identification brings forgiveness and playfulness.
  8. 13:34 Empty center, open heart - He closes by linking ethical alignment, non-ownership, and the widening of care, ending with the invitation to explore how understanding and love support each other.
