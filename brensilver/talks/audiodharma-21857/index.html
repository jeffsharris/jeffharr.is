Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: The Divine Abodes (2 of 5) Metta

AudioDharma 15:08 2024-12-10

Matthew Brensilver explores metta as something distinct from liking or wanting: not another form of preference, but a different stance of the heart. He traces how fear and craving contract the mind, then shows how loving-kindness can become an approach to pain, a purification practice, and a way of abiding with less control and more openness. The talk closes by linking metta to moral elevation, the recognition of goodness, and the possibility of resting in a warm, spacious heart.

Chapters

  1. 00:35 Love beyond liking - He opens by separating metta from ordinary liking and desire, arguing that love is not just another point on a spectrum of preference.
  2. 01:49 Wanting and fear - He connects wanting and fear as forms of clinging that contract the mind and turn experience into a transaction.
  3. 03:36 Metta as approach - Loving-kindness is presented as a move toward what is avoided, easing anxiety and helping pain loosen its grip.
  4. 05:38 What love is not - He clarifies that metta is not niceness, but an open and receptive love that lets go of control.
  5. 06:59 Attachment and family - Using a mentor’s distinction and a few literary references, he acknowledges that some love remains bound up with attachment and hurt.
  6. 10:19 Goodness as source - He turns toward a love that seeks no control and arises from seeing goodness and honesty clearly.
  7. 11:39 Moral elevation - He brings in the psychology of moral elevation to describe how witnessing goodness can move us into greater devotion to it.
  8. 13:23 Abiding in love - The talk closes by describing metta as an abode of rest and warmth that makes hatred less and less tenable.
