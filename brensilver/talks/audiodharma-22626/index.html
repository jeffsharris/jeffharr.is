Talk: Knowing, Not-Knowing, Selfing

AudioDharma 47:56 2025-03-20

Matthew Brensilver explores Dharma as a disciplined movement between knowing and not knowing: learning enough to see suffering clearly, while loosening the need to control experience with certainty. He connects self-knowledge to dialogue, sangha, and the willingness to let familiar identity become less solid. The talk gradually shifts from ideas about practice into a close examination of how selfing is built from habit, feeling tone, and subtle assumptions.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Knowing and Not Knowing - Brensilver introduces the talk as a dance between knowing and not knowing, and frames selfing as something shaped by grasping and ignorance.
  2. 02:30 Safety and Power - He develops knowing and not knowing as complementary qualities, showing how practice needs both precision and an openness that does not cover over mystery.
  3. 06:15 Dialogue and Inquiry - Using Agnes Callard, he argues that self-understanding requires other people, honest questioning, and the courage to unsettle fixed answers.
  4. 08:44 Narrative Fractures - He turns to the ways self-narratives break down, and how practice reveals that our summaries of ourselves are always incomplete.
  5. 11:43 Ignorance and Dukkha - Drawing on the Buddha's teachings, he links ignorance to unskillful qualities, the Four Noble Truths, and the need to understand conditioning in order to be free.
  6. 17:39 Learning What Matters - He contrasts ordinary learning with Dharma learning, quoting Yeats, Ajahn Chah, and the Dhammapada to show that practice means loosening the mind's grip on security and certainty.
  7. 24:14 Renunciation of Control - Brensilver describes how compulsive knowing is driven by fear, and how surrender is the dissolution of volitional energies rather than a strategy for getting somewhere.
  8. 28:03 The Two Selves - He distinguishes narrative and sensorial selfing, then shows how the self becomes more visible when the mind gets quiet and what was transparent begins to look opaque.
  9. 36:36 Listening Through Identity - With Nisargadatta and Ajahn Sucitto, he emphasizes deep listening to feeling tone, mood, and habit energies, ending with a gentler view of self and more compassion for its arising.
