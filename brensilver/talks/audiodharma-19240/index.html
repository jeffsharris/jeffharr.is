Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Friday Morning Instructions

AudioDharma 38:39 2023-11-03

Matthew Brensilver explores how perception is shaped by motivation, then turns toward letting go as a way of seeing more clearly. He moves from the innocence of clinging and the body’s affective patterns into a steadier, more compassionate relationship with experience, including the repeated refrain that we are not doing our life wrong. The talk closes on trust: awareness is not a story, and when we fall, love may be what catches us.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Wanting Shapes Seeing - He begins by pointing out that what we see and what we want are not really separate, and that motivation infuses knowing.
  2. 01:37 Letting Go - The teaching shifts to letting go as a practical necessity for clearer seeing, while refusing the idea that this means approving future harm.
  3. 03:14 Clinging and Self-Knowledge - He argues that clinging blocks self-understanding and that awareness must meet the body and mind without preference or self-reproach.
  4. 09:21 Affect and Innocence - The talk examines how clinging arises through arousal and valence, then softens into the repeated reassurance that we are not doing life wrong.
  5. 17:52 Suffering and Delusion - He describes how bodily suffering, resolution, and persistence relate to greed, hate, and the ways delusion rationalizes them.
  6. 20:42 Mind In Images - The teaching turns to how thinking works through words, pictures, and bodily orientation, along with the many assumptions that shape the next moment.
  7. 27:43 Awareness Is Not Story - He emphasizes that awareness is not an autobiography and that there are many Dharma contexts where our usual knowing cannot be carried forward.
  8. 29:50 Faith and Being Held - The talk ends in a long invocation of trust: that the Dharma will not harm us, and that love may catch us when we fall.
