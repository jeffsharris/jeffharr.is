Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Wholesome Doubt and Faith

AudioDharma 53:44 2014-02-06

Matthew Brensilver explores wholesome doubt as a necessary companion to faith rather than its opposite. Using the Kalama Sutta, he contrasts paralyzing uncertainty with a testing, inquiry-based trust that grows through practice and direct experience. The talk moves from early doubt and confidence, to bright faith and its maturing, and then into audience questions about historical evidence and the challenge of right speech.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Opening the Question - He introduces doubt and faith as intertwined qualities and frames the talk around wholesome doubt.
  2. 04:09 The Kalama Sutta - Matthew uses the Buddha’s guidance to the Kalama villagers as the main example of testing teachings by their effects.
  3. 07:18 Come See For Yourself - He reflects on the tension between being invited to find out for oneself and being guided by teachers.
  4. 09:59 Practice as Inquiry - Drawing on Sam Harris and his own language, he describes Buddhism as something to test in experience rather than accept as belief.
  5. 17:26 Doubt and First Steps - He turns to suffering, confidence, and the beginning of practice, including the way doubt can launch a search for freedom.
  6. 27:45 Bright Faith - Using Sharon Salzberg’s account and the idea of bright faith, he describes an energetic, hopeful phase that can propel commitment.
  7. 33:33 Letting Faith Mature - He speaks about a steadier faith that no longer needs to defend itself and remains open to revisiting what is actually known.
  8. 40:52 Questions and Right Speech - In the question period he addresses historical uncertainty about the Buddha, then responds to a listener struggling with right speech.
  9. 46:49 Mudita and Closing - A final question about sympathetic joy leads to a warning about skeptical counterexamples, then the talk closes with a short dedication.
