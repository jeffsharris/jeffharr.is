Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Insight of Not Self and Relationships

AudioDharma 43:15 2016-10-23

Matthew Brensilver explores how insight into not-self often stays narrow until it is tested in relationship. He moves from psychotherapy's idea of generalization to the ways selfing shows up in eye contact, shame, family life, and love, using a few stories and references to show how ego quietly presses on others. The talk ends by widening not-self into not-other, suggesting that letting go can make room for more safety, tenderness, and ease in relationship.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Practice Beyond the Cushion - Matthew opens by describing how insights learned in meditation need time to spread into ordinary life.
  2. 02:16 Wisdom That Generalizes - He uses a Sam Harris quote to frame the gap between knowing what is wise and actually living that way.
  3. 04:53 Relationship Reveals Habit - Other people expose the habitual energies that remain hidden when we are alone, especially through moods and subtle conditioning.
  4. 07:58 Silence and Selfing - A retreat memory shows how quiet can soften self-view, while ordinary conversation quickly reactivates pride and compulsion.
  5. 13:24 Anatta in the Eyes - He turns to anatta and argues that the gaze of another can make the self both more vivid and more visible.
  6. 19:01 Shame and the Empty Boat - Shame, ego pressure, and a Chinese allegory all point to the way self-clinging spills into relationships.
  7. 23:53 Becoming Safe for Others - Matthew develops the idea of being safe for others as a practice of non-harm, less fragility, and less pressure in the room.
  8. 31:47 Not-Self and Not-Other - A story from Ajahn Sumedho extends the teaching beyond self-construction to the way we make others fixed and solid.
  9. 34:54 Love, Possession, Letting Go - Using the film Her, he explores love without possession and closes by reflecting on how letting go can deepen care in relationship.
