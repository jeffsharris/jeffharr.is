Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Wholesome Qualities of Mind (4of5); The Parami of Metta

AudioDharma 14:43 2025-08-21

Matthew Brensilver reflects on metta as a kind of love that is joined with wisdom, not sentimentality. He moves from a playful critique of Hallmark-style love into a broader account of how kindness steadies us, protects us from hatred, and helps us meet reality with courage. The talk closes by framing loving-kindness as something we discover in lived experience and in the nervous system, not as an idea to admire from afar.

Chapters

  1. 00:11 Opening the Theme - Matthew begins by setting aside a recent thread on non-ownership and turning toward the day's focus on the Pāramīs and love.
  2. 00:48 Why Love Needs Defending - He explains why the word love can trigger skepticism and clarifies that he is not talking about sentimental or sugary affection.
  3. 01:39 Not Hallmark Love - Using a Christmas-movie bingo joke, he distinguishes the love he means from cliché romance and names it as something more serious.
  4. 03:57 Love and Wisdom Together - Matthew argues that Dharma love is infused with wisdom and cites Michelle McDonald as an example of that unity in practice.
  5. 05:30 What Love Sustains - He describes love as what gives us the strength to face things as they are and as a secure place to fall back into when life does not go well.
  6. 07:04 Training Against Hatred - The talk turns to how love is cultivated, why aversion keeps looking for a target, and how non-hatred connects to letting go, renunciation, and ethical conduct.
  7. 09:39 The Brahmavihāras - Matthew summarizes Ajahn Sucitto's embodied descriptions of metta, karuna, mudita, and upekkha as different shapes of a malleable love.
  8. 11:25 Metta As Necessity - He narrows to metta as willingness and an attunement to goodness, then closes by arguing that love is not merely moral but necessary, even in a finite life.
  9. 14:05 Closing Blessing - Matthew offers a brief closing reflection and invites listeners to notice goodness and rejoice in it.
