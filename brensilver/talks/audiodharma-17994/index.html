Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharma Talk: Delusion on the Path

AudioDharma 54:24 2023-03-17

Matthew Brensilver explores delusion not as a single mistake, but as a whole pattern of mind: self-deception, identification with thought, confabulated stories, and the subtle ways greed and aversion get justified. He moves from retreat as a form of travel, through Buddhist and psychological models of mind-wandering, toward a quieter practice of receptivity, humility, and learning from experience. The talk ends by loosening the grip of self-view and asking what wakefulness looks like when we are willing to be wrong.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening on Deception - Matthew introduces the talk with Oscar Wilde and frames delusion as something painful, instructive, and strangely liberating when seen clearly.
  2. 03:03 Retreat as Travel - Using Camus, he describes retreat as a kind of travel that leaves familiar habits behind and makes the mind more visible and porous.
  3. 06:07 What Delusion Is - He sketches several ways to understand delusion, from being lost in thought to being caught in a bubble of self-identification.
  4. 13:12 Ignorance and the Plot - The talk turns to Buddhist personality types, the three basic tendencies, and delusion as a broader ignorance or loss of the plot.
  5. 17:41 Unknown Unknowns - With Rumsfeld's famous distinction, Matthew emphasizes that much of practice concerns what we do not know we do not know, and how overconfidence distorts life.
  6. 22:07 Learning from Everything - He reflects on humility, openness to other people and other forms of wisdom, and the idea that life itself can become a continuous feedback loop.
  7. 27:22 Effort and Willfulness - Matthew warns that practice can become efforting, performance, or a hidden rage to control experience, and he asks what effort looks like after surrender.
  8. 34:24 The Meditator as Program - Drawing on Ajahn Sucitto and a confabulation experiment, he questions the fixed inner agent and shows how stories about choice and preference are often fabricated.
  9. 39:24 Body, Feeling, and Laundering - He leans on Tim Wilson's work on unconscious mental life to suggest practical ways of detecting delusion through the body, feeling tone, and the ways greed and hatred get laundered into acceptable stories.
