Equanimity 1

AudioDharma 1:10:35 2025-02-16

Matthew Brensilver explores equanimity as something more active and intimate than calm or passivity: a way of meeting unpleasantness, helplessness, and feeling without trying to control experience. He moves from examples in retreat life, parenting, politics, and prison work to a practical claim that equanimity softens reactivity, reduces the pressure of preference, and supports wiser action. The talk includes short audience reflections and a long guided sitting that turns toward bodily sensation, models of self and world, and the felt sense of urgency.

Chapters

  1. 01:20 What retreat teaches - Matthew opens by contrasting lecture with the experiential work of retreat and frames the day as practice with the problem of suffering.
  2. 05:18 First meanings of equanimity - He introduces equanimity through Michelle McDonald’s phrase and begins distinguishing it from preference, grit, and emotional flattening.
  3. 11:40 Softening instead of freezing - The talk develops equanimity as a heart that softens in the face of imperfection rather than hardening into grim endurance.
  4. 19:35 Helplessness and wise action - Using questions from participants, including prison work, politics, and family life, he explores equanimity as love in the face of helplessness and as a basis for response rather than reaction.
  5. 29:25 Sitting with experience - Matthew leads a long period of guided silence, inviting attention to the body, the room, and the models we build of self, world, and future.
  6. 40:32 Friction falls away - He returns to the theme of equanimity as a reduction in compulsive friction, especially when the mind no longer waits for pleasure to rescue it.
  7. 54:45 Love it, not escape it - A playful dialogue with the Buddha and a quotation from the Satipatthana Sutta sharpen the claim that mindfulness begins by setting down covetousness and displeasure.
  8. 58:42 Feeling at the center - Matthew closes by linking equanimity to de-reification, the move from story to contour, and the pervasive role of feeling tone in every moment of mind.
