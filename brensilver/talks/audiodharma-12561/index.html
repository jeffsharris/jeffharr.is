Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Friday Morning Instructions: The non-compulsion of awareness

AudioDharma 53:59 2020-12-11

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet instruction on how awareness can hold experience without being driven by it. He moves from settling into posture and breath, to the way habit and memory color the present, to a more somatic recognition of clinging as pressure, urgency, and drivenness in the body. The talk then widens into a gentle reframe of liberation, ending with a reminder that the Dharma is flexible and that practice is about noticing what is skillful now.

Chapters

  1. 00:30 Settling In - The talk begins with posture, breath, and an invitation to let the body feel welcomed rather than arranged.
  2. 03:40 Breath and Body - He settles the room into silent practice and points toward a simple groundedness in breath and body.
  3. 07:53 The Past in the Present - Matthew explains how the present moment carries habit, memory, retreat conditions, and pandemic life into this sit.
  4. 15:31 Awareness Without Compulsion - He contrasts the compulsive force of thoughts and emotions with awareness, which does not push or command.
  5. 24:21 Clinging in the Body - Clinging is described as bodily friction, tension, pressure, and urgency, so it can be recognized directly.
  6. 34:15 Freeing Experience - The teaching turns outward from self-improvement toward freeing clinging and all experience with kindness.
  7. 40:24 Putting Down Judgment - Using the phrase 'The All-Knowing Mind,' he speaks about relinquishing the burden of sorting everything into right and wrong.
  8. 48:34 Skillful Means - He closes by naming the Dharma as many skillful means and encouraging listeners to notice what is helpful in each moment.
