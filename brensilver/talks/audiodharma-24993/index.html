Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: non-referentiality

AudioDharma 26:37 2026-03-04

Matthew Brensilver leads a guided meditation that gradually shifts from settling with a subtly pleasant sensation into a broader invitation to release reference points altogether. The practice moves through body-based cues, softening the mind’s tendency to measure and orient, and then toward a sense of unpartitioned space, non-referential awareness, and less bracing against pain or time. Near the end, the language becomes more spacious and poetic, with reminders that this moment is innocent and that the trees, as a final image, seem to arrive where they are going.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Settling with the pleasant - The practice begins by finding something subtly pleasant and letting that be enough, even if it exists alongside difficulty.
  2. 01:17 A soft smile - He suggests a tiny, almost invisible smile and noticing how bodily ripples can shape the mind's tone.
  3. 02:38 Dropping the vantage point - The meditation questions the habit of taking a position inside experience and begins to loosen the sense of body-mind as fixed territory.
  4. 04:35 Renounce orientation - The instruction turns more direct, inviting a release of representation and referentiality and a return to immediate sensation.
  5. 06:19 Fear and love - He contrasts the narrowing of fear with the leveling quality of love, then points to the ways we keep score even in this moment.
  6. 08:31 Relaxing more deeply - The meditation continues in a quieter register, inviting deeper relaxation and a less defended relationship to experience.
  7. 17:09 Not bracing against pain - The emphasis returns to bodily sensation and the practice of not bracing against time or pain.
  8. 21:06 The grammar of self - He points to 'away from me' as an artifact of grammar, loosening the reflexive structure of ownership and separation.
  9. 25:49 Trees and arrival - The closing image evokes trees and future journeys as a gentle way of sensing movement without strain or self-reference.
