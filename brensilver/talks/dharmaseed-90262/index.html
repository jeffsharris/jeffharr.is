Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Fear, Thinking, Safety and Samadhi (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 40:40 2025-05-12

Matthew Brensilver explores how fear, selfing, and compulsive thinking arise together as ways the mind tries to stay safe. He moves from early attachment and the formation of identity into the defensive uses of language, then toward samadhi as a more integrated, less ambivalent way of abiding. The talk gradually turns from analysis to trust: courage, faith, and a willingness to let silence, groundlessness, and even enjoyment be non-threatening.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 How self forms - Brensilver opens by sketching early human vulnerability and the way identity develops through attunement, misattunement, and reattunement.
  2. 03:57 Fear and regulation - He develops the idea that the self is less a fixed essence than a regulatory process shaped by fear, survival, and the need for safety.
  3. 09:10 Feeling as alarm - The talk turns to body sensation, emotion, and language as rapid responses to threat and uncertainty, with thinking framed as a coping mechanism.
  4. 14:09 The safety of thinking - He examines why narration and conceptualization can feel protective, even when they are painful, and how this habitual problem-solving runs against practice.
  5. 18:32 Letting practice work - Brensilver distinguishes willfulness from willingness and describes the kind of effort needed as silence and self-concern become more visible.
  6. 24:15 Healing and mortality - He speaks about reducing the charge of past wounds, working with fear directly, and acclimating the heart to finitude and death.
  7. 30:43 Courage and faith - The talk draws on the Buddha, Aquinas, Dogen, and others to frame courage, surrender, and trust as supports for deeper stillness.
  8. 36:56 Samadhi and clarity - He closes by describing samadhi as the resolution of ambivalence, a state where the mind is no longer split and clarity can show what is here.
