Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Awareness and Effortlessness

AudioDharma 30:47 2024-05-01

Matthew Brensilver explores the shift from narrating experience to resting in awareness itself, drawing on Thomas Metzinger to describe silence as non-conceptual, non-reactive knowing. The talk moves from theory into guided sitting, where effort, choicelessness, and relaxation are treated as supports that can eventually fall away. Later he turns toward equanimity, frictionlessness, and the sense that the moment is no longer a problem to solve but something to trust.

Chapters

  1. 00:30 Narrative Self - He begins by noticing how meditation is easily folded into the story of who we are and what is happening.
  2. 02:23 Silent Knowing - Using Thomas Metzinger as a reference, he describes awareness as silent, non-conceptual, and free of grasping.
  3. 05:07 Instructions And Fruit - The meditation instructions are framed as gestures toward qualities that become natural when effort drops away.
  4. 07:18 Settling To Sit - He invites the group to sit, breathe, and relax whatever can be relaxed.
  5. 09:17 Friction And Equanimity - The teaching turns to frictionlessness, imperfection, and equanimity as a way of not fighting experience.
  6. 12:32 Effort Toward Release - He emphasizes that some effort may be needed at first in order to relinquish willfulness and choice-making.
  7. 18:19 Trusting Silence - The practice deepens into a quieter trust, where the moment stops feeling like a task and silence feels more central than sound.
  8. 25:26 Resting Safely - In the closing portion, he says life and awareness do not depend on personal control, and ends by noting the intended audience for these sessions.
