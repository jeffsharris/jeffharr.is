Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Meaning and Purpose

AudioDharma 14:52 2024-08-21

Matthew Brensilver explores meaning and purpose through a practical lens, beginning with a Seneca line about having a destination before asking which parts of life are inherently meaningful. He then draws on self-determination theory to name three basic needs — autonomy, competence, and relatedness — and shows how Dharma practice can strengthen each one. The talk closes by turning from self-focus toward love, friendship, and service as sources of purpose.

Chapters

  1. 00:09 Seneca’s question - A Stoic line about sailing toward a destination opens the talk’s central question about direction and purpose.
  2. 00:44 Why meaning matters - Matthew explains why he wanted to speak on meaning and purpose, and why the topic is difficult to prescribe from the outside.
  3. 02:57 Intrinsic motivation - Using self-determination theory, he distinguishes intrinsic from extrinsic motivation and suggests meaning is tied to what is rewarding in itself.
  4. 05:42 Autonomy and freedom - He names autonomy as a basic need, connecting it to willingness, choice, and even the meaningfulness of rest and attention.
  5. 08:42 Competence and understanding - Matthew turns to competence, linking a sense of efficacy to wisdom, self-understanding, and learning to navigate human life skillfully.
  6. 11:25 Relatedness and love - He describes relatedness as feeling cared for and connected, then points to sangha, family, service, and love as the heart of meaning.
  7. 13:44 A life less centered - The talk closes by tying autonomy, competence, and relatedness together and inviting listeners to consider how life might become richer.
