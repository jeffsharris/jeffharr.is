Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Letting Go Cultivates Stability

AudioDharma 36:15 2021-12-05

Matthew Brensilver offers a gently guided sit centered on landing in the body, gathering attention with the breath, and loosening the habit of trying to improve the moment. The talk moves from settling and gratitude into a clear explanation of why letting go can stabilize attention rather than weaken it. It then opens into an extended period of loving-kindness phrases, ending with the suggestion to trust awareness instead of endlessly optimizing experience.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Settling In - The sit begins with a soft welcome and a gradual settling into the body, practice, and a sense of connection.
  2. 05:24 Breath as Home - Attention gathers around the natural rhythm of breathing, with the point that this is less about effortful focus than about release.
  3. 11:21 Letting Go of Better - The teaching turns toward how clinging feeds thinking and how letting go means releasing the many ways we imagine this moment could be improved.
  4. 17:21 Truce with Imperfection - Brensilver describes letting go as making a deep truce with imperfection and softening the need for conditions to be different.
  5. 23:40 Loving-Kindness Phrases - A long stretch of repeated well-wishing phrases supports a steady, receptive mood and a quieter relationship to experience.
  6. 29:10 Trusting Awareness - The closing reflections emphasize renouncing the impulse to optimize the moment and trusting awareness instead, while the loving-kindness continues.
