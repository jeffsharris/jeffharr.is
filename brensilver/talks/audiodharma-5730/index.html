Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Not-Self

AudioDharma 49:53 2015-06-03

Matthew Brensilver explores not-self as a practical shift in how we relate to experience, not as a new belief to adopt. He moves from self-love and self-esteem into the Buddha’s critique of clinging, then uses psychology, personal stories, and sutta passages to show how the self is constructed and protected. The talk closes by linking centerlessness with quiet, relief, and a more open kind of love.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Love and selflessness - He opens by placing confidence and self-love on a continuum with selflessness, rather than treating them as opposites.
  2. 01:58 Evaluating the self - Psychology and ordinary practice both show the problem of turning the self into an object to be judged and compared.
  3. 04:51 Ego and insecurity - He argues that even a well-adjusted self still carries dukkha, and that egoic life is built around anxiety and clinging.
  4. 07:31 Not a new view - Using Thanissaro Bhikkhu and personal examples, he warns against turning not-self into another position or philosophy.
  5. 12:43 Image and possession - Humorous stories about a polished bio and a teaching mishap show how fragile self-image is and how quickly insight gets owned.
  6. 19:36 Narrative gravity - He shifts to the idea of self as a center of narrative gravity, a useful way to see how identity is constructed.
  7. 24:30 Grasping makes self - The talk deepens into how grasping, not a solid self, generates the sense of 'I am' and the tension around it.
  8. 30:41 Quiet and anicca - Nisargadatta and sutta material point toward stepping off the ground of self, with impermanence as a gateway to release.
  9. 37:49 Centerless peace - Douglas Harding and Rumi close the talk with images of not-finding, facelessness, and a spaciousness that opens into love and joy.
