Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Attention, Effort, Choice and Selfing

AudioDharma 30:46 2024-04-17

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation framed around a subtle paradox: meditation asks for attention, effort, and choice, while also pointing toward choicelessness, effortlessness, and surrender. He keeps returning to the way practice can strengthen a sense of doer and meditator, then gently loosens that grip by treating selfing as something to study rather than defeat. The guidance moves from settling posture and narrowing attention to breathing, through reminders and rebalancing, and toward a quieter release of the need to manage the meditation at all.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Opening orientation - The talk begins by welcoming listeners and setting up the basic movement from speaking into guided practice.
  2. 00:42 Attention and effort - He names meditation as directing attention, making effort, and using intention, while noting how these moves can evoke self.
  3. 02:26 Selfing and practice - The teaching reframes self and not-self as different capacities, not opposites to win or lose, and prepares for the meditation instructions.
  4. 06:12 Settling with breath - Instructions turn to finding a sustainable posture, choosing an anchor, and gently returning attention to breathing and away from discursive thought.
  5. 09:03 Stability and reminders - He acknowledges ordinary choice-making and gives room for small reminders and support while stability develops.
  6. 14:33 Doer and deed - The meditation explores the natural sense of a meditator and object, then points to a deeper surrender that leaves less room for the doer to congeal.
  7. 19:33 Surrender to grace - Brensilver says surrender is not something the ego can accomplish by force, and invites a yielding to the Dharma's grace.
  8. 24:40 Beyond hierarchy - The closing repetition pushes beyond simple comparisons of self and not-self, warning that trying to rank them can strengthen the very selfing the practice is seeing.
