Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Lightly Guided Meditation

AudioDharma 30:25 2022-04-11

Matthew Brensilver offers a lightly guided meditation centered on letting attention rest differently: not as a spotlight from behind the eyes, but as the body being pervaded by awareness. He moves through images of earth, vibration, and spaciousness, then gently points to how present-moment experience can loosen control and soften the heart. In the closing reflections, he emphasizes that practice does not remove life’s complexity, but can resolve suffering in this moment.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Welcome and format - Matthew greets the group and briefly names the lightly guided structure of the sit and reflections that follow.
  2. 00:56 Settling the posture - He invites participants to settle into posture and begin with simple bodily presence before any deliberate effort.
  3. 02:44 Awareness in the body - He suggests sensing the body as pervaded and porous to awareness, rather than as an object viewed from a fixed point.
  4. 05:00 Earth and space - The guidance shifts between grounded earth-element imagery and the body as vibrating space, allowing awareness to be felt in different ways.
  5. 08:43 Control and time - He reflects on the discomfort of presence, the impulse to control, and the way the mind moves through past, future, and present.
  6. 12:55 Letting the sit be - He asks listeners to put down narratives about how the sit is going and become radically available to the moment.
  7. 16:31 Softening through imperfection - In the reflections, he points to the way practice can leverage imperfection itself, letting life soften the heart rather than harden it.
  8. 23:19 Confidence in letting go - He closes by describing letting go as a delight, a growing faith, and a way to reduce suffering even if it does not solve everything.
