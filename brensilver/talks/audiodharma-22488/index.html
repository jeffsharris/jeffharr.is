Guided Meditation: Peace is Close

AudioDharma 29:43 2025-03-12

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet guided meditation centered on settling the body, setting worries aside without force, and softening into immediate experience. The practice moves from simple breathing to a felt sense of defenselessness, then to noticing how the mind elaborates and constructs a self. In the later portion, the teaching opens into a subtle contemplation of being unseen, empty, and no longer held together by familiar identity.

Chapters

  1. 00:14 Arriving Together - He welcomes the group and invites them to practice without needing to explain much.
  2. 00:51 Landing In Breath - The meditation begins with deeper breaths and a simple settling into the body.
  3. 02:45 Putting Worries Down - He asks listeners to make room for worries without abandoning them, and to stand defenseless before the moment.
  4. 05:06 Offering The Heart - The instruction becomes repeated and plain: lay down life-projects and offer the heart to what is here.
  5. 07:04 Breathing Without Elaboration - He explores how experience gets elaborated and points back to the simplicity of just being a body breathing.
  6. 12:07 Small Shifts Matter - He acknowledges how hard practice can feel, then points to subtle, nearby shifts that can be noticed and appreciated.
  7. 17:59 Constructing Self - The talk turns to how selfing is built from quiet assumptions and unnoticed sense data.
  8. 22:07 Unseen And Empty - He invites a contemplation of never being seen as an object and closes with the image of empty phenomena rolling on.
