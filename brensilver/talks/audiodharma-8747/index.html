Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Letting Go of Becoming

AudioDharma 48:24 2018-03-23

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet guided meditation on how mindfulness can soften the urge to become someone, acquire something, or fix experience. The practice moves from simple questions about what it means to stop meditating or stop being still, into resting with safety, silence, and awareness, and then into a more pointed reflection on clinging to the future and the self. Later, the tone becomes more intimate and paradoxical, with reminders that suffering matters and doesn’t, that we can’t fill the heart’s hole from the outside, and that love remains after letting go.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Entering Stillness - The talk opens with repeated questions that loosen assumptions about meditating, being still, and what actually needs to begin.
  2. 03:57 Safety and Simplicity - Mindfulness is framed as a way to make the inner life feel safe enough to relax into the simplicity of this moment.
  3. 06:47 Awakening as Letting Go - Awareness is presented as release from clinging to the next moment, the future, becoming, and the wish to be someone or nothing.
  4. 13:06 Space Through the Mind - The meditation turns toward letting space and silence move through the body and meeting the chatter and solidity of mind with care.
  5. 18:27 The Heart's Hole - The teaching deepens into the paradox that suffering matters and does not, and that we cannot fill the hole in the heart from outside ourselves.
  6. 24:24 Love After Letting Go - After letting go, the emphasis shifts toward love as what remains, with a stark, repeated reflection on hatred, absurdity, and what is left.
  7. 36:15 Closing Praise - The talk winds down with the lingering refrain on love, a few closing promotional lines, and a gentle, personal farewell.
