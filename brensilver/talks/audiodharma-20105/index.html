Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: What's Already Here?

AudioDharma 28:16 2024-04-03

Matthew Brensilver opens with a brief reflection on war, cooperation, and the idea that peace may be something human beings have to invent. He then turns the talk into a guided meditation centered on a recurring instruction: stop looking, and let awareness, breath, silence, and even healing "find" you. The practice keeps returning to the ordinary fact that what we seek is already present, and that freedom and suffering meet in the same place.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening reflection - He introduces the sit with a brief reflection on war, cooperation, and the possibility that peace must be intentionally invented.
  2. 02:25 Freedom in suffering - Ajahn Chah's teaching frames the practice: freedom is found in the same place as suffering, so the invitation is to turn toward what's already here.
  3. 04:13 Settling the body - The guided meditation begins with a sustainable posture and a deliberate shift away from efforting.
  4. 06:13 Letting breath arrive - He repeatedly invites the listener not to search for the breath, but to let the breath find them and suffuse the body.
  5. 10:39 Being found - The emphasis deepens into a release of duality and self-doubt as the experience of being found softens the sense of a seeker.
  6. 17:10 Resting in presence - He returns to the theme that freedom and suffering share the same place, encouraging the body to embody that freedom rather than pursue it.
  7. 22:44 Healing the search - The practice closes by resting in what is already here, with stillness and silence offering a gentle healing of fundamental alienation.
