Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Stillness and the Dissolution of the Predictive Mind

AudioDharma 29:40 2025-04-02

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation that begins with settling the body and gradually shifts toward the breath as a simple object of awareness. He links relaxation to a softer relationship with dukkha, then moves into a teaching on how meditation loosens the mind’s habit of predicting the next moment and imagining continuity. The talk closes by pointing toward samadhi as a quiet dissolution of that predictive architecture and a more direct falling into the present.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening and settling in - Matthew greets the group and invites everyone into practice with a quiet, communal tone.
  2. 01:31 Doing much less - He frames meditation as a response to life feeling overwhelming, a way of doing less and becoming open to experience.
  3. 02:48 Softening the body - The practice turns to releasing tension in the face, jaw, shoulders, and belly, with repeated reminders to soften and let go.
  4. 07:06 Grounding and openness - He includes the hips, pelvis, feet, and hands, emphasizing a sense of energy moving through the body without obstruction.
  5. 10:38 Breath and aliveness - The breath becomes the main object of awareness, and the talk settles into enjoying simplicity and the pleasure of breathing.
  6. 16:48 Predicting the future - The teaching shifts from bodily calm to the mind’s subtle habit of leaning forward with expectations and projections.
  7. 19:46 Samadhi and craving - Matthew describes samadhi as a dissolution of predictive architecture and connects craving with the word 'next.'
  8. 23:38 Falling into presence - He softens the assumption that 'I am next,' encouraging a direct return to the present moment and a release of self-making.
