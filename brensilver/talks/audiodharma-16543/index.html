Guided Meditation

AudioDharma 36:47 2022-07-03

This guided meditation begins with settling the body and attention, using Thich Nhat Hanh’s image of nectar sinking to the bottom of a glass to suggest a natural rather than forced calm. Matthew then moves between gentle instruction and long stretches of silence, first inviting a more precise focus and then a wider opening to whatever needs patience, love, and rest. In the closing section, he names the effort involved in directing attention and points toward an effortless dimension of awareness that does not need to be propped up.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and settling - After a brief introduction, the meditation begins with silence and a gradual settling into the practice.
  2. 02:49 Nectar image - Matthew introduces Thich Nhat Hanh’s image of nectar settling, framing the practice as natural settling rather than strain.
  3. 03:13 Precise or open - He contrasts using a concrete technique with opening more broadly to see what needs attention, patience, and love.
  4. 04:49 Quiet guidance - The meditation continues with extended silence and sparse prompts, allowing the mind and body to steady.
  5. 08:22 Forgiving phenomena - He invites a forgiving stance toward experience and the release of entanglement with thought, sensation, affect, and sound.
  6. 11:05 Deepening rest - After more silence, the emphasis stays on unhooking from experience and letting the practice deepen without efforting.
  7. 18:42 Effort and ease - In the closing reflection, he names the effort of attention and points toward awareness that is already present and requires no support.
