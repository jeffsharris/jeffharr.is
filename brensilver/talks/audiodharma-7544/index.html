Guided Meditation: Breath

AudioDharma 29:07 2017-02-24

This guided meditation begins by inviting a gentle, nonjudgmental relationship to experience and then settles into the breath as a simple place to collect attention. Matthew Brensilver repeatedly encourages curiosity, noting, and quiet returning, while also normalizing distractions, thoughts, and feeling as part of being human. In the later part of the practice, he widens the field again, softening effort and ending with whole-body awareness and letting go.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Arriving Kindly - The meditation opens with a gracious welcome and a nonjudgmental attitude toward whatever is present.
  2. 03:39 Simplifying Attention - He explains that awareness becomes clearer when the mind is stabilized and attention is simplified around the breath.
  3. 06:31 Breath as Object - The instruction turns toward curious contact with the breath and a spacious, full-coverage awareness of inhalation and exhalation.
  4. 10:38 Noting In and Out - He offers simple mental notes and emphasizes that the words are meant to deepen direct contact with sensation rather than add commentary.
  5. 16:18 Returning Gently - After a long stretch of silence, the talk addresses distraction, encourages bracketing other experience, and returns again to the breath with patience.
  6. 22:05 Winds of Experience - The teaching shifts to the changing winds of thought, feeling, and sensation, and how practice can hold steady as they move through.
  7. 27:15 Letting Go Wider - The close softens effort further, invites repeated letting go, and broadens from the breath to the whole body in awareness.
