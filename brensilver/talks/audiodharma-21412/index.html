Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Gentleness & Equanimity

AudioDharma 27:20 2024-10-02

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation on how gentleness and equanimity can work together: honest about what is here, and non-violent toward experience. The practice moves from settling into silence and breath, through softening the impulse to control, toward a deeper trust in allowing pleasant and unpleasant to come and go. Near the end, he points to a quiet contrast between clinging and love, and suggests that not trying to do anything with the mind may be the most gentle move of all.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Held by Silence - The meditation begins by settling into silence and breathing, with an invitation to feel held even amid noise.
  2. 01:14 Radical Honesty - He frames the practice as refusing pretence and meeting experience with clear, honest attention.
  3. 03:22 Not Fighting to Heal - The teaching turns toward trust: healing and waking up do not require force, but a patient softening over time.
  4. 07:03 Gentle Strength - Gentleness is presented as compatible with courage and strength, with awareness blessing its objects rather than grasping them.
  5. 10:03 Connecting, Not Controlling - He contrasts control with connection and invites staying present with ambivalence instead of pushing experience away.
  6. 14:41 Hands Soft, Clear - Using the image of washing a baby's head, he describes how to hold experience with tenderness, clarity, and surrender to pleasant and unpleasant states.
  7. 18:51 Knowing the Mind - The practice deepens into the insight that the mind is known best when we stop trying to manipulate it.
  8. 21:40 Clinging and Love - The close turns quiet and spare, distinguishing busy clinging from still love and ending with a simple blessing of shared sitting.
