Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Equanimity and Samadhi

Dharma Seed 51:30 2019-06-05

Matthew Brensilver explores how equanimity and samadhi grow from letting go rather than from effortful control. He moves from the Buddha’s language of clinging and non-clinging into a practical discussion of worry, self-referential thought, and the ways modern attention gets fragmented. The talk gradually turns toward steadiness, embodied concentration, and a softer relationship to experience, ending with a quiet emphasis on surrender, simplicity, and peace.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Resting In Silence - The talk opens by asking how we come to rest and find refuge, framing silence as something that beckons surrender.
  2. 04:11 Clinging And Safety - Matthew turns to the Buddha’s teaching on clinging, showing how the search for security often moves through grasping at conditions.
  3. 06:32 When Consciousness Lands - Using the Ati Raga Sutta’s image of light not landing anywhere, he describes non-clinging as a kind of peace.
  4. 09:45 Samadhi As Fruit - Samadhi is presented as the fruit of letting go, not something forced into existence by will or by judging the mind's activity.
  5. 13:44 Restlessness And Worry - He connects samadhi with the body’s settling and with the deeper undercurrent of worry, impermanence, and being constantly stimulated.
  6. 21:57 The Self At Rest - A citation from Jennifer Beer helps him describe the resting mind’s self-evaluating habit and how concentration loosens that pattern.
  7. 27:57 Thought Without Shame - Matthew emphasizes that thoughts are not enemies, while also questioning the faith we place in thinking to solve everything.
  8. 35:07 Equanimity And Pleasure - He broadens the discussion to equanimity and quotes the Buddha on samadhi as a lake fed from below, pleasurable but not to be fetishized.
  9. 40:56 Practice In Daily Life - The closing section warns against chasing past experiences, then offers practical guidance on patience, restraint, noting, and surrendering to stillness.
