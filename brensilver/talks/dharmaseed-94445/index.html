Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Wise Intention

Dharma Seed 1:36:52 2025-12-22

Matthew Brensilver explores wise intention as something more gradual and relational than a single decision. After a long guided sit, he turns to the gap between intention and behavior, the role of habit, and why sincere practice is less about self-improvement than about letting go of grasping. The talk ends with a wide-ranging dialogue on surrender, addiction, sincerity, and how renunciation, metta, and harmlessness shape daily life.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling the posture - Matthew opens with guided meditation, emphasizing a sustainable posture, gentle attention, and the balance of relaxation with alertness.
  2. 07:53 Attending with gentleness - He continues the sit by encouraging careful attention to breath and body, meeting worry and distraction without exclusion.
  3. 15:21 Breath as the anchor - The guidance narrows to conscious breathing, treating other phenomena as false alarms and settling into simplicity.
  4. 23:06 Sincerity over striving - He shifts into talk, arguing that spiritual maturity depends on sincerity rather than accumulation, drawing on Rodney Smith, Ajahn Sucitto, the Buddha, and Trungpa Rinpoche.
  5. 38:29 Habits and values - Matthew develops the idea that practice changes us through repeated small gestures, not one dramatic decision, with support from William James, Adler, and Agnes Callard.
  6. 52:36 Intention and behavior - Using behavioral science, he describes the gap between intention and action, the force of habit, and the limits of willpower under stress and depletion.
  7. 01:05:51 Three wise intentions - He presents the Buddha's three intentions—renunciation, metta, and harmlessness—and explains renunciation as freedom from compulsivity.
  8. 01:15:26 Questions on consequences - In dialogue, he responds to a question about good intentions and explains that wholesome intention still requires clear attention to actual impact and delusion.
  9. 01:21:29 Willingness and metta - The conversation returns to surrender, recovery, sincerity, and the move away from transactional practice, then closes with a brief metta practice and holiday encouragement.
