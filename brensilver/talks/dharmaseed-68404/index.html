Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation

Dharma Seed 52:16 2021-05-19

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation framed by brief reflections on why practice can be simple, restful, and honest about suffering. The talk moves from settling into the body and breath into a wider instruction on renunciation, boredom, and continuity of awareness in movement and daily life. He repeatedly returns to non-striving: just practice, just arrive, and let mindfulness build its own momentum.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Practice as Simplicity - Matthew opens by describing meditation as rest, insight, unification, letting go, and a simple willingness to show up rather than optimize the experience.
  2. 06:58 Settling In - He invites everyone to settle and arrive in the body before the more formal guidance begins.
  3. 08:25 Body and Breath - The guidance turns to posture, softening the face and belly, and using the breath as a steady ally for grounding and soothing.
  4. 17:21 Relaxation and Release - He explores how deeper breathing can carry relaxation through the body while noting the natural tension and urgency that can arise at the top of the inhale.
  5. 24:36 Returning Again - The talk names the mind's tendency to wander toward planning and control, then emphasizes that letting go is simple, not easy, and rooted in moment-by-moment attention.
  6. 35:50 Offering the Heart - Matthew points toward a more receptive attitude, encouraging practitioners to offer the heart to the moment instead of trying to extract something from it.
  7. 40:30 Rest and Intensity - He reflects on why real rest can reveal more of the human condition, including memory and emotion, and frames practice as acclimatizing to that intensity.
  8. 42:10 Walking and Continuity - The final section turns to movement and walking meditation, emphasizing awareness in the feet, openness of the eyes, working with boredom, and building continuity of mindfulness through daily practice.
