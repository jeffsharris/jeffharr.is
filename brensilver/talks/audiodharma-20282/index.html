Dharmette: Fear and Language

AudioDharma 14:33 2024-05-01

Matthew Brensilver explores the uneasy link between fear and language, suggesting that much of thinking is not just a cause of suffering but also a response to it. He moves from the value of words, to the mind’s urge to reduce uncertainty, to the way clinging, comparison, and selfing are organized through language. The talk closes by pointing toward a quieter trust: letting go, receiving phenomena as invitations to release, and placing faith in silence and awareness.

Chapters

  1. 00:09 Words and nourishment - He opens by reflecting on sadhu and the way words can be received as nourishment rather than opposed to silence.
  2. 01:23 Silence and language - He questions the habit of idealizing silence and dismissing language, arguing that careful speech can make the Dharma more palpable.
  3. 03:06 Fear beneath thought - He turns to the relation between fear and thought, suggesting that thinking is both a cause and a product of suffering.
  4. 04:50 Anicca and uncertainty - Using anicca as a frame, he describes the mind’s hunger to reduce uncertainty by sorting experience into categories and explanations.
  5. 07:14 Clinging and self - He develops the idea that language helps orchestrate clinging, create problems, and keep a subject at the center of experience.
  6. 09:37 No problems - He sketches awakening as the absence of problems in the moment, where language and selfing are no longer needed to manage experience.
  7. 11:31 Trusting release - He nudges the listener toward letting go, treating phenomena as false alarms and placing faith in silence and the momentum of practice.
  8. 13:36 Closing blessings - He ends with a brief reminder that teachings can be used or misused and offers a quiet wish for benefit.
