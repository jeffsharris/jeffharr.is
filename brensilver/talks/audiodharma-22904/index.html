Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Non-referentiality

AudioDharma 29:57 2025-05-07

Matthew Brensilver begins with a light, self-aware basketball anecdote and then turns quickly into a guided meditation on settling, relaxing, and sensing the body as light and gravity. The main teaching explores how the mind locates itself through reference points—body, preference, craving, aversion—and invites a period of practice “without referentiality.” The closing section softens into endings, dissolution, and a Rumi line about living in the nowhere you come from, returning to spaciousness without fixed self-reference.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and arrival - Matthew opens with a brief basketball story and then invites everyone to settle in for the meditation.
  2. 02:20 Brightness and body - The practice begins by relaxing everything except brightness, filling the body with light, and letting the center of gravity drop into the belly.
  3. 05:33 Held by earth - He emphasizes gravity, the earth holding us fast, and fuller breaths through the belly.
  4. 07:09 Softening more - The instruction becomes simple and repetitive, encouraging a gradual softening into the sitting.
  5. 08:39 Reference points - Matthew uses the image of a phone pinging towers to explain how we locate ourselves through body, preferences, plans, craving, and aversion.
  6. 10:59 Without referentiality - The meditation shifts into a long refrain asking what it means to be here without reference points and to lay down sight, sound, sensation, and thought.
  7. 21:41 Endings and dissolution - Near the end, he invites tolerance for endings, things dissolving, and not needing to pick anything else up.
  8. 22:52 Nowhere you come from - A Rumi quotation deepens the final spaciousness, and the closing returns again and again to non-identification, including the meditator and the act of teaching itself as just another ping.
