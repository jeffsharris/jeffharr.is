Guided Meditation: Radical Permission to Arrive

AudioDharma 57:03 2020-10-14

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet guided meditation on "radical permission": beginning by settling attention in the room and in the body, then widening into the idea that nothing in experience needs to be excluded. The instruction moves from breath and posture into a spacious acceptance of thought, feeling, and sensation, before returning to practical guidance for walking meditation and continuity of mindfulness through the day. The tone is gentle and steady, with repeated emphasis on allowing what is here and trusting that practice can be both simple and complete.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Arrive in Space - The talk begins by orienting to the screen, the room, and the body as part of one field of experience.
  2. 02:26 Nothing Excluded - He settles into the central teaching that every part of experience has a place, including breath, body, sound, and feeling tone.
  3. 05:21 Permission to Be Here - The practice opens into a fuller acceptance of who we are, with the reminder that the Dharma does not ask us to leave parts of ourselves out.
  4. 11:50 Radical Self-Acceptance - He describes meditation as blessing life moment by moment, then guides attention to breath and body with a deep yes to whatever appears outside the focus.
  5. 21:22 Breath Trains the Mind - The meditation deepens into resting with the breath as teacher, meeting craving and aversion without resistance and trusting that what seems unbearable can be borne.
  6. 40:21 Gently with the First Day - He speaks to retreat energy, discouragement, and the unevenness of the beginning of practice, encouraging patience and gentleness.
  7. 47:24 Walking as Continuity - The second speaker turns to walking meditation as a support for sitting, continuity, and a steady heat of mindfulness across the whole day.
