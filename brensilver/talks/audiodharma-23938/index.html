Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: 'Protect Empathy at All Costs and Live Groovy Lives'

AudioDharma 14:39 2025-10-01

Matthew Brensilver opens with a reflection on Jim Jarmusch and a remark about art changing the world by opening people to one another. From there he develops a quiet argument that Dharma, sangha, and practice can be understood as forms of cultivating beauty and protecting empathy, both inwardly and socially. A personal story about Joe Strummer becomes the talk’s central refrain: protect empathy at all costs and live groovy lives.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Art Opens the Heart - Brensilver begins with Jim Jarmusch and an idea that art can be political by making people more connected and empathetic.
  2. 01:46 Strummer's Charge - He tells the story of Joe Strummer's advice to protect empathy at all costs and live groovy lives, and explains how that sentence has shaped him.
  3. 03:08 Dharma as Art - The talk turns toward the claim that Dharma has an aesthetic dimension and can be understood as a way of turning the heart toward beauty.
  4. 04:17 Beauty and Sangha - He reflects on how beauty can appear in teachers, students, and sangha, describing sangha as collective sensitization to goodness.
  5. 05:40 Abiding in What We Love - Brensilver describes the wish not to be loved as a teacher, but to abide in what is loved together, linking that to spiritual friendship and self-directed empathy.
  6. 07:31 Expanding the Circle - He traces empathy from self-care outward across space and time, contrasting egocentric life with a more open and connected way of being.
  7. 09:40 Seeing Others Clearly - The talk examines how easily we box others into roles, then offers a working definition of empathy that includes perspective-taking, feeling with another, and caring about their welfare.
  8. 11:44 A Lineage of Care - Brensilver returns to the Buddha, to a detailed map of goodness and neurosis, and to the need for an unbroken lineage of love and care in public life.
