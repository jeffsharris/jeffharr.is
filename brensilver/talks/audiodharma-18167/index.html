Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Awareness Without Willfulness

AudioDharma 31:16 2023-04-27

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation that keeps returning to the difference between effort and willfulness. After a brief, candid opening about being emotionally caught up in a basketball game, he settles into posture, body, and breath, then invites a steady awareness of the residues of the day without trying to fix them. The practice gradually shifts toward relaxing the impulse to do something to experience, resting instead in an awareness that feels less personal and less effortful.

Chapters

  1. 00:35 Opening candor - He begins with a light, honest story about being caught up in a Warriors game before turning toward the meditation.
  2. 02:29 Settle the body - The instruction moves into finding a sustainable posture and arriving in the body with steadiness.
  3. 04:40 Taking stock - He asks listeners to notice the residues of the day and awaken to what is already present.
  4. 07:03 Allowing residue - The invitation is to relax and be willing to feel whatever remains without pushing it away.
  5. 07:59 Breath and balance - Breath is used to smooth, unify, cool, and enliven the body-mind as a way of gathering the field.
  6. 11:41 Effort and grace - He explores the role of effort in practice and warns against letting willfulness obscure the grace of the moment.
  7. 17:19 Relaxing the doer - The emphasis deepens into sensing the abrasiveness of always doing something to experience and relaxing that habit.
  8. 22:51 Awareness without self - Attention is contrasted with a more open awareness that does not taste like 'me,' ending with a place to rest beyond dependence on effort.
  9. 30:03 Closing bells - The meditation closes quietly with the sound of bells and silence.
