Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Establishing Tranquility

AudioDharma 29:07 2024-03-11

This guided meditation settles into posture, then turns attention toward silence as a quieter guide than the mind's usual instructions. Matthew Brensilver repeatedly narrows the attentional field to the breath, inviting the listener to relax what can be relaxed, allow what remains, and feel how even a small measure of tranquility changes the way experience appears. Near the end, he connects that shift to a Buddhist teaching that mind conditions what is known, closing with a gentle emphasis on refuge, nourishment, and a softer inner gaze.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Settling Into Silence - The meditation begins by settling into posture and listening for what silence can reveal instead of immediately telling the mind what to do.
  2. 02:01 Relaxing the Body - He invites a simple release of tension, while allowing whatever cannot relax yet to remain without struggle.
  3. 03:00 Tranquility Changes View - The talk opens out into the idea that even a little tranquility changes perspective, without making life's difficulties disappear.
  4. 06:22 Narrowing Attention - Attention is described as a spotlight: rather than rejecting experience, the practice makes room for a smaller field centered on the breath.
  5. 11:22 Faith in Silence - Matthew names the fatigue of constant problem-solving and invites a kind of non-worry, softening into a smaller aperture of attention.
  6. 20:52 Stabilizing the Inner Gaze - With eyes closed, the listener is guided to steady a gaze that often scans for threat and to feel nourished by breathing.
  7. 26:04 Tranquility as Refuge - He links hyperarousal and suffering to the need for refuge, then closes by citing the Buddha on how mind shapes experience.
  8. 28:22 Closing Bells - The meditation ends with bells, leaving time for the quieter state to be felt.
