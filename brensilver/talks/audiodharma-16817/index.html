Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Meditation as an Atelic Activity

AudioDharma 30:00 2022-08-17

Matthew Brensilver begins with a philosopher’s critique of goal-directed living and connects it to the Buddha’s teaching on dukkha: the strain of always completing one thing in order to chase the next. He then turns that frame toward meditation, emphasizing that practice is not only a tool for improvement but also a chance to step out of striving and rest in present-moment experience. The guided portion keeps returning to the same point: the breath, the body, and even thinking itself are reminders to loosen achievement and let this moment be enough.

Chapters

  1. 00:55 Goals and Their Limits - Brensilver introduces Kieran Setiya’s argument that goal-centered life can become self-defeating because completed goals stop guiding us.
  2. 02:22 Dukkha and Fatigue - He connects that idea to the Buddha’s teaching on dukkha, naming the tiredness of continually replacing one completed goal with another.
  3. 03:02 Meditation Beyond Goals - The talk shifts to meditation as both goal-directed and not goal-directed, with atelic practice defined as simply being present with nowhere to go.
  4. 04:21 Arriving in the Body - Listeners are invited to settle posture, arrive through the breath, and gently put down the impulse to achieve anything.
  5. 05:08 Settling and Surrendering - In the extended guided period, he repeatedly releases progress, concentration, insight, and even the wish to get somewhere else.
  6. 11:39 When Striving Returns - He names how deeply habit can turn meditation into a self-improvement project, and invites surrender of all ideas of progress or regress.
  7. 13:27 Breath as Reminder - The anchor of breath, body, or sound is presented not as something to master but as a cue to relax striving and stay here.
  8. 17:27 Thoughts Beside the Point - He notes that thinking can be useful for planning and solving problems, but in practice it can quietly reintroduce goal-making and should be gently set aside.
  9. 24:29 Rest in Imperfection - The closing settles on the refuge of this moment’s imperfection, where striving and self-evaluation can finally rest.
