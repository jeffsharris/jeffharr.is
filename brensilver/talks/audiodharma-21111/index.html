Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Introduction to Theme - Abiding in Care

AudioDharma 57:32 2024-08-17

Matthew Brensilver frames this talk around a simple but demanding question: what kind of happiness are we actually living for? He moves from the tension between feeling good and feeling bad into a spacious account of love, showing how the Brahma-viharas, renunciation, and even painful experience can all become part of abiding in care. The talk ends in a quieter register, with vipassana described as radical non-violence toward experience and mindfulness as remembering what matters.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening the day - Matthew introduces the day of practice and frames it as moving through both pleasant and difficult experience.
  2. 04:39 Secret philosophy - He suggests that beneath our stated practice we often live by a hidden philosophy of happiness, and he ties Buddhist practice to careful attention to experience and the body.
  3. 10:34 Anger and its limits - Using a fever analogy, he makes space for anger as sometimes adaptive while insisting that hatred is never the deepest answer.
  4. 14:23 The Brahma-viharas - He expands loving-kindness, compassion, sympathetic joy, and equanimity into a practical map of love that can rest in many conditions.
  5. 24:33 Understanding as love - Matthew turns to attunement, reverence for present-moment life, and the Buddha's teaching that peace is the highest happiness.
  6. 30:30 Many paths to care - He summarizes both direct cultivation and the slower path through patience, renunciation, surrender, and purification.
  7. 37:27 Non-violence toward experience - After the sit, he defines vipassana as radical non-violence toward all experience and invites listeners to bow to their lives and see what kind of love arises.
  8. 46:28 Closing reminders - The talk settles into closing guidance on continuity, remembering the Dharma, and staying close to what needs to be remembered.
