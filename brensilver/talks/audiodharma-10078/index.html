Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: The Ground of Loving Acceptance

AudioDharma 45:09 2019-05-17

Matthew Brensilver leads a guided meditation on how the sense of self keeps seeking ground through activity, and how that search can soften when met with loving acceptance. The practice moves from noticing strain and self-fixing, to resting in breath and body, to working directly with feeling tone and the emotional body as the place where clinging gathers. Near the end, he turns toward ease, non-striving, and a quieter relationship with whatever is present, closing with a Mary Oliver line about letting the body love what it loves.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Selfing and strain - The talk opens by naming the restlessness of self-construction and the stress of always trying to work things out.
  2. 02:11 Loving acceptance begins - He introduces intentionality and a simple sense of open, grounded, caring attention as the first taste of acceptance.
  3. 03:05 Finding the ground - The meditation rests in the recognition that the ground is not in strategies or identity, but in receiving oneself and one’s strategies with kindness.
  4. 06:28 No pressure to be someone - In silence and reflection, he points to the relief of not needing to prove, curate, or become anyone.
  5. 14:14 Feeling tone and clinging - He explains that emotional sensations and vedana are where clinging starts, and that waking to them can short-circuit suffering.
  6. 18:39 Breathing into feeling - The practice turns toward breath, body, sound, and metta, then directly touches the urge to fix, own, improve, or protect.
  7. 25:17 Softening the body - He broadens the view to the body’s front axis and the emotional circuits that carry longing, then uses a Mary Oliver quotation to frame non-striving.
  8. 34:55 Ease and closing - The final section emphasizes relaxation, minimal effort, and a simple instruction to stop anything that feels unsustainable before ending with a quiet reminder to stay close to oneself.
