Guided Meditation: Urgency of Feeling

Dharma Seed 32:37 2025-09-18

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation centered on the "urgency" that can arise from feeling. The practice begins with settling the body and breathing, then turns toward noticing hidden agendas, control strategies, and the way emotional surges can seem like commands. As the meditation deepens, he repeatedly returns to relaxing, absorbing feeling, and letting breathing become safe again.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling the body - The meditation opens by softening the body through fuller breaths and a soothing exhale.
  2. 02:52 Relaxation cues - Attention moves through the face, jaw, shoulders, belly, and a sense of brightness in the spine as the body yields to earth and sky.
  3. 06:05 Hidden agendas - The teaching invites noticing the semi-conscious agendas and efforts that keep trying to fix pain, peace, or the future.
  4. 10:25 Feeling as urgency - Brensilver describes how bodily feeling creates a sense that something must happen, and how to let feeling remain feeling.
  5. 14:23 False alarms - He frames sensations and phenomena as false alarms and emphasizes the difference between control and letting go.
  6. 19:50 Breath and equanimity - The practice returns to the breath, with repeated reminders to feel breathing in a relaxed way and to stay with each beginning, middle, and end.
  7. 28:39 Relaxing the threat - The closing section focuses on tension as an innocent control impulse and on relaxing the thorny quality of feeling until breathing feels safe again.
