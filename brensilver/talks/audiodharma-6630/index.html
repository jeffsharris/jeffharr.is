Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation (Day 6): Poems, Reflections, Silence

AudioDharma 39:03 2016-04-09

This guided meditation moves from poems about sorrow and emptiness into a quiet practice of carrying what is heavy without closing the heart. Matthew Brensilver returns several times to refrains about surrender, softness, and the redemption of pain through love and understanding. The later part settles into long stretches of silence and ends with Rumi’s urging toward wholehearted devotion.

Chapters

  1. 02:00 Sorrow and Emptiness - Leonard Cohen’s lines about burden and emptiness introduce the talk’s central tension: emptiness may lighten suffering, but it does not erase heaviness.
  2. 05:02 Open the Heart - Matthew reflects on how to meet what is heavy, emphasizing that the task is not to deny pain but to open to it.
  3. 08:44 Lord of Song Refrain - A repeated devotional line about standing before the Lord of Song with nothing on the heart becomes a practice of simplicity and release.
  4. 18:18 Softening the Heart - He names the way life can harden or soften the heart and lets the meditation deepen into extended silence.
  5. 25:24 Pain Redeemed - The talk turns explicit about practice: pain is to be folded into love and understanding rather than resisted or left outside awareness.
  6. 34:29 Rumi's Invitation - Rumi’s lines about gambling everything for love intensify the teaching into wholehearted commitment and letting go of hesitation.
  7. 35:44 Closing Silence - The meditation settles into long, spacious silence, allowing the earlier words to resonate without further instruction.
