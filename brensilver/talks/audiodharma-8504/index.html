Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Bhavatanha Mortality and Letting Go

AudioDharma 39:52 2017-12-17

Matthew Brensilver explores bhava tanha, the craving for existence, through mortality, selfhood, and the ways the mind keeps arranging a future. He moves from a film about the dead choosing one life moment, to Buddhist and psychological perspectives on death anxiety, identity, and clinging. The talk stays practical: what changes when we sit as if there were no future, and when the self is seen less as a solid fact than as something conditioned by fear and grasping.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening Framing - Matthew sets up the talk by speaking plainly about relevance, aliveness, and why the original plan was set aside.
  2. 01:48 The Film Premise - He uses Kore-eda’s After Life as a frame for asking how we choose, arrange, and cling to the conditions of a life.
  3. 04:15 Mortality and Tension - He turns to the rabbi’s two pockets image and to fragility, dukkha, anicca, and anatta as ways of naming the tension between longing and reality.
  4. 09:30 Existence Bias - Drawing on Thomas Metzinger and the Buddha’s language of bhava tanha, he describes the mind’s strong bias toward staying alive and securing the self.
  5. 15:50 Death Anxiety Shapes Culture - He brings in death anxiety, terror management theory, and the social effects of mortality salience, including prejudice and worldview defense.
  6. 20:29 Sitting Like the Dying - An early instruction to sit like a dying person leads into an inquiry about what is still worth clinging to when the future drops away.
  7. 26:15 Bhava Tanha in Practice - He follows Ajahn Sumedho’s emphasis on noticing becoming in meditation and shows how practice can quietly become a self-improvement project.
  8. 33:46 Self as Conditioned - A quoted reflection on no fixed self and the image of the glass tunnel points toward a less imprisoned, more open relation to others.
  9. 36:24 Cessation of Becoming - He closes with nibbana as the cessation of becoming and invites a final sitting with the question of what this moment is when the future is released.
