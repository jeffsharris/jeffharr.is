Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Refuge

AudioDharma 28:16 2026-03-11

Matthew Brensilver leads a quiet guided meditation on refuge, beginning with repeated invitations to settle in Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha, and to let love, gratitude, or reverence soften the heart. The practice gradually widens from the immediate body and breath to a larger sense of being held by the stream of Dharma across time, before ending with a brief reflective closing that points toward the split between subject and object. The tone stays simple and intimate, with long stretches of repetition and spaciousness rather than a lot of discursive explanation.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Entering Refuge - The meditation begins with repeated invitations to take refuge and set aside concern about anything outside that frame.
  2. 01:56 Love and Reverence - Matthew asks listeners to call in love, gratitude, or reverence as a way to signal the deep mind to relax and let go.
  3. 03:46 Settling the Body - Attention is guided to a resting place in the body, breath, and the felt edge between body and space, with an emphasis on simplicity.
  4. 06:10 Return to Refuge - The refrain of refuge continues, holding the practice in a steady, repetitive rhythm.
  5. 09:57 Dharma Across Time - The talk opens into a broader perspective, suggesting that Dharma has held countless moments and that a life needs a vantage point larger than the life itself.
  6. 15:05 Less Identified - Matthew describes how loosening identification with one's circle of concern can make the mind more spacious and less pinched by time.
  7. 22:22 Closing Insight - The closing turns briefly to a Krishnamurti quote about the split between subject and object, then ends in bell sounds and silence.
