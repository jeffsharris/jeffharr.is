Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Disenchantment & Engagement

AudioDharma 15:03 2023-12-13

Matthew Brensilver explores how Buddhist practice can hold two movements at once: disenchantment with samsara and a real care for the world. He begins with the vulnerability of human life, turns to the Bahhiya Sutta and the idea of samvega, and then asks how renunciation and engagement fit together in a modern lay path. The talk closes by arguing that letting go of grasping can make room for compassion, not indifference.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Opening the Theme - Matthew introduces the talk’s central tension between disenchantment and abiding care for the world.
  2. 01:10 Human Vulnerability - He frames the Buddhist path around aging, sickness, and death as the defenseless side of life.
  3. 02:22 Sensory Experience - He explains the instruction to stay closely with sensory experience and how that can lead beyond ordinary identification.
  4. 02:53 Bahhiya Sutta - The Bahhiya teaching is used to show how seeing and knowing can remain simple, ungrasping, and free.
  5. 04:03 Lay Path Questions - Matthew asks how disenchantment, renunciation, and love of the human condition might fit together in a contemporary practice life.
  6. 06:52 Samvega and Urgency - He turns to Thanissaro Bhikkhu’s account of samvega as shock, dismay, and the urgency to find a way out of the cycle.
  7. 09:01 What Is Enough - Using Ajahn Sucitto, he reflects on endless wanting and the hard discipline of recognizing sufficiency.
  8. 10:29 Disenchantment Into Care - He broadens the view to modern conditions, suggesting that letting go of grasping can create space for practical compassion.
  9. 13:28 Ryokan’s Wish - The talk ends with Ryokan’s image of a robe too small to hold all suffering, paired with the wish to care anyway.
