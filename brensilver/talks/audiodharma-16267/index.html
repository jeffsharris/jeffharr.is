Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Lovingkindness Practice

AudioDharma 30:09 2022-05-10

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet guided lovingkindness practice that begins by clarifying what metta is and is not: not niceness, and not pretending. He then turns attention to the body as a place of real goodness and generosity, before moving into the core phrases of metta and the felt sense of letting go of self-patrolling, self-hatred, and vigilance. The practice ends by emphasizing that this kindness is not earned but simply offered.

Chapters

  1. 01:08 Metta, Not Niceness - Matthew distinguishes lovingkindness from social niceness and invites a practice grounded in authenticity rather than pretense.
  2. 03:38 Kindness Toward the Body - He turns attention to the body’s steady work and speaks of the body as a kind of gift, encouraging a softer, more appreciative view.
  3. 06:40 Noticing Quiet Goodness - The instruction settles into sensing ordinary goodness in the body and heart without inflation or self-importance.
  4. 16:45 The Bell of Phrases - He explains that repeating 'may I be happy' is not about a future result, but about touching the heart in the present moment.
  5. 20:25 May I Be Happy - The metta phrases begin, paired with instructions to relax vigilance and allow oneself to be moved by one’s own goodness.
  6. 22:20 May I Be Safe - The practice continues through safety and ease, with repeated reminders to drop self-surveillance and self-hatred.
  7. 27:42 Unconditional Offering - Matthew closes by emphasizing that metta is not given because of achievement or deserving, but simply offered.
