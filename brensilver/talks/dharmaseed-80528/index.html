Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Knowing and Not-Knowing

Dharma Seed 50:28 2023-10-11

Matthew Brensilver reflects on teaching from the inside of uncertainty, using a retreat-planning story and a cliffside teaching image to make not-knowing the entry point for practice. From there he unfolds a quiet argument that Dharma asks for both confidence and humility, for knowing and for relinquishing the need to know. The talk moves through ignorance, self-study, love, and the role of surrender in insight and samadhi.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Cliffside Opening - Matthew opens with a teaching story that frames the difficulty and absurdity of explaining Dharma from inside practice.
  2. 01:53 Not Knowing as Theme - He turns a retreat-planning conversation into a case for teaching from uncertainty rather than from a fixed method.
  3. 06:33 How Practice Works - Using a medicine analogy, he asks what the mechanism of meditation is and suggests insight is only part of a larger training.
  4. 08:17 Balancing the Teachings - He warns against reducing Dharma to one slogan and names paired qualities like effort and surrender, technique and ease.
  5. 12:53 Intimate Not-Knowing - A Zen story leads into his central theme: confidence and humility are companions, not opposites.
  6. 16:26 Ignorance and Self-Knowledge - He draws on the Samyutta Nikaya and dependent co-arising to show how ignorance, fear, and conditioning shape suffering.
  7. 23:42 Study Yourself - Larry Rosenberg and Thich Nhat Hanh help him connect vipassana, understanding, and love as forms of seeing clearly.
  8. 29:24 Everything Teaches - Ajahn Chah's line becomes a way to treat life as feedback, while prediction and security are shown to narrow the mind.
  9. 35:43 Relinquishment and Surrender - He brings in the Dhammapada and a Buddha teaching on conceiving to show how knowing is softened by letting go, leading toward samadhi and a less defended way of being.
