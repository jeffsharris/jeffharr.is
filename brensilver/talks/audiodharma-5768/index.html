Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Sincerity

AudioDharma 02:44 2015-06-24

Matthew Brensilver reflects briefly on sincerity as the living basis of practice, not as earnest striving but as a willingness to be surprised by our own experience. He connects that openness with insight: learning to look again, experiment, and meet practice with fresh curiosity. The talk closes by linking surprise, continuity of mindfulness, and the unfolding of insight in ordinary practice.

Chapters

  1. 00:20 Sincerity in practice - He begins by naming sincerity as the lifeblood of the path and asking what it really means.
  2. 00:50 Willingness to be surprised - Sincerity is framed as being open to being surprised by ourselves rather than fixed in prior expectations.
  3. 01:18 Learning to investigate - He points to experimentation and a fresh way of looking as part of sincere practice.
  4. 01:49 Insight starts surprising - Insight is described as something startling and newly discovered, not merely accumulated.
  5. 02:08 Continuity and curiosity - Mindfulness and a willingness to be surprised are named as ways to nourish insight.
  6. 02:26 Seeing in a new way - The talk ends with a quiet emphasis on curiosity and a blessing for the morning of practice.
