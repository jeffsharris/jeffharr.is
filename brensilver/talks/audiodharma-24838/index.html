Guided Meditation: The Safety of Letting Go

AudioDharma 27:36 2026-02-11

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation that moves from settling the body into a deeper kind of listening, where thought is seen less as a problem than as a protective habit. He gradually reframes thinking, emotion, and even control itself as things that can be met with permission, forgiveness, and mindfulness. The talk ends by opening into the safety that can come from relinquishing control and resting with experience as it is.

Chapters

  1. 00:09 Settling In - Begin by finding posture and allowing the breath to enliven numbness and soothe hyper-activation.
  2. 01:29 Unifying the Body - The practice turns toward gentle unification, letting the whole body be touched by the Dharma without demanding that everything feel pleasant.
  3. 04:23 Deep Listening - Attention shifts to hearing thought itself, including moments of listening outward toward silence and inward toward mental talk.
  4. 08:45 Thinking as Protection - Thought is presented as a safety behavior—an innocent way the mind checks threat and opportunity—and the practice becomes forgiving the mind and returning to listening.
  5. 12:33 Permission for Feeling - The meditation opens to bodily affect and emotional alarm, allowing sensations to arise without needing to manage or change them.
  6. 15:20 This Moment - A prolonged refrain of 'this moment' settles the listener into presence, emphasizing immediacy, imperfection, and poignancy.
  7. 20:38 Relinquishing Control - The talk widens into the recognition that life eventually outruns our control, and practice becomes willingly opening to reality and the safety of letting go.
