Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Concentration, Clarity, Equanimity

AudioDharma 27:31 2024-09-13

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation framed by gratitude for practice, then builds the session around three qualities of attention: concentration, clarity, and equanimity. He uses simple images and practical instructions to show how attention can stabilize, sharpen, and become less reactive to pleasure, pain, and change. The meditation gradually turns toward a more open stance: meeting each moment without landing on conclusions, reference points, or grasping.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Opening Gratitude - The meditation begins with gratitude for having a path and letting that gratitude soften the heart.
  2. 03:37 What Attention Needs - Matthew shifts from the object of attention to the qualities of attention, introducing concentration as the first part of the practice.
  3. 05:25 Higher-Resolution Clarity - He describes clarity as perceiving experience with greater resolution, both in time and in the detail of the object.
  4. 06:54 Equanimity and Frictionlessness - The talk turns to equanimity as becoming less reactive to changing conditions, while preferences can remain.
  5. 13:17 Practice Moment by Moment - He returns to direct instruction on choosing an anchor and cultivating stability, clarity, and equanimity despite distraction.
  6. 17:11 Rehearsing Freedom - The instruction becomes more encouraging, presenting each moment as a chance to rehearse freedom and trust the path.
  7. 21:36 Open-Ended Release - The closing points toward not landing the mind anywhere, then ends with a Rumi line that supports non-clinging and spaciousness.
