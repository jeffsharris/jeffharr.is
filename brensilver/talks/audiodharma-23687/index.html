Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Abiding in Care

AudioDharma 30:51 2025-08-22

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation centered on care as a quiet, stabilizing quality of mind and body. The practice moves from settling posture and softening tension into a broader sense of kindness, non-contention, and heartfulness that does not need to perform or fix anything. He then connects love with breath, grief, and the full range of feeling, suggesting that compassion can make pain more bearable without reducing its poignancy.

Chapters

  1. 00:27 Settling In - The meditation begins with an invitation to notice what the word meditation brings up and to find a stable posture.
  2. 01:43 Softening Tension - He guides repeated softening of the body, agitation, clinging, and rigidity, using kindness as the means.
  3. 03:44 The Heart's Realm - The teaching turns toward the heart as a sacred realm where kindness softens self-view without needing to argue with it.
  4. 06:17 No Need to Perform - Brensilver emphasizes that love and kindness do not need to be used as a strategy against fear, but can become a quiet ambience of non-contention.
  5. 08:47 Finding a Support - Listeners are invited to choose an image, phrase, or bodily sense that resonates with care and lets the body ease into that tone.
  6. 11:57 Love and Spaciousness - The practice expands into the body and awareness, describing love as something that can make awareness feel less held and more open.
  7. 16:51 Four Forms of Love - He names love in relation to goodness, suffering, good fortune, and helplessness, then brings the practice back to the breath.
  8. 19:37 Breath and Presence - The guided meditation continues with breath, silence, and repeated reminders to stay with the felt sense of care.
  9. 28:01 Grief and Poignancy - The talk closes by relating grief to love, saying that care can soothe agitation while deepening the poignancy of being alive.
