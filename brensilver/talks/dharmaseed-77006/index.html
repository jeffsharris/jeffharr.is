Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Trajectory of your life and trajectory of freedom

Dharma Seed 40:37 2023-03-30

Matthew Brensilver frames the meditation around two trajectories: the anxious, habit-driven story of a life, and the quieter training that leads toward freedom. The talk moves from this reflection into guided practice, emphasizing relaxation, sensing the whole body, and meeting feeling states without treating them as evidence that something is wrong. The practice repeatedly returns to softness, awareness, and the possibility that you do not have to become anyone in order to be present.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Two Trajectories - Brensilver introduces the contrast between the trajectory of life and the trajectory of freedom, and how they can differ.
  2. 06:09 Letting Go - He describes freedom as a training in interchangeable experience, where every moment invites release rather than control.
  3. 13:25 Settling Into Practice - The guided meditation begins with posture, body awareness, and relaxation in mind and body.
  4. 18:11 Undefended Presence - He points to mindfulness as being undefended against the moment and trusting the logic of Dharma over compulsive accounting.
  5. 23:50 False Alarms - The practice turns toward distraction, checking, and the body’s alarm signals, treating them as feeling rather than a problem to solve.
  6. 29:36 Softening the Heart - The meditation closes by letting feeling soften the heart and resting in the sense that no one needs to be performed here.
