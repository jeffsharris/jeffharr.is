Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Entering the Dharma

AudioDharma 30:24 2023-02-14

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet guided meditation centered on “entering the Dharma” as a bodily, felt act rather than a project of self-improvement. The practice moves through settling the body, recollecting Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha, and opening to a softer stance of receptivity and surrender. Thich Nhat Hanh’s image of the nectar settling in a glass becomes a recurring touchstone for letting experience clarify on its own.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling In - After a brief welcome, the talk turns toward the idea of entering the Dharma as a simple beginning rather than a task to accomplish.
  2. 01:40 Entering the Dharma - He frames practice as a ritual reminder that is tactile and lived, and invites a posture that is not burdened by fixing or busyness.
  3. 04:06 Nectar Settles - Using Thich Nhat Hanh’s image of nectar settling in juice, he points to allowing the mind to clear by not overworking it.
  4. 07:13 Recollecting Refuge - The meditation moves into recollection of Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha, presented as a bodily sense of lineage and blessing that can hold us.
  5. 09:38 Breath and Blessing - Breath becomes a reminder of devotion, change, and the preciousness of being born, letting the recollection suffuse body and mind.
  6. 11:04 Receptive Attention - After a long quiet stretch, the guidance returns to awareness and holding experience, emphasizing a gentle, receptive way of attending.
  7. 16:30 Surrender and Acceptance - The closing teaching leans into surrender to imperfection and acceptance, suggesting that safety can be found within receptivity itself.
  8. 23:36 Trusting Openness - In the final section, he reflects on the mind’s habit of shoring up life and points toward faith in openness and receptivity.
