Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Being Held, Settling In

AudioDharma 50:10 2025-03-17

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation that begins with the felt sense of being held by the world, using rain, earth, and bodily support as doorways into settling. The practice moves through breath awareness, working with urgency and false alarms, and then broadens into noticing suffering more clearly without being overwhelmed by it. In the second half, he introduces walking meditation, alternating anchors like seeing, hearing, feet, whole body, and loving-kindness, and closes by emphasizing freshness, renunciation, and trust in the Dharma.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Feeling Held - He begins with rain and the sense of being held by the world, inviting a bodily experience of support.
  2. 02:01 Settling In - The instruction turns toward making peace with imperfection and resting in what is subtly pleasant.
  3. 08:17 Letting Go Of Time - He asks listeners to release orientation to retreat days and fall more fully into the present.
  4. 10:41 Breath Cycle - Attention is gathered on the breath, including the beginning and end of inhalation and exhalation and the brief release at the bottom.
  5. 15:08 False Alarms - He names the flood of signals in body and mind and suggests treating phenomena as false alarms rather than commands.
  6. 22:16 Working With Urgency - A whirlpool of urgency is met with recognition, blessing, and breathing until the pull loosens and breathing is available again.
  7. 31:03 Suffering As Dukkha - If suffering arises, he reframes the moment as an occasion to realize dukkha more clearly.
  8. 39:51 Walking Meditation - The talk shifts to walking practice, explaining posture, open eyes, visual stimulation, boredom, and the quiet accrual of goodness.
  9. 47:41 Fresh Anchors - He closes by encouraging variation in the walking anchor, creative freshness, and a final trust in the logic of Dharma.
