Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Solid, Liquid, Gas

AudioDharma 45:53 2020-10-16

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation on meeting experience without agenda, beginning with a gentle invitation to welcome what is here and to put down the burden of self-improvement. The practice then moves into an embodied reflection on impermanence, using the language of solid, liquid, and gas to show how suffering, craving, breath, body, and self-view continually change. In the final movement, the talk opens toward compassion, suggesting a tender way of meeting what feels heavy rather than trying to force it open.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Arriving Without Agenda - The meditation opens by inviting a simple, welcoming attention and setting aside the urge to improve or extract something from the moment.
  2. 02:23 Putting Down the Story - Matthew points to the burden of updating autobiography moment by moment and softens the insistence on reference to past and future.
  3. 05:30 Allowing What Remains - He acknowledges that letting go may not happen all at once and encourages a patient, non-forceful relationship to the practice.
  4. 11:13 Solid, Liquid, Gas - The talk turns to impermanence, describing how meditation reveals experience as shifting between congealed and flowing states.
  5. 16:25 Anicca in the Body - Through repeated chanting and close attention to breath, body, and suffering, the teaching of anicca becomes an embodied way of seeing change.
  6. 27:12 Nothing to Hold - The reflection deepens into the image of the river, with no stable place to stand and no thing to snag upon.
  7. 36:57 Compassion for the Heavy - Matthew shifts from insight to compassion, recalling a childhood injury and his mother's simple, tender response.
  8. 38:51 Closing Bells - The meditation ends quietly with bells and a brief closing exchange, leaving the listener with the practice's tone rather than a conclusion to analyze.
