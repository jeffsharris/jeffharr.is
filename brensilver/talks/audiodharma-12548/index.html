Wednesday Evening Reflection: Rohini's Enlightenment Poem

AudioDharma 08:48 2020-12-09

Matthew Brensilver reflects on a line from Rohini’s enlightenment poem: the path is not a line on a map, but a great shining world, enter wherever you like. He uses that image to gently undo the pressure to fit Dharma practice into a fixed shape, suggesting instead that we discover our own belonging within it. The talk closes with an encouragement to affirm one’s efforts, rest well, and continue noticing how apparent walls may become doorways.

Chapters

  1. 03:26 A shining world - Matthew introduces the poem’s image of the path as a great shining world rather than a fixed route.
  2. 03:54 Not fitting in - He examines the tendency to feel we must mold ourselves to match Dharma or Dharma culture.
  3. 04:36 Enter anywhere - The teaching turns toward the freedom to begin practice from whatever conditions we actually have.
  4. 05:19 Belonging and home - He links belonging with a powerful human motivation and describes Dharma as something that can come to feel like home.
  5. 06:05 Nothing left behind - Matthew emphasizes that ultimately we are not forced to abandon any part of the heart in order to practice.
  6. 06:47 From every direction - He repeats the invitation to enter from anywhere, even when we feel awkward, remote, or contorted.
  7. 07:25 Affirming effort - The talk softens into encouragement to value one’s efforts without turning that recognition into self-absorption.
  8. 08:05 Rest and continue - He ends with a blessing to practice well, rest well, and keep going as new Dharma doors appear.
