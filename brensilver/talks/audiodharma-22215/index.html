Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Freeing Experience

AudioDharma 29:14 2025-02-05

Matthew Brensilver leads a guided meditation built around a simple reversal: freedom is not getting a self to win, but loosening the tendency to own experience. He begins with posture and breath, then gradually widens the field to include sensations, feelings, thoughts, and the sense of being a protagonist. The practice settles into a quieter intimacy with awareness, where experience can arise and pass without being seized.

Chapters

  1. 00:25 Freedom Reframed - The talk opens with Gil Fronsdal’s line and a Majjhima Nikaya reference to frame liberation as freeing experience rather than securing a self.
  2. 01:20 Settling the Body - He guides posture, lengthening the spine, grounding into the earth, and arriving with the breath.
  3. 03:23 Including What Appears - The practice expands into letting experience take its place in awareness without being pushed away or caught.
  4. 08:37 Freeing Experience - He names images, sounds, pain, tranquility, feelings, and thoughts as things to be freed rather than possessed.
  5. 13:45 Less Possession - The meditation turns toward the strain of possessiveness and the release of the sense that experience must be held as mine.
  6. 15:39 Impersonal Arising - Arising and passing come to feel less personal, with awareness taking the foreground and continuity of self loosening.
  7. 24:28 Equanimity and Control - He reflects on control, delusion, and how equanimity lets the self thin out.
  8. 25:57 Resting in Awareness - The final minutes are mostly silence and bell chimes, allowing the meditation to settle without further instruction.
