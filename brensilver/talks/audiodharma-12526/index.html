Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Monday Morning instructions

AudioDharma 54:36 2020-12-07

Matthew Brensilver opens this retreat-day talk by reflecting on what meditation is for: rest, refuge, insight, steadiness, renunciation, and a more honest relationship to suffering and freedom. He then shifts into practical instruction for sitting and walking practice, emphasizing softness, continuity, patience, and a gentle willingness to meet boredom, clinging, and the changing conditions of the mind without forcing outcomes. The talk closes with encouragement to treat each moment of mindfulness as the next support for practice, rather than judging the whole day by how it feels.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 What meditation is - Matthew sketches meditation as rest, refuge, clarification, and a way of learning what freedom and suffering feel like.
  2. 06:33 Technique and trust - He says the goodness of practice unfolds through sincerity and time, not through forcing a special state.
  3. 07:28 Settling the body - The instructions move into posture, quiet, and a gradual softening of the face, shoulders, and belly.
  4. 12:14 Truce with effort - He invites relaxed attention to the body and sensation, making peace with imperfection rather than trying to banish tension.
  5. 22:18 Awareness of sensation - The sit broadens into holding the field of body sensations and then the breath, with care as a form of love for one's life.
  6. 37:29 Breath and basic aliveness - He frames attention to breathing as an intimate way of staying with the facts of the moment and the basic energy of being alive.
  7. 44:01 Continuity in walking - Turning to walking practice, he stresses continuity across postures, citing Sayadaw U Tejaniya and Stephen Levine on boredom and simple attention.
  8. 51:02 Another moment of mindfulness - The talk ends with a plain encouragement to keep going gently, trusting that one moment of mindfulness can lead to the next.
