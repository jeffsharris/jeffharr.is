Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Unsustainable Trajectories & Love

AudioDharma 15:20 2025-01-15

Matthew Brensilver begins with the Buddha’s recognition that a life organized around pleasure was unsustainable, then widens that lens to social, technological, and ecological conditions that can also feel unstable. He sits with fear, anger, and the pull toward nihilism, but keeps returning to a steadier response: practice, hope stretched across longer time scales, and a commitment to love, non-harming, and compassion. The talk closes with a local story about a rescued goose on Mount Shasta, which becomes a small but affecting image of care and spiritual friendship.

Chapters

  1. 00:09 Unsustainable beginnings - Matthew starts with the Buddha’s realization that his own way of life could not last and draws a parallel to the instability people feel now.
  2. 02:00 A larger trajectory - He expands the idea of unsustainability from one life to society, technology, inequality, and climate.
  3. 03:21 Anger and confusion - The talk turns toward evidence, overconfidence, and the way anger can simplify the world into agents and causes.
  4. 05:06 Powerlessness and nihilism - Matthew names the emotional strain of the moment, including the temptation to collapse into futility or escape.
  5. 07:38 Freedom and hope - He contrasts freedom independent of conditions with the need, at times, to keep hope alive without clinging to the wrong things.
  6. 09:14 Planting long seeds - Hope is widened beyond a single lifetime, and love is framed as patient seed-planting rather than immediate rescue.
  7. 10:52 Back to fundamentals - He returns to the Dhammapada’s simple instruction to do no evil, do what is skillful, and purify the mind.
  8. 11:38 The goose on Shasta - A rescued goose becomes a gentle story of effort, care, tenderness, and the necessity of stewardship.
