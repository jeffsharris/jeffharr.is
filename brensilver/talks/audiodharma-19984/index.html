Guided Meditation: Attention as Prayer

AudioDharma 30:40 2024-03-13

Matthew Brensilver frames this guided meditation around Simone Weil’s line that attention at its highest degree is prayer, then unfolds that idea through body, breath, and sound. The practice moves from a somewhat deliberate, instructed beginning toward a softer sense that awareness is not owned or controlled by a separate meditator. Along the way he weaves in forgiveness, devotion, and the gradual loosening of selfing until attention feels more like surrender than effort.

Chapters

  1. 01:05 Attention as Prayer - Matthew introduces Simone Weil’s line and names it as the conceptual pivot for the meditation.
  2. 02:53 View and Devotion - He explains how wise view and attention converge, and how the practice takes on a devotional quality.
  3. 04:53 Settling the Body - The guided practice begins with posture, relaxation, and a gentle anchoring in body, breath, or sound.
  4. 07:38 Forgiveness and Recommitment - He invites forgiveness for imperfection and then recommits attention to the sacred within ordinary practice.
  5. 10:44 Love in Disguise - Attention is described as a form of love that can appear as worry, protectiveness, or longing for safety.
  6. 14:11 Less Control, More Grace - The language turns toward the clunkiness of selfing and the felt shift from effortful directing to receptive awareness.
  7. 17:56 Letting Awareness Find Us - He returns repeatedly to the sense that the meditator can soften, making room for awareness that is not possessed.
  8. 26:12 Groundless and Held - The close names self as agenda and responsibility, then ends with trust that when we let go, something will catch us.
