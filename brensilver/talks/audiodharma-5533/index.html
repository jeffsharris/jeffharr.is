Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Practice Questions

AudioDharma 22:01 2015-03-26

Matthew Brensilver takes up a set of practice questions from the room and moves through them in a practical, unsentimental way. He starts with sleepiness in meditation, then explains why retreat precepts matter, how to choose among practices like metta and breath, and finally how to work with anger by noticing the mood beneath the emotion. The talk is grounded in everyday experience and leans toward small, workable adjustments rather than fixed formulas.

Chapters

  1. 00:14 Opening and context - Matthew sets up a time for questions and notes that this class assumes some meditation foundation rather than serving as a basic introductory course.
  2. 02:02 Working with sleepiness - He responds to torpor by suggesting posture, breath focus, opening the eyes, standing, walking before sitting, and not fighting every part of tiredness.
  3. 04:19 Fatigue and retreat - Using Shinzen Young's teaching, he distinguishes useful relaxation from sinking consciousness and describes the rough edges of practice through the night.
  4. 06:14 Why precepts matter - In answer to a question about retreat precepts, he explains safety, the visibility of craving, and the long-term momentum of renunciation.
  5. 13:06 Choosing a practice - He reassures a questioner that committing to metta is fine, and says sincerity matters more than turning practice choice into a big issue.
  6. 16:03 Anger and mood - Matthew distinguishes emotions from moods, uses the fish-and-water image to show how background states shape behavior, and suggests attending to the subtler mood before the overt anger.
  7. 20:16 Soothing the system - He closes by emphasizing physiological deactivation, soothing practices, and making the mind-body environment less reactive and more workable.
