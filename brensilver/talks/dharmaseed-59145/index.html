Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Day 6 Morning Guided Instructions: Pervasiveness of Feeling (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 48:22 2019-10-23

In this guided morning instruction, Matthew Brensilver points the listener toward a quieter allegiance to awareness than to the ordinary pull of life. He builds the practice around feeling: how it shapes thought, how it generates suffering and motivation, and how meeting it directly can soften rather than harden the heart. The talk moves from settling the body and grounding in gravity, into a close investigation of pleasant and unpleasant feeling, and then into a steadier confidence in non-abiding and radical permission.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Choosing Awareness - Matthew opens by contrasting the ordinary preoccupations of life with a practice that cares more about awareness.
  2. 02:52 Taking The Seat - He settles the listener into posture, grounding, breathing, and surrendering to the body's support.
  3. 08:10 Why Investigate - The talk turns to attention stability and the Buddha's advice to investigate rather than blame or despair.
  4. 12:04 The Power Of Feeling - Matthew explains that freedom requires reworking our relationship with pleasant and unpleasant feeling, because so much of life grows from it.
  5. 15:04 Ajahn Chah's Instruction - He recounts Ajahn Chah's teaching to Ajahn Sumedho that love, hate, greed, and aversion can become aids and partners in wholesome development.
  6. 21:23 Finding Feelings In The Body - The instruction becomes more precise as he points to affective circuits, front-of-body sensations, and the way awareness can bathe dukkha.
  7. 26:58 Thought And Cascade - Matthew shows how thoughts and images can trigger bodily feeling, and how feeling can in turn generate more thought.
  8. 34:07 Blooming And Collapsing - He emphasizes impermanence and the groundlessness of experience, inviting the listener to see change as continuous.
  9. 38:57 Radical Permission - The talk closes by clarifying that acceptance means saying yes to this moment of experience, then weaving in Antonio Damasio to underline the ubiquity of feeling before returning to the instruction to stay close and continue the practice.
