Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Beyond Judging Comparing and Fixing: Judging

AudioDharma 40:26 2025-06-02

Matthew Brensilver examines the difference between judgment and discernment, beginning with mindfulness and the question of what it means to meet experience without extra defensiveness. He turns to the First Noble Truth, the two arrows, and the ways pain gets layered with aversion, self-protection, and story, then broadens the lens to anger, forgiveness, and self-judgment. The talk closes by reframing enlightenment as deep acceptance and self-love as a form of non-clinging rather than self-idealization.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening the Question - Matthew introduces the theme of judging and asks what peace at the gate of the mind would mean.
  2. 01:57 Mindfulness and Judgment - He uses Kabat-Zinn and Thanissaro Bhikkhu to distinguish non-judgmental awareness from discernment.
  3. 04:05 Judgment Versus Discernment - The talk develops a contrast between egoic judgment and clear seeing of causes, conditions, and what leads to peace.
  4. 09:07 Learning Peace - Using Ajahn Chah and the First Noble Truth, Matthew shows how understanding suffering loosens the need for judgment.
  5. 15:44 The Two Arrows - He explains how raw pain is often mixed with resistance and how practice can reveal less suffering than expected.
  6. 18:59 Pain as Practice - Matthew reflects on working skillfully with pain, finding meaning in it, and seeing how suffering appears in groups and daily life.
  7. 24:15 Anger and Accountability - He draws on Ruth King and Shantideva to show why anger may start something but cannot transform it, and why accountability matters without self-blame.
  8. 27:16 Forgiveness and Equanimity - The discussion turns toward forgiveness, making space for unpleasantness, and loosening the habit of explaining everything as wrong.
  9. 31:47 Self-Love and Freedom - Matthew examines self-view, recounts a Nisargadatta story, and ends with Lao Tzu to frame self-love as radical acceptance and non-clinging.
