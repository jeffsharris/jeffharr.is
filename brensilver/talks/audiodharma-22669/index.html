Guided Meditation: Relaxing the tension called 'me'

AudioDharma 29:37 2025-04-03

This guided meditation invites a softening around the felt tension of selfing, moving from settling the body into a careful inquiry into how control is maintained through feeling, thought, and image. Matthew Brensilver repeatedly returns to surrender as a simple laying down of strategy, and toward the end he emphasizes relaxing the contraction that accompanies uncertainty. The practice stays quiet and direct, with the instruction to notice what can relax and to allow the rest to remain.

Chapters

  1. 00:13 Settling In - After a brief welcome, the practice begins by finding posture and arriving in stillness.
  2. 01:28 Touching Tranquility - He points to the muscle memory of Dharma practice and suggests recollecting tranquility and openheartedness.
  3. 04:01 Relax What Can - The instruction narrows to relaxing whatever can be relaxed while allowing remaining tension to stay.
  4. 05:24 Self as Headquarters - The talk turns to the sense of self as a headquarters for control, and surrender as the self laying down.
  5. 09:38 Seeing the Mechanics - He names the body, mental images, words, and auditory thought as the tools that organize clinging and control.
  6. 15:16 From Control to Wakefulness - The emphasis shifts from acquiring and strategizing to simply being awake, without a hidden agenda.
  7. 23:44 Contraction and Uncertainty - Near the end, he asks listeners to notice self-contraction as weight and to relax the uncertainty that fuels control.
  8. 25:00 Habitualizing Impermanence - The closing line gestures toward habituating to anicca as the practice continues.
