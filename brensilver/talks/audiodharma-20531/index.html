Dharmette: Reflections on words from Ajahn Sucitto

AudioDharma 16:38 2024-05-29

Matthew Brensilver reflects on a short teaching from Ajahn Sucitto and uses it to unfold a quiet map of the path: generosity, ethics, renunciation, discernment, persistence, and patience. He moves from the felt meaning of sharing to the practical question of how to direct energy, make choices, and time action well. The talk stays grounded in ordinary examples and in the sense that practice is less about intensity than about steady, responsive care.

Chapters

  1. 00:20 Reading the quote - Matthew introduces the Ajahn Sucitto passage and names the cluster of qualities it gathers together.
  2. 02:39 Generosity as interdependence - He expands generosity beyond material giving into an attitude shaped by relation, example, and shared blessing.
  3. 04:01 Sharing Dharma wisely - The talk turns to how advice lands, why example matters, and how generosity can be expressed without attachment to view.
  4. 06:58 Ethics and kindness - Matthew links interdependence with ethical choice, then pauses with a story and a study of random acts of kindness.
  5. 09:15 Morality and renunciation - He moves through self-respect, non-regret, and the question of enough, using personal anecdotes to show discernment in action.
  6. 12:01 Steady persistence - The emphasis shifts from peaks of effort to the daily steadiness that supports lasting change.
  7. 14:06 Patience and timing - Matthew closes with patience as sensitivity to ripeness, causality, and when a move is truly called for.
  8. 15:55 Closing reflections - He thanks Ajahn Sucitto, offers the teaching for consideration, and ends with a brief farewell.
