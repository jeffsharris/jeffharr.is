Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Abiding in Goodness

AudioDharma 27:59 2025-02-19

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation on abiding in goodness, beginning with the Dalai Lama’s claim that kindness and compassion are necessities rather than luxuries. The practice moves from recollecting love as nourishment, to sensing the ethical atmosphere of sangha, to widening into the shared wish of all beings to be well. Later he brings in the Buddha and Joan Halifax to connect self-care, non-harming, and the embodied balance of a "strong back, soft front."

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Love as Necessity - The talk opens by asking what it means to treat kindness and compassion as essential nourishment rather than optional comfort.
  2. 06:17 Abiding in Love - Matthew turns toward sangha and invites listeners to settle into an image, phrase, or felt sense of love and remain there.
  3. 12:22 Self and Others - The practice widens into the recognition that every movement of mind and body is a search for happiness, leading into compassion for oneself and others.
  4. 18:29 Grief and Courage - He notes that contact with goodness can feel like grief or ecstasy, then introduces Joan Halifax’s "strong back, soft front" as an embodied balance.
  5. 23:03 Deep Faith in Love - The meditation settles into the sense that love may feel tender or may simply allow whatever needs to be felt, both expressing confidence in love.
  6. 26:15 Closing Refrain - He repeats the body-based image of softness and strength, ending with the reminder that love may feel like love or may simply make room for experience.
