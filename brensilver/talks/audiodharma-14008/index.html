Guided Meditation: Resting in Goodness

AudioDharma 30:07 2021-08-13

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet guided meditation oriented toward resting in goodness: recalling the wholesome qualities already present in one’s life, feeling gratitude for the Dharma lineage, and settling attention into the soundscape. The practice then turns toward a more open field of sound, silence, and the ease of not clinging. Near the end, he emphasizes that the heart needs rest and that relaxing into our human imperfection is not indulgent, but necessary.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and settling - A brief welcome and invitation to take the seat as the meditation begins.
  2. 01:21 Remembering goodness - The guidance turns to recalling the practice already done, the patience, equanimity, stillness, and determination already present.
  3. 02:46 Lineage and gratitude - He widens the view to the Dharma lineage of understanding, love, and compassion, and to the gratitude that comes with being a beneficiary and a cause for more goodness.
  4. 04:42 Resting in blessing - The meditation lingers in contemplation of the blessings of the path and the longing for freedom that made it possible to arrive here.
  5. 12:36 Sound as one field - Attention shifts to the whole soundscape, including outer sounds, inner sounds, and moments of silence, without separating them so much.
  6. 13:47 Deepening into ease - The practice continues in a quieter open attention, allowing the field to be simply known without grasping.
  7. 24:47 Letting the heart rest - He closes by naming how clinging keeps the heart from resting and encourages surrender to imperfection and human limits.
