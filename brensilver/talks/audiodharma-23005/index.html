Dharmette: Sila Reflections

AudioDharma 16:42 2025-05-21

Matthew Brensilver reflects on sila as more than ethical rule-following: a way of becoming a refuge, softening around suffering, and letting love and wisdom mature together. He moves through grief, humility, and the willingness to be wrong, then widens the lens to moral imagination, cultural conditioning, and the limits of ego. The talk ends by turning back to completion, suggesting that practice is learning to die before we die and to let this life be enough.

Chapters

  1. 01:07 Becoming a refuge - He reads a short reflection on making oneself a refuge for all beings and connects sila with samadhi and panna.
  2. 02:20 Love, grief, and goodness - The talk dwells on how goodness ripens through letting go, grief, and the way pain and care can become inseparable.
  3. 05:03 Ethics and moral growth - He returns to sila as a doorway into wisdom, noting that ethical development requires clarity, humility, and a willingness to admit wrongdoing.
  4. 07:20 Pain becoming love - Matthew describes the growing pains of Dharma as a kind of grieving and suggests that unpleasant feeling can transform into the brahmaviharas.
  5. 08:51 Skillful means and belonging - He broadens the teaching to many kinds of practice, emphasizing that different people need different upaya and that true belonging in Dharma is idiosyncratic.
  6. 11:38 Dreamlike awakening - Using the image of lucid dreaming, he asks how love changes when life feels more dream-like and unfinished.
  7. 13:15 Moral imagination - With Heschel and Parfit, he explores prophetic insight, temporal separation, and the ego's distortions of truth and conscience.
  8. 14:45 Completing this life - The closing turns toward death and the possibility of a life that feels complete, enough, and willingly let go.
