Dharmette: How We Incarnate in Different Contexts

AudioDharma 14:56 2026-02-11

Matthew Brensilver uses a line from Ajahn Chah’s aspiration verses to explore how we “incarnate” differently from moment to moment and in different roles. He moves from the apparent stability of personality to the subtler ways sankharas, emotion, and social context shape the self we become in each situation. The talk closes with practical questions for noticing what gets amplified, what gets forgotten, and how Dharma is present or distant in different settings.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Ajahn Chah's Line - Matthew begins with the aspiration verse and lingers on the phrase “in every kind of birth” as the talk’s central image.
  2. 01:18 Identity and Variability - He questions the idea of a single fixed self and notes how personality is stable in some ways but changes across contexts.
  3. 02:52 Roles of Daily Life - He brings the question into ordinary life, naming different roles and asking how we take birth in each one.
  4. 04:42 Foucault and Selfhood - A brief Foucault reference supports the point that one need not have a single face or remain the same in every setting.
  5. 05:30 Formative Energies - He turns to sankharas as subtle formative energies that shape perception and identity before grasping becomes obvious.
  6. 07:04 When Arousal Changes - The talk becomes more experiential as he describes how emotional tone, flexibility, and hardness can shift by role and situation.
  7. 08:51 Interrogating the Implicit - He asks listeners to notice the views, needs, stories, and assumptions that quietly organize a given context.
  8. 11:01 What Dharma Recalls - Matthew suggests asking what teachings are near or far, and what is remembered or forgotten in each incarnation.
  9. 13:22 Homework in Awareness - He ends by inviting careful observation of which contexts magnify greed, hate, and delusion, and where subtle energies can be noticed.
