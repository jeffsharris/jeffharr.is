Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Sensitivity all the way down

AudioDharma 30:01 2023-02-17

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet guided meditation on receptivity, using chitta as a way of understanding the heart-mind as something inherently open and affected. The practice moves from settling the body, to recognizing how we brace against pain and self-protection, and then toward a steadier openness that does not need to land in conclusions. Near the end, he emphasizes that goodness is already here, and that the Dharma can be trusted through direct feeling rather than striving.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling In - A brief opening invitation to arrive, settle, and begin the meditation.
  2. 01:22 Chitta as Receptivity - Matthew introduces chitta, drawing on Ajahn Sucitto’s framing of heart-mind as a receptivity system.
  3. 03:22 Porous and Poised - He encourages a posture of openness that resists settling too quickly into conclusions about self or life.
  4. 05:28 Always Being Touched - The talk turns to embodiment, sensitivity, and the way compassion and quivering are already part of lived experience.
  5. 10:00 False Alarms - He names the many urgencies and impulses that arise, and invites a steady stance that does not overreact to them.
  6. 16:40 Bracing and Protection - Matthew examines how we brace against pain and protect identity, describing this as sensitivity all the way down.
  7. 18:57 Openness Without Defense - The meditation softens into a direct felt sense of openness, with less urgency and less need for protection.
  8. 24:50 Goodness Already Here - He closes by pointing to receptivity as a way of not trying to become anything, trusting that goodness and Dharma are already present.
