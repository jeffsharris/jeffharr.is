Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Untitled (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 47:09 2018-11-23

Matthew Brensilver uses Martha Nussbaum’s account of human dependence to open a wider reflection on shame, vulnerability, and ethics. From there he moves through mindfulness, sila, and the way practice shows up in ordinary conduct, arguing that ethical life is where the Dharma becomes most concrete. The talk settles into questions of confidence, feedback, defensiveness, and moral incoherence, and ends by returning to the possibility of a life of benefit without grand certainty.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Dependence and Shame - Matthew begins with Martha Nussbaum’s account of human incompleteness and the emotions that arise from dependence.
  2. 02:52 Belonging and Mindfulness - He links interdependence with peace, then shifts from trying to remember mindfulness to the longer-term training of heart and habit.
  3. 06:47 Ethics as Practice - Using Bob Thurman’s joke about the 'concert,' he argues that ethical life is the real expression of practice and that mindfulness and sila support each other.
  4. 15:53 Wisdom and Humility - He brings in Gil Fronsdal, Ajahn Sumedho, and research on overconfidence to question rigid views and to point toward a middle way of calibrated confidence.
  5. 24:39 Feedback and Self-View - Matthew describes ethics as a field guide to clinging, emphasizing feedback, defensiveness, and the way the self becomes visible when it is challenged.
  6. 32:05 Freefall and Courage - He explores the discomfort of having our self-image rattled and names ethical growth as a courageous willingness to stay with disorientation.
  7. 36:39 One Fruit of Practice - Ajahn Chah’s 'one fruit' saying frames sila, samadhi, and panna as inseparable, and the talk turns toward responsibility, moral incoherence, and hope that is not dependent on outcomes.
  8. 41:51 Radical Hearts - The talk closes with the question of how to live for the benefit of others, ending in a quiet appeal for mutual care and reconciliation.
