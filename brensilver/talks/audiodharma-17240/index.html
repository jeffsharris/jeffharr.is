Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Whole Presence Samadhi

AudioDharma 50:17 2022-11-04

Matthew Brensilver speaks about samadhi as more than concentration: a gathered, easeful, undistracted presence that can become non-separate, embodied, and whole. Using stories of a Zen teacher on a bus, a temple drum, Suzuki Roshi, and his own experience with striving in meditation, he shows how pressure and self-clinging can block the very stillness we want. The talk gradually shifts from defining samadhi to showing how kindness, acceptance, and working gently with hindrances make it available in ordinary life.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening Samadhi - The talk begins with the plan for a shared Dharma conversation and a first attempt to define samadhi as gathered, easeful presence rather than effortful concentration.
  2. 03:44 Bus Ride Presence - A story about a calm older monk on a Kyoto bus shows how radiance and settled presence can quietly draw others in.
  3. 08:33 Undistracted Awareness - Matthew develops samadhi as continuous presence and distinguishes it from simple momentary mindfulness, using everyday absorption as an example.
  4. 15:17 Becoming One - The temple drum story illustrates non-separation, with samadhi described as becoming one with the action rather than standing apart from it.
  5. 21:38 Ordinary Acts - Suzuki Roshi is briefly invoked as an example of total presence in ordinary movement, and samadhi is linked to softness that can hold whatever arises.
  6. 25:10 Softness Through Suffering - A story from Crooked Cucumber reframes Suzuki Roshi’s spaciousness as something hard-won through tragedy, leading into samadhi as healing the divisions in the heart.
  7. 29:50 Striving and Craving - In the dialogue, Matthew reflects on how talk of bliss can create more suffering, and how chasing states or technique can become another form of clinging.
  8. 38:02 Hindrances and Wholeness - The conversation turns to the hindrances, embodied sensitivity, and the insight that samadhi is already present when resistance softens and the heart is allowed to be simple and open.
  9. 47:49 Closing Blessing - The talk closes with a short blessing and a final reminder that samadhi helps us see things as they are, meeting the moment with patience and kindness.
