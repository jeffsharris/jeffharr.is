Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Deep Embodiment

Dharma Seed 41:11 2024-07-11

Matthew Brensilver offers a quietly insistent talk on inhabiting the body before turning toward the story of the self. He begins with gravity, breath, and the felt sense of aliveness, then repeatedly returns to the ways the mind tries to manage unpleasantness, uncertainty, and the urge to fix. The talk gradually opens into a simpler view: life and death are not fully owned or governed, and this moment can be received as Dhamma rather than solved.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling Into Gravity - The talk opens by inviting attention to the earth’s support and the subtle soothing of being held.
  2. 01:16 Breath In The Body - He guides a fuller, slower breath and points to breathing as both calming agitation and enlivening what feels flat.
  3. 03:53 Front And Back - The practice widens beyond the front of the body to include the back, and beyond body-image and self-consciousness to raw felt sensation.
  4. 08:33 Aliveness Without Story - Attention is poured into immediate bodily experience, where there is aliveness but no fixed self or narrative to be found.
  5. 12:56 Leaving Measurement Behind - He emphasizes stepping out of the habit of assessment and description, and staying with embodied presence rather than trying to improve life.
  6. 18:47 The Urge To Fix - A felt sense of problem is traced to arousal in the body and the compulsion to repair, with anicca named as the uncertainty behind it.
  7. 25:15 Receiving This Moment - The teaching turns toward renouncing the impulse to tinker with conditions and toward seeing the moment as given rather than owned.
  8. 34:16 Rewrite The Autobiography - He suggests stepping out of meaning-making for now, then opens a more porous relation between body and space.
  9. 38:18 Announcements - The talk closes with retreat logistics, small-group information, and meeting details.
