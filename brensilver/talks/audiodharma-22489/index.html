Dharmette: Fear at the Gateway of Stillness

AudioDharma 15:56 2025-03-12

Matthew Brensilver responds to a question about fear that arises as meditation deepens and stillness begins to gather. He describes why letting go can feel unsafe, how vigilance and self-protection become fused with consciousness, and why the edge of practice can bring spiritual vertigo. The talk moves toward practical ways of meeting fear: knowing it clearly, softening it with love, acclimatizing to mortality and change, and trusting the silence rather than resisting it.

Chapters

  1. 00:17 The question of fear - Matthew introduces a listener's question about fear arising in meditation and names the basic problem.
  2. 01:46 Safety and vigilance - He explores how survival conditioning, caretaking pressure, and cultural expectations can make vigilance feel necessary.
  3. 03:00 Why letting go feels unsafe - The talk turns to the way clinging, thinking, and worrying can feel like safety when uncertainty is present.
  4. 05:21 At the gateway - Matthew describes how deeper stillness can trigger the sense that one must do something, even though willfulness only adds contraction.
  5. 06:59 Spiritual vertigo - He names the jarring quality of attenuated self-states and the feeling of standing near an abyss as the ordinary self thins out.
  6. 09:31 Working with fear - Using the image of being 'on the cusp,' he suggests releasing the need for something to happen and making fear an object of clear awareness.
  7. 11:32 Training the nervous system - He notes that practice is often gradual, aimed at de-electrifying the past so old pain no longer ambushes us.
  8. 13:28 Mortality and faith - With references to Ajahn Sucitto and Suzuki Roshi, he shifts from hidden monsters to mortality, change, and the possibility of faith.
  9. 15:03 Surrender to silence - The talk ends by inviting trust in Dharma and lineage, and by framing silence as something that beckons rather than threatens.
