Monday Dharma Talk: Relationship between the Body and Thinking

AudioDharma 47:16 2022-06-20

Matthew Brensilver explores how bodily affect and thinking shape one another, arguing that insight comes less from thinking our way through experience than from gathering data in awareness, especially through the body. He moves from basic Buddhist framing to a close look at valence, neutrality, prediction, and the stories the mind builds around feeling. The talk gradually becomes practical: how to recognize false alarms, meet unpleasantness without narrative inflation, and let bodily signals inform wiser action.

Chapters

  1. 00:32 Investigating the human realm - Matthew begins by framing practice as investigation rather than thought alone, with the body as a primary source of data.
  2. 04:11 Affect and meaning - He defines affect broadly, explains how stimulation becomes meaningful through feeling, and introduces the role of neutral experience.
  3. 07:48 Thought and feeling - The talk turns to the two-way relationship between thought and affect, including a comparison with cognitive therapy and wise view.
  4. 13:56 Views and assumptions - Matthew shows how Buddhist practice exposes hidden beliefs, normalizes dukkha, and loosens unrealistic ideas about happiness and self.
  5. 20:18 Body as prediction - He develops a more detailed account of how bodily states shape view, using examples from anxiety, caffeine, fear, and prediction-making.
  6. 29:28 Without story - The focus shifts to the compulsion to conclude and narrate, and to the practice of meeting unpleasantness directly instead of building more meaning around it.
  7. 36:40 Making wise meanings - Matthew uses a DAC analogy and Ajahn Sucitto’s teaching to suggest that practice helps translate bodily signals into wiser thought and action.
  8. 39:52 Training discernment - He closes with embodied wisdom, mixed signals, and the gradual development of trust and intuitive knowing through practice.
