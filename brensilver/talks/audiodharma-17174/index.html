Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Self and Space

AudioDharma 45:26 2022-10-28

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet guided meditation built around non-striving, resting in awareness, and easing the habit of making something out of the moment. He gradually moves from the simplicity of being alive, through repeated rests and reflections on self-making, toward a practice of leaning into what does not depend on the self. The talk closes by widening this into a daily practice of sincerity and asking, moment by moment, what opens space now.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Simple aliveness - The meditation opens with a Suzuki Roshi attribution and an invitation to stop extracting anything from the moment.
  2. 02:57 Giving up acquisition - Matthew contrasts generosity with acquisitiveness and suggests that movement in practice unfolds when the need to get somewhere is relaxed.
  3. 07:49 Rest where awareness stays - He introduces the instruction to rest in what cannot be stopped and repeats it as a sustained practice cue.
  4. 15:59 Putting down life - A poetic image of the 'rag and bone shop of our heart' leads into the instruction to put down the notion of my life and rest.
  5. 20:11 Hindrances and disenchantment - Hindrances are framed as innocent but unworkable, and the result is a sober kind of disenchantment.
  6. 24:27 How self is reiterated - Matthew describes selfing as a kind of proprioception built from reference points like body, preferences, and life story.
  7. 29:26 Rest in dependence - He turns toward resting in what does not depend on the self, using that as a bridge into deeper openness.
  8. 34:15 Love and surrender - The meditation settles into prolonged silence and repeated 'okay,' pointing to the tenderness needed to make this bearable.
  9. 42:07 Practice in daily life - In the closing section, Matthew reframes meditation as an exploration of freedom, tailored to the heart in the present moment.
