Matthew Brensilver, Randy Fernando: Navigating A.I. from a Buddhist Perspective

Dharma Seed 1:28:33 2023-09-23

Matthew Brensilver and Randy Fernando frame AI as a Buddhist problem of attention, suffering, and conditions rather than novelty alone. The talk moves from a brief opening sit, into a sober overview of generative AI and its risks, and then into a Dharma lens on dependent co-arising, sense-making, and the First Noble Truth. It closes with questions about anatta, the future of teaching and practice, and a return to the Brahma Viharas as a steadier ethical orientation.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening and welcome - The speakers settle in, acknowledge the audience, and describe the event format before beginning a short sit.
  2. 03:34 Attention and practice - Matthew grounds the talk in a Buddhist teaching on attention, then leads a brief meditation on posture, breath, and embodied stability.
  3. 09:44 Why AI feels urgent - He recalls an earlier technology talk, argues that attention is being monetized, and names AI as a powerful amplifier of greed, hatred, and delusion.
  4. 15:44 Existential risk context - Drawing on Toby Ord and the language of risk, Matthew frames AI as a large-scale human concern and hands the conversation to Randy for background.
  5. 21:19 What AI can do - Randy gives a clear introductory tour of chatbots, image generation, audio synthesis, filters, and translation, showing how quickly these systems have advanced.
  6. 29:34 Truth, power, and misuse - The discussion turns to synthetic media, trust, jobs, jailbreaking, military uses, and the ways AI can scale existing harms faster than law can respond.
  7. 42:47 Accelerating conditions - Matthew emphasizes binding power with responsibility, then links AI to extractive economics, climate damage, and the way devices mediate our minds.
  8. 51:37 A short sit and conditions - After a brief pause, Randy introduces dependent co-arising as the core lens for understanding technology, users, and social effects as conditioned phenomena.
  9. 01:04:21 Questions on capitalism - Audience questions bring the talk to regulation, externalities, jobs, UBI, and whether capitalism can be redirected without amplifying harm.
