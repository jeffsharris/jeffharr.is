Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Sound and Silence

AudioDharma 26:20 2025-12-10

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation centered on listening as a way of meeting sound, thought, and silence without grasping. The practice moves from simple instruction into a wider field of hearing, including external sounds, internal sounds, equanimity, and a gentle bowing to fear and preoccupation. Near the end, the attention turns toward very distant sounds and the edge where hearing and silence are no longer easy to separate.

Chapters

  1. 01:00 Entering Listening - He introduces the practice as a simple act of listening and invites settling into sound without extra effort.
  2. 02:30 Hunger and Sound - The talk explores how hearing can feel less hungry than seeing or thinking, opening a different relationship to experience.
  3. 04:00 Listening in All Directions - Attention widens to sounds around, behind, above, and below, with silence becoming more noticeable.
  4. 06:15 Self With No Job - He points to how the sense of self has little to do and encourages a kind of listening that does not depend on identity or role.
  5. 07:30 No Inside or Outside - External sound and internal sound are treated alike, with an emphasis on not propping up awareness or getting caught in grammar.
  6. 12:20 Equanimity and Nonclinging - The practice becomes one of not picking up phenomena, letting sounds and thoughts remain present without becoming sticky.
  7. 15:37 Bowing to Preoccupations - He suggests bowing to fear, needs, and preoccupations so they can be held with less neglect and less chewing.
  8. 22:33 Edge of Silence - The meditation closes by experimenting with distant sounds and the moment when it is hard to tell whether one is hearing sound or silence.
