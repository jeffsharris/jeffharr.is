Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Wednesday morning instructions

AudioDharma 45:11 2023-11-01

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet set of instructions for sitting with experience as something moving and changing rather than fixed. He keeps returning to posture, nervous system, and the practice of meeting feeling with awareness, patience, and sensitivity, then opens that ground into equanimity, love, and a frank view of the human condition. The talk includes a few clearly garbled transcript passages; the teaching itself remains steady and coherent.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Changing experience - He begins by describing experience as fluid and unstable, more like a blooming and collapsing process than something fixed.
  2. 03:19 Returning to the body - The talk turns toward posture and embodiment, with instructions to bring mind into the body and let relaxation begin there.
  3. 09:03 Thought as aftermath - Brensilver describes thought as something like exhaust from feeling, and introduces the practice of bathing feeling in awareness and patience.
  4. 16:14 Soothing the system - He speaks about stimulation, the effects of samsara, and how receptivity can soften and soothe the nervous system.
  5. 24:52 Being changed - Rather than trying to change the world, he emphasizes letting experience change us and digesting intensity before it hardens into story.
  6. 29:12 Equanimity’s by-products - He names the possible fruits of open awareness meeting intensity: tranquility, peace, and sometimes love.
  7. 31:38 The necessity of love - The teaching widens into the many forms love can take, the compatibility of awareness and love, and a sober account of wisdom, violence, and the human condition.
  8. 44:01 Awareness and helplessness - He closes by saying awareness practice is never far from love, especially where helplessness is most felt.
