Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Active and Receptive Dimensions of Practice

Dharma Seed 44:26 2025-09-18

Matthew Brensilver frames practice as a balance between doing and receiving, using the Middle Path to explore how attention, patience, and renunciation each need their counterpart. He draws on William James, Ajahn Sucitto, Thich Nhat Hanh, Adler, and Mahasi-style effort language to show both the usefulness and the limits of willpower in Dharma practice. The talk gradually moves toward a quieter trust: that healing depends on conditions, that time can become an ally, and that practice often works by softening rather than forcing.

Chapters

  1. 00:49 Middle Path Framing - Brensilver opens by extending the Middle Path beyond obvious extremes into the balance of wholesome qualities that can also tip out of alignment.
  2. 04:01 Activity and Receptivity - He names the central tension of the talk: the pull between doing and letting be, and why Dharma is often about holding two truths at once.
  3. 07:39 Letting Go Gradually - Using Ajahn Sucitto and a rope-climbing image, he describes awakening as a careful, conscious descent out of assumptions and self-stories.
  4. 09:55 Training Attention - He turns to attention as the first active cultivation, drawing on William James and the Buddha to show how attention shapes agency, energy, and presence.
  5. 15:34 Patience With the Path - A reflection on Thich Nhat Hanh leads into a case for patience: with pain, with unfolding, and with the fact that healing is not a simple decision.
  6. 21:45 Renunciation and Enough - Brensilver argues that renunciation is a positive simplification, not a grim no, and that practice asks what is enough and what we are trading peace for.
  7. 27:14 What We Cannot Do - He emphasizes that much of the path is not made by our effort alone, and that the receptive side of practice is hard for the doer to trust.
  8. 30:30 Limits of Willfulness - The talk deepens into a critique of compulsive self-engineering, showing how willfulness can distort both practice and healing.
  9. 39:54 Receiving the Blessings - He closes by inviting trust in the Dharma field itself, where time becomes an ally and the heart can rest in support, connection, and surrender.
