Guided Meditation: Resting in Goodness

AudioDharma 29:47 2025-01-22

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet guided meditation on resting in goodness rather than bracing against experience. He emphasizes letting ordinary goodness soothe agitation, forgiving the mind at a deep level, and trusting awareness and love to carry the practice. The talk gradually widens from settling posture to a softer relationship with thought, clinging, and whatever can be enjoyed in the present field of experience.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Settling In - He welcomes the group and invites a simple settling into posture and presence.
  2. 01:05 Goodness as Soothe - The meditation begins by letting ordinary goodness calm and soften the heart.
  3. 03:14 Attention and Agitation - He contrasts the usual focus on breath and body with allowing goodness to ease worry and reveal what remains.
  4. 05:50 Pressure on Self - The talk turns toward how meditation can become a self-improvement project and how the self can be relaxed.
  5. 08:19 Meditation by Itself - He encourages surrendering the sense of self to awareness, then deep forgiveness of the mind and trust in awareness and love.
  6. 13:46 Clinging and Aliveness - He points to the goodness of mind as independent of effort, and explores how clinging narrows experience while non-clinging feels more alive.
  7. 25:05 Enjoy What Can Be - Near the end, he invites listeners to enjoy whatever is available in experience and let the practice rest in simple appreciation.
