Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Resting in Goodness

AudioDharma 29:41 2026-02-04

Matthew Brensilver opens with a brief, wry set of Melissa Broder quotations that hold dread and humor together, then turns toward a guided meditation on settling and appreciating what is already present. The practice moves from posture and breath to resting with goodness in the body, noticing ordinary life as quietly sufficient, and softening the mind’s habit of organizing around distress. In the closing stretch, he ties enjoyment, letting go, and the Dharma idea of anicca together, suggesting that fear and worry can relax when the mind stops needing to manage everything.

  8. 23:27 Fear and Uncertainty - Matthew names fear and worry as meaning-making around anicca, then points toward the relief that follows letting go.
