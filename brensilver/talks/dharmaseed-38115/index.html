Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Spirit Rock-Metta in the Post Obama Era

Dharma Seed 28:37 2016-11-20

Matthew Brensilver begins with an unexpected reflection on the political mood after the 2016 campaign, and then turns toward a simple but demanding aspiration: to be safe for others. From there he moves into loving-kindness practice as both a personal refuge and a way of meeting the pain, fear, and longing shared by others. The talk includes a guided metta sequence and closes by widening from self-care to a wish for peace and freedom for all beings.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and settling - He opens with a brief settling-in and a soft transition from the previous day’s rain image into the talk’s quieter tone.
  2. 03:42 Safe for others - Matthew recalls a monk’s aspiration to be safe for others and relates it to the unease and urgency he feels in the country after the campaign.
  3. 08:56 Self-love and longing - He turns to the Buddha’s teaching on self-love, describing how inward attention reveals vulnerability and the deep wish to be happy.
  4. 13:42 Metta phrases - He offers the core loving-kindness phrases and invites listeners to repeat them gently and feel their effect in the body.
  5. 18:08 Listening to the body - The practice slows into repetition, with an emphasis on listening for what the words evoke and allowing pain, grief, and hope to soften the heart.
  6. 22:44 Suffering and companionship - He uses an African proverb to suggest that suffering is never alone, and that something else stands beside it.
  7. 24:39 Widening to all beings - The final movement expands the wish from personal safety and peace to compassion for all beings, including those facing oppression, war, and poverty.
