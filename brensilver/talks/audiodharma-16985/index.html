Guided Meditation

AudioDharma 36:23 2022-09-25

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation that moves from settling the body into a spacious way of meeting sound, sensation, and emotion. The talk gently reframes bracing and aversion as habits of protection, then invites a more trusting openness in which experience can arise without being organized around a defended center. In the second half, the practice softens toward a sustained sense of awareness as something that can hold and mellow the forces of suffering.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling In - The meditation opens with orientation, a comfortable posture, and a gradual settling into the room and the body.
  2. 02:23 Welcoming Experience - He invites a welcoming mind toward sounds, bodily intensity, and the ordinary feeling of arriving.
  3. 04:14 Breath and Ease - The guidance turns to the breath as a gentle way to smooth the energy of body and mind.
  4. 08:48 Letting Winds Blow - Bracing, self-protection, and the winds of sensation, thought, and affect are introduced as experiences to allow rather than resist.
  5. 12:15 Grasping and Aversion - He extends the teaching to unpleasantness and the pull of grasping and aversion, emphasizing openness instead of reaction.
  6. 16:38 No Need To Pretend - The talk states plainly that practice does not ask us to fake neutrality or force liking, only to meet experience clearly.
  7. 22:49 Softening The Heart - A more affective phase emerges, with the heart being softened by greed and aversion held within awareness.
  8. 27:49 Sustained Open Awareness - The closing stretch remains with the same simple refrain, letting awareness do the work of holding and softening experience.
