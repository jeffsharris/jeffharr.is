Beyond Judging, Comparing and Fixing: Fixing

AudioDharma 44:18 2025-06-03

Matthew Brensilver explores the hidden drive to fix, improve, and become somebody better, and how that impulse shapes meditation, self-story, and even our relationship to pain. He distinguishes necessary practical adjustment from compulsive fixing, then slowly turns the talk toward receptivity, equanimity, and a non-gaining way of practicing. The talk closes by tying this stance to healing the past, meeting uncertainty, and letting life be enough as it is.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 One Fidget Away - Matthew opens with the sense that peace always seems just a small adjustment away, then questions the tragedy of living by constant fidgeting and progress.
  2. 02:55 The Fixing Impulse - He examines how progress becomes central to ordinary life and practice, naming the urge to fix the heart, body, mind, and world as a deep samsaric pattern.
  3. 08:14 Pain and Pressure - The talk turns to how tension is often normalized, why the mind keeps evaluating threat and opportunity, and how non-intervention can feel unbelievable at first.
  4. 13:33 Secret Motives - Matthew looks at the language and strategies of clinging, including hidden spiritual ambition, and shares how practice often still needs practical adjustments rather than rigid refusal.
  5. 19:40 Non-Gaining Practice - He distinguishes healthy conditions from compulsive striving, then uses stories and Suzuki Roshi to show how real progress comes when the self stops trying to manufacture it.
  6. 25:35 Curiosity Over Outcome - The teaching deepens into intrinsic motivation, secret intentions, and the wish to use Dharma to repair brokenness, especially through the handling of personal history.
  7. 31:25 Blessing the Past - Matthew describes turning ghosts into ancestors, integrating memory without being haunted by it, and learning not to fight with experience at any level.
  8. 37:03 Enough, Just This - He brings the talk toward equanimity, ethical steadiness, and non-fixing as receptivity, surrender, peace, and love in the face of uncertainty and impermanence.
