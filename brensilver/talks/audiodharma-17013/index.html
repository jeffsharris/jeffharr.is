Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Attention & Awareness

AudioDharma 29:52 2022-09-30

Matthew Brensilver leads a guided meditation that moves from settling into practice to a close look at attention, clinging, and the ease that comes from making peace with the present moment. He distinguishes the “spotlight” of attention from awareness itself, then gradually shifts toward resting in the knowing that cannot be turned off. The talk is quiet and reflective, with extended meditation space and a final emphasis on relinquishing control.

Chapters

  1. 00:25 Arriving to meditate - The session begins by noticing the transition from ordinary conversation into formal practice and allowing that shift to be simple.
  2. 02:22 Natural beginning - The meditation starts with a natural, unforced attitude, trusting that wisdom and love are already present.
  3. 08:22 Relaxing and letting go - The instruction turns toward relaxing what can be relaxed and releasing some of the agitation held in attention.
  4. 10:03 Attention settles - As peace with the imperfection of the moment grows, the spotlight of attention becomes steadier and more fluid.
  5. 14:43 Less control - The practice deepens by relinquishing the need to direct attention so tightly.
  6. 15:38 Resting in awareness - The focus shifts from the objects of attention to awareness itself, which cannot really be stopped.
  7. 20:30 Spotlight and clinging - Matthew reflects on how attention can support concentration, but also how it can serve clinging when it is turned into a self.
  8. 26:31 Unpartitioned knowing - The meditation opens into the sense that knowing is already happening, not owned by a separate self, and that awareness no longer tastes like 'me'.
