Dharmette: Wholesome Qualities of Mind (2of5); The Parami of Patience

AudioDharma 16:03 2025-08-19

Matthew Brensilver uses patient, practical examples to explore the parami of patience as something more than waiting: a steadiness that can hold suffering without immediate reaction. He moves from a sweat-lodge story and Thich Nhat Hanh’s embodied presence into a wider teaching on how practice changes us gradually, often beneath awareness. The talk closes by reframing meditation, self-kindness, and commitment as ways of slowly learning to love something new.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and framing - Matthew greets the group and sets a casual, unscripted tone for a short Dharma talk on patience.
  2. 00:38 The hard path - A sweat-lodge story shows that a difficult path can still be easier than having no path at all.
  3. 01:22 Thich Nhat Hanh's example - Matthew recalls Thich Nhat Hanh’s teaching about the sacred being available now and describes patience as the quality most strongly embodied in his movement.
  4. 03:23 Patience and suffering - Patience is presented as foundational for understanding dukkha and for receiving pain without rushing to convert it into concepts.
  5. 05:52 Gradual cultivation - The talk turns to the mismatch between our craving for quick results and the reality that goodness often accrues slowly and unnoticed.
  6. 07:03 Holding the heart still - With Ajahn Sucitto’s definition, patience becomes the restraint that keeps the heart steady in the presence of suffering.
  7. 08:07 States become traits - Matthew distinguishes temporary states from durable traits and argues that practice matters insofar as it reshapes character over time.
  8. 11:58 Learning new love - Using Agnes Callard’s ideas, he explains that coming to value meditation, self-kindness, or simplicity is a slow process of small gestures and repeated commitment.
  9. 15:29 Closing blessing - He ends with a brief blessing to be patient with our own hearts and with others, and to keep one another honest.
