Guided Meditation: In the Presence of Love (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 45:12 2024-10-13

This guided meditation begins by settling the body with fuller breaths and a felt sense of love, then repeatedly returns to gravity, breath, and the present moment. Matthew Brensilver gently explores how hidden agendas, self-fixing, and projection can create tension, and he invites a more direct trust in awareness that is not transactional. The talk closes by widening practice into daily life, with an emphasis on tenderness, walking, and letting wisdom and love meet the human condition.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Settling into breath - The practice opens by softening the breath and sensing a wave of awareness and love across the body.
  2. 01:18 Giving over to gravity - Brensilver invites surrender to gravity and Dharma, gradually orienting the body and heart toward ease.
  3. 05:14 Hidden agendas - He names subtle agendas to avoid pain, improve practice, or control experience, and shows how they interfere with contact.
  4. 11:17 Nothing to fix - The talk turns toward the idea that the defective self is not something to manage, but a symptom of deeper pain.
  5. 16:09 Nontransactional awareness - Awareness is presented as something that does not bargain for results, but rests simply in the present.
  6. 21:01 Letting reference points go - He explores how projection and craving sustain a sense of continuity, then asks the listener to relax fixed points of identity.
  7. 30:12 Falling into love - The practice deepens into surrender, with faith that love will catch what is released into the present moment.
  8. 38:27 Love in daily life - In the closing reflections, he speaks about tears, poignancy, wisdom, and bringing the softness of practice into walking and everyday tasks.
