Dharmette: Five Spiritual Faculties: Samadhi (4 of 5)

AudioDharma 15:13 2023-01-26

Matthew Brensilver explores samadhi as more than narrow concentration, describing it as a gathered, receptive, and internally unified mind. He moves through how samadhi relates to mindfulness, why it depends on letting go rather than force, and how it can support faith and deeper insight. The talk closes by suggesting that learning on a stable mind leaves a deeper mark and lasts longer in the body and practice.

Chapters

  1. 00:09 What Samadhi Is - Brensilver introduces the five faculties and begins by asking how mindfulness and samadhi relate.
  2. 01:29 Not Separate Practices - He uses Thanissaro Bhikkhu and the Buddha’s teaching to argue that mindfulness and concentration are not opposed.
  3. 03:18 Attention and Receptivity - Shinzen Young’s three-part definition and images of spotlighting, aiming, and opening the hand frame samadhi as poised receptivity.
  4. 04:53 Aligned Inner Energies - He describes samadhi as a state where intentions align, impulses quiet, and the heart no longer needs to cope in the same way.
  5. 07:43 Faith Through Stillness - Samadhi is presented as a source of confidence in practice, showing that the path is real and not something only for others.
  6. 09:32 Letting Go, Not Forcing - He cautions against trying to cling into stillness and emphasizes that concentration is a pleasure born of letting go.
  7. 11:38 Ground for Insight - Brensilver turns to samadhi’s role in making the mind fertile for learning, so insight into impermanence and self can land more deeply.
  8. 14:16 Closing Encouragement - He offers the talk as something to take or leave, noting that people differ in how easily samadhi comes and ending with gentle encouragement to continue.
