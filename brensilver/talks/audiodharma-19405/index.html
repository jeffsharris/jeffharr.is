Dharmette: Effort & Effortlessness

AudioDharma 15:21 2023-12-12

Matthew Brensilver reflects on the tension between effort and effortlessness in Dharma practice, arguing that early training requires energy but that practice cannot be sustained by willpower alone. He describes how effort can tangle with self-view, how habits and the nervous system gradually become allies, and how awareness and wholesome action can become more natural over time. A cited passage from William James helps frame the talk as a movement from strain toward trained ease.

Chapters

  1. 00:17 Opening the pair - He introduces the theme of effort and effortlessness by linking it with yesterday's pair of confidence and humility.
  2. 00:55 Why effort matters - He explains why practice takes energy: feeling experience directly, renouncing immediate comfort, and working against evolutionary habits.
  3. 02:44 Renunciation and honey - He explores the 'honey tip and poisoned root' of painful habits and how giving them up requires effort against the stream.
  4. 04:32 When effort becomes selfing - He turns to the ways striving can merge with self-view, making effort feel tied to a defended and inadequate self.
  5. 07:28 Limits of willfulness - He asks what effort looks like beyond willfulness and argues that constant pressure is not sustainable.
  6. 10:09 William James on habit - A William James passage is used to show that education and practice work by ingraining habits and making the nervous system an ally.
  7. 11:16 Effortless awareness - He distinguishes marshaling attention from the quieter access to awareness that does not burn energy in the same way.
  8. 13:31 Practice as conditioning - He closes by describing Dharma as training the body-mind so wholesomeness becomes more pleasurable, letting go feels easier, and love becomes the default.
