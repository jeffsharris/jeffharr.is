Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Tranquility

AudioDharma 28:09 2024-09-18

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation oriented toward settling the body into tranquility without losing aliveness. The practice moves from posture and breath into the belly, then into working gently with hyper-arousal, urgency, and scattered attention. Later he invites resting with whatever pleasantness appears so that ease and tranquility can reinforce one another.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Settling in - He begins by orienting the body and naming how to work with sleepiness or restlessness.
  2. 01:20 Posture and gravity - The guidance turns to posture, giving the body to gravity and letting the spine rise gently.
  3. 02:58 Dropping to the belly - He invites attention down from the head into the belly and explores a softer center of gravity.
  4. 04:47 Softening the headspace - The practice emphasizes releasing tension and noticing the perspective that seems to live in the head.
  5. 07:31 Working with arousal - He names the body’s hyper-arousal and urges the listener to meet active zones with breath and awareness.
  6. 10:38 Urgency and false alarms - The talk reframes everyday agitation as something to acknowledge without automatically obeying.
  7. 15:58 Accountability without blame - He encourages radical but blameless attention to what body and mind are doing moment by moment.
  8. 21:46 Following pleasantness - The meditation shifts toward any thread of comfort, inviting the listener to rest in it and let tranquility grow.
