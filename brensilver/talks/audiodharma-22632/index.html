Evening Reflections

AudioDharma 06:41 2025-03-22

In this short evening reflection, Matthew Brensilver invites a simple shift of orientation: to appreciate one’s own goodness without turning that into self-indulgence. He explores the tension around self-love, deserving, shame, and pride, and then points toward a more basic sense of worthiness that comes before those judgments. The talk closes by encouraging that felt sense of goodness to accompany the listener into the night, sleep, and dreams.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Appreciating Goodness - The talk opens by saying it is not self-indulgent to appreciate your own goodness, even briefly.
  2. 01:27 Loving Yourself - The reflection extends the same invitation into self-love and notes that this can feel unfamiliar or charged.
  3. 02:16 Beyond Debates - The speaker names the inner arguments about deserving and undeserving, along with the pull of pride and shame.
  4. 03:34 A Deeper Worthiness - Rather than staying with moral judgments, the talk points to a kind of worthiness that comes before deserving and undeserving.
  5. 04:24 Carry It Into Sleep - The practice becomes an evening blessing: let that goodness follow you into bed, into the mind, and into dreams.
  6. 05:38 Closing Thanks - The talk ends quietly with thanks.
