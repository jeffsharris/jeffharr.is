Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Life without Words

AudioDharma 41:37 2025-03-19

Matthew Brensilver leads a quiet guided meditation centered on the question of what life feels like without words. The practice moves from noticing the mind’s urge to narrativize experience, through working with urgency and craving, into a long stretch of silence and a gradual turn toward stillness. Near the end, he explicitly frames the meditation in terms of relinquishing ownership and self-making, invoking the Buddha’s instruction to give up what is not yours.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Opening question - The meditation begins by asking what life would feel like without words, setting the tone of inquiry and nonverbal attention.
  2. 01:46 Meaning and narration - He reflects on how words make meaning, and how practice can loosen the compulsion to turn each moment into a story.
  3. 04:31 Sense experience - Attention is guided toward raw sense experience and the habit of checking what is okay and not okay.
  4. 06:18 Fewer words - The instruction narrows toward breathing and repeated reminders to use fewer and fewer words.
  5. 11:29 Working with urgency - A pool of feeling and urgency is acknowledged, with an invitation to listen without being overtaken by it.
  6. 15:42 Silence pervades - A long silent stretch deepens the practice, including the reminder that nothing needs to be acquired, proved, or become anyone.
  7. 24:24 Give up ownership - The Buddha reference to giving up what is not yours opens into a repeated inquiry about ownership, answered with 'everything.'
  8. 36:44 Stillness as object - The practice turns to stillness directly, with repeated instructions to take stillness as the object and not go anywhere.
  9. 40:20 Closing and transition - The meditation ends by easing out of formal practice while staying with the body, the visual field, and a non-grasping attitude.
