Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Expansiveness of Awe

AudioDharma 16:04 2025-04-02

Matthew Brensilver reflects on awe as a kind of pleasant disorientation that widens experience rather than narrowing it. He moves from nature and virtuosity to moral beauty, the history of the Dharma, and the spaciousness that can appear when suffering and convention are seen more lightly. The talk closes by linking awe with freedom, love, and a less claustrophobic way of living.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Disorientation and Awe - He begins by reframing disorientation as something Dharma practice can acclimatize us to, then introduces awe as a pleasant but slightly unsettling widening of mind.
  2. 01:19 What Awe Does - Using Dacher Keltner’s definition, he describes awe as encounter with something vast and notes its links to generosity, nature, art, and moral elevation.
  3. 02:56 Nature, Skill, Goodness - He gives examples from mountains, Simone Biles, and ordinary goodness to show how awe can come through landscape, virtuosity, and moral beauty.
  4. 05:10 Beyond Claustrophobia - Awe is presented as a way out of the claustrophobic self, opening a broader sense of life, practice, and lineage.
  5. 06:37 Dharma Across Time - He turns to gratitude for the Dharma and recalls an early retreat experience that made the vastness of practice feel emotionally overwhelming in a good way.
  6. 08:33 Closeness of Peace - Awe becomes tied to the proximity of peace, the preciousness of human life, and the strangeness of simply being here at all.
  7. 10:56 Freedom and Convention - Drawing on existential psychotherapy and the fetter of attachment to convention, he asks how to live when so much of what feels solid is self-made.
  8. 13:24 Stars and Smallness - A retreat memory under the stars becomes a meditation on vast space and time, making personal worries feel small and life feel more open.
  9. 15:32 Living in Awe - He closes by suggesting that we do not fully acclimate to awe and that its openness can remain a living question rather than a settled state.
