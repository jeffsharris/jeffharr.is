Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Morning sit with instruction (Retreat at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 59:53 2022-08-03

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided morning sit centered on three facets of practice: attitude, attention, and awareness. The instruction moves from goodwill and bodily softening into several ways of placing attention—breath, whole body, visual field, and soundscape—while repeatedly returning to the easeful knowing that does not depend on effort. Later he turns to questions from retreatants, especially around pain, eating, and walking meditation, and emphasizes gentleness, imperfection, and the limits of control.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and orientation - He briefly orients the group to listen and settle into the morning sit.
  2. 00:44 Three facets of practice - He introduces attitude, attentional focus, and effortless awareness as the core frame for the meditation.
  3. 04:20 Softening the body - The guidance turns toward goodwill, innocence, and relaxing the face, shoulders, belly, and spine.
  4. 08:14 Breath and stability - He offers breath-based anchors, explains why steadiness matters, and encourages noting and returning when doubt or distraction arise.
  5. 19:14 Whole-body and sound - The sit widens from the nostrils to whole-body breath, then to open listening, mental imagery, and the soundscape.
  6. 29:50 Resting in awareness - He points to what remains when the effortful spotlight is dropped and asks what is effortless and persisting.
  7. 36:17 Questions on practice - Retreatant questions begin, with attention to walking, pain, and how to work with discomfort without hardening.
  8. 43:56 Eating and wanting - He speaks candidly about eating on retreat, pleasure, longing, control, and the ways practice meets ordinary desire.
  9. 53:34 Walking and closing - He closes with brief guidance on walking meditation, then makes retreat announcements and sends the group onward.
