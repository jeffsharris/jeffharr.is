Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Faith in Our Dharma Path (1 of 5)

AudioDharma 12:44 2024-03-11

Matthew Brensilver reflects on faith as something quieter and sturdier than belief or optimism. He traces his own suspicion of the word, then shows how practice can become a surrender that does not deny pain or uncertainty. The talk moves toward trust in awareness, truth, love, and a sense of belonging in the Dharma rather than in borrowed views.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Opening the Theme - Matthew introduces the week’s theme of faith in Dharma practice and begins from his own hesitation around the word.
  2. 00:46 From Suspicion to Surrender - He describes early practice as both intoxicating and carefully approached, with faith emerging through a willingness to let go.
  3. 02:37 What Faith Is Not - Matthew distinguishes faith from naïve hope, denial, or pretending, and argues that Dharma asks for openness rather than self-deception.
  4. 04:35 Dropping Defensiveness - He reframes faith as a heart gesture of surrender that can tolerate not knowing and places the question of where we invest our hope.
  5. 06:19 The Path in the Heart - Using Ajahn Sucitto’s teaching, Matthew suggests the path truly begins when the Buddha enters the heart, and links faith with a grounded optimism about human potential.
  6. 08:09 Truth Without Harm - He notes that awareness can reveal painful realities while still deepening love and confidence that truth may hurt but need not damage the heart.
  7. 10:10 Falling into Trust - Matthew closes with faith as the willingness to fall, trusting that love, awareness, and the Dharma will catch us and make belonging possible.
  8. 12:16 Closing Blessing - He names the week’s themes—faith emerging from doubt, faith in love, confidence in oneself, and faith in letting go—and offers a brief farewell.
