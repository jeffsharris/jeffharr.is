Guided Meditation: Relaxing and Letting Feeling be Feeling

Dharma Seed 37:39 2025-09-17

This guided meditation invites a simple, careful relationship to pleasant and unpleasant feeling: know it as it is, and notice what softens when it no longer has to mean something about you. The instruction moves from relaxing obvious tension into a narrower and then wider field of attention, including breath, body, and difficult experience with a light touch of kindness. It ends with a quiet emphasis on not bracing against experience and on the felt necessity of love.

Chapters

  1. 00:01 Know Feeling Simply - The meditation begins by naming pleasant and unpleasant feeling as they are, without needing to do anything else with them.
  2. 01:22 Relax the Urgency - Matthew repeats the invitation to let sensations mean less about identity, and notices how this allows the mind to relax.
  3. 05:24 Soften the Body - Attention turns to relaxing what can be relaxed, especially tension in the mind, jaw, and belly.
  4. 08:53 Feel the Breath - The guidance shifts toward the felt body and the natural rhythm of breathing, with subtle imagery used to support direct sensation.
  5. 14:16 Uncontrived Breathing - The breath is approached as unforced and uncontrolled, with tension understood as a cue for further softening.
  6. 18:06 Subtle In-Breath and Out - The in-breath and out-breath are noticed for their small effects of brightening and soothing, with attention to subtlety.
  7. 21:25 Widen the Aperture - The practice alternates between a small focus on the breath and a wider field that can hold pain, fear, fatigue, and confusion with mindfulness and love.
  8. 28:35 Radical Inclusion - The teaching states that Dharma does not ask us to pretend, but to include what is here and find a sense of belonging with experience.
  9. 33:50 Necessity of Love - Near the end of the sit, the talk points toward the simple dawning that love is necessary, and closes with a final reminder not to brace.
