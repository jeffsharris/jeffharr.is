Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Monday Night Meditation Talk (Drop-in at Spirit Rock)

Dharma Seed 65:23 2017-10-23

Matthew Brensilver explores delusion as the hardest of the three poisons to notice, moving from recent research on greed, aversion, and confusion into close, practical reflections on how self-story, certainty, and secrecy keep the mind from seeing clearly. He folds in voices from Catherine Schultz, U Tejaniya, Philip Moffitt, and others to show how honesty, humility, and feedback can become part of meditation rather than obstacles to it. The talk closes with a quiet emphasis on learning from life without self-punishment, and on how that learning can soften the heart.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Research and the three poisons - Matthew begins by connecting Dharma language with contemporary research on greed, aversion, and delusion, and introduces the talk’s focus on confusion.
  2. 03:14 Delusion as habit - He explores how greed and aversion are easier to spot than delusion, and why ignorance is the deeper pattern underneath them.
  3. 08:09 Water we cannot see - Using David Foster Wallace and Catherine Schultz, he shows how our default sense of being right makes delusion hard to recognize.
  4. 11:52 Willingness to be wrong - Matthew turns toward practice as a gradual thawing: interest, humility, and the willingness to be wrong open the path to clearer seeing.
  5. 17:48 Wise view and self-story - He describes shifting the frame from ordinary judgments to suffering and freedom, and noticing how greed and aversion are often rationalized by story.
  6. 26:41 Meditating with honesty - With U Tejaniya, he names delusion as a labeler of experience, and with Freud and Sam Harris he points toward secrecy, honesty, and feedback as supports for practice.
  7. 34:23 Disruption as opportunity - Matthew reflects on moments when the self-story is rattled, then offers Shinzen Young’s advice to stop fighting and to take feedback as a central discipline.
  8. 47:48 Humility in the heart - In questions, he returns to pain, innocence, and the way practice can soften rather than harden the heart over time.
  9. 55:47 Keep looking - The closing exchange widens the theme to teachers, correction, and social life, ending with a quiet encouragement to keep learning from life.
