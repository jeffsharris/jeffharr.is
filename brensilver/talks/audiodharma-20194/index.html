Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: The Clinging Before Clinging

AudioDharma 14:21 2024-04-17

Matthew Brensilver reflects on how clinging can begin long before obvious craving or aversion appears. Drawing on the Dhammapada, dependent origination, and a Zen saying about the disappearing subject, he traces how subtle volitional habits shape perception, selfing, and the feeling that the world has hardened into fixed things. The talk closes by pointing toward relinquishment: a simpler, less comparative way of meeting experience that is both disenchanted and quietly amazed.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Returning to old loves - Brensilver opens by reflecting on how practice can bring old interests back into view and sets up a philosophical pivot about mind and reality.
  2. 00:48 Mind and idealism - He recalls a philosophy class, mentions Berkeley, and contrasts idealism with the Buddha's emphasis on the reality of things while still treating suffering as plastic and workable.
  3. 02:12 Experience is shaped - Using the Dhammapada, he argues that mind precedes and mediates experience, making suffering more fluid than we tend to assume.
  4. 03:25 Clinging before clinging - He distinguishes obvious grasping from the subtler clinging that arises before contact and feeling, when the mind is already leaning toward experience.
  5. 05:00 Models that never idle - Brensilver describes how our world-models keep reiterating themselves moment by moment and how dependent origination often unfolds before we notice it.
  6. 06:39 Formative energies - He introduces sankharas as formative energies, using Ajahn Sucitto's phrasing to show how motivations and attention shape consciousness before coarse craving appears.
  7. 08:03 Making the world solid - The talk turns to how subtle wanting divides the world into useful and harmful, reifies the self, and generates a sense of dualistic alienation.
  8. 10:44 Relinquishment and simplicity - He quotes a Zen saying about the disappearance of the subject, then explains how relaxing subtle clinging weakens measuring, comparing, and narrativizing.
  9. 13:03 Wonder without selfing - The closing points toward surrender, simplicity, and the amazement that can remain when the heart's gesture of relinquishment is not turned into another self-project.
