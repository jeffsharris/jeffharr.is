Dharmette: Functions of dharma pleasure

AudioDharma 15:41 2024-06-05

Matthew Brensilver reflects on the role of wholesome pleasure in Dharma practice, beginning with a story about a longtime practitioner who argues that people need teaching on pleasure, not only impermanence and suffering. He then names several kinds of Dharma pleasure—path, sangha, ethical alignment, non-shame, love, concentration, and reverence—and explains how they support steadiness, broaden attention, and help practice continue. The talk closes by suggesting that pleasure can make room for purification and for digesting pain, rather than feeding craving.

Chapters

  1. 00:09 Why teach pleasure - Matthew opens with an anecdote that reframes Dharma teaching around wholesome pleasure rather than only suffering and uncertainty.
  2. 02:07 Kinds of Dharma pleasure - He names several forms of Dharma pleasure, from trusting the path to being seen in sangha and aligning ethics with action.
  3. 03:22 Non-shame and love - The talk turns to non-shame, fearless self-investigation, and the pleasure of kindness, compassion, joy, and gratitude.
  4. 05:46 Concentration and reverence - Matthew describes the pleasure of samadhi and reverence, and briefly invokes the Buddha’s line that peace is the highest happiness.
  5. 07:06 What pleasure does - He shifts from listing pleasures to asking how they function, arguing that Dharma pleasure keeps practice going without reinforcing craving.
  6. 09:09 Pleasure as support - The talk explains how pleasure can replace compulsive sense chasing, consolidate motivation, and prepare the mind for deeper growth.
  7. 12:06 Broadening awareness - Matthew contrasts the narrowing effect of pain with the widening effect of pleasure, which opens the field of awareness and insight.
  8. 13:53 Making room for pain - He closes by suggesting that deepening pleasure gives more room for purification and helps us digest difficult experience without fear.
