Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Morning Instruction: Equanimity precedes Mindfulness

AudioDharma 43:50 2025-06-17

Matthew Brensilver offers a quiet morning instruction centered on the idea that equanimity comes before mindfulness: first loosening the habit of control, then becoming available to the present moment. He returns again and again to breathing, forgiveness of imperfection, and the way awareness can be simple, effortless, and nonjudgmental. The talk gradually moves from instruction to a gentle invitation to notice the beginning and ending of each breath, and to stay open even when experience includes helplessness, fear, or aversion.

Chapters

  1. 00:27 Befriending the Moment - The talk opens by inviting a simple friendliness toward this moment rather than immediate improvement.
  2. 01:24 The Urge to Optimize - Brensilver names the deep impulse to tweak conditions and explains how that keeps us from seeing the present clearly.
  3. 03:57 Equanimity Before Mindfulness - He develops the claim that equanimity precedes mindfulness and that relinquishing control makes the moment newly available.
  4. 07:27 Effortless Knowing - The teaching turns toward awareness and breathing as processes that do their work without demand, asking us to notice ease.
  5. 11:02 Forgiving Imperfection - He emphasizes forgiving the world, bodily alarm, craving, fear, and self-judgment so attention can settle more simply.
  6. 15:14 The Innocence of Mind - The talk deepens into a more compassionate view of mind, where even greed and delusion can be held without blame.
  7. 21:23 Drifting and Returning - He describes unconsciousness as losing contact with how the mind represents the world, then points back to the breath as a way to reconnect.
  8. 27:37 Beginning and Ending - The instruction narrows to noticing the start and finish of each inhalation and exhalation, especially as connection begins to fray.
  9. 33:00 Helplessness and Open Heart - In the closing movement, he asks listeners to soften around helplessness, keep the heart open, and offer all of it up to awareness.
