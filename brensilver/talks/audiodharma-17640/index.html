Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Five Spiritual Faculties - Energy (2 of 5)

AudioDharma 14:02 2023-01-24

Matthew Brensilver reflects on the energy faculty as a middle way between forceful striving and passivity. He moves from a recent retreat theme of doing and non-doing into a careful look at how willpower can tangle with self-view, then offers a quieter definition of right effort as relaxed perseverance guided by wisdom. The talk closes by connecting habit, craving, and the energy needed to put down suffering’s causes rather than just endure their effects.

Chapters

  1. 00:13 From Faith to Energy - He introduces the second faculty and situates it in the larger sequence of the five faculties.
  2. 00:32 Doing and Non-Doing - A recent retreat with Inez Friedman becomes a concrete frame for the balance between effortful practice and letting go.
  3. 01:30 The Buddha's Relentless Effort - He quotes the Buddha on sustained striving, then asks what kind of effort this actually points to.
  4. 02:38 When Effort Becomes Self - The talk turns toward the ways willfulness, conceit, and spiritual comparison can get woven into practice.
  5. 05:21 Witnessing the Limits - Matthew describes the pain that comes with self-harshness and the need to see where willpower fails.
  6. 07:14 Relaxed Perseverance - He offers underdoing, right effort with wisdom, and continuous awareness as a gentler form of energy.
  7. 08:42 Why Effort Matters - Effort is placed within the suttas and within ordinary life, where habits and neurosis often run on their own.
  8. 09:58 Habit and Craving - William James and Shantideva help explain how patterns repeat, why clinging feels reinforced, and why renunciation takes energy.
  9. 12:58 Loving the Causes Less - He closes by saying energy is often about loosening our attachment to the causes of suffering and sourcing effort from goodness.
