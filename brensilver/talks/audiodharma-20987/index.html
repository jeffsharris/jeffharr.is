Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Pervasiveness of Craving

AudioDharma 29:13 2024-07-31

Matthew Brensilver leads a quiet guided meditation on craving, control, and the tendency to shape experience. The practice moves from settling the body into a posture of surrender, to noticing the tension of holding on, to softening around heartache and the wish for life to be different. He repeatedly returns to the possibility that awareness can be trusted, and that this moment can be received as a source of wisdom rather than a problem to solve.

Chapters

  1. 00:56 Settling Into Posture - The practice begins with posture, breath, and the balance between alertness and tranquility.
  2. 03:21 Noticing Holding On - He names craving, clinging, and the tension created by trying to control experience.
  3. 05:59 Surrendering to This Moment - The guidance turns toward accepting imperfection, letting go of self-shaping, and relaxing.
  4. 09:03 Breath as Pleasure - Attention to breathing is reframed as a simple pleasure rather than a task or test.
  5. 13:12 Responsibility Without Power - He reflects on the anxiety of feeling responsible for life while not fully in control of it.
  6. 15:30 Trusting Awareness - The meditation asks whether awareness can be trusted, while allowing coping without self-judgment.
  7. 20:21 Receiving Wisdom - In the closing stretch, the repeated invitation is to let this moment be received and trusted as wisdom.
