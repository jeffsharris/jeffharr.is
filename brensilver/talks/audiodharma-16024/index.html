Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Mindfulness, Dharma, Mental Health & Science Part 1 of 2

AudioDharma 1:07:12 2022-04-02

Matthew Brensilver explores how mindfulness has moved between Dharma, science, medicine, and mental health, without treating any one frame as the whole story. He looks at the growth of meditation research, the public-health use of mindfulness, and the question of what gets preserved or lost when contemplative practice enters clinical and secular settings. The talk moves from William James and Maslow to Marsha Linehan and Jon Kabat-Zinn, then opens into a discussion of attrition, approach orientation, and whether reverence for the sacred can coexist with empiricism.

Chapters

  1. 00:12 Opening the question - Brensilver sets up the morning’s theme and points toward the larger conversation between meditation, science, and practice.
  2. 01:00 James and attention - He uses William James to frame attention as trainable and to suggest that contemplative practice may open forms of consciousness beyond ordinary waking mind.
  3. 03:27 Meditation in public life - The talk turns to the rise of meditation in research and everyday use, including how apps, digital tools, and wellness language shape who comes to practice.
  4. 08:52 Well-being and dukkha - Brensilver broadens the frame from clinical suffering to the ordinary range of dukkha, arguing that Dharma matters both for intense distress and for the subtle dissatisfaction of normal life.
  5. 11:40 Dharma and science - He asks how to situate Buddhism with empiricism, medicine, and psychology, and offers the idea that dukkha itself is an interdisciplinary problem.
  6. 15:55 Ways of relating - He reviews several possible stances between traditions—separation, competition, non-overlapping domains, and selective appropriation—before favoring a real conversation that allows mutual influence.
  7. 25:10 Linehan as example - Marsha Linehan’s suffering, clinical work, and workbook dedication are used as an example of a sincere integration that keeps compassion and skillful means at the center.
  8. 30:38 Mindfulness programs - He reads from a 2016 paper on mindfulness-based programs and Kabat-Zinn’s account of MBSR as one skillful means, then addresses questions about endurance, dosage, and what approach orientation really means.
  9. 41:00 Questions and tensions - In the Q&A, Brensilver responds to concerns about health-framed mindfulness, betrayal of Dharma, and the role of Buddhist language, ending with a nuanced defense of reverence and sincere integration.
