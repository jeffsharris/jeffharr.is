Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Dharma Practice & Beginning Again

AudioDharma 14:00 2023-02-17

Matthew Brensilver reflects on practice as something non-linear, iterative, and repeatedly renewed. He moves from mindfulness as memory to the ordinary ways we forget, make mistakes, get discouraged, and then begin again in practice, in relationships, and in life. The talk closes by softening perfectionism and encouraging a simple commitment to start, continue, and trust the path without needing to finish it all at once.

Chapters

  1. 00:51 An Iterative Path - He introduces his final theme as one more factor in a made-up list, emphasizing that Dharma understanding keeps changing and that comfort can turn into stagnation.
  2. 02:32 Beginning Again - He describes practice as a continuous feedback loop and says the path is not linear, so beginning again is necessary at both the level of meditation and the level of life.
  3. 05:21 Remembering the Dharma - He connects mindfulness with memory, suggesting that suffering and despair can feel like forgetting, and that practice begins again through remembering.
  4. 06:57 Messes and Forgiveness - He turns to greed, hate, delusion, embarrassment, and guilt, then reframes them as part of learning and calls for complete self-forgiveness before beginning again.
  5. 08:15 Relationships and Loose Ends - He applies the same attitude to conflict and unresolved relationships, acknowledging that some things can be worked through while others must be left as they are.
  6. 09:20 No Perfect Finish - Citing Norman Fisher, he says it is hard being human and invites listeners to release the demand for a perfect outcome and simply start.
  7. 10:32 Changing Intentions - He speaks about how practice motivations shift over time, how old interests can be left behind, and how practice may bring back earlier loves in a new form.
  8. 12:17 Each Moment Anew - He closes by returning to the larger point that practice begins again in each moment and thanks the group for their flexibility and support.
