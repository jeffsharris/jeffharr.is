Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Dharmette: Surrender

AudioDharma 16:23 2025-04-03

Matthew Brensilver reflects on surrender as a quiet but central part of practice: not resignation, but a willing release of control, habit, and the need to know. He moves from Leonard Cohen and St. Augustine into a Buddhist account of mindfulness, samadhi, and insight as forms of letting the self soften. The talk stays grounded in ordinary practice, where surrender becomes a way to meet experience honestly and find a different kind of power.

Chapters

  1. 00:31 Cohen and the search - Matthew opens with Leonard Cohen as an image of a life where striving gives way and even the search dissolves.
  2. 01:17 Restless heart - He turns to St. Augustine to evoke longing, humility, and the wish to be enlarged and remade by what is greater than the self.
  3. 02:42 Surrender in Dharma - Matthew reframes surrender in Buddhist terms as yielding to the moment, love, not-self, and the path rather than to a deity.
  4. 03:32 Discernment and trust - He balances surrender with the Kalama Sutta’s emphasis on verification, while warning that surrender can be distorted by bad ideas, habits, or people.
  5. 05:59 The will to control - Using Carl Rogers and his own language, he describes how practice often serves the ego until a phase shift begins and control starts to loosen.
  6. 08:16 Changed by experience - Matthew explores the point when the ego runs out of moves and practice becomes an openness to being changed by the moment and the breath.
  7. 11:08 Not knowing and refuge - He links surrender with letting go of ownership, relinquishing stories that no longer serve, and resting in groundlessness without losing dignity.
  8. 13:33 Gateway to absorption - Near the end, he describes how effort can become agitation at the threshold of samadhi and jhana, where trust in silence and the present becomes essential.
  9. 15:24 True power - Matthew closes by saying that the path still cultivates power, but the deepest power appears through surrender rather than control.
