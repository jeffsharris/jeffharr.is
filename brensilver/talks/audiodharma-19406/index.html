Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Renouncing the Narrative Impulse

AudioDharma 30:15 2023-12-12

This guided meditation invites steady attention into bodily feeling and the tendency to turn sensation into a story. Matthew Brensilver emphasizes renouncing the narrative impulse—not by suppressing thought, but by meeting feeling as feeling and softening the reflex to search for meaning, threat, or command. The practice gradually opens toward defenselessness, spaciousness, and the possibility that nothing needs to happen right now.

Chapters

  1. 00:00 Opening and Welcome - The meditation begins with a brief welcome and settling into the shared sit.
  2. 00:50 Inviting Goodwill - He turns attention toward the guided meditation and the sense of collective goodwill and gratitude.
  3. 03:13 Landing in the Body - The guidance emphasizes breathing, arriving in the body, and becoming available to the present moment.
  4. 06:56 Feeling Without Story - He points to the body's feeling-circuits and explains the practice of letting feeling remain feeling without adding narrative.
  5. 10:23 Narrative and Vigilance - The talk names the natural habit of interpreting feeling through threat, opportunity, longing, and aversion, and the vulnerability of renouncing that habit.
  6. 16:18 Experimenting with Defenselessness - The meditation asks for direct contact with feeling, softening compulsive energies and the felt need to make something happen.
  7. 20:50 Nothing Needs to Happen - He guides a sustained period of resting with experience, opening to the possibility that nothing is required in this moment.
  8. 27:46 Distilling Wisdom - The closing notes suggest that this way of meeting feeling helps separate wisdom from compulsivity and supports living well.
