Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Monday Night Dharma Talk

Dharma Seed 55:53 2018-12-10

Matthew Brensilver explores grief as a central mechanism of Dharma practice: not a detour from awakening, but part of how the heart matures through letting go. He moves from a retreat on anger that revealed grief, through poetry and teacher quotations, into a clear argument that non-clinging often feels like loss before it feels like freedom. The talk widens from personal practice to collective life, then closes with comments and a short period of sitting that emphasizes radical permission for whatever the heart is carrying.

Chapters

  1. 00:54 Three Questions - Matthew frames the talk around three questions about mindfulness, usefulness, and mechanism, then says he will focus on how practice actually works.
  2. 02:21 Grief on Retreat - He tells the story of teaching an anger retreat where the room felt more like grief than anger, and uses that experience to name grieving as part of spiritual unfolding.
  3. 05:57 Why Grief Matters - He argues that consciously feeling grief is liberating and necessary, connecting it to compassion, insight, and the failure to grieve at both personal and collective levels.
  4. 10:36 Poetry of Loss - Leonard Cohen and Mary Oliver help him show how grief can break the heart open, enlarge love, and make impermanence feel intimate rather than abstract.
  5. 16:55 Letting Go - Using Joseph Goldstein and Steve Armstrong, he describes non-clinging as the painful unclenching of a fist and the humbling collapse of fantasies about control and identity.
  6. 22:24 Ownership and Gift - A passage by Andy Olinsky on ownership leads into a wider reflection on greed, delusion, and the way life becomes more like a gift once possession loosens.
  7. 28:26 No Steps For Grief - He quotes Leonard Cohen and Stephen Levine to show that grief has no fixed method, can only be met partially, and may hurt without damaging the heart.
  8. 34:35 The Valley of Lost Things - Reading Catherine Schultz, he presents loss as built into reality itself and says that noticing this makes attention, gratitude, and ethics more urgent.
  9. 40:25 Questions and Closing Sitting - After a brief exchange with the audience about mourning and presence, he leads a short sit and closes by blessing the heart's capacity to be softened by letting go.
