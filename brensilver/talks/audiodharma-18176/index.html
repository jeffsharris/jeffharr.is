Matthew Brensilver Dharma Talks

Guided Meditation: Listening to the Mind of Buddha

AudioDharma 29:46 2023-04-26

Matthew Brensilver offers a guided meditation that gradually shifts from settling into the room toward a practice of listening: first to breath, body, sound, and the darkness behind closed eyes, then to a deeper receptivity he links to Leonard Cohen’s hummingbird lines. The talk emphasizes trusting awareness, letting silence beckon, and resting without over-managing experience. It closes by naming practice as a way to stop endlessly coping and simply be awake.

Chapters

  1. 00:11 Arriving and Settling - A brief welcome leads into the invitation to sit and settle into stillness.
  2. 01:30 Silence Beckons - He names the silence and begins orienting attention toward the meditative field.
  3. 02:58 Simple Objects of Attention - The instruction turns to breath, body, sound, darkness behind the eyes, and possibly phrase or mantra as supports for attention.
  4. 05:04 Listening to Cohen - Leonard Cohen’s hummingbird lines become a doorway into the teaching about listening beyond the speaker and toward what does not need to be forced.
  5. 06:23 Trusting Awareness - He frames practice as listening to the mind of a Buddha, emphasizing receptivity and trust in awareness rather than effortful control.
  6. 15:23 Forgiveness and Rest - The meditation deepens into forgiving the mind, resting in silence, and meeting sorrow with a more spacious stance.
  7. 17:33 Patience and Urgency - He explores the balance between spiritual urgency and abiding patience as practice matures.
  8. 23:35 Beyond Coping - The close points to resting from endless coping, releasing the habit of sorting everything out, and finding wakefulness in simple being.
